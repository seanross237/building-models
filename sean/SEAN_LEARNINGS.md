## Focus / Productivity
- Once a thing is visualized and exists, it's easy to make small changes but hard to make structural changes. I get pigeonholed into seeing only what's in front of me.
- There are two modes of work: **in-the-weeds focus work** (deep, narrow, executing) and **expansion** (zooming out, big-picture envisioning, seeing beyond the current level). Caffeine drives me deeper into focus mode — great for grinding on a thing, but makes expansion harder.
- I can only do two expansions per day: one in the morning, one during sunset. Prioritizing counts as the morning expansion if I give myself spaciousness. Between those windows, it's focus work — I can grind hard within a context but shouldn't expect to zoom out into something new.
- I really like having quantifiable success metrics to work towards. They provide a positive feedback loop that keeps the engine fueled.
- Common pattern: I love philosophizing into the unknown and high-level planning, but when a project has 10 threads and some get bogged down in different types of work (debugging, UI, reviewing, etc.), I resist going in and cutting myself off from the higher levels. Ideal system: go straight from envisioning to a playable thing. Also — I should spend required time daily using the product and watching PostHog sessions. Would be useful to classify what types of next steps each thread needs, and develop an understanding of which work types I like and which I don't (accounting for the bias that I'm more excited about flavors similar to recent wins).
