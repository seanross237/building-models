JUSTIFICATION: The Thermal Time Hypothesis (TTH) posits that the modular flow of a state $\rho$ corresponds to the physical time evolution. While this holds for thermal equilibrium states, it fails for excited states. For an excited state, the modular flow $\sigma_t^\rho$ deviates from the physical Hamiltonian evolution $e^{-itH}$ because the modular Hamiltonian $K = -\ln \rho$ is not proportional to the physical Hamiltonian $H$. Specifically, for highly excited states, the discrepancy scales with the energy density or the excitation level, leading to a mismatch in the dynamical evolution. Furthermore, the modular flow is intrinsically tied to the preparation history of the state; since the modular Hamiltonian encodes the specific density matrix used to prepare the state, the resulting flow reflects the state's specific correlations rather than the universal Hamiltonian dynamics required for physical time evolution.
