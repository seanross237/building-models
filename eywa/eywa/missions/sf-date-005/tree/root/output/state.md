# State — Root Planner

## What I decided and why

4-step plan: three independent archetype date designs, then one synthesis step. This maps directly onto the "analysis tasks" pattern from the plan design guidance (N independent sub-analyses → synthesis). The goal statement explicitly calls this out: "multiple independent research tracks that should be investigated separately before synthesis."

I kept steps 1–3 fully independent so they can (eventually) run in parallel. Step 4 gates on all three.

## Scoring rationale

Steps 1–3 scored C=5, I=8 → product 40. At depth 1, adjusted to (5-1)×(8-1) = 4×7 = 28 < 30 → executor children. This is the right call: designing a single date itinerary with specific SF locations is well-defined creative research that a single executor can handle with web search.

Step 4 scored C=5, I=9 → product 45. Adjusted at depth 1: (5-1)×(9-1) = 4×8 = 32 > 30 → would become a planner. That's slightly more than I want for a synthesis step, but the importance is genuinely high — this is the meta-analytical deliverable the whole mission is building toward. If it sub-plans, the sub-plan would probably just be: (a) compare, (b) extract principles, (c) write — which is fine. Alternatively I could lower I to 8, making 4×7=28 → executor. I left it at 9 because synthesis failure would mean the whole mission output is incomplete.

## Alternatives considered

**Flat executor approach:** Could have made root an executor and just done all four tasks in one shot. Rejected because: the task explicitly asks for independent research tracks, and the three archetypes are genuinely independent — no reason to serialize them or let one color the others.

**5-step plan with separate "research SF venues" step:** Could have added a dedicated step to gather SF venue options before designing each date. Rejected because: the archetype-specific date design steps already naturally scope the venue research (adrenaline junkie step won't waste time researching bookstores). Splitting it out adds overhead without benefit.

**Separate "compare" and "extract principles" steps:** Could have made step 4 two steps. Rejected: too granular. A single executor agent can hold three date plans in context and synthesize without needing to sub-plan.

## Uncertainties

- SF venue currency: executor agents will need to search for real, currently operating venues. Some options I suggested in the goals (climbing gyms, specific restaurants) may have closed. The goals note "currently operating" so agents should verify.
- Depth-1 executor at C=5 is borderline detailed work — agents will need to write a full itinerary with timing, per-stop reasoning, and arc narrative. This is achievable but they should budget appropriate output length.

## What I'd do next

Watch step 4. If the synthesis executor produces thin principles ("have a theme," "pick good restaurants"), that's the weak point. Could replan step 4 as a planner with an explicit adversarial self-critique sub-step.

No replan needed. Proceeding to step 3.

**Decision: continue**

All four deliverables present: 4 primary named locations with addresses (Ferry Building/Heath Ceramics, Balmy Alley, Mission Chinese Food, Trick Dog) plus optional afternoon gallery/ceramics stops bringing it to 5-6. These are fixable details. The recurring venue accuracy pattern across all three steps reinforces that the synthesis step (step 4) should include a venue verification pass or at minimum flag unverified venues. This is something to note in step 4's input.

Quality comparison across all three: Step 3 has the strongest conceptual coherence and archetype reasoning. Step 2 had the most nuanced emotional arc. Step 1 had the most energetic physical arc. All three meet the bar. The synthesis step has strong material to work with.

All three independent steps now complete. Proceeding to step 4 (synthesis).

## Synthesis

All four children complete. Step 4 produced a comprehensive document containing:
- All three full itineraries (reproduced from steps 1–3)
- Per-archetype 5-dimension analysis (emotional arc, pacing, sensory engagement, location coherence, archetype alignment)
- Comparative synthesis across all three dates
- 6 reusable design principles
- 4 meta-insights about human psychology and date design
- A generalizable formula for designing dates for any archetype

The synthesis quality is high — the extracted principles are sharp and specific ("Transition points matter more than destinations," "No forced fun or performance anxiety"), not generic platitudes. The meta-analysis identifies genuine cross-cutting patterns (all three dates escalate through acts of witnessing; all optimize for depth over breadth; all create conditions for archetype-specific vulnerability).

Venue accuracy caveat added to root final output. The design work and analytical principles are structurally sound regardless of individual venue substitutions.

## What surprised me

Nothing surprising in the decomposition — this is a clean analysis-then-synthesis task. The main risk was output quality at the executor level, not structural. All three executors exceeded the bar, and the synthesis captured genuine cross-cutting insights rather than restating what each date did.
