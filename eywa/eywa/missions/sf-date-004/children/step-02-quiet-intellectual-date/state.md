# State — step-02-quiet-intellectual-date

## What I decided and why
Executed directly. Single-domain creative task with clear deliverables — splitting would have been over-engineering.

## What I considered and rejected
- **Planning route:** Considered decomposing into "research locations" + "write itinerary" + "verify details." Rejected because the research and writing are inseparable for this kind of task — you design the narrative arc and select locations simultaneously, not sequentially.
- **Richmond District route:** Considered building the date around the Columbarium + Green Apple Books + Sutro Baths/Lands End in the Richmond. Rejected because the North Beach literary corridor is more walkable, more cohesive thematically, and has a richer density of stops for a book/history person. The Richmond route would require more transit time between stops and lose the narrative thread.
- **Including The Interval at Long Now:** A bar/museum by the Long Now Foundation at Fort Mason — perfect fit for an intellectual. Excluded because I'm uncertain whether it's still operating at the same location as of 2026, and Fort Mason would break the walkable geographic flow.
- **Including Vesuvio Cafe:** Iconic Beat bar next to City Lights. Excluded in favor of Specs' because Vesuvio is better known and more tourist-visited. Specs' is the hidden-gem version of the same experience.

## My hunches
- The Mechanics' Institute opener is the strongest single choice in the itinerary. It sets the tone perfectly and most people don't know it exists.
- The "buy each other a book" move at City Lights is the emotional centerpiece — it's the moment that turns browsing into something personal.
- The Filbert Steps stop might be weather-dependent in practice. I included a weather note but if someone follows this in January rain, that stop won't work.

## What I would do next
N/A — execution complete. Result is written and self-contained.

## What surprised me
Nothing major. The itinerary came together naturally because North Beach is genuinely one of the best neighborhoods in any US city for this personality type. The hard part was restraint — there are more good stops than a single afternoon can hold.

## What uncertainty remains
- **Business hours/closures:** I used locations that are long-established institutions, but any of them could have changed hours, closed, or moved. The Mechanics' Institute, City Lights, Specs', and Coit Tower are all decades-old, so probability of closure is low but nonzero.
- **Ideale restaurant:** Less certain this is still open compared to the other stops. Tosca Cafe is provided as an alternative.
- **Coit Tower mural hours:** Noted as variable by season. Could be closed by the time the itinerary reaches it (~5:15 PM). Included as optional with a bypass.
