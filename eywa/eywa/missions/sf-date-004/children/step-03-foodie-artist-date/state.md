## Key decisions
- **Execute, not plan.** Single-domain creative task with clear success criteria. Decomposing would have been over-splitting.
- **Mister Jiu's over Palette Tea House for dinner.** Palette's rainbow xiaolongbao are more "Instagram foodie," but Mister Jiu's has the stronger total package: Michelin star, stunning historic space, deeper culinary substance, and the Waverly Place location enables the dusk walk that ties the evening together. For a foodie *artist* (not just a foodie), the space matters as much as the plate.
- **Vesuvio over True Laurel for closing drinks.** True Laurel has a better cocktail program, but Vesuvio has irreplaceable atmosphere and is geographically perfect at the end of the Chinatown→North Beach walk. True Laurel would require backtracking to the Mission. Vesuvio's maximalist interior also provides better contrast to the refined earlier stops — it's a more satisfying narrative close.
- **Dropped 20th Century Cafe.** Confirmed closed via Yelp search. Replaced with Craftsman and Wolves (same neighborhood, still open, strong visual identity).
- **Saturday timing (2:00–8:30 PM).** Mister Jiu's only does dinner Tue–Sat starting at 5:00 PM. Starting at 2 PM gives enough runway for pastry + art before the 5:30 dinner seating.
