# Decision Log

## Execute vs. Plan Decision

**Decision: Execute**

**Factors weighed:**
- The task is self-contained in a single domain (date planning / creative writing)
- Clear path from start to finish: research venues → design narrative arc → write detailed plan
- Success criteria are explicit in the goal (specific locations, timing, personality fit, narrative cohesion)
- No sub-problems that require fundamentally different expertise or approaches
- Decomposing into steps (e.g., "research venues" → "write plan") would be over-splitting — the research and writing are tightly coupled

**What I considered and rejected:**
- Planning with steps like "Step 1: Research SF food scene, Step 2: Research SF art scene, Step 3: Synthesize into date plan" — this is the over-splitting anti-pattern. The research informs the plan in real-time; separating them creates artificial handoff points.
- Planning with parallel venue research agents — overkill for a task I can complete with web searches and domain knowledge in a single session.

**Confidence:** High. This is a classic leaf-node execution task.

**What would change my mind:** If the goal required coordination with external systems (e.g., actually making reservations, checking real-time availability), planning would be warranted. But the goal is to produce a detailed written plan, not execute logistics.
