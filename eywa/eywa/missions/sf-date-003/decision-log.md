## 2026-03-31 Decision: execute-vs-plan

Type: execute-vs-plan
Options considered: execute, plan (decompose into research + synthesis)
Chosen: execute
Reasoning: This is a self-contained task in a single domain (date planning for a specific city and personality). I can see a clear path from start to finish — identify the best convergence of sporty + nerdy + cliffs + sunsets in SF, then build a complete date arc around it. A plan decomposition (e.g., "research cliff locations" → "research nerdy venues" → "combine into date") would be over-splitting — the sub-tasks are trivial and tightly coupled, and I'd lose the holistic design sense that makes a great date feel like one flowing experience rather than a list of stops. The task's success criteria are clear: a specific, detailed, complete date plan with locations, timing, and reasoning.
Confidence: high
What would change my mind: If this required real-time research (e.g., checking current restaurant hours, verifying trail closures), planning might help by parallelizing lookups. But for designing the date itself, execution is the right call.
