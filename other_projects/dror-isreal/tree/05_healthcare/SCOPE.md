# Scope: 05 — Healthcare

Healthcare continuity across the move: from the last period of Israeli
coverage, through the gap while traveling or waiting for status, into the
destination country's public or private system. This branch is about the
end-to-end continuity problem, not only one side of the border.

Some overlap with `01_exiting-israel` is expected. The distinction:
`01` covers the Israeli-side closure or suspension procedures; `05`
covers how a person stays medically covered and clinically stable across
the whole move.

## What belongs here

- **Coverage gap planning** — what happens between the end of Israeli
  coverage and full enrollment in the destination system; travel
  insurance, bridge coverage, waiting periods, exclusions, and typical
  failure modes.
- **Destination public healthcare registration** — NHS, SNS, statutory
  health insurance, provincial plans, municipal registration tie-ins, and
  what visa or residence status is required before enrollment.
- **Private and expat insurance** — when it is required by a visa, when
  it is only a bridge, what "comprehensive" usually means, and how local
  supplemental insurance interacts with the public baseline.
- **Continuity of care for chronic conditions** — diabetes, epilepsy,
  autoimmune disease, cancer follow-up, pregnancy, psychiatric care,
  disability-related services, and any condition where a break in care is
  dangerous.
- **Prescription transfer** — how to travel with medications, how much
  supply to bring, when an Israeli prescription is useless abroad, and how
  to get a local doctor to reissue continuing medication lawfully.
- **Medical records** — obtaining records from Israeli providers, Hebrew
  vs English translation, vaccination records, specialist letters, imaging,
  lab histories, and apostille/translation questions when relevant.
- **Mental-health continuity** — therapy, psychiatry, controlled
  medications, waiting lists, and what to do if the destination system
  does not recognize the Israeli prescribing context.
- **Dental / vision / allied health** — what the public system usually
  excludes, when separate dental or vision cover is common, and how to
  handle glasses, contacts, physiotherapy, occupational therapy, and
  similar recurring needs.
- **Emergency contingencies** — what to do if arrival is delayed, a visa
  is refused, a child gets sick en route, or a person lands without local
  coverage active yet.

## What does NOT belong here

- The formal act of suspending or ending Kupat Holim membership in Israel
  as an administrative procedure → `01_exiting-israel`
- Tax treatment of insurance premiums or employer-paid healthcare benefits
  → `02_money-and-tax`
- First-30-days arrival sequencing in general → `04_destination-landing`
- School enrollment and special-education placement for children
  → `07_family-and-education`
- The emotional experience of illness and loneliness abroad
  → `10_emotional-and-identity`

## Language note

Hebrew matters for the Israel-side medical paperwork and Kupat Holim
terminology. The destination-country local language matters for enrollment
rules, public-system categories, formularies, and provider availability.
English-language expat summaries are often helpful but should not replace
the official local enrollment page.
