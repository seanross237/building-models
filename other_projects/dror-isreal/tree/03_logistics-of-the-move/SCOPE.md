# Scope: 03 — Logistics of the move

The physical move: getting your stuff (and your pets, and potentially your
vehicle) from Israel to the destination country.

## What belongs here

- **International movers and shipping companies** — air vs sea freight,
  full-container vs shared-container, what's available from Israeli ports
  (Haifa, Ashdod), typical costs, timelines, insurance. Major players:
  Ocean, AGS, Crown, Sixt, Shoval, etc.
- **Customs** — what you can bring duty-free as a returning or new
  resident (each destination has different thresholds), the documentation
  required, inventory lists, proof of ownership, the interaction with
  visa status.
- **Pet relocation** — EU pet passports, the rabies titer requirement
  (Israel → EU path), IATA pet travel rules, costs, quarantines,
  specialized pet relocation services. The Israel-to-EU path is relatively
  well-trodden; Israel-to-UK, Israel-to-Australia, Israel-to-US all have
  their own wrinkles.
- **Vehicle shipping or sale** — whether to sell in Israel or ship, taxes
  and fees at the destination, safety standards and homologation (EU
  destinations are especially strict), registration timelines.
- **Storage** — storing belongings in Israel for an indefinite return,
  storing in the destination before housing is finalized.
- **What to sell, give away, or donate before leaving** — a practical
  pre-departure purge.
- **Shipping valuables** — art, Judaica, jewelry, collectibles; insurance,
  customs disclosure, CITES for anything with endangered-species material
  (ivory, tortoise shell, etc.).
- **Timeline** — the 3–6 month window before departure, what to do when.
- **Moving yourself** — flights from Israel, baggage allowances (especially
  for long-term moves), what to carry vs ship.

## What does NOT belong here

- Customs at the destination FOR YOURSELF (residence permit, etc.)
  → `04_destination-landing`
- Breaking the lease on your Israeli apartment → `01_exiting-israel`
- Tax implications of selling vs shipping a car → `02_money-and-tax`
