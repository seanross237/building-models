# Scope: 07 — Family and education

Leaving Israel with other people attached to the decision: spouse,
partner, children, elderly parents, or an entire household rather than a
single individual. This branch is about the family-unit consequences of
the move and the education systems that family members must enter.

## What belongs here

- **Partner and dependent status** — spouse visas, dependent residence,
  family reunification for a non-Israeli partner, rights of unmarried
  partners where recognized, and the documentation burden for proving the
  relationship.
- **Children's immigration and status** — dependent child rules, stepchild
  complications, age cutoffs, adoption records, shared-custody paperwork,
  and what happens when one parent remains in Israel.
- **Custody and relocation consent** — court approval, notarized consent,
  Hague Convention issues, travel consent letters, and the line between
  practical family agreement and formal legal permission.
- **Schools** — public, private, international, bilingual, religious, and
  Jewish day schools; how admissions work; deadlines; catchment rules; and
  whether the child needs proof of address first.
- **Recognition of Israeli schooling** — bagrut recognition, report cards,
  transcripts, diploma translation, university entrance implications, and
  where Israeli qualifications do or do not map cleanly.
- **University transfers** — transfer credit, language requirements,
  tuition classification, gap-year effects, and what an Israeli student
  needs to carry from the home institution.
- **Childcare and early-years systems** — daycare, gan, nursery waitlists,
  subsidies, state preschool age thresholds, and after-school care.
- **Special-needs continuity** — IEP-equivalent documentation, therapy or
  aide continuity, disability services, accessible schooling, and waiting
  lists for public support.
- **Elderly parents** — family sponsorship where possible, long-term care
  implications, visa limits, healthcare dependency, and when bringing an
  older parent is legally or financially unrealistic.
- **Household-scale move planning** — extra documents, school-year timing,
  who travels first, temporary split-family arrangements, and why a family
  move is not just a scaled-up solo move.
- **Name changes and mixed-document families** — Hebrew/Latin spellings,
  different surnames across passports, marriage certificates, and proving
  family linkage across jurisdictions.

## What does NOT belong here

- The underlying visa route itself as a standalone immigration pathway
  → `00_eligibility-and-right-to-enter`
- Work authorization or professional licensing for the moving adults
  → `06_work-and-income`
- The mechanics of shipping furniture, pets, or vehicles
  → `03_logistics-of-the-move`
- The inner psychological meaning of uprooting a family
  → `10_emotional-and-identity`

## Language note

Hebrew matters for school records, family-court documents, Israeli
academic transcripts, and the underlying paperwork families already hold.
Destination-local language matters for school admissions, childcare rules,
custody procedures, and elder-dependency pathways, which are often poorly
documented in English.
