# Scope: 00 — Eligibility and right to enter

Every legal pathway by which an Israeli can obtain the right to live in
another country. This covers the *legal permission* dimension only —
actually arriving, landing, and settling in belong to other branches.

## What belongs here

- **Ancestry-based citizenship** — German (StAG §15, §116), Polish,
  Romanian, Hungarian, Austrian, Czech, Bulgarian, Lithuanian, Portuguese
  (Sephardic law — note the 2022 tightening), Spanish (Sephardic law —
  closed 2019 but open exceptions), and any other country where Israeli
  citizens with ancestors born there can claim citizenship.
- **Work visas** — employer-sponsored work permits by destination, including
  the EU Blue Card, UK Skilled Worker visa, US H-1B, Canada Express Entry,
  Australia skilled migration, etc.
- **Study visas** — student permits that provide a pathway to longer-term
  residence.
- **Investor / golden / passive-income visas** — Portugal D7, Italy elective
  residence, Spain non-lucrative, Greece golden visa, Malta, etc.
- **Digital nomad visas** — Portugal D8, Spain, Greece, Estonia, Croatia,
  UAE, etc.
- **Startup / entrepreneur visas** — France Tech Visa, Portugal StartUp
  visa, UK Innovator Founder, etc.
- **Family reunification** — visas based on a non-Israeli partner, parent,
  or child who already has status in the destination.
- **Asylum and humanitarian** — rare and usually not applicable, but the
  branch should at least note it exists and when it might apply.
- **Naturalization timelines after arrival** — how long from first arrival
  until permanent residency and full citizenship, per country.

## What does NOT belong here

- Tax implications of choosing a particular visa → `02_money-and-tax`
- Healthcare implications of different residency statuses → `05_healthcare`
- The in-person paperwork you do after arriving → `04_destination-landing`
- Choosing which country to pursue → `09_deciding-where-to-go`

## Partition hint (the branch agent may deviate)

A natural partition is by pathway type:
- `01_ancestry-based-citizenship`
- `02_work-visas`
- `03_study-visas`
- `04_passive-income-and-golden-visas`
- `05_digital-nomad-visas`
- `06_startup-and-entrepreneur-visas`
- `07_family-reunification`
- `08_asylum-and-humanitarian`

But if a branch agent finds a better partition (e.g. splitting ancestry by
continental region, or combining passive-income with digital-nomad), that
is fine. The branch agent owns its own sub-partition.

## Country-specific notes go to _countries/

For any country-specific rule (e.g. "German §15 requires documentation of
Jewish persecution of the ancestor"), the leaf that researches it should
write a short summary locally AND a full page in
`tree/_countries/germany/ancestry.md`, cross-referenced.
