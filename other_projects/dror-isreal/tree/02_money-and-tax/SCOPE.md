# Scope: 02 — Money and tax

Cross-border financial life for an Israeli leaving Israel. Covers both
sides of the border — Israeli tax and banking rules AND destination-country
tax residency — because they're coupled.

Some overlap with `01_exiting-israel` (tax residency exit) is fine. The
distinction: `01` covers the formalities of exit FROM Israel; `02` covers
the ongoing cross-border picture and the rules of the destination side.

## What belongs here

- **Tax residency rules by destination** — the criteria each destination
  uses to decide when someone becomes tax-resident (183-day rule variants,
  center-of-life tests, domicile rules in the UK, etc.).
- **Double taxation treaties** — Israel's network of DTAs, how they
  allocate taxing rights, tie-breaker rules, how to claim treaty relief,
  Form 2001 and equivalents.
- **Exit tax (mas yetzia)** — Israeli rules on unrealized gains at the
  point of ceasing to be tax-resident, valuation, deferrals, and the
  interaction with destination-country rules.
- **Pensions** — Keren Pensia, Keren Hishtalmut, Bituach Mankalim,
  provident funds: what happens to them when you leave, whether to
  withdraw or maintain, tax treatment in Israel and destination,
  transferring to a foreign pension scheme.
- **Moving money out of Israeli bank accounts** — wire transfer rules,
  reporting thresholds (both Israeli and destination), anti-money-
  laundering documentation, maintaining vs closing Israeli accounts.
- **Opening foreign bank accounts** — requirements for Israelis in each
  major destination, proof of address, proof of income, typical friction
  points (Spain, Portugal, Germany are notably fussy).
- **Currency and FX** — converting shekels, using Wise/Revolut/etc.,
  FX timing, hedging, shekel-denominated assets retained after departure.
- **Investment accounts** — Israeli brokerage account treatment, US
  brokerage for Israeli expats, European investment account access,
  PFIC rules for dual US-Israeli citizens.
- **Social security coordination** — Israel's totalization agreements
  with destination countries (few exist; record which ones do).
- **Estate planning across jurisdictions** — wills, inheritance tax,
  forced heirship rules (Israel has them, some EU countries have them,
  US does not).
- **Dual US-Israeli citizens** — special case: PFIC, FATCA,
  FBAR/FinCEN 114, US estate tax. Deserves its own leaf or sub-branch.
- **Ongoing Israeli filings from abroad** — if you retain any Israeli
  income source (rental, freelance for Israeli clients), what you must
  file annually.

## What does NOT belong here

- The act of CLOSING Israeli bank accounts → `01_exiting-israel`
- Getting paid by an Israeli employer from abroad → `06_work-and-income`
- Physical cash transport → `03_logistics-of-the-move` (customs)
