# Scope: 04 — Destination landing

The first 30 days in the destination country. What paperwork must be done
in person, what can be done online, and in what order. This is the most
action-oriented branch in the whole tree — the content here drives a
day-by-day checklist.

## What belongs here

- **Address registration** — most European countries require registering
  your address within days of arrival (Anmeldung in Germany, empadronamiento
  in Spain, junta de freguesia in Portugal, etc.). This is almost always
  the first and most important step because other paperwork depends on it.
- **Local ID / residence card / NIE / NIF / CPF / BSN / TIN** — the
  tax-or-identification number the destination requires; how to get it,
  where, what documents, typical timelines. Different countries use different
  names and frequencies.
- **Opening a local bank account** — requirements, typical friction,
  which banks are friendly to new arrivals (N26, Revolut, Wise work in
  some countries; local majors may require in-person visits and proof
  of address you don't yet have).
- **Phone and SIM** — buying a local SIM, porting a number, prepaid vs
  contract, what ID is required.
- **Housing** — short-term rental vs long-term; Airbnb as a bridge;
  deposit amounts; how rental contracts differ; broker fees; what rights
  a tenant has; how to pass the background check when you have no local
  credit history.
- **Utilities** — electricity, water, gas, internet; who contracts with
  whom (tenant vs landlord varies); setup timelines.
- **Post / mail address setup.**
- **First healthcare registration** — enough to be covered but the
  deeper content is in `05_healthcare`.
- **What documents to carry in person from Israel** — birth certificates,
  marriage certificates, academic diplomas, vaccination records, driver's
  license — and which need apostille before departure.
- **The sequencing problem** — most of these depend on each other
  (address registration needs a bank account needs proof of address
  needs a phone number needs a local SIM needs ID). The branch should
  surface viable sequences by country.

## What does NOT belong here

- Deeper healthcare enrollment → `05_healthcare`
- Finding work → `06_work-and-income`
- Enrolling kids in school → `07_family-and-education`
- Visa itself → `00_eligibility-and-right-to-enter`

## Language note

This is where the destination-country LOCAL language sources become
essential. A Portuguese leaf about NIF registration in Lisbon should cite
the Portuguese official page, not only English-language expat blogs.
