# Scope: 08 — Community and language

Finding people, institutions, and language footholds in the destination.
This branch is about social infrastructure: the communities an Israeli can
plug into, the language resources that make daily life possible, and the
translation layer between Israeli documents and destination systems.

This branch is more concrete than `10_emotional-and-identity`. The focus
here is not the inner experience of yerida, but the practical resources
and tactics that help a person build belonging in a new city.

## What belongs here

- **Israeli communities abroad** — formal or informal Israeli networks,
  meetup groups, Facebook groups, Telegram channels, WhatsApp groups,
  Israeli cultural centers, and local organizations that reliably connect
  newcomers to other Israelis.
- **Jewish community infrastructure** — synagogues, JCCs, Chabad houses,
  youth groups, Hebrew schools, and community centers that often function
  as landing pads even for secular Israelis.
- **Language learning resources** — state-provided integration courses,
  subsidized classes, municipal language schools, university extension
  programs, private tutors, online platforms, and immersion formats.
- **Translation and apostille services** — where to find recognized
  translators, sworn translators, notarization channels, apostille help,
  and document-prep services that bridge Hebrew paperwork into local use.
- **Integration programs** — city newcomer offices, migrant welcome
  centers, civil-society programs, free orientation courses, and any
  state-funded integration pathway relevant to new residents.
- **Finding your people in a new city** — neighborhood-level tactics,
  coworking spaces, volunteering, hobby communities, parenting circles,
  alumni networks, and other concrete ways to build a human network fast.
- **Community density by destination or city** — where Israelis or Jews
  are concentrated and where a user is more likely to find familiar
  services, Hebrew-speaking professionals, or holiday community.
- **Practical language thresholds** — which destinations are livable in
  English for a while and which require local language quickly for housing,
  healthcare, school, or work.

## What does NOT belong here

- The deeper emotional struggle of stigma, guilt, belonging, or identity
  → `10_emotional-and-identity`
- The visa or legal right to immigrate through a family or ancestry path
  → `00_eligibility-and-right-to-enter`
- Formal school placement decisions for children
  → `07_family-and-education`
- Country-choice scoring as a strategic comparison framework
  → `09_deciding-where-to-go`

## Language note

All three surfaces matter here. Hebrew is important because many community
resources are discoverable only through Hebrew search or Hebrew-speaking
social channels. Destination-local language is essential for integration
classes, municipal services, and certified translation requirements.
