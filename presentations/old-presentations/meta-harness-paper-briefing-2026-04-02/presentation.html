TL;DR

The Story in 30 Seconds

1
Harnesses matter as much as models
The paper treats the wrapper around an LLM as a first-class optimization target, not just an implementation detail.
2
Compressed feedback is the bottleneck
Most prior optimizers only see short summaries or scores, which is too little signal for long-horizon harness failures.
3
Meta-Harness gives the proposer full history
The coding agent reads prior source code, evaluation traces, prompts, tool calls, and scores directly from a filesystem.
4
That richer access produces better harnesses
The paper reports gains on text classification, retrieval-augmented math reasoning, and TerminalBench-2 agentic coding.
Takeaway

The strongest idea is not search over prompts. It is search over executable harness code with selective access to full prior experience.

Limits and takeaway

What Looks Strong, and What Still Needs Caution

Strongest evidence
Ablation says raw traces matter: 50.0 median accuracy for full Meta-Harness versus 34.6 and 34.9 for compressed variants
The discovered harnesses are readable procedures, not opaque weight changes
The proposer appears to self-correct by comparing regressions and isolating confounds
Caution
TerminalBench search and final evaluation happen on the same public benchmark
The system relies on a strong coding-agent proposer
This is persuasive evidence for harness search, not a final answer on general automated system design
Bottom line

The paper makes a strong case that future gains may come from agents redesigning their own scaffolding, with full prior experience available as searchable external memory.
