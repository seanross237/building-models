The outer loop in action
After Strategy 1 produced an answer, the missionary read the final report and decided: good, but not stress-tested against a serious rival. It autonomously designed Strategy 2 as a tournament. This is exactly the behavior the outer loop is designed for — the system questioning its own conclusions.

Strategy 1

Dialectical Construction

10 explorations. Generate bold theses, smash them together, stress-test what survives.

Phase 1: Three Independent Theses (E001–E003)
Built three competing interpretations from scratch: entanglement-based, process/becoming, and computational. Key insight: they were more complementary than contradictory. Each excelled where the others were weak.
Phase 2: Adversarial Attacks (E004–E005)
Devil's advocate found a novel flaw in the entanglement thesis (uses "entanglement" in two incompatible ways). Stress-test found a fatal circularity — resolved by Hoehn's quantum reference frame proof that "static" and "evolving" are equivalent perspectives.
Phase 3: Synthesis & Rigorization (E006–E010)
Merged all three theses. Five-attack stress-test produced 4 mandatory modifications. Final articulation: a unified computational interpretation explaining 6 properties of time from a single framework.
Strategy 1 conclusion
Time is the computationally irreducible processing of information in a finite universe. Grounded in experimentally confirmed physics. But — weak on explaining the "now" and the arrow of time. The missionary wanted better.

Key Discovery

Two Roads, Same Destination

The mission's most important finding isn't the final answer — it's that two independent intellectual traditions converged on the same deep structure of time.

TRADITION 1: Physics + Information Theory
Landauer, Bennett, Bekenstein, Margolus-Levitin, Wolpert. Time is the irreversible processing of information in a finite system. Sequential, bounded, no shortcuts.
TRADITION 2: Process Philosophy + Causal Physics
Whitehead, Heisenberg, Sorkin, Smolin, Husserl. Time is the ongoing resolution of possibilities into actualities. Inherently directed, irreversible, real becoming.

Takeaways

How the System Performed

What worked, what broke, and what we learned about running autonomous philosophical research.

Outer Loop Self-Correction
The missionary autonomously decided Strategy 1's answer needed challenging and designed a tournament. This is the first time the outer loop rejected its own result and tried harder.
Methodology Evolution
S-001 used dialectical construction (thesis → stress-test → synthesis). S-002 used a tournament (build rival → fight → merge). The two-strategy arc turned out to be the strongest approach.
Adversarial Testing = Highest Value
Every overclaim across both strategies was caught by adversarial explorations. The devil's advocate attacks were consistently the most valuable explorations in the entire mission.
Crashes & Recovery
2 explorers crashed due to rate limits (E007, E010 in S-001). Both times, the strategizer was nudged to read partial reports and continue. Recovery pattern is now established.
The meta-lesson
Dialectical Construction → Challenger Tournament is a powerful two-strategy arc for interpretive research. Build the best answer from one direction, then build the best rival and let them fight. The convergence or divergence is itself the most important finding.
