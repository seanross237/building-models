Three Moves That Define the Theory

1
Split "time" into two. Physical time (the block universe's timelike dimension) and phenomenal time (the felt quality of temporal experience) have different properties and different existence conditions.
2
Asymmetric constitution, not identity. Time and consciousness are not the same thing, not independent things, and not symmetrically dependent. They are linked by a one-way chain.
3
Ground phenomenal features in physical mechanisms. Felt passage comes from memory asymmetry, felt duration from integration windows, felt arrow from the entropy gradient.

Theory v2

What Changed

The architect addressed all 10 must-fix and should-fix items. Three structural moves defined the revision.

Move 1: TEP Language for Block Compatibility
Added "Temporally Extended Pattern" (TEP) — a four-dimensional structure in the block with specific information-integration properties across its temporal parts. All process language replaced with TEP language. Consciousness corresponds to certain TEPs, not to dynamic processes.
Move 2: Stripped Definition, Honest Premises
Consciousness definition reduced to the Nagelian minimum ("something it is like to be"). Temporal extension and integration moved to premises P4/P5 — now substantive, falsifiable claims rather than definitional consequences.
Move 3: Temporal Structure ≠ Temporal Phenomenology
New premise P12: consciousness requires temporal structure (must be a TEP) but does not always produce temporal phenomenology (the felt sense of time). Meditation evidence forced this distinction. This actually strengthened the asymmetry — C can exist without rich Tphen, but Tphen cannot exist without C.

The Philosophy

What Is the Relationship?

The most interesting content from TCT — what the theory actually claims about time and consciousness.

Consciousness sits between two times
The asymmetric chain Tphys → C → Tphen places consciousness as the mediating layer. It is constituted by physical temporal structure (it must be a temporally extended pattern) and it constitutes the felt experience of time (passage, now-ness, duration).
The Tphys / Tphen distinction dissolves most paradoxes
"Does time require an observer?" "Is time real?" "Do we create time?" — these all dissolve once "time" stops meaning one thing. Physical time and phenomenal time have different properties, different existence conditions, and different relationships to consciousness.
The "flow" of time is real but phenomenal
The direction of felt flow is explained by the entropy gradient and memory asymmetry. Its structure (why time feels like it has a direction, a width, distortions) is accounted for. But why there is felt passage at all remains the hard problem applied to time — TCT is honest about this gap.
Temporal structure and temporal phenomenology come apart
Meditation forced a key refinement: consciousness requires temporal structure (it must unfold over time) but does not always produce temporal phenomenology (the felt sense of time can be attenuated). You can be conscious without feeling time pass.
Framework-dependent
The chain direction depends on your metaphysics. Under idealism, it reverses entirely. Under panpsychism, it may collapse.
Hard problem remains
TCT explains the structure of temporal experience but not why there is felt temporal experience at all. This is the deepest unsolved issue.

Takeaways

System Performance

What this run tells us about the adversarial pipeline itself.

Judge continues to earn its place
Both "Fatal" attacks were downgraded to Major — each had a clear resolution path. The adversary rated B+: thorough and systematic, but occasionally overstated severity. Same pattern as Run 001. The judge prevented overcorrection again.
The adversary caught a genuine structural flaw
The block-universe / process-language tension was a real internal inconsistency in v1. The fix (TEP language) required introducing a new definition and reformulating the entire theory. This is the kind of deep structural catch the system is designed to find.
Meditation forced a non-obvious refinement
The adversary's "meditation / timelessness" attack led to the structure-vs-phenomenology separation (P12), which strengthened the theory's asymmetry argument rather than weakening it. The adversarial pressure produced a better theory, not just a patched one.
