TL;DR

The full story in five points.

What we tried
The spectral approach to RH: find a self-adjoint operator whose eigenvalues are the Riemann zeros. Strategy 1 mapped constraints, Strategy 2 tried to build operators that satisfy them, Strategy 3 verified and stress-tested the best candidates.
The central obstacle
Zeta zeros are 47% more "rigid" in their long-range spacing than any matrix we can construct. Berry's 1985 formula explains why analytically — it comes from prime orbit structure. But no one knows how to encode that structure into a matrix so the eigenvalues inherit it.
The self-correction highlight
Atlas generated 4 novel claims across 3 strategies. Its own adversarial review phase caught and retracted 3 of them — including one that looked promising at 4/5 novelty score before being definitively refuted by a follow-up computation. 1 modest claim survived.

Conclusion
Mission closed at Tier 3. No finding advances a proof strategy. The missionary's assessment: further progress requires genuinely new mathematical ideas, not more computation within the same paradigm. 3 strategies is the natural scale for a hard open problem.

The Challenge: The 47% Gap

Why the spectral approach is so hard — in one picture.

The key insight
Getting GUE statistics is easy — any sufficiently random complex matrix does it. Getting the right GUE statistics is hard. The Riemann zeros have 47% stronger long-range "rigidity" than any matrix construction. That gap is the unsolved problem.

Rigidity (Δ₃) — lower is more rigid

Δ₃ measures how regular the spacing of a spectrum is over long ranges. Zeta zeros are anomalously regular.

Why the gap exists
Berry's formula (1985) explains it analytically: Δ₃ = (1/π²)log(log(T/2π)). The rigidity comes from prime orbit structure — sums of (ln p)/p over primes. Atlas confirmed this at 0.6% accuracy.
Why it's hard to bridge
The extra rigidity lives in the τ > 1 region of the spectral form factor — requiring off-diagonal orbit pair corrections (Sieber-Richter pairs). No matrix construction has solved how to encode this.

The Science

What the System Confirmed

These are prior art results — not novel — but form the quantitative foundation of the mission.

Berry Formula — 0.6% accuracy
Δ₃_sat = (1/π²)log(log(T/2π)) predicts 0.154 at T=600. Measured: 0.155. This formula analytically explains the super-rigidity from prime orbit saturation. Berry (1985) confirmed computationally.
Li Coefficients — all positive
λ_n > 0 for n = 1…500 using 2000 zeros. Consistent with RH. Convergence happens by phase cancellation (|1−1/ρ| = 1 on the critical line) — not amplitude decay. This is the mechanism.
10-Point Constraint Catalog
GUE symmetry class (β=2), Montgomery pair correlation (9% MRD), Wigner surmise spacing (4% MAD), spectral rigidity saturation, form factor ramp-plateau, periodic orbit structure. Any valid operator candidate must pass all 10.
Δ₃ / β Decoupling
Long-range rigidity (Δ₃) and local level repulsion (β) are independent statistics. Matrices with similar β can have 1.46× different Δ₃. This is why "passing GUE" is necessary but not sufficient.
Definitively ruled out
Real symmetric arithmetic matrices → Poisson or GOE, never GUE.   Dirichlet character phases → algebraically collapse to real symmetric.   Gauss sum phases → GOE enhancement collapses at large N.   Berry-Keating perturbative → requires T >> 1010, unusable.

Self-Correction

4 Claims Made. 3 Self-Refuted.

The adversarial review phase is working. Here's the full lifecycle of every novel claim.

RETRACTED Claim A: N²/p Gauss Sum Scaling Law
S2 found β peaks at N²/p ≈ 275 for N=500. S3 E004 showed peak β_max decreases monotonically with N and peak position varies 1.5× across sizes. Favorable fluctuation at one N, not a law.
RETRACTED C1 Pair Correlation + Intermediate Δ₃
Two related claims from S2: that the C1 matrix passed a specific pair correlation test and showed "intermediate" rigidity. S2's own adversarial review phase caught both. Generic GUE finite-size behavior — not Von Mangoldt-specific.
REFUTED Li Coefficient Crossover (λ_n^zeta < λ_n^GUE)
S3 E002 found ratio = 0.95 at n=500. E007 confirmed it was genuinely novel — no prior paper compares Li coefficients across ensembles. Rated 4/5 novelty. Then E008 found truncation sensitivity. E009 delivered the kill shot: ratio crosses 1.0 at K~2500, reaches 1.09 at K=5000. It was a truncation artifact.
SURVIVED Claim B: Dirichlet Character Algebraic Impossibility
For Hermitian matrices H_{jk} = Λ(|j-k|+1)·f(j,k), both multiplicative and factorizable Dirichlet character constructions algebraically collapse to real symmetric matrices. Therefore Dirichlet characters cannot produce GUE — they are restricted to GOE at best. Algebraically proven + numerically verified. Modest but genuine.
