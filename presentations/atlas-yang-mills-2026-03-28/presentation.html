ELI5

The Yang-Mills problem is one of 7 Millennium Prize problems (worth $1M each). It asks: why do the particles that carry the strong nuclear force — gluons — have mass?

Proving this mathematically is incredibly hard. We proved that gluons do have mass in a simplified digital version of the universe (a lattice), and our proof works for a wider range of conditions than anyone has published before — 8 times wider than the 2023 record and 4 times wider than the 2025 record.

We also found the exact shape that makes the proof hardest (the staggered mode), and showed that the obvious way to extend it further is mathematically impossible (trace invariant).

There's one specific inequality left to prove to push even further.
