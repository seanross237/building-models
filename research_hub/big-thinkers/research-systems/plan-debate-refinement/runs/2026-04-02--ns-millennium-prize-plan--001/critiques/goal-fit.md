# Goal-Fit Critique

This plan is disciplined, but it is not yet strongly aligned with the user’s intended outcome: a serious path toward solving the 3D incompressible Navier-Stokes Millennium Prize problem. It mostly optimizes for formal cleanliness inside a narrow internal object system, not for generating a theorem that is visibly on a path to the actual regularity problem.

## Strongest Objections

- The plan is too deeply committed to ledger-specific artifact work without proving that those artifacts are mathematically load-bearing for Navier-Stokes regularity. Proving identities like `Delta_tmpl(F_SL(rho)) = 1/4` may be exact, but exactness alone does not make the work prize-relevant unless there is a demonstrated reduction from those objects to a genuine NS contradiction or a new a priori bound.
- The route ranking appears to treat the current status-file taxonomy as if it were already a validated research program. That is not enough for goal-fit. A serious prize plan needs an explicit bridge from each route to an accepted target statement about the PDE, not just to “better object definitions” or “firewall lemmas” on a frozen ledger.
- The first two routes look like audits of a curated formalism rather than attacks on the core problem. If the opening milestones are internal consistency checks on a narrow exact-sheet language, the plan risks spending its best effort proving that the proxy is coherent while never proving that the proxy controls the actual dynamics.
- The plan is overconfident about exact object promotion criteria and underconfident about whether those promoted objects are even the right ones. Canonicity and same-currency comparison are good hygiene, but they are not substitute evidence that the objects sit on a path to regularity or blowup exclusion.
- The sidecar search is too constrained relative to the risk that the main Tao-adjacent branch is a dead end. Capping non-Tao work at 10 percent while the main routes remain unvalidated is not an aggressive prize strategy; it is a bet that the narrowest frontier is already the right frontier.

## Missing Assumptions Or Prerequisites

- No route is explicitly required to produce a theorem that changes the status of actual Navier-Stokes regularity, not just the status of a ledger object. That prerequisite should be stated up front.
- The plan assumes that a canonical extraction lemma, firewall lemma, or intrinsic triad theorem can be built before showing that any such theorem implies a quantitative consequence for the PDE. That is backwards unless the implication chain has already been established.
- The plan does not state what accepted critical norm, blowup criterion, or regularity mechanism each route is meant to impact. Without that, “prize-relevant progress” remains rhetorical.
- It assumes the current status file is complete enough to justify deprioritizing other avenues. For a problem of this difficulty, that assumption needs external justification or at least a deliberate falsification step.
- It does not require an early proof that the chosen objects are not only canonical but also mathematically sufficient to recover the information needed for the target theorem.

## Concrete Revisions

- Add a phase-0 bridge requirement: every route must state the exact theorem it is trying to imply about Navier-Stokes regularity, the accepted norm or criterion it touches, and the logical step that would connect the local object theorem to that PDE statement.
- Demote all “frozen ledger” targets to subordinate milestones unless they are accompanied by a precise implication to a recognized NS theorem target. If they are only proxy identities, say so explicitly and cap the time spent on them.
- Replace the current route ranking with a ranking by downstream theorem leverage, not by internal exactness or apparent cleanliness.
- Expand the non-Tao sidecar budget unless the main routes produce a concrete implication chain early. The current allocation is too conservative for a frontier this uncertain.
- Add a hard stop rule that triggers if a route yields only object-definition refinement, bookkeeping consistency, or “firewall language” without a theorem that sharpens the actual PDE status.
- Require at least one route to attempt a direct bridge to an accepted critical framework from the start, rather than deferring that bridge until after multiple internal subgoals are completed.

## Salvageability

The plan is salvageable without a full rewrite, but only if the center of gravity changes. The exactness discipline, kill criteria, and insistence on canonical objects are worth keeping. What needs rewriting is the route hierarchy and success definition: right now they reward internal coherence more than actual leverage on Navier-Stokes. If those are corrected, the plan could become a serious research program; as written, it is too likely to produce refined artifacts instead of progress toward the prize problem.
