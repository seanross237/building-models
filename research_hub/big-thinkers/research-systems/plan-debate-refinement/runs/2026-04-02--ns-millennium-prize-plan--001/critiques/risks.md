# Risks Critique

## Strongest Objections

1. The plan is still organized around status-file labels, not around a mathematically stable route to Navier-Stokes regularity. Routes 1-3 mostly rename the frontier taxonomy into work packages, but they do not yet show that the named objects are canonical, comparison-stable, or even capable of carrying a theorem to the actual PDE problem.

2. The draft repeatedly assumes that exact formulas on a "frozen ledger" will survive promotion into theorem-bearing objects. That is a major unsupported leap. If the ledger depends on hidden normalization, witness choice, relabeling, or hand-maintained bookkeeping, then the apparent theorem target is just curated arithmetic, not durable math.

3. Route 1 and Route 2 are overfit to exact local identities, but the plan never shows how either identity would connect to a genuine regularity obstruction or a contradiction for 3D incompressible Navier-Stokes. Even if both subclaims were true, they may still be dead ends unless they imply a robust invariant or a transferable obstruction principle.

4. Phase 5 is the real hard problem, but it is deferred until after three earlier phases that are themselves likely to stall. That is backwards risk management. The critical-element bridge is not a later dependency; it is the gating prerequisite for whether any of the earlier object work can ever matter.

5. The sidecar phase is too broad and too weakly specified to be a real hedge. "Alignment + vorticity-direction regularity," "direct pressure-Hessian analysis," and "vorticity-direction Lipschitz regularity" are not concrete theorem programs yet. Without a sharper target, the sidecar will consume time without producing falsifiable output.

## Missing Assumptions Or Prerequisites

- A formal definition of the ledger objects that proves canonicity under every allowed refinement and relabeling.
- A precise statement of how "same-currency comparison" is enforced, not just asserted.
- A proof that the promoted packet-family or leakage statements are not artifacts of a chosen witness, chosen channel, or chosen coordinate convention.
- A demonstrated bridge from any Route 1/2 theorem to a genuine NS obstruction, not merely to a better local description of the ledger.
- A reason to believe the shell-crossing triad formulation is not just another equivalent reformulation of already-closed estimate machinery.
- A prerequisite canonical extraction lemma before compactness-rigidity language is allowed at all.

## Concrete Revisions

- Move the canonical extraction problem to the front of the plan. If the extraction lemma does not exist, Route 5 is not a late-stage option; it is a blocker for the entire program.
- Require every route to declare its invariant, its comparison currency, and its downstream theorem role before any subproblems are assigned.
- Replace the current exact ledger targets with a two-stage test: first prove that the object is canonical and stable; only then ask for exact identities on it.
- Tighten the sidecar into one precise theorem per direction, with a hard deliverable and a hard no-go criterion that is not just "felt interesting."
- Add a kill rule that actually closes a route after repeated failure to produce a theorem-bearing statement, rather than allowing the route to remain alive as a descriptive artifact.

## Failure Modes Most Likely To Stall Or Damage The Program

- The work becomes an internal consistency exercise on the frozen ledger rather than a route to a prize-level theorem.
- The plan accumulates exact-looking identities that are not invariant enough to survive refinement, so later phases cannot reuse them.
- The team spends most of its time on Route 1 and Route 2 because they are concrete, while the real blocker - canonical extraction - remains unresolved.
- Route 3 remains a conceptual moonshot with no clear bridge to contradiction, and it absorbs attention because it sounds more intrinsic than the packet routes.
- The capped sidecars turn into novelty hunting because the plan does not define what counts as a successful obstruction test.

## Salvage Assessment

Salvageable without a full rewrite, but only with a substantial refocus. The current structure can survive if the plan is re-anchored around one foundational question: can any candidate object be made canonical enough to support extraction or obstruction? Without that correction, the draft is not a serious research program; it is a ranked list of locally sharp but globally ungrounded probes.
