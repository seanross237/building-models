# Summary: Revisions, Critique Drivers, Residual Risks

## Main Revisions

1. The extraction-first `L^3` critical-element route was demoted from main route to conditional route.
   The final plan now says explicitly that no prize-facing main route is justified until a short gate is passed.

2. The active mainline was rewritten as a bounded decision/no-go campaign.
   Its job is to decide whether the Tao-adjacent one-bridge line can enter accepted PDE theorem language at all, not to assume that bridge already exists.

3. The plan now follows the repository’s actual near-term ranking.
   The frozen template-defect theorem and the leakage firewall lemma are promoted because they are the only Tao-adjacent next moves already close to theorem form.

4. A compactness/pressure feasibility gate was inserted ahead of any object-freezing or critical-element contradiction work.
   The plan now requires an intrinsic observable or quotient object that survives critical compactness, pressure reconstruction, relabeling, and harmless refinement, or else a no-go theorem proving the current packet family cannot do this.

5. The shell-crossing phase/coherence route was retained only in its stricter recursive-spill form.
   The earlier first-branch phase-frustration hope was not preserved because the status file already records compatible quadrature locking on the first branch and first recursive emission layer.

6. The vague geometry fallback was removed.
   The final plan allows a non-Tao PDE route only if it is written immediately as one exact CF/DHY-adjacent theorem statement that explicitly avoids collapse back to the blocked `e_2`-alignment plus `s_2 > 0` mechanism.

7. Failure modes were upgraded from “stop and switch” to theorem-shaped negative outcomes.
   Failed extraction or failed bridge work must now cash out as a robust no-go theorem, not as another internal dead note.

## Which Critiques Caused Which Revisions

### Critical-spaces / compactness-rigidity critique

Caused:

- demotion of the critical-element route to conditional status,
- insertion of the compactness compatibility audit,
- replacement of “canonical one-bridge extraction” by the weaker prerequisite “intrinsic observable or quotient object,”
- earlier hard stop if no critical-topology-stable observable appears.

### Harmonic-analysis / pressure / nonlinear-estimate critique

Caused:

- insertion of the pressure/nonlinearity gate before object work,
- removal of the `L^3` default as if it were neutral,
- reframing template-defect and leakage as kill tests unless they produce PDE-stable one-sided inequalities,
- rejection of vague alignment/coherence fallback language.

### Triad-mechanism dependencies critique

Caused:

- promotion of mechanism-preservation concerns ahead of canonicalization,
- retention of the shell-crossing recursive-spill route as the only active intrinsic moonshot,
- explicit hard stop against arbitrary finite truncation, hidden source-basis choices, and desired-channel annotation masquerading as intrinsic structure.

### Proof-architecture / theorem-object stability critique

Caused:

- freezing of the minimum theorem surface before any contradiction program,
- quotient-first language replacing representative-first language,
- insistence on one actual theorem statement before activating any bridge,
- explicit statement that Phase 0-type object work is itself theorem risk, not mere setup.

### Prize-strategy / goal-fit critique

Caused:

- the new top-level judgment that no prize-facing main route is currently justified,
- the short bounded decision sprint,
- preservation of value in the failure case via no-go theorems,
- harsher route ranking aligned with the authoritative status file rather than field-legible presentation.

## Residual Risks

1. The packet theorems may close cleanly and still fail to yield any PDE-stable bridge.
   In that case they remain mathematically real but prize-strategically local.

2. The shell-crossing intrinsic route is still a moonshot.
   The first branching module and first recursive emission layer already admit coherent quadrature locking, so any future theorem must come from later recursive spill or spectator burden, not small local incompatibility.

3. The critical-element bridge may be fundamentally impossible for this object family.
   The strongest current risk is that the mechanism information is supercritical relative to any reasonable compactness topology, especially once pressure is restored.

4. The external PDE fallback is not yet mature.
   The vorticity-direction line has genuine survivors, including Theorem C3 and the curvature-based DHY picture, but no single new theorem statement is yet frozen that clearly escapes the known `s_2 > 0` obstruction.

5. The correct outcome of the gate may be negative.
   That would not mean the sprint failed; it would mean the plan succeeded in replacing speculative extraction work with a sharper impossibility boundary.
