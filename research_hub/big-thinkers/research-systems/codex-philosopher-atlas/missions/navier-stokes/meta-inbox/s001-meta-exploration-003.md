# Meta-Learning: Step 1, Exploration 003 (Vasseur 2007)

## What Worked
- Flagging Vasseur as "the most structurally different of the three" in the goal properly directed the explorer's attention to what's genuinely novel
- The explorer found the beta = 3/2 threshold and Conjecture 14 — the most actionable finding of the entire step — because the goal asked specifically about "structural advantage for potential improvement"
- The Tran-Yu (2014) lead was surfaced naturally by asking about subsequent work

## What Could Be Improved
- The explorer correctly noted that some detailed calculations (P_k^{22,23} decomposition) couldn't be fully resolved. A separate math explorer could verify these, but it wasn't necessary for the structural analysis
- The goal could have explicitly asked "what is the precise bottleneck exponent and what would full regularity require?" — the explorer found this anyway, but a direct question would have guaranteed it

## Generalizable Lesson
**For the most novel/different item in a comparison set, explicitly ask about structural advantages and paths to improvement.** This is what surfaced the beta = 3/2 threshold — the most valuable finding for the overall mission. Goals for "the different one" should be more forward-looking than goals for "the standard ones."
