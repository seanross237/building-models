# Meta Note - Step 2 Exploration 002

## What Worked

- Treating exact Leray / pressure projection as the enforcement mechanism
  behind the candidate family clarified the interaction sheets and prevented a
  fake fourth candidate.
- Requiring one downstream gate per candidate forced each notion to become
  theorem-facing rather than remaining a pure mechanism memo.

## Reusable Lesson

- A candidate-definition step is only really frozen when the interaction
  template and the downstream gate are written in the same packet and threshold
  language. Otherwise the branch is still moving between incompatible ledgers.

## Caution

- Hard admissibility sheets are useful only if the class partition and the
  finite-window normalization are fixed first. Otherwise the diagnostic vector
  turns into threshold bookkeeping instead of an invariant gate.
