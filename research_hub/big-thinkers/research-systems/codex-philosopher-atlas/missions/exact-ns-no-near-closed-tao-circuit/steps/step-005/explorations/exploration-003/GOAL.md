# Exploration 003 Goal - Freeze The Post-Repair Shortlist And Step-6 Readiness

## Objective

Using the inherited Step-4 dossier plus the outputs of `exploration-001` and
`exploration-002`, produce the final Step-5 shortlist and determine whether
Chain Step 6 is now well posed.

## Required Output

Produce a report that does all of the following:

1. Restates the final post-repair candidate set:
   repaired survivors,
   unchanged candidates,
   split candidates,
   and any discarded notions.
2. Classifies each post-repair candidate on the three axes:
   `precision`,
   `robustness`,
   and
   `usefulness`,
   with one short reason per axis tied to the frozen dossier.
3. Rejects any candidate whose downstream gate is still vague, cosmetic, or
   bookkeeping-dependent.
4. Names, for each surviving candidate, one concrete downstream theorem or
   counterexample gate, the smallest meaningful test object or parameter
   family, and the invariant observable or admissibility sheet that Step 6
   should freeze next.
5. States one explicit Step-5 verdict:
   either
   `Step 6 can start`
   with an exact survivor set and exact Step-6 object-freeze tasks,
   or
   `branch stops after the repair pass`
   with the sharpest earned negative memo.

## Success Criteria

- The shortlist is smaller and sharper than the inherited Step-4 menu.
- Any survivor has a concrete downstream gate on the same frozen burden
  currency.
- The final verdict stays inside the audited packet class and does not
  generalize beyond the local record.

## Failure Criteria

- The exploration fails if it promotes a survivor whose only gate is an
  admission-filter slogan with no concrete next object to freeze.
- It fails if it confuses branch-local survival with mission-level theorem
  success.
- It fails if it silently carries forward more than one renamed version of the
  same family.
