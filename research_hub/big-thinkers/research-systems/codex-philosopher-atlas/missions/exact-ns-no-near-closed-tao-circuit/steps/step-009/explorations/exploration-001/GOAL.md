# Exploration 001 Goal - Recover The Inherited Fixed Ledger And Remaining Freedoms

## Objective

Recover the exact one-bridge ledger that Step 9 must inherit unchanged, and
separate it from:

- witness-local conclusions tied only to the old `F_SS(1/12)` branch;
- classwise exact-closure burden that Step 8 earned but did not make
  support-level explicit; and
- open freedoms that still must be frozen before any honest exact audited
  realization exists.

## Success Criteria

- One clean inherited-ledger memo lands for the canonical one-bridge packet.
- The memo states exactly which items are already frozen:
  role labels,
  packet semantics,
  mandatory conjugate completion,
  normalization,
  sign / phase / amplitude anchors,
  desired Tao-like core,
  and classwise forced-extra categories.
- The memo distinguishes:
  inherited packet semantics,
  `F_SS(1/12)` witness-local data,
  and Step-8 classwise closure conclusions that are not yet support-level
  explicit.
- The memo lists the still-open realization variables Step 9 must freeze:
  explicit wavevectors,
  helicity choices,
  amplitude and phase freedoms,
  zero/degeneracy subvarieties,
  and the finite-closure convention.
- Each major item is attached to the strongest local authority path.

## Failure Criteria

- The report quietly treats witness-local `F_SS(1/12)` data as canonical packet
  semantics for the full audited ledger.
- The report treats Step-8 classwise burden totals as if they were already one
  finite support-level exact closure ledger.
- The report invents new packet semantics, wavevector families, or closure
  conventions not already authorized by the local record.
