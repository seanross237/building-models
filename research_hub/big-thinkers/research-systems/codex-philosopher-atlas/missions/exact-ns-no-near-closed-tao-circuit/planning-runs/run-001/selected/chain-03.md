# Chain 03 - Adversarial Near-Circuit Construction Search

## Central Premise

The fastest way to falsify the firewall idea is to search directly for the strongest exact NS or helical packet that approximates Tao's logic while paying full exact bookkeeping costs. If a small explicit near-circuit survives that audit, the obstruction idea should be downgraded immediately.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a concrete exact helical/Fourier packet with explicit coefficients and dynamics that honestly approximates Tao's pump / seed / amplifier / rotor / transfer logic; or
- a ranked failure table showing that every small candidate dies for one exact reason, thereby sharpening the positive obstruction routes.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is constructive and adversarial. It is not trying to prove impossibility first. It tries to beat the obstruction with the smallest counterexample family available.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Define a Tao-likeness score that can be optimized

Depends on: none.

Action: choose a small set of quantitative criteria for approximate success: desired sign pattern, separation between seed and amplifier channels, rotor dominance, small spectator leakage, and one delayed-threshold proxy.

Action: keep the score simple enough to compute on many candidate packets.

Expected output: a Tao-likeness scorecard with explicit formulas and pass/fail thresholds.

Kill condition: if the score cannot distinguish a genuine near-circuit from a generic triad network, it is useless and the chain must be reset.

### Step 2 - Enumerate the smallest exact candidate families

Depends on: Step 1.

Action: generate a bounded search family, such as one linked-triad packet, two linked-triad packets, and one shell-bridging packet, each with full conjugate completion.

Action: restrict the search budget up front so the chain cannot hide failure inside an open-ended parameter sweep.

Expected output: a search-family memo naming exact variables, parameter ranges, and what counts as one candidate.

Kill condition: if every honest candidate family is already too large for exact bookkeeping, report that the construction route is uninformative at this mission scale.

### Step 3 - Compute exact interaction tables and score the candidates

Depends on: Step 2.

Action: for each candidate family, derive the exact projected coupling table and evaluate the Tao-likeness score before any dynamical simulation.

Action: discard candidates that fail immediately on signs, forced feedback, or overwhelming spectators.

Expected output: a ranked interaction table with best candidates and exact reasons weaker ones fail.

Kill condition: if no candidate gets meaningfully above a trivial baseline, stop early and export the dominant failure modes to Chains 01 and 02.

### Step 4 - Dynamically test only the top survivors

Depends on: Step 3.

Action: integrate the reduced exact amplitude systems for the best one or two candidates and check whether they actually exhibit delayed growth, threshold behavior, and transfer without immediate spectator takeover.

Action: compare observed behavior against the scorecard rather than improvising qualitative claims.

Expected output: a construction memo with explicit trajectories or a falsification memo showing why the apparent algebraic survivor fails dynamically.

Kill condition: if a candidate survives only because the exact forced modes were omitted or renormalized away, reject it as dishonest.

### Step 5 - Promote the best exact verdict

Depends on: Step 4.

Action: if one candidate genuinely works, state it as a clean negative kill of the firewall idea, with exact packet definition and measured near-closure metrics.

Action: if all candidates fail, summarize the strongest recurring obstruction pattern and feed that back into a theorem-style claim.

Expected output: either a counterexample candidate or a tightly bounded failure atlas for small exact packets.

Kill condition: if the final result is only "search did not find anything," the chain failed to produce a usable outcome.
