# Chain 01 - Minimal Helical Coefficient-Rigidity Theorem

## Central Premise

A Tao-like circuit needs a deliberately ordered hierarchy of effective channels:

`weak pump << tiny seed << strong amplifier << stronger rotor << next-shell pump`.

In exact Navier-Stokes, those channels arise from one helical/Fourier interaction law whose coefficients are rigid functions of triad geometry, helicity signs, and Leray projection. The route is to show that a minimal exact packet cannot realize the required hierarchy with the needed sign pattern.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a proposition-scale impossibility statement for a minimal exact helical packet that tries to implement Tao's five roles; or
- a sharp failure memo showing that coefficient rigidity alone is too weak and that leakage, not ratio incompatibility, is the real obstruction.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is about coefficient hierarchy inside the desired channels themselves. It does not primarily rely on spectator-mode proliferation, numerical search, or definition-only critique.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze one exact notion of a Tao-like channel hierarchy

Depends on: none.

Action: define one concrete near-circuit object in the minimal language needed for this chain: a finite helical packet, five labeled effective roles, admissible sign pattern, and one explicit dominance vector comparing desired channel sizes to each other.

Action: keep the definition narrow enough that it can fail honestly. Do not bundle spectator leakage into the object beyond a small bookkeeping parameter.

Expected output: a one-page definition sheet specifying target roles, hierarchy inequalities, and admissible normalizations.

Kill condition: if no non-arbitrary hierarchy definition can be written without already assuming the conclusion, stop and hand the mission to the definition-level kill route.

### Step 2 - Choose the smallest exact helical support that could possibly realize those roles

Depends on: Step 1.

Action: enumerate the smallest plausible support families, likely one or two linked helical triads plus the conjugate completion forced by reality.

Action: reject larger families unless a smaller family is proven incapable even before coefficient analysis.

Expected output: a minimal-support memo naming wavevector relations, helicity choices, and which exact modes are assigned to Tao's roles.

Kill condition: if the minimal support already explodes into an uncontrolled large packet before any coefficient work, record that as evidence for Chain 02 and stop this chain.

### Step 3 - Derive the exact projected amplitude equations and coefficient identities

Depends on: Step 2.

Action: write the exact helical amplitude system for the chosen support, including all cyclic coefficients and any identities forced by energy/helicity conservation and Leray projection.

Action: express the key effective ratios that would need to mimic Tao's pump, seed, amplifier, rotor, and transfer strengths.

Expected output: an interaction table with exact coefficients, sign constraints, and the derived ratio relations among the targeted channels.

Kill condition: if the exact equations cannot be written in a closed finite form for the chosen support, the setup is not minimal enough and the chain should be reset rather than stretched.

### Step 4 - Test incompatibility of the Tao hierarchy against exact ratio constraints

Depends on: Step 3.

Action: prove or disprove whether the exact ratio relations can satisfy a Tao-like separation such as "slow pump plus much larger rotor plus exponentially smaller seed" under any allowed geometry and helicity assignment.

Action: isolate the first obstruction precisely: sign conflict, bounded ratio, coupled cyclic feedback, or inability to separate seed and amplifier scales.

Expected output: either a proposition draft with assumptions and conclusion, or an explicit parameter family that escapes the naive rigidity claim.

Kill condition: if a tunable geometric family seems able to realize the needed ratios within the frozen definition, this chain fails as a theorem route and should feed the construction chain.

### Step 5 - Promote the strongest honest conclusion

Depends on: Step 4.

Action: if rigidity wins, state the cleanest theorem object possible, naming the minimal packet, the exact forbidden hierarchy, and the first missing lemma.

Action: if rigidity loses, write a bounded negative result explaining exactly which ratio freedom survives and why coefficient rigidity alone is not the right firewall object.

Expected output: a proposition candidate or a narrow negative memo that redirects later work toward leakage or construction.

Kill condition: if the final claim still reads like "exact NS is too coupled" without a concrete forbidden ratio pattern, downgrade it to failure.
