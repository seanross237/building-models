# Refined Chain 03 - Invariant-Gated Adversarial Exact Packet Search

## Central Premise

The constructive search route is worth keeping, but only if it stops optimizing an ad hoc "Tao-likeness" surrogate and instead searches within a fixed exact admissibility framework. The goal is to find the smallest honest exact packet class that survives exact closure and still shows meaningful Tao-like delayed transfer behavior, or else to produce a bounded diagnostic atlas of why such small packets fail.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- an explicit exact packet, with full forced-mode closure and tested dynamics, that provides serious anti-obstruction evidence within the searched class; or
- a bounded failure atlas for normalized small exact packet classes, listing the exact mechanisms that block Tao-like behavior under honest closure.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain remains constructive and adversarial, but it no longer treats heuristic search outputs as mission-level verdicts. It is a stress test against the obstruction idea under exact bookkeeping, with claims scoped to the searched class unless a genuinely invariant mechanism emerges.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze an invariant admissibility sheet before searching

Depends on: none.

Action: fix one exact closure rule, one normalization convention for equivalence classes, and one representation-invariant admissibility sheet for this search. The admissibility sheet should be a vector of hard conditions, not a single scalar score.

Action: include only features that can be stated honestly before dynamics, such as exact channel presence, forbidden feedback absence, spectator-class bounds in a fixed normalization, and symmetry conventions. Do not include a delayed-threshold proxy here.

Expected output: an admissibility memo defining exact closure, normalization, pass/fail filters, and comparison conventions.

Kill condition: if no invariant-enough admissibility sheet can be stated for the searched class, stop and hand off rather than optimizing an ad hoc surrogate.

### Step 2 - Enumerate normalized exact candidate families with full forced closure

Depends on: Step 1.

Action: choose a bounded menu of small candidate families and specify their true parameter spaces after quotienting obvious symmetries and cosmetic reparameterizations.

Action: for each family, expand the full forced closure honestly, not just conjugate completion, and record the resulting exact packet size.

Expected output: a normalized search-family memo naming each family, its parameters, its exact closure rule, and what counts as one genuinely distinct candidate.

Kill condition: if every honest candidate family blows up beyond tractable exact bookkeeping at this mission scale, record that as a construction-budget finding, not as theorem evidence.

### Step 3 - Screen by exact hard filters and retain a diagnostic vector

Depends on: Step 2.

Action: compute exact interaction tables for all candidates and apply only hard admissibility filters at this stage: exact closure, required channel presence, forbidden feedback exclusion, and spectator bounds in one fixed norm or activation window.

Action: retain a non-aggregated diagnostic vector for each survivor so later interpretation can see which constraints are barely passed and which are robustly passed. Do not collapse this into one scalar ranking.

Expected output: a screened candidate ledger with pass/fail reasons, exact interaction data, and diagnostic vectors for survivors.

Kill condition: if no candidate survives the hard filters above a clearly stated baseline, stop and export the family-local failure reasons as a bounded diagnostic result.

### Step 4 - Simulate an honest diversified shortlist

Depends on: Step 3.

Action: choose a small but diversified shortlist of survivors across different geometric or helical regions, not just the top one or two by static neatness.

Action: integrate only the honest exact closed systems, including all forced modes. Test whether the candidates show delayed transfer, threshold behavior, and spectator subordination over a stated window.

Action: run limited robustness checks over initial phases, amplitude ratios, and other predeclared normalizations so that apparent success is not just a fine-tuned trajectory artifact.

Expected output: a dynamic test memo showing either a credible class-local survivor or exact reasons the algebraic survivors fail dynamically.

Kill condition: if apparent success requires omitted modes, hidden renormalization, or fine tuning outside the predeclared tolerance, reject it as non-presentable.

### Step 5 - Interpret outcomes with symmetric evidence standards

Depends on: Step 4.

Action: if a candidate survives, present it as serious anti-obstruction evidence within the searched class, with exact packet definition, closure data, and robustness limits. Do not call it a global disproof unless the mission-level object has already been fixed and the candidate meets it.

Action: if all candidates fail, present the output as a class-local failure atlas. Upgrade it toward obstruction evidence only if the failure mechanism is invariant across the normalized family and independent of score choices or truncation artifacts.

Expected output: either a bounded anti-obstruction packet memo or a bounded class-local failure atlas.

Kill condition: if the final writeup claims more than the searched class and exact evidence support, downgrade the conclusion before release.

### Step 6 - Feed the result cleanly into final comparison

Depends on: Step 5.

Action: state explicitly what the chain established, what it did not establish, and whether the result should strengthen the obstruction side, weaken it, or merely sharpen the search space for the other chains.

Expected output: a comparison-ready conclusion with scoped claims and clear handoff language.

Kill condition: if the conclusion cannot be stated without ambiguous scope, the chain is not ready for final comparison.

## Presentable-Result Outlook

Most likely presentable output: a bounded diagnostic memo explaining why normalized small exact packets fail to realize Tao-like delayed transfer under honest closure.

Best-case output: a class-local exact survivor that materially weakens the obstruction narrative.

Least likely output: a decisive mission-level refutation or theorem-grade obstruction from this chain alone.
