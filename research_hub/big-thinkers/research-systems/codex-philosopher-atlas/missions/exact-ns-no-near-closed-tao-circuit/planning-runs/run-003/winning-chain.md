# Canonical Winning Chain - Exact One-Bridge Closure Audit With Provenance, Cancellation, And Scope Control

## Central Premise

Step `008` already earned a classwise exact non-isolability verdict on the
frozen canonical one-bridge ledger, but it did not freeze one explicit
wavevector-level realization, one recursive closure convention, or one exact
cancellation audit with promoted same-currency provenance.

So the honest next branch is not "support bookkeeping alone proves no finite
closure," and it is not a pure relation-status memo.
It is to extend the already frozen one-bridge packet sheet into one audited
exact-realization protocol and then decide whether finite closure fails
absolutely, fails generically outside an explicit exceptional set, survives on
a robust loophole family, or cannot yet be reduced canonically at all.

If the branch never freezes a theorem-ready closure object, it must say so
plainly.
In that case, repaired
`G_tmpl`
and repaired
`G_leak`
remain screening tools rather than closure-implication theorems on the audited
ledger.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- one explicit audited realization protocol for the canonical one-bridge
  packet, including wavevector data, helicities, amplitude and phase freedoms,
  conjugation constraints, a precise finite-closure convention, and the source
  basis that authorizes each ingredient;
- a scoped exact obstruction theorem or generic obstruction theorem showing
  that the audited realization cannot close finitely without extra support,
  stated at no broader scope than actually proved;
- an exact lower bound in one declared metric only, such as unavoidable extra
  mode count or shell displacement, for the audited realization class;
- a robust loophole family that survives the full recursive audit and deserves
  constructive handoff; or
- a precise no-canonical-finite-reduction memo stating both why the exact audit
  cannot yet be promoted and why repaired `G_tmpl` / repaired `G_leak` stay
  screening-only for closure claims on this ledger.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is an exact-realization audit.
It does not assume that support enumeration settles exact closure, it does not
default to an obstruction theorem, and it does not settle for bare packaging.
Its job is to decide whether the Step-8 non-isolability story can be upgraded
only after recursive closure, coefficient provenance, nonvanishing, and
cancellation structure have been controlled.

## Scope Lock

Before Step 1 begins, freeze all of the following:

- the inherited canonical one-bridge packet semantics already on the ledger;
- the rule that any newly used realization data or coefficient formula must
  come with one same-currency promoted derivation or citation path;
- the rule that no theorem statement may exceed the audited realization or
  audited family unless a genuine reduction argument is proved; and
- the rule that if no theorem-ready closure object is frozen, the branch may
  conclude only a negative or scope-control memo, not an admissibility-to-
  closure implication theorem.

If these locks cannot be maintained, stop and package a source-basis or
no-canonical-finite-reduction memo.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze The Inherited Ledger, Provenance Burden, Remaining Freedoms, And Claim Scope

Depends on: none.

Action: import as fixed background everything the mission has already frozen
about the canonical one-bridge packet:
role labels,
mandatory conjugate completion,
normalization,
the sign and phase sheet,
the desired Tao-like core,
and the classwise forced-extra categories already earned by Step `008`.

Action: state exactly what is still unfrozen and relevant to exact closure:
explicit wavevector family,
helicity choices,
amplitude and phase freedoms,
possible coefficient-zero subvarieties,
and the finite-closure convention.
Separate inherited packet semantics from any newly imposed restrictions.

Action: require that every realization datum or exact coefficient identity used
later has one same-currency promoted derivation or explicit citation path.
Do not allow stalled draft arithmetic, stale threshold sheets, or hand-entered
checkers to become theorem authority by implication.

Action: declare the maximum claim scope in advance:
the single audited realization,
an explicitly parameterized audited family,
or the full one-bridge class only if a genuine reduction argument is proved.

Expected output: one realization-and-provenance memo listing fixed data, open
variables, closure notion, source basis, and maximum scope.

Kill condition: if making the realization explicit already changes the packet
semantics, quietly removes known freedoms, depends on an ad hoc closure
convention, or lacks promoted same-currency provenance, stop and package a
source-basis / no-canonical-finite-reduction memo.

### Step 2 - Build A Recursive Exact Closure And Coefficient Ledger

Depends on: Step 1.

Action: define an exhaustive recursive closure operator for the audited
realization:
start from the frozen core support and mandatory conjugates,
add every triad with nonvanishing exact coefficient,
iterate on any newly forced modes,
and record when the process stabilizes, exits the audited class, or becomes
non-canonical.

Action: compute or symbolically freeze the exact helical or Leray coefficient
sheet needed to tell apart structural forcing, structural zeros, and merely
possible outputs.
Keep desired-channel and spectator or companion contributions separate whenever
that distinction matters for the closure verdict.

Action: if "minimal completion" language is used at all, define it precisely in
terms of the Step-2 closure operator rather than by informal bookkeeping
buckets.

Expected output: one recursive interaction ledger with either a stabilized
exact completion, an explicit class-exit diagnosis, or a finite-reduction
failure note.

Kill condition: if the branch still relies on first-generation spill, informal
triad categories, an undefined stopping rule, or coefficient data weaker than
the Step-1 provenance burden, stop.

### Step 3 - Audit Cancellation Regimes And Loophole Types Before Any Theorem Claim

Depends on: Step 2.

Action: analyze whether apparently forced outputs can actually vanish through
amplitude tuning,
phase relations,
helicity sign coincidences,
conjugate pairing,
or geometric degeneracy.
Partition the parameter space into robust forcing regions, explicit exceptional
cancellation sets, and support-changing degenerate cases.

Action: classify loopholes by strength:
robust family,
generic family inside the audited class,
or knife-edge exceptional artifact.
Do not treat those as interchangeable.

Action: if the audit shows that the closure target itself still fails to become
theorem-ready, record that consequence explicitly rather than smuggling it past
the theorem step.

Expected output: one cancellation and loophole taxonomy stating whether any
later obstruction can honestly be absolute, generic, conditional, or blocked by
missing closure authority.

Kill condition: if the branch cannot distinguish robust forcing from explicit
exceptional cancellations, prohibit "no finite closure" rhetoric and demote the
endpoint to a conditional or negative memo.

Action: state the relation consequence for repaired
`G_tmpl`

Kill condition: if the claimed advance over Step `008` is only a restatement of

### Step 5 - Package Only A Relevance-Screened Endpoint

Depends on: Step 4.

Action: package exactly one of four honest endpoints:
a scoped obstruction theorem,
a generic obstruction with explicit exceptional set,
a robust loophole handoff,

Action: state the evidentiary tier of any loophole.

Action: keep the final writeup explicit about what was proved for the single
audited realization or audited family and what remains unproved for the full

If the endpoint is negative or conditional, state explicitly that repaired
`G_tmpl`
and repaired
`G_leak`

Expected output: one comparison-ready exact-closure package with honest scope,
honest metric, provenance note, and explicit non-claims.

Kill condition: if the final product still conflates support-level completion
with exact dynamical closure, mixes lower-bound currencies, hides source-basis
weakness, or depends on a hand-picked micro-family without explaining why that
family matters, the chain fails.

## Presentable-Result Outlook

Most likely presentable output:
an audited no-canonical-finite-reduction memo or a generic obstruction package
with an explicit exceptional cancellation set.

Best-case output:
a scoped exact obstruction theorem or a robust loophole family that survives
full recursive and cancellation audit.

Least likely output:
a theorem for the full one-bridge class from support bookkeeping alone, or any
closure claim that outruns the audited realization and its provenance.
