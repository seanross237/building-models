# Chain 04 - Adversarial Exact Near-Circuit Construction Beyond `F_SS`

## Central Premise

Step `008` killed one carried witness, not the whole constructive side of the
mission.
The right moonshot now is not to reopen `F_SS(1/12)` by fiat.
It is to search for a **new** explicit exact support family, still finite and
canonically scored, that can satisfy repaired `G_tmpl`, repaired `G_leak`, and
honest finite closure at once.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- an explicit exact packet family with full support ledger, forced-triad table,
  and repaired-gate values strong enough to count as a real near-circuit
  challenger; or
- a constructive failure atlas showing that every searched family dies by one
  named exact mechanism:
  forced packet growth,
  leakage overload,
  template drift,
  coefficient misordering,
  or closure-stage collapse.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is constructive and adversarial.
It does not try to prove a theorem before looking for a packet, and it does
not stay inside the invalidated `F_SS` support.
Its value is that a single honest explicit survivor would weaken the whole
firewall idea far more sharply than another audit memo.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze The Search Space And Hard Scorecard

Depends on: none.

Action: freeze a broader but still finite search class than the old `F_SS`
sheet:
for example,
alternative bridge geometries,
different helical sign choices,
or scale-separated exact supports with one small number of geometric degrees of
freedom.

Action: keep repaired `G_tmpl`, repaired `G_leak`, and honest finite closure as
hard filters from the start.
No threshold retuning, post hoc packet repair, or silent currency changes are
allowed.

Expected output: one exact search specification with explicit design variables
and no-repair rules.

Kill condition: if the search class is too loose to enumerate and verify exact
closure case by case, stop.

### Step 2 - Build One Exact Evaluator For Supports, Triads, And Gates

Depends on: Step 1.

Action: implement or extend a reproducible evaluator that,
for each explicit candidate support,
enumerates forced triads,
computes the exact coefficient or burden ledger,
and evaluates repaired `G_tmpl`, repaired `G_leak`, and finite-closure status
on the same packet semantics.

Action: verify the evaluator against the known carried ledgers for
`F_SS(1/12)`,
`F_SL(1/16)`,
and
`F_DT(delta, eta)`.

Expected output: one executable exact-evaluation pipeline plus a verification
sheet on the known families.

Kill condition: if the evaluator cannot reproduce the known carried ledgers on
the frozen same-currency sheet, the branch should stop before any search claims
are made.

### Step 3 - Search, Rank, And Re-Audit Only Exact Survivors

Depends on: Step 2.

Action: run the search over the frozen design space and keep only candidates
that survive exact re-audit with all forced modes included.

Action: separate heuristic pre-screening from authoritative pass/fail.
A candidate that looks good before forced-support completion does not count.

Expected output: one ranked shortlist of explicit candidate packets or a
structured no-survivor map over the searched class.

Kill condition: if apparent survivors disappear once full forced closure is
restored, package that as failure rather than soft-promoting them.

### Step 4 - Package Either A Challenger Packet Or A Constructive Failure Atlas

Depends on: Step 3.

Action: if one candidate survives, package its full support ledger, gate
values, and exact closure data narrowly and honestly.

Action: if none survive, classify the leading failure modes across the search
space and say whether Step-8-style non-isolability, template failure, or
leakage overload is the dominant constructive bottleneck.

Expected output: one exact challenger memo or one constructive failure atlas.

Kill condition: if the branch ends with only
`search did not find anything`
without one explicit candidate ledger or one explicit failure map, it fails.
