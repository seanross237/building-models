# Chain 02 - Exact One-Bridge Closure Audit With Cancellation And Scope Control

## Central Premise

Step `008` already earned a classwise exact non-isolability verdict on the
frozen canonical one-bridge ledger, but it did not freeze one explicit
wavevector-level realization, one recursive closure convention, or one exact
cancellation audit. The honest next branch is therefore not "prove no finite
closure from support bookkeeping alone." It is to extend the already frozen
one-bridge packet sheet into one audited exact-realization protocol and then
decide whether finite closure fails absolutely, fails generically outside an
explicit exceptional set, survives on a robust loophole family, or cannot yet
be reduced canonically at all.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- one explicit audited realization protocol for the canonical one-bridge packet,
  including wavevector data, helicities, amplitude and phase freedoms,
  conjugation constraints, and a precise finite-closure convention;
- a scoped exact obstruction theorem or generic obstruction theorem showing that
  the audited realization cannot close finitely without extra support, stated at
  no broader scope than actually proved;
- an exact lower bound in one declared metric only, such as unavoidable extra
  mode count or shell displacement, for the audited realization class;
- a robust loophole family that survives the full recursive audit and deserves
  constructive handoff; or
- a precise memo that no canonical finite-reduction protocol is currently
  available from the frozen Step-8 record.

## Why This Refined Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is an exact-realization audit. It does not assume that support
enumeration settles exact closure, and it does not default to an obstruction
theorem. Its job is to decide whether the Step-8 non-isolability story can be
upgraded only after recursive closure, coefficient nonvanishing, and
cancellation structure have been controlled.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze The Inherited Ledger, Remaining Freedoms, And Claim Scope

Depends on: none.

Action: import as fixed background everything the mission has already frozen
about the canonical one-bridge packet:
role labels,
mandatory conjugate completion,
normalization,
the sign and phase sheet,
the desired Tao-like core,
and the classwise forced-extra categories already earned by Step `008`.

Action: state exactly what is still unfrozen and relevant to exact closure:
explicit wavevector family,
helicity choices,
amplitude and phase freedoms,
possible coefficient-zero subvarieties,
and the finite-closure convention.
Separate inherited packet semantics from any newly imposed restrictions.

Action: declare the maximum claim scope in advance:
the single audited realization,
an explicitly parameterized audited family,
or the full one-bridge class only if a genuine reduction argument is proved.

Expected output: one realization-audit memo listing fixed data, open variables,
closure notion, and maximum scope.

Kill condition: if making the realization explicit already changes the packet
semantics, quietly removes known freedoms, or depends on an ad hoc closure
convention not justified by the current record, stop and package a
no-canonical-finite-reduction memo.

### Step 2 - Build A Recursive Exact Closure And Coefficient Ledger

Depends on: Step 1.

Action: define an exhaustive recursive closure operator for the audited
realization:
start from the frozen core support and mandatory conjugates,
add every triad with nonvanishing exact coefficient,
iterate on any newly forced modes,
and record when the process stabilizes, exits the audited class, or becomes
non-canonical.

Action: compute or symbolically freeze the exact helical or Leray coefficient
sheet needed to tell apart structural forcing, structural zeros, and merely
possible outputs.

Action: if "minimal completion" language is used at all, define it precisely in
terms of the Step 2 closure operator rather than by informal bookkeeping
buckets.

Expected output: one recursive interaction ledger with either a stabilized exact
completion, an explicit class-exit diagnosis, or a finite-reduction failure
note.

Kill condition: if the branch still relies on first-generation spill,
informal triad categories, or an undefined stopping rule, it has not repaired
the Step-8 gap and should stop.

### Step 3 - Audit Cancellation Regimes And Loophole Types Before Any Theorem Claim

Depends on: Step 2.

Action: analyze whether apparently forced outputs can actually vanish through
amplitude tuning,
phase relations,
helicity sign coincidences,
conjugate pairing,
or geometric degeneracy.
Partition the parameter space into robust forcing regions, explicit exceptional
cancellation sets, and support-changing degenerate cases.

Action: classify loopholes by strength:
robust family,
generic family inside the audited class,
or knife-edge exceptional artifact.
Do not treat those as interchangeable.

Expected output: one cancellation and loophole taxonomy stating whether any
later obstruction can honestly be absolute, generic, or only conditional.

Kill condition: if the branch cannot distinguish robust forcing from exceptional
cancellations, prohibit "no finite closure" rhetoric and demote the endpoint to
a conditional or negative memo.

### Step 4 - Prove The Strongest Honest Exact Claim In One Metric

Depends on: Step 3.

Action: prove the strongest claim actually justified by the audited ledger and
cancellation taxonomy:
no finite exact closure in the audited realization,
generic no finite exact closure outside an explicit exceptional set,
an exact lower bound in one metric only,
or an explicit robust loophole family.

Action: compare the result immediately against the existing Step-8
non-isolability verdict and the carried Step-6 to Step-8 objects, making clear
whether the new statement genuinely strengthens the record, only qualifies it,
or merely repackages it.

Expected output: one scoped theorem, lemma, loophole memo, or exact negative
memo with a single declared metric and an explicit scope statement.

Kill condition: if the claimed advance over Step `008` is only a restatement of
classwise forced extras without new exact control of recursive closure or
cancellation, demote it to bookkeeping rather than theorem status.

### Step 5 - Package Only A Relevance-Screened Endpoint

Depends on: Step 4.

Action: package exactly one of four honest endpoints:
a scoped obstruction theorem,
a generic obstruction with explicit exceptional set,
a robust loophole handoff,
or a no-canonical-finite-reduction memo.

Action: state the evidentiary tier of any loophole.
A knife-edge degeneracy is a cautionary exception, not a constructive handoff.

Action: keep the final writeup explicit about what was proved for the single
audited realization or audited family and what remains unproved for the full
one-bridge class.

Expected output: one comparison-ready exact-closure package with honest scope,
honest metric, and explicit mission relevance.

Kill condition: if the final product still conflates support-level completion
with exact dynamical closure, mixes lower-bound currencies, or depends on a
hand-picked micro-family without explaining why that family matters, the chain
fails.
