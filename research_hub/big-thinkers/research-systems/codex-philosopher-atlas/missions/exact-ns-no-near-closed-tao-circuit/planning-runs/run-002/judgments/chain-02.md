# Judgment for Chain 02

## Overall Assessment

The attacker is right about the central defect. The original chain talked as if
it might prove the smallest friendly-admissible leakage sheet, but its actual
evidence base is still a frozen pair of friendly witnesses. That is a real
quantifier mismatch. The attacker is too strong, however, when it treats the
whole box program as intrinsically illegitimate or effectively redundant. A
minimal same-currency box can still be a legitimate theorem object if the scope
is narrowed to the carried pair or an explicitly verified friendly subclass and
if canonicity is audited first.

The refined chain should therefore stop trying to certify the repaired vector
as a class-wide friendly boundary by default. Its honest job is to decide
whether the repaired leakage sheet is:

- a canonical same-currency object with a narrow minimality theorem,
- a corrected coupled frontier or corrected box,
- only a calibration lemma for the carried witnesses, or
- a dossier artifact that fails canonicity or relevance.

## Major Critique Assessments

### 1. Step 1 treats the freeze as neutral and canonical

**Verdict: Partially valid**

Freezing a currency is necessary for theorem work. The flaw was not freezing
itself. The flaw was freezing without first auditing dependence on window
length, normalization, denominator conventions, conjugate completion, packet
representation, and spectator regrouping. The refined chain should therefore
turn Step 1 into a canonicity audit before promotion.

### 2. Two curated friendly witnesses are too thin to support class-wide "friendly-admissible" language

**Verdict: Valid**

This is the attack's strongest point. `F_SS(1/12)` and `F_SL(1/16)` can support
a calibration pair or perhaps a verified friendly subclass. They do not by
themselves justify language about the whole friendly class. Any broader claim
needs extra coverage or a structural argument reducing the whole class to that
pair.

### 3. Step 2 exact ledgers prove less than the chain claims

**Verdict: Partially valid**

The attacker is right that exact ledgers, coordinate saturation, and per-witness
slack do not by themselves show that the pair spans the true admissible
boundary. But the ledger work is still necessary. The repair is to add
witness-commensurability and local robustness checks, and to stop requiring
every coordinate to appear sharply on both witnesses.

### 4. Step 3 has a real quantifier error and needs demotion rules

**Verdict: Valid**

Showing that lowering one coordinate excludes one carried witness proves only
pair-relative or subclass-relative tightness. The original wording overstated
that as a route to the smallest friendly-admissible sheet. The refined chain
must separate pair-relative minimality, subclass minimality, and class-wide
minimality, and it must demote the claim when only the narrower scopes are
earned.

### 5. The attacker says the box is the wrong geometric object

**Verdict: Partially valid**

This is a serious possibility, but not a knockout. If the frozen currency
already exhibits coordinate tradeoffs, the chain should switch to a coupled
frontier or tradeoff memo. But a minimal same-currency box remains a coherent
theorem object when the scope is honestly limited and the currency is shown to
be canonical enough.

### 6. One hostile ledger is too weak for broad hostile overload rhetoric

**Verdict: Partially valid**

One hostile ledger can support a witness-level separation statement, not a
family-wide hostile theorem. The original chain already said "to the extent
actually earned," so the attacker overstates the defect somewhat. The repair is
to require explicit labeling of witness-level versus local-family hostile
conclusions and to treat unresolved hostile comparison as a major relevance
downgrade.

### 7. The chain lacked a relevance kill condition

**Verdict: Valid**

The original chain had rigor kills but not a clear rule against proving a
precise yet strategically empty result. The refined version needs a screen that
demotes friendly-only bookkeeping to a calibration lemma unless it fixes a
canonical leakage currency or materially sharpens later joint-frontier work.

### 8. Predetermined-conclusion bias and exact-number brittleness

**Verdict: Partially valid**

Starting from the repaired vector does create confirmation pressure, and exact
fractions like `1/12`, `1/16`, and `1/24` may turn out to be bookkeeping
artifacts. But this is exactly what an audit branch is supposed to test. The
right fix is to allow corrected constants, a corrected box, or a coupled object
rather than treating the repaired vector as presumptively right.

### 9. Redundancy with Chain 03 is decisive

**Verdict: Invalid**

The selector's rationale is sound. A leakage-only audit branch is not redundant
if it determines whether the promoted leakage object is canonical, too local,
or in need of correction before the joint-frontier chain relies on it. The
branch just needs narrower claims to earn that complementary role.

## Refinement Adopted

The refined version makes five structural changes:

1. It replaces the original freeze step with a canonicity audit of window,
   normalization, denominator, representation, and spectator partition choices.
2. It narrows the main theorem target from class-wide friendly minimality to
   the strongest honest scope actually justified: carried pair, verified
   friendly subclass, or full class only if genuinely earned.
3. It explicitly decides whether the correct theorem object is an axis-aligned
   box or a coupled frontier before trying to prove minimality.
4. It downgrades hostile comparison to witness-level or local-family strength
   unless stronger same-currency evidence is actually proved.
5. It adds a relevance screen so that a friendly-only result survives as a
   presentable output only when it fixes the promoted leakage object in a way
   the rest of the mission can genuinely use.

## Probability Assessment

**Estimated probability this refined chain yields a presentable result: 0.64**

Reasoning:

- The refined chain now has several respectable outputs: a canonical narrow
  minimality theorem, a corrected coupled-frontier memo, a calibration lemma
  that honestly demotes the repaired sheet, or a precise negative memo.
- The probability is not higher because the strongest outcomes still look hard:
  class-wide minimality and mission-central hostile separation are not well
  supported by the current two-witness record.
- The most plausible successful endpoint is a narrow pair-relative or
  subclass-relative theorem, or a precise memo explaining why the repaired
  leakage sheet is not canonical enough or not relevant enough for broader
  obstruction rhetoric.
