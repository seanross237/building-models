# Attack on Chain 02 - Classwise Leakage Sharpness And Friendly-Minimality Theorem

## Bottom line

This chain has one real virtue: it tries to force the repaired leakage sheet to
earn theorem status or get demoted. But the current plan is calibrated to
vindicate a pre-existing vector on a tiny hand-picked witness set. The likely
honest output is "this box is tight for these two friendly witnesses in this
frozen bookkeeping," which is materially weaker than "this is the smallest
friendly-admissible sheet" and much weaker than an obstruction theorem.

## What is genuinely strong

- It insists on one frozen interaction currency instead of letting the
  bookkeeping drift.
- It explicitly distinguishes coordinate sharpness from joint necessity.
- It leaves room for a negative memo instead of pretending the repaired sheet is
  already a theorem object.

## Step-by-step critique

### Step 1 - Freeze The Exact Leakage Currency

This step is not just setup; it already bakes in most of the desired answer.

- Freezing the one-bridge packet, `I = [0, 1]`, the `D_on` / `D_off` split, the
  spectator partition, and the repaired vector is not neutral bookkeeping.
  Those are exactly the choices that made the current record look clean in the
  first place.
- The chain assumes the four spectator classes are stable and meaningfully
  separated. That is not obvious. `mirror`, `companion`, `feedback`, and
  `cross` can exchange burden under regrouping, conjugate completion, or
  different exact routing conventions.
- Fixing `F_SS(1/12)` and `F_SL(1/16)` as the carried friendly calibration set
  is a very thin evidentiary base. Two selected points from two families are
  enough for calibration, not enough to justify "friendly-admissible" language.
- The frozen window `I = [0, 1]` may be doing more work than the chain admits.
  If classwise leakage is time-integrated or ratio-based, the budget vector can
  shift with window choice even when packet semantics do not.
- The chain says the object is obstruction-facing, but Step 1 is still entirely
  friendly-centric. Nothing here checks whether the chosen currency is also
  natural for hostile comparisons.

Kill-condition problem:

- The kill condition is too narrow. Dependence on window length, normalization,
  denominator convention, or conjugate-packet representation should be as fatal
  as dependence on a changed spectator partition.
- If this step fails, the issue is not merely that the object is "not stable
  enough for theorem work." It means the repaired vector may never have been
  canonical enough to deserve theorem promotion.

### Step 2 - Derive Exact Classwise Ratio Ledgers For The Friendly Set

This step overstates what two witness ledgers can prove.

- "Compute the exact classwise leakage vector" sounds stronger than it is. If
  the coordinates are ratios, exactness depends on exact denominator choices and
  possibly on nontrivial integrated quantities. That is not just arithmetic
  cleanup.
- A witness saturating a coordinate does not show that coordinate is
  structurally necessary. It may only show that the witness parameters were
  already tuned near the recorded threshold.
- Slack coordinates on each witness individually do not imply the pair jointly
  explains the repaired vector. A third friendly witness could expose that the
  true frontier cuts across the current box in a different direction.
- The chain quietly treats the two witnesses as commensurable probes of the same
  boundary. They may not be. If `F_SS` and `F_SL` use materially different
  support geometry or role logic, the same nominal classwise currency may still
  compare unlike objects.
- The step also ignores robustness. Endpoint saturation at one parameter value
  says much less than a local family-level statement showing the same boundary
  is stable nearby.

Kill-condition problem:

- Requiring every repaired coordinate to be recoverable on both witnesses is
  oddly calibrated. If the two witnesses are supposed to probe different facets,
  one should not expect every coordinate to show up sharply on both.
- The real failure mode is not missing symmetry across the pair. It is that the
  pair may not span the claimed admissible boundary at all.

### Step 3 - Prove Or Correct The Repaired Budget Vector

This is the chain's central theorem step, and it has a quantifier error.

- Showing that lowering one coordinate excludes at least one carried friendly
  witness proves tightness relative to those two witnesses. It does not prove
  "the smallest friendly-admissible sheet" except under the hidden assumption
  that the carried pair is already a sufficient proxy for the whole friendly
  class.
- The theorem object is probably wrong. A classwise vector is a box. If the real
  admissible set has tradeoffs between coordinates, then the natural object is a
  coupled frontier or polytope, not independent coordinate caps.
- Because of that, coordinate-by-coordinate sharpness can be misleading. A
  coordinate may be individually non-lowerable while still being too loose once
  another coordinate is adjusted.
- "Replace it with the strongest simpler corrected vector still supported by the
  same frozen witness set" keeps the whole program trapped inside the current
  calibration pair. A simpler box can be prettier and still be strategically
  empty.
- Exact threshold equalities are brittle. A theorem based on isolated endpoint
  saturation may be formally true and still fail the mission's robustness test.

Kill-condition problem:

- Rejecting "almost saturation" is fair, but exact inequalities alone do not
  rescue the theorem. They can certify the wrong theorem object with great
  precision.
- The missing kill condition is: if minimality only holds as a box around two
  special witnesses and not as a real friendly-class boundary, demote the result
  to a calibration lemma rather than calling it a minimality theorem.

### Step 4 - Pin One Honest Hostile Same-Currency Ledger

This step is too small to support the rhetoric around hostile overload.

- One explicit hostile `F_DT(delta, eta)` ledger can at best give a witness of
  separation. It cannot support a broad hostile clause unless there is a
  quantified statement over a family or at least a stable region in
  `(delta, eta)`.
- The asymmetry is glaring: the friendly side gets two curated witnesses used to
  calibrate sharpness, while the hostile side gets one ledger used to suggest
  overload. That is not balanced evidence.
- Overload on a coordinate is not automatically meaningful. If the friendly
  witnesses already run close to the same threshold, then a hostile crossing may
  be numerically true but strategically weak.
- The same-currency requirement is correct in principle, but the chain uses it
  mostly as a veto against hostile comparison. If the hostile object only looks
  natural in a broader packet semantics, that may indicate the frozen friendly
  currency is the wrong theorem object, not merely that the hostile side is
  unresolved.
- The plan does not distinguish a hostile point example from a hostile-family
  obstruction. Those are very different theorem outcomes.

Kill-condition problem:

- The listed kill condition is fair as an anti-cheating rule, but too permissive
  strategically. The more likely failure is not invented numerics. It is that a
  same-currency hostile ledger exists but gives no clean separation margin.
- If the hostile side stays unresolved, the chain should treat that as a major
  downgrading of mission relevance, not a mild packaging choice.

### Step 5 - Package The Obstruction-Facing Result

The packaging step is cleaner than the evidentiary base beneath it.

- A classwise minimality theorem without hostile separation is mostly a
  statement about friendly bookkeeping, not about the firewall idea.
- The chain says it will report whether the result supports friendly
  admissibility, friendly-plus-hostile separation, or neither. But it never says
  when friendly admissibility alone is worth keeping rather than filing as a
  dossier refinement.
- The negative memo branch is the more honest branch. The theorem branch is too
  easy to reach if Step 3 proves only pair-relative box tightness.
- Forcing one named failure type is also too tidy. The likely outcome is mixed:
  unstable partition choices, a noncanonical box, and unresolved hostile data
  all at once.

Kill-condition problem:

- The final kill condition only guards against vague anti-circuit rhetoric. It
  does not guard against the opposite failure: a perfectly precise theorem with
  negligible bearing on the mission.
- The chain needs a relevance kill condition, not just a rigor kill condition.

## Structural weaknesses of the whole chain

- Quantifier mismatch: the plan talks as if it is proving the smallest
  friendly-admissible sheet, but the actual evidence base is two frozen friendly
  witnesses. That is a theorem about a calibration pair unless much more is
  shown.
- Wrong geometric object: the chain treats the leakage constraint as a
  coordinatewise box problem. If the real exact-NS tradeoffs are coupled, the
  correct object is a frontier, not a five-number sheet.
- Predetermined-conclusion bias: the repaired vector is presented first, then
  the rest of the chain asks how to certify it. That is confirmation workflow,
  not neutral discovery.
- Friendly-hostile asymmetry: two curated friendly witnesses against one hostile
  ledger makes it easy to produce a strong-looking friendly theorem and a weak
  hostile story.
- Exact-number brittleness: fractions like `1/12`, `1/16`, and `1/24` may be
  dossier artifacts of the current witness bookkeeping rather than intrinsic
  constants worth theorem packaging.
- Redundancy risk: this overlaps heavily with the selected joint-frontier chain
  on the canonical one-bridge class. If that chain is done honestly, it already
  asks where `F_SS`, `F_SL`, and `F_DT` sit relative to the promoted screens.
  Chain 02 only earns separate value if it proves something more invariant than
  a frontier slice.
- Misstated distinctiveness: the chain claims it is not optimizing the joint
  feasible set, but Step 3 is exactly a feasible-set boundary question, just
  phrased as coordinate minimality.
- Kill-condition imbalance: the chain has several rigor kills but no equally
  sharp criterion for strategic emptiness. Its most likely failure is not
  algebraic inconsistency. It is proving a precise statement that is too local,
  too box-shaped, and too witness-specific to matter.

## Fair verdict

This chain is worth running only as a hard audit of whether the repaired leakage
sheet is a nonaccidental summary of the current friendly witnesses. That is a
useful housekeeping result. It is not yet a credible route to "smallest
friendly-admissible sheet" or to a serious hostile obstruction theorem.

The strongest honest endpoint would be a much narrower claim: the repaired
vector is the minimal same-currency box that preserves the specific witnesses
`F_SS(1/12)` and `F_SL(1/16)`, with any hostile comparison stated only at the
strength actually proved. Anything broader risks laundering a calibration
artifact into a theorem.
