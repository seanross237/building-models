# Judgment on Selected Chain 01 - Joint Feasibility Frontier On The Canonical One-Bridge Class

## Overall Verdict

The attacker is correct on the main issue: as an immediate post-Step-6 branch,
the selected chain is misordered. Step 6 froze two promoted objects and named
two first exact tests; it did not yet justify a full joint-frontier program on
the whole canonical one-bridge class.

That said, the original chain is not empty or confused about the strategic
question. The joint `G_tmpl` / `G_leak` interaction question is real, and a
later synthesis branch still has value. The right refinement is therefore not
to discard the chain, but to stage it behind record reconciliation and the two
object-local exact closures already named in the Step-6 handoff.

## Critique Labels

### 1. Step 1 - Freeze The Full One-Bridge Parameter Ledger

**Partially valid.**

The critique is right that the chain assumes too quickly that the whole
canonical one-bridge class can be turned into one finite exact frontier ledger.
That is not something Step 6 actually hands off. It is also right that there
is real record drift on the leakage side:
`step-005/explorations/exploration-001/REPORT.md` keeps
`Lambda_cross = 1/12`,
while `step-005/RESULTS.md` and `step-006/RESULTS.md` use `1/24`.

But the critique overstates the damage slightly. Step 6 does freeze canonical
packet semantics and two promoted objects on one ledger, so asking for a shared
parameter sheet is not inherently illegitimate. The fix is to narrow the first
joint step to an authority memo plus a smaller shared sheet, not to assume the
entire class is unusable.

### 2. Step 2 - Express Both Gates In One Exact Variable System

**Valid.**

This is the attacker's strongest point. Step 6 explicitly says the next
template-facing work still needs the exact `F_SL(rho)` defect ledger, and the
next leakage-facing work still needs the exact friendly-stress-set ratio
ledger and sharpness proof. A global joint sheet before those are closed would
mostly be placeholders.

### 3. Step 3 - Map The Joint Frontier With Known Witnesses And Anti-Witnesses

**Valid.**

As written, this step is too early and too broad. The current dossier mixes
exact friendly witnesses with partly symbolic hostile information, especially
on the template side. It also duplicates the core closure work of Chains 01
and 02 before those narrower ledgers are settled. The fallback is also staged
incorrectly: if the map turns into a loose sweep, the honest redirect is back
to object-local closure, not immediately to the constructive moonshot.

### 4. Step 4 - Decide Complementarity, Redundancy, Or Separation

**Partially valid.**

The critique is right that this asks for a logical relation verdict too early
and gets too close to implication/equivalence language that Step 6 explicitly
withholds. But an explicit separation witness or a narrow interaction memo is
still a legitimate later goal. What fails here is timing and burden control,
not the entire idea of comparing the gates.

### 5. Step 5 - Package The Frontier Verdict

**Valid.**

The attacker is right that the branch keeps incompatible endpoint types alive
for too long and never defines what "strongest" means. A theorem, a survivor,
and a separation memo have different proof burdens. A refined chain must force
that burden to narrow earlier and must declare any survivor-ordering rule
before using it.

### 6. Structural Critique - It Ignores The Actual Step-6 Handoff

**Valid.**

`step-006/RESULTS.md` and
`step-006/explorations/exploration-002/REPORT.md`
plainly hand off two smaller exact tests:
`G_tmpl` on `F_SL(rho)` and `G_leak` on
`{F_SS(1/12), F_SL(1/16)}`,
while warning against equivalence claims. The selected chain jumps past that.

### 7. Structural Critique - It Depends On Record Stability The Repo Does Not Fully Have

**Partially valid.**

This is correct for a joint-frontier theorem, but too strong if read more
broadly. The record is stable enough to keep two promoted objects alive and to
support narrower theorem-facing work. It is not stable enough for an immediate
global frontier program while the `L_cross` threshold drift and hostile-side
exactness gaps remain unresolved.

### 8. Structural Critique - It Overestimates What "Same Packet Class" Buys

**Valid.**

Shared ambient class does not by itself yield a useful joint geometry,
optimization problem, or implication theorem. The joint branch has to earn
those with exact ledgers, not assume them from common packet semantics.

### 9. Structural Critique - It Has Bad Dependency Order

**Valid.**

This is the core judgment. The joint synthesis question should come after the
two smaller Step-6 closures or at least after they are reduced to exact shared
data on a controlled subclass.

### 10. Structural Critique - It Is More Redundant With Chains 01 And 02 Than Advertised

**Partially valid.**

There is real overlap, because the selected chain cannot honestly advance
without reusing the exact closure work that Chains 01 and 02 isolate. But the
selected chain still has distinct portfolio value once those prerequisites are
met: it can ask whether the two promoted objects genuinely interact, separate,
or merely co-orient on a closed carried record.

### 11. Structural Critique - Its Kill Conditions Are Badly Staged

**Valid.**

The chain is too tolerant early, when exact ledgers and threshold authority
should already be enforced, and too ambitious late, when it suddenly demands a
clean frontier package. The refined version should fail early on missing exact
inputs.

### 12. Structural Critique - The File-Label Mismatch Signals A Workflow Weakness

**Valid, but minor.**

`selected/chain-01.md` contains original Chain 03, and the selector explains
that mapping. The traceability issue is real, but it is secondary to the
mathematical dependency-order problem.

## Refined Judgment

Keep the branch, but downgrade it from
"solve the full joint frontier on the whole canonical one-bridge class"
to
"run a staged joint interaction test after record reconciliation and the two
Step-6 object-local closures."

That preserves the original chain's upside while removing the parts that are
currently premature:

- no immediate full-class frontier language;
- no equivalence/redundancy packaging before exact shared data exists;
- no survivor ranking without a declared order;
- and no broad search until the carried ledgers close exactly.

## Probability Assessment

**Probability that the refined chain yields a presentable result: 0.67**

Reasoning:

- The chance of a clean, fully restricted joint interaction memo or bounded
  deferral memo is fairly good once the branch is staged honestly.
- The chance of a true restricted frontier theorem is materially lower because
  it still depends on closing two exact ledgers and resolving record drift.
- The chance of an immediate full frontier theorem or strongest-survivor result
  on the whole canonical one-bridge class is low on the current record.

Operationally, this refined branch has a solid floor and a moderate ceiling:
it is much more likely to produce an honest presentable memo than the original
selected version, but it is still not the likeliest route to the strongest
mathematical theorem in the portfolio.
