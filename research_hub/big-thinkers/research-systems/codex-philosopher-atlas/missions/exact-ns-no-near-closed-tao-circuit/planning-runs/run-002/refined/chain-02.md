# Chain 02 - Canonical Classwise Leakage Audit And Narrow Minimality Theorem

## Central Premise

The repaired leakage sheet deserves theorem status only if it survives a
canonicity audit and then earns the strongest statement its evidence base can
honestly support. The likely honest endpoint is not a universal
friendly-admissible box. It is a canonical same-currency leakage object
together with either a pair-relative or subclass-relative minimality statement,
a corrected coupled frontier, or an exact memo saying the repaired sheet is
only calibration.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a theorem or lemma that the repaired vector, or a corrected replacement, is
  the minimal same-currency box preserving the carried friendly pair
  `F_SS(1/12)` and `F_SL(1/16)`, or preserving an explicitly verified friendly
  subclass;
- a corrected coupled frontier or tradeoff memo showing that a box is not the
  right theorem object in the frozen currency;
- a witness-level or local-family hostile separation statement in the same
  currency, at no broader scope than actually proved; or
- a precise negative memo showing that the leakage sheet fails canonicity,
  witness commensurability, or mission relevance.

## Why This Refined Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is an audit of the leakage object itself. It does not optimize the
joint frontier and it does not try to build a survivor. Its job is to decide
whether the promoted leakage sheet is canonical theorem material, a narrower
calibration lemma, or a dossier artifact.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Audit And Freeze A Canonical Leakage Currency

Depends on: none.

Action: test the repaired leakage vector against all bookkeeping choices that
are still plausibly allowed by the record:
window length,
normalization,
denominator convention,
conjugate completion,
packet representation,
and spectator regrouping.

Action: freeze one canonical protocol only if the coordinate values and witness
comparisons are stable enough under those choices. State explicitly whether the
hostile packets can be written in that same currency.

Expected output: a canonicality memo plus one frozen leakage protocol.

Kill condition: if the classwise vector or overload pattern changes materially
under allowed bookkeeping choices, stop and demote the repaired sheet to dossier
status rather than theorem promotion.

### Step 2 - Build Exact Friendly Ledgers And Check Witness Commensurability

Depends on: Step 1.

Action: compute the exact classwise leakage vectors for `F_SS(1/12)` and
`F_SL(1/16)` in the Step 1 currency. Record which coordinates each witness
probes sharply, which remain slack, and whether nearby parameter movement
preserves the same facet interpretation.

Action: verify that the two witnesses are honestly comparable as boundary
probes in that currency:
same denominator logic,
same packet semantics,
same role interpretation,
and enough local robustness to avoid isolated endpoint numerics masquerading as
structure.

Expected output: a two-witness friendly ledger table with facet roles and a
commensurability note.

Kill condition: if the witnesses cannot be compared honestly in one currency,
or if the apparent sharpness exists only as isolated endpoint numerics with no
stable local meaning, stop the pair-boundary program and downgrade to
per-witness calibration only.

### Step 3 - Decide The Honest Theorem Object And Scope

Depends on: Step 2.

Action: determine whether the frozen record supports an axis-aligned box or
already exhibits coordinate tradeoffs that require a coupled frontier or
tradeoff object.

Action: distinguish three scopes explicitly:
the carried pair,
an explicitly verified friendly subclass,
or the full friendly class.
Promote only the strongest one actually justified by the record.

Expected output: a theorem-object memo fixing box versus coupled frontier and
the exact scope of any later theorem language.

Kill condition: if the strongest justified scope is only the carried pair,
prohibit "friendly-admissible" language and demote the endpoint to a
calibration lemma unless later steps earn more.

### Step 4 - Prove Minimality Or Correction At The Declared Scope, Then Test Hostile Comparison Honestly

Depends on: Step 3.

Action: if the object is a box, prove which coordinates are individually sharp
and which are only jointly necessary at the Step 3 scope. If the object is
coupled, state the explicit tradeoff relation or corrected frontier slice.

Action: write hostile `F_DT(delta, eta)` comparisons only in the same currency
and label them as witness-level separation, local-family separation, or
unresolved. Record the separation margin relative to friendly slack rather than
merely noting a coordinate crossing.

Expected output: a narrow minimality theorem or corrected object, plus any
hostile clause stated only at its earned strength.

Kill condition: if the hostile side only yields numerically tiny or
bookkeeping-dependent crossings, exclude hostile rhetoric from the final theorem
and record mission relevance as limited.

### Step 5 - Package Only A Relevance-Screened Endpoint

Depends on: Step 4.

Action: package one of four honest outputs:
a canonical pair-relative or subclass-relative leakage theorem,
a corrected coupled frontier memo,
a calibration lemma saying the repaired vector is only a witness-set box,
or a negative memo naming one or more failure types:
noncanonical currency,
noncommensurable witnesses,
wrong geometric object,
or unresolved hostile relevance.

Action: keep a friendly-only output only if it materially sharpens later
joint-frontier work by fixing the leakage currency or correcting the promoted
sheet. Otherwise file it as dossier maintenance rather than theorem progress.

Expected output: a theorem candidate, corrected memo, calibration lemma, or
precise negative memo with exact scope.

Kill condition: if the final product is only
"the repaired sheet looks tight on two chosen friendly witnesses"
without a canonicity audit,
scope correction,
or relevance screen,
the chain failed.
