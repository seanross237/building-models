# Chain 01 - Family-Level Template-Defect Theorem On `F_SL(rho)`

## Central Premise

Step `006` already froze the first exact theorem question for repaired
`Template-Defect Near-Closure`:
compute the exact
`rho`-dependence of
`G_tmpl(P_n; I) = (Delta_tmpl, Delta_spec)`
on the carried scale-separated family
`F_SL(rho)` for `0 < rho <= 1/16`.

The cleanest next result is therefore not another audit. It is one
family-level admissibility theorem, or a sharp family-level failure memo, on
the same frozen packet sheet and window.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a proposition-scale statement that the frozen family
  `F_SL(rho)`
  satisfies the repaired template sheet
  `Delta_tmpl <= 1/4`,
  `Delta_spec <= 49/256`
  uniformly for
  `0 < rho <= 1/16`,
  with the extremal case identified exactly; or
- a bounded negative result showing that the recorded friendly boundary witness
  `F_SL(1/16)` is not truly extremal, the repaired sheet is not exact on this
  family, or the observable cannot be closed without hidden bookkeeping.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is purely about the role-template observable on one carried family.
It does not try to prove a leakage theorem, search a whole admissible region,
or construct a counterexample dynamically.
