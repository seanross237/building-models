# Chain 02 - Bottleneck-First Helical-Triad Route With Quantitative Reconstruction Test

## Central Premise

Exact helical-triad geometry is worth pursuing only against one fixed theorem-level bottleneck and under a presumption of obstruction. The branch is live only if one exact Leray-projected triad feature can be written as an explicit gain in one named estimate or contradiction step and still survive full shell summation, bad-class restoration, nonlocal interactions, and any required localization.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a proposition-scale candidate inequality naming one interaction class, one theorem-level bottleneck term, one gain currency, the exact reconstruction regime, and the first missing lemma; or
- a sharp obstruction memo stating exactly where the triad mechanism fails: endpoint mismatch, prior-art redundancy, effect-size insufficiency, shell-summation collapse, multiplicity collapse, bad-class contamination, nonlocal-interaction collapse, or localization collapse.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain stays at the exact Fourier-symbol level, but it no longer treats exactness or helical bookkeeping as evidence by itself. It is built to decide whether any Tao-sensitive triad feature produces theorem-grade leverage after honest return to the full Navier-Stokes interaction.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Fix the theorem bottleneck, framework, representation, and gain threshold

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one exact theorem-level bottleneck before symbolic exploration begins, such as one named flux estimate, one stretching bound, one enstrophy-production inequality, or one contradiction point inside a fixed solution framework.
- Action: write the target in near-inequality form, including the exact term where a triad feature would have to improve the baseline estimate.
- Action: choose one representation only for execution: helical decomposition with explicit Leray-projected coefficients, or an exactly equivalent triad-symbol form.
- Action: define one admissible gain currency inside the chosen estimate and state the minimum size needed to matter after reconstruction.
- Expected output: a setup sheet naming the theorem bottleneck, framework, target term, representation, gain currency, and minimum success threshold.
- Kill condition: if the branch cannot freeze one proof-relevant bottleneck and one quantitative success threshold at the start, terminate with an endpoint-selection obstruction.

### Step 2 - Build an operational triad ledger and the precise Tao comparator

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: decompose the exact Navier-Stokes nonlinearity into the chosen triad variables and record which sign, helicity, angle, resonance, and Leray-projector features are exact.
- Action: define the Tao-style comparator operationally relative to the chosen bottleneck: what averaging is being compared, what it preserves, and at what frequency granularity the comparison is made.
- Action: test each candidate feature for bottleneck relevance, not just symbolic visibility, and reject features that are only basis effects or classificatory symmetries.
- Action: assign each surviving feature a maximal plausible pre-reconstruction effect size on the chosen bottleneck term.
- Expected output: a triad ledger with columns for exact structure, comparator sensitivity, bottleneck relevance, and maximal plausible pre-reconstruction gain.
- Kill condition: if no exact feature changes the chosen bottleneck term in a quantitatively meaningful way even before reconstruction, terminate with a symbol-level obstruction.

### Step 3 - Promote one mechanism, screen prior art, and write the candidate inequality

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: promote at most one mechanism, allowing either one exact feature or one explicitly quantified aggregate bias, but not a loose stack of suggestive effects.
- Action: identify the nearest prior-art analogue before promotion and reject mechanisms that merely rename standard same-helicity, Beltrami-depletion, or shell-transfer folklore.
- Action: write the exact candidate inequality, transfer estimate, or contradiction line where the mechanism enters, together with the claimed sign and size improvement over baseline.
- Action: if the mechanism relies on a restricted interaction family, quantify what share of the relevant transfer, shell sum, or interaction measure that family carries.
- Expected output: one theorem-gap note containing the explicit inequality, the promoted mechanism, the prior-art screen, the relevant interaction class, and the claimed gain.
- Kill condition: if the mechanism cannot be written as an explicit candidate inequality, or if it only lives on an unquantified thin class, terminate with a relevance obstruction.

### Step 4 - Charge the full reconstruction bill before any promotion

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: restore full dyadic shell summation, angular multiplicity, mixed-helicity interactions, and nonlocal triads, and compute whether the Step 3 gain survives with positive margin.
- Action: charge contamination from non-favorable classes explicitly rather than treating them as side remarks.
- Action: if the theorem bottleneck needs physical-space localization, add the associated cutoff, commutator, local-energy, or reconstruction losses and test whether the mechanism survives them.
- Action: perturb away from exactly favorable interaction geometry and decide whether the gain reflects real full-equation structure or only a narrow regime artifact.
- Expected output: a reconstruction ledger showing net gain or a precise failure mode after honest restoration of the full equation.
- Kill condition: if the gain is annihilated, drowned by multiplicity, neutralized by bad classes, or lost under localization, terminate with an obstruction memo rather than carrying a positive route forward.

### Step 5 - State the narrowest earned proposition or obstruction

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: if one mechanism survives, state a proposition-like claim naming the exact interaction class, theorem bottleneck, explicit gain, reconstruction regime, known inputs, and first missing lemma.
- Action: if nothing survives, state the sharpest negative result earned, distinguishing among effect-size failure, sparse-class irrelevance, shell collapse, bad-class domination, nonlocal collapse, and localization collapse.
- Action: keep the final claim tied to the single fixed bottleneck and the single promoted mechanism. Remove all unevaluated alternatives.
- Expected output: a final-comparison-ready proposition blueprint or a precise obstruction statement.
- Kill condition: if the final claim still depends on vague references to favorable triads, special cancellations, or unspecified summation regimes, the chain has not earned promotion.
