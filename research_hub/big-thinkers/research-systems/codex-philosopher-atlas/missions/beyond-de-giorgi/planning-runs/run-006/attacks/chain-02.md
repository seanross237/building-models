# Attack on Chain 02 - Helical-Triad Symbol Depletion Route

This chain has one genuine strength: it correctly identifies a real discriminator, namely whether there is any exact Navier-Stokes triad-level structure that a Tao-style averaging would wash out. That is a legitimate fault line.

The problem is that the chain is still too permissive where it should be rigid, and too optimistic where the known graveyards already are. It can generate a tidy memo trail without ever pinning down a theorem-relevant gain.

## Step 1 - Freeze the symbolic language, target bottleneck, and gain currency

This step looks disciplined, but it is also the first place the chain can quietly game itself.

- The choice set is too broad. "Helical decomposition," "triad-symbol coefficients," shell flux, enstrophy production, stretching transfer, sign, cancellation, coercive depletion, and derivative-count improvement are not comparable objects. A branch can survive by picking the easiest symbolic package and the softest notion of gain.
- The step never requires that the chosen bottleneck be tied to an actual regularity obstruction rather than a merely interesting interaction ledger. That is a major gap. A clean shell-flux statement is worthless if it does not sit on a known proof bottleneck.
- The kill condition only guards against drifting across architectures. It does not guard against cherry-picking within the architecture. A branch can stay fully inside "triad geometry" and still opportunistically choose whichever bottleneck/gain pair makes the memo sound best.
- There is no quantitative threshold for what would count as a meaningful gain. A sign on a sparse triad class, a cancellation that loses under shell multiplicity, and a derivative improvement too small to close any estimate are all currently allowed to look live.

Net: Step 1 freezes language, but not standards. It prevents some opportunism while still allowing the most dangerous kind.

## Step 2 - Build the exact triad ledger and the Tao comparator

This is the conceptual center of the chain, and it is under-specified.

- The "Tao comparator" is not fixed tightly enough. Which averaged model, preserving which invariants, at which comparison granularity? Without that, "Tao-sensitive" can become a rhetorical label instead of a mathematically checkable distinction.
- The separation into "exact structure," "Tao-sensitive structure," and "generic quadratic leftovers" is heavily representation-dependent. Helical coordinates are useful, but they also make bookkeeping artifacts look structural. The chain never states an invariant test for whether a feature is real or merely a basis effect.
- The step ignores the possibility that the attractive structure is exact at the single-triad level but irrelevant once conjugation symmetry, multiplicity, and cross-class compensation are included. That is precisely the trap in this area.
- The rejection rule for "bookkeeping symmetries" is directionally right, but it is too subjective. Without a frozen bottleneck estimate in hand, there is no rigorous way to tell whether a feature is cosmetic or potentially coercive.

Net: this step can produce a sharp-looking triad ledger while still failing to identify anything that survives honest comparison or invariant reformulation.

## Step 3 - Attach one promoted triad feature to one theorem gap

This is where the route should become serious, and it still does not.

- "One theorem gap" is too vague. The chain should demand an explicit estimate gap, with the norm, the scale weights, and the exact place in a regularity argument where the gain would enter. As written, the branch can attach a mechanism to a narrative gap rather than a proof gap.
- Restricting to one promoted mechanism is good discipline, but it happens too late. The prior-art benchmark should come before promotion, not after. Otherwise the chain can spend time promoting a mechanism that is just a repackaging of Waleffe-type helical interaction lore, Beltrami depletion rhetoric, or decimated-shell intuition.
- The allowed gain types remain too elastic. A gain that is only local-in-frequency or conditional on one interaction family is usually not close to enough. The hard question is whether the gain is strong enough after summation and bad-class contamination, and this step does not force that calculation.
- The kill condition is too weak. "Attached to a concrete theorem gap" is not enough if the attachment is only schematic. The branch should have to state why the proposed gain is of the right sign and size to improve a specific bound, not merely where it would be inserted.

Net: Step 3 can still certify a descriptive mechanism as theorem-relevant without earning that label.

## Step 4 - Stress-test shell summation, bad triads, and localization debt

This is the step where most triad-geometry ideas die, and the chain understates how terminal that is.

- The route postpones the hardest issues until after mechanism promotion. That is backwards. Shell multiplicity, nonlocal triads, and physical-space reconstruction are not cleanup details; they are the main obstruction.
- "Restore the full triad sum" is the real proof problem, but the chain gives no criterion for what would count as surviving it. Without an explicit summability target, a favorable-sign subset can look encouraging even when it is drowned by the rest of the ledger.
- Bad-triad contamination is treated as one item on a checklist, but in practice it can dominate. Unless there is a counting lemma, a weight, or a coercive imbalance proving favorable classes beat unfavorable ones, the whole mechanism is likely cosmetic.
- Nonlocal triads are named but not structurally handled. That is a serious omission, because many shell-local heuristics collapse precisely when high-low and nonlocal interactions are put back in.
- The warning about localization debt is correct, but the chain still smuggles in hope. If the mechanism needs a physical-space statement, that usually means the Fourier-level effect was not strong enough on its own. The reconstruction cost is often the entire problem, not an add-on cost.
- The failure taxonomy is neat, but mostly descriptive. It classifies collapse after the fact rather than imposing proof obligations early enough to prevent false positives.

Net: Step 4 knows where the bodies are buried, but it does not require enough to avoid burying another idea there.

## Step 5 - Promote only a proposition-scale survivor

This final filter is necessary, but still too soft.

- A "proposition-scale target" sounds rigorous, but a proposition can still be mathematically true and strategically useless. The chain should require that the first missing lemma plausibly advances the mission rather than merely isolating a decorative triad effect.
- The negative endpoint is better than nothing, but it still risks being too coarse. There is a difference between "single-triad signal does not survive shell summation," "survives on a sparse class but loses to multiplicity," and "requires localization whose cost exceeds the gain." Those are materially different obstructions.
- The final kill condition only bans vague language like "favorable triads" or "special cancellations." That is good housekeeping, but it is not enough. The branch should also have to freeze constants, interaction classes, and the exact summation regime under which the claim is supposed to matter.

Net: Step 5 protects against hand-waving, but not against strategically empty rigor.

## Structural Weaknesses of the Whole Chain

- The central premise is already biased. It assumes that if Tao-style averaging destroys a decisive structure, the "sharpest candidate" is exact triad-symbol or helical geometry. That is a strong prior, not an established fact. Other exact structures could be more decisive: pressure coupling, incompressibility constraints in physical space, or localization/intermittency interactions.
- The chain is symbol-first rather than bottleneck-first. It starts by choosing a representation and only later asks whether the chosen object actually touches a real theorem bottleneck. That encourages elegant algebra before strategic relevance.
- The route is highly exposed to prior-art overlap. Helical-sign classes, Beltrami depletion, restricted interaction geometry, and shell-transfer rhetoric have been mined for years. The chain recognizes this, but too late. The literature filter should be front-loaded, not attached after mechanism promotion.
- The kill-condition calibration is uneven. Early steps are permissive enough that weak ideas can survive, while later steps can kill a route only after substantial memo-writing. Conversely, a failure on one frozen bottleneck may not actually show the broader triad route is dead; it may just show that the chosen bottleneck was the wrong test case. The chain does not separate those two failure modes cleanly.
- There is no explicit quantitative standard for success. The route never states the minimum gain size needed to matter after shell summation, what norm is being improved, or what summability threshold must be beaten. Without that, "gain currency" is too metaphorical.
- The chain is structurally tempted to overvalue exactness. Exact symbolic structure is not the same thing as usable coercivity. Many exact triad identities are mathematically real and strategically irrelevant.

## Fair Verdict

This chain is not empty. Its comparison against Tao-style averaging is a real and worthwhile discriminator, and Step 4 at least acknowledges the actual graveyard: full summation, bad classes, and localization debt.

But as written, the route is still too easy to keep alive with elegant symbolic bookkeeping, and too hard to force into a theorem-relevant estimate. The most likely failure mode is not that the triad geometry is false; it is that it is exact, interesting, and ultimately cosmetic once the full equation is restored.
