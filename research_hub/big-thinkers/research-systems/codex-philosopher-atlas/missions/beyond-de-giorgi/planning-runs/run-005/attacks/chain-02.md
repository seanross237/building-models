# Attack on Chain 02

## Overall verdict

This is a better-designed chain than a typical speculative PDE route. It fixes a narrow object class, it names explicit kill conditions, and it is at least aware of the central failure mode: a pretty triad-level effect that disappears after reconstruction of the full equation.

The problem is that the chain is still structurally biased toward finding a "distinguished" symbol-level feature before it has earned a theorem-level bottleneck. In practice, the helical-triad literature is already full of exact-looking interaction geometry that becomes weak, non-sign-definite, or irrelevant once one restores mixed triads, shell summation, and localization. So the positive route here is weak; the negative route is much stronger. If this chain produces anything honest, it is more likely to be a sharp obstruction memo than a usable regularity mechanism.

## Step-by-step critique

### Step 1 - Fix the representation, comparison class, and gain currency

This step is underconstrained in exactly the wrong place.

- "Helical decomposition" and "triad symbol coefficients" are not yet a research commitment. They are representations of the same quadratic interaction, and switching emphasis between them can still hide opportunism.
- The admissible solution framework is doing most of the real work, but the step treats it like setup. A mild-solution Fourier analysis, a critical-element contradiction setup, and a local-energy argument do not expose the same bottlenecks. If the framework is not fixed together with the theorem target, the later "gain" can be retrofitted to whichever framework looks friendliest.
- The proposed bottleneck menu is too heterogeneous. Energy flux, enstrophy production, stretching transfer, and a rigidity quantity in a concentration-compactness argument are not comparable currencies. Choosing among them is already choosing the proof architecture.
- The gain currencies are also incommensurable. A sign, a derivative saving, and a propagation-time improvement are not interchangeable. Without a target estimate, the chain can call almost anything a "gain."

The kill condition is too weak. Merely fixing one representation and one bottleneck "without switching architectures opportunistically" is not enough. A bad chain can satisfy that condition by making an arbitrary early choice and then marching forward. The real requirement should be stronger: name the exact theorem-level inequality or contradiction point now, or stop.

### Step 2 - Build the exact triad-interaction ledger

This step risks producing taxonomy instead of leverage.

- A ledger of helicity signs, resonance constraints, and Leray-symbol identities is easy to compile and easy to overinterpret. Exactness at the single-triad level is cheap.
- The distinction between "Tao-sensitive" and "preserved by averaging" is not operationally defined here. Which averaging? Preserving what comparison norm? At what scale resolution? Without that, the chain can declare a feature "destroyed by averaging" simply because the bookkeeping changed.
- Many symbol-level structures are exact but non-actionable. Anti-symmetries, cancellation identities, and selection rules often control the algebra before summation, then wash out once one sums over geometrically large triad classes.
- The step only rejects features with "no bottleneck relevance," but it does not say how relevance is measured. Relevance should mean: the feature changes the sign, size, or summability of a specific term in a specific estimate. Anything weaker is decorative.

The kill condition is again too permissive. A feature can survive as genuinely Tao-sensitive and still be useless because its quantitative effect is far below summation and localization losses. This step should not just sort structure into bins; it should estimate the maximal plausible size of the effect.

### Step 3 - Attach one surviving triad feature to one bottleneck theorem gap

This is the right ambition, but it arrives too late and remains too memo-like.

- The chain says "attach" the mechanism to a theorem gap, but the theorem gap was never fixed sharply enough in Step 1. That means Step 3 can quietly redefine the target around the mechanism instead of testing the mechanism against an already-fixed obstruction.
- "Choose at most one promoted triad mechanism" helps discipline, but it may also force a false picture. Some symbol-level effects are only visible as aggregate statistical bias, not as a single heroic cancellation. If the route only allows a one-feature narrative, it may either cherry-pick or prematurely discard diffuse effects.
- Allowing the intended gain to be "conditional on one restricted triad family" is dangerous. Restricted families are exactly where helical intuition looks strongest and full NS relevance looks weakest. Unless the family carries a quantified proportion of the relevant transfer, this is a built-in cherry-picking loophole.
- Naming prior art is necessary but not sufficient. The real risk is not just rediscovering helical decimation or shell-model folklore; it is repackaging known qualitative preferences of same-helicity or near-resonant interactions as if they were new theorem-grade control.

The expected output should not be an "insertion memo." It should be one candidate inequality with the new term written explicitly. Without that, the chain still has not crossed from story to proof.

### Step 4 - Stress-test summation, localization, and robustness

This is the strongest step in the chain and the place where the route will probably die.

- "Charge the full cost" is the right instinct, but the chain underspecifies the bill. It needs explicit dyadic summation, angular multiplicity, and the loss from reintroducing all mixed-helicity interactions. Otherwise the stress test stays rhetorical.
- Localization is a serious threat that the chain mentions too lightly. Helical-triad structure is most transparent in global Fourier variables. Many of the hoped-for cancellations are not stable under spatial cutoffs, local energy identities, or profile decompositions. If the eventual theorem bottleneck needs localization, this step is not a side check; it is the main obstacle.
- The perturbation test around favorable helicity or resonance classes is likely fatal. Full Navier-Stokes does not let one isolate clean same-helicity or near-resonant sectors for free. If the gain is only visible on a thin interaction set, then the chain is probably seeing a regime artifact, not a structural mechanism.
- The classification of failure modes is sensible, but "regime-only signal" is too mild a label. For a regularity route, a mechanism that only works in a narrow spectral or helicity regime is usually already dead unless the theorem target was explicitly restricted to that regime.

Fairly, this step is where the chain shows intellectual honesty. It knows the real enemy is reconstruction of the full equation. But that also means the earlier steps are mostly pre-screening for a failure that the author already suspects.

### Step 5 - Convert any survivor into proposition scale

By this point, a real survivor should already look proposition-scale. Waiting until Step 5 is a weakness.

- If the mechanism cannot be formulated as a theorem-like statement immediately after Step 3 or Step 4, it usually means the chain never had a real mechanism, only a promising interpretation.
- "State the first missing lemma" can hide the actual impossibility. In these problems, the first missing lemma is often the whole problem: a sign-definite shell-summed estimate, a coercive bound after localization, or a frequency-envelope gain that does not exist.
- The negative branch is much more credible than the positive branch. A careful memo saying "exact helical geometry creates triad-level preferences, but they are quantitatively annihilated by mixed-triad summation and localization" would be a real output. A positive proposition here would require a very unusual piece of structure, and the chain has not given reason to expect one.

The final kill condition is correct but not strong enough. Vagueness about "favorable triads" should kill the chain much earlier, not only at the end.

## Structural weaknesses of the whole chain

- The chain is still searching for a mechanism before fixing a theorem endpoint. That invites post hoc bottleneck selection.
- It overestimates how much evidential weight exact single-triad geometry should carry in a full-equation problem.
- It treats "different from Tao-style averaging" as evidence of relevance, when it may only show model sensitivity rather than theorem leverage.
- It has a built-in bias toward elegant algebraic stories. Helical-triad routes are especially vulnerable to mistaking symbolic sharpness for analytic control.
- The chain's positive and negative branches are not symmetric in plausibility. The negative branch is realistic; the positive branch depends on finding a rare effect that survives every known place where such effects usually die.

## Fair assessment

What is genuinely strong here is the discipline. The chain names the object, insists on one bottleneck, and explicitly forces summation/localization stress tests. That is good design.

What is weak is the underlying bet. The exact Leray-projected helical symbol has been studied enough that one should start from a presumption of obstruction, not a presumption that a hidden triad feature is waiting to unlock a theorem gap. This route should therefore be run mainly as an obstruction-extraction program. If it is evaluated as a positive regularity route, it is probably overrated.
