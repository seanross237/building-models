# Judgment - Chain 03

## Overall Assessment

The attack is mostly correct about where this branch is weak. The original chain already has two real strengths: it insists on the full stretching term `S omega . omega`, and it refuses to hide the pressure Hessian. But the attack correctly identifies that the chain remains too permissive before Step 4, checks novelty too late, and risks treating "find the right observable" as a live default rather than as a claim that must itself be screened early.

The right refinement is not to discard the chain. It is to tighten it into an obstruction-first tensor program with earlier prior-art screening, a quantitative admission rule, and a mandatory cross-representation check so the route cannot survive on coordinate preference alone.

## Major Critique Rulings

### Step 1 critique
**Verdict: Partially valid.**

The attack is right that a tensor ledger is bookkeeping, not leverage, and that bookkeeping the pressure debt is not the same as paying it. It is also right that a single frozen representation would risk coordinate bias. But fixing a small representation set remains good discipline if the chain requires any claimed mechanism to survive a second natural formulation rather than relying on just one.

### Step 2 critique
**Verdict: Valid.**

This is the softest part of the original chain. The attack is right that the candidate family sits close to known alignment literature and that "bounded observable" is far too weak a filter. The refinement needs an explicit quantitative gate: each admitted candidate must point to a concrete inequality or sign/coercivity statement on the full stretching term, not just geometric plausibility.

### Step 3 critique
**Verdict: Valid.**

The original Tao screen is underdefined, and the attack is right that labels like sign coupling or cancellation are not yet criteria. The refinement should demote Tao-sensitivity to secondary evidence after definability, invariance near degeneracy, and contradiction power are already established. The eigenvalue-degeneracy point is also decisive enough that it must be handled before any positive sales pitch.

### Step 4 critique
**Verdict: Valid.**

The attack correctly identifies this as the decisive step and the most likely collapse point. The original chain knows that the pressure debt may be the same order as the asset, but it lets earlier steps remain too qualitative. The refinement should force a full-mechanism control attempt earlier and require the first missing lemma to be named precisely once the real estimate is written down.

### Step 5 critique
**Verdict: Valid.**

The novelty check is indeed too late in the original version. In a literature-heavy area like stretching, alignment, and pressure-Hessian geometry, overlap must be audited before candidate selection hardens. The attack is also right that theorem-shaped propositions can disguise circularity unless the missing theorem is proved to be narrower than full regularity.

### Structural weakness 1 - privileged observable assumption
**Verdict: Partially valid.**

The attack is right that the original framing remains somewhat survivor-biased. Still, the chain already allowed a negative obstruction outcome, so it was not purely promotional. The refinement should replace "find the right observable" with "admit at most one or two tensor packages only if they survive early quantitative and overlap screening."

### Structural weakness 2 - anti-circularity machinery bites too late
**Verdict: Valid.**

This is correct. Terms like full stretching and nonlocal debt only become operational when they are attached to an explicit estimate. The refinement should therefore make anti-circularity bite at the admission stage, not only at the end.

### Structural weakness 3 - novelty screening is late
**Verdict: Valid.**

This is fully correct and easy to fix. Prior-art and internal-overlap auditing should happen before the chain promotes any candidate family.

### Structural weakness 4 - kill conditions are under-calibrated
**Verdict: Valid.**

The original kill conditions are honest but too interpretive. The refinement should replace broad phrases with more operational failure types: overlap collapse, representation dependence, degeneracy collapse, only-correlation-without-control, pressure debt same-order nonclosure, or missing lemma equivalent to the whole problem.

### Structural weakness 5 - bias toward a polished obstruction memo
**Verdict: Partially valid.**

The chain is indeed structurally well suited to producing a strong obstruction memo. That is not a defect for this mission, because a presentable obstruction is an intended success mode. The real correction is to state that bias openly and organize the chain so a negative result arrives quickly rather than after a long exploratory loop.

## What The Refined Chain Must Change

- Move prior-art and internal-overlap screening before observable selection.
- Replace the single-observable framing with a tensor-package framing:
  representation choice, observable, and exact debt split must travel together.
- Require a quantitative admission target tied to the full `S omega . omega` term.
- Freeze one primary representation only together with a mandatory alternate-representation robustness check.
- Demote Tao-sensitivity from main gate to secondary support.
- Force the chain to identify the first missing lemma as soon as the full control attempt is written down.

## Probability Assessment

Probability that the refined chain yields a presentable result: **0.68**.

Most likely presentable result: a sharp obstruction memo showing that tensor-level alignment or misalignment data remains informative but non-coercive once representation dependence, eigenvalue degeneracy, and full pressure-Hessian debt are charged honestly.

Lower-probability upside: a conditional tensor criterion or reduced proposition whose missing theorem is genuinely narrower than full regularity, but this is substantially less likely than the obstruction outcome.
