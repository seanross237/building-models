# Judgment on Selected Chain 01

## Overall verdict

The attacker is directionally right. The original chain is much stronger as a
falsification and obstruction-mapping branch than as a live positive program.
Its discipline is real: one host space, one critical object, one rigidity
quantity, one theorem-like endpoint. But the run-005 record already makes clear
that the branch does not currently have an audited starting point, and the
original wording understated that.

That said, the attack sometimes overstates terminality. A compactness-rigidity
chain can still be worth running if its honest purpose is to certify whether the
existing no-host verdict really stands under one frozen framework and, if not,
where the rigidity stage fails. The repair is therefore to narrow the branch,
not to discard it.

## Major critique assessments

### 1. Step 1 understates how close the branch already is to a no-host verdict

**Verdict: Valid.**

This is the strongest critique. The local run-005 context already points toward
`L^3` and `\dot H^{1/2}` carrying stacked architecture debts rather than one
clean missing theorem, while `BMO^{-1}` is not seriously admissible. The
original Step 1 read as if a positive branch remained equally live. That is not
well calibrated.

The only limit to the critique is that the existing audit is still framework
dependent. So the right correction is not "Step 1 is pointless," but "Step 1
must explicitly allow an early no-host verdict under one frozen framework."

### 2. Step 2 treats minimal-object formulation as bookkeeping when it is still foundational

**Verdict: Valid.**

The attacker is right that the original chain moved too quickly from "choose a
host space" to "state the minimal blowup object." In this problem the temporal
architecture, pressure language, symmetry handling, and compactness statement
are part of the burden, not clerical details.

The refined chain should therefore require that the delivered compactness and
almost-periodicity statement be named exactly, including lifespan and time
normalization, before the critical object counts as operational.

### 3. Step 3 is conceptually right but underpowered, especially on mission fit

**Verdict: Partially valid.**

The definability and limit-stability screen is correct, and the attacker is
right that without a frozen critical-element class this step can drift back to
generic rigidity slogans. The warning about prior-art graveyards is also fair.

What is overstated is the claim that Tao-sensitivity should outrank the
mathematical operability checks. For this mission, Tao-relevance matters, but a
rigidity quantity that is undefined, unstable, or purely cosmetic should die
before any averaged-model discussion can save or kill it. The right fix is a
two-stage gate: operability first, Tao-screen second.

### 4. Step 4 risks laundering the hard part of the problem into a “missing lemma”

**Verdict: Valid.**

This criticism lands. The original chain asked for a lemma narrower than full
regularity, but it did not define what counts as a genuine complexity drop or
how to treat coupled failures. That creates real room for one-missing-lemma
theater.

The refined chain should declare a progress currency explicitly and should
permit the honest conclusion that the obstruction is architectural and diffuse,
not falsely localized to a single elegant missing lemma.

### 5. Step 5 is strong on presentation discipline but too optimistic on the positive endpoint

**Verdict: Partially valid.**

The theorem-like endpoint is still a strength. It prevents vague research-memo
closure and forces the branch to say exactly what was earned. So the attack is
too strong if read as rejecting theorem form itself.

But it is correct that the positive endpoint was overstated. Given the run-005
state of evidence, the negative endpoint is much better calibrated than a
presentable positive theorem blueprint.

### 6. The chain is stale against the repo’s own evidence

**Verdict: Valid.**

This is a process-hygiene issue and the attacker is right to press it. Once the
current run already contains a Step-1-style audit trending to no-host, the
chain cannot keep selling itself as a broad high-upside positive route without
explaining why that audit was too strict or misframed.

The refined chain should explicitly absorb that prior audit as an input, not
silently start over.

### 7. The chain is still a Kenig-Merle template without a new coercive backbone

**Verdict: Partially valid.**

This warning is fair, but only partly disqualifying. The branch is indeed a
compactness-rigidity template, and the absence of a new NS-specific coercive
backbone sharply lowers the positive ceiling.

Still, as a kill-chain this is not fatal. One legitimate use of the branch is
to determine whether any coherent starting package exists before searching for a
new backbone elsewhere.

### 8. Tao is demoted too far for this mission

**Verdict: Partially valid.**

The attacker is right that mission fit requires an explicit account of what
averaging destroys and why that matters. The original chain did not push that
early or clearly enough.

But Tao cannot be the main admissibility test by itself. A route can fail
because it is mathematically non-operative long before Tao even becomes the
relevant discriminator. The correct repair is to make Tao a mandatory secondary
screen once a candidate is operational.

### 9. The chain is biased toward one-missing-lemma theater

**Verdict: Valid.**

This is substantially the same pressure point as the Step 4 critique, and it is
valid at the structural level as well. The original chain leaned too hard on
the narrative appeal of isolating one missing lemma when the more honest answer
may be that no coherent package exists.

### 10. Positive and negative standards are misbalanced

**Verdict: Valid.**

The attacker is right that the negative branch is realistically specified while
the positive branch is largely formal. That asymmetry should be stated rather
than hidden. The refined chain should present itself first as an obstruction
test with a narrow conditional positive branch.

### 11. Prior-art overlap is high and only partly controlled

**Verdict: Partially valid.**

The original chain did not ignore overlap, so the critique is too strong if it
implies total blindness. But overlap control was weaker than it needed to be,
especially because most candidate host spaces and rigidity templates sit very
close to already-familiar literature families.

The fix is to make overlap accounting part of admission rather than a trailing
note.

### 12. The chain has a hidden bias toward narratively neat conclusions

**Verdict: Partially valid.**

This is a real bias risk, especially toward "almost viable except for Lemma X."
But it is not yet a demonstrated failure of the chain. It becomes manageable if
the refined version accepts early no-host verdicts, stacked architectural
obstructions, and zero positive survivors without treating them as disappointing
or second-rate outcomes.

## Accepted refinements

The refined chain should incorporate the following changes:

1. Recast the branch as falsification-first: its main job is to test whether a
   coherent compactness-rigidity starting point exists at all.
2. Freeze one primary solution framework before comparing host spaces.
3. Re-run the host-space audit with separated burden columns:
   extraction, temporal/symmetry architecture, pressure or LEI compatibility,
   and overlap.
4. Accept an early no-host verdict if every candidate requires a stacked
   multi-theorem repair rather than one narrow missing ingredient.
5. Define the minimal object only after one host-framework pair survives, and
   force an explicit temporal architecture.
6. Screen rigidity quantities in two stages:
   mathematical operability first, Tao-sensitivity second.
7. Operationalize "genuine complexity drop" with a declared progress currency.
8. Allow the final obstruction to be stacked or diffuse if that is the honest
   shape of failure.
9. Keep theorem-like closure, but state openly that a negative obstruction memo
   is the most credible endpoint.

## Probability assessment

**Probability that the refined chain yields a presentable result: 0.76**

The likely presentable result is not a live Kenig-Merle-style forward program.
It is a sharper no-host or no-rigidity obstruction memo tied to one frozen
framework and to the run-005 evidence already on disk. That is still a strong
outcome for this branch, and it is materially more credible than the original
positive framing.
