# Judgment on Selected Chain 01

## Overall verdict

The attacker is mostly right. The selected chain has a serious core idea: kill decorative reformulations early and force any surviving variable package to make contact with a theorem-facing bottleneck. That is a real strength. But the original version is still too discretionary in three places that matter: it freezes the setup too sharply and too early, it does not operationalize the burden ledger tightly enough, and it under-polices prior-art overlap and endpoint insertion debt.

The right repair is not to abandon the chain's anti-repackaging posture. It is to make the screening protocol more reproducible. The refined version should keep the demand for exact equations and endpoint contact, while allowing a small comparison slate, requiring a typed burden ledger, and forcing quantified gain-versus-debt accounting before any positive promotion.

## Major critique assessments

### 1. The chain's key standards are too verbal rather than operational

**Verdict: Valid.**

Terms such as "estimate-level gain," "same burden currency," and "decorative commutator" are directionally right but not yet decision-ready. The refined chain needs a typed burden ledger, an explicit admissible gain notion, and a rule for when currencies are comparable and when they are not.

### 2. Step 1 freezes the setup too early and makes the search path-dependent

**Verdict: Valid.**

The attacker is right that choosing one endpoint and one burden before seeing any exact candidate behavior can manufacture false negatives. The repair is not to remove endpoint discipline, but to permit a tightly bounded endpoint window, for example one primary endpoint plus one backup, before collapsing to a single theorem-facing target.

### 3. The anti-conjugation rule is right in spirit but too coarse in the middle ground

**Verdict: Partially valid.**

The original rule correctly targets cosmetic repackaging. But the critique is right that many candidates sit between trivial conjugation and genuine mechanism change. The refined chain should classify candidates at least into equivalent redistribution, ambiguous redistribution, and potentially live mechanism shift, rather than forcing an immediate binary verdict.

### 4. Step 2 narrows to one candidate family too early, but exact-equation derivation is still necessary

**Verdict: Partially valid.**

The path-dependence criticism lands. A wide-funnel planning loop should not let first choice decide the outcome. But the attack overstates the problem if it is read as downgrading the value of exact derivation itself. Exactness is still a necessary gate; the repair is to derive full and localized equations for a small slate rather than for only one family.

### 5. Step 3 has the right instinct, but the gain gate is not typed tightly enough

**Verdict: Valid.**

This is one of the strongest critiques. Gains across derivative count, locality, cancellation, and admissibility are not automatically commensurable. The refined chain should require one exact bottleneck, one gain currency, one minimum meaningful margin, and one robustness test after legitimate inequalities and reconstruction.

### 6. Step 4 under-specifies endpoint insertion and does not quantify debt strongly enough

**Verdict: Valid.**

One displayed localization line is not enough. A candidate has to survive a mini proof skeleton containing localization, pressure handling, reconstruction, admissibility, and compactness debt. The refined chain should require a quantified or at least explicitly budgeted gain-versus-debt ledger at this stage.

### 7. Step 5's artifact standard is too heterogeneous to support promotion

**Verdict: Valid.**

A prototype inequality, a toy model, and a failed lemma are not equal forms of evidence. The refined chain should tier them. Promotion should require either a prototype insertion or a toy-model artifact with an explicit transfer map. A failed lemma is strong evidence for obstruction, not for promise.

### 8. Step 6 uses Tao comparison in a sensible secondary role, but still risks over-reading it

**Verdict: Partially valid.**

The original chain is correct not to let Tao comparison dominate the route. Still, the attacker is right that Tao sensitivity alone does not show proof relevance. The refined chain should treat Tao comparison as a discriminator, never as a positive proxy, and should tie each closing label to a concrete failed term, estimate, or transfer step.

### 9. Prior-art overlap is underweighted

**Verdict: Valid.**

This omission is serious. In an area full of exact-but-nonproductive reformulations, the chain needs a dedicated overlap audit at the level of mechanism, not just nomenclature. A candidate should have to state its nearest known analogue and the exact mathematical delta that keeps it live.

### 10. The SQG lesson is doing hidden agenda-setting work

**Verdict: Partially valid.**

As a branch-specific heuristic, the mechanism-change premise is acceptable. This chain does not need to represent the whole mission. But the critique is right that the heuristic should be stated as a motivating bet, not as a procedural truth. The refined chain should allow the honest outcome that no reformulation outperforms the inherited old-variable burden ledger.

## Accepted refinements

The refined chain should incorporate these changes:

1. Freeze one primary endpoint and one backup endpoint, not an artificially overcommitted single choice.
2. Replace vague burden language with a typed burden ledger and an explicit comparability rule.
3. Replace the binary anti-conjugation rule with a three-way classification that handles middle cases.
4. Audit a small candidate slate, not just one first-picked family.
5. Require exact and localized equation sheets, including pressure, cutoff, reconstruction, and admissibility debt.
6. Add a dedicated prior-art overlap gate before promotion.
7. Force one exact bottleneck, one gain currency, one minimum meaningful margin, and one robustness test for each live candidate.
8. Require a quantified or explicitly budgeted gain-versus-debt ledger inside the LEI / CKN insertion test.
9. Tier the artifact standard so that obstruction evidence and promotion evidence are not conflated.
10. Keep Tao comparison secondary and tie every final label to a concrete mathematical obstruction or insertion success.

## Probability assessment

**Probability that the refined chain yields a presentable result: 0.77**

That probability is driven mostly by the chance of a clean obstruction memo or a narrow "no reformulation beats the inherited ledger" conclusion. A positive route remains possible, but only if one candidate survives both the quantified burden ledger and a non-cosmetic endpoint insertion test.
