# Chain 04 - Adapted-Package Geometric Vorticity Route From Local Beltrami Deficit To Criterion Scale

## Central Premise

The old neutral-Eulerian geometry package is already weak, so geometry is live only if the package change is stated openly and audited honestly. This chain asks whether one adapted localization package can turn local Beltrami deficit, direction coherence, or tube persistence into a theorem-facing geometric criterion or full-stretching reduction without hiding the gain inside the package itself.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This is the only geometry-first branch, and unlike the killed Eulerian route it treats the package change itself as part of the burden. It is not a commutator search, not a packet-symbol audit, not a material observable route, and not a global compactness architecture.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Declare the package change and fix the comparator

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one adapted package only: tube-following, Lagrangian high-vorticity tracking, or another equally explicit localization.
- Action: name the killed neutral-Eulerian package as the mandatory comparator.
- Action: state what exact burden the package change is supposed to improve: persistence, criterion compatibility, or full-stretching control.
- Expected output: a package-change memo with one comparator and one claimed advantage.
- Kill condition: if the new package cannot say what it buys beyond hidden normalization or relabeling, stop and write a package-theater obstruction.

### Step 2 - Freeze one geometric observable family and one criterion endpoint

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: choose one promoted observable family only: local Beltrami deficit, direction coherence, Beltrami-plus-concentration, or direction-coherence-plus-persistence.
- Action: choose one endpoint family, preferably a Constantin-Fefferman-type geometric criterion or one fixed full-stretching target.
- Action: define the exact scenario class in advance: vortex-tube concentration, sheet-to-filament transition, or another comparably sharp class.
- Expected output: an observable-and-endpoint table.
- Kill condition: if the branch needs several loosely related observables to sound plausible, stop and write an under-specified geometry obstruction.

### Step 3 - Run the persistence and admissibility screen under the new package

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: ask what actually persists under the adapted package and what derivative or tracking debt is introduced.
- Action: charge hidden `nabla xi` debt, threshold sensitivity, boundary leakage, and any tube-identification ambiguity immediately.
- Action: reject observables that only look strong after retrofitting the geometry to the observed structure.
- Expected output: a persistence ledger with one promoted survivor at most.
- Kill condition: if persistence requires hidden relocalization, implicit axis fitting, or stronger derivative control than the route admits, stop and write a persistence obstruction.

### Step 4 - Test the promoted observable on the full stretching mechanism

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: freeze one stretching representation and test whether the observable acts on the full mechanism rather than only on local Lamb-vector depletion or a descriptive fragment.
- Action: distinguish near-field control, exterior/nonlocal remainder, and localization-evolution debt.
- Expected output: one stretching-control memo.
- Kill condition: if the observable controls only local depletion while the full stretching burden remains free, stop and write a full-stretching obstruction.

### Step 5 - Bridge to criterion scale or contradiction scale

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: write one explicit criterion-scale proposition or contradiction line using the promoted observable and the chosen package.
- Action: compare it against the neutral-Eulerian benchmark and the closest Constantin-Fefferman-type prior art.
- Expected output: a theorem-facing bridge memo.
- Kill condition: if the gain never rises above descriptive geometry or prior-art direction regularity, stop and write a prior-art-collapse obstruction.

### Step 6 - State the narrowest earned claim

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: if positive, write one conditional route naming the package, observable, scenario class, endpoint, and first narrower missing lemma.
- Action: if negative, classify failure as package theater, persistence collapse, hidden derivative debt, full-stretching failure, or prior-art collapse.
- Expected output: a final-comparison-ready geometry route or obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the final claim depends on silently keeping the geometry adapted at every step without charging that cost, downgrade to obstruction.
