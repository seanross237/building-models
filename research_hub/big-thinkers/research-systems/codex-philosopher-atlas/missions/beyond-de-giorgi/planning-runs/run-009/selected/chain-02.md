# Chain 03 - Deterministic Material Stretching Route With Viscous-Defect And LEI Admission Gates

## Central Premise

The stochastic transport corridor failed on solution-class admission, but that does not kill transport-side structure altogether. This chain is live only if one deterministic material observable can be defined on an admissible theorem floor and yields a mechanism-facing gain on cumulative stretching or local concentration without importing smooth-flow or filtration machinery.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This is the only deterministic transport-first branch. It works with deformation, cumulative positive strain, or related material observables rather than symbolic packet geometry, scalarization, explicit geometric criteria, or compactness contradiction.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze one material observable, one solution framework, and one endpoint

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one material observable only, such as cumulative positive strain, deformation growth, or another comparably specific quantity.
- Action: state exactly which solution framework admits that observable: inherited suitable weak / LEI floor if possible, otherwise a stronger class with an explicit bridge burden.
- Action: freeze one theorem-facing endpoint: local concentration exclusion by default, continuation only if the observable already behaves like norm control.
- Expected output: a scope-lock memo with one observable, one framework, and one endpoint.
- Kill condition: if the observable is not honestly definable on an admissible theorem floor, stop and write a solution-class setup obstruction.

### Step 2 - Derive the exact evolution and isolate the viscous defect

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: write the exact transport or deformation identity, including the viscous defect and nonlocal strain terms.
- Action: distinguish what is pathwise, what is only formal, and what already assumes stronger regularity than the endpoint allows.
- Expected output: an exact-evolution sheet with a defect ledger.
- Kill condition: if the observable is exact only for smooth flow maps far above the target singular regime, stop and write a definability obstruction.

### Step 3 - Run the coercivity gate before localization

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: ask whether the observable yields one-sided control, an exclusion principle, or a monotonicity surrogate on one named bad term.
- Action: compare that gain directly to a frozen baseline burden on the inherited endpoint ledger.
- Expected output: at most one promoted material gain plus one failure mode table.
- Kill condition: if the observable is exact but analytically non-coercive, stop and write a transport-without-leverage obstruction.

### Step 4 - Localize on the inherited theorem ledger and pay all debt early

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: insert the promoted gain into one LEI / CKN concentration or stretching line.
- Action: charge cutoff, pressure, nonlocal strain freedom, and any temporal-window debt before claiming progress.
- Action: reject gains that control only a trajectory-level surrogate with no endpoint insertion.
- Expected output: a localization-and-insertion memo.
- Kill condition: if localization or nonlocal strain freedom erases the gain, stop and write a localization or nonlocal-freedom obstruction.

### Step 5 - Demand one hard artifact and only then check Tao sensitivity

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: produce one hard artifact: a prototype estimate, toy deformation scenario, or failed lemma identifying the precise obstruction.
- Action: ask whether the surviving material feature is plausibly destroyed by Tao-style averaging only after the artifact exists.
- Expected output: an evidence packet plus a Tao-comparator note.
- Kill condition: if the branch is relying on transport rhetoric without an artifact, stop and write a documentary obstruction.

### Step 6 - State the narrowest earned claim

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: if positive, write one conditional statement with the exact observable, framework, endpoint, insertion point, and first narrower missing lemma.
- Action: if negative, classify failure as solution-class mismatch, viscous-defect collapse, non-coercivity, nonlocal-strain freedom, or endpoint-transfer failure.
- Expected output: a final-comparison-ready route or obstruction.
- Kill condition: if the remaining missing lemma is just full regularity in renamed transport language, downgrade to obstruction immediately.
