# Chain 03 - Critical Element / Concentration-Compactness with NS-Specific Rigidity Screen

## Central Premise

If every local mechanism stalls, the right object may be a minimal blowup profile rather than a sharper estimate. This chain asks whether current NS profile-decomposition machinery can isolate a critical element in a workable phase space and whether any rigidity mechanism survives Tao's averaged-model obstruction.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a concrete critical-element program for NS with one viable rigidity lever that is not obviously Tao-preserved; or
- an obstruction map explaining exactly why the supercritical gap or the absence of an NS-specific rigidity mechanism still blocks this route.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This is the only chain that starts from the blowup hypothesis and works globally through compactness, symmetry reduction, and rigidity. It is not a local estimate branch, not a geometry-of-tubes branch, and not a tensor-identity branch.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Choose a candidate critical phase space and front-load the Tao screen

- Depends on: none.
- Action: map the existing NS profile-decomposition and critical-space literature, focusing on spaces such as `L^3`, `dot H^{1/2}`, `BMO^{-1}`, or other realistic candidates where compactness modulo symmetries might be available.
- Action: for each candidate space, ask immediately whether there is any plausible rigidity ingredient that uses exact NS structure beyond energy plus harmonic analysis.
- Expected output: a phase-space memo ranking candidate spaces by extraction feasibility and naming the first plausible NS-specific rigidity handle in each.
- Kill condition: if no phase space offers both a realistic extraction package and a candidate rigidity lever that clears the Tao pre-screen, terminate with a compactness obstruction memo.

### Step 2 - Build the minimal-counterexample package

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: formulate the hypothetical minimal blowup object, normalize the symmetries, and record exactly what compactness, almost-periodicity, or concentration statements would be needed.
- Expected output: a critical-element dossier spelling out assumptions, invariances, normalization, and the precise theorem stack already available versus missing.
- Kill condition: if the minimal element cannot even be formulated cleanly in the chosen space, stop and record the failure precisely.

### Step 3 - Stress-test one rigidity route against Tao's barrier

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: choose one route such as backward uniqueness, Liouville exclusion, self-similar rigidity, or a geometric concentration law, and test whether it uses more than Tao-preserved structure.
- Expected output: a route memo listing the required lemmas, known precedents, and the first missing ingredient that is genuinely NS-specific.
- Kill condition: if every available rigidity route collapses to energy, compactness, and harmonic analysis alone, stop and write that as the decisive obstruction.

### Step 4 - Convert a surviving route into a concrete contradiction program

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: if one rigidity route survives, organize it into a stepwise contradiction program from extracted critical element to exclusion, with explicit dependence on the chosen phase space.
- Expected output: a contradiction blueprint naming the critical element, the rigidity mechanism, the proof bottleneck, and the next execution target.
- Kill condition: if the surviving route still requires an unearned theorem whose statement is no sharper than the original regularity problem, narrow the claim to an obstruction map and stop.

### Step 5 - Close with a calibrated landscape claim

- Depends on: Step 4, or directly on Step 3 if the chain dies there.
- Action: state either the strongest viable critical-element path or the sharpest currently supportable reason that concentration-compactness does not yet escape Tao's obstruction.
- Expected output: either a concrete critical-element program with a named rigidity lever or a polished negative result mapping the exact compactness and rigidity failures.
- Kill condition: if the final statement blurs together multiple phase spaces or rigidity routes that were not actually tested, narrow it until each claim is anchored.
