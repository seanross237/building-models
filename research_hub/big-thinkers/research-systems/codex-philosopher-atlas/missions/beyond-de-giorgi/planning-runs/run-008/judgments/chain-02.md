# Judgment on Chain 02 - SQG-Blueprint Commutator And Scalarization Route

## Overall judgment

The attack is mostly right. The original chain was serious in the right way: it
looked for leverage from an exact reformulation rather than another prettier
estimate in the original variables, and it allowed obstruction as an honest
output. But it was still too loose where this branch is most exposed to false
progress: candidate admission, anti-repackaging discipline, early Tao
calibration, and the transfer from a formal commutator to an endpoint-facing
gain once pressure and vector reconstruction re-enter.

The fair refinement is to keep the branch alive only as a harsh
reformulation-screening route. The default expectation should be obstruction
unless one concrete unknown survives an anti-conjugation audit, a preliminary
Tao-sensitivity screen, full pressure/vector re-entry, and a benchmarked
endpoint insertion.

## Major critique rulings

### 1. Step 1 is underconstrained and invites cherry-picking among candidate unknowns

**Ruling: Valid**

This critique lands. The candidate menu was too broad, the LEI-rewrite filter
was too weak, and the endpoint categories were too coarse to stop later
goalpost shifting. The refined chain should freeze:

- one exact endpoint;
- one named baseline criterion to beat or sharpen; and
- one exact gain type.

It should also reject any candidate whose exact equation is just the original
nonlinearity conjugated by a projection, inverse operator, or nonlocal change
of variables.

### 2. Step 2 understates how likely the commutator package is to collapse, especially through pressure and undecorative leftovers

**Ruling: Valid**

The attack is right that the original chain treated the nonlinear decomposition
too optimistically. In this branch, a displayed commutator is irrelevant unless
it is leading rather than decorative. Pressure also should not be presumed to
be a remainder; in many scalarized or projected packages it is exactly where
the full quadratic coupling returns.

One nuance: the remainder ledger in the original chain was good discipline and
should be preserved. The fix is not to abandon the decomposition, but to demand
an earlier plausibility gate and a sharper fatality standard for leftovers.

### 3. Step 3 applies Tao's screen too late and with too weak an operational test

**Ruling: Valid**

This is correct. It is not enough to note that some formula changes under Tao's
averaging. The refined chain has to identify one exact algebraic feature,
symmetry, or identity and ask whether the actual candidate gain disappears when
that feature is averaged away. That screening should begin before the branch
spends much time deriving a full reformulated equation.

### 4. Step 4 is right to demand endpoint insertion, but the success bar is too weak and too vague

**Ruling: Valid**

The attack is correct that an explicit line is necessary but not sufficient.
The insertion must close in the already available regularity class and must be
benchmarked against a named baseline burden in a named norm or scale.

The critique is especially strong for localized endpoints: cutoff commutators,
pressure localization, and transfer back to the original vector field are not
cleanup. They are often the whole obstruction.

### 5. Step 5 is too permissive about what counts as a genuinely narrower remaining gap

**Ruling: Valid**

Agreed. "Narrower than arbitrary finite-energy regularity" is too weak. The
remaining missing lemma must be strictly narrower than the original burden in
scope, norm strength, or structural content. A lemma that effectively asks for
control of the full vectorial nonlinearity after reconstruction should count as
failure immediately.

The attack is also right that prior-art and disguised-burden screening should
not be deferred this late.

### 6. The whole chain assumes a live candidate class before earning it

**Ruling: Valid**

This is a fair structural criticism. The original central premise leaned too
hard on the idea that an SQG-like reformulation is a live possibility. For this
mission, the prior should be the opposite: scalarization or extension is
presumed analytically inert until a concrete exact unknown survives immediate
anti-conjugation and endpoint-transfer screening.

### 7. The branch needs a stronger anti-repackaging filter much earlier

**Ruling: Valid**

Also correct. Reformulation-first branches are especially vulnerable to elegant
relabeling. The refined chain should front-load a hard exclusion test: if the
new variable merely redistributes the same quadratic singular-integral burden,
the branch should terminate before full derivation.

### 8. The original wording was biased toward a positive SQG-style narrative

**Ruling: Partially valid**

The attack is right that terms like "SQG-like package" and "commutator gain"
tilt the branch rhetorically toward success. But the original chain was not
pure salesmanship. It did include honest kill conditions and allowed a negative
obstruction memo.

So the valid fix is tonal and procedural, not total rejection: rewrite the
branch so that obstruction is the default output unless a concrete survivor is
earned.

## Refined judgment

Chain 02 survives only as an obstruction-first reformulation audit.
Reformulation-specific leverage should count for nothing unless all of the
following happen early:

- one exact endpoint, baseline criterion, and gain type are frozen at the start;
- one candidate unknown survives definability and anti-conjugation screening;
- a preliminary Tao screen identifies an exact NS-specific feature whose loss
  destroys the candidate gain rather than merely changing the presentation;
- the full equation is written with pressure re-entry, vector reconstruction,
  and other quadratic leftovers treated as default fatal mechanisms rather than
  secondary debts; and
- one benchmarked endpoint-facing line survives with those debts included.

If any of those fail, the correct output is a calibrated obstruction memo.

## Probability assessment

Probability that the refined chain yields a presentable result: **0.74**.

Breakdown:

- Probability of a presentable negative result, most likely a sharp
  anti-repackaging or endpoint-transfer obstruction: **0.62**
- Probability of a presentable positive conditional route with a genuinely
  narrower missing lemma: **0.12**

Reasoning: the branch is worth keeping because it targets one of the few
conceptually distinct escape routes, but the attack is persuasive that most
concrete candidates will collapse quickly once Tao-sensitivity, pressure
re-entry, and endpoint transfer are made early admissibility tests.
