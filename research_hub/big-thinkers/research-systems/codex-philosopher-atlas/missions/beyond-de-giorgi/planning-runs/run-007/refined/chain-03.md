# Refined Chain 03 - Coupled Energy-Helicity Route With Early Coercivity Gate

## Central Premise

This branch is live only if full 3D energy-helicity transport yields more than exact cascade bookkeeping. Before investing in endpoint packaging, the chain must exhibit one candidate coupled quantity or transfer constraint that is:

- one-sided or coercive enough to matter;
- stable under mixed-helicity restoration and low-helicity regions;
- compatible with the localization required by the endpoint;
- and not merely a decomposition-specific restatement of known helical folklore.

If no such quantity survives, the correct output is an obstruction memo saying that the coupled invariant structure is exact but non-coercive for theorem-facing purposes.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- one conditional proposition tying a specific coupled energy-helicity quantity or forbidden transfer pattern to one named continuation, concentration-exclusion, or stretching/enstrophy bottleneck; or
- one calibrated obstruction memo showing that the route fails by non-coercivity, mixed-helicity restoration, low-helicity escape, localization collapse, prior-art collapse, or a stacked combination of these.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This version keeps the original ambition of testing full-equation invariant geometry, but it no longer assumes that joint flux language is itself progress. A candidate survives only if it earns theorem relevance under full-equation and localization pressure.

## Scope Lock

Before substantive execution begins, fix all of the following:

- one provisional endpoint family only at the level of burden type:
  continuation criterion,
  concentration exclusion,
  or stretching/enstrophy bottleneck;
- one invariant package to audit first:
  coarse-grained local balances,
  dyadic coupled fluxes,
  or one other sharply defined ledger;
- one explicit admission rule:
  no candidate is promoted unless it names one one-sided gain on one named bad term or bad transfer pattern;
- one novelty rule:
  favorable same-helicity or polarized sectors do not count unless they carry a quantitatively relevant portion of the full burden near the chosen endpoint family;
- one default hypothesis:
  exact energy-helicity coupling is non-coercive unless shown otherwise.

If these items cannot be fixed concretely, stop and write a setup obstruction.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Audit the invariant package before freezing the endpoint

- Depends on: none.
- Action: write the exact energy and helicity transfer identities in the chosen package, and separate exact structure from mere bookkeeping choices.
- Action: define one exact Tao-sensitive feature in advance, such as a coupling of scale and chirality or a sign-sensitive transfer constraint that averaging should destroy.
- Action: screen immediately for prior-art overlap with Waleffe-type triad analysis, helical decimation, shell-model intuition, and same-helicity favorable-sector lore.
- Expected output: an audit memo listing exact structure, Tao-sensitive claim, and prior-art risk.
- Kill condition: if the package already reduces to known folklore, a decomposition-dependent sign ledger, or generic cascade language, stop and write an overlap or bookkeeping obstruction.

### Step 2 - Run the coercivity gate

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: ask whether the chosen coupled quantity yields any one-sided, endpoint-relevant gain before absolute values, generic inequalities, or sign averaging erase it.
- Action: require that gain to point to one named bad term or bad transfer pattern:
  stretching production,
  enstrophy growth,
  concentration of active scales,
  or another precisely stated bottleneck.
- Action: reject any candidate whose content disappears once helicity is compared generically to energy and enstrophy.
- Expected output: at most two candidate gains, each tied to one named bottleneck.
- Kill condition: if no one-sided or exclusionary gain survives this gate, stop and write a non-coercivity obstruction.

### Step 3 - Front-load the full-equation debts

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: test the surviving candidate against mixed-helicity interactions, low-helicity regions, viscosity, and pressure effects as first-order constraints rather than late corrections.
- Action: state explicitly whether the candidate controls a non-negligible share of the full nonlinear burden or only a thin favorable sector.
- Action: reject any mechanism that works only in decimated, filtered, or strongly polarized regimes without a theorem-relevant burden estimate.
- Expected output: a full-equation viability ledger.
- Kill condition: if mixed-helicity restoration, low-helicity escape, or viscosity already destroys the gain, stop and write the obstruction.

### Step 4 - Test localization before promoting the mechanism

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: insert the localization machinery required by the endpoint:
  cutoffs,
  local flux commutators,
  pressure terms,
  and any coarse-graining losses.
- Action: check whether the candidate quantity remains one-sided or exclusionary after localization, rather than merely exact at the global or formal level.
- Action: reject candidates whose gain survives only in unlocalized or poorly localized form.
- Expected output: a localization ledger with a yes/no judgment on stability.
- Kill condition: if localization erases the gain or turns it into energy-only control, stop and write a localization obstruction.

### Step 5 - Freeze one endpoint and write the mechanism dossier

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: choose one endpoint only after a candidate survives the previous gates.
- Action: write one mechanism dossier naming:
  the exact coupled quantity,
  the exact bad term or bad pattern it constrains,
  the portion of the nonlinear burden it captures,
  and the first lemma needed to connect that gain to the endpoint.
- Action: classify the result as:
  real leverage,
  partial but endpoint-irrelevant leverage,
  or exact but non-coercive structure.
- Expected output: one promoted mechanism dossier or a typed obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the endpoint bridge is still only descriptive cascade language, stop and write a no-bridge obstruction.

### Step 6 - State the narrowest earned claim

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: if positive, write one proposition-scale conditional statement with:
  the endpoint,
  the coupled quantity,
  the exact gain,
  the localization assumptions,
  and the first genuinely narrower missing lemma.
- Action: if negative, write one obstruction memo with the narrowest honest labels:
  non-coercivity,
  mixed-helicity restoration,
  low-helicity escape,
  localization collapse,
  prior-art collapse,
  or a stacked combination.
- Action: reject any final claim that merely says helicity is interesting, or that assumes a persistently polarized regime strong enough to encode the hard case away.
- Expected output: a final-comparison-ready positive route or a sharp no-go.
- Kill condition: if the final claim hides the main difficulty inside the hypothesis or conclusion language, narrow it further until the debt is explicit.

## Success Standard

This branch succeeds only if it does one of two things:

- isolates one coupled energy-helicity mechanism that is one-sided enough, localizable enough, and endpoint-relevant enough to earn a serious conditional proposition; or
- shows cleanly that the exact coupled invariant geometry is too non-coercive or too fragile under full-equation restoration and localization to improve on known folklore.
