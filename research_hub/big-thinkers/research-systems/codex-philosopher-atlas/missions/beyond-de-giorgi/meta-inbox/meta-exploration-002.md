# Meta-Learning: Step 1, Exploration 002 (Tao Gate)

## What Worked

- A capped candidate list made the Tao screen decisive instead of turning into a broad literature survey.
- Forcing every candidate to act on the exact bad coefficient `C_far` prevented identity-level repackagings from surviving by rhetoric alone.
- Reusing the mission's own preserved-structure list was enough to screen the branch without spending budget on reconstructing Tao 2016 from scratch.

## What Could Be Improved

- The Tao gate should be phrased even more mechanically in future steps: for each candidate, ask "what estimate changes?" and "what term becomes smaller?".
- If the branch depends on a nonlocal moment problem, say that in the goal. It makes local geometry claims easier to kill quickly.

## Generalizable Lesson

For Tao-filter steps, demand an estimate-level lever on the exact live obstruction. If a candidate cannot name the quantity it shrinks, treat it as dead even if it is genuinely NS-specific in some broader sense.
