# Exploration 002 Goal

## Objective

Write the exact invariant ledger for the chosen package as far as the local
repository supports it, and make the Tao screen operational with one passing
and one failing example.

This exploration is not allowed to sell exact identities as leverage. Its job
is to separate exact structure from bookkeeping, identify the theorem-facing
bad transfer pattern, and decide whether a concrete NS-sensitive feature
survives once the Tao screen is stated precisely.

## Required Questions

Answer all of the following from local repository materials:

1. What are the exact energy and helicity transfer identities in the frozen
   package, stated as sharply as the on-disk packet permits?
2. Which parts of that ledger are exact NS structure, and which are only
   decomposition or bookkeeping choices?
3. What named bad term or bad transfer pattern is the most honest target for
   the frozen endpoint family?
4. What exactly counts as "destroyed by averaging" at the comparison level the
   repository supports?
5. What is one clear passing Tao-calibration example?
6. What is one clear failing Tao-calibration example?
7. Are those two examples enough to make the Tao screen operational rather
   than rhetorical for this chain?

## Working Hypotheses To Test, Not Assume

- The strongest exact ledger available locally is dyadic/helical and derives
  from the exact-NS packet material rather than from a pre-existing
  beyond-De-Giorgi identity memo.
- The likely Tao-sensitive feature is exact role-labeled helical packet
  coupling with mandatory conjugate completion, not generic energy or helicity
  flux language.
- A passing case should involve exact-NS spectator/rigidity constraints that
  prevent Tao-style independent pump/seed/rotor tuning.
- A failing case should be generic same-helicity or shell-model folklore that
  survives as packaging without theorem-facing NS specificity.
- The ledger may remain exact but still non-coercive; if so, the report should
  say that directly.

Confirm, refine, or reject these hypotheses from source-backed evidence.

## Success Criteria

- Produce one invariant memo with sections:
  `exact ledger`,
  `bookkeeping choices`,
  `named bad transfer pattern`,
  `Tao screen`,
  `pass example`,
  `fail example`.
- Label major claims `[VERIFIED]`, `[INFERRED]`, or `[PROPOSED]`.
- State explicitly whether the ledger contains a real NS-sensitive feature or
  collapses to exact-but-noncoercive bookkeeping.

## Failure Criteria

- The report confuses a decomposition choice with an exact identity.
- The Tao screen depends on undefined words like `averaging destroys geometry`
  without a concrete comparison statement.
- The report gives pass/fail examples that do not actually separate NS-specific
  structure from generic reduced-model packaging.

If failure occurs, say so directly and recommend
`calibration obstruction`.
