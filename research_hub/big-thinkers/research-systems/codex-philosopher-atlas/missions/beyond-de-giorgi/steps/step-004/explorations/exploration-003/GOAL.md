# Exploration 003 Goal — Fragility-Screen Audit And Kill Memo

## Objective

Using the fixed Step-3 package and the outcomes of Explorations 001 and 002,
audit the fragility-screen candidates:

- `local Beltrami / alignment`
- `Beltrami deficit + concentration`
- `Beltrami deficit + anisotropy`

This exploration is not trying to rescue them. It must confirm whether they
remain fragility screens even after dynamic considerations are added, and use
them to state the earliest honest branch-kill condition if the primary hybrid
fails.

For each fragility-screen candidate, record:

- the best available transport identity or propagation heuristic, if any;
- the main diffusion loss;
- the main localization-evolution, commutator, or hidden-normalization burden;
- whether any apparent persistence story depends on hidden tube adaptation,
  stronger derivative control, or threshold tuning;
- whether its Tao discriminator survives dynamically or collapses into static
  alignment or geometry language;
- and the first dynamic failure mode.

The report must end with a branch-kill memo that says whether the primary
hybrid is still honest enough to carry into Chain Step 4, or whether the chain
should stop here because every surviving candidate is dynamically weak or
static-only.

## Success Criteria

- Produces an explicit fragility memo, not just a recap of Step 2.
- Uses `[VERIFIED]`, `[INFERRED]`, and `[PROPOSED]` labels consistently.
- Names exact source files for every major dynamic or kill-condition claim.
- Makes hidden-normalization and stronger-derivative-control risks explicit.
- Ends with:
  - a final rating for each fragility-screen candidate
  - the earliest honest branch-kill condition
  - and a recommendation:
    continue narrowly to Step 4, or invalidate/replan now

## Failure Criteria

- Re-promotes a Beltrami-family quantity as a live primary route.
- Treats apparent local alignment persistence as enough without addressing the
  full stretching target.
- Leaves the kill condition implicit.
- Ignores the results of Explorations 001 and 002.
