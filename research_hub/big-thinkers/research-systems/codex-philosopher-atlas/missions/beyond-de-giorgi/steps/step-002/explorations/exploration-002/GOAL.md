# Exploration 002 Goal — Tao-Screen Table for Geometry Ingredients

## Objective

Operationalize the Tao screen for the geometry route in a way later steps can apply mechanically.

Classify the route's candidate ingredients into the chain's three buckets:

- transport or propagation structure
- exact algebraic structure
- multiscale coherence or concentration structure

For each bucket and for each concrete candidate family named locally

- local Beltrami deficit or alignment
- vorticity-direction coherence
- tube coherence or persistence
- hybrid geometric candidates explicitly suggested by the local chain materials

state:

- what Tao-style averaged or toy models plausibly preserve
- what they plausibly destroy
- what estimate-level target the ingredient would need to move
- and whether the candidate is `live`, `weak but informative`, or `collapsed to Tao-robust/static geometry`

## Success Criteria

- Produces a usable Tao-screen table, not just prose.
- Distinguishes static singular-integral geometry from genuinely NS-specific transport/stretching/coherence dynamics.
- Reuses the step-001 lesson mechanically:
  ask what estimate changes and what term becomes smaller.
- Gives a short reason for every verdict.
- Ends with a clear recommendation on whether Step 2 should proceed and, if so, which ingredients survive.

## Failure Criteria

- Keeps a candidate alive without naming a target interaction or controlled term.
- Treats "looks NS-specific" as sufficient without saying what Tao-style averaging destroys.
- Collapses transport, algebraic, and coherence claims into one undifferentiated geometry label.
- Ignores the chain's requirement that the eventual target is the full stretching mechanism `S omega . omega`.
