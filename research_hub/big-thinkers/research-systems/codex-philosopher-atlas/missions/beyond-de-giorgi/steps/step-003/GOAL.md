# Step 3 Goal: Chain Step 2 Fix Scenario Class And Localization Before Defining Observables

## Mission Context

**Mission:** Beyond De Giorgi — What Structural Property Could Break the NS
Regularity Barrier?

**Active chain:** `Winning Chain - Calibrated Geometry Route with Explicit Tao
Discriminator and Narrow Claim Discipline`

**Why this step exists now:** `step-002` completed Chain Step 1 and kept the
geometry branch alive only in a narrowed form. The benchmark and Tao screen
eliminated broad standalone geometry claims and left one primary survivor:
`direction coherence + tube persistence`. The branch should not move into
dynamic screening or kernel-level stretching analysis until it first fixes the
scenario class and localization protocol that this hybrid would actually live
on.

## This Step Covers

This strategizer execution covers **Chain Step 2 only**:

- choose one or two concrete singular-scenario classes for the narrowed
  geometry branch;
- fix one explicit localization protocol before defining observables;
- define a bounded observable family only after those choices are fixed;
- and state which candidates remain stable enough to justify the later dynamic
  plausibility screen.

Do **not** treat this step as permission to run Chain Step 3 or Chain Step 4.
Do not do kernel-level `S omega . omega` control testing yet. Do not promote a
route because it sounds geometric before the scenario/localization package is
fully specified.

## Required Deliverables

Produce all of the following inside `RESULTS.md`:

1. **Scenario-class decision memo**
   - choose one primary scenario class and optionally one secondary comparator
     scenario;
   - candidates may include filament or tube concentration, sheet or pancake
     concentration, or another concretely justified singular-scenario class
     supported by the local record;
   - explain why the chosen scenario is the right test bed after `step-002`,
     especially for the primary survivor `direction coherence + tube
     persistence`;
   - state one explicit failure mode for each chosen scenario.

2. **Localization protocol specification**
   - fix:
     - the intensity threshold;
     - the spatial scale;
     - the time window;
     - and whether localization is Eulerian, Lagrangian, or tube-adapted;
   - explain why this protocol is the right fit for the chosen scenario and
     why it does not quietly bake in the hoped-for conclusion;
   - include a short stability check under reasonable perturbations of the
     localization choices.

3. **Bounded observable table**
   - define observables only after the scenario and localization choices are
     fixed;
   - include:
     - primary candidate: `direction coherence + tube persistence`
     - secondary comparators: `vorticity-direction coherence`,
       `tube coherence / persistence`
     - fragility screens only: `local Beltrami / alignment`,
       `Beltrami deficit + concentration`,
       `Beltrami deficit + anisotropy`
   - for each item, record:
     - scaling or normalization status;
     - what localization data it depends on;
     - what scenario it is meant to address;
     - its intended leverage point on the future full-stretching target;
     - its Tao discriminator;
     - and its first obvious instability or hidden-normalization risk.

4. **Step-3 readiness recommendation**
   - say whether the branch should advance to the dynamic plausibility screen;
   - identify which candidates are still primary, secondary, or effectively
     dead after the scenario/localization choices;
   - if the primary hybrid collapses under honest formulation, recommend branch
     invalidation or replanning rather than drifting forward.

## Exploration Tasks

Use **2-4 explorations total** unless a kill condition fires early.

### Exploration A: Choose and justify the scenario class

Tasks:
- compare the most plausible scenario classes still compatible with the
  narrowed branch;
- decide which scenario gives the fairest test of
  `direction coherence + tube persistence`;
- record why rejected scenarios were inferior, overfitted, or too weakly tied
  to the survivor set.

Success standard:
- the chosen scenario class is specific enough to constrain later transport and
  stretching questions rather than serving as decorative imagery.

### Exploration B: Fix the localization protocol and stress-test it

Tasks:
- specify the threshold, scale, time window, and localization type;
- test whether the protocol is robust under reasonable alternative choices or
  whether its apparent strength comes from hidden normalization;
- identify where the protocol favors the primary hybrid and where it exposes
  fragility.

Success standard:
- the localization protocol is explicit, auditable, and not secretly chosen to
  rescue the branch.

### Exploration C: Define the bounded observable table

Only run this after A and B are fixed.

Tasks:
- define the primary hybrid, the secondary comparators, and the Beltrami-based
  fragility screens inside the chosen scenario/localization package;
- record each item's scaling posture, data requirements, intended leverage
  point, and Tao discriminator;
- rank which candidates remain honest inputs for Chain Step 3.

Success standard:
- later steps inherit a bounded, explicit candidate table rather than vague
  geometry language.

## Kill Conditions

Trigger an early negative conclusion for this step if any of the following
happens:

- no reasonable scenario class keeps the primary hybrid
  `direction coherence + tube persistence` concrete enough to test without
  collapsing back into descriptive tube language;
- the apparent strength of the primary hybrid or any promoted comparator comes
  mainly from hidden normalization choices, smuggled stronger derivative
  assumptions, or localization choices tailored to manufacture coherence;
- after fixing scenario and localization, only prior-art comparator routes or
  static descriptors remain, with no primary candidate worth carrying into the
  dynamic plausibility screen.

If a kill condition fires, count that as a successful obstruction result for
this step and recommend branch invalidation or replanning rather than continuing
blindly.
