# Step 12 Goal: Chain Step 1 Fix The Mechanism And Prior-Art Burden Before Formalism Expansion

## Mission Context

**Mission:** Beyond De Giorgi — What Structural Property Could Break the NS
Regularity Barrier?

**Active chain:** `Canonical Winning Chain - Stochastic Transport Route With
Early Mechanism And Coercivity Gate`

**Why this step exists now:** Planning run `run-008` replaced the invalidated
energy-helicity branch with the stochastic-transport chain as the next
canonical branch. That choice was not a license to roam across stochastic
representations. It was a tightly conditioned admission:

- the branch is live only if one exact stochastic observable can be tied to
  one named blowup mechanism;
- the observable must promise more than expectation-level repackaging;
- the route must clear prior-art and anti-repackaging burdens before formal
  stochastic identities are expanded; and
- a setup failure here is a successful bounded negative result, not a reason to
  push deeper into the branch.

This step is therefore the setup-and-admissibility gate for the stochastic
route. If it cannot freeze one mechanism, one primary stochastic family, one
candidate observable, one closest comparator, and one concrete beyond-prior-art
claim, the branch should stop before Chain Step 2.

## This Step Covers

This strategizer execution covers **Chain Step 1 only**:

- name one specific blowup mechanism or bad term the branch wants to obstruct;
- choose one primary stochastic representation family only after explaining why
  it is the best candidate for that mechanism;
- freeze one candidate observable and one sentence explaining why it might
  constrain the chosen mechanism;
- record the closest prior-art stochastic Navier-Stokes formulation and the
  exact gap this branch claims beyond it;
- freeze one admissibility rule, one endpoint-family priority, and one
  anti-repackaging rule that later steps can enforce mechanically; and
- decide whether the branch has earned expansion into the exact-identity and
  coercivity gate.

Do **not** derive the full stochastic identity yet. Do **not** run the
coercivity gate yet. Do **not** keep multiple stochastic families alive as a
portfolio inside the step. Do **not** use Tao sensitivity by itself as proof of
theorem relevance.

## Kill Conditions

Trigger an early negative conclusion for this step if any of the following
happens:

- the step cannot name one specific blowup mechanism or bad term in burden
  language;
- the step keeps multiple stochastic families alive instead of choosing one;
- the candidate observable is attractive only as representation or only at the
  expectation level, with no plausible local or pathwise consequence;
- the closest prior-art comparator already contains the claimed gain;
- the route chooses a continuation endpoint without already looking
  near-coercive enough to justify that priority;
- the setup remains too dependent on smooth stochastic flows, strong
  filtrations, or stopping machinery that already presuppose the protected
  regularity.

If a kill condition fires, count that as a successful bounded negative result
for this step and recommend branch invalidation before Chain Step 2.
