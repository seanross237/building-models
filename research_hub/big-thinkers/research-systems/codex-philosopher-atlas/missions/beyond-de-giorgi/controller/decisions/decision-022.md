# Decision Memo - beyond-de-giorgi / decision-022

## Decision

`proceed`

## Mission-Control Verdict

This is a post-step review of `step-013`, the current chain's calibration gate.
The relevant question is whether that step merely produced a tidy summary or
actually earned continuation into the first candidate audit.

It earned continuation.

`step-013` answered the branch's Step-1 gate in the way `CHAIN.md` required:

- it froze one primary endpoint family and one backup endpoint family on the
  inherited LEI / CKN floor;
- it fixed one theorem-facing baseline burden on the full localized
  `I_flux[phi]` bundle;
- it made same-currency comparison and transfer-lemma requirements explicit;
- it fixed one solution class and one localization regime for later candidate
  work; and
- it wrote the anti-repackaging and nearest-prior-art packet formats tightly
  enough to govern later screening.

That is not a branch kill or even a branch weakening. It is exactly the scope
lock this chain needed before candidate reformulations could be tested. The
current chain therefore remains active.

## Why `replan` Is Wrong

Replanning now would ignore the main fact learned at `step-013`: the branch's
largest setup risk did not materialize. The chain said it should stop if the
endpoint window, ledger, comparability rule, or audit format could not be
frozen concretely before algebra began. The latest result says the opposite.

There is no new evidence that the current reformulation-audit branch has
collapsed into under-specified bookkeeping, prior-art absorption, or endpoint
drift before candidate testing. Reopening branch comparison now would be
premature.

## Why `terminate` Is Wrong

The mission-level question remains open. `step-013` did not produce either a
live reformulation packet or a terminal negative result showing that all
plausible reformulation families fail after localized debt accounting.

What it produced was the admission protocol for that audit. That is valuable,
but it is not mission completion.

## Required Next Controller Move

Proceed into `step-014` as Chain Step 2 of the active reformulation-audit
branch.

That step should stay narrow. It must:

- keep at most two active candidate families and one reserve;
- derive, for each active candidate, the exact evolution and the localized form
  relevant to the frozen `I_flux[phi]` burden;
- write an endpoint-exchange memo showing how any native-variable gain would
  have to cash out in the frozen theorem-facing currency;
- charge pressure re-entry, cutoff commutators, reconstruction, and
  admissibility debt immediately; and
- attach the nearest prior-art comparator and exact claimed delta for each
  candidate before any later promotion.

If every candidate turns out to be a recycled reformulation, an identity-level
rewrite, or an exchange-dead package with no credible route back to the frozen
burden currency, the branch should stop there rather than drifting into
Step 3.
