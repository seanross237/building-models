explicitly designed to front-load the Tao discriminator before any estimate

fired the branch-kill condition honestly.

`I_flux[φ] = ∬_{Q_r} (|u|^2 + 2p) u · ∇φ`, none of the three frozen candidates

an earned next move.

## Why `proceed` Is Wrong

Proceeding would contradict the chain's own gatekeeping standard. Chain Step 2
was supposed to decide whether any frozen rewrite had a concrete
NS-versus-averaged discriminator tied to a smaller effective coefficient on
the already-fixed localized LEI bad term. `step-006` answered that question
negatively for the entire candidate family.

That means the next ordered step of the active chain is no longer the best
next mission move. The branch has already earned its bounded obstruction
result.

## Why `terminate` Is Wrong

This is a branch kill, not a mission kill. The broader mission-level question
remains open: what NS-specific structure, if any, could still bypass the De
Giorgi barrier outside this failed exact-reformulation audit?

The latest step closes one live loophole cleanly, but it does not produce a
terminal positive mechanism and it does not prove that every beyond-De-Giorgi
route is exhausted.

## Required Next Controller Move

Mission control should replan now.

The replanning pass should treat the current exact-reformulation chain as
invalidated and compare the remaining live alternatives afresh. It should not
auto-advance to Chain Step 3, and it should not preserve this branch through a
quiet architecture change or hybrid rescue that the current chain explicitly
forbids.
