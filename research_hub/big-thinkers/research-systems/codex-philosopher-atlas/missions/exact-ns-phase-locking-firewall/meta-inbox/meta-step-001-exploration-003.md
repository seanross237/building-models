# Meta Note - Step 001 Exploration 003

## What Worked

- Defining delayed transfer through exact closed-support energy-balance
  quantities produced a metric sheet that does not depend on packet-itinerary
  rhetoric.
- Freezing the event threshold, spectator burden, and robustness rule before
  support enumeration makes later positive claims much harder to overread.

## Reusable Lesson

- If a branch needs "delayed transfer" as a later success condition, force the
  metric sheet to use intrinsic clocks and exact inflow observables before the
  search starts; otherwise ordinary fast transfer or one-orbit luck will be
  mispackaged as evidence.

## Caution

- A later transfer partition may be chosen after the support is fixed, but the
  partition and scoring formulas must be frozen before any trajectory is judged.
