# Mission: Exact NS Phase-Locking Firewall

## Why This Mission Exists

The current Tao-adjacent exact-NS program has now reached a sharper frontier.

One branch in `codex-philosopher-atlas` succeeded in freezing two honest
packet-level downstream objects:

- repaired `Template-Defect Near-Closure`
- repaired `Windowed Spectator-Leakage Budget`

That is real progress. But it still does **not** give an intrinsic
theorem-facing NS object. It gives packet-family gates on one frozen canonical
ledger.

At the same time, the broader packet-object program keeps hitting the same
wall:

- desired-channel bookkeeping drifts
- packet boundaries drift
- representative choices matter
- support annotations are not intrinsic enough

So the next serious moonshot should not be "one more packet labeling trick."
It should ask whether the real missing object is **dynamical and intrinsic**:

```text
phase-locking or phase decoherence in exact Navier-Stokes triad transfer
```

This is whacky, but it is not random. Tao's averaged model keeps the rough
energy-transfer architecture while destroying exact NS-specific interaction
structure. Phase-sensitive delayed transfer is one plausible place where exact
NS may differ in a way that is both dynamical and theorem-facing.

## The Question

Can exact 3D Navier-Stokes support the kind of sustained phase coherence needed
for Tao-like delayed threshold transfer, or does exact NS force a phase
decoherence tax that kills programmable near-circuit behavior?

The target is **not** a full regularity proof directly. The target is one exact
NS-specific dynamical object that could matter for the prize problem:

- a lower bound on unavoidable phase slippage,
- a rigidity statement that sustained Tao-like transfer needs incompatible
  phase alignment,
- an intrinsic coherence functional that exact NS cannot keep small while
  preserving the desired transfer direction,
- or a counterexample showing that a small exact NS subsystem can in fact stay
  phase-locked long enough that the whole firewall idea should be dropped.

Either a positive obstruction or a negative kill is a valid success state.

## What Success Looks Like

One of the following:

1. **Positive obstruction**

   A precise exact-NS statement of the form:

   - any exact triad family carrying sustained one-way delayed transfer must
     incur phase decoherence at least `...`,
   - or any candidate Tao-like delayed-transfer episode requires mutually
     incompatible phase-locking constraints across the exact interacting triads,
   - or low spectator leakage plus strong delayed transfer forces a positive
     lower bound on one intrinsic phase defect.

2. **Negative kill**

   A concrete exact helical/Fourier subsystem shows that phase locking can in
   fact persist long enough to realize the hoped-for delayed transfer behavior,
   so this firewall idea should be dropped.

3. **Definition-level obstruction**

   No intrinsic phase/coherence object can be defined sharply enough without
   smuggling packet labels, desired channels, or other non-intrinsic
   annotation back in.

- the mission produces only slogans like "maybe NS decoheres phases" without a
  concrete invariant, lower bound, or counterexample.

These are still valid bounded negative outcomes if written sharply.

## Core Mission Structure

### Step 1: Define the smallest intrinsic phase/coherence object

Use the inherited Tao mechanism record and exact-NS helical/triad language to
define the smallest candidate object that is:

- intrinsic to exact transfer,
- dynamical rather than static bookkeeping,
- capable of distinguishing desired delayed transfer from spectator burden,
- and not dependent on post hoc packet labels.

Possible examples include:

- triad-phase slippage across a carried interaction family,
- a delayed-transfer phase-locking window,
- an intrinsic transfer-direction coherence functional,
- or a phase-defect quantity tied to exact Leray-projected triad interactions.

**Deliverable:** one concrete object or a very small family of objects.

**Kill condition:** if every candidate depends essentially on non-intrinsic
packet annotation, stop and report that sharply.

### Step 2: Derive the exact NS evolution / constraint ledger

Work in the best exact framework, likely:

- Fourier triads,
- helical decomposition,
- exact Leray-projected bilinear interactions,
- and, if useful, exact phase-amplitude evolution for small triad families.

Identify:

- what exact phase combinations control the desired transfer direction,
- which phase relations can persist,
- what spectator or cross-triad terms automatically perturb them,
- and whether viscosity or exact coupling geometry creates an unavoidable drift.

**Deliverable:** one exact interaction / phase ledger for the chosen object.

### Step 3: Test obstruction versus construction

Choose the smallest meaningful exact subsystem or parameterized family and ask:

- can the needed phase locking persist long enough for Tao-like delayed transfer?
- does exact NS force a lower bound on phase slippage or coherence loss?
- can one prove an incompatibility between strong delayed transfer and low
  decoherence?
- or does a concrete helical/Fourier family exhibit sustained phase coherence,
  killing the firewall idea?

Prefer small explicit families over diffuse symbolic rhetoric.

**Deliverable:** either

- a phase-obstruction statement,
- or a concrete phase-locked construction / counterexample,
- or a bounded negative memo explaining why the object fails.

### Step 4: Final report

One of three outcomes:

1. **Concrete phase-obstruction statement found**
2. **Concrete phase-locked counterexample found**
3. **Definition-level failure: no intrinsic phase object survives**

## Planner Steering Note

During planning and selection, explicitly consider at least these two high-value
directions:

1. **Phase-decoherence theorem route**
   Try to prove that exact Navier-Stokes cannot maintain the phase coherence
   needed for Tao-like delayed transfer without paying a positive decoherence
   tax or violating exact interaction constraints.

2. **Phase-locked construction route**
   Treat the firewall idea adversarially and search for the smallest exact
   helical/Fourier subsystem that stays phase-locked long enough to realize the
   required delayed transfer behavior. If such a subsystem survives honest
   exact accounting, the whole phase-firewall idea should be dropped quickly.

A planning run that omits both of these and drifts toward only language cleanup
is under-ambitious for this mission.
