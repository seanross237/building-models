# Chain 02 - Canonical Finite Compatibility Audit For Exact Phase-Locking Candidates

## Central premise

A finite exact compatibility problem can still matter, but only after fixing a
canonical encoding and declaring the coefficient/amplitude regime it is allowed
to use. The honest job of this chain is to decide whether a chosen exact
candidate encoding is already incompatible, whether it admits a compatible
sheet worth later dynamic testing, or whether the phase-only reduction is too
weak to carry mission-relevant claims. By itself, this is not a universal
firewall theorem route.

## Verifiable result target

Either:

- an encoding-level incompatibility theorem for a canonically specified finite
  network or explicit finite family, under declared coefficient/amplitude sign
  hypotheses;
- an explicit compatible phase sheet together with the geometric, coefficient,
  and amplitude hypotheses needed for later exact-dynamics testing; or
- a negative memo showing that either no canonical finite encoding exists, or
  the mission-relevant constraints depend essentially on amplitude hierarchy or
  time persistence rather than phase-only data.

## Why this refined chain is meaningfully different

This chain audits finite exact compatibility before long-time dynamics. It is
not the construction branch and it is not an intrinsicity audit in the strong
sense. Its value is to kill bad finite encodings early, or to hand forward a
concrete compatibility sheet with honest hypotheses.

## Ordered steps

### Step 1 - Fix a canonical finite encoding and scope

- Depends on: none.
- Actions:
  - fix one exact candidate role embedding only if it is generated by a clear
    closure rule, and record any admissible finite family of variants;
  - define repeated-mode identifications, gauge conventions, conjugate
    completion rules, and the enlargement tests that any claimed obstruction
    must survive;
  - state exact scope: any result applies to this encoding or explicitly
    described family, not to all Tao-like candidates by default.
- Expected output:
  - one canonical finite graph or hypergraph, or a short explicit family;
  - one closure and enlargement memo;
  - one scope statement.
- Kill condition:
  - if no non-arbitrary closure rule exists, or small admissible changes of
    embedding materially alter the finite object, stop and report
    noncanonicity.

### Step 2 - Separate genuine phase constraints from auxiliary dynamic hypotheses

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Actions:
  - write the exact helical triad transfer terms including coefficient signs
    and amplitude factors for desired, mirror, and spectator channels;
  - determine which desired transfer and suppression conditions reduce to honest
    phase inequalities after freezing geometry, coefficient, and amplitude
    regime, and which remain time-integrated or amplitude-driven;
  - classify assumptions as hard exact constraints, frozen auxiliary
    hypotheses, or nonreducible dynamical requirements.
- Expected output:
  - one ledger of genuine phase constraints;
  - one ledger of auxiliary amplitude, coefficient, and time hypotheses.
- Kill condition:
  - if delayed activation, one-way transfer, or spectator control require
    essential amplitude hierarchy or persistence assumptions that cannot be
    factored out cleanly, stop and report that the finite phase-only route is
    too weak.

### Step 3 - Build the honest reduced compatibility system

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Actions:
  - impose conjugate completion, helical sign relations, repeated-mode
    identities, and any exact coefficient or geometry constraints from the
    chosen encoding;
  - include conservation laws only insofar as the reduced system still carries
    the amplitude variables they constrain;
  - run at least one admissible enlargement check to test whether any apparent
    frustration is stable or disappears when forced extra channels are added.
- Expected output:
  - one reduced exact system;
  - one note distinguishing exact NS closure from encoding-dependent closure;
  - one stability result under admissible enlargement.
- Kill condition:
  - if exact NS adds no meaningful closure beyond the chosen encoding, or any
    inconsistency disappears under admissible enlargement, demote the branch to
    a weak pre-screen or stop.

### Step 4 - Solve or classify with negative-result discipline

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Actions:
  - solve the reduced system symbolically or classify feasible sheets under the
    declared auxiliary hypotheses;
  - distinguish sharply among incompatible encodings, compatible sheets with
    explicit hypotheses, and broad compatible regions showing finite phase
    frustration is not the intrinsic obstruction;
  - record whether any compatible sheet leaves open a realistic path to later
    delayed-transfer testing.
- Expected output:
  - either an incompatibility theorem,
  - or a compatible sheet with hypotheses,
  - or a negative memo against the finite-frustration premise.
- Kill condition:
  - if the system remains only algebraically messy with no resolved
    obstruction, no concrete compatible sheet, and no principled negative
    diagnosis, the chain failed.

### Step 5 - Promote only scope-correct outputs

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Actions:
  - if incompatible, state the result as an obstruction to the named encoding
    or finite family only;
  - if compatible, hand forward the sheet as a construction pre-screen, not as
    a dynamical survivor;
  - if the route fails on noncanonicity or hidden amplitude/time dependence,
    state that as the main result.
- Expected output:
  - one of:
    obstruction memo at encoding or family scope,
    compatible-sheet handoff with hypotheses,
    or negative memo that the phase-only finite audit is too weak or too
    noncanonical.
- Kill condition:
  - if the final writeup claims a firewall for exact NS without a separate
    universality argument, or treats static compatibility as proof of Tao-like
    delayed transfer, stop and rewrite at the correct scope.
