# Planner Portfolio - exact-ns-phase-locking-firewall / run-001

## Mission framing

This run should preserve three inherited facts without letting them dictate the
answer:

- Tao's mechanism is a delayed-threshold, role-structured circuit, not a generic
  forward cascade.
- Exact Navier-Stokes contributes rigid helical/triad geometry, compulsory
  conjugate completion, and unavoidable spectator couplings.
- The predecessor mission earned two packet-level downstream gates
  (`Template-Defect Near-Closure` and `Windowed Spectator-Leakage Budget`), but
  those are not yet intrinsic theorem-facing objects. In this run they should be
  used only as test fixtures and normalization scaffolds.

The portfolio below is intentionally split across four different success modes:

1. a dynamical phase-decoherence obstruction theorem;
2. an adversarial exact phase-locked construction that would kill the firewall;
3. a finite algebraic compatibility/frustration theorem;
4. a definition-level audit of whether any intrinsic phase/coherence functional
   survives without hidden packet labels.

## Portfolio summary

| Chain | Title | Best-case result | Major difference |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 01 | Dynamic Phase-Decoherence Tax | An exact lower bound showing that sustained Tao-like delayed transfer forces positive phase slippage or coherence loss | Dynamical lower-bound route based on evolution and time-integrated defect |
| 02 | Small Exact Phase-Locked Counterexample | A concrete helical/Fourier subsystem that stays phase-locked long enough to realize delayed transfer, killing the firewall | Adversarial construction route; tries to falsify the firewall directly |
| 03 | Finite Phase-Frustration Compatibility Theorem | An exact incompatibility between required transfer-direction phase relations and the mirror/spectator constraints of exact NS | Static/algebraic route; no long-time ODE control needed at first |
| 04 | Intrinsic Coherence Functional Audit | Either one genuinely intrinsic phase/coherence observable or a sharp memo that every candidate smuggles packet labels back in | Definition-level route; guardrail against fake intrinsicity |

## Recommended portfolio logic

Chain 01 is the top moonshot because it most directly matches the mission's
desired success state: a theorem-facing exact NS obstruction. Chain 02 should
stay equally alive early because a real small exact phase-locked subsystem would
kill the firewall faster than any obstruction proof. Chain 03 is a second
theorem-shaped route that attacks phase locking at the finite compatibility
level rather than through a dynamical inequality. Chain 04 is the bounded
negative guardrail that prevents the run from spending budget on phase language
that turns out to be disguised packet bookkeeping.

## Distinctness notes

- Chains 01 and 03 are both obstruction routes, but they are not variants of
  the same proof. Chain 01 seeks a time-integrated decoherence tax from exact
  phase evolution; Chain 03 seeks an immediate finite constraint inconsistency
  before any long-time dynamics are analyzed.
- Chain 02 is the only route that treats the firewall adversarially and tries to
  build the smallest honest exact subsystem that survives.
- Chain 04 is the only route whose success can be a sharp definition-level
  failure. It exists to prevent superficial progress on non-intrinsic objects.

## Execution posture

The most efficient wide-funnel run is:

1. open Chain 01 and Chain 02 immediately;
2. keep Chain 04 early as a filter on any proposed phase object;
3. open Chain 03 if Chain 01's dynamical object exists but the evolution ledger
   becomes too diffuse.

That ordering keeps both moonshot directions alive while still allowing an
early negative kill if "intrinsic phase object" turns out to be ill-posed.
