# Attack on Selected Chain 03

This selected file is actually original Chain 04, not Chain 03. That is not a
mathematical objection by itself, but it is a real process weakness: a branch
whose purpose is to issue a decision-grade kill memo should not already be
carrying bookkeeping ambiguity in its own selection artifact.

## Overall assessment

The premise is legitimate. An early intrinsicity audit is a real need, and a
definition-level negative is an honest mission outcome if it is earned. The
problem is that this chain, as written, cannot support the scope of the
negative it wants to deliver. It can at best audit a tiny hand-picked family of
candidates under a partly subjective canonicalization test. That is far short
of "no intrinsic phase object survives."

## Step-by-step critique

### Step 1 - Generate a minimal candidate family of intrinsic observables

- "At most three" candidate classes is arbitrary and too narrow. A failure over
  three planner-chosen objects is not evidence of nonexistence; it is evidence
  of a thin search.
- The example candidates are already biased toward the mission's preferred
  delayed-transfer language. "Transfer-direction coherence," "phase-window
  occupancy," and "coherence-versus-spectator budget" are not neutral object
  classes. They already bake in desired-channel and spectator semantics.
- Requiring an "intended theorem use" at the candidate-generation stage invites
  reverse engineering: instead of asking what intrinsic exact object exists,
  the chain asks what object would be convenient for the theorem story.
- The kill condition is miscalibrated. Failure to write a clean candidate on the
  first pass could reflect a poor search basis, not a genuine definition-level
  impossibility.

### Step 2 - Run the intrinsicity and canonicalization screen

- The three output buckets are too soft. "Canonical-but-honest" is a loophole
  big enough to keep almost any bookkeeping-dependent object alive.
- Allowing dependence on a "declared canonical packet sheet" directly risks
  violating the mission's own warning against turning this into another
  packet-canonicity audit. If a packet sheet is required, the object is already
  not cleanly intrinsic.
- The step names many things to test against: exact symmetries, gauge choices,
  conjugate completion, helical sign conventions, frozen normalization rules.
  But it never states the actual invariance criterion. Without an explicit
  equivalence relation, this becomes a verbal audit with movable standards.
- The kill condition is asymmetric. The chain can kill all candidates quickly,
  but it provides no equally sharp standard for certifying that a survivor is
  genuinely intrinsic rather than merely well-normalized.

### Step 3 - Derive the exact evolution or balance law for survivors

- This step quietly changes the target. Step 1 asks for an intrinsic observable;
  Step 3 demands an observable with a tractable exact evolution law. Those are
  not the same requirement.
- Most natural exact NS functionals will generate open or ugly interaction
  ledgers. The chain does not say when that counts as honest complexity and
  when it counts as failure.
- The theorem-use filter appears again. A candidate can be real and intrinsic
  without being immediately useful for an obstruction theorem or construction
  test.
- The kill condition risks selecting only those candidates that collapse back
  into familiar packet bookkeeping, because those are the ones most likely to
  have easy ledgers.

### Step 4 - Audit survivors on inherited friendly and hostile fixtures

- Auditing intrinsic survivors on `F_SL`, `F_SS`, and `F_DT` reimports the old
  packet-template worldview. That is acceptable as a sanity check, but the
  chain gives it too much gatekeeping power.
- "Adds genuine new information" is underspecified and unstable. If the object
  correlates with prior packet gates, it can be dismissed as redundant. If it
  does not, it can be dismissed as irrelevant. That is not a fair test.
- The fixture suite is thin and mission-biased. There is no reason to believe
  these three inherited cases are exhaustive enough to validate or kill a new
  intrinsic object.
- The kill condition is too strong. Failing to distinguish itself from prior
  packet gates does not prove the object is not the desired phase/coherence
  quantity. It may only show the fixture suite is poorly chosen.

### Step 5 - Promote a survivor or close the branch

- The final fork is overconfident relative to the evidence collected. Promoting
  one survivor after this audit is risky; declaring a branch-closing negative is
  even riskier.
- The proposed negative statement is much broader than the actual search. Unless
  the chain defines a mathematically closed candidate class, it cannot honestly
  conclude that no intrinsic phase object survives without hidden transfer
  annotation.
- The rhetoric kill condition is fine but secondary. The real failure mode here
  is logical overreach, not sloppy prose.

## Structural weaknesses of the whole chain

- The chain is less differentiated than advertised. It mostly formalizes the
  mission's already-required intrinsicity screen, and it overlaps with other
  branches on the same object-definition bottleneck.
- It conflates four distinct questions:
  definability,
  invariance under canonicalization,
  dynamical usefulness,
  and discrimination on inherited fixtures.
  Failure on any one of these is treated as if it disqualifies the object
  absolutely.
- The branch is structurally biased toward a predetermined negative. The search
  family is tiny, the central audit bucket is subjective, and the fixture test
  is inherited from packet-level work that the mission itself warns against
  repeating.
- The bookkeeping mismatch matters. `selected/chain-03.md` contains Chain 04.
  That does not refute the plan, but it weakens confidence in cross-chain
  handoff and in any later claim that the branch produced a precise mission
  decision.

## Fair verdict

This chain has real value as an early guardrail. It is right to punish fake
intrinsicity and to allow a negative outcome. But as written it does not earn
the scope of its advertised negative. Tightened properly, it could support a
scoped statement such as "no intrinsic object survives within this defined
candidate class under this formal equivalence test." Untightened, it is too
easy for it to output a sharp memo that is really just a sharply written audit
of three favored failures.
