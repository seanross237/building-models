### Winning Chain

# Chain 01 - Intrinsic Exact Closed-Support Delayed-Transfer Stress Test

## Central Premise

As the single next chain, pursue the moonshot directly:
search for the smallest honest exact helical/Fourier subsystem that could
realize Tao-like delayed transfer strongly enough to kill the firewall.

But do not begin with packet labels, desired-channel bookkeeping, or arbitrary
finite encodings. Before any dynamics are trusted, the chain must:

1. fix an intrinsic candidate phase/coherence object,
2. state a formal equivalence and admissibility test for that object,
3. declare a canonical exact support class and enlargement rule,
4. require recursive exact closure,
5. and make delayed transfer quantitative relative to intrinsic clocks and
   spectator burden.

Failure is class-limited unless an obstruction survives the declared
escalation ladder.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- an exact invariant helical/Fourier subsystem with an intrinsic
  phase/coherence object, quantified delayed-transfer behavior, controlled
  spectator burden, and robustness strong enough to count as a firewall
  counterexample;
- a class-limited obstruction memo proving that no support in one explicitly
  declared exact search class or budget realizes those standards; or
- a bounded definition-level or methodology memo identifying the precise
  blocker:
  no intrinsic candidate object,
  noncanonical encoding,
  recursive closure explosion,
  phase incompatibility,
  coefficient rigidity,
  spectator overload,
  auxiliary-hypothesis dependence,
  or lack of robust delayed transfer.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This branch keeps the only decisive existential upside in the finalist set, but
it no longer buys that upside with narrative looseness. It front-loads the two
failure modes that would otherwise waste the run:
nonintrinsic phase objects and noncanonical finite reductions.

Tao's five roles may still appear later, but only as a diagnostic language for
an already earned mechanism, never as a gate on what counts as a viable exact
construction.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Fix The Intrinsic Object, Equivalence Test, Search Class, And Metrics

Depends on: none.

Action: define one very small candidate family of exact phase/coherence objects
built from exact helical/Fourier interaction data, with no desired-channel
labels, packet sheets, or frozen transfer annotations in the raw definition.

Action: state the formal equivalence and admissibility test for those objects,
covering exact symmetries, conjugate completion, helical sign relabeling,
gauge conventions, and normalization changes. Use three statuses:
intrinsic,
canonically representable but non-intrinsic,
and inadmissible.

Action: choose the exact search objects. Default to finite Fourier/helical mode
supports. Declare closure conventions, admissible enlargements, smallest-first
ordering, and an explicit escalation ladder.

Action: define the quantitative success metrics up front:
what counts as delayed transfer relative to turnover and viscous times,
what spectator burden threshold is allowed during the window,
and what robustness standard is required.

Expected output: one intrinsic-object memo, one equivalence test, one exact
search-class memo, and one quantitative metric sheet.

Kill condition: if no candidate object survives intrinsic admissibility, or no
clean canonical search class and metric sheet can be stated, stop and write a
sharp definition-level or methodology-failure memo.

### Step 2 - Enumerate Canonical Recursively Closed Supports Inside The First Budget

Depends on: Step 1.

Action: starting from the first smallest support budget, recursively close each
seed family under conjugate completion, Leray projection effects, and all newly
forced triad interactions until the support is genuinely invariant or provably
spills over.

Action: treat spectator channels as part of this closure audit from the start.
Closure is recursive, not first-generation only.

Action: run at least one admissible enlargement test on each apparent survivor
so that any claimed locking or frustration is not an artifact of over-pruning
the finite encoding.

Action: record the exact support ledger for every survivor and the precise
reason each failed seed died.

Expected output: one ledger of genuinely invariant finite supports inside the
current budget, or a proof that no such support exists at that budget.

Kill condition: if every seed either loses exactness, spills outside the
current budget, or fails the admissible enlargement check, package only that
budget-limited negative result and either move to the next declared budget or
stop.

### Step 3 - Derive Exact Dynamics And Separate Genuine Phase Constraints From Auxiliary Hypotheses

Depends on: Step 2.

Action: for each genuinely invariant support, write the exact projected ODEs
with all coefficients, repeated-mode identifications, and symmetries explicit.

Action: compute conserved quantities and gauge freedoms, then determine which
transfer and locking requirements remain honest phase constraints after the
geometry, coefficient regime, and amplitude regime are frozen, and which still
depend essentially on amplitude hierarchy or persistence in time.

Action: solve the algebraic compatibility conditions for constant-phase or
quantitatively slow relative-phase regimes. Any slow-drift claim must come with
an explicit drift bound relative to the transfer window.

Action: track transfer directions and energy routing without requiring Tao's
five-role decomposition. If a mechanism appears later, the Tao roles may be
used only as a retrospective summary.

Expected output: one exact dynamics ledger plus one compatibility sheet that
separates genuine phase constraints from auxiliary coefficient, amplitude, and
time hypotheses.

Kill condition: if mission-relevant behavior depends essentially on
uncontrolled amplitude hierarchy or time persistence, or if no compatible phase
regime exists beyond transient coincidence, stop and package that obstruction
sharply.

### Step 4 - Test Delayed Transfer Against Intrinsic Clocks, Spectator Burden, And Robustness

Depends on: Step 3.

Action: for each admissible regime, compare the transfer delay to the support's
own turnover time and viscous decay time. Delayed transfer must be defined by
the metric sheet from Step 1, not by narrative convenience.

Action: enforce the spectator burden threshold during the same window. A
candidate fails if leakage or auxiliary channels overwhelm the mechanism before
the delayed transfer event lands.

Action: test robustness at the declared standard. Do not count a single finely
tuned favorable trajectory as success unless the declared standard explicitly
allows it.

Action: allow merged-role or non-Tao-shaped mechanisms. The chain is testing
for exact delayed-transfer behavior, not for a predetermined role diagram.

Expected output: one best exact candidate regime or one named obstruction:
no robust locking,
no delayed window,
spectator overload,
coefficient rigidity,
or auxiliary-hypothesis dependence.

Kill condition: if the best survivor only produces short-lived favorable phases
without robust delayed transfer under the declared metrics, the chain fails as
a counterexample route at the current budget.

### Step 5 - Compare The Outcome To The Firewall With Bounded Claims And Explicit Handoff

Depends on: Step 4.

Action: declare success only if an honest exact support, an intrinsic
phase/coherence object, and a robust delayed-transfer window all satisfy the
metric sheet. That endpoint is existential and does not require exhausting all
other support classes.

Action: if no success appears, claim only what has actually been shown:
no counterexample inside the searched exact support classes and budgets,
plus the exact obstruction or definition-level failure that caused the route to
stop.

Action: if a real exact survivor remains but the unresolved bottleneck becomes
finite compatibility classification rather than exact dynamics, hand forward the
compatible sheet as a scoped reserve input rather than overclaiming.

Expected output: either a counterexample memo, a class-limited obstruction
memo, or a bounded escalation or handoff memo.

Kill condition: if the final writeup makes a firewall-level negative claim from
an unexhaustive search, or counts static compatibility or unquantified
promising transients as evidence, stop and rewrite at the correct scope.
