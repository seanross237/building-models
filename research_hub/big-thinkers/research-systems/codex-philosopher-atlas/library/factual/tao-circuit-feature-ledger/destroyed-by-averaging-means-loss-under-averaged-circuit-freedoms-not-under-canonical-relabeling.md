# Destroyed By Averaging Means Loss Under Averaged-Circuit Freedoms, Not Under Canonical Relabeling

Status: `INFERRED` operational comparison rule with `VERIFIED` bookkeeping exclusions

For exact-NS versus Tao packet audits, a feature counts as `destroyed by
averaging` only if all three conditions hold:

- it survives true exact symmetries and one fixed canonical packet sheet;
- it depends on exact constraints coming from the real triad law, such as
  geometry-fixed coefficients, exact projection, mandatory conjugate
  completion, or unavoidable spectator couplings;
- it disappears once those exact constraints are replaced by Tao-style
  averaged-circuit freedoms that let the delayed-threshold role hierarchy be
  retuned independently.

A candidate does not count as destroyed by averaging if the apparent mismatch
can be recreated by shell regrouping, sign-sheet drift, packet relabeling,
same-helicity filtering, or any other move already classified as bookkeeping
or as a different packet model.

This turns `destroyed by averaging` into a concrete packet-comparison test
instead of a slogan about any exact-NS/Tao difference.
