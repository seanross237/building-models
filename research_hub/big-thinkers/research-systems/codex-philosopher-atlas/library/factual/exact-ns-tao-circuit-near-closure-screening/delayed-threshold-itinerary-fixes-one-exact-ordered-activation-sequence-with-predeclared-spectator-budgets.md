# Delayed-Threshold Itinerary Fixes One Exact Ordered Activation Sequence With Predeclared Spectator Budgets

Status: `INFERRED`

The third honest Step-2 candidate notion is `Delayed-Threshold Itinerary`.

It asks for one exact finite ordered activation itinerary
`clock -> trigger -> rotor -> next-stage transfer`
on a declared window, with one ordered-threshold admissibility sheet
`(t_clk, t_trig, t_rot, t_next; sign sheet; spectator pass/fail)` and all
spectator budgets fixed in advance.

This keeps the behavior-based candidate tied to one exact packet history and
one declared leakage policy, rather than treating `Tao-like behavior` as a
free-floating summary of the mechanism.

At Step 3, the honest invariant is the existence of the same ordered itinerary
and gate logic on the coherently transported sheet. Under whole-sheet scaling,
that means the transported ordered event tuple rather than literally unchanged
numeric timestamps inside the unscaled display.

Dependence on the frozen sign sheet, amplitude anchor, phase anchor, role
order, and conjugate-completion rule is acceptable only because Step 1 fixed
them. Retuning `theta_B`, `theta_C`, `theta_D`, `theta_E`, `Lambda_itin`, or
`kappa_rot`, changing `T_act`, redefining the spectator screen, changing the
sign sheet, regrouping the packet, or importing Tao-style delayed-threshold
freedoms defines a different model rather than a rewrite.

The completed Step-3 audit therefore classifies
`Delayed-Threshold Itinerary` as `use-case-limited but honest`.
Its gate remains meaningful only as a finite-window, construction-facing
admission filter on one frozen exact sheet, not as a coercive theorem
observable by itself.
