# Step-3 Robustness Only Quotients A Narrow Exact-Symmetry Tier And Treats Later Sheet Drift As A Different Model

Status: `INFERRED` narrow symmetry policy with `VERIFIED` drift exclusions

For the promoted exact-NS near-closure candidates, Step-3 robustness should
quotient only a narrow exact-symmetry tier:

- role-preserving rigid rotation of the whole packet geometry, with vector
  amplitudes, mandatory conjugate completion, and the candidate/gate sheet
  transported together
- full coupled Navier-Stokes scaling of the whole packet sheet, including
  geometry, amplitude normalization, and time scaling

Under those symmetries, the honest invariant is the candidate truth value on
the coherently transported packet/candidate/gate sheet, not literal unchanged
coordinates inside one frozen display.

Everything else stays outside that symmetry tier unless the local record
justifies it more directly. On the current record, phase rotations,
helical-basis changes, conjugate-representative changes, packet regrouping,
sign-sheet swaps, and Tao-style averaged freedoms are not automatically
cosmetic.

This means Step-3 may tolerate dependence on the frozen canonical packet sheet
only because that sheet was declared up front. Later packet regrouping,
support-semantics changes, spectator re-partitioning, threshold or window
retuning, sign-sheet or relative-phase-sheet changes, and imported Tao-style
gate freedoms should be counted as different candidate models rather than as
harmless rewrites.
