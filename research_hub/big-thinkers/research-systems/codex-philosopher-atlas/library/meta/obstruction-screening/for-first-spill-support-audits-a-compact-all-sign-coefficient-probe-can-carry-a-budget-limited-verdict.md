# For First-Spill Support Audits, A Compact All-Sign Coefficient Probe Can Carry A Budget-Limited Verdict

When an exact-support audit has already identified the first new wavevector
targets on a frozen ledger, a small representative helical-coefficient probe
across all seed sign assignments can be enough to certify that at least one of
those first spill targets stays live.

That kind of all-sign probe is often sufficient for the current-budget claim.
If every seed sign sheet still forces some first new target with nonzero exact
projected coefficient, the branch can file the spill verdict without first
reconstructing full projected ODEs.

Keep the claim budget-limited. The compact coefficient check justifies the
first-spill obstruction on the frozen budget; it does not by itself prove a
full infinite-closure theorem.
