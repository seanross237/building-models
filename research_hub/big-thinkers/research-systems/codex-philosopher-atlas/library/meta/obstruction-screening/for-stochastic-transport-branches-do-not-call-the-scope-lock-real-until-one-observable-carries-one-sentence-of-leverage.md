# For Stochastic Transport Branches Do Not Call The Scope Lock Real Until One Observable Carries One Sentence Of Leverage

When a stochastic transport branch narrows honestly to one burden and one
comparator neighborhood, do not promote that narrowing to a real Step-1 scope
lock unless the local record also freezes both:

- one theorem-facing observable, rather than only family labels or observable
  aliases;
- and one sentence explaining how that observable acts on the chosen burden
  beyond expectation-only rewriting.

If the archive only gets as far as names such as `backward stochastic flow`,
`back-to-label`, or `Constantin-Iyer`, file the result as
`menu-level setup obstruction` even if one family is clearly the best-supported
test.

For stochastic transport branches, `local concentration exclusion first` is the
sharper default filter than generic continuation language. Continuation should
stay secondary until the candidate observable already looks near-coercive or
norm-adjacent.

Even that endpoint-ordering success does not yet admit the branch on the
inherited theorem-facing solution floor. If the record still lacks an explicit
bridge from the suitable-weak / Leray-Hopf plus LEI setting into the chosen
stochastic observable class, keep the branch at `solution-class setup
obstruction` rather than upgrading it on endpoint-priority grounds alone.
