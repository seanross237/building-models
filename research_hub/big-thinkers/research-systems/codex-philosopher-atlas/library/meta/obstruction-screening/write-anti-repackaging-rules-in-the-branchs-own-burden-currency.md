# Write Anti-Repackaging Rules In The Branch's Own Burden Currency

Anti-repackaging warnings are strongest when they are written in the exact
burden currency the branch already uses.

Instead of saying only that a route feels cosmetic or representational, state
the failure buckets mechanically in the branch's own language and judge them
by net effect on the full theorem-facing burden after localization,
reconstruction, admissibility debt, and any claimed transfer are restored:
expectation-only, filtration-only, localization-only, or
parasitic-framework-only consequences do not count unless they cash out as a
local or pathwise gain on the named live burden after later debt is charged.

That makes the stop rule auditable. It also prevents elegant reformulation
work from functioning as a hidden substitute for the same deterministic burden
the branch was supposed to improve.

If a branch keeps an `ambiguous` middle bucket, reserve it for one exact
missing transfer step on the frozen ledger. Vague future promise should
collapse into equivalent redistribution rather than stay alive as apparent
novelty.
