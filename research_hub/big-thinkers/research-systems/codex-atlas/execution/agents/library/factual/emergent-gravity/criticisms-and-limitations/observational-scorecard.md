# Emergent Gravity: Observational Scorecard

## Supporting

- Galaxy lensing (Brouwer et al. 2017): No-free-parameter EG prediction matches 33,613 galaxy lensing profiles
- Cluster inner regions (Govind & Desai 2024): EG accurate for SMACS J0723 inner dynamical mass
- Radial acceleration relation (McGaugh et al. 2016): Universal relation in 153 galaxies consistent with MOND/EG
- Analog Hawking radiation (Steinhauer 2019): Thermal spectrum at Hawking temperature supports horizon thermodynamics

## Opposing

- Galaxy clusters (Tamosiunas et al. 2019): GR+CDM strongly preferred on cluster scales
- Galaxy-type splitting (Brouwer et al. 2021): 6-sigma early/late-type discrepancy at fixed mass
- Dwarf spheroidals (Pardo 2017): Unrealistic mass-to-light ratios required
- Bullet Cluster: Lensing-baryonic offset unexplained

## Untested

CMB anisotropies, BAO, matter power spectrum, gravitational waves, structure formation, non-spherical/time-dependent systems, decoherence at larger masses.

## Assessment

Promising at galaxy scales. Problematic at cluster scales. Incomplete for cosmology. Not yet a viable alternative to Lambda-CDM.
