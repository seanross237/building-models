## Finding

Positive geometry does NOT cleanly extend to N=1 or N=2 SYM. The obstacle is structural (not technical), arising directly from UV divergences in the loop integrand.

## The Benincasa-Gordo Obstacle (arXiv:1609.01923, JHEP 2017)

On-shell diagrams for N<4 SYM have the same combinatorial (Grassmannian) structure as N=4 — BCFW recursion works, positive matrices appear, the same cells are visible. But the **differential form (measure) is deformed** by extra factors encoding UV singularities. Four specific obstacles:

1. **UV poles at infinity**: Under BCFW shifts in N<4 theories, the amplitude does not vanish as z → ∞ at loop level. This produces "boundary terms" / poles at infinity — absent in N=4.

2. **Deformed measure on Grassmannian**: The on-shell diagram measure has extra factors beyond the standard d log form. These are not logarithmic singularities on boundaries of a geometric object — they are "extra" poles that corrupt the canonical form interpretation.

3. **Helicity loops in on-shell diagrams**: In N<4, helicity can flow in closed loops within on-shell diagrams. These produce higher-order poles entirely absent in N=4, directly associated with UV divergences and rational terms at loop level.

4. **Bubble contributions**: Non-maximal SUSY generates "bubble-cut" ambiguities in the loop integrand definition.

**Net result**: The amplituhedron and its clean geometric interpretation do not extend to N<4. The measure is not a canonical form with only logarithmic singularities.

## The Dian-Heslop Near-Miss (arXiv:2106.09372, 2021)

Dian and Heslop study "amplituhedron-like geometries" with non-maximal winding numbers. These canonical forms equal **A_{n,k} × A_{n,n-k}** — products of parity-conjugate N=4 amplitudes (amplitude squared). This is NOT an N=2 extension: it uses N=4 SYM data exclusively and produces amplitude squares, not amplitudes.

## Bottom Line

For N=1/N=2 SYM: no clean positive geometry exists. UV divergences physically cannot be "hidden" inside a positive geometry — they produce poles that do not admit a log-form interpretation. This is fundamental, not technical.
