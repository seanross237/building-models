# Complex Numbers from Indivisibility for N>2

## Core Result

Barandes (arXiv:2602.01043, Feb 2026, "A Deflationary Account of Quantum Theory") provides a structural explanation for why QM uses complex numbers: they are the minimal algebraic extension needed to represent quantum transitions (unistochastic matrices) as amplitude-squared for systems with N>2 states.

## Technical Structure

1. **Markovian embedding:** A non-Markovian system can be made Markovian by expanding the state space (standard dynamical systems technique).
2. For N-dim Hilbert space, the Strocchi-Heslot formulation maps QM to a classical system with 2N coupled harmonic oscillators -- a Markovian embedding of dimension 2N.
3. **Stochastic-quantum direction:** Given Gamma_ij >= 0 (non-negative transition matrix), find Theta with Gamma_ij = |Theta_ij|^2. For N>2, unistochastic matrices are generally non-orthostochastic (no real orthogonal matrix works). Complex numbers are needed.
4. For N=2, real orthogonal matrices suffice (the qubit = real case).

## Pseudo-Quaternion Observation

Barandes notes that the Hilbert space algebra is actually defined over "pseudo-quaternions" (generated by i, K, iK satisfying i^2 = K^2 = (iK)^2 = 1), where K is the complex-conjugation operator needed for time-reversal. This structural insight about the full algebra of QM is obscured in the standard Hilbert space formulation.

## Prior Art

The inadequacy of real Hilbert spaces for full QM was known since Stueckelberg (1960). Experimental confirmation: Renou et al. (2021, arXiv:2101.10870) showed real QM fails specific entanglement tests. Barandes' angle (complex numbers needed for the Markovian embedding) is novel as a derivation path, not as a result.

## Assessment

Structurally interesting but not a new prediction. Provides the ISP-specific explanation for WHY QM needs C rather than R. The pseudo-quaternion observation is a minor structural finding. No testable consequences.
