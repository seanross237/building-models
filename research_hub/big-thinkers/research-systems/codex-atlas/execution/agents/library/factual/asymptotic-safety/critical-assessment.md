# Critical Reflections on Asymptotically Safe Gravity

This file summarizes the critical assessment published in Frontiers in Physics (2020) by Bonanno et al., "Critical Reflections on Asymptotically Safe Gravity" (DOI: 10.3389/fphy.2020.00269).

## Arguments Supporting the Program

- **Theoretical Foundation**: The approach identifies the Reuter fixed point in the space of diffeomorphism-invariant actions, suggesting a genuine non-perturbative UV completion exists. This mechanism has proven successful in non-gravitational examples like the Gross-Neveu model in lower dimensions.
- **Predictive Power**: Fixed point properties constrain free parameters. "Irrelevant directions fix one parameter in initial conditions for Gamma_k, whereas relevant directions correspond to free parameters," limiting the theory's degrees of freedom and enhancing predictability.
- **Consistency**: Recent truncation calculations consistently identify the fixed point across multiple approximation schemes.

## Critical Challenges

- **Methodological Uncertainties**: The functional renormalization group approach relies on truncations -- approximations whose systematic errors remain "uncontrollable." The background field method's validity in quantum gravity contexts faces ongoing scrutiny.
- **Observable Computation Difficulties**: Extracting physical predictions proves exceptionally challenging. Computing observables and matching predictions with observations represents a major unresolved obstacle.
- **Lorentzian Signature Gap**: Most calculations occur in Euclidean signature. The absence of rigorous Lorentzian formulations leaves fundamental questions about spacetime's real structure unanswered.
- **Unitarity and Ghosts**: The unitarity status and higher-derivative ghost issues remain open questions.
- **Effective Field Theory Matching**: Connecting the UV fixed point regime to low-energy effective field theory predictions is not yet fully resolved.

## Current Status

The asymptotic safety program is positioned as an active research frontier with:

- Established evidence for the Reuter fixed point across multiple truncation schemes
- Growing connections to particle physics, cosmology, and black hole physics
- Unresolved foundational questions about truncation convergence, Lorentzian signature, and unitarity
- Active debate between proponents and skeptics regarding asymptotic safety's viability as quantum gravity's solution
