# Verlinde's Entropic Gravity (2010)

Erik Verlinde's 2010 paper "On the Origin of Gravity and the Laws of Newton" (arXiv:1001.0785) proposes that gravity is not a fundamental force but an entropic force caused by changes in the information associated with the positions of material bodies.

## Core Argument

Gravity is explained as an entropic force arising in a theory where space is emergent through a holographic scenario. The central equation is:

**F = T (dS/dx)**

where T is temperature and S is entropy. A particle near a holographic screen experiences a force because displacing it changes the entropy encoded on the screen.

## Derivation of Newton's Law

Verlinde combines three ingredients:
1. **Bekenstein's entropy bound**: A particle of mass m displaced by one Compton wavelength changes the screen entropy by dS = 2pi k_B (mc/hbar) dx
2. **Unruh temperature**: T = hbar a / (2pi c k_B)
3. **Holographic principle**: The number of degrees of freedom on a screen of area A is N = Ac^3/(G hbar), with total energy distributed by equipartition E = (1/2) N k_B T

From F = T dS/dx with the Unruh temperature, Newton's second law F = ma emerges. Combining with the holographic energy-mass relation E = Mc^2, Newton's gravitational law F = GMm/r^2 follows.

## Einstein's Equations

A relativistic generalization of the entropic arguments directly leads to the Einstein equations. When space is emergent, even Newton's law of inertia needs to be explained, and through the equivalence principle, the origin of inertia is itself entropic.

## Key Assumptions

- Space is emergent (not fundamental)
- The holographic principle holds generally
- Gravity is not quantized in the usual sense -- it is emergent like temperature or pressure

## Reception

Harvard string theorist Andrew Strominger noted: "Some people have said it can't be right, others that it's right and we already knew it." The paper generated substantial discussion, with some researchers arguing there is no convincing analogy between gravity and entropic force since neither the holographic screen nor test particle satisfies all requirements for entropic force in a thermodynamic system.
