# Classical Gravity Entanglement Controversy (2025)

## The Claim

**Marletto et al. (Nature, 2025)** claim that classical gravity + QFT can generate entanglement between massive particles. This challenges the standard interpretation that gravitationally-induced entanglement (GIE) is evidence for quantum gravity.

## The Dispute

Diósi, Oppenheim et al. dispute the claim — active debate ongoing as of 2025.

## Implications

If correct:
- The standard GIE/BMV test for quantum gravity is compromised as a discriminator
- The observation of GIE would NOT conclusively prove gravity is quantum
- This reinforces the "gravitize the quantum" perspective: classical gravity might be sufficient

Related: the Diósi-Penrose model (classical gravity + modified QM) independently predicts GIE with entanglement surviving >1 day (see [`diosi-penrose-gravitational-collapse.md`](diosi-penrose-gravitational-collapse.md)).

## Status

The classical/quantum boundary for gravity is far from settled. This controversy underscores that the assumption "gravity must be quantum" is not as well-established as commonly assumed.
