# Limitations and Open Problems for Emergent Gravity

A synthesis of the major limitations, challenges, and open problems facing emergent gravity approaches, drawn from multiple critical assessments.

## Theoretical Limitations

1. **Vagueness of "Emergence"**: The definition of emergent gravity suffers from ambiguity regarding what qualifies as "very different" degrees of freedom or "novel" microstructure (Carlip 2012).

2. **Gauge Symmetry Problem**: How does a system without gauge symmetries ab initio develop diffeomorphism invariance effectively? This is a central unresolved question for Type I emergent gravity models.

3. **No-Go Theorems**: The Weinberg-Witten theorem constrains the emergence of massless spin-2 particles from theories with Lorentz-covariant stress-energy tensors. Marolf's theorem provides further constraints.

4. **Thermodynamic Inconsistencies**: Holographic screen surfaces described by emergent gravity theory do not actually behave thermodynamically -- ordinary surfaces (including holographic screens) generally do not obey the first law of thermodynamics, undermining a key assumption.

5. **Cosmological Constant Problem**: Sakharov-style induced gravity predicts a cosmological constant ~100 orders of magnitude too large. No emergent gravity approach has solved this.

## Observational Limitations

6. **CMB Predictions**: No emergent gravity theory has reproduced the detailed predictions of Lambda-CDM for CMB temperature anisotropies. This is a critical gap, since standard dark matter models match CMB data with precision.

7. **Dwarf Galaxy Problem**: Verlinde's emergent gravity underpredicts maximum velocities in massive dwarf spheroidal galaxies and requires unrealistically high mass-to-light ratios for some systems.

8. **Cluster-Scale Issues**: Emergent gravity predictions have not been systematically tested at galaxy cluster scales, where dark matter phenomenology is well established.

## The Shared UV Completion Failure

All entropic/information-theoretic gravity programs — Jacobson (1995), Verlinde (2010), Padmanabhan, and Bianconi (2025) — share a common failure mode: they derive the classical Einstein equations from thermodynamic or information-theoretic arguments but never provide UV completion. The hard problem of quantum gravity is not "how to derive Einstein's equations" — it's "how to quantize them consistently." Bianconi's 2025 proposal (Phys. Rev. D 111, 066001) is the latest case study: a genuinely novel action principle (gravitational action = quantum relative entropy) that fails Tier 1 validation because it is entirely classical, with no quantization, no UV completion, and unanalyzed ghost instabilities from fourth-order equations. See detailed assessment at [`../bianconi-entropic-action.md`](../bianconi-entropic-action.md).

## Technical Challenges

9. **No Microscopic Theory**: A fully developed microscopic theory that gives rise to spacetime and gravity is still under development. All current proposals are effective or heuristic.

10. **Limited to Specific Scenarios**: Emergent gravity simulations are currently limited to Schwarzschild-de Sitter scenarios and require insertion of four-dimensional spacetime by hand.

11. **No Graviton-Level Falsification**: There is no prospect for graviton-level falsification at Planck energies with foreseeable technology.

12. **Quantum Coherence Challenge**: Kobakhidze's argument that neutron quantum states disprove entropic gravity remains debated but unresolved.
