## Lesson

Use standard explorers for literature surveys — they complete in ~20 minutes vs. 50+ minutes for math explorations. Reserve math explorers for computational, dimensional analysis, and symbolic manipulation tasks. For "map the landscape of papers/results on topic X" tasks, the standard explorer is both faster and more appropriate.

**Extended lesson**: The standard explorer handles **multi-part analysis tasks** (survey → analysis → assessment → synthesis) not just pure literature surveys. A 4-part structure (mechanism, deformations, consequences, assessment) across a single exploration is within scope.

## Evidence

- **amplituhedron strategy-001 exploration 003** — Standard explorer completed a comprehensive 563-line literature survey covering 7 theory classes with ~20 cited arXiv papers (including recent 2024-2025 work) in approximately 20 minutes. Previous math-type explorations (same mission) took 50+ minutes for comparable scope and depth.

- **amplituhedron strategy-001 exploration 004** — Standard explorer completed a 362-line, 16-cited-paper report covering 4 distinct cognitive tasks in sequence: (1) mechanism analysis, (2) deformation exploration, (3) physical consequences survey, (4) comprehensive claim assessment with status table. Completed in ~25 minutes. Found a cutting-edge March 2025 arXiv paper (2503.03805) that was directly relevant. Demonstrates that standard explorers handle analysis and synthesis as well as survey — the constraint is "no computation/code," not "no synthesis."

- **amplituhedron strategy-001 exploration 007** — Standard explorer completed a 466-line deep-dive survey of the surfaceology program covering 15+ papers (Sep 2023 – Mar 2026) in ~15 minutes. Correctly identified the CRITICAL distinction between surfaceology and the amplituhedron (parallel, not nested) without prompting. Produced an excellent unification map table showing how each prior framework (ABHY, string theory, NLSM, YM, Yukawa, fermions) connects to surfaceology. This was the 4th successful standard exploration in a row — establishing strong statistical confidence in the pattern.

## When to Apply

- Literature mapping: "what exists on topic X, especially recent papers"
- Landscape surveys: "what theories/approaches exist and how do they relate"
- Extension maps: "where does framework X extend to theory Y and where does it fail"
- Paper inventory: "find and characterize recent papers by specific authors"
- **Multi-part analysis**: "investigate mechanism X, assess its consequences, and evaluate the key claims" — all in one exploration if no computation is required

## When NOT to Apply

- Dimensional analysis (use Math Explorer — see math-explorer-dimensional-analysis.md)
- Symbolic computation or numerical verification (use Math Explorer)
- Any task requiring plots, calculations, or code execution
- **NEW (vasseur-pressure E006):** When the literature survey includes perturbation analysis or computational verification that could be done in the same exploration, consider using a math explorer instead. E006 noted: "Should have been a math explorer — the perturbation analysis and Beltrami deficit questions could have been computationally verified within this exploration." When the goal combines survey + derivation + quantitative check, a math explorer handles all three; a standard explorer handles only the first two.

## Timing Reference

- Standard explorer on 7-theory literature survey: ~20 min, 563 lines, ~20 papers cited
- Standard explorer on 4-part analysis (mechanism + assessment): ~25 min, 362 lines, 16 papers cited
- Standard explorer on 15+-paper program survey (2.5 years of output): ~15 min, 466 lines
- Math explorer on comparable scope computation: 50+ min
