## Lesson

Verification explorations don't just confirm -- they catch errors in the existing knowledge base. When the library says "X differs from Y," always verify. The difference may dissolve upon closer inspection. Check whether the PREMISES of an incompatibility argument are correct before testing the implications.

## Evidence

- **strategy-003 exploration s3-004** — Explorer found that the library's claim "AS predicts r up to ~0.01" was misleading. Most AS papers predict Starobinsky inflation with r ~ 0.003. Pre-loaded context was corrected by the verification.
- **strategy-003 exploration s3-006** — The supposed tension (QG+F breaks analyticity, AS needs it) was based on a false premise: AS doesn't need standard analyticity and already has non-standard analytic structure. Checking premises before testing implications saved effort.

## When to apply

Whenever using library entries as the basis for strategic decisions. Especially important for claims about incompatibilities between frameworks, prediction comparisons based on approximate calculations, and any library entry that hasn't been independently verified. The cost of a verification exploration is low; the cost of building on a wrong premise is an entire wasted strategy.
