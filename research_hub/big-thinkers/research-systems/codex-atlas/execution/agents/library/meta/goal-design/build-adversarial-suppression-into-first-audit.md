## Lesson

When the working hypothesis is an obstruction or impossibility claim about leakage, the first exact interaction audit should include at least one configuration chosen specifically to suppress the alleged leakage. Otherwise the exploration only rules out naive arrangements and risks mistaking a bad first ansatz for a genuine barrier.

## Evidence

- **exact-ns-no-near-closed-tao-circuit exploration-001** — Once the near-closed Tao circuit was stated exactly, the next risk became obvious: an impossibility claim based on one convenient helicity/sign choice would be too weak. The first Phase 1 ledger should therefore test at least one arrangement chosen adversarially in favor of the construction, not in favor of the obstruction.
- The same exploration identified this as the cheapest meaningful screen before escalating to any global impossibility story.

## Goal Template

Add language like:

> Before accepting any obstruction or impossibility conclusion, test at least one configuration chosen specifically to suppress the suspected leakage or companion interaction. If the suppression attempt fails, say why. If it succeeds partially, report exactly which terms were reduced and which were rigid.

## When to Apply

- exact-support obstruction tests
- coefficient-rigidity or leakage-lower-bound missions
- constructive-vs-impossibility questions where geometry, helicity, sign, or symmetry choices may matter
