# Meta-Learning: Exploration 006

## What worked well
- Asking "what EXACTLY do they prove" with emphasis on theorem statements produced the most precise output of any exploration so far
- The "four layers of obstruction" structure was discovered by the explorer, not suggested by the goal — proving that open-ended technical analysis works when the question is specific enough
- Asking for "honest assessment of proximity to proof" got a 20-50+ year timeline that feels authentic
- 17 papers analyzed in one exploration is impressive — the scope was right for a deep analysis

## What didn't work
- Nothing failed. This was the most successful exploration so far.

## Lessons
- "What specifically fails for continuous groups" is a powerful prompt pattern that forces mathematical precision
- For deep dives into recent literature, the explorer is extremely effective — web search finds arXiv papers reliably
- The distinction between structural and technical obstructions is critical and should be requested explicitly
- Even when the answer is "this is a 50-year problem," the exploration is valuable if it precisely identifies WHY
