# Meta-Learning: Strategy-003 Exploration 009

**Type:** Sharpening discriminating predictions (response to devil's advocate)

**What worked well:** The exploration honestly assessed what's discriminating vs what's a consistency check. Found exactly one strong discriminator (ghost propagator computation). The null hypothesis development was insightful — particularly the realization that the QCD analogy actually supports unification rather than separation.

**Key insight:** There's a sharp distinction between "consistency check" (could be explained by compatible-but-separate) and "discriminating prediction" (only explained by unification). Most of the framework's 7 predictions are consistency checks, not discriminators.

**Lesson for future strategies:** When constructing theoretical frameworks, always develop the null hypothesis ALONGSIDE the framework. If you can't distinguish your framework from the null, it's not a scientific theory yet — it's a philosophical preference.
