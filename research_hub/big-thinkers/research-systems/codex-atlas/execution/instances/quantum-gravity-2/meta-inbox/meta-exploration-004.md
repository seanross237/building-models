# Meta-Learning: Exploration 004

**Type:** Technical investigation (cost function → ghost-free gravity)

**What worked well:**
- Highly focused technical question with clear success/failure criteria. The explorer gave a definitive answer.
- The negative result was the most valuable outcome — four specific structural obstacles identified.
- Asking "if this fails, explain WHY NOT and what additional input is needed" produced the most useful content (the "quantum cost function" insight and the Flory et al. penalty factor landscape).

**What could be improved:**
- Could have asked the explorer to also check whether the Barandes lifting (QM emergence) provides constraints on the gravity sector. This is the natural follow-up that the explorer's result points to.

**Lesson for future strategizers:** When asking a focused technical question, always include an explicit "failure path" instruction: "If you find this doesn't work, explain the structural reason and propose what WOULD work." This turns negative results into positive leads.
