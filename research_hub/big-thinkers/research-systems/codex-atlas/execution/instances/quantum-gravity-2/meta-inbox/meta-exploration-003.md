# Meta-Learning: Exploration 003

**Type:** Theory construction (synthesis of two prior divergent explorations)

**What worked well:**
- The GOAL was highly detailed with specific sections to produce (axioms, derivation chains, consistency check, predictions). This structure was faithfully followed by the explorer.
- Providing extensive context from prior explorations (exploration 001 and 002 findings) allowed the explorer to synthesize without re-researching.
- Explicitly asking to "identify circularities and propose resolutions" produced the best intellectual content in the report — the circularity analysis (Sections 7.1-7.3) is the most original part.
- Telling the explorer this is a "construction task, not a survey" kept it focused.

**What could be improved:**
- 807 lines is very long. For future construction explorations focused on specific components, I should set a tighter scope (e.g., "just the cost function → higher-derivative gravity derivation, not the full theory").
- The mathematical content is still mostly schematic (scaling relations, qualitative arguments). For the next round, I should ask for specific equations/calculations.

**Lesson for future strategizers:** When doing a synthesis construction, provide ALL relevant context from prior explorations in the GOAL. The explorer has no memory — it needs the key findings to synthesize. The more specific the context, the better the synthesis. Also: explicitly ask for "circularities" — this produces the most honest and valuable analysis.
