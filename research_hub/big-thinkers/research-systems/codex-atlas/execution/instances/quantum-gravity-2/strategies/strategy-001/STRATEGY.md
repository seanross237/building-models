# Strategy 001: Axiom-Breaking Construction from Novel Foundations

## Objective

Construct a genuinely novel theory of quantum gravity by starting outside the standard QFT axiom set. The prior mission (quantum-gravity, strategies 001-002) exhaustively proved that within {Lorentz invariance, diffeomorphism invariance, locality, perturbative renormalizability, unitarity}, the unique theory is QG+F (quadratic gravity with fakeon quantization). That landscape is fully mapped. This strategy therefore works in the space BEYOND those axioms — not by tweaking them, but by starting from fundamentally different conceptual foundations about what spacetime and gravity actually are.

The aim is not to rediscover or extend QG+F, but to produce a theory that a domain expert would recognize as a fresh contribution — one that offers new explanatory power, makes intuitive sense at its core, and suggests predictions that differ from existing approaches.

## Methodology: Diverge-Construct-Stress

The exploration cycle has three phases, repeated as the Strategizer sees fit within 10 explorations.

### Phase 1: Divergent Conceptual Exploration (2-3 explorations)

Generate multiple radically different foundational pictures of what spacetime and gravity might be. Each exploration should start from a different conceptual seed — NOT from a mathematical formalism, but from a physical or philosophical question:

- What if spacetime is not fundamental but emergent from something non-geometric?
- What if gravity is not a force or curvature but a manifestation of something information-theoretic, computational, or thermodynamic at a level deeper than Jacobson/Verlinde (whose programs were shown to fail UV completion)?
- What if the right starting point is not "quantize gravity" but "gravitize the quantum" — deriving quantum mechanics from gravitational principles?
- What if discreteness is fundamental but not in the way causal sets or LQG assume?
- What if the dimensionality of spacetime is not fixed but dynamically determined?

The Strategizer should choose 2-3 of the most promising seeds and run one exploration each. Each exploration should:
1. Develop the conceptual picture into a concrete physical framework (not just philosophy — it needs mechanisms)
2. Identify what the framework predicts that is different from GR and from QG+F
3. Honestly assess whether this is genuinely novel or secretly a repackaging of existing work
4. Propose specific next steps for construction

**Key constraint:** If an exploration starts converging toward QG+F or any well-known framework, the explorer must explicitly flag this and pivot to what makes the new picture DIFFERENT, not what makes it the same.

### Phase 2: Focused Construction (4-5 explorations)

Take the 1-2 most promising conceptual pictures from Phase 1 and build them into actual theories. Each exploration in this phase should:

1. Start from the conceptual framework and derive concrete physical consequences
2. Check internal consistency (no contradictions between claims)
3. Check compatibility with known physics (Newton's law at large scales, gravitational waves at c, equivalence principle)
4. Identify at least one prediction that differs from both GR and QG+F
5. Develop the theory's explanatory power — can it explain WHY gravity behaves the way it does in a way that makes intuitive sense?

Construction explorations should be tightly scoped: one exploration = one major construction task (e.g., "derive the effective field equations," "check compatibility with black hole thermodynamics," "work out the cosmological implications"). Do NOT try to build the entire theory in one exploration.

If a direction hits a fatal inconsistency, the Strategizer should pivot to the next most promising direction from Phase 1, or run a new divergent exploration if the pipeline is dry.

### Phase 3: Stress-Testing and Synthesis (2-3 explorations)

Subject the best theory to adversarial attack:

1. **Devil's advocate exploration:** Try to break the theory. Find internal contradictions, compatibility failures, or hidden assumptions that undermine the whole thing. Be ruthless.
2. **Comparison exploration:** How does this theory compare to QG+F on every validation tier? Where does it do better? Where does it do worse? Is there any domain where it offers genuinely superior explanatory power?
3. **Synthesis exploration:** Assemble the full theory with all results. Write it up clearly enough that a thoughtful non-physicist could understand the core idea.

## Validation Criteria

### Minimum success (strategy worth continuing)
- At least one conceptual framework that is genuinely novel (not a repackaging of existing work) AND internally consistent AND compatible with basic observational facts (Newton's law, GW speed, equivalence principle)

### Good success
- A novel theory that passes validation Tiers 1-3 (novelty, logical consistency, explanatory power) and makes at least partial progress on Tier 4 (compatibility with reality, predictions)

### Breakthrough
- A novel theory that passes all 5 validation tiers — including depth (Tier 5), meaning it has internal structure, handles edge cases, and suggests new questions

### Strategy exhaustion signals
- All divergent directions converge back to known frameworks despite genuine effort to avoid them
- Every construction attempt hits fatal inconsistencies that can't be resolved
- The Strategizer has tried 3+ substantially different foundational pictures and none produce viable theories

## Context from Prior Work

### What the prior mission established (DO NOT re-derive):
- QG+F is the unique perturbative QG within standard axioms. This is settled.
- The spectral dimension d_s = 4 → 2 is universal across QG approaches because they all approximate QG+F.
- Lee-Wick QG is dead (collapses onto QG+F).
- Entropic gravity (Verlinde, Jacobson, Padmanabhan) fails UV completion. The classical thermodynamic derivation works but doesn't give you a quantum theory.
- The cosmological constant cannot be explained by any continuum QFT approach.
- QG+F's only near-term testable prediction is r (tensor-to-scalar ratio), measurable by LiteBIRD ~2037.
- The six-derivative extension of QG+F resolves the n_s tension.

### What this means for this strategy:
- Don't try to build a perturbative QFT of gravity. That road leads to QG+F uniquely.
- Don't try to extend or modify QG+F. That's incremental, not novel.
- DO start from genuinely different conceptual foundations — something that wouldn't even be expressible as "quantize a classical field theory."
- The most promising escape routes from the prior work: (1) abandon perturbative framework entirely, (2) start from non-geometric foundations, (3) derive spacetime itself from something deeper, (4) take the CC problem seriously as a clue rather than an unsolvable nuisance.
