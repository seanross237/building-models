# Exploration 003 Summary: Ghost Fate at Strong Coupling

## Goal
Determine what the non-perturbative / AS literature says about the fate of the massive spin-2 ghost at strong coupling — does confinement, mass running, or spectral suppression naturally emerge?

## What Was Tried
Surveyed the key non-perturbative AS literature: Bonanno et al. (graviton spectral reconstruction), Becker-Ripken-Saueressig (Ostrogradski ghost decoupling), Platania-Wetterich (fictitious ghosts), Draper-Knorr-Ripken-Saueressig (complex pole towers and finite amplitudes), Holdom-Ren (ghost confinement conjecture), CDT results, and the Frasca mass gap paper.

## Outcome: INCONCLUSIVE, leaning SUPPORTS

The AS literature has **not directly confronted** the massive spin-2 ghost from the C² term. The spin-2 ghost occupies a blind spot — multiple frameworks *could* address it, but no paper has carried out the definitive calculation. Four candidate mechanisms exist:

1. **Ghost mass → ∞ at NGFP** (Becker et al. 2017): Proven for scalar ghosts, not for spin-2
2. **Fictitious ghost** (Platania & Wetterich 2020): Truncation-artifact argument, not demonstrated for Stelle's ghost
3. **Complex pole tower** (Draper, Knorr et al. 2020): Ghost-like poles at complex momenta forming infinite towers compatible with unitarity — most concrete mechanism
4. **Ghost confinement** (Holdom & Ren 2016): QCD analogy, zero direct evidence for gravity

## Key Takeaway
**The bridge between QG+F and AS at the ghost level is unbuilt.** The AS framework has multiple *consistent* mechanisms for ghost removal and no result requiring the ghost to persist, but no one has shown that AS dynamics *forces* the ghost out. The Draper et al. complex pole tower is the most promising lead — it shows ghost-like poles naturally migrating to complex momenta in the full form-factor treatment. Computing the full spin-2 propagator pole structure within the C²-extended AS truncation is the single most important open calculation for the QG+F = AS conjecture.

## Leads Worth Pursuing
- The Draper-Knorr-Ripken-Saueressig form factor program should be extended to explicitly track the C² ghost pole
- Becker et al.'s scalar ghost decoupling mechanism needs to be tested in the gravitational spin-2 sector
- Platania-Wetterich's fictitious ghost criterion could be applied systematically to Stelle's ghost across truncation orders
