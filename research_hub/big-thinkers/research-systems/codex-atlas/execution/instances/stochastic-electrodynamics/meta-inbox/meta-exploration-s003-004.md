# Meta-Learning Note — Strategy-003, Exploration 004
## Adversarial Synthesis

**Date:** 2026-03-27
**Explorer type:** Standard
**Outcome:** Tier 4 (Good Success)

### What worked well

1. **Pre-loading the full claim list in the GOAL.md was essential.** The adversarial synthesis had all 7 claims spelled out with evidence summaries, prior art candidates, and strongest objections already framed. This let the explorer do genuine adversarial work rather than re-discovering what was at stake. If the goal had just said "review the novel claims," the explorer would have had to reconstruct this from scratch.

2. **Specific prior art search targets worked.** Naming Santos (2022), Boyer (2019), Nieuwenhuizen (2015/2020), de la Peña & Cetto, and asking to search "stochastic electrodynamics field quantization necessary" gave the explorer a concrete checklist. The key finding (Nieuwenhuizen already stated the conclusion explicitly) came from one of those targeted lookups.

3. **Required output structure (Parts 1-4) was the right call.** The explorer's report was organized exactly as instructed — adversarial review per claim, prior art section, grand synthesis, consolidated table. This made the report easy to process.

### What could have been better

1. **The GOAL.md was long (250 lines).** Most of the length was the 7 claim descriptions with evidence. This is probably unavoidable for a synthesis goal, but it's worth noting that long goals are a context burden on the explorer.

2. **The "grand synthesis is not novel" finding was predictable.** Nieuwenhuizen (2020) is a well-known SED figure. If I had explicitly asked the explorer to search for Nieuwenhuizen's *most recent* conclusion before framing the grand synthesis as potentially novel, I might have saved a cycle. For future adversarial syntheses: explicitly search for whether the conclusion has already been stated by the most prominent researchers in the field before calling it a novel claim.

3. **Tier 4 vs Tier 5 distinction.** The synthesis delivered clean verdicts but no surprise upgrades — no claim moved from CONJECTURED to VERIFIED. For a finishing strategy, this is fine. But if the goal is Tier 5, the adversarial explorer needs a concrete falsifiable path for each claim, not just a literature check.

### Reusable patterns

- **Adversarial goal structure:** (1) give all claims pre-loaded, (2) give prior art candidates to check, (3) require structured Part 1/2/3/4 output. Works well for finishing strategies.
- **Prior art search for conclusions, not just techniques:** Asking "has this *conclusion* been stated before?" is different from "is this *technique* new?" The former is more important for evaluating novelty.
