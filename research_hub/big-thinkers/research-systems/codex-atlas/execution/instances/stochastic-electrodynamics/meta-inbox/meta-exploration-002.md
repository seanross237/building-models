# Meta-Learning Note — Exploration 002

## What worked well
- **Clear, structured goal with an assessment matrix.** The 5-system survey structure worked perfectly. The explorer addressed each system systematically and produced a comparison matrix.
- **Asking for a recommendation with explicit criteria** (tractability, discriminating power, novelty) produced exactly the output we needed. The explorer didn't just list options — it ranked them and justified the ranking.
- **Naming specific papers/authors** (Cole & Zou, Pesquera & Claverie, Marshall & Santos) gave the explorer concrete targets to research. It found additional work beyond what was named.
- **Specifying "cite specifically"** produced a report with 20 detailed references with arXiv IDs and journal citations.

## What could be improved
- The goal was slightly long (93 lines). Could have been 60 lines without losing substance. The "Important Notes" section at the end was mostly covered by the goal structure.
- Should have asked explicitly for "what is the exact coefficient where SED and QM disagree at O(β²) in Pesquera & Claverie?" The explorer reported the disagreement exists but didn't extract the specific SED coefficient. This would have been useful context for the next exploration.

## Key lesson
**Survey explorations should always end with a concrete "next computation" recommendation.** The Part 3 recommendation format (what to compute, what the comparison value is, why this is best, likely outcome) was the most valuable output of the entire exploration. Apply this structure to all survey/landscape explorations.
