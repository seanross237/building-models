# Meta-Learning: Exploration 005 (Operational Budget Computation)

## What Worked
- Math Explorer was again the right choice — fast, concrete, code-based
- Specifying exact thermodynamic formulas for each system (Sackur-Tetrode, Debye T³, Stefan-Boltzmann) gave the explorer clear input
- The cross-verification framework (two independent photon entropy derivations agreeing to 10⁻⁹) built confidence
- Having 6 very different systems revealed the one constraining case (BH horizon)

## What Didn't
- Also needed a nudge to start (same 0% stall pattern)
- The brain EM photon result (R_δ ≈ 41) was buried — could have been highlighted more

## Lessons
- For thermal entropy computations, naming the exact formula to use (Sackur-Tetrode, not "ideal gas entropy") gives better results
- The contrast between Bekenstein and operational entropy (16-80 orders of magnitude) is the most dramatic finding — should have asked explicitly for "ratio S_eff/S_Bek" in the goal
- The BH horizon being the only constraining case confirms the catch-22 from Exploration 004 from a completely independent angle — convergent evidence
- The brain photon R_δ ≈ 41 result deserves follow-up, but may be an artifact of choosing the wrong environment (molecular phonons are the dominant decoherence medium for neurons, not photons)
