# Exploration 006: Black Hole Horizon Implications — What Does the Classicality Budget Say About Complementarity?

## Goal

The classicality budget has one regime where it produces physically meaningful results: **near black hole horizons.** Using operationally relevant entropy (Hawking radiation), we found that S_Hawking ≈ 0.003 bits near a solar-mass BH, giving R_δ ≈ −1 — meaning **no classical reality in the quantum Darwinism sense is possible near the horizon using only Hawking radiation.**

Explore what this means for the black hole information paradox, black hole complementarity, and the firewall debate. Is the classicality budget saying something non-trivial about these foundational problems?

## Context from Previous Explorations

1. **The formula R_δ ≤ S_max/S_T − 1 is correct** (Exploration 001) — derived from tensor product Hilbert space, Zurek's redundancy, Bekenstein bound, Holevo bound.

2. **With Bekenstein bound**: BH gives S_max ~ 10^77 bits, R_δ ~ 10^77 — absurdly generous. But this is the TOTAL information capacity, not the accessible one.

3. **With Hawking radiation entropy** (Exploration 005): Near a solar-mass BH, the Hawking radiation environment gives S_Hawking ≈ 0.003 bits in the near-horizon region. The Hawking photon wavelength (~0.3 AU) vastly exceeds the Schwarzschild radius (2.95 km), so there's typically less than 10⁻³ photons near the horizon. R_δ ≈ −1 for ALL fact sizes.

4. **The catch-22** (Exploration 004): The tensor product assumption in the derivation breaks down in quantum gravity regimes — precisely where the budget is physically interesting.

5. **Tank (2025)** gives the structural formula in abstract QD but doesn't connect to gravity. No one has combined QD with Bekenstein in this context.

## Your Task

### Part A: Physical Implications Near BH Horizons

1. **Interpret the R_δ ≈ −1 result.** What does it physically mean that the Hawking radiation environment cannot support even a single redundant copy of any classical fact? Specifically:
   - Does this mean that no external observer can use Hawking radiation to establish classical facts about the near-horizon region?
   - How does this connect to the "no-hair theorem" — the idea that external observers can only know mass, charge, and angular momentum of a BH?
   - Is this a new way of stating an old result, or does it add content?

2. **Connection to black hole complementarity.** BH complementarity (Susskind, 't Hooft) says that infalling and external observers see different physics, but no single observer sees a contradiction. The classicality budget says:
   - External observers using Hawking radiation: R_δ ≈ −1 (no classical reality possible)
   - External observers far from the BH: R_δ >> 1 (classical reality abundant)
   - Does the budget provide a quantitative version of complementarity? Can it say WHERE the transition happens as you approach the horizon?

3. **Connection to the firewall paradox.** AMPS (2012) argued that either (a) there's a "firewall" at the horizon, (b) BH complementarity fails, or (c) the no-drama principle fails. Does the classicality budget add anything to this debate?
   - The budget says "no QD-objectivity near the horizon." Is this equivalent to saying "no classical drama" (supporting the no-drama principle)?
   - Or does it say something more specific?

4. **Connection to the information paradox.** If the Hawking radiation environment has S ≈ 0.003 bits per near-horizon region, how does information get OUT of the BH during evaporation? Does the budget constrain the rate of information release?

### Part B: Is This Non-Trivial?

For EACH implication above, assess honestly:
- Is this just a restatement of something already known (like no-hair or complementarity)?
- Does the classicality budget formulation add any NEW prediction or constraint?
- Is there a specific quantitative prediction that differs from existing results?

**The bar for novelty:** A result is novel if it makes a PREDICTION that the Bekenstein bound alone, or the no-hair theorem alone, does not make. If every implication can be derived more simply from existing results, the BH application is not adding value.

### Part C: Evaporation Track

Compute (or estimate) how the classicality budget evolves as a BH evaporates:
- At what BH mass does S_Hawking first exceed 1 bit near the horizon?
- This defines a "classicality onset mass" — below this mass, the Hawking radiation environment can support at least one redundant copy of a 1-bit fact
- Is this mass close to the Planck mass? Higher? Lower?
- What does this say about the endpoint of BH evaporation?

### Part D: Connection to Page Time

- The Page time is when half the BH entropy has been radiated. After the Page time, the radiated Hawking photons (collectively, not individually) begin to carry information about the BH interior.
- Does the classicality budget say something about objectivity AFTER the Page time? The early radiation has very low individual entropy, but the collective radiation has high entropy.
- Is there a "classicality phase transition" at the Page time?
