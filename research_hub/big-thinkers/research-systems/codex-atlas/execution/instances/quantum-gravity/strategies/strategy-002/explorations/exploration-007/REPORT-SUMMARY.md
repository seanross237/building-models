# Exploration 007 Summary: Non-Perturbative QG+F — The Gravitational Confinement Analogy

## Goal
Investigate how far the QCD-gravity analogy extends and identify non-perturbative predictions invisible to perturbative QG+F.

## What Was Tried
Systematically investigated: (1) the QCD-QG+F analogy (Holdom-Ren ghost confinement conjecture), (2) non-perturbative AS predictions (Bonanno-Reuter black holes, singularity resolution, vacuum condensate, inflaton-free inflation), (3) gravitational bound states (graviballs, dark matter candidates), (4) gravitational phase transitions (CDT phase diagram, spectral dimension crossover), (5) novel non-perturbative predictions absent from perturbative QG+F.

## Outcome: SUCCEEDED — Clear map of non-perturbative predictions

### Key findings:

**The QCD analogy is structurally tight but dynamically unproven.** Ghost confinement (Holdom-Ren) is the central conjecture: the spin-2 ghost is removed from the physical spectrum by non-perturbative effects, with the fakeon prescription being the perturbative shadow of this confinement. The Planck mass plays the role of Λ_QCD. However, no proof of gravitational ghost confinement exists, and key QCD features (compact gauge group, color quantum numbers) don't map cleanly.

**Four distinct predictions invisible to perturbative QG+F identified:**
1. **Planck-mass black hole remnants** — Hawking evaporation terminates at T→0 remnant (from non-perturbative running G→0 in UV). Potential dark matter candidate.
2. **Power-law running of G at cosmological scales** — From the gravitational vacuum condensate (Hamber, lattice gravity). Running is power-law, not the negligible logarithmic running of perturbative QG+F.
3. **Inflation without an inflaton** — Driven by the Reuter fixed point, predicts r up to ~0.01 (vs QG+F's r < 0.004). Distinguishable by next-generation CMB experiments.
4. **Higgs mass from UV boundary condition** — Shaposhnikov-Wetterich predicted m_H ≈ 126 GeV (2010, before discovery). Requires non-perturbative fixed-point structure.

**Phase transitions:** CDT lattice gravity shows a rich phase diagram (phases A, B, C) with a second-order B-C transition as candidate UV critical point. The d_s=4→2 flow may be a genuine phase transition, not just a crossover. A mass gap in non-perturbative R² gravity has been demonstrated via Dyson-Schwinger equations (2025).

## Key Takeaway
The non-perturbative sector makes testable predictions that perturbative QG+F cannot reproduce. The sharpest discriminator is the tensor-to-scalar ratio: AS inflation predicts r up to ~0.01, QG+F predicts r < 0.004. CMB-S4 and LiteBIRD can distinguish these in the 2030s. The open question remains whether QG+F and AS are truly the same theory in different regimes or genuinely distinct — the SWY (2022) two-fixed-point result leaves this unresolved.

## Leads Worth Pursuing
- Compute the tensor-to-scalar ratio from AS inflation more precisely — this is the most testable discriminator
- The Hamber vacuum condensate prediction for power-law running G deserves detailed comparison with cosmological data
- Explore whether the CDT B-C phase transition can be mapped to ghost confinement in the continuum
- The March 2026 IHO (inverted harmonic oscillator) interpretation of the ghost (arXiv:2603.07150) claims to resolve CMB anomalies — worth scrutinizing
