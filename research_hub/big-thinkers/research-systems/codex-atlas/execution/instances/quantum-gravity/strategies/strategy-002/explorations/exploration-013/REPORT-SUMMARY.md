# Exploration 013 Summary: Dimensional Transmutation and the Cosmological Constant in Agravity/QG+F

**Goal:** Investigate whether agravity's dimensional transmutation mechanism (which generates M_P) can also predict Λ, and assess a "cascading dimensional transmutation" mechanism.

**What Was Tried:** Analyzed agravity's Coleman-Weinberg mechanism for M_P generation, calculated whether the effective potential can produce Λ ~ 10⁻¹²²M_Pl⁴, reviewed Salvio's 2024 unimodular QG+F treatment, evaluated cascading exponential suppressions, and checked the numerology of multi-stage dimensional transmutation.

**Outcome: Clear negative result. The CC cannot be predicted within agravity/QG+F.**

Key findings:
1. **Agravity's CW mechanism gives Λ = 0 at tree level** — the conditions λ_S(s)=0 and β_{λ_S}(s)=0 ensure V_E vanishes at the minimum. This solves the old CC problem but predicts Λ = 0, not 10⁻¹²².
2. **One-loop corrections give Λ ~ f₂⁴M_Pl⁴ ~ 10⁻³⁴M_Pl⁴** — 88 orders of magnitude too large. Scale invariance is necessary but insufficient (Kugo 2020).
3. **Salvio (2024) invokes unimodular gravity + anthropics** — the state-of-the-art for agravity's CC. Old CC solved (vacuum energy decouples); new CC requires anthropic selection.
4. **Cascading transmutation fails physically** — while four exponential suppressions exp(-c/g²) can match 10⁻³⁰ numerically, QG+F has NO coupling that confines or runs to strong coupling at ~meV scales. The mechanism has no physical basis in QG+F.
5. **Maggiore's nonlocal gravity (2015)** is the closest existing idea — R² coupling transmutation generates m ~ H₀ — but goes beyond QG+F and doesn't predict the value.

**Key Takeaway:** The CC problem is definitively the Achilles heel of QG+F/agravity. No mechanism within the framework can predict Λ ~ 10⁻¹²². The best the theory can do: unimodular formulation (old CC solved) + anthropic reasoning (new CC). This puts QG+F on equal footing with every other continuum QG approach — none predicts the CC. The cascading transmutation idea is creative numerology but lacks a physical mechanism within QG+F.

**Leads:** The one constructive insight is that if f₀'s RG flow could be made asymptotically free (by non-perturbative effects or modified matter content), Maggiore's nonlocal gravity mechanism would become predictive within a QG+F-like framework. This would require non-perturbative lattice calculations of the f₀ beta function — a well-defined but technically demanding project.
