# Exploration 012 Summary: QG+F and the Standard Model

**Goal:** Investigate how QG+F connects to particle physics — matter coupling constraints, Shaposhnikov-Wetterich Higgs mass prediction, agravity program, fakeon collider phenomenology, and novel connections (GUTs, dark matter, baryogenesis, strong CP).

**Outcome: SUCCEEDED — Comprehensive map of QG+F ↔ SM connections established.**

## Key Findings

1. **QG+F does NOT predict the SM gauge group or constrain matter field numbers.** The f₂ (Weyl-squared) coupling is asymptotically free for ANY matter content — all spin species contribute with the same negative sign. More fields = more AF. The gravitational sector accepts any gauge group. Strict renormalizability (Anselmi 2025) selects among theories but does not derive gauge structure.

2. **The Shaposhnikov-Wetterich Higgs mass prediction (m_H ≈ 126 GeV, published 2010) is the single most impressive success of the QG+F/AS program.** Mechanism: AS boundary condition drives λ_H → 0 at the UV fixed point; running down gives m_H = 126 GeV. This is a NON-PERTURBATIVE AS result — QG+F is consistent with it but cannot derive it perturbatively. It explains SM vacuum near-criticality.

3. **Agravity (Salvio-Strumia) IS QG+F** with classical scale invariance plus the SM. All masses from dimensional transmutation. Hierarchy problem addressed via technical naturalness (small f₀, f₂ ≲ 10⁻⁸). Salvio (2016) showed that adding 3 right-handed neutrinos below the EW scale addresses neutrino oscillations, dark matter, baryogenesis, AND strong CP simultaneously. CC old problem solved in unimodular version; new CC problem unsolved.

4. **Fakeon collider phenomenology is severely limited.** The gravitational spin-2 fakeon is near Planck mass — unreachable at colliders. Fakeons produce NO missing energy, NO resonance peaks, and evade Z-pole constraints. Observable effects only at loop level: modified h→γγ and Higgs trilinear coupling (fake doublet model). Peak uncertainty ΔE ≈ Γ_f/2 is a novel prediction but essentially untestable for gravitational fakeons.

5. **Grand unification within AS is the most promising future direction.** Eichhorn & Held (2018, 2020) showed AS boundary conditions can predict the fine structure constant and convert free GUT parameters into predictions. This is actively developing and could yield the next confirmed prediction after the Higgs mass.

**Key Takeaway:** QG+F/AS provides the ONLY quantum gravity framework with a confirmed particle physics prediction (Higgs mass). The perturbative QG+F framework is not very constraining on the SM — the strong constraints come from the non-perturbative AS regime (UV fixed point boundary conditions). The most promising path forward for new predictions is asymptotically safe grand unification. Fakeon collider phenomenology is interesting theoretically but has very limited near-term testability.
