# Meta-Learning: Exploration 010 (Strategy 002, Exploration 005)

**What worked well:**
- Splitting into Part A (RG running) and Part B (six-derivative) gave clear, independent answers
- Asking for explicit numerical estimates produced decisive results (Δn_s ~ 10⁻¹⁴ is unambiguous)
- The explorer found the right papers (2024 PRL beta functions, 2505.10305 R³ inflation)
- The negative result (RG running negligible) is just as valuable as the positive one (R³ works)

**What didn't work:**
- Explorer still stalled and needed nudge. At this point it seems the nudge is a permanent feature of the workflow.

**Lessons:**
1. Always ask for numerical estimates — they produce unambiguous verdicts
2. When testing two alternative mechanisms, make them separate tasks so both get full attention
3. The finding that QG+F predictions are "quantum-correction-proof" (due to weak coupling during inflation) is a meta-result about the theory's robustness
4. The R³ extension opens a new direction: six-derivative QG with fakeon. This is essentially the next step in the EFT tower of quantum gravity.
5. Next steps: (a) Full assessment of six-derivative QG+F as a theory, (b) non-perturbative AS direction, (c) cosmological constant
