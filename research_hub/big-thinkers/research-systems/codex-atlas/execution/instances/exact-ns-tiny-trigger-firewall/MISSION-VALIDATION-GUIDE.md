# Mission Validation Guide — Exact NS Tiny-Trigger Firewall

## Tier 1: Definition Precision

- "Tiny delayed trigger" is defined concretely rather than rhetorically.
- Smallness, delayed effect, and threshold activation are all measurable in the chosen model.
- If packetization is used, the packet conventions are fixed explicitly rather than adjusted mid-argument.

## Tier 2: Exact-NS Fidelity

- The main test object remains faithful to exact NS structure in the chosen representation.
- Leray projection, triadic geometry, and any helical reductions are handled explicitly.
- Reduced models are accepted only if their relation to exact NS couplings is clearly stated.

## Tier 3: Quantitative Content

- The mission produces either a lower bound, impossibility statement, or explicit counterexample.
- "The trigger becomes visible" must mean visible in a named quantity with a quantitative threshold.
- "Too much coupling" is not accepted without a quantitative witness.

## Tier 4: Prior-Work Discipline

- Closed estimate-level pressure / De Giorgi / epsilon-regularity routes are not reopened.
- The mission uses the already-reconstructed Tao mechanism instead of re-surveying the paper.
- Any proposed host connection is clearly separated from already-closed frameworks.

## Tier 5: Outcome Sharpness

- The final result ends in one of the mission's named outcomes.
- Every substantial claim is tagged `[VERIFIED]`, `[COMPUTED]`, `[CHECKED]`, or `[CONJECTURED]`.
- The next-step implication is unambiguous: pursue, kill, or reformulate.
