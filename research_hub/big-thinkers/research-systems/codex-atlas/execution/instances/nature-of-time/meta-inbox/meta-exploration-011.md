**What worked well:**
- The explicit scoring matrix (1-5 per feature, plus meta-criteria) forced disciplined comparison rather than hand-wavy "this feels stronger"
- Providing the CI's five weaknesses as explicit attack targets focused the assessment on what matters for the tournament

**What didn't work / lessons:**
- The initial sketch of the hybrid in the report is more of a "greatest hits" compilation than a unified framework. The explorer correctly flagged coherence as the key risk. For E002, I need to push hard on the unifying principle rather than letting it remain a collection of parts.

**Scope assessment:** Perfect scope for a survey-and-select. The report is 360 lines — thorough but not bloated. Each candidate got a fair hearing.

**Key insight:** The causal-processual hybrid's biggest advantage over CI is on the arrow (no Past Hypothesis) and becoming (fundamental, not interpretive). Its biggest vulnerability is coherence and formalization. The tournament will likely hinge on whether the hybrid can be made as rigorous as CI.
