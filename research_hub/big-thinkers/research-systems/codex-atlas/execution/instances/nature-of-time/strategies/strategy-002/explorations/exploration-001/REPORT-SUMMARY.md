# Exploration 001 Summary: Survey and Select the Challenger

## Goal
Survey 4+ non-computational interpretations of time, score them rigorously, and select the strongest challenger to the computational interpretation.

## What Was Tried
Assessed five candidate interpretations across all six features of time (one-dimensionality, irreversibility, directionality, open future, flow, dilation), plus meta-criteria (physics grounding, strength against CI weaknesses, vulnerability, novelty). Candidates: (A) Causal Fundamentalism (Maudlin/Sorkin), (B) Thermodynamic Realism (Prigogine), (C) Experience-First (Bergson/Husserl/IIT), (D) Causal-Processual Hybrid (Whitehead + CST + Prigogine + Smolin), (E1) Smolin's Temporal Naturalism standalone, (E2) Retrocausal/TSVF. Also identified that Rovellian relational time is too close to CI to serve as a genuine challenger.

## Outcome: Selected — Causal-Processual Hybrid (D)

**Grand total scores:** D (36) > A (35) > C (30) > E1 (27) > B (26). E2 disqualified (9).

The Causal-Processual Hybrid wins because it's the only candidate that scores 3+ on ALL six features. Its core claim: "Time is the irreversible causal process of becoming — the ongoing generation of novel actualities from the integration of causal past and open possibility." It combines causal structure (CST) for one-dimensionality and directionality, process metaphysics (Whitehead) for flow and the open future, and generalized irreversibility for the arrow — all without needing the Past Hypothesis.

## Key Takeaway

The hybrid is the strongest challenger because of its *coverage* — it has something substantive to say about every feature of time, unlike competitors that are strong on 2-3 features but blank on others. Its biggest risk is *coherence*: it draws on four traditions (Whitehead, CST, Prigogine, Smolin) that weren't designed to fit together. Exploration 002 must find a unifying principle — the top candidate is "actualization" (potential → actual) as the single primitive — and begin mathematical formalization. A 2025 paper (Baron & Le Bihan) arguing that CST involves genuine actual causation (elements bringing each other into existence) suggests the Whitehead-CST connection is more natural than expected.

## Leads for Exploration 002
1. Develop "actualization" as the unifying primitive
2. Formalize actual occasions as CST elements with processual dynamics (category theory?)
3. Articulate the "now" as relational (each occasion IS a now for itself)
4. Engage head-on with the objection that "becoming" is metaphysical projection, using CST's actual causation result
5. Build out all six feature explanations to CI-level precision
