# Exploration History

## Exploration 001: Survey and Select the Challenger

**Goal:** Survey 4+ non-computational interpretations of time, score them rigorously, and select the strongest challenger to the computational interpretation.

**What Was Tried:** Assessed five candidate interpretations across all six features of time (one-dimensionality, irreversibility, directionality, open future, flow, dilation), plus meta-criteria (physics grounding, strength against CI weaknesses, vulnerability, novelty). Candidates: (A) Causal Fundamentalism (Maudlin/Sorkin), (B) Thermodynamic Realism (Prigogine), (C) Experience-First (Bergson/Husserl/IIT), (D) Causal-Processual Hybrid (Whitehead + CST + Prigogine + Smolin), (E1) Smolin's Temporal Naturalism standalone, (E2) Retrocausal/TSVF. Also identified that Rovellian relational time is too close to CI to serve as a genuine challenger.

**Outcome: Selected — Causal-Processual Hybrid (D)**

Grand total scores: D (36) > A (35) > C (30) > E1 (27) > B (26). E2 disqualified (9).

The Causal-Processual Hybrid wins because it's the only candidate that scores 3+ on ALL six features. Its core claim: "Time is the irreversible causal process of becoming — the ongoing generation of novel actualities from the integration of causal past and open possibility." It combines causal structure (CST) for one-dimensionality and directionality, process metaphysics (Whitehead) for flow and the open future, and generalized irreversibility for the arrow — all without needing the Past Hypothesis.

**Key Takeaway:** The hybrid is the strongest challenger because of its *coverage* — it has something substantive to say about every feature of time, unlike competitors that are strong on 2-3 features but blank on others. Its biggest risk is *coherence*: it draws on four traditions that weren't designed to fit together. Exploration 002 must find a unifying principle — the top candidate is "actualization" (potential → actual) as the single primitive — and begin mathematical formalization.

## Exploration 002: Deep Construction of the Causal-Processual Interpretation

**Goal:** Build the Causal-Processual Interpretation in full: find the unifying principle, develop all six feature-explanations to full depth, and strengthen the weak points (flow, dilation, the "now").

**Outcome: Succeeded**

The interpretation is now a unified, coherent framework organized around a single primitive: **actualization** — the irreversible creative transition from indeterminate possibility to determinate fact. The four traditions (CST, Whitehead, Prigogine, Smolin) are no longer separate modules but five perspectives on this single operation. Heisenberg's potentia (quantum measurement as potentiality→actuality) provides independent support from quantum foundations.

**Scores improved:** Irreversibility: 4→5, Flow: 3→4, Dilation: 3→4. Total: 23→26/30.

**Strongest features vs. CI:** (1) No Past Hypothesis needed for directionality. (2) Genuine phenomenological account of flow via the concrescence-specious-present bridge (Husserl mapped onto Whitehead). (3) Ontological "now" — present = active becoming, past = settled, future = potential. (4) Novel predictions (CST discreteness, cosmological natural selection).

**CI's advantages remain:** (1) Stronger physics grounding (ML, LBB, Wolpert are established results vs. speculative CST + philosophical Whitehead). (2) Less metaphysically exposed. (3) More parsimonious.

**Remaining weaknesses:** Mixed physics grounding, panexperientialism tension, no mathematical formalization, convergence risk with CI ("is actualization just computation redescribed?").

**Key Takeaway:** The causal-processual interpretation is now a genuine competitor. The tournament should be genuinely contested — CI is safer and better-grounded; CP is deeper on directionality, flow, and the "now."

## Exploration 005: Build the Meta-Interpretation — Time as Irreversible Determination

**Goal:** Build the strongest possible synthesis of CI and CP.

**Outcome: SUCCEEDED — scores 57.0/65 (vs CI 47.5, CP 48.5)**

**Core claim:** "Time is irreversible determination — the ongoing, physically constrained process by which the indeterminate becomes determinate, as encountered from within."

The key insight: CI described the *constraints* on determination (LBB, ML, Bekenstein, Wolpert). CP described the *ontology* of determination (actualization, real potentialities). "Computation" and "actualization" are two aspects of one thing: determination.

**Three tensions resolved:** (1) Computation vs. actualization = constraint-level vs. ontology-level of same process. (2) Arrow: LBB provides mechanism, intrinsic direction of determination provides direction, Past Hypothesis reframed as "minimal initial determination." (3) "Now": CI's computational frontier = CP's locus of active determination; CP's ontology gives the frontier its weight.

**Honest limits:** Hard problem remains, ontological commitment to real potentialities is genuine, no novel predictions, factorization problem persists, "natural initial condition" is reframing not derivation.
