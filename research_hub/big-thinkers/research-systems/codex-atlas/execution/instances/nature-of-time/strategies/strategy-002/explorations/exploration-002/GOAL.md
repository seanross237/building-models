# Exploration 002: Deep Construction of the Causal-Processual Interpretation

## Mission Context

We are building a challenger interpretation of time to compete against the "computational interpretation" which claims: "Time is the computationally irreducible processing of quantum correlations, as encountered from within a relational perspective."

In Exploration 001, we surveyed five candidate interpretations and selected the **Causal-Processual Hybrid** as the strongest challenger. Its initial sketch:

**Core claim:** "Time is the irreversible causal process of becoming — the ongoing generation of novel actualities from the integration of causal past and open possibility."

**Components:** Combines causal structure (CST/Maudlin), process metaphysics (Whitehead), physical irreversibility (Prigogine), and temporal naturalism (Smolin).

**Initial feature scores (E001):** One-dimensionality: 4, Irreversibility: 4, Directionality: 5, Open future: 4, Flow: 3, Dilation: 3. Total: 23/30.

**Key weakness identified:** Coherence — draws on four traditions that weren't designed to fit together. Needs a unifying principle.

## Your Task

Build the Causal-Processual Interpretation in full. This means three things:

### 1. Find the Unifying Principle

The hybrid cannot be a grab-bag of four traditions. It needs a single core idea that makes the components cohere. E001 suggested "actualization" (the transition from potential to actual) as the candidate unifying primitive. Evaluate this and develop it — or replace it with something better.

Key questions to answer:
- What IS the single primitive operation? (Actualization? Causal generation? Creative synthesis?)
- How do the four traditions (CST, Whitehead, Prigogine, Smolin) relate to this primitive?
- Is there a recent paper or framework that provides the integration? (E001 found Baron & Le Bihan 2025 arguing CST involves genuine actual causation.)

The test: if you can explain the entire interpretation in terms of ONE primitive that the four traditions are different aspects of, you've succeeded.

### 2. Build Each Feature-Explanation to Full Depth

For each of the six features of time, provide:
- The causal-processual explanation (not just a sketch — a full argument)
- The physics anchor (what established physics grounds this claim?)
- How it compares to the computational interpretation's explanation
- Its honest limits

The computational interpretation's explanations for reference:
- **One-dimensionality:** Sequential processing (Margolus-Levitin bound)
- **Irreversibility:** Information erasure in finite universe (Landauer-Bennett-Bekenstein triad)
- **Directionality:** LBB engine + Past Hypothesis (engine = mechanism, PH = fuel)
- **Open future:** Computational irreducibility + Wolpert's impossibility
- **Flow:** CI from inside bounded subsystem
- **Dilation:** ML bound + gravitational redshift

Your explanations should be AT LEAST as detailed and physics-grounded as these.

### 3. Strengthen the Weak Points

E001 scored the hybrid 3/5 on flow and 3/5 on dilation. These need to be strengthened:

**Flow:** The hybrid says "flow IS becoming/concrescence." But this needs more depth. Consider incorporating insights from the experience-first tradition (which scored 5/5 on flow):
- Husserl's specious present (retentions/protentions structure)
- The logical argument that flow is performatively undeniable
- Bergson's durée as the lived quality of processual becoming
Can you build a flow account that combines processual becoming with phenomenological precision?

**Dilation:** The hybrid says "dilation = varying density of causal events along worldlines." This needs physics grounding. Explore:
- How CST's "number of elements = volume" gives causal event density
- Whether the Myrheim-Meyer dimension formula provides a structural argument
- How proper time along a worldline relates to the number of causal set elements

**The "now":** E001's assessment was "somewhat better than CI but not resolved." Can the interpretation do better? Consider: each actual occasion IS a now while it is concrescing. The present is not a slice of time but an event of becoming. This relational/perspectival "now" avoids the need for a global present. Push this idea further.

## What NOT To Do

- Don't spend time on mathematical formalization (category theory, etc.) — that's premature
- Don't write a literature review — build the interpretation
- Don't try to prove Whitehead or Prigogine "right" — synthesize their insights into something new
- Don't ignore weaknesses — identify them honestly

## Success Criteria

- A unified, coherent interpretation organized around a single core principle
- All six features explained with physics grounding (not just hand-waving)
- Flow and dilation explanations substantially improved from E001's sketches
- The interpretation is a genuine competitor to CI — if you've built it properly, a thoughtful reader should feel genuine tension about which is stronger

## Failure Criteria

- The interpretation is still a grab-bag of four traditions without unity
- Feature explanations are at the same depth as E001's initial sketch (they need to be deeper)
- The flow or dilation accounts remain vague
