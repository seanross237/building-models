## Exploration 001: Survey and Select the Challenger

### What I considered

Strategy 002 is a Challenger Tournament. Phase 1 is about building the strongest non-computational interpretation of time to compete against Strategy 001's computational interpretation. Exploration 001 is the survey and selection step.

The computational interpretation's five acknowledged weaknesses:
1. **The "now"** — structural past/future asymmetry but not why any point IS the present (weakest point)
2. **Factorization problem** — presupposes well-defined subsystems
3. **Arrow direction** — needs Past Hypothesis for direction, LBB only gives mechanism
4. **No novel predictions** — purely interpretive
5. **Flow claim is interpretive** — "CI IS flow" is philosophical identification, not deduction

A strong challenger must be strong where the computational interpretation is weak. I'm considering:

**Option A: Causal fundamentalism** — Time IS causal order (Maudlin, CST, interventionism). Strong on: "the now" (causal frontier), arrow (causal order is directed by definition, no Past Hypothesis needed), avoids factorization (causal structure is primitive). Weak on: one-dimensionality, time dilation. Grounded in real physics (CST).

**Option B: Thermodynamic realism** — Time is fundamental irreversibility (Prigogine). Strong on: arrow (fundamental, not statistical), flow (becoming via dissipative processes). Weak on: one-dimensionality, "the now," time dilation. Has a Nobel laureate's program behind it.

**Option C: Experience-first** — Time is a feature of consciousness (IIT, phenomenology, Bergson). Strong on: "the now," flow. Very weak on: physics grounding, one-dimensionality, time dilation, arrow.

**Option D: Hybrid causal-processual** — Combine Maudlin/CST causal order with Whitehead's process philosophy and Prigogine's irreversibility. Could potentially cover all the weaknesses.

### What I chose and why

I'm asking the explorer to do a genuine survey of all four (plus any alternative it identifies), score each against the six time-features, and select the most promising one to begin building. The strategy explicitly calls for this survey-then-select approach.

My intuition: causal fundamentalism (Option A) or the hybrid (Option D) are most likely to win. But I want the explorer to make this call after rigorous assessment, not me pre-committing.

### What I rejected and why

I rejected pre-selecting a challenger myself. The strategy says exploration 001 should survey and select. The explorer doing fresh web research may find a framing I haven't considered.

I'm NOT asking for mathematical formalization yet — that's a distraction at this stage. The priority is a unified, coherent philosophical framework with physics grounding.

Rejected circularity attack (already addressed in Strategy 001 for the CI side — same issue applies to all interpretations). Rejected unfalsifiability (CP actually has more predictions than CI via CST). Rejected trying to do too many attacks — four is enough per meta-learning.
