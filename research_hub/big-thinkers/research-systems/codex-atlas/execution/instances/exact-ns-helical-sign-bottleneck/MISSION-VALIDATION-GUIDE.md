# Mission Validation Guide — Exact NS Helical Sign-Bottleneck

## Tier 1: Canonicity

- The packet object is frozen before theorem claims are made.
- Packet families, conjugate/sign bookkeeping, desired edges, and leakage accounting do not change under harmless representative choices.
- The sign functionals are canonical enough that a failed theorem would still be meaningful.

## Tier 2: Exact-NS Fidelity

- The mission stays anchored to exact NS helical-triad bookkeeping.
- Any reduced packet object states explicitly which exact features it preserves and which it discards.
- The mission does not silently collapse back to the already-killed singleton exact circuit.

## Tier 3: Quantitative Content

- The mission computes or proves at least one nontrivial packet-level relation among `Leak`, the global sign-coherence defect, and the desired-edge heterochirality defect.
- Any claimed bottleneck is stated as an inequality, incompatibility statement, or explicit minimization/counterexample.
- Purely qualitative claims about homochiral or heterochiral behavior are not enough.

## Tier 4: Prior-Work Discipline

- Closed estimate-level routes are not reopened in disguise.
- The first failed clustered packet model is treated as prior negative evidence, not ignored.
- The singleton sign-defect prototype is used only as a clue, not as theorem evidence by itself.

## Tier 5: Outcome Sharpness

- The final report ends in one of the mission's named outcomes.
- Every substantial claim is tagged `[VERIFIED]`, `[COMPUTED]`, `[CHECKED]`, or `[CONJECTURED]`.
- The recommendation is unambiguous: pursue, kill, or reformulate.
