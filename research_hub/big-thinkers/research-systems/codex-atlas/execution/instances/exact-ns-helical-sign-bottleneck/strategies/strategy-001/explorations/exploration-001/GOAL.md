## Exact Question

Can one freeze a preferred packet/sign object with all of the following fixed in a way that does not move under harmless packet refinements, representative relabelings, or equivalent bookkeeping choices?

1. one preferred packet family for the five Tao-like roles,
2. one support/refinement convention,
3. one conjugate-pair representative quotient or equally canonical sign-bookkeeping rule,
4. one desired Tao-like role graph,
5. one exact-ledger leakage functional `Leak(I)`,
6. one global sign-coherence functional `SD_part(I)`,
7. one desired-edge heterochirality functional `SD_target(I)`,
8. one activation-window convention,
9. one exact-vs-tolerance ledger separating exact NS data from user-chosen parameters.

The central risk is inherited from the predecessor mission: packetization previously failed because support choice, packet projection, leakage scalarization, and restored exact network remained materially open. The central possible repair is the new two-functional split `SD_part` / `SD_target`. Test whether that split genuinely stabilizes the object or whether it only renames the old freedom.

## Required Deliverables

Produce exactly one of these two outputs:

1. Preferred-object output:
   - one preferred packet/sign object and at most one genuinely inequivalent backup,
   - a short notation table,
   - a canonicity checklist covering support, refinement, representative quotient, desired-edge bookkeeping, leakage scalarization, and both sign functionals,
   - a precise statement of what would count next as positive bottleneck, counterexample, model-level failure, or canonicity failure on this object.
2. Failure output:
   - a clean `canonicity failure` verdict,
   - the minimal list of freedoms that remain load-bearing and non-canonical,
   - an explanation of why the new sign-functional split does not remove those freedoms,
   - and a statement of why later quantitative work would otherwise be testing a moving target.

## Required Checks

1. Treat the first failed packet mission as real negative evidence, not as a soft warning.
2. Keep the analysis on exact NS helical-ledger language. If you use any reduced packet summary, state exactly which exact structures it preserves and which it discards.
3. Test at least one harmless refinement or representative relabeling chosen specifically to threaten canonicity. If the proposed object survives, say why. If it moves, say exactly what moved.
4. Do not collapse `Leak`, `SD_part`, and `SD_target` into one scalar. The point of the exploration is whether the two sign functionals can be made canonical separately.
5. Do not widen into Phase 1 coefficient optimization or packet minimization campaigns. This exploration is only about whether there is one fixed object worth carrying forward.
