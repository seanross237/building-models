# Mission Validation Guide — Far-Field Pressure Harmonic Loophole

## Tier 1: Mathematical Precision

- The exact far-field obstruction is stated in equations, not only prose.
- The pressure pairing, recurrence slot, and exponent arithmetic are all explicit.
- Function spaces and domains are stated precisely.
- The distinction between local and global `H^1` control is maintained sharply.
- Claims about harmonicity are clearly separated from claims about norm improvement.

## Tier 2: Fidelity to Prior Results

- Atlas VP `beta = 4/3` sharpness is cited rather than re-derived.
- Patlas VP `H^1` dead-end findings are cited rather than re-tested from scratch.
- Patlas NS epsilon-regularity universality is used accurately.
- The mission does not quietly drift into a new proof architecture.

## Tier 3: Tao Filter Correctness

- Tao's averaged NS construction is represented accurately.
- The effect of averaging on `-Δp = ∂_i ∂_j (u_i u_j)` is analyzed concretely.
- The report answers whether the far-field harmonic structure survives averaging.
- Any claim of Tao-compatibility or Tao-incompatibility is backed by direct argument or explicit model evidence.

## Tier 4: Falsification Quality

- The mission closes the loophole cleanly if it fails.
- If the loophole survives Step 2, the model in Step 3 is explicit and minimal rather than aspirational.
- Any claimed gain is compared term-by-term against the original obstruction.
- "Improvement" is rejected if it is only a notational reformulation.

## Tier 5: Output Quality

- Negative results are explicit about what is now closed.
- Positive results identify the exact quantitative gain and why it matters.
- Every substantial claim is tagged `[VERIFIED]`, `[COMPUTED]`, `[CHECKED]`, or `[CONJECTURED]`.
- The strongest counterargument is identified and addressed.
- The final report makes the next-step implication unambiguous: closed lead, or precise follow-up.
