## Lesson

When a goal includes synthesis or construction, explicitly ask for specific equations and derivations rather than conceptual architecture. Without this, the explorer produces compelling but schematic synthesis — conceptually organized but lacking the mathematical specificity needed for follow-up work.

## Evidence

- **riemann-hypothesis strategy-001 exploration 001** — The synthesis section was "compelling but schematic." The strategizer noted: "Next time, for a construction exploration, I should ask for specific equations/derivations rather than conceptual architecture."

## Variant: "Derive WHY" Alongside Literature Survey

**NEW (vasseur-pressure E006):** When a computational finding identifies a mechanism (e.g., DNS shows ABC flows have anomalously high beta), the follow-up literature exploration should include an explicit derivation request: "derive WHY this happens." In E006, the derivation (Lamb vector = 0 implies pressure = Bernoulli, 3 lines) provided the rigorous explanation for the DNS outlier — far more valuable than the literature survey alone. The derivation is often brief but provides the mechanism that connects the numerical observation to analytical understanding.

## When to apply

Any exploration where the output will be a constructed framework, theory, or formal synthesis. Not needed for pure surveys or adversarial assessments, where conceptual analysis is the appropriate output format. Also: when a follow-up literature survey investigates a computationally identified mechanism, always include a derivation request.
