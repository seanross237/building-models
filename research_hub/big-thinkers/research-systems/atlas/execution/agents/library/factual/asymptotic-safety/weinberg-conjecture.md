# Weinberg's Asymptotic Safety Conjecture (1976/1979)

Steven Weinberg introduced asymptotic safety as a potential resolution to quantum gravity's divergence problems. The fundamental idea: gravity might achieve ultraviolet safety through a non-perturbative fixed point rather than traditional perturbative renormalizability.

## Original Proposal

According to Weinberg's asymptotic-safety conjecture, gravity could result in a non-perturbatively renormalizable quantum theory if the gravitational renormalization group flow attains an interacting fixed point in the ultraviolet regime. Although originally proposed to find a theory of quantum gravity, the idea of a nontrivial fixed point providing a possible UV completion can be applied also to other field theories, in particular to perturbatively nonrenormalizable ones.

## Key Mathematical Concepts

- **Running Coupling Constants**: Couplings flow according to renormalization group equations, with asymptotically safe theories approaching fixed points at high energies.
- **Fixed Point Structure**: Non-trivial fixed points allow infinite-dimensional theory spaces to reduce to finite-dimensional predictive subsets. The UV-critical hypersurface has finite dimensionality equal to the number of infrared-relevant couplings.
- **Beta Functions**: These determine coupling evolution; asymptotic safety requires specific beta-function behavior near fixed points.

## Distinction from Asymptotic Freedom

In asymptotic freedom (as in QCD), the fixed point is Gaussian (free/non-interacting) -- couplings go to zero at high energies. In asymptotic safety, the fixed point is non-Gaussian (interacting) -- couplings approach finite nonzero values at high energies. Asymptotic freedom is a special case of asymptotic safety.

## Critical Challenges Noted

1. **Truncation Dependence**: All computations rely on truncating coupling space, making extrapolation to the full theory uncertain.
2. **John Donoghue's criticism**: The handling of Newton's constant running exhibits potential problems -- power-divergent corrections vanish in dimensional regularization, different processes yield inconsistent running, and energy-scale definitions prove ambiguous.
3. **Entropy Scaling**: Bekenstein-Hawking entropy's area-law scaling has been argued to contradict local field theory assumptions fundamental to asymptotic safety.
