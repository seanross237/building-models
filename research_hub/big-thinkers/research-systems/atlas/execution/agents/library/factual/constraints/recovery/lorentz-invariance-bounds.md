# Lorentz Invariance Bounds (Constraint B6 / D6)

**Statement:** The theory must preserve local Lorentz invariance, or any violations must be below current experimental bounds.

**Quantitative bounds:**

Modified dispersion relation: E² = p²c² + m²c⁴ ± E² (E/E_QG)^n

| Measurement | Bound | Source |
|-------------|-------|--------|
| Linear (n=1) subluminal | E_QG > 5.9 × E_Pl | GRB 221009A / LHAASO (2024) |
| Linear (n=1) superluminal | E_QG > 6.2 × E_Pl | GRB 221009A / LHAASO (2024) |
| Quadratic (n=2) subluminal | E_QG > 5.8 × 10⁻⁸ E_Pl | LHAASO (2024) |
| DisCan algorithm (n=1) | E_QG/10¹⁹ GeV > 14.6 (subluminal) | 2025 |
| Hausdorff dimension (electron g-2) | d_H > 4 - 5 × 10⁻⁷ | — |
| Hausdorff dimension (Lamb shift) | d_H > 4 - 3.6 × 10⁻¹¹ | — |

**Restrictiveness: MAXIMALLY RESTRICTIVE**

The GRB 221009A results push the linear LIV scale **above the Planck energy**, meaning any Planck-scale Lorentz violation with n=1 dispersion modification is essentially ruled out. This is among the most powerful constraints in all of physics.

## Theories Constrained

- **Horava-Lifshitz gravity:** Explicitly breaks Lorentz invariance; must show violations are suppressed at low energies
- **Some LQG variants:** Certain semiclassical limits predict modified dispersion relations
- **Causal set swerves:** Lorentz invariance is preserved statistically but local violations exist

## Dependency

Genuinely independent — cannot be derived from other constraints.
