# LQG Classical Limit Problem

## The Problem

One of the most significant open problems in LQG is demonstrating that general relativity emerges as the classical (semiclassical, continuum) limit of the theory. Presently, no complete semiclassical limit recovering general relativity has been rigorously shown to exist.

## Specific Technical Difficulties

1. **No infinitesimal diffeomorphism operator**: There is no operator corresponding to infinitesimal spatial diffeomorphisms, because the theory predicts discrete spatial geometry. The diffeomorphisms must be approximated by finite transformations, and so the Poisson bracket structure of the classical theory is not exactly reproduced in the quantum theory.

2. **Discrete-to-continuum transition**: The discrete combinatorial nature of spin network states must somehow give rise to the continuous smooth geometry of classical GR. Nobody has yet demonstrated how to "zoom out" from the pixelated quantum geometry and recover smooth spacetime.

3. **Hamiltonian constraint controversy**: There are serious difficulties arising from the structure of the Poisson brackets involving the spatial diffeomorphism and Hamiltonian constraints. The classical limit of Thiemann's Hamiltonian operator "might fail to yield classical general relativity" — this remains controversial and unresolved.

4. **Dynamics proposals**: Multiple proposals exist for implementing the dynamics (Hamiltonian constraint), and they are intensely debated. No consensus on the correct dynamics has been reached.

## Recent Progress

- Han and Liu (arXiv:1705.09030) proposed a new limit coupling both the semiclassical and continuum limits of spinfoam amplitudes, showing that solutions to Einstein's equations can be approached by dominant configurations in spinfoam amplitudes. This suggests GR emerges in the infrared regime from quantum geometry.

- Work on reduced phase space LQG (arXiv:2005.00988) has shown correct semiclassical limits for a new path integral formulation, indicating semiclassical consistency of this particular quantization.

These are partial results for specific models and formulations, not a general proof.

### Vacuum State Problem
The theory cannot identify its vacuum state -- a fundamental requirement for any quantum theory. Background independence, while a strength conceptually, creates this difficulty: without a fixed background spacetime, there is no natural notion of "empty space" to define a vacuum.

### Time Problem
An additional complication: the quantum theory of spacetime in LQG is fundamentally a quantum theory of space, and the spin network framework has difficulty incorporating time. Leading researchers hold incompatible views -- Smolin considers time fundamental while Rovelli considers time emergent -- a basic disagreement about the theory's interpretation that remains unresolved.

### Master Constraint Approach
The problem of infinitesimal diffeomorphisms can be circumvented with the introduction of the "master constraint," which combines all constraints into a single operator. In the alternative formulation of Algebraic Quantum Gravity (AQG), semiclassical states control the fluctuations of all degrees of freedom, making AQG's semiclassical analysis superior over that of standard LQG.
