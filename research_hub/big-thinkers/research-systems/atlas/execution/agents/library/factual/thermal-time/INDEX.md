# Thermal Time (Connes-Rovelli TTH)

The Connes-Rovelli Thermal Time Hypothesis (TTH): physical time is the modular flow of the quantum state of the universe. This folder covers the mathematical foundation (modular Hamiltonian catalog), the normalization resolution (τ = β·t_modular), the ΔK_A correction structure, the direct full-QM vs. local-TTH comparison (83% L² discrepancy at λ=0.3 — structural, not quantitative), **lattice Rindler/BW verification (all predictions confirmed within discretization limits)**, **non-equilibrium TTH test (post-quench: modular time = pre-quench time; structural spectral mismatch; product-state identity; squeezed state contrast)**, **excited-state modular flow (modular clock ticks at entanglement frequencies, not physical; discrepancy GROWS as ~N^{+0.33} in continuum limit; VERIFIED — Gaussian caveat resolved)**, **Gaussian caveat resolution (squeezed vacuum with EXACT modular Hamiltonian reproduces same mismatch; N^{0.44} scaling; robust across squeezing parameters)**, **adversarial claims assessment (s003-E002: novelty ratings 2.5/1/4/3/4; Claim 3 strongest at 4/5; "preparation-history time" novel at 4/5; Claim 2 demoted to known; three attacks tested — all fail or are weakened)**, and **distance-from-Gibbs characterization (s003-E003: relative entropy does NOT determine TTH discrepancy; discriminant is spectrum preservation — unitary deformations quantitative, non-unitary deformations immediately structural; 22-point survey across squeezed and quench families)**. 10 findings.

## Findings

- **modular-hamiltonian-catalog.md** — Explicit modular Hamiltonians for 4 canonical systems: Rindler wedge (BW theorem: K = 2π K_boost, modular flow = Lorentz boost, TTH exact by theorem here), two-qubit Bell state (K = log(2)·I, trivial modular flow), harmonic oscillator thermal state (K = βH, modular flow = Heisenberg at rate β), CFT vacuum on interval (Casini-Huerta: K = 2π∫[(L-x)x/L]T_{00}dx, non-local, Möbius flow). Key structural insight: modular Hamiltonians range from trivial (maximally mixed) to non-local (CFT); TTH is established (not conjectured) for the Rindler case.

- **tth-normalization-and-discriminating-observable.md** — Normalization resolved: τ_physical = β × t_modular, confirmed by BW theorem and three papers with explicit citations (Connes-Rovelli 1994 eq. 43, Martinetti-Rovelli 2003 eq. 18, Haggard-Rovelli 2013 eq. 13). With correct normalization, TTH = QM exactly at λ=0 (product state); discriminating observable is position autocorrelation period, which differs for entangled states (λ≠0). TTH = QM iff K_A = βH_A + const·I.

- **tth-deltaK-and-period-shift.md** — For bilinearly coupled oscillators in global thermal state ρ_AB = e^{−βH}/Z: ΔK_A = K_A − βH_A ≠ 0 for λ≠0; O(λ²) analytically (O(λ¹) vanishes because ⟨q_B⟩_thermal = 0); band-2 structure (diagonal = temperature renormalization β_eff = β − 1.36λ², off-diagonal = squeezing (a†²+a²)); period shift Δτ/τ ≈ 0.71λ² vs. free oscillator (6.4% at λ=0.3). **CORRECTION:** this period shift was vs. C_free (H_A evolution), not C_full (H_AB evolution) — see tth-full-qm-vs-local-tth.md for the correct comparison.

- **tth-full-qm-vs-local-tth.md** — Direct comparison C_full_QM (H_AB evolution) vs. C_local_TTH (K_A/β evolution). Control check C_global = C_full at machine zero confirms K_AB = βH_AB. Main result: STRUCTURAL discrepancy — C_full has two-frequency normal-mode beating at ω_± = √(ω²±λ), C_local has single frequency ω_eff < ω_A (no beating). L² discrepancy: 9.1% (λ=0.1), 82.7% (λ=0.3), >100% (λ=0.5). Not fixable by parameter tuning. Two TTH interpretations: global TTH (K_AB) ≡ QM by construction; local TTH (K_A) ≠ QM structurally. Best discriminant: ‖C_full − C_local‖/‖C_full‖ (not period shift). Verified: Fock truncation convergence 1 ppb (N=15→N=20).

- **rindler-bw-lattice-verification.md** — Quantitative lattice BW verification: free massless scalar 1+1D, N=50/100/200. BW profile matches boost generator within 0.1% at d≤1.5 (lattice discretization effect, N-independent). Modular-boost correlator converges with N at d=1.5 (23%→19%→15%). KMS condition exact to machine precision (confirming Unruh temperature). Entanglement entropy matches Calabrese-Cardy at 1.5% (slope 0.1642 vs. predicted 0.1667). Key insight: modular flow ≈ boost ≠ time translation. Entanglement spectrum extremely sparse (2 modes carry 91% of entropy).

- **nonequilibrium-tth-post-quench.md** — Non-equilibrium TTH test: post-quench state (thermal state of uncoupled system under coupled dynamics). TTH ≠ QM for all λ>0 (102% at λ=0.3, 174% at λ=0.5). Discrepancy is STRUCTURAL: C_QM has coupled normal-mode frequencies ω_±, C_global has only uncoupled ω — completely disjoint spectra. Modular time = pre-quench time (K encodes preparation Hamiltonian). Product-state identity: C_global = C_local exactly for product states. Squeezed state contrast: global TTH 7.8% (quantitative, correct frequencies) vs. 102% post-quench (structural, wrong frequencies). Exact analytical formulas verified to 3e-13.

- **excited-state-modular-flow.md** — One-particle excitation on N-site lattice. Modular flow oscillates at entanglement spectrum frequencies eps_k/(2π), NOT at physical mode frequency omega_m — target frequency absent from modular response (amplitude ratio 0.0001 at N=100). Discrepancy GROWS in continuum limit: delta_disc ~ N^{+0.33} for fixed physical frequency. Mode-0 apparent convergence (~N^{-0.46}) is an IR artifact. Pi-coupling perturbation ~30% persistent. **VERIFIED** — Gaussian approximation caveat resolved by s003-E001 (see gaussian-caveat-resolution.md).

- **gaussian-caveat-resolution.md** — Resolves the Gaussian approximation caveat on the excited-state finding. Squeezed vacuum S_m(r)|0> — a Gaussian state where the modular Hamiltonian is EXACT — reproduces the same structural mismatch: modular flow at modular frequencies, physical frequency absent. Discrepancy grows as N^{0.44} (up to N=400). Coherent state control confirms pipeline (delta_C_local = constant to machine precision). Squeezing parameter sweep shows mismatch is structural, independent of squeezing strength (discrepancy 13-66 across r=0.1 to 1.0). Physical interpretation: boost vs. translation symmetries are fundamentally different.

- **distance-from-gibbs-characterization.md** — Relative entropy does NOT determine TTH discrepancy: 22-point survey (squeezed r=0–1.0, quench delta_lambda=0–0.5). At comparable S_rel (~0.05), squeezed has 0% discrepancy vs. quench ~140%. Discriminant is spectrum preservation: unitary deformations (squeezed) preserve modular Hamiltonian spectrum → quantitative only (max 6.8%); non-unitary deformations (quench) change spectrum → immediate structural failure (step-function onset at any nonzero delta_lambda). Extends single-point squeezed contrast from E002 to systematic mapping.

- **tth-adversarial-claims-assessment.md** — Systematic adversarial review of all TTH claims: prior art search, three conceptual attacks, null hypothesis testing. **Claim 3 (excited-state modular flow) is the strongest result (novelty 4/5); "preparation-history time" interpretation is novel (4/5); Claim 2 (product-state identity) demoted to known (1/5, Takesaki).** Closest prior art for Claim 3: Lashkari-Liu-Rajagopal 2021 (modular flow operators, but no correlator dynamics comparison). Attack 1 ("TTH not for non-equilibrium") fails — Connes-Rovelli 1994 covers all faithful states. Attack 2 ("modular flow IS correct time") partially succeeds for QG contexts but fails for operationally defined settings. Attack 3 ("lattice ≠ type III") weakened by growing discrepancy with N. Claim priority: 3 > Central > 4 > 1 > 2.
