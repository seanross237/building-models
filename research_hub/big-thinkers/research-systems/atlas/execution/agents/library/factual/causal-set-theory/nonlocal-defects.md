# Spacetime Defects and Nonlocal Effects in Causal Set Theory

## The Defects Ansatz

Carone and Donald (2023) proposed an ansatz for encoding the physics of nonlocal spacetime defects in the Green's functions for a scalar field theory defined on a causal set. They numerically studied the effects of these defects on the discrete Feynman propagator in 1+1 dimensions.

## Key Findings

When spacetime defects are present in the causal set, "two points with the same invariant spacetime interval can have different propagation amplitudes, depending on whether the propagation is between two ordinary spacetime points, two defects, or a defect and an ordinary point."

This means the effective physics depends not just on the causal relationship between events but also on whether the events lie on defects in the underlying discrete structure.

## Renormalization from Defects

A central result: "a coarse-grained description that is only sensitive to the average effect of the defects can be interpreted as a defect-induced mass and wave-function renormalization of the scalar theory."

This demonstrates that what appears as renormalization in continuum QFT could have its origin in the discrete microstructure of spacetime -- the defects in the causal set induce effective mass corrections and wave-function rescalings when viewed at coarse-grained scales.

## Continuum Limit

In the defect-free limit, the discrete propagator approaches the expected continuum results on average as the causal set size increases, confirming consistency with standard physics when defects are absent.

## Broader Significance

This work is part of a broader program exploring how the characteristic nonlocality of causal set theory -- arising from the combination of discreteness and Lorentz invariance -- might produce observable effects. If spacetime defects exist at the Planck scale, their cumulative effects could potentially influence phenomenological models of cosmology and particle physics.
