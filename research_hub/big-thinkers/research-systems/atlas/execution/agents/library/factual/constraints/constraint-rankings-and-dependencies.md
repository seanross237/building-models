# Constraint Rankings and Dependencies

## Top 5 Maximally Restrictive Constraints

1. **UV completion (A5):** Eliminates naive perturbative quantization entirely; forces string theory, asymptotic safety, LQG, or similar escape route.
2. **Unitarity + Ghost freedom (A1 + A2):** Eliminates most higher-derivative gravity theories; creates tension with renormalizability.
3. **Diffeomorphism invariance (A4):** Combined with Weinberg's theorem, uniquely selects the structure of graviton interactions at leading order.
4. **Lorentz invariance bounds (B6/D6):** GRB 221009A linear bounds exceed E_Pl, ruling out O(1) Planck-scale Lorentz violation.
5. **Bekenstein-Hawking entropy (C2):** Any theory must count the correct microscopic degrees of freedom to reproduce S = A/(4G).

## The Key Tension: Renormalizability vs. Unitarity ("The Ghost Problem")

The most important structural tension in quantum gravity:
- Adding R² terms makes gravity renormalizable (Stelle 1977) but introduces ghost modes → unitarity violation
- **String theory** resolves by being UV-finite without higher derivatives in the action
- **Asymptotic safety** attempts non-perturbative resolution — ghosts may be fictitious
- **LQG** sidesteps entirely through non-perturbative, background-independent quantization
- **Infinite derivative gravity (IDG)** uses entire functions to avoid new poles

## Dependency Map

**Independent constraints** (cannot be derived from each other):
- Unitarity (A1)
- Diffeomorphism invariance (A4)
- UV completion (A5)
- Lorentz invariance (B6)
- Equivalence principle (B5)
- Bekenstein-Hawking entropy (C2)
- Graviton mass bounds (D1)
- GW speed (D2)

**Derived/dependent constraints:**
- Ghost freedom (A2) ← unitarity (A1) + positive-definite Hilbert space
- Correct DOF (A3) ← diffeomorphism invariance (A4) + unitarity (A1)
- Newtonian gravity (B1) ← graviton propagator IR limit (B3)
- Linearized Einstein (B2) ← diffeomorphism invariance (A4) + graviton propagator (B3)
- Three-graviton vertex (B4) ← diffeomorphism invariance (A4)
- PPN parameters (B7/D3/D4) ← post-Newtonian expansion of the field equations
- Holographic entropy bounds (C5) ← may follow from Bekenstein-Hawking (C2) + covariant entropy counting
- Page curve (C6) ← unitarity (A1) applied to black holes
- Jacobson derivation (C4) ← Bekenstein-Hawking (C2) + thermodynamics + Rindler structure → uniquely implies Einstein gravity
