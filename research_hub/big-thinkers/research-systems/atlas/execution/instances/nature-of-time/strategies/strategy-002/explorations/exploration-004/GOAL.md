# Exploration 004: Head-to-Head Tournament — CI vs. CP

## Mission Context

We are running a formal tournament between two interpretations of time:

**The Computational Interpretation (CI):**
> "Time is the computationally irreducible processing of quantum correlations, as encountered from within a relational perspective."

Built on: QRF dissolution (Hoehn), Landauer-Bennett-Bekenstein triad, Margolus-Levitin bound, computational irreducibility (Wolfram), Wolpert's impossibility theorem. All established physics. Three-layer architecture: QRF dissolution → computational structure → phenomenology (CI from inside = becoming).

Five acknowledged weaknesses: (1) The "now" — weakest point. (2) Factorization problem. (3) Arrow needs Past Hypothesis. (4) No novel predictions. (5) Flow claim is interpretive.

**The Causal-Processual Interpretation (CP):**
> "Time is the ongoing process of actualization — the irreversible creative transition from indeterminate possibility to determinate fact."

Built on: CST causal structure, Whiteheadian concrescence, Prigoginian irreversibility, Smolinian temporal naturalism, Husserlian temporal phenomenology. Unified around the single primitive of "actualization." Physics grounding mixed (CST speculative, Whitehead philosophical, Prigogine contested).

Scores 25/30 on six features (post-adversarial-attack). Strongest on directionality (no Past Hypothesis), "the now" (ontological), and flow (Husserlian bridge, at Level 2+).

Key fork from CI: ontological status of quantum potentialities. CP says real; CI says epistemic/computational.

## Your Task

**Run a fair, rigorous head-to-head tournament.** You are the IMPARTIAL JUDGE. You do not advocate for either side.

### Part 1: Feature-by-Feature (Six Rounds)

For each of the six features of time, both interpretations present their BEST case. You score each on a 1-5 scale (1 = no explanation, 5 = deep, grounded, compelling explanation). Justify every score.

The six features:
1. **One-dimensionality** — Why time has one dimension
2. **Irreversibility** — Why processes can't run backward
3. **Directionality (arrow)** — Why time has a preferred direction
4. **Open future** — Why the future is undetermined
5. **Flow** — Why time feels like it passes
6. **Dilation** — Why time runs slower near massive objects

### Part 2: Hardest Questions (Four Rounds)

For each of the four hardest questions about time, both interpretations give their best answer. You evaluate which answer is more satisfying for a thoughtful person.

The four questions:
1. **Why does time flow?** — The most basic experiential datum
2. **Why can't we go back?** — The most puzzling physical fact
3. **What is "now"?** — The most philosophically challenging question
4. **Does time exist without observers?** — The deepest metaphysical question

### Part 3: Meta-Criteria (Three Rounds)

Score each on three meta-criteria:
1. **Physics grounding** — How much established physics supports the interpretation?
2. **Explanatory depth** — Does it explain time or just redescribe it?
3. **Boldness with honesty** — Does it take real positions and acknowledge real limits?

### Scoring Rules

- Score each interpretation 1-5 on each criterion (13 criteria total)
- Justify EVERY score with 2-3 sentences
- If the scores are close, say so honestly
- At the end, tally the grand total and declare a winner (or a draw)
- The winner is the interpretation that would leave a thoughtful, scientifically literate person feeling they understood something new about time
