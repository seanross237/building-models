# Exploration 007: Non-Perturbative QG+F — The Gravitational Confinement Analogy

## Mission Context

QG+F (quadratic gravity + fakeon) is asymptotically free in the UV, analogous to QCD. Strategy 001 noted the QCD analogy: perturbative QG+F = perturbative QCD (asymptotic freedom), while AS = non-perturbative QCD (confinement). In QCD, the non-perturbative sector produces hadrons, chiral symmetry breaking, quark-gluon plasma, and a rich phenomenology invisible to perturbation theory. What are the analogues for gravity?

## Your Specific Goal

Investigate the non-perturbative structure of QG+F/AS and identify what predictions or phenomena might be invisible to the perturbative (fakeon) framework.

### Task 1: The QCD-QG+F Analogy — How Far Does It Go?
- In QCD: perturbative = asymptotic freedom, non-perturbative = confinement + hadrons + χSB
- In gravity: perturbative = QG+F (asymptotically free in f₂), non-perturbative = AS (Reuter FP)
- What corresponds to "confinement" in gravity? What would be "confined"?
- What corresponds to "hadrons" — composite gravitational bound states?
- Is there a gravitational analogue of chiral symmetry breaking?
- How seriously should we take this analogy? Where does it break down?

### Task 2: Non-Perturbative AS Predictions
- What predictions does asymptotic safety make that perturbative QG+F does NOT?
- RG-improved black holes (Bonanno-Reuter): What do they predict? Are these invisible to QG+F?
- Singularity resolution: Does AS resolve BH singularities differently from QG+F?
- Graviton condensate: Is there an analogue of the gluon condensate ⟨G²⟩?
- Cosmological predictions: Does AS predict features in the primordial power spectrum beyond what QG+F gives?

### Task 3: Gravitational Bound States
- In QCD, glueballs are bound states of gluons. Are there "graviton glueballs" — gravitational bound states from non-perturbative dynamics?
- In AS, does the graviton self-coupling at the NGFP create bound states?
- Would gravitational bound states be dark matter candidates?
- What mass scale would they have?
- Are there lattice gravity results (CDT, Regge calculus) that show bound state formation?

### Task 4: Phase Transitions in Quantum Gravity
- QCD has the deconfinement phase transition at ~150 MeV
- Does gravity have a phase transition? At what scale?
- Could the inflationary epoch be a gravitational phase transition?
- What about the transition from the d_s=2 UV phase to the d_s=4 IR phase?
- Recent literature (2024-2025) on gravitational phase transitions?

### Task 5: Novel Predictions
- Identify at least 3 predictions that the non-perturbative sector makes that are ABSENT from perturbative QG+F
- For each: what is the prediction, how could it be tested, and what is the theoretical basis?
- Are any of these predictions unique to the non-perturbative sector (not reproducible by any perturbative calculation)?

## Success Criteria
- Clear assessment of what the QCD-gravity analogy predicts
- At least 2-3 specific non-perturbative predictions distinct from perturbative QG+F
- Assessment of which predictions are testable
