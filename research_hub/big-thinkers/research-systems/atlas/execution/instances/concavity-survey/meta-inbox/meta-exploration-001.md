# Meta-Learning: Exploration 001

## What Worked
- Naming specific theorems and authors in the goal (Davis, Lewis, Ando, Löwner, Fan, Weyl) gave the explorer clear targets. The catalog was thorough.
- Pre-loading the geodesic concavity falsification result focused the explorer on conditional/restricted alternatives rather than wasting time on the full concavity path.
- Asking for "applicability to functions on compact Lie groups" as a specific criterion for each theorem was effective — the explorer correctly identified the domain gap as the fundamental issue.

## What Didn't Work / Could Improve
- The goal asked for "closest applicable theorem" but the answer was essentially "none directly apply." A better framing would have been "closest techniques that could be adapted, even if they don't directly apply."
- The explorer found a relevant 2024 paper (arXiv:2408.16906) but didn't go deep enough into its proof technique. For a follow-up, I would ask specifically to analyze the proof method and assess transferability.

## Key Lesson
When surveying a field for applicability to a specific problem, include "what proof techniques are transferable even if the theorems don't directly apply?" as an explicit success criterion. The explorer naturally focuses on theorem statements rather than proof methods unless instructed otherwise.
