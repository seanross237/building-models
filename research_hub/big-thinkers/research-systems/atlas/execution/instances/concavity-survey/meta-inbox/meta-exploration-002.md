# Meta-Learning: Exploration 002

## What Worked
- Structuring the goal around "alternatives to full concavity" rather than just "geodesic convexity" was the right framing. The explorer quickly identified the impossibility theorem and then spent most of its time on the actually useful frameworks.
- Listing specific authors/references (Helmke, Moore, Brockett, Absil, Łojasiewicz) gave the explorer concrete starting points.
- Asking "what framework can certify local max = global max?" turned out to be a much better question than "when is λ_max concave?"

## What Didn't Work / Could Improve
- The "critical context" section worked well but could have been even more specific — I could have given the Hessian eigenvalue formula at Q=I and the exact dimension of SU(2)^E so the explorer could compute Betti numbers directly rather than stating them abstractly.
- The goal listed ~13 specific topics; the explorer covered most but some (Prékopa-type theorems, spectral radius convexity) got minimal treatment. For a 3-hour explorer, 8-10 topics is probably the sweet spot.

## Key Lesson
When the obvious approach is impossible (concavity on compact manifolds), frame the goal around the actual need (global optimality certification) rather than the technique. The explorer will find much more useful alternatives when freed from the original framing.
