# Meta-Learning: Exploration 001 (Derivation)

## What Worked
- Single-task focus (just the derivation) produced a thorough 546-line report with every axiom stated
- Requesting "theorem-level precision" and explicit assumption listing delivered exactly that
- The explorer naturally found the Holevo bound as the critical bridge — didn't need to be told
- Including boundary cases in the goal led to useful physical insights (the "−1" interpretation)

## What Didn't
- Report took ~12 minutes to produce — the explorer spent most time in long thinking phases with little visible progress. Not a problem per se, but monitoring was opaque.
- The initial 41 lines were written early, then nothing for several minutes, then the full report was written at once. The file-write pattern made it look stalled when it wasn't.

## Lessons
- For derivation tasks, trust the long-think pattern. Don't nudge too early.
- The "compare with Zurek's results" instruction was valuable — it gave a saturation check that wouldn't have happened otherwise.
- Requesting claim attribution (SOURCED/DERIVED/CONJECTURED) worked well.
