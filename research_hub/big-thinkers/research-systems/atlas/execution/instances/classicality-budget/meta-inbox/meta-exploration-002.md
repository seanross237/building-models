# Meta-Learning: Exploration 002 (Numerical Computation)

## What Worked
- Math Explorer type was the right choice — it wrote Python code, ran it, and produced concrete numbers
- Giving explicit formulas, physical constants, and system parameters in the goal meant the explorer could compute immediately without research
- The 7/7 sanity check framework was very effective for building confidence
- Cross-checking BH entropy against published values (~10⁷⁷) provided an independent validation

## What Didn't
- Nothing major. Fastest of the three explorations (~8 minutes).

## Lessons
- For computation tasks, be maximally specific about inputs. Provide all formulas and constants. The explorer shouldn't need to look anything up.
- The "tag results as COMPUTED/ESTIMATED/CHECKED" instruction produced useful metadata.
- Having 6 systems with varying scales (Planck to universe) gave a clear story about where the bound matters. The spread of scales was more important than having many systems at similar scales.
- The key insight (budget only constraining at Planck scale) emerged from the computation — it would have been hard to predict without numbers.
