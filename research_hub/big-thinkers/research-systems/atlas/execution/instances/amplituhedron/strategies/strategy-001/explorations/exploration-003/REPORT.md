## Section 1: Features of N=4 SYM That Make the Amplituhedron Possible

To understand where the framework extends and where it breaks, we must first understand what makes N=4 SYM special enough to admit a clean amplituhedron.

### 1.1 Dual Conformal Symmetry and the Yangian

The key structural ingredient is **Yangian symmetry** — an infinite-dimensional algebra Y(psu(2,2|4)) generated by combining the ordinary superconformal symmetry (psu(2,2|4)) with **dual superconformal symmetry**. Dual superconformal symmetry acts on "dual coordinates" xᵢ defined via pᵢ = xᵢ - xᵢ₊₁, and it is a non-local symmetry of scattering amplitudes that has no analog in a standard Lagrangian framework.

The combination:
- Ordinary conformal symmetry in momentum space
- Dual conformal symmetry in dual (coordinate) space
- These together close into Y(psu(2,2|4))

**This Yangian symmetry is essential to the amplituhedron and appears to be unique to planar N=4 SYM** (among 4D gauge theories). It does NOT appear in N<4 SYM, pure Yang-Mills, or gravity.

A key review: Herrmann and Trnka, "SAGEX Review Chapter 7: Positive Geometry" (arXiv:2203.13018, 2022).

### 1.2 Planarity

The amplituhedron is defined only for **planar (large-N_c) N=4 SYM**. The planar limit:
- Restricts to color-ordered amplitudes (single-trace operators)
- Enables the amplitude/Wilson loop duality: planar loop amplitudes = null polygonal Wilson loops in dual space
- This duality is what makes dual conformal symmetry visible

**Non-planar amplitudes** (subleading in 1/N) do not have a known amplituhedron. There is evidence (2016) that analogous "zero conditions" hold non-planarly, but no complete geometric formulation exists.

### 1.3 Maximal Supersymmetry (N=4)

Maximal supersymmetry suppresses all UV divergences in the planar sector. This is crucial:
- UV divergences would appear as **poles at infinity** in the BCFW/Grassmannian framework
- These poles break the clean identification of amplitude = canonical form of positive geometry
- In N<4, UV poles appear and deform the geometric measure (Benincasa-Gordo, arXiv:1609.01923)

N=4 is special: the β-function vanishes to all orders, and amplitudes in the planar limit are IR-divergent but UV-finite. The IR divergences exponentiate cleanly (BDS ansatz), allowing a clean geometric picture of the remainder.

### 1.4 The Superamplitude / Momentum Twistor Framework

N=4 SYM admits a superspace formulation where all helicity components are packaged into a **superamplitude** A_n(Λ). In momentum twistor space (Zᵢ = (λᵢ, μᵢ, χᵢ)), the superamplitude has positive-definite geometric properties.

**Essential vs. Convenient features:**
- **Essential**: Yangian symmetry, planarity, UV finiteness
- **Convenient**: Specific superspace formulation, momentum twistors (can be reformulated in spinor helicity via momentum amplituhedron)
- **Not essential**: The specific particle content beyond maximal SUSY requirement

## Section 2: Reduced SUSY — N=1 and N=2 SYM

### 2.1 What BCFW Tells Us

BCFW recursion relations work for **any gauge theory** with good large-z falloff (spin ≤ 1 particles). This means:
- N<4 SYM has valid BCFW recursion at tree level
- On-shell diagrams exist (by construction from BCFW)
- The Grassmannian structure (positive matrices, etc.) still appears

This suggests some residual geometric structure exists for N<4.

### 2.2 The Benincasa-Gordo Obstacle

**Key paper**: Benincasa & Gordo, "On-shell diagrams and the geometry of planar N < 4 SYM theories," arXiv:1609.01923 (2016, JHEP 2017).

This paper identifies the specific obstacles:

1. **UV poles at infinity**: When BCFW shifts are applied in N<4 theories, the amplitude does not vanish as z → ∞ at loop level. This produces "boundary terms" / poles at infinity — contributions absent in N=4.

2. **Deformed measure on Grassmannian**: On-shell diagrams for N<4 SYM have the same combinatorial (Grassmannian) structure as N=4, but the **differential form (measure) is deformed** by extra factors encoding UV singularities.

3. **Helicity loops in diagrams**: In N<4 theories, helicity flows can form closed loops in on-shell diagrams. These produce higher-order poles that are entirely absent in N=4, directly associated with UV divergences and rational terms at loop level.

4. **Bubble contributions**: Non-maximal SUSY generates "bubble-cut" ambiguities in the loop integrand definition.

**Net result**: The positive geometry of N=4 SYM does NOT cleanly extend to N<4. The measure is not a canonical form with only logarithmic singularities; it has extra poles that corrupt the geometric interpretation.

### 2.3 Is There Any N=2 Geometry?

Dian and Heslop (arXiv:2106.09372, 2021) study **amplituhedron-like geometries with non-maximal winding numbers**. These correspond to:
- Canonical forms of modified geometric objects (lower winding number than the standard amplituhedron)
- These canonical forms equal **products of parity-conjugate N=4 amplitudes**: Aₙ,ₖ × Aₙ,ₙ₋ₖ (amplitude squared at equal helicity)

This is **not** an extension to N=2 — it is a mathematical variant of the N=4 amplituhedron that happens to produce amplitude squares. The construction still uses N=4 SYM data exclusively.

**Bottom line for N=1/N=2 SYM**: No clean positive geometry exists. The obstacle is fundamental: UV divergences physically cannot be "hidden" inside a positive geometry — they produce poles that don't admit a log-form interpretation.

## Section 3: Pure Yang-Mills (No SUSY)

### 3.1 BCFW and Color-Ordered Gluon Amplitudes

Pure Yang-Mills (no matter, no SUSY) has:
- Valid BCFW recursion at tree level for color-ordered gluon amplitudes (Britto-Cachazo-Feng-Witten, 2005)
- Parke-Taylor formula for MHV amplitudes (1986) — identical to N=4 SYM MHV sector
- All-multiplicity tree-level gluon amplitudes computable

**Crucial observation**: Tree-level gluon amplitudes in pure YM are **identical** to tree-level N=4 SYM gluon amplitudes in the MHV sector (the SUSY partners decouple at tree level for pure-gluon external states). So any geometric structure for tree-level gluon amplitudes in YM is "inherited" from N=4.

### 3.2 Scattering Forms and Color-Kinematics Geometry (Tree Level)

**Key paper**: Arkani-Hamed, Bai, He, Yan, "Scattering Forms and the Positive Geometry of Kinematics, Color and the Worldsheet," arXiv:1711.09102 (2017, JHEP 2018).

This paper provides a partial geometric picture for Yang-Mills:

1. **Color is Kinematics**: Kinematic "wedge products" in the scattering form satisfy the **same Jacobi relations** as color factors. This gives a geometric origin for BCJ/color-kinematics duality.

2. **YM scattering form**: The Yang-Mills amplitude (summed over color orderings) corresponds to a differential form in kinematic space whose pullback to the associahedron boundary recovers gluon amplitudes.

3. **CHY connection**: The worldsheet associahedron (from the Cachazo-He-Yuan moduli space) maps to the kinematic associahedron via scattering equations. This is a diffeomorphism, giving a geometric encoding of YM tree amplitudes.

**What this achieves**: YM tree amplitudes have a geometric description via scattering forms on kinematic space, connected to the associahedron. This is a genuine partial success.

**What this is not**: A clean amplituhedron-style positive geometry where the amplitude = canonical form. The scattering forms for YM require the CHY pushforward machinery and are more complex than the scalar case.

### 3.3 Corolla Polynomial Extension (2024)

**Key paper**: Laddha & Suthar, "Positive Geometries, Corolla Polynomial and Gauge Theory Amplitudes," arXiv:2405.10601 (May 2024, JHEP 02(2025)).

This recent paper claims:
- The S-matrix of Yang-Mills theory is obtained by contracting the canonical form of the ABHY associahedron with a multi-vector field (MVF) in kinematic space
- The MVF components are determined by the **Corolla polynomial** (from Kreimer-Sars-van Suijlekom)
- This extends to **one-loop planar Yang-Mills integrands** via a D̂ₙ polytope
- Constructs curve integral formulae for tree-level and 1-loop gluon amplitudes

**Assessment**: This is significant — it extends positive geometry to loop-level YM amplitudes via the corolla polynomial. However, it's "messier" than the N=4 case: the canonical form requires decoration with the corolla polynomial rather than being a clean geometric object.

### 3.4 The Loop-Level Obstruction

At loop level, pure Yang-Mills suffers from:
- UV divergences (theory is not UV-finite)
- No amplitude/Wilson loop duality
- No dual conformal symmetry
- Rational terms in loop amplitudes not captured by cut-based methods

These fundamentally prevent an amplituhedron-style clean geometric encoding of loop-level YM amplitudes. The corolla polynomial approach (§3.3) provides a workaround but not a fundamental resolution.

## Section 4: The Associahedron and Bi-Adjoint Scalars

This is the **most successful extension** beyond N=4 SYM.

### 4.1 The ABHY Associahedron (The Foundation)

**Key paper**: Arkani-Hamed, Bai, He, Yan, "Scattering Forms and the Positive Geometry of Kinematics, Color and the Worldsheet," arXiv:1711.09102 (2017).

For bi-adjoint scalar ϕ³ theory (scalars transforming in the adjoint of two gauge groups):
- The partial amplitudes m(α|β) are exactly the **canonical form** of an (n-3)-dimensional associahedron embedded in kinematic space
- The associahedron is defined by requiring all planar Mandelstam variables sᵢⱼ ≥ 0 in the ordering
- No SUSY, no Yangian, no dual conformal — this works from pure geometry of the kinematic space

This is a **genuine extension**: bi-adjoint scalar theory is a non-supersymmetric, non-gauge theory, and positive geometry works perfectly.

**Why does this work?** The bi-adjoint scalar amplitude has no UV divergences at tree level (relevant for a scalar cubic theory in 6D or less), and the color structure exactly matches the combinatorics of associahedron boundaries.

### 4.2 ϕ⁴ Theory — Stokes Polytopes

**Key paper**: Banerjee, Laddha, Raman, "Stokes Polytopes: The positive geometry for ϕ⁴ interactions," arXiv:1811.05904 (2018, JHEP 2019).

For scalar ϕ⁴ theory:
- Planar amplitudes correspond to canonical forms of **Stokes polytopes** (a different class of polytopes)
- Multiple Stokes polytopes must be summed with specific weights to get the full amplitude
- This is messier than the ϕ³ case but still a valid positive geometry

**Follow-up**: Aneesh et al., arXiv:1911.06008 (2019): Shows Stokes polytopes embed into ABHY associahedra as lower-dimensional faces. The projectivity of scattering forms and their kinematic space associahedron capture quartic planar amplitudes.

### 4.3 Multi-Scalar — Accordiohedra

**Key paper**: Jagadale & Laddha, "Towards Positive Geometry of Multi Scalar Field Amplitudes: Accordiohedron and EFT," arXiv:2104.04915 (2021, JHEP 2022).

For multi-scalar interactions (mixed ϕ₁³ + ϕ₁²ϕ₂ terms):
- Introduces **accordiohedra** — a class of polytopes generalizing associahedra
- In decoupling limits, projects onto accordiohedra for ϕ³ or ϕ³ + gϕ⁴ theories
- Extends the framework to theories with multiple scalar fields

**2025 extension**: "Towards Positive Geometries of Massive Scalar Field Theories" (arXiv:2206.07979, 2022) begins extending to massive scalars.

### 4.4 Is the Associahedron Universal?

A key structural claim in the literature is that the ABHY associahedron is a "universal" positive geometry from which others are derived:
- Stokes polytopes for ϕ⁴ embed into associahedra
- Scattering forms for YM, NLSM connect to the same kinematic space
- The CHY worldsheet associahedron maps to the kinematic associahedron

**Assessment**: The associahedron is NOT the universal object — but it is a universal *scaffold*. Other positive geometries for different theories are obtained by:
1. Embedding/projecting onto faces of the associahedron
2. Decorating the associahedron canonical form with polynomial factors (corolla)
3. Deforming the associahedron geometry (accordiohedra, Stokes polytopes)

**2025 stringy extension**: "Positive Geometry for Stringy Scalar Amplitudes" (arXiv:2508.20161) introduces the **associahedral grid** — a positive geometry capturing the full α'-dependence of string amplitudes, including the inverse KLT kernel. This is a direct extension of the associahedron to string theory.

## Section 5: Gravity (SUGRA)

### 5.1 The Core Problem

Graviton amplitudes in pure gravity (or N=8 SUGRA) present severe obstacles:
1. **No Yangian symmetry**: Gravity lacks the dual superconformal structure that generates the Yangian in N=4 SYM
2. **Harder singularity structure**: Graviton amplitudes grow as z² at large z in BCFW (for non-adjacent shifts), not z⁻¹ — fewer recursion relations work directly
3. **KLT double-copy**: Gravity amplitudes are "squares" of YM amplitudes (Kawai-Lewellen-Tye), but "squaring a geometry" has no clear geometric meaning

### 5.2 Trnka's Gravituhedron Program

**Key paper**: Trnka, "Towards the Gravituhedron: New Expressions for NMHV Gravity Amplitudes," arXiv:2012.15780 (2020, JHEP 2021).

Trnka defines **G-invariants** (gravity analogs of the R-invariants in N=4 SYM) and uses them to write explicit formulas for NMHV gravity amplitudes up to **8 points**. The formulas are expressed in a way suggestive of triangulations of a putative geometric object.

**What works**:
- Explicit NMHV gravity amplitude formulas (up to 8 points)
- Factorization diagram methods (from YM) applied to gravity context
- Structural parallels between YM and gravity amplitude expressions

**What doesn't work**:
- No actual geometric object (the "gravituhedron") has been constructed
- Only NMHV amplitudes, not all-multiplicity
- Loses dual superconformal symmetry (fundamental, not technical)
- The paper explicitly states this is "towards" the gravituhedron — speculative

**Assessment**: The gravituhedron is an aspirational program, not an established result. The structural similarities between G-invariants and R-invariants suggest a geometric object might exist, but after 5+ years (2020–2025) it has not been constructed.

### 5.3 CHY Scattering Equations as Partial Geometry

**Key paper**: Lukowski, Moerman, Stalknecht, "Pushforwards via Scattering Equations with Applications to Positive Geometries," arXiv:2206.14196 (2022, JHEP).

The CHY formula gives a compact expression for graviton amplitudes. This paper shows:
- For theories captured by CHY, the canonical form of a positive geometry in moduli space (worldsheet) **pushes forward** via scattering equations to the kinematic space canonical form
- This provides a CHY-based positive geometry for gravity amplitudes — but as a pushforward, not a direct geometric object

**Assessment**: This is a partial geometric description — gravity amplitudes are computable from a geometric object, but the geometry lives in moduli space and requires the non-trivial scattering equations map to reach kinematic space.

### 5.4 Associahedral Grid and Inverse KLT

**Key paper**: Bartsch, Kampf, Podivin, Stalknecht, "Positive Geometry for Stringy Scalar Amplitudes," arXiv:2508.20161 (Aug 2025).

This paper introduces the **associahedral grid** as a positive geometry that captures the full α'-dependence of string amplitudes, including:
- The inverse KLT kernel has a geometric interpretation via the associahedral grid
- Gravity amplitudes (as double-copies) could potentially be approached via this grid

**Assessment**: This is the most recent and promising development for connecting positive geometry to gravity via KLT. However, it still doesn't give a direct "graviton amplituhedron" — it gives the KLT map a geometric home.

### 5.5 N=8 SUGRA and Maximal SUSY Gravity

N=8 SUGRA (maximal SUSY gravity in 4D) has many properties analogous to N=4 SYM:
- UV finite through 6 loops (possibly all loops)
- Color-kinematics duality applies
- KLT = (N=4 SYM)² at amplitude level

But there is still no amplituhedron for N=8 SUGRA. The lack of dual conformal symmetry (gravity backgrounds break it) is the primary obstacle.

## Section 6: QCD-Like Amplitudes

### 6.1 The Color Decomposition Reduction

Multi-parton QCD amplitudes decompose into **color-ordered partial amplitudes** via the color decomposition:

M_n(g₁, g₂, ..., gₙ) = Σ_{σ ∈ Sₙ₋₁} Tr(T^{a_{σ(1)}}...T^{a_{σ(n)}}) × A_n(σ(1),...,σ(n))

The color-ordered partial amplitudes Aₙ satisfy BCFW recursion (same as the color-ordered amplitudes in N=4). So at **tree level**, the geometric tools (BCFW recursion, associahedra for scalars) apply to the kinematic structure.

### 6.2 Leading Color (Planar) Limit of QCD

In the large-N_c (planar) limit, QCD is dominated by single-trace contributions. In this limit:
- Color-ordered gluon amplitudes = those of pure YM (no quarks)
- These are geometrically accessible via scattering forms (Section 3.2) and corolla polynomials (Section 3.3)

**Assessment**: The planar limit of QCD at tree level has partial positive geometry via the YM scattering forms. This is inherited from the bi-adjoint scalar / associahedron structure.

### 6.3 Full Color-Full QCD — The Fundamental Obstacles

Beyond the planar limit or beyond tree level, QCD presents fundamental obstacles:
1. **Non-planar diagrams**: The amplitude/Wilson loop duality is gone; no Yangian structure
2. **UV divergences**: QCD is UV divergent at loop level (requires renormalization)
3. **IR divergences**: More complex IR structure than N=4 SYM (no BDS-like exponentiation)
4. **Quarks**: Fundamental-representation matter fields break the cyclic color structure
5. **Confinement**: Non-perturbative physics that positive geometry has no handle on

**Assessment**: No positive geometry formulation for full, color-full, loop-level QCD is known or expected in the near future. The obstacles are fundamental, not technical.

### 6.4 Partial Progress: Color-Kinematics Duality

The geometric origin of **color-kinematics (BCJ) duality** is now understood via scattering forms (arXiv:1711.09102). This is relevant to QCD because:
- BCJ duality applies to YM amplitudes (and hence QCD gluon amplitudes)
- Gravity amplitudes from QCD via double-copy implicitly use this duality
- The geometric understanding (color = kinematics in wedge products) is a structural insight even if not a full positive geometry

## Section 7: Cosmological Polytopes and the Wavefunction of the Universe

### 7.1 The Foundational Paper

**Key paper**: Arkani-Hamed & Benincasa, "Cosmological Polytopes and the Wavefunction of the Universe," arXiv:1709.02813 (2017).

This extends positive geometry to **cosmological correlators** (equal-time correlation functions in an FRW spacetime), specifically:
- The wavefunction coefficient Ψₙ(k₁,...,kₙ) for n external momenta
- For a **massless scalar in a flat FRW universe**, these wavefunction coefficients equal canonical forms of a class of polytopes

The cosmological polytope for a graph G is defined by:
- One simplex per vertex (containing energy variables for legs)
- Intersections corresponding to edge energies
- The canonical form = wavefunction coefficient

**Key connection**: The flat-space scattering amplitude appears as a **higher-codimension face** of the cosmological polytope, where the "total energy" E_tot → 0. This geometrically explains why flat-space limits of cosmological correlators give S-matrix elements.

### 7.2 Extensions and Limitations (2019–2025)

**Massive scalars**: Benincasa et al. (arXiv:1909.02517, 2019): extends to massive scalars with light masses. The flat-space amplitude still appears as a face; the Laurent expansion in total energy is geometrically controlled.

**Cosmohedra** (JHEP 2025, arXiv ~2412.19881): Introduces "cosmohedra" as the positive geometry underlying the cosmological wavefunction for **Tr(ϕ³) theory** (matrix scalar). The structure is a "russian doll" of non-overlapping subpolygons, generalizing associahedra. The cosmohedra are related to surfacehedra (from string theory).

**Geometry of Cosmological Correlators** (SciPost Physics, 2025): Applies the framework to compute cosmological correlators at loop level using positive geometry techniques.

**What breaks for de Sitter / inflation**:
- The framework works cleanly for flat FRW spacetimes
- **De Sitter** (curved spacetime, positive cosmological constant) makes the wavefunction more complex: time-ordering and mode functions introduce complications
- **Interacting inflation** (non-Gaussianities from slow-roll inflation) partially maps onto cosmological polytopes, but the non-linearities and tensor modes (gravitational waves) complicate things significantly
- The "total energy" pole structure breaks for massive spinning particles

**Assessment**: Cosmological polytopes represent a **genuine, working extension** of positive geometry beyond N=4 SYM — to cosmological correlators of scalar theories. This is not a toy model; it computes physically meaningful quantities. The extension to gravitational inflation is a major open problem.

## Section 8: Other Extensions

### 8.1 ABJM Theory — 3D Maximally-Supersymmetric Amplituhedron

**Key paper**: He, Huang, Kuo, "The ABJM Amplituhedron," arXiv:2306.00951 (2023, JHEP with erratum 2024).

ABJM theory is 3D planar N=6 Chern-Simons-matter theory — a 3D analog of N=4 SYM with maximal supersymmetry for a 3D theory.

**Construction**: The ABJM amplituhedron is obtained from the N=4 SYM amplituhedron by:
1. **Sign change**: Flip the overall sign of the positive region (from "positive" to "negative" geometry in 4D)
2. **Symplectic reduction**: Project from 4D to 3D kinematics

**What works**:
- All-loop, all-multiplicity amplituhedron is constructed
- Explicit 1-loop integrands up to 10 points; 2-loop integrands up to 8 points
- Unitarity cuts and soft limits emerge from geometry

**What's limited**:
- Not truly independent: the ABJM amplituhedron is entirely **derived from** the N=4 SYM one via dimensional reduction
- Only bipartite-graph negative geometries survive the symplectic reduction
- The "parent" N=4 SYM structure is essential — ABJM's own symmetries don't generate the object independently

**Related**: The orthogonal momentum amplituhedron (JHEP 01(2022)141) and ABJM momentum amplituhedron (arXiv:2306.07312) provide the same physics in spinor helicity variables.

### 8.2 Momentum Amplituhedron (N=4 SYM, Spinor Helicity Space)

**Key paper**: Damgaard, Ferro, Lukowski, Parisi, "The Momentum Amplituhedron," arXiv:1906.10700 (2019, JHEP).

The standard amplituhedron lives in **momentum twistor space** (a projective twistor space). The momentum amplituhedron reformulates it in **spinor helicity space** (the "physical" on-shell variables λ, λ̃).

**Why this matters for extensions**: Spinor helicity variables are defined for any massless theory in 4D. This suggests the momentum amplituhedron formulation could be the right starting point for N<4 extensions — but this has not been realized.

**Loop extension**: Ferro & Lukowski, "The Loop Momentum Amplituhedron," arXiv:2210.01127 (2022, JHEP). Extends the momentum amplituhedron to include loop momenta. The two-loop MHV version was achieved in 2025 (JHEP 02(2025)044).

### 8.3 Stringy Canonical Forms — Connecting to String Theory

**Key paper**: Arkani-Hamed, He, et al., "Stringy Canonical Forms," arXiv:1912.08707 (2019/2020, JHEP 2021).

This paper extends canonical forms from polytopes to **stringy deformations**:
- Replace the canonical form of a polytope P by ∫_P e^{−X/α'} dX (a "stringy" deformation)
- When applied to the ABHY associahedron in kinematic space, this produces the **Koba-Nielsen string integral** (open string n-point amplitude)
- No reference to worldsheet physics — pure geometry of kinematic space

**Assessment**: This gives a direct connection between the positive geometry of bi-adjoint scalars and open string theory, without needing the worldsheet formulation.

### 8.4 Tropical Amplituhedron

**Key paper**: Akhmedova & Tessler, "The Tropical Amplituhedron," arXiv:2312.12319 (2023).

Introduces a **tropical (piecewise-linear) analog** of the amplituhedron. The tropical amplituhedron:
- Is computationally more tractable than the full amplituhedron
- Preserves essential structural properties
- Connects to tropical Grassmannians and cluster algebras

This is primarily a mathematical tool for analyzing amplituhedron combinatorics, not a physical extension.

### 8.5 Cluster Algebras and the Grassmannian Structure

**Key paper**: Williams, "The Positive Grassmannian, the Amplituhedron, and Cluster Algebras," arXiv:2110.10856 (2021).

The positive Grassmannian Gr(k,n)≥₀ underlying the amplituhedron has deep connections to cluster algebras:
- Cluster X-coordinates parametrize the Grassmannian
- Cluster adjacency encodes amplitude singularity structure
- The amplituhedron "knows about" cluster algebras of type A_{n-3} (for the Grassmannian Gr(2,n))

This is mathematically significant but doesn't extend the framework to new theories.

## Section 9: Cases Where Geometric Approach Exceeds Standard Methods

The GOAL asked: are there cases where the geometric approach achieves things standard methods cannot? Yes — several:

### 9.1 Color-Kinematics Duality Explained

The geometric origin of BCJ/color-kinematics duality (arXiv:1711.09102) provides something standard Feynman diagram methods cannot: **an explanation of why** color and kinematic numerators satisfy the same algebraic relations. This is not just a computational tool — it's structural insight inaccessible from the Lagrangian.

### 9.2 Stokes Polytopes Give ϕ⁴ Amplitudes Without Feynman Diagrams

For ϕ⁴ theory, the Stokes polytope canonical form (arXiv:1811.05904) directly computes partial amplitudes without summing Feynman diagrams. More importantly, **the relative weights of different ϕ⁴ diagrams are fixed by the geometry** — you don't need to know the Feynman rules to recover them. This is conceptually beyond what standard methods provide.

### 9.3 ABJM All-Loop Results from Geometry

The ABJM amplituhedron (arXiv:2306.00951) gives **all-loop, all-multiplicity** amplitude integrands. While these are formally obtainable by other methods, the geometric construction unifies them in a single object — something not achievable by standard perturbative loop-by-loop computation.

### 9.4 Cosmological Polytope Predicts S-Matrix from Wavefunction Structure

The cosmological polytope reveals that the flat-space S-matrix is encoded as a **codimension-1 boundary** of the cosmological wavefunction geometry (arXiv:1709.02813). This structural connection between cosmological observables and scattering amplitudes is not transparent in standard QFT in curved space — it requires the geometric picture.

### 9.5 Associahedral Grid and Inverse KLT Kernel (2025)

The associahedral grid (arXiv:2508.20161, 2025) provides the inverse KLT kernel a geometric interpretation. This means the mysterious double-copy structure of gravity (why gravity = YM²?) has a geometric home for the first time — something standard methods provide as a formula but not as a geometric fact.

## Section 10: Synthesis — Is Positive Geometry a General Principle?

### 10.1 The Evidence

Based on this survey, the picture is:

**Positive geometry IS a general principle for tree-level amplitudes** in a broad class of theories with:
- Planarity (or color-ordering)
- Tree-level (UV finite)
- Scalar / gauge / string theories connected via CHY or KLT

The associated "geometries" differ:
- ϕ³: Associahedron (clean, first-principles)
- ϕ⁴: Stokes polytopes (messy but valid)
- YM: Scattering forms + corolla polynomial (requires decorations)
- NLSM: Modified scattering forms
- String theory: Associahedral grid / stringy canonical forms

**Positive geometry is NOT a general principle for loop-level amplitudes** in theories with UV divergences. N=4 SYM at loop level works precisely because UV finiteness allows a clean canonical form interpretation. Pure YM and QCD at loop level fail this condition.

### 10.2 The Essential Wall: UV Finiteness

The deepest structural result from this survey is:

> **Positive geometry at loop level requires UV finiteness.**

This is not a technical obstacle — it's structural. UV divergences produce poles in the integrand that are NOT logarithmic singularities on boundaries of a geometric object. They are "extra" poles that break the canonical form interpretation. The only 4D theories that are UV-finite at all loop orders are:
- N=4 SYM (planar)
- N=8 SUGRA (believed UV-finite, but no amplituhedron found)

N=8 SUGRA's lack of an amplituhedron suggests UV finiteness is necessary but not sufficient — you also need dual conformal / Yangian symmetry.

### 10.3 The Two Tiers of Success

**Tier 1 — Full amplituhedron (works only for):**
- Planar N=4 SYM (tree + loop)
- ABJM (via N=4 reduction, tree + loop)
- These require: Yangian symmetry, planarity, maximal SUSY

**Tier 2 — Scattering forms / partial positive geometry (works for):**
- Bi-adjoint ϕ³ (associahedron — cleanest)
- ϕ⁴, ϕᵖ (Stokes polytopes, accordiohedra)
- YM tree amplitudes (scattering forms)
- Gravity tree amplitudes (CHY pushforward, gravituhedron program)
- Cosmological wavefunctions (cosmological polytopes)
- String amplitudes (stringy canonical forms)
These require: tree-level, planarity/color-ordering, but NOT Yangian symmetry

**Tier 3 — Structural insights only (no full geometry, but something):**
- Non-planar N=4: Zero conditions (2016 evidence), no geometry
- Loop YM: Corolla polynomial (messy extension, 2024)
- N<4 SUSY: Deformed measure (not a canonical form)
- Gravity multi-loop: KLT structure partially geometric (2025)

**Complete failures:**
- Color-full QCD
- Non-planar theories at loop level
- Theories with explicit Lorentz violation or background dependence

### 10.4 Why the Amplituhedron Is Narrow But Deep

The amplituhedron (full Tier 1) is narrow — it applies to a very small class of theories. But for those theories, it reveals **physics that cannot be seen from the Lagrangian**:
- Locality and unitarity are derived, not assumed
- Yangian symmetry organizes all-loop structure
- The geometry of the positive Grassmannian directly encodes quantum corrections

The scattering forms / Tier 2 approach is broader but shallower — it gives efficient computations but doesn't reveal the same depth of physical structure.

### 10.5 Open Problems (2025 Status)

1. **Gravituhedron**: No complete geometric object constructed. The G-invariants (Trnka 2020) hint at structure but after 5 years, no polytope has been found. This is the major open problem.

2. **Non-planar amplituhedron**: Conjectured "zero conditions" hold (2016) but no geometry. Requires understanding amplitude/Wilson loop duality beyond the planar limit.

3. **Inflation and de Sitter**: Cosmological polytopes work for flat FRW; extension to dS with gravitons unknown.

4. **ϕ³ at loop level**: Bi-adjoint ϕ³ in 6D is UV divergent. No positive geometry for its loop amplitudes.

5. **Positive geometry for non-perturbative physics**: No framework for confinement, condensates, or strong-coupling dynamics.
