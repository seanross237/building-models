# Strategy 001: Final Report — Axiom-Breaking Construction from Novel Foundations

## Executive Summary

Over 9 explorations (1 timed out), this strategy attempted to construct a genuinely novel theory of quantum gravity by starting outside the standard QFT axiom set. The result is **Stochastic Computational Gravity (SCG) v2.0** — a research program (not a completed theory) that proposes both quantum mechanics and spacetime geometry emerge from a single stochastic computational process on a finite configuration space with causal structure.

**The honest verdict:** SCG v2.0 is the most ambitious synthesis attempted, but it cannot compete with QG+F (quadratic gravity with fakeon quantization) on any quantitative criterion. QG+F wins 16/21 sub-criteria in head-to-head comparison. SCG's value lies in the questions it asks (why QM? why spacetime? why 4D?) and the connections it reveals between existing programs — not in its answers, which remain unproven.

## What Was Accomplished

### Phase 1: Divergent Exploration (Explorations 001-002)

Two radically different conceptual seeds were surveyed:

1. **"Gravitize the Quantum" (001):** Surveyed 7+ programs that derive QM from gravity/spacetime. Finding: most don't actually derive QM from gravity. The viable pipeline runs through stochastic spacetime → Nelson diffusion → Barandes-Doukas lifting → Hilbert space. Three most promising: Oppenheim's postquantum classical gravity (most testable), Adler-Singh trace dynamics (most rigorous), Barandes-Verlinde stochastic emergence (most novel).

2. **"Computational Spacetime" (002):** Surveyed the complexity-geometry program (2014-2026). Finding: complexity genuinely extends beyond entanglement — it accesses black hole interiors, post-thermalization dynamics, and full nonlinear Einstein equations (Pedraza et al. 2023). This is NOT just entanglement in different language. The program is more mature than expected, now in a "construction era."

### Phase 2: Construction (Explorations 003-004)

3. **SCG Theory Construction (003):** Built a unified theory combining both seeds. Five axioms: (1) finite configuration space, (2) indivisible stochastic dynamics, (3) cost function as metric, (4) optimization principle, (5) irreducible noise. Key innovation: defining "complexity" as stochastic transition cost (not quantum circuit depth) breaks the QM-complexity circularity. Six predictions differ from GR/QG+F.

4. **Cost Function → QG+F (004):** Attempted to show cost function constraints select ghost-free gravity. **FAILED.** Four fundamental obstacles: the fakeon prescription operates at the quantization level, not the Lagrangian level. Classical cost functions cannot distinguish Stelle gravity from QG+F. This is a structural limitation.

### Phase 3: Stress-Testing and Repair (Explorations 005-009)

5. **Devil's Advocate (005):** Ruthless attack found one FATAL flaw (Lorentzian signature — positive-definite cost function cannot produce Lorentzian spacetime) and several near-fatal issues (QM emergence is reformulation not derivation, continuum limit unproven, Pedraza only proven in 2D, no unique predictions).

6. **Repair Attempt — Broad (006):** Timed out. Goal too ambitious (full theory rewrite + multiple flaw fixes). Key meta-learning: one task per exploration.

7. **Repair — Narrow (007):** Successfully fixed the fatal Lorentzian flaw by replacing the symmetric cost function with a causal partial order + directed cost + volume measure. Via Malament's theorem, this gives Lorentzian geometry naturally. All four derivation chains survive. SCG moves from "structurally broken" to "ambitious but unproven."

8. **SCG v2.0 vs QG+F (008):** Systematic 5-tier comparison. QG+F wins on 4 of 5 tiers. SCG's only advantage: conceptual scope (asks deeper WHY questions). "SCG is to QG+F as natural philosophy is to physics."

9. **Final Synthesis (009):** Clean statement of SCG v2.0 as research program, with 5 precisely stated open problems and 7 lessons about the QG landscape.

## The Theory: SCG v2.0

### Core Insight (for a non-physicist)
Imagine reality as a cosmic random walk through an immense maze of possible states, where each step has a cost and can only go in certain directions (like time — forward only). Quantum mechanics is what you get when you try to predict where the walker goes: the randomness and the maze's memory force you to use probability amplitudes rather than ordinary probabilities. Gravity and spacetime are what you get when you zoom out and look at the shape of the maze: the costs define distances, and the cheapest paths are the trajectories objects follow. Both quantum weirdness and curved spacetime come from the same source.

### Axioms (v2.0)
1. **Configuration Space:** Finite set Ω of N configurations
2. **Stochastic Dynamics:** Indivisible (non-Markovian) stochastic process on Ω
3. **Causal Cost Structure:** Partial order ≺ (causal precedence) + directed cost c(x,y) + volume measure v(x)
4. **Optimization:** Macroscopic dynamics extremizes total cost along causal paths
5. **Irreducible Noise:** Fundamental noise amplitude σ > 0

### What It Predicts (differently from GR/QG+F)
- No graviton (gravity is emergent)
- Spacetime diffusion (irreducible metric fluctuations)
- Decoherence-diffusion trade-off (testable within a decade)
- Complexity plateau replacing singularities
- Modified dispersion relations at Planck energies
- Higher-derivative gravity coefficients from cost function

### What Remains Broken
- QM emergence may be reformulation, not derivation
- Continuum limit unproven (causet → 4D Lorentzian manifold)
- Pedraza derivation only proven in 2D
- No unique predictions (all inherited from component programs)
- Self-consistency fixed point not solved
- Cost function not determined
- No Standard Model content

## Most Promising Findings (Independent of SCG)

Beyond the SCG synthesis itself, the exploration cycle produced findings valuable for any QG research:

1. **Complexity genuinely extends beyond entanglement.** The Pedraza et al. (2023) result — deriving full nonlinear Einstein equations from complexity optimization — overcomes the "linearization barrier" that limits entanglement-based approaches. This is the most important technical finding.

2. **The "gravitize the quantum" direction has experimental traction.** Oppenheim's postquantum classical gravity is the only QG-adjacent program with concrete near-term predictions (decoherence-diffusion bounds being constrained now).

3. **The fakeon prescription cannot be derived from classical geometry.** This is a negative but fundamental result: no classical cost function, variational principle, or geometric observable can select between Stelle gravity and QG+F. The fakeon is inherently quantum.

4. **Causal structure is mandatory for emergent gravity.** Any program that derives spacetime from something deeper must include causal order from the start. Positive-definite structures cannot produce Lorentzian geometry.

5. **The Barandes stochastic-quantum correspondence** is mathematically powerful but physically ambiguous. It's an isomorphism, not a derivation. The direction (stochastic → QM or QM → stochastic) is a metaphysical choice, not a mathematical result.

## Directions for the Next Strategy

1. **The Pedraza program deserves dedicated investigation.** The complexity → gravity derivation is the single most concrete bridge between computational principles and gravitational physics. Extending it to 4D and understanding what cost function constraints it imposes would be high-value.

2. **Oppenheim's framework should be tracked experimentally.** The decoherence-diffusion bounds are tightening. If experiments close the parameter space, it rules out an entire class of "classical gravity" theories.

3. **The QM emergence question (is QM fundamental or emergent?) is the deepest open question.** It's bigger than quantum gravity — it's about the foundations of physics. Any progress here would be transformative.

4. **A different methodology might work better.** This strategy used "diverge-construct-stress" — generating conceptual seeds, building a theory, then attacking it. The construction produced a well-defined research program but not a competitive theory. An alternative: start from QG+F's known structure and work outward, asking what deeper principle explains why QG+F has the form it does.

## Methodology Assessment

### What worked
- **Divergent surveys (001, 002):** Excellent for mapping unexplored territory. The "survey + deep dive + synthesis attempt" pattern consistently produced high-quality results.
- **Tightly scoped constructions (003, 007):** One task per exploration is the golden rule.
- **Devil's advocate with severity ranking (005):** Essential and should be done right after construction.
- **Systematic comparison (008):** The 5-tier framework provides clear, honest assessment.

### What didn't work
- **Multi-task repair (006):** Timed out. Never combine research + rewrite + multiple fixes.
- **Attempting to derive the fakeon from something deeper (004):** This is structurally impossible for classical cost functions. The negative result was valuable but could have been predicted.

### Key meta-learning
- One task per exploration. Period.
- Attack the theory immediately after constructing it.
- Compare to the benchmark (QG+F) early, not late.
- Specify line count targets for focused output.
- Include "if this fails, explain WHY" in every technical goal.
