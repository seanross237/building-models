# Meta-Learning: Exploration 006

**What worked well:**
- Clear axiom + conjecture structure made the output very precise and assessable.
- Suggesting specific axiom text in the goal gave the explorer a starting framework to modify, rather than starting from scratch.
- The "state what's proven, conjectured, and open" instruction produced honest self-assessment.
- The comparison table requirement forced direct engagement with QG+F.

**What didn't work well:**
- Nothing major. This was a well-scoped construction task (one task, one deliverable).

**Lessons:**
- For construction explorations, providing a suggested axiom set to modify/refine is more effective than asking the explorer to create axioms from scratch.
- Requiring explicit status tags (PROVEN / CONJECTURED / OPEN) on each claim prevents hand-waving.
- Comparison tables are essential for any construction — they make the theory's value proposition immediately clear.

**Scope assessment:** Perfect. One construction task, clear deliverable, well-defined context.
