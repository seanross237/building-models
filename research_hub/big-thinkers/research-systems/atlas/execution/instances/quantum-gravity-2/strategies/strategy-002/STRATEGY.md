# Strategy 002: Focused Theory from QG+F's Explanatory Gaps

## Objective

Build a focused, deep novel theory around ONE phenomenon or question that QG+F cannot address — rather than attempting a theory-of-everything from scratch. Strategy-001 proved that building a complete alternative to QG+F is too ambitious: SCG v2.0 was a creative research program but couldn't compute anything, couldn't make unique predictions, and lost 16/21 on head-to-head comparison. The lesson: don't compete with QG+F on breadth. Compete on depth and explanatory clarity in a domain QG+F is silent about.

This strategy uses QG+F's known limitations as a MAP: wherever QG+F has nothing to say, there's room for genuine novelty. The goal is to find the most fertile gap and build a theory that fills it with something intuitive, predictive, and original.

## Methodology: Gap-Mine → Concept-Sprint → Deep-Build → Harden

### Phase 1: Gap-Mining (2 explorations)

**Exploration 1: Map QG+F's Explanatory Debts.**
Comprehensively catalogue what QG+F cannot do:
- What phenomena does it calculate correctly but explain with zero physical intuition? (e.g., WHY does the fakeon prescription work? WHAT is a fakeon physically?)
- What questions does it simply not address? (e.g., why 4D? why this gauge group? what is spacetime made of? what happens inside black holes?)
- What predictions does it make that are essentially untestable? (e.g., only the tensor-to-scalar ratio r, testable ~2037)
- Where does it punt to "that's just how the math works"?

The goal is a ranked list of QG+F's gaps, ordered by how much explanatory value a novel theory could provide.

**Exploration 2: Extract Viable Conceptual Moves from Strategy-001.**
Review SCG v2.0's full development (FINAL-REPORT.md and the individual exploration reports). Extract:
- Conceptual ideas that were RIGHT even though the implementation failed (e.g., "gravity as computational cost optimization" — the Pedraza result proves this has legs, even though SCG's version couldn't derive anything quantitative)
- Specific technical results from Strategy-001 that any future theory should incorporate or at least address (e.g., the fakeon cannot be derived from classical structures; causal order is mandatory; Barandes lifting is isomorphism not derivation)
- Patterns in what FAILED — which conceptual moves repeatedly led to dead ends?

This exploration is a knowledge extraction, not a construction. Its output is a design brief for Phase 2.

### Phase 2: Concept Sprint (3 explorations)

Three independent explorations, each developing a genuinely novel mechanism for ONE of the most fertile gaps identified in Phase 1. Each operates independently — they are competing concepts, not parts of one theory.

**Rules for each concept exploration:**
1. **Novelty required.** The core mechanism must be genuinely new — not a synthesis of existing programs, not a repackaging of known ideas. If the concept turns out to exist in the literature, the explorer must flag this and explain what SPECIFICALLY is new about their version.
2. **One phenomenon, explained deeply.** Each concept targets exactly one gap from Phase 1. It should explain that phenomenon more intuitively and more deeply than any existing theory.
3. **Physical intuition first, formalism second.** The core idea must be explainable to a thoughtful non-physicist in 2-3 paragraphs. Only AFTER the physical picture is clear should the explorer develop any mathematics.
4. **One testable prediction.** Even speculative, the concept must predict something that could in principle be measured and that differs from QG+F's prediction (or lack thereof).
5. **Honest self-assessment.** At the end, the explorer rates their own concept on: (a) genuine novelty (1-10), (b) internal consistency (1-10), (c) explanatory clarity (1-10), (d) viability for further development (1-10).

The Strategizer should choose the 3 gaps that are most likely to yield genuinely novel theories. Prefer gaps where QG+F is completely silent over gaps where it gives an unsatisfying answer — silence means more room.

### Phase 3: Deep Build (3-4 explorations)

Select the strongest concept from Phase 2 (highest combined score on novelty + consistency + clarity + viability). Build it into a focused theory through a sequence of tightly scoped explorations:

1. **Foundation:** Lay out the axioms/principles, derive the main physical consequences, check that basic observational facts are compatible (Newton's law at large scales, GW speed, equivalence principle at minimum).
2. **Depth:** Develop the theory's internal structure. Handle the hard cases — black holes, cosmology, the Planck scale. Each case gets its own exploration if needed.
3. **Prediction:** Derive at least one concrete, quantitative prediction that differs from QG+F.

**Critical scoping rule (from Strategy-001's meta-learning):** ONE task per exploration. Never combine "research new approach" + "apply to theory" + "fix multiple issues" in a single exploration. Break it down ruthlessly.

**Pivot rule:** If the selected concept hits a fatal inconsistency during Deep Build (equivalent to SCG's Lorentzian signature flaw), the Strategizer should pivot to the second-best concept from Phase 2 rather than spending multiple explorations on repair. Strategy-001 spent 3 explorations repairing SCG's Lorentzian problem — that budget could have explored an entirely different direction.

### Phase 4: Harden (1-2 explorations)

1. **Devil's advocate:** Try to break the theory. Find contradictions, hidden assumptions, compatibility failures. Be ruthless. Do this IMMEDIATELY after the first construction exploration, not after multiple rounds of development.
2. **Final synthesis:** If the theory survives, write the definitive statement: core insight (for non-physicists), axioms, predictions, comparison to QG+F, open questions.

## Validation Criteria

### Minimum success
- A focused theory that passes Tiers 1-2 (genuinely novel + internally consistent) and partially passes Tier 3 (explains at least one thing more intuitively than QG+F)

### Good success
- Passes Tiers 1-3 fully, with at least one concrete prediction (Tier 4 partial) and enough internal structure to suggest further questions (Tier 5 partial)

### Breakthrough
- A theory that passes all 5 tiers: novel, consistent, explains something better than anything else, compatible with observations, and has rich internal structure

### Strategy exhaustion signals
- All 3 concept sprints produce concepts that already exist in the literature (novelty failure)
- The winning concept hits fatal inconsistency during Deep Build AND the backup concept also fails
- QG+F turns out to have no significant explanatory gaps (unlikely but possible)

## Context from Strategy-001

### Key findings to build on:
1. **Complexity → Einstein equations (Pedraza et al. 2023)** is the most concrete result: full nonlinear Einstein equations derived from complexity optimization. Only proven in 2D. Extending to 4D is high-value.
2. **The fakeon is inherently quantum** — cannot be derived from any classical structure, cost function, or variational principle. This is a HARD constraint: any theory that derives gravity from classical principles will produce Stelle gravity, not QG+F.
3. **Causal structure is mandatory.** Any theory with emergent spacetime must build in causal order from the start (Malament theorem).
4. **Barandes stochastic-quantum correspondence** is real mathematics but physically ambiguous — isomorphism, not derivation.
5. **Oppenheim's postquantum gravity** has the most concrete near-term predictions (decoherence-diffusion bounds).

### What failed in Strategy-001 (don't repeat):
- **Over-ambitious synthesis.** SCG tried to explain everything (QM + gravity + CC + singularities) and explained nothing quantitatively. Be focused.
- **Premature convergence.** 2 divergent → 7 on one theory. Maintain more options longer.
- **Devil's advocate too late.** Should happen right after first construction, not after multiple development rounds.
- **Multi-task explorations time out.** One task per exploration. Period.
- **"Synthesis of existing programs" is not novelty.** SCG combined Barandes + Pedraza + causal sets + Oppenheim — creative, but a domain expert would recognize every piece. The next theory needs at least one genuinely new idea.

### What Strategy-001 got right (keep doing):
- Divergent surveys with "survey + deep dive + synthesis attempt" scope — excellent results.
- Narrow, single-task construction explorations succeed consistently.
- Systematic comparison to QG+F using the 5-tier framework.
- Including "if this fails, explain WHY" in technical goals.
- Specifying line count targets for focused output.
