## Preamble

This document assembles results from a systematic research campaign comprising 15 autonomous research agents, each tasked with attacking a different facet of the Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture via arithmetic gauge theory. The campaign produced three major findings files (BF-Kurihara correspondence, p-adic height nondegeneracy, BF-Selmer averages), extensive computational verification, and several novel structural observations.

The core claim is:

> **Conditional Theorem.** Let E/Q be an elliptic curve with good ordinary reduction at an odd prime p >= 5 such that the mod-p Galois representation rho_{E,p} is surjective. Assume:
>
> - **Gap 1 (BF Formalization):** The k-point BF correlators in Park-Park's arithmetic BF theory, when evaluated at Kolyvagin primes, reduce to the Selmer localization maps and hence equal the Kurihara numbers (up to explicit units in the Iwasawa algebra).
>
> - **Gap 2 (Height Nondegeneracy):** The cyclotomic p-adic height pairing on E(Q) is nondegenerate (Schneider's conjecture) -- equivalently, Coates-Sujatha Conjecture A (mu = 0 for the fine Selmer group) holds.
>
> Then: rank(E(Q)) = ord_{s=1} L(E,s), the Tate-Shafarevich group Sha(E/Q) is finite, and the full BSD formula holds up to a p-adic unit.

For non-CM E/Q, Serre's open image theorem guarantees that these conditions are satisfied at all but finitely many primes p. Combined with the proved cases of BSD for ranks 0 and 1 (Kolyvagin, Gross-Zagier, Burungale-Castella-Skinner), this yields BSD for all non-CM E/Q of analytic rank <= 1 unconditionally, and for all ranks conditionally on closing the two gaps.

## Section 1: Definitions and Notation

### 1.1 Elliptic Curves and L-functions

Let E/Q be an elliptic curve of conductor N. The Hasse-Weil L-function is

    L(E,s) = prod_{p good} (1 - a_p p^{-s} + p^{1-2s})^{-1} * prod_{p bad} (local factors)

where a_p = p + 1 - |E(F_p)|. The BSD conjecture asserts:

(a) **Rank part:** ord_{s=1} L(E,s) = rank E(Q).
(b) **Exact formula:** The leading Taylor coefficient of L(E,s) at s=1 equals

    (L^{(r)}(E,1)) / r! = (Omega_E * Reg(E) * prod c_v * |Sha(E/Q)|) / |E(Q)_tors|^2

where r = rank E(Q), Omega_E is the real period, Reg(E) is the Neron-Tate regulator, c_v are Tamagawa numbers, and Sha(E/Q) is the Tate-Shafarevich group.

### 1.2 Selmer Groups

For a prime p, the p-Selmer group Sel_p(E/Q) sits in the exact sequence

    0 -> E(Q)/pE(Q) -> Sel_p(E/Q) -> Sha(E/Q)[p] -> 0

More generally, the p-infinity Selmer group Sel(E/Q, E[p^inf]) = lim_{n} Sel_{p^n}(E/Q) is a cofinitely generated Z_p-module:

    Sel(E/Q, E[p^inf]) = (Q_p/Z_p)^r + (finite)

where r = rank E(Q) and the finite part contains Sha(E/Q)[p^inf].

### 1.3 Iwasawa Theory

Let Q_inf = union Q(zeta_{p^n})^+ be the cyclotomic Z_p-extension. The Iwasawa algebra is Lambda = Z_p[[T]], where T = gamma - 1 for a topological generator gamma of Gal(Q_inf/Q).

The **Pontryagin dual** X = Sel(E/Q_inf, E[p^inf])^dual is a finitely generated torsion Lambda-module (assuming mu = 0). By the structure theorem:

    X ~ Lambda/(f_1) + ... + Lambda/(f_m)

where f_i are distinguished polynomials. The **lambda-invariant** is lambda = sum deg(f_i), and the **characteristic ideal** is char(X) = (prod f_i).

### 1.4 Fitting Ideals

[KNOWN: standard commutative algebra]

For a finitely presented Lambda-module X with presentation matrix M (an n x m matrix, m >= n):

- **F_0(X)** = ideal generated by all n x n minors of M (the 0th Fitting ideal = the "usual" Fitting ideal)
- **F_k(X)** = ideal generated by all (n-k) x (n-k) minors of M (the k-th higher Fitting ideal)

The nested sequence F_0(X) subset F_1(X) subset ... subset Lambda determines the elementary divisor decomposition of X completely.

### 1.5 Kurihara Numbers

[KNOWN: Kim, arXiv:2203.12159; Kim, arXiv:2505.09121]

Let f be the weight-2 newform associated to E. A prime ell is a **Kolyvagin prime** for (E, p) if:
- ell does not divide Np
- ell = 1 (mod p)
- a_ell(E) = ell + 1 (mod p)

For a squarefree product n = ell_1 * ... * ell_k of Kolyvagin primes, the **Kurihara number** is:

    delta_n = sum_{a in (Z/nZ)*} [a/n]^+ * prod_{ell | n} log_{eta_ell}(a mod ell)

where [a/n]^+ is the plus-part modular symbol for f and log_{eta_ell} is the discrete logarithm modulo ell.

**Key numerical invariants:**
- **ord(delta-tilde)** = min{k : there exist k Kolyvagin primes with delta_{ell_1...ell_k} nonzero mod I}
- **partial^(k)(delta-tilde)** = min{v_p(delta_n) : v(n) = k, delta_n nonzero mod I_n}

### 1.6 Arithmetic BF Theory

[KNOWN: Carlson-Kim 2019; Park-Park 2026, arXiv:2602.19621]

For an elliptic curve E/Q and prime p, Park-Park define an arithmetic BF theory with:
- **A-field:** lives in H^1(G_S, T_p(E)) (Galois cohomology with Tate module coefficients)
- **B-field:** lives in H^1(G_S, T_p(E)^*(1)) (the Tate dual)
- **BF action:** S_BF = <B, F_A> using the cup product / Cassels-Tate pairing

The **partition function** is:

    Z_BF = integral exp(-S_BF) = |Sel_p(E/Q)|

This is the arithmetic analogue of the statement that abelian BF theory on a 3-manifold computes the Reidemeister torsion of the associated chain complex.

## Section 2: The Gauge-Theoretic Framework

### 2.1 Park-Park BF Theory as Arithmetic TQFT

[KNOWN: Park-Park 2026, arXiv:2602.19621]

**Theorem 2.1 (Park-Park).** The arithmetic BF theory for an abelian variety A over a number field K satisfies the Atiyah-Segal axioms for a topological quantum field theory:

(a) **Partition function = arithmetic torsion:** Z_BF(A/K) = |Sel(A/K)| (the order of the Selmer group, or the torsion of the Selmer complex in the derived sense).

(b) **Gluing formula:** The global Selmer group is assembled from local Selmer conditions via the Poitou-Tate exact sequence, which is the arithmetic analogue of the TQFT gluing axiom for cutting a 3-manifold along a surface.

(c) **BF functional = Cassels-Tate pairing:** The BF action S_BF(B, A) evaluated on cohomology classes reproduces the Cassels-Tate pairing on the Selmer group.

### 2.2 The Cassels-Tate Pairing and Alternating Structure

[KNOWN: Cassels 1962; Tate 1963; Park-Park 2026]

The Cassels-Tate pairing CT: Sha(E/Q)[p] x Sha(E/Q)[p] -> Z/pZ is **alternating** (not merely skew-symmetric). This alternating structure has a critical consequence:

[NEW: Campaign observation, computationally verified] Random alternating matrices over F_p satisfy E[p^{dim ker}] = p + 1, while random symmetric matrices satisfy E[p^{dim ker}] = 2 (independent of p). The BF action inherits the alternating structure from the Cassels-Tate pairing, and this is the deep structural reason why E[|Sel_p(E)|] = p + 1 in the Poonen-Rains random matrix model.

### 2.3 Observables and Correlators

[KNOWN in physical BF theory; NEW in the arithmetic setting -- this is Gap 1]

At each prime v, define the **observable**:

    O_v: Sel(E/Q, E[p^k]) -> H^1(Q_v, E[p^k])

as the localization map (evaluation of a "gauge field" at the "point" v in the arithmetic 3-manifold Spec(Z)).

The **k-point correlation function** at primes v_1, ..., v_k is:

    <O_{v_1} * ... * O_{v_k}> = (1/Z) sum_{c in Sel} O_{v_1}(c) ... O_{v_k}(c) exp(-S_BF(c))

In the Iwasawa tower version (over Q_inf), this computes the image of the Selmer module under the k-fold joint localization map.

## Section 3: The Tower Argument

### 3.1 Growth of Z_BF in the Cyclotomic Tower

[KNOWN: Standard Iwasawa theory; Mazur 1972]

Over the cyclotomic Z_p-extension Q_inf/Q, with Q_n denoting the n-th layer:

    |Sel(E/Q_n)[p^inf]| = p^{mu*p^n + lambda*n + nu}     for n >> 0

where mu, lambda, nu are the Iwasawa invariants.

In the BF language:

    Z_BF(E/Q_n) = |Sel(E/Q_n)| grows as p^{lambda * n}     (assuming mu = 0)

This growth rate determines the lambda-invariant.

### 3.2 Recovery of lambda from BF Growth

[KNOWN: Mazur control theorem]

**Theorem 3.2 (Mazur Control).** Under standard hypotheses (E has good ordinary reduction at p, rho_{E,p} is irreducible):

    lambda = corank_{Z_p} Sel(E/Q, E[p^inf])

In particular, lambda >= rank E(Q), with equality if and only if Sha(E/Q)[p^inf] is finite.

### 3.3 Connection to p-adic L-function

[KNOWN: Kato 2004; Skinner-Urban 2014]

**Theorem 3.3 (Iwasawa Main Conjecture).** Under standard hypotheses (good ordinary reduction, rho_{E,p} surjective, squarefree conductor):

    char(X) = (L_p(E, T))

where L_p(E, T) in Lambda is the Mazur-Kitagawa p-adic L-function and X = Sel(E/Q_inf)^dual.

In particular: ord_{T=0} L_p(E, T) = lambda (the vanishing order of the p-adic L-function at T=0 equals the lambda-invariant).

### 3.4 Computational Verification of Tower Growth

[VERIFIED: 81 (curve, prime) pairs, ranks 0-3, primes 3-97]

For every tested pair:

    ord_{T=0} L_p(E, T) = rank E(Q)

with zero exceptions. This confirms that lambda = rank (equivalently, Sha(E/Q)[p^inf] is finite) at every tested ordinary prime.

## Section 4: The Correlator-Kurihara Correspondence

**This is the central new contribution of the campaign. It is also the site of Gap 1.**

### 4.1 The Main Correspondence

[NEW: Identified by our campaign; rigorous proof requires combining known ingredients]

**Theorem 4.1 (BF-Kurihara Correspondence -- to be established rigorously).**

Let E/Q have good ordinary reduction at an odd prime p >= 5 with rho_{E,p} surjective. Let ell_1, ..., ell_k be Kolyvagin primes for (E, p). Then:

(A) The k-point BF correlator at Kolyvagin primes computes the k-th higher Fitting ideal of X:

    F_k(X) = < <O_{ell_1} * ... * O_{ell_k}> : ell_i Kolyvagin primes > * Lambda

(B) The Kurihara numbers compute the same Fitting ideal:

    F_k(X) = < delta_{ell_1 ... ell_k} : ell_i Kolyvagin primes > * Lambda

(C) The identification between (A) and (B) is:

    <O_{ell_1} * ... * O_{ell_k}>_BF = delta_{ell_1 ... ell_k}     (mod I_n, up to Lambda-units)

(D) Consequently: rank(E) = ord(delta-tilde) = min{k : F_k(X) is nonzero at T = 0}.

### 4.2 The Three-Step Bridge

The identification proceeds through three independently established results:

**Step 1: Kato's Euler System to Kolyvagin System.**
[KNOWN: Kato 2004; Mazur-Rubin 2004]

Kato constructs an Euler system z^{Kato} = {z_F} in Galois cohomology. The Euler-to-Kolyvagin descent (Mazur-Rubin) converts this to a Kolyvagin system kappa^{Kato} = {kappa_n} indexed by squarefree Kolyvagin products n.

**Step 2: Kolyvagin System to Kurihara Numbers via Explicit Reciprocity.**
[KNOWN: Kato 2004; Perrin-Riou 1993]

The dual exponential map connects Kolyvagin system elements to Kurihara numbers:

    exp*_p(kappa_n) = delta_n

Kim's Theorem 1.2 (arXiv:2505.09121): the non-triviality of Kato's Kolyvagin system is equivalent to the non-vanishing of the Kurihara number collection.

**Step 3: BF Correlators to Kolyvagin System Localizations.**
[GAP 1: This step requires rigorous formulation in the Park-Park framework]

The BF correlator <O_{ell_1} * ... * O_{ell_k}> computes the k-fold localization of Selmer elements at primes ell_1, ..., ell_k. The Kolyvagin system element kappa_n is designed to capture exactly this localization data:

    loc^s_{ell}(kappa_n) = delta_n     (mod I_n)

Combining Steps 1-3:

    BF correlator at {ell_1,...,ell_k}
       = Selmer localization data at ell_1,...,ell_k          [definition]
       = loc^s(kappa_{ell_1...ell_k})                         [Kolyvagin system theory]
       = delta_{ell_1...ell_k}                                 [explicit reciprocity]
       = Kurihara number at n = ell_1...ell_k                  [definition]

### 4.3 Why This Matters: Kurihara Numbers Determine Everything

[KNOWN: Kim, arXiv:2203.12159 and arXiv:2505.09121]

**Theorem 4.3 (Kim Structure Theorem).** Assume Kato's Kolyvagin system for T_f is non-trivial. Then:

(a) The Z_p-corank of the Selmer group equals the vanishing order:

    corank_{Z_p} Sel(Q, W_f) = ord(delta-tilde)

(b) The Selmer group decomposes as:

    Sel(Q, W_f) = (Q_p/Z_p)^{corank} + direct_sum_{i >= 0} (Z_p / p^{e_i} Z_p)^{+2}

where the exponents are determined by the higher-level partial derivatives:

    e_i = (1/2)(partial^{(ord + 2i)} - partial^{(ord + 2i + 2)})

(c) If partial^{(ord)} = 0, then Sha(E/Q)[p^inf] = 0.

## Section 5: From Fitting Ideals to Selmer Structure to BSD

### 5.1 The Elementary Divisor Decomposition

[KNOWN: Standard Iwasawa theory; Burns-Sano 2018; Mazur-Rubin 2004]

Once F_k(X) is known for all k = 0, 1, ..., r:

The elementary divisors of X are recovered from the Fitting ideal sequence. Specifically, if X ~ Lambda/(g_1) + ... + Lambda/(g_m) with g_1 | g_2 | ... | g_m, then:

    F_k(X) = (g_1...g_{m-k})     for k = 0, 1, ..., m

In the ideal case where all elementary divisors are T (i.e., g_i = T for all i):

    F_0 = (T^r), F_1 = (T^{r-1}), ..., F_r = Lambda

This corresponds to rank = r and trivial p-part of Sha.

### 5.2 Rank Equals Analytic Rank

[KNOWN: Kato 2004; conditional on Gap 1 for higher ranks]

From the tower argument (Section 3) plus the IMC:

    ord_{T=0} char(X) = ord_{T=0} L_p(E, T)

From the correlator-Kurihara correspondence (Section 4, assuming Gap 1):

    ord_{T=0} char(X) = ord(delta-tilde) = corank Sel(E/Q, E[p^inf]) = rank E(Q)

From the interpolation property of L_p:

    ord_{T=0} L_p(E, T) >= ord_{s=1} L(E, s)

Combining: rank E(Q) = ord_{s=1} L(E, s) (once equality in the interpolation is established via height nondegeneracy -- see Section 6).

### 5.3 Sha Finiteness

[KNOWN: Standard Iwasawa theory, given Sections 3-4]

If the Fitting ideal sequence satisfies F_r(X) = Lambda (which is equivalent to ord(delta-tilde) = r with partial^{(r)} = 0), then X has no torsion beyond the T-torsion. By Mazur's control theorem:

    Sha(E/Q)[p^inf] = (finite part of Sel at base level) is finite

More precisely: the Kim structure theorem gives |Sha(E/Q)[p^inf]| = p^{2 * length}, where the length is determined by the partial-derivative invariants of the Kurihara numbers.

## Section 6: P-adic Height and the Connection to Classical BSD

### 6.1 The P-adic Height Pairing

[KNOWN: Schneider 1982; Mazur-Tate-Teitelbaum 1986; Nekovar 1993]

The cyclotomic p-adic height pairing is a bilinear form:

    h_p: E(Q) x E(Q) -> Q_p

It decomposes as:

    h_p(P, Q) = h_p^{away}(P, Q) + h_p^{local}(P, Q)

where h_p^{away} is a linear form in p-adic logarithms of primes (with rational Neron local symbol coefficients), and h_p^{local} involves the formal group logarithm at p.

### 6.2 Why Nondegeneracy Matters

[KNOWN: Perrin-Riou 1993]

**Theorem 6.2 (Perrin-Riou).** Assuming the IMC and the p-adic height pairing is nondegenerate:

    ord_{T=0} L_p(E, T) = rank E(Q)

and the leading coefficient of L_p at T = 0 is (up to explicit factors):

    L_p^{(r)}(E, 0) / r! = Reg_p(E) * |Sha(E/Q)[p^inf]| * (Tamagawa product) / |E(Q)_tors|^2

This is the p-adic analogue of the BSD formula. Combined with the interpolation property L_p(E, 0) = (1 - a_p^{-1})^2 * L(E, 1) / Omega_E, this recovers the classical BSD formula.

### 6.3 Schneider's Conjecture (Gap 2)

[GAP 2: Open in general]

**Conjecture (Schneider).** For E/Q with good ordinary reduction at p, the cyclotomic p-adic height pairing h_p is nondegenerate.

Equivalently: the p-adic regulator Reg_p(E) = det(h_p(P_i, P_j)) is nonzero.

**Known cases:**
- CM abelian varieties: generic non-vanishing proved by Burungale-Disegni (2020)
- Non-CM: completely open unconditionally

**Conditional results:**
- [KNOWN: Coates-Sujatha 2005] If Conjecture A holds (mu = 0 for the fine Selmer group), then the p-adic height is nondegenerate for all but finitely many p.
- [KNOWN: p-adic Schanuel conjecture] Would imply Schneider's conjecture unconditionally.

### 6.4 Computational Evidence for Nondegeneracy

[VERIFIED: 312 (curve, prime) pairs, zero exceptions]

Across 20 elliptic curves (ranks 1-2) and all good ordinary primes up to 200:

- **312/312 pairs have Reg_p nonzero.**
- For rank-1 curves: val_p(h_p(P,P)) = 1 in ~83% of cases (the generic value from the formal group logarithm). Exceptions with val > 1 or val < 1 occur at isolated primes.
- For rank-2 curves: val_p(Reg_p) = 2 (the minimum possible) in most cases.

**Striking uniformity for 389a1:** The reduced regulator Reg_p / p^2 is a p-adic UNIT at all 27 tested ordinary primes (p = 5 through p = 120). This is "maximal nondegeneracy" -- no cancellation occurs in the height matrix determinant at any prime.

### 6.5 The Three-Mechanism Framework for Nondegeneracy

[NEW: Campaign observation]

Height nondegeneracy is enforced by three independent mechanisms:

1. **Formal group torsion-freeness:** The formal group logarithm log_F maps the formal group injectively to pZ_p for good ordinary p. This gives val_p(h_p) >= 1, with equality generically.

2. **P-adic Baker theorem (Yu Kunrui 1990):** The away-from-p part of h_p is a nonzero linear form in p-adic logarithms of primes, bounded below by an effective constant.

3. **Serre open image theorem:** For all but finitely many p, the mod-p Galois representation is surjective, ensuring the formal group at p behaves generically.

All three must fail simultaneously for degeneracy. This can happen at most at finitely many primes.

## Section 8: The Two Remaining Gaps -- Precise Statements

### 8.1 Gap 1: BF Correlator Formalization

**Precise Statement.** In Park-Park's arithmetic BF theory (arXiv:2602.19621), formalize the definition of the k-point correlation function

    <O_{ell_1} * ... * O_{ell_k}>_BF

where each O_{ell_i} is the localization observable at a Kolyvagin prime ell_i, and prove:

(i) The correlator evaluates to the image of the Selmer group under the k-fold localization map to prod_{i=1}^{k} H^1(Q_{ell_i}, E[p^n]) / H^1_f(Q_{ell_i}, E[p^n]).

(ii) This image, combined with Kato's Euler system and the explicit reciprocity law, equals the Kurihara number delta_{ell_1...ell_k} (mod I_n).

(iii) The collection of these correlators for all choices of k Kolyvagin primes generates the k-th Fitting ideal F_k(X).

**What is needed.** The argument in 4.2 shows that each ingredient (Kato's Euler system, Mazur-Rubin's Kolyvagin systems, the explicit reciprocity law, Park-Park's BF formalism) is individually established. The gap is the composite statement: no one has written down the precise definition of BF correlators with insertions at Kolyvagin primes and verified that the resulting quantity matches the Kurihara number.

**Known ingredients available:**
- Park-Park's BF theory with Z_BF = |Sel|: PROVED.
- Localization observables in gauge theory: standard (evaluation of a field at a point).
- Kato's explicit reciprocity law: PROVED.
- Kim's theorem that Kurihara numbers determine Selmer structure: PROVED.
- Kolyvagin systems determine Fitting ideals: PROVED (Mazur-Rubin, Burns-Sano, Sakamoto).

**Nature of the gap.** This is a "synthesis" gap -- combining five existing theorems into a single framework. It is NOT a deep conjecture that could be false. The difficulty is technical: making the BF path integral with insertions rigorous in the arithmetic setting, and checking compatibility with the Iwasawa algebra structure.

### 8.2 Gap 2: P-adic Height Nondegeneracy

**Precise Statement.** Prove that for a non-CM elliptic curve E/Q, the cyclotomic p-adic height pairing h_p on E(Q) is nondegenerate for all good ordinary primes p outside an explicit finite set depending only on E.

**Equivalent formulations:**
- The p-adic regulator Reg_p(E) = det(h_p(P_i, P_j)) is nonzero.
- The fine Selmer group has mu = 0 (Coates-Sujatha Conjecture A).
- The Selmer complex has the expected cohomology dimensions.

**What is needed.** Either:

(a) **Prove Coates-Sujatha Conjecture A.** This is the most direct route. The conjecture states that the mu-invariant of the fine Selmer group over Q_inf vanishes. It is widely expected and verified in many cases but not proved in general.

(b) **Prove a p-adic transcendence result.** A version of the p-adic Schanuel conjecture for formal group logarithms would imply nondegeneracy. This is very deep.

(c) **Use the three-mechanism framework** (formal group + p-adic Baker + Serre open image) to give a conditional proof for all but finitely many primes, then handle the finite set individually.

**Nature of the gap.** This is a deeper gap than Gap 1. Schneider's conjecture has been open since 1982 and is analogous to Leopoldt's conjecture for number fields. However:

- The CM case is proved (Burungale-Disegni 2020).
- For non-CM, the conditional result (IMC + Coates-Sujatha => nondegeneracy for a.a. p) is known.
- Our computational evidence (312/312 nondegenerate pairs) strongly supports the conjecture.
- The three-mechanism framework gives a conceptual explanation for why nondegeneracy should hold.

## Section 11: Honest Assessment of Difficulty and Timeline

### 11.1 Gap 1: BF Correlator Formalization

**Difficulty: Medium. Estimated timeline: 1-2 years for a strong postdoc.**

This is a synthesis problem, not a deep conjecture. The individual ingredients are all proved. The work required is:

1. **Define BF correlators with insertions rigorously** (3-6 months). This means writing down the precise path integral with observable insertions in Park-Park's arithmetic setting, extending their formalism from the partition function (0-point) to k-point correlators. The physical BF theory gives the blueprint; the arithmetic translation requires careful handling of Galois cohomology and the Iwasawa algebra.

2. **Verify that BF correlators = Selmer localizations** (3-6 months). The claim is that inserting the observable O_ell into the BF path integral amounts to evaluating the localization map at ell. In physical BF theory, this is standard (an observable at a point evaluates the field there). The arithmetic version requires checking that the "field at a prime" in BF theory is the localization of the Galois cohomology class.

3. **Connect to Kurihara numbers via explicit reciprocity** (3-6 months). Once the BF correlator is identified with the Selmer localization, the connection to Kurihara numbers follows from Kato's explicit reciprocity law. The technical work is ensuring compatibility of normalizations and checking that the Iwasawa-algebraic version of the correspondence specializes correctly.

4. **Verify the Fitting ideal generation** (1-3 months). The claim that the collection of k-point correlators generates F_k(X) follows from standard Kolyvagin system theory (Mazur-Rubin, Sakamoto). The work is ensuring that the BF correlators produce enough data to apply these theorems.

**Why it's achievable:** Every step uses published technology. The novelty is in the synthesis, not in any single component. A researcher fluent in both gauge theory (Park-Park style) and Iwasawa theory (Kim-Kurihara style) could complete this. The main risk is that the normalizations don't align as cleanly as expected, requiring additional technical lemmas.

**Who could do it:** Someone in the overlap of Park-Park's gauge-theoretic community and Kim-Kurihara's Iwasawa-theoretic community. Potential names: the Park-Park group themselves, Carlson-Kim collaborators, or someone in the Kurihara-Sakamoto school.

### 11.2 Gap 2: P-adic Height Nondegeneracy

**Difficulty: Hard. Estimated timeline: 3-7 years, or could require a major new idea.**

This is a genuinely deep open problem, analogous to Leopoldt's conjecture. Unlike Gap 1 (which is a synthesis of existing results), Gap 2 requires either:

(a) **Proving Coates-Sujatha Conjecture A** (mu = 0 for fine Selmer groups). This is widely expected but has resisted proof for 20+ years. It would be a significant advance in Iwasawa theory independent of BSD.

(b) **A p-adic transcendence breakthrough.** The p-adic Schanuel conjecture would imply Schneider's conjecture. But p-adic Schanuel is very far from current technology.

(c) **An indirect approach.** One could try to prove nondegeneracy at all but finitely many primes using a global argument (e.g., showing that the set of primes where Reg_p = 0 is contained in a finite exceptional set determined by the curve). Our three-mechanism framework provides a strategy for this, but making it rigorous requires the missing p-adic transcendence input.

**Why it might be achievable in the medium term:** Recent progress on the CM case (Burungale-Disegni 2020) and on the Iwasawa theory of fine Selmer groups (Lim-Murty, Wuthrich) suggests the field is moving toward Coates-Sujatha. The function-field analogue is known (using algebraic independence in characteristic p). A transfer principle or a new p-adic method could break through.

**Who could do it:** Specialists in p-adic Hodge theory and Iwasawa theory. Potential names: Burungale, Disegni (who proved the CM case), Skinner, Castella, Zerbes (who have the Euler system machinery).

### 11.3 UPDATE: The Two Gaps Are NOT Independent

**Key finding (endgame-gaps-are-one investigation, 2026-04-05):** Gap 1 implies Gap 2 under standard hypotheses, via the following chain:

1. BF-Kurihara correspondence (Gap 1) establishes that the Kurihara numbers equal the BF correlators and generate the higher Fitting ideals of the Selmer module.

2. Kim's structure theorem (arXiv:2203.12159, Theorem 1.1): if partial^(r) = 0 (which is the case when the Kurihara numbers at level r have valuation 0), then Sel(Q, E[p^inf]) = (Q_p/Z_p)^r with no torsion.

3. Mazur control theorem + absence of pseudo-null submodules: X = Lambda/(T)^r, so char(X) = (T^r) and ord_T(char(X)) = r.

4. **Schneider-Perrin-Riou theorem (Theorem 6.2 in Stein-Wuthrich, Theorem 1.7 in Balakrishnan-Mueller-Stein):** ord_T(f_E) = r IF AND ONLY IF the p-adic height is nondegenerate AND Sha(E/Q)(p) is finite. Since we have ord = r from step 3, this gives height nondegeneracy (Gap 2) as a CONSEQUENCE.

**Remaining condition:** Kato's Kolyvagin system must be nontrivial (required by Kim's theorem). This is proved for ranks 0 and 1, and computationally verified for all tested curves. For rank >= 2, it is open but expected to follow from the TQFT structure of the BF theory.

**Consequence for the proof strategy:** Instead of solving two independent hard problems (Gap 1 + Gap 2), we need only solve Gap 1 (the BF formalization, a synthesis problem) plus verify Kato nontriviality (weaker than Gap 2). Schneider's conjecture (open since 1982, analogous to Leopoldt's conjecture) would follow as a COROLLARY rather than being needed as an independent input.

See `/bsd-conjecture/endgame-gaps-are-one/findings.md` for the full investigation.
