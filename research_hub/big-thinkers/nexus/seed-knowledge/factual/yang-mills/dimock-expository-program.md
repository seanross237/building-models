## Overview

Jonathan Dimock (SUNY Buffalo) has written the most active ongoing effort to extend and apply Balaban's RG methods. His work makes Balaban's notoriously dense original papers more accessible.

## Expository Series: "The Renormalization Group According to Balaban" (2011-2014)

Three-part series illustrating Balaban's method on scalar φ⁴₃ (simpler but structurally analogous):

1. **Part I: Small fields** (arXiv:1108.1335, Rev. Math. Phys. 25, 2013) — Small-field contribution to partition function
2. **Part II: Large fields** (arXiv:1212.5562) — Large-field contribution via Balaban's R operation
3. **Part III: Convergence** (arXiv:1304.0705, Ann. Henri Poincaré, 2014) — Convergence of expansion, stability bound

**Limitation:** Treats scalar fields, not gauge fields. Gauge theory involves additional complications (gauge fixing, Gribov copies, non-linear configuration space).

## Extensions to Gauge Theories

- **UV Stability for QED in d=3** (arXiv:2009.01156, Ann. Henri Poincaré 2022) — Balaban's RG for spinor QED on 3D toroidal lattice. UV stability bound in fixed finite volume, weak coupling. Significant: applies method to fermions + gauge field, not just pure gauge.
- **Non-linear sigma models** (arXiv:2204.08252, 2022) — Background-field/variational framework for NLSMs (share key features with gauge theories: non-linear target space).
- **Generating functions with scaled fermions and bosons** (Ann. Henri Poincaré 2019) — Generating function methods within Balaban's framework.

## Significance for the Millennium Problem

Dimock's work is the primary channel through which Balaban's methods might eventually be extended to close Gap 1 (observable control on T⁴). His φ⁴₃ treatment serves as a tutorial/testbed for the gauge theory generalization.
