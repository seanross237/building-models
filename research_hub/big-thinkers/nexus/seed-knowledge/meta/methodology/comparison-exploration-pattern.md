## Lesson

When two approaches or frameworks seem related, a direct comparison exploration is highly productive. Structure the comparison across multiple explicit dimensions (e.g., propagator, fixed points, RG flow, ghosts, spectral dimension, recent literature) and force a definitive conclusion — "same, complementary, or distinct." This produces clear, actionable results in a single exploration.

## Evidence

- **yang-mills strategy-001 exploration 004** — Compared lattice numerical evidence against rigorous mathematical results using a 6-dimension framework. The structured comparison produced the precise classification table (numerical vs. rigorous status) and identified the key gap (finite → continuous gauge groups). The explorer found the key settling paper (Sen-Wetterich-Yamada 2022) because the framework directed attention to the right comparison dimensions.

## When to apply

When two approaches, results, or frameworks appear to overlap and you need to understand the relationship. Especially productive for:
- Numerical results vs. rigorous proofs (as in lattice YM)
- Competing approaches to the same problem
- Results from different subfields that may be related

## Key design elements

1. **Multi-dimensional framework:** Give the explorer explicit dimensions to compare along. This prevents cherry-picking similarities or differences.
2. **Force a verdict:** Asking "same, complementary, or distinct?" prevents the explorer from hedging indefinitely.
3. **Pre-load context:** Results from earlier explorations provide essential background — the explorer can synthesize rather than re-research. The spectral dimension connection in the QG mission was "our unique contribution" and only possible because prior exploration results were available.

## Additional Evidence

- **compton-unruh strategy-001 exploration-005** — Detailed comparison of T_U/T_dS approach vs.
  Verlinde (2016) required a dedicated exploration (E005) and revealed facts not visible from the
  survey (E003). The comparison showed: Verlinde derives only the deep-MOND limit (not a full
  interpolation function), fails the RAR (Lelli 2017), and predicts a₀ = cH₀/6 via an equation
  (M_D² = (cH₀/6G)r² d(rM_B)/dr) that has no analog in the T_U/T_dS approach. The two theories
  are genuinely distinct on 6 dimensions. The initial survey had characterized Verlinde as "most
  theoretically grounded surviving proposal" — the structured comparison revealed that characterization
  was incomplete (Verlinde fails RAR; T_U/T_dS gives better galaxy phenomenology but worse numerics).
  **Lesson reinforced:** Reputation and phenomenological superficiality require detailed comparison
  to expose. The structured comparison is the only way to determine whether two approaches are
  genuinely different.
