# What We Care About Right Now

Eywa is being built as a self-evolving AI systems harness for hard problems: a durable
agent-orchestration system that breaks work into structured steps, retrieves
knowledge, plans, executes, evaluates, and improves itself over time through
experimentation and tight feedback loops.

When scoring relevance, prioritize material that helps answer questions like:

- How do agent systems improve themselves safely and measurably over time?
- What system structures create durable improvement loops instead of one-off wins?
- How should planning, execution, evaluation, retrieval, and escalation interact?
- What kinds of experiments, A/B tests, and regression checks actually improve performance?
- How do we make agent workflows inspectable, stateful, and artifact-driven?
- How do strong research systems use agents to solve hard math and physics problems?
- What lessons transfer from Atlas-style science systems into Eywa?

Material can be relevant without being directly about Eywa if it sharpens how we build:

- self-evolving agent systems
- eval and feedback loops
- multi-agent orchestration
- durable research systems
- agent-based systems for hard science
