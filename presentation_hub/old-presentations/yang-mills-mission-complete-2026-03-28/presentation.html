The Setup

There's a $1M math prize that's been open for 23 years

🏆
Millennium Prize — Yang-Mills Mass Gap
One of 7 unsolved math problems with a $1M prize each. Posted by the Clay Mathematics Institute in 2000. Only one has ever been solved (Poincaré Conjecture, 2003).
⚛️
What is Yang-Mills, in plain English?
The equations that describe how particles like quarks and gluons interact. The "mass gap" question asks: why do some particles have mass when the theory seems to predict they shouldn't? Physics has used the answer for 60 years — but no one has ever proved it rigorously.
🎯
What Atlas was asked to do
Not solve the full prize — that's decades away. Make concrete, defensible, rigorous progress. Find where the frontier actually is and push it forward.

How did they get 8×? (expand for details)
SZZ bounded a quantity called H_norm at ≤ 1 (too conservative). Atlas found the true bound is ≤ 1/8, using a tighter version of the same triangle inequality. The improvement is elementary once you know where to look — the hard part was knowing which bound to target. Strategy 001 mapped to SZZ; Strategy 002 found the slack; Strategy 003 tried to close the remaining gap to 1/12.
Important context: The physical deconfinement transition is at β ≈ 2.3. These bounds are all deep in the strongly-confined regime. The Millennium Prize requires the continuum limit (β → ∞). This is real progress on the mathematical frontier — not a prize solution.
