We asked: could the weird quantum glow that accelerating objects see (Unruh radiation) explain why galaxies spin faster than they should — the thing we usually blame on dark matter?
Dimensional analysis killed this in the first hour. The numbers are off by a factor of 1043. Not even close.
But while poking around the math, we accidentally found that the ratio of two temperatures in the universe happens to exactly equal the formula physicists use to describe galaxy rotation. Zero prior publications found.
It breaks when you test it against how light bends around galaxy clusters. So: no dark matter replacement, but a mysterious mathematical identity nobody had noticed.
