The Drum Nobody Can Build

The Riemann Hypothesis is one of the biggest unsolved problems in math — it's about where certain special numbers (zeta zeros) live on the number line.

One approach: maybe these numbers are secretly the vibration frequencies of some mathematical drumhead. If we could find the drum, we'd prove the hypothesis.

We tested every proposed drum design against 10 specific requirements the real zeros satisfy. Every design failed the same test: the real zeros are 47% more "organized" than any drum can produce.

We now know exactly why they're so organized (it's because of prime numbers), but nobody knows how to build a drum that organized.

We also caught ourselves making 3 false claims during the investigation — the system's error-checking worked.
