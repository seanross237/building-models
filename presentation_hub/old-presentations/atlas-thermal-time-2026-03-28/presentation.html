The Question

Connes-Rovelli Thermal Time Hypothesis (1994)
In generally covariant quantum theories, there is no background time parameter. TTH proposes that physical time emerges from the modular flow of the quantum state — the same mathematical structure that governs thermal equilibrium. Time isn't fundamental; it's what you experience because the universe is in a particular state.

Core Finding

Modular Time Is Preparation-History Time

✅

The Central Discovery
Across all 8 regimes, modular flow generates evolution under the Hamiltonian that prepared the state, not the one currently governing it. Post-quench: modular flow ticks at pre-quench frequencies. Excited states: entanglement-spectrum frequencies set at preparation. The discrepancy grows in the continuum limit (N0.33–N0.44).

Explain Like I'm 5

The Watch Factory Analogy

There's a theory that says time isn't fundamental — it's just what you experience because the universe is in a particular quantum state. Like how a hot cup of coffee has a "direction" (it cools down) even though the laws of physics work the same backwards.

We tested this by computing what "time" this theory predicts for 8 different physical setups. For systems in equilibrium, it gets time exactly right — but that's trivially true (it's basically the definition).

For anything out of equilibrium — like a system that just got disturbed — the theory's clock ticks at completely wrong frequencies. It remembers how the system was prepared, not what's happening now.
