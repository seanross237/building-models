The Run

Best Adversary Attacks

11 attacks total. Three stood out — including the quantum-woo instruction paying off.

Fatal Attack 3 — P5 is a bare assertion
The key philosophical claim — that temporal experience requires a time-directed substrate — was asserted, not argued. The adversary used a devastating counter-analogy: color experience doesn't require a colored substrate, so why should time experience require a time-directed one?
Major Attack 8 — Quantum is window dressing
The theory promised a three-way connection involving quantum mechanics, but only engaged with decoherence — the destruction of quantum features. The whole theory could be restated in classical thermodynamic terms. The quantum-woo instruction made the adversary catch this bait-and-switch.
Major Attack 11 — ISR definition is thermodynamically backwards
D3 defined ISR as moving to "fewer degrees of freedom," but thermodynamic irreversibility involves entropy increase (more accessible states). The core definition was inconsistent with the physics it claimed to describe.

Theory v2 — What Changed

All 4 must-fix and all 5 should-fix items addressed. A more honest, more modest theory.

The Key Move: Structural vs. Qualitative
v1's fatal weakness was asserting P5 without argument. v2 splits it into a 5-step argument: temporal direction is a structural feature of experience (unlike color, which is qualitative), and structural features require structurally matching substrates. This single argument resolved the fatal attack and grounded the consciousness connection.

Takeaways

System Learnings

What this run reveals about the adversarial system itself.

Quantum-Woo Instruction Worked
Attack 8 ("quantum is window dressing") was the adversary's sharpest methodological catch. It identified a bait-and-switch that would have survived without the domain-specific priming. The instruction produced a targeted, high-quality attack rather than generic skepticism.
Judge Recalibrated Correctly
The adversary rated Attack 1 as Fatal; the judge downgraded it to Major (the modesty premise partially addressed it). Attack 9 (hard problem) was downgraded from Major to Minor. The judge prevented overcorrection on 3 attacks while confirming the 1 true fatal.
Theory Got More Honest, Not Just Better
v2's biggest wins were admissions: R1-R3 are acknowledged as established results, the quantum connection is honestly narrowed, and the hard problem is explicitly declined. The pipeline pushed the theory toward intellectual honesty, not just logical repair.
The Adversary's Remaining Bias
Attack 9 used the hard problem of consciousness as a specific indictment of this theory. The judge flagged this as unfair — no theory solves it. Domain-specific priming (quantum woo) helped on one axis, but generic philosophical complaints still leak through.
