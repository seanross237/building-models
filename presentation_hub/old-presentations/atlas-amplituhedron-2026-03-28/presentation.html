Strategy

Compute First, Then Break It
Verify the amplituhedron reproduces known QFT results exactly, then systematically probe where it diverges, contributes new physics, or reveals structure invisible to Feynman diagrams.

Mission Question

Does the amplituhedron encode physical principles that standard QFT cannot access — or is it a mathematically elegant reformulation that produces identical predictions?

Findings

Three-Layer Answer

The answer depends on which theory you're working in.

Layer 1 — Within N=4 SYM
Exact Reformulation
Identical predictions to Feynman diagrams. Verified computationally to 10⁻¹⁵ precision across three independent methods (Parke-Taylor, BCFW, Grassmannian). Locality and unitarity emerge from positivity — but no experiment can tell the difference.
Same answers Proven all n, L, k
Layer 2 — Beyond N=4 SYM
Three Real Physical Contributions
The geometry does things Feynman diagrams cannot:
1
UV finiteness as selection principle. Full amplituhedron requires UV finiteness — selects only N=4 SYM and ABJM from all gauge theories.
2
EFT-hedron positivity bounds. Constrains which effective field theories can exist. Verified for QED (c₂/c₁ = 7/4 exactly).
3
Hidden zeros. 2024–2025 discovery connecting phi³, pion, and gluon amplitudes through shared geometric zeros. Locality and unitarity emerge at 1-loop.
Layer 3 — The Emerging Paradigm
Surfaceology
All amplitude calculations in colored theories = integrals over moduli spaces of surfaces. Handles all loops, all multiplicities, non-planar. Its relationship to the amplituhedron is the field's biggest open question.
All loops Non-planar Non-SUSY

Explain Like I'm Five

The Jewel vs The Pictures

ELI5

When particles collide, physicists calculate what comes out using Feynman diagrams — thousands of complicated pictures. The amplituhedron says: forget the pictures, the answer is just the volume of a jewel-like shape in a higher-dimensional space.

We tested whether this jewel gives different answers than the pictures. For one specific theory (N=4 super Yang-Mills), it gives exactly the same answers — it's just a much prettier way to get them.

But it also does three things the pictures can't:

🚫
Which theories can exist
🚧
What new physics is possible
🔗
Hidden connections between theories

It's not just a calculator — it knows things Feynman diagrams don't.
