# Calibration

## What This Run Is Probably Right About

- The local memo strongly narrows the frontier.
- Reopening classical estimate programs would ignore the main evidence.
- The object-definition bottleneck is real and should dominate route scoring.

## What This Run Is Less Certain About

- Whether the exact synthesis is truly historically novel.
- Which critical space is the right home for the minimal singular object.
- Whether microlocal observables can be made rigorous enough for the needed
  leakage bound.

## Overconfidence Risks

- Treating a good synthesis as if it were already a viable proof path.
- Assuming the microlocal literature is closer to theorem utility than it may be.
- Assuming the Tao mechanism is the only meaningful blowup archetype.

## Adjustments

- Keep the claim at "most promising apparently underexplored route found in
  checked sources."
- Frame the microlocal component as a support technology, not as the route's
  entire engine.
- Keep a backup branch alive in which the packet variables are defined by a more
  classical profile-decomposition plus frequency-envelope extraction, without
  the newer microlocal machinery.
