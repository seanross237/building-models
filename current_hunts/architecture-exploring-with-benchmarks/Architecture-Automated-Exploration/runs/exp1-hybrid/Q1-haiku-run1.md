# Q1 Analysis: Tardigrade Protein Gelation FTIR Study

## Step 1: Assign FTIR Peaks (Ground in Literature)

**AMIDE I region assignments (1600-1700 cm⁻¹):**
- 1645 cm⁻¹ (broad) → random coil / disordered structure
- 1652 cm⁻¹ (sharp) → alpha helix
- 1618 cm⁻¹ (sharp) → intermolecular beta sheet (antiparallel)
- 1680 cm⁻¹ (sharp) → antiparallel beta sheet component

**Evidence basis:** FTIR amide I band position directly reports secondary structure due to C=O stretch frequency variation with hydrogen bonding geometry. Random coils ~1645, alpha helices ~1650-1658, beta sheets ~1610-1640.

---

## Step 2: Analyze Initial State (Room Temperature)

**Given:** "They are initially disordered"

**FTIR evidence confirms:**
- 1645(br) is DOMINANT initially
- 1652, 1618, 1680 are present but weak (sh = sharp, indicating structured species exist)

**Starting state = DISORDERED with minor ordered components**

---

## Step 3: Heating Experiment

**Observation:**
- 1645 grows stronger upon heating
- 1618 and 1680 disappear upon heating

**Interpretation:**
- Disappearance of 1618 + 1680 → beta sheets DISASSEMBLE
- Growth of 1645 → disorder INCREASES
- This is helix unfolding/sheet melting → disorder return

**Conclusion from heating:** Confirms beta sheets were formed during gelation; heating melts them back to disorder.

**Critical test:** 1652 (helix) behavior NOT explicitly stated as disappearing. This is a constraint: helix may persist OR partially unwind. The plan states helix forms during gelation (see next step).

---

## Step 4: Concentration Titration (Critical Evidence)

**Observation:** "dual increase in 1652 cm⁻¹ and 1618 cm⁻¹ as concentration increases"

**What this means:**
- 1652 (alpha helix) INCREASES
- 1618 (intermolecular beta sheet) INCREASES
- Both increase TOGETHER during gelation

**Starting point:** Disordered (1645 dominant)

**Transition:** Disordered → mixture of alpha helix + beta sheet

**This RULES OUT:**
- A (helix unfolding) — helix FORMS, not unfolds
- B (disorder → helix only) — beta sheets also form
- C (coiled-coils) — no evidence
- D (helix → sheets) — both form simultaneously
- E (sheets → helix) — both form simultaneously
- F (disorder → sheets only) — helices also form
- G (parallel → antiparallel sheets) — not the direction of change
- H (sheets → disorder) — opposite of observed increase
- **I (disorder → sheets AND helices) — MATCHES**

---

## Step 5: Opposite Test (EXCLUSION Logic)

**Could this be option F (disorder → beta sheet only)?**
- No. Concentration titration shows DUAL increase of 1652 AND 1618.
- If only sheets formed, 1652 would stay flat or decrease as helix converts to sheet.
- The fact that 1652 increases proves helix forms, not just sheets.

**Could this be option B (disorder → helix only)?**
- No. Concentration titration explicitly shows 1618 increases.
- 1618 is assigned to beta sheet. Beta sheet formation is observed.

**Could the 1652 peak be something other than helix?**
- FTIR literature strongly assigns 1650-1658 to alpha helix.
- This is not ambiguous in the amide I region.

---

## Conclusion

**Evidence chain:**
1. Starting state = disordered (stated + 1645 dominant)
2. Heating experiment = helix + sheets disassemble → disorder (1618, 1680 vanish; 1645 grows)
3. Concentration titration = 1652 + 1618 increase together (both helix and sheet form)

**The transition is:** Disordered → Alpha helix + Beta sheet

**ANSWER: I**
