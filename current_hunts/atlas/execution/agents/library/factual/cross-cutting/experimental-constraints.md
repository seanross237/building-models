# Experimental Tests of Quantum Gravity: Current Status (2025-2026)

## GQuEST: Tabletop Quantum Gravity Detection

The GQuEST (Gravity from the Quantum Entanglement of Space Time) experiment aims to conduct the first direct observations of quantum gravity effects using an ultra-sensitive tabletop laser interferometer. Construction is underway at Caltech (optical and mechanical components) and Fermilab (readout systems, control mechanisms, cryostats, and vacuum vessels). The instrument extends sensitivity beyond the standard quantum limit by counting individual photons.

GQuEST targets "geontropic" spacetime fluctuations proposed by Verlinde and Zurek that emerge from quantum entanglement entropy. The photon-counting technique uses superconducting nanowire single-photon detectors (SNSPDs) with narrowband optical filter cavities providing more than 200 dB suppression of carrier light. This achieves detection in less than a minute compared to approximately 2 months for conventional homodyne readout at equivalent significance. The design targets arm length L = 5 meters with 10 kW circulating power, optimizing detection around 15.6 MHz.

Published in Phys. Rev. X 15, 011034 (Feb 2025).

**For QG+F:** GQuEST tests holographic/entropic spacetime fluctuations — a qualitatively different prediction. QG+F is a perturbative QFT on a classical background with no pixellon field. QG+F modifications are at ~10⁻³⁵ m, 17 orders below GQuEST sensitivity (~10⁻¹⁸ m). A null result is consistent with QG+F; a positive result would be evidence AGAINST QG+F.

## Unified Framework for Spacetime Fluctuations (January 2026)

A team led by the University of Warwick developed the first unified framework for detecting spacetime fluctuations, published in Nature Communications. The study sorts spacetime fluctuations into three broad categories with distinct, measurable signatures appearing in laser interferometers -- from the 4 km LIGO to compact laboratory systems such as QUEST (Cardiff University) and GQuEST (Caltech). LIGO serves as an excellent "yes/no" detector due to its long arm cavities, while the smaller QUEST and GQuEST provide more detailed information about the nature of the fluctuations due to their wide frequency coverage.

Publication: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-025-67313-3

## Primordial Gravitational Waves

The tensor-to-scalar ratio in the CMB constrains the amplitude of primordial gravitational waves and their QG completions. Current bound: **r < 0.036** (BICEP/Keck 2021, 95% CL). This constrains inflationary models and their quantum gravity completions (e.g., asymptotic safety inflation).

**Key experiments:**
- **Simons Observatory**: 3 SATs operational since 2024, 6 total by 2027. Target σ(r) ≤ 0.003 (5yr), optimistic σ(r) ~ 0.0007 (10yr with delensing)
- **BICEP Array**: target σ(r) ≤ 0.003, results ~2028
- **LiteBIRD** (JAXA): launch JFY 2032, target σ(r) < 0.001, first results ~2036-2037
- ~~**CMB-S4**~~: **CANCELLED (July 2025)** — DOE and NSF jointly withdrew support. Was expected to achieve σ(r) ≈ 0.0005. Its cancellation shifts the QG+F vs. AS discrimination timeline from "definitive by 2035" to "possible by 2034, definitive by 2037" via SO + LiteBIRD.
- **DECIGO**: targets stochastic GW background, n_T measurement. 2040s.

See [`cmb-experimental-timeline.md`](cmb-experimental-timeline.md) for the full timeline.

Source: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/JHEP12(2024)024

## Gravity-Induced Entanglement Tests (BMV)

Experiments testing whether gravity can entangle two massive objects (Bose 2017, Marletto-Vedral 2017) are becoming feasible, though recent analysis (October 2025) shows that interpreting such entanglement as evidence for quantum gravity is "not unambiguous." Key developments: nanodiamond interferometers with NV centers (arXiv:2410.19601, Oct 2024); some semi-classical theories may also produce entanglement (arXiv:2510.20991, Oct 2025); new proposal without observable spacetime superpositions (arXiv:2506.21122, June 2025).

**For QG+F:** BMV tests whether gravity is quantum at all — a binary yes/no question. QG+F predicts YES, but so do ALL quantum gravity theories. At BMV energies (E ≪ M₂), only the massless graviton mediates; fakeon and scalaron don't contribute. **Cannot discriminate QG+F from alternatives.**

## First GW Ringdown Constraints on Quadratic Gravity (2025)

**arXiv:2506.14695 (June 2025):** First gravitational wave ringdown constraints on quadratic-gravity theories. Analyzed GW150914, GW190521, GW200129 with ringdown-only SNR ≲ 7. 90% C.I. bounds on coupling length scales: ℓ < 34-49 km (depending on variant). Constrains M₂ > 5×10⁻¹² eV — **40 orders of magnitude weaker** than QG+F's expected M₂ ~ M_Planck, but an important methodological milestone.

Source: https://phys.org/news/2025-10-entanglement-gravity-quantum-complicated.html; arXiv:2506.14695

## Cross-Cutting Significance

Experimental quantum gravity research is inherently cross-cutting: interferometric searches for spacetime fluctuations test predictions from holographic models, string theory, and loop quantum gravity simultaneously. Gravitational wave observations from LIGO-VIRGO-KAGRA can constrain quantum corrections to black hole area theorems predicted by loop quantum gravity. The convergence of tabletop experiments, astrophysical observations, and cosmological measurements creates a multi-scale experimental program that does not privilege any single theoretical approach.
