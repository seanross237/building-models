# Holographic Principle: Recent Developments in Quantum Gravity (2025-2026)

## Swampland Constraints from Holographic Principles

Researchers (Upadhyay, Reshetnyak, Moshin, Castro) established how fundamental holographic principles, including constraints on energy conditions, translate into limitations on viable gravitational theories. They developed a unified holographic inequality encapsulating the Distance, Weak Gravity, and de Sitter conjectures, showing that all Swampland bounds derive from a single geometric condition governing holographic effective action convexity. A central finding reveals that moduli-space geometry is an emergent information metric determined entirely by boundary entanglement properties -- the field-space metric functions as a Fisher information metric on boundary density matrices.

## Emergent Holographic Spacetime from Quantum Information

Tadashi Takayanagi (June 2025, arXiv:2506.06595, published in Physical Review Letters) demonstrated how holographic duality describes gravitational theories in terms of quantum many-body systems. Quantum information theory provides a crucial tool connecting microscopic structures to the geometries of gravitational spacetimes, with entanglement entropy in quantum many-body systems calculable from the area of an extremal surface in the corresponding gravitational spacetime. The work establishes that spacetime geometry may not be fundamental but rather emerges from underlying quantum correlations, positioning quantum information as more fundamental than classical spacetime.

## Extending Beyond AdS/CFT

Research published on ResearchGate (publication 383697161) develops a comprehensive framework extending holographic principles beyond AdS/CFT to general spacetimes by incorporating quantum information measures such as entanglement entropy and complexity into modified Einstein field equations, creating a Universal Holographic Principle applicable to diverse geometries including cosmological spacetimes.

## Mathematical Forms of Entropy Bounds

Three key formulations of the holographic entropy bound:

- **Bekenstein bound:** S ≤ 2πRE/(ℏc) for a system of size R and energy E
- **Spherical bound:** S ≤ A/(4G) for a system that fits inside a sphere of area A
- **Bousso (covariant) bound:** S[L] ≤ A/(4G) for any lightsheet L of a 2-surface of area A

The Bousso bound is the most precisely formulated version and appears to hold in all known contexts. Key developments (2024-2025):
- Bousso bound proved for arbitrary diffeomorphism-invariant gravitational theories with higher-derivative corrections (2025) — significantly strengthens its status as a universal constraint
- Quantum Bousso bound verified in JT gravity (2024)

## Cross-Cutting Significance

The holographic principle is arguably the most powerful cross-cutting theme in quantum gravity. Originally discovered in string theory through AdS/CFT, holographic ideas now inform loop quantum gravity (through holographic entanglement entropy at horizons), causal set theory, and emergent gravity programs. The connection between entanglement and geometry -- the Ryu-Takayanagi formula and its generalizations -- provides a common language across approaches.
