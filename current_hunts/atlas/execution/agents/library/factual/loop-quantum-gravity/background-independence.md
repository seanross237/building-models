# LQG Background Independence and Diffeomorphism Invariance

## Background Independence

LQG is formally background independent: its equations are not embedded in or dependent on a fixed background spacetime metric. There is no background metric on spacetime. Instead, spacetime geometry is expected to emerge dynamically at distances much larger than the Planck length (~10^-35 m).

This implements Einstein's core insight from general relativity: physical objects are localized in space and time only with respect to each other, not with respect to any absolute structure. Quantum states in LQG cannot be localized at specific points — they represent excitations of spacetime itself rather than excitations within a pre-existing spacetime.

## Diffeomorphism Invariance

Background independence is manifested as diffeomorphism invariance of the action. In GR, displacing all dynamical objects in spacetime simultaneously does not generate a different physical state but merely an equivalent mathematical description of the same state.

LQG takes this symmetry seriously: physical states must remain invariant under the generators of diffeomorphisms. The diffeomorphism constraint is imposed as one of three quantum constraints (along with the Gauss constraint for SU(2) gauge invariance and the Hamiltonian constraint for dynamics).

## Contrast with String Theory

This is a key distinguishing feature from perturbative approaches like string theory, which are typically formulated on a fixed background spacetime. LQG's background independence is considered one of its primary conceptual strengths, directly encoding the lesson of general relativity that spacetime geometry is dynamical.

## Technical Implementation

The diffeomorphism-invariant Hilbert space is constructed by taking equivalence classes of spin network states under spatial diffeomorphisms (s-knot states). Two spin networks related by a smooth deformation of the underlying graph represent the same physical state.

## Historical Context

The concept of background independence traces back to the philosophical debate between Newtonian absolute space and Leibnizian relationalism. Einstein's general relativity vindicated the relational viewpoint by making spacetime geometry dynamical rather than fixed. Background independence means that "space and time are no fixed structure, but take part in the dynamical evolution of the world." LQG inherits and extends this principle into the quantum domain.

## Detailed Contrast with String Theory

String theory approaches differ fundamentally on this point:
- String theory generally starts with quantized excitations on top of a classically fixed background spacetime, making it **background dependent**.
- This "may be a useful approximation, but it cannot be the formulation of a fundamental theory" according to background-independence advocates.
- Recent string theory research (1990s onward) has pursued background-independent formulations through M-theory, but this program remains incomplete.
- LQG, by contrast, is manifestly background independent from the start, eliminating the need for a background metric.

## Knot Theory Connection

Diffeomorphism invariance eliminates redundancy in the kinematical Hilbert space, reducing it from a non-separable to a separable space labeled by knot invariants. Physical states become characterized by topological properties -- knot theory naturally emerges from this mathematical structure.
