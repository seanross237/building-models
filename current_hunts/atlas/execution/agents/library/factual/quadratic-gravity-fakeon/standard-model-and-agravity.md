# Standard Model Connections and the Agravity Program

How QG+F connects to the Standard Model of particle physics: coupling structure, beta functions with matter, gauge group non-prediction, the agravity (adimensional gravity) program, baryogenesis, dark matter, and the strong CP problem.

## QG+F + Standard Model Coupling

The QG+F action coupled to matter:

    S = ∫d⁴x √g [-Λ_bare + (M_P²/2)R + (1/2f₂²)C² - (1/6f₀²)R² + L_SM]

Non-minimal couplings ξ_a φ_a² R between scalar fields and the Ricci scalar are **required by renormalizability** — generated by quantum corrections even if set to zero classically (Salvio 2018).

### Beta Functions with Matter

**f₂ (Weyl² coupling):** With N_S real scalars, N_f Weyl fermions, N_V vectors:

- Vectors contribute -(N_V/5) f₂⁴
- Fermions contribute -(N_f/20) f₂⁴
- Scalars contribute -(N_S/60) f₂⁴

All contributions have the **same negative sign** as gravitational self-interactions. **f₂ is always asymptotically free regardless of matter content.** More matter = more AF. No upper bound on species from f₂ AF.

For the SM: N_V = 12, N_f = 45 (48 with right-handed neutrinos), N_S = 4.

**f₀ (R² coupling):** NOT asymptotically free in general. Resolution (Salvio-Strumia 2017): f₀ can grow to large values where conformal mode decouples, theory flows to conformal gravity at infinite energy. Requires all scalars be asymptotically conformally coupled (ξ_a → -1/6).

See `physical-beta-functions.md` for the physical (gauge-invariant) beta functions.

### Gauge Group Non-Prediction

**QG+F does NOT predict the SM gauge group SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1).** Gravity couples universally to the stress-energy tensor and does not distinguish between gauge groups. Confirmed by Anselmi (2025, JHEP 07, 175): "strict renormalizability" serves as a selection principle among already-specified theories, not as a predictor of particle content. Gauge anomaly cancellation restricts fields, but this is a SM constraint, not gravitational.

### Total Asymptotic Freedom (TAF)

TAF — the requirement that ALL couplings flow to zero at infinite energy — does constrain BSM physics (Giudice, Isidori, Salvio, Strumia, JHEP 2015). The pure SM is NOT TAF-compatible:

- U(1)_Y coupling g₁ runs to a Landau pole
- Top Yukawa y_t is not AF
- Higgs quartic λ_H is not AF

TAF requires SM extensions. Salvio (2016) showed adding **3 right-handed neutrinos with sub-EW masses** is a minimal viable extension.

## The Agravity Program (Salvio-Strumia)

"Agravity" = "Adimensional gravity." The core idea: **the fundamental theory contains no dimensionful parameters.** The action contains only R²/f₀² and C²/f₂² — no Einstein-Hilbert term (M_P² R), no cosmological constant (Λ), no mass terms. All are generated by dimensional transmutation.

Key papers: Salvio & Strumia, JHEP 06 (2014) 080; EPJC 78 (2018) 124

### Scalar Field Content

The theory requires at least one additional scalar field S (the "Higgs of gravity") beyond the SM Higgs H:

    ℒ_BSM^adim = |D_μS|² - λ_S|S|⁴ + λ_HS|S|²|H|² - ξ_S|S|²R

Key scalars: **S** (gravitational Higgs, generates M_P via VEV), **H** (SM Higgs, generates v = 246 GeV), **scalaron** (spin-0 from R²/f₀², mass M₀ = f₀M̄_Pl/√2), conformal mode (from four-derivative kinetic term).

### How Dimensional Transmutation Generates M_P

The mechanism is a gravitational Coleman-Weinberg (CW) process. In a classically scale-invariant theory with gravity, the Einstein-frame potential is:

    V_E(S) = M̄_Pl⁴ · λ_S(s) / ξ_S²(s)

where s ≡ ⟨S⟩ and all couplings run with RG scale μ̄ ≈ s. Consistent vacuum conditions:

    λ_S(s) = 0           → vanishing CC at minimum
    β_{λ_S}(s) = 0       → stationarity condition (minimum of V_E)
    ξ_S(s)⟨S⟩² = M̄_Pl²  → observed Planck mass

Near the minimum: λ_S(μ̄ ≈ s) ≈ (b/2) ln²(s/⟨s⟩), where b = d(β_{λ_S})/d(ln μ).

**Critical difference from non-gravitational CW:** In standard CW (e.g., scalar QED), V_min < 0 always. In agravity, V_E can vanish at the minimum when λ_S(s) = 0, because the gravitational coupling ξ_S enters differently. This allows (near-)zero CC rather than O(M_Pl⁴).

### Hierarchy Problem Solution

In a classically scale-invariant theory, there is no hierarchy problem (no fundamental mass scales to compare). ALL masses generated by dimensional transmutation:

1. **Planck mass:** Gravitational CW mechanism via S-field VEV (see above)
2. **Weak scale:** Higgs mass via Coleman-Weinberg mechanism at a much lower scale
3. **Technical naturalness:** Small f₀, f₂ (≲ 10⁻⁸) enhance conformal symmetry — technically natural
4. **f₂ is dynamically small:** The beta function (4π)²df₂²/d(ln μ̄) = -f₂⁴(199/15 + N_V/5 + N_f/20 + N_s/60) < 0 is **asymptotically free** — f₂ is driven to zero by RG running. This is a genuine dynamical explanation, not fine-tuning.

Naturalness requires f₂ ≲ 10⁻⁸ (Higgs naturalness condition), trading the hierarchy problem for why f₂ is small — answered by AF.

### Cosmological Constant in Agravity — The Achilles Heel

**Tree level: Λ = 0.** The CW condition λ_S(s) = 0 at the minimum means Λ_eff = V_E(⟨S⟩) = M̄_Pl⁴ · λ_S(⟨s⟩)/ξ_S²(⟨s⟩) = 0. This solves the "old" CC problem — the CC is NOT O(M_Pl⁴).

**One-loop: Λ ≫ observed.** Radiative corrections from the heaviest particles (spin-2 ghost ~ M₂⁴ ~ f₂⁴M_Pl⁴, scalaron ~ M₀⁴ ~ f₀⁴M_Pl⁴) give V_1-loop ~ f₂⁴M_Pl⁴/(16π²) ~ 10⁻³⁴ M_Pl⁴. Even with f₂ as small as possible, this is **~10⁸⁸ times too large** vs. observed Λ ~ 10⁻¹²² M_Pl⁴. Salvio-Strumia explicitly acknowledge: "the cosmological constant is at least 60 orders of magnitude larger than the observed value" even with M₀ as light as possible.

**Why scale invariance is insufficient (Kugo 2020, arXiv:2004.01868):** Classical scale invariance is *necessary* (without it, CC receives uncontrollable power-law divergences) but *insufficient* — radiative corrections destabilize any flat direction. An additional symmetry or mechanism is needed. Weinberg's no-go theorem has been generalized: no continuous symmetry in a gravitational theory can protect the CC.

**Cascading dimensional transmutation (explored and FAILS):** The idea that multiple stages of CW-like exponential suppressions could produce the hierarchy M_Pl/Λ^{1/4} ~ 10³⁰ was investigated. A four-stage cascade (UV→M_Pl→M_GUT→v→Λ^{1/4}) produces the right numerology IF each stage gives ~10⁻⁷·⁵ suppression. **The mechanism fails physically: QG+F has no IR-active gauge group that confines at meV scales.** f₂ is AF (generates UV scale, not IR), f₀ grows toward UV, and no SM coupling confines at 10⁻³ eV. A hidden gauge sector with confinement scale Λ_hidden ~ 10⁻³ eV would be needed — this goes beyond QG+F.

**Closest existing idea:** Maggiore et al. (2015, arXiv:1506.06217) proposed that the R² coupling undergoes dimensional transmutation in the deep IR, generating a nonlocal mass m ~ H₀ that produces dark energy. But this requires nonlocal gravity (beyond QG+F), and m must be fit to data, not predicted.

**Three approaches attempted:**
1. **Original agravity (2014):** CC tuned to zero at tree level by λ_S = 0. Radiative corrections destroy this.
2. **Agravity up to infinite energy (2018):** f₀ → ∞ decouples conformal mode; accidental SUSY can cancel Planckian CC but only with ad hoc conditions.
3. **Unimodular QG+F (Salvio 2024, PLB 855, 138920):** Old CC problem genuinely solved — vacuum energy does not gravitate, Λ is an integration constant independent of Λ₀. New CC problem NOT solved; Salvio invokes "multiverse made by different eras in a single big bang" with anthropic selection.

**Bottom line:** Dimensional transmutation can set Λ = 0 at tree level (valuable — solves old CC problem), but cannot explain why Λ ≈ 10⁻¹²² M_Pl⁴ rather than zero. No mechanism within QG+F generates a scale at ~meV. The CC remains the Achilles heel of all continuum QG theories. No work on predicting Λ from agravity's dimensional transmutation appears in the 2024-2026 literature.

## Fakeon Collider Phenomenology

The dressed propagator of a fakeon near its mass shell has irreducible energy uncertainty:

    ΔE ≳ Γ_f/2

The Breit-Wigner peak is **smeared** by Γ_f/2 — a fundamental quantum gravity prediction, not experimental limitation.

A fake scalar doublet coupling to the muon can explain the muon anomalous magnetic moment discrepancy, with masses below the EW scale allowed.

## Baryogenesis and Leptogenesis

Three mechanisms connecting QG+F to baryon asymmetry:

1. **Scalaron decay → leptogenesis:** The scalaron (mass ~3×10¹³ GeV) decays during reheating into right-handed neutrinos (if M_N < m_scalaron), which generate lepton asymmetry → baryon asymmetry via sphalerons. Naturally embedded in QG+F/Starobinsky inflation.

2. **Gravitational baryogenesis:** The coupling ∂_μR × J^μ_B generates baryon asymmetry during the inflation-to-radiation transition. Purely gravitational, requires no new particles.

3. **Reheating temperature:** Starobinsky inflation gives T_reheat ~ 10⁸-10⁹ GeV, high enough for standard thermal leptogenesis (requires optimization for M_N ~ 10⁹ GeV).

## Dark Matter Connections

1. **Scalaron as DM mediator:** Purely gravitational production from Starobinsky reheating is viable for a range of DM masses. Constrained by isocurvature perturbations and Lyman-α data.
