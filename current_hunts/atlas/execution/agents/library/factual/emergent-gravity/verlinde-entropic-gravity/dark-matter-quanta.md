# Verlinde's Emergent Gravity vs Dark Matter (Quanta Magazine)

A detailed 2016 Quanta Magazine article ("The Case Against Dark Matter") explains Verlinde's emergent gravity theory and its challenge to the dark matter paradigm.

## Theoretical Mechanism

Verlinde proposes that gravity is not fundamental but emerges from quantum information encoded in "qubits." Space-time and its contents represent a hologram arising from an underlying network of quantum bits, much as the three-dimensional environment of a computer game is encoded in classical bits on a silicon chip. Rather than invisible dark matter particles, the apparent extra gravity comes from dark energy interactions with matter.

As Verlinde states: "dark matter is an interplay between ordinary matter and dark energy."

When matter disrupts long-range entanglement between underlying qubits (modeled as thermal energy), dark energy responds elastically -- creating gravitational effects that mimic dark matter's influence on galaxy rotation.

## MOND Recovery

Verlinde's calculations recover the equations of Modified Newtonian Dynamics (MOND), a 30-year-old alternative gravity theory. His framework explains why gravity transitions from Newton's inverse-square law to an inverse-distance relationship at a specific acceleration threshold: 0.00000000012 meters per second squared (1.2 x 10^{-10} m/s^2), or 100 billion times weaker than Earth's surface gravity.

## Supporting Observational Evidence

- **Galaxy Rotation Study (McGaugh)**: Analysis of 153 galaxies revealed a universal relationship between rotation speeds and visible matter distribution, suggesting dark matter may be unnecessary
- **Gravitational Lensing (Brouwer et al.)**: Dutch researchers tested Verlinde's predictions against data from 30,000+ galaxies, finding his formulas "correctly predict the gravitational distortion" of light, with no free parameters

## Criticisms

- **Incompleteness**: Verlinde acknowledges unfinished calculations, particularly regarding cosmic microwave background predictions -- a major test where dark matter models succeed
- **Theoretical Gaps**: Physicist Brian Swingle notes Verlinde "lacks a concrete model universe" with the mathematical rigor available for simpler frameworks
- **Dark Matter Defenders**: Some astrophysicists argue dark matter and visible matter quantities correlate during galaxy formation, explaining the McGaugh findings without abandoning dark matter
