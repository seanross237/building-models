# Ryu-Takayanagi Formula and Gravity from Entanglement

Shinsei Ryu and Tadashi Takayanagi proposed in 2006 (arXiv:hep-th/0603001) a holographic formula relating entanglement entropy in a conformal field theory to the geometry of an associated anti-de Sitter spacetime. This is a cornerstone of the modern "gravity from entanglement" program.

## The Formula

The entanglement entropy S_A of a region A in a d+1 dimensional conformal field theory is given by:

**S_A = Area(gamma_A) / (4 G_N)**

where gamma_A is the minimal-area codimension-2 surface in the dual AdS_{d+2} bulk that is anchored on the boundary of region A, and G_N is Newton's constant. This is directly analogous to the Bekenstein-Hawking formula for black hole entropy but replaces the event horizon with a minimal surface.

## Validation

The proposal was verified by:
- Agreement with known results in 2D conformal field theory applied to AdS_3
- Comparison with predictions for N=4 super Yang-Mills in the AdS_5 x S^5 context

## Implications for Emergent Gravity

The Ryu-Takayanagi formula implies that:
1. **Spacetime geometry encodes entanglement**: The geometric data on the gravitational side is "emergent" and can be reconstructed from entanglement observables in the dual field theory
2. **Gravity from quantum information**: A gravitational spacetime can emerge from an enormous number of entangled qubits
3. **ER=EPR connection**: Mark Van Raamsdonk and others have argued that spatial connectivity itself emerges from quantum entanglement -- disentangling regions of a CFT corresponds to tearing apart the dual spacetime

## Modern Status

The Ryu-Takayanagi conjecture has been proven within the AdS/CFT framework (Lewkowycz-Maldacena 2013) and generalized to the covariant Hubeny-Rangamani-Takayanagi (HRT) formula for time-dependent cases. It remains one of the strongest pieces of evidence that spacetime geometry is emergent from quantum information.
