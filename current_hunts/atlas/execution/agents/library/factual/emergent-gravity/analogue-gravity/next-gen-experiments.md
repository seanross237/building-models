# Next-Generation Analogue Gravity Experiments

A comprehensive review of the next generation of analogue gravity experiments (published in Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society A, 2020) documents how laboratory condensed matter systems are being used to simulate gravitational phenomena and test emergent spacetime ideas.

## Experimental Systems Currently in Use

1. **Fluids and superfluids** -- the original platform for analogue gravity
2. **Bose-Einstein condensates (BECs)** -- the most productive experimental system since ~2008
3. **Superconducting circuits** -- used for dynamical Casimir effect
4. **Ultra-cold atoms** -- precision platforms for Hawking radiation studies
5. **Optical systems** -- light in moving dielectrics and optical fibers
6. **Polaritons in semiconductor microcavities** -- newer platform

## Key Observed Phenomena

**Hawking Radiation**: The primary experimental focus since 2008, with observations in multiple media:
- Surface waves in open channel flows (2011)
- Optical fibers (2012-2019)
- Bose-Einstein condensates (2016 onward, Steinhauer at Technion)
- Extraction of Hawking correlations from classical noise in water systems

**Superradiance**: Amplification of waves from rotating black hole analogues, demonstrated with water vortices and rotating geometries.

**Negative Frequency Waves**: Generated in optical fibers and bulk crystal systems between 2012-2018.

**Cosmological Effects**:
- Dynamical Casimir effect in superconducting circuits
- Cosmological redshift in expanding condensates (2018)
- Phonon pair creation mimicking cosmological particle production (2019)

## Three Phases of the Field

1. Theoretical work (1981-2008): Beginning with Unruh's 1981 sonic black hole proposal
2. Experimental demonstrations of Hawking effects (2008-2017)
3. Current "next generation": Pursuing broader phenomena and refined measurements

## Core Theoretical Insight

The crucial insight from analogue gravity is that Hawking radiation represents "a fundamental curved-space quantum field theory phenomenon that occurs whenever a horizon is present in an effective geometry" -- independent of general relativity specifically. Even though the underlying fluid dynamics is Newtonian, nonrelativistic, and takes place in flat space-plus-time, the fluctuations (sound waves) are governed by a curved (3+1)-dimensional Lorentzian spacetime geometry.
