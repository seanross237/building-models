# Graviton Propagator in Asymptotic Safety

The momentum-dependent graviton propagator is a central object for extracting physical predictions from asymptotically safe gravity. Recent work has reconstructed the Lorentzian graviton propagator from Euclidean functional RG data.

## Propagator Decomposition

The graviton field decomposes into distinct modes:
- **Transverse-traceless (TT) mode**: the physical spin-2 graviton
- **Spin-0 (trace) mode**: a scalar degree of freedom
- **Pure gauge vector mode**: unphysical, removed by gauge fixing

Each mode has its own propagator and spectral function.

## Key Result: Spectral Function Properties

Bonanno, Denz, Pawlowski, and Reichert (SciPost Phys. 12, 001, 2022) reconstructed the graviton propagator and found:

1. **Dynamical (fluctuation) graviton propagator**: Its spectral function is positive. This is the propagator that enters physical cross sections and scattering amplitudes.

2. **Background graviton propagator**: Its spectral function necessarily has negative parts, similar to the gluon spectral function in QCD. This is a gauge artifact.

## Unitarity Implications

The positivity of the dynamical graviton's spectral function is a necessary condition for unitarity (the optical theorem requires positive spectral weight for physical states). The authors argue this "may hint at the unitarity of asymptotically safe quantum gravity."

This is significant because a major concern about asymptotic safety is whether higher-derivative terms generated by the RG flow introduce ghost-like degrees of freedom that would violate unitarity. The positive spectral function of the physical graviton suggests this may not occur.

## Momentum Dependence

The dressed graviton propagator shows nontrivial momentum dependence:
- In the IR (momenta far below the Planck scale): standard 1/p^2 behavior (massless graviton)
- In the UV (momenta above the Planck scale): modified by quantum corrections encoded in momentum-dependent form factors

## Open Issues

- The detailed form of the propagator depends on gauge choice and truncation
- Full resolution of unitarity requires going beyond the spectral function to compute scattering amplitudes
- Background independence of the results needs further investigation
