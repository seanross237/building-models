# Swerves: Phenomenological Predictions from Causal Set Discreteness

## The Swerves Model

Dowker, Henson, and Sorkin (2004) developed a phenomenological model in which massive particles propagating through Minkowski spacetime undergo small random deviations from geodesic motion due to the underlying causal set discreteness. This effect is called "swerving," named after Lucretius's concept of atomic swerves (clinamen).

In the continuum, massive particles travel on timelike geodesics. But the discrete causal set structure induces fluctuations in the particle's worldline. Crucially, this diffusion occurs in momentum space and is Lorentz invariant -- it does not select a preferred reference frame.

## The Key Argument: Discreteness Without Lorentz Violation

The central thesis of the paper challenges a widespread misconception. As the authors state: "a fundamental spacetime discreteness need not contradict Lorentz invariance." They demonstrate that "a causal set's discreteness is in fact locally Lorentz invariant."

This is the opposite of what happens with regular lattice discretizations, which break Lorentz symmetry. The random Poisson sprinkling used in causal set theory preserves the symmetry.

## Observable Consequences

The momentum-space diffusion is governed by a single phenomenological parameter (the diffusion constant). The authors speculated that this mechanism "could have any bearing on the origin of high energy cosmic rays," specifically the puzzle of trans-GZK cosmic ray events -- detections of cosmic rays with energies above 5 x 10^19 eV that appear to have no obvious astrophysical source within the GZK horizon of about 20 Mpc.

Since swerving causes particle trajectories to deviate slightly from exact geodesics, distant point sources would appear "fuzzed" -- their apparent positions would be spread out, potentially explaining why some ultra-high-energy cosmic ray sources cannot be identified.

## Current Status and Observational Constraints

At large scales, the microscopic swerves manifest as Lorentz invariant diffusion in energy-momentum. **The swerves parameter is severely constrained:** k ≤ 10⁻⁵⁶ kg²m²s⁻³ from spontaneous heating bounds on hydrogen gas. The original hope that cosmic ray anomalies could be attributed to swerves has been **ruled out** — the effect is inconsistent with laboratory bounds. Direct observational confirmation remains far beyond current technology.
