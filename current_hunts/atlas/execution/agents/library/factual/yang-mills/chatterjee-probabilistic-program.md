## Overview

Sourav Chatterjee (Stanford) has pursued a probabilistic reformulation of the constructive Yang-Mills problem, distinct from Balaban's RG approach.

## Key Results

1. **"Yang-Mills for probabilists"** (arXiv:1803.01950, 2018) — Survey reformulating the constructive YM problem in probabilistic language. Authoritative bibliography of Balaban's papers.

2. **Wilson loop expectations for finite gauge groups** — Exact leading-order behavior:
   - Z₂ gauge group (Chatterjee 2020)
   - Finite abelian groups (Forsström-Lenells-Viklund 2020)
   - All finite groups (Cao 2020)

3. **Strong mass gap ⟹ quark confinement** — Proved that if a strong form of the mass gap holds, then Wilson's area law follows. Important conceptual link but conditional on the unproven mass gap.

4. **Scaling limit of SU(2) lattice Yang-Mills-Higgs** (arXiv:2401.10507, 2024) — First construction of a scaling limit of a non-abelian lattice Yang-Mills theory in dimension > 2, with Higgs field. **However, the limit is Gaussian (trivial).**

## Assessment

Important for conceptual framework and finite/abelian groups. Has NOT produced a non-trivial continuum limit for non-abelian gauge theories. The trivial SU(2) YMH limit (2024) is notable as the first non-abelian construction in d > 2 but does not advance the mass gap or non-trivial continuum limit problems.
