# Constraints on Viable Quantum Gravity Theories

Systematic catalog of constraints that any viable quantum gravity theory must satisfy. Organized into structural (mathematical consistency), recovery (reproducing known physics), and precision (quantitative experimental bounds). 32 total constraints identified, with 15 genuinely independent. Five are maximally restrictive: UV completion, unitarity + ghost freedom, diffeomorphism invariance, Lorentz invariance bounds, and Bekenstein-Hawking entropy.

The intersection of all constraints is remarkably tight — structural constraints alone (unitarity + correct DOF + UV completion) rule out most naive quantization attempts. Combined with recovery and precision constraints, the surviving theory space is extremely narrow.
