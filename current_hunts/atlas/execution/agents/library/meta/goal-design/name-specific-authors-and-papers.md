## Lesson

When the goal involves literature search, name specific authors and papers to target. This gives the explorer a directed search strategy instead of broad, unfocused web searches. Combine named targets with "and any others you find" to get both thoroughness and independence.

## Evidence

- **strategy-001 exploration 001** — Providing 7 named programs plus "any others you find" let the explorer be both thorough and independent. It found 5 additional programs beyond what was specified (Oppenheim, Barandes, Doukas, Singh's aikyons, Doubly QM).
- **strategy-003 exploration s3-002** — Named specific authors (Falls, Knorr, Platania) gave the explorer a targeted search strategy. Combined with pre-loaded context, this produced a clean 176-line report.
- **strategy-004 exploration s4-003** — Naming specific papers (Codello et al., Falls-Litim-Schroder) gave targeted searches that let the explorer quickly evaluate specific claims.
- **yang-mills strategy-001 exploration 001** — Naming Balaban, Jaffe-Witten, Chatterjee, and other specific targets led the explorer to independently discover Dimock, Chandra-Hairer, and Faria da Veiga-O'Carroll — confirming the "named targets + 'any others you find'" pattern works across very different domains.

## When to apply

Any exploration involving literature search or verification. Especially important for narrow technical questions where the relevant authors are known. Less critical for broad divergent surveys where discovery is the point, though even there, naming starting points helps.
