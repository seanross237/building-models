## Lesson

Devil's advocate explorations should run immediately after theory construction, not at the end of the cycle. Finding fundamental flaws early prevents wasting explorations on less important problems. Give the devil's advocate the FULL framework document to attack, not a summary -- summaries hide weaknesses.

## Evidence

- **strategy-001 exploration 005** — Found the Lorentzian signature flaw. Retrospective: doing this at exploration 005 instead of earlier would have redirected exploration 004 (which focused on a less fundamental issue).
- **strategy-003 exploration s3-008** — Found "Predictive FAIL" and the ~60% tautology concern. The "is this trivially true?" attack was the most insightful. Explorer read the full 415-line construction and found weaknesses specific to the details.
- **strategy-003 exploration s3-009** — Response to devil's advocate revealed that most "predictions" were consistency checks, not discriminators. This honest assessment was only possible because the attacks had been mounted first.
- **strategy-004 exploration s4-006** — Full prediction catalog attack. Null hypothesis test showed H0 explains everything the framework claims.
- **strategy-004 exploration s4-007** — Having the devil's advocate BEFORE the synthesis document meant the document could honestly incorporate the attacks.

## When to apply

After every construction or synthesis exploration. The sequence is: construct -> attack -> revise (or synthesize). Never skip the attack step. Never summarize the construction for the attacker.
