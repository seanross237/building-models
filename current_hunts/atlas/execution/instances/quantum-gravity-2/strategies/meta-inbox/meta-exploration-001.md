# Meta-Learning: Exploration 001

**What worked well:**
- Providing 12 known debts as starting context was very effective — the explorer verified/expanded them rather than starting from scratch, saving significant time.
- The four-criteria rating system (severity, specificity, alternative coverage, explanatory opportunity) produced a well-structured output that's directly actionable for Phase 2.
- Asking for a TOP 5 ranking with justification gave a clear decision input for the strategizer.
- The explorer found genuine new findings from 2025-2026 literature (analyticity sacrifice, causality abandonment essay, IHO interpretation).

**What didn't work well:**
- Explorer took ~3 minutes on initial thinking before writing anything to REPORT.md. The "write skeleton first" instruction was eventually followed but there was a delay. May need to say "IMMEDIATELY write a skeleton before doing ANY research."
- The 200-400 line target was exceeded (510 lines), but the extra content was valuable, so this wasn't a real problem.

**Lessons for future gap-mapping surveys:**
- Pre-loading known context from the library accelerates the explorer significantly.
- The comparative analysis (what do OTHER frameworks do for each gap?) was the most valuable part of the ranking — it separates universal hard problems from QG+F-specific opportunities.
- Having clear success/failure criteria (≥15 debts, ≥3 new, all rated, ranking included) made it easy to assess the output.

**Scope assessment:** Scope was right. One comprehensive survey exploration, well-defined deliverable.
