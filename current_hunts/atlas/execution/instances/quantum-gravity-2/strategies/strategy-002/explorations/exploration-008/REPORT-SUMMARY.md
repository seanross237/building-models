# Exploration 008 Summary: Fakeon Interpretation Landscape — Unified Assessment

## Goal
Synthesize findings from Explorations 003–007 across three approaches to interpreting QG+F's fakeon prescription (causal order, indivisibility, entanglement). Verify Wheeler propagator bridge novelty. Assess convergence, unique contributions, open problems, and honest value to QG+F.

## Outcome: SUCCEEDED

## Key Findings

**Convergence:** All three approaches converge on "the ghost can't be real" but through genuinely different intermediate mechanisms. The convergence is non-trivial in mechanism (causal order provides a constructive derivation, indivisibility explains the analyticity sacrifice, entanglement embeds the fakeon in emergent gravity) even though the conclusion is guaranteed by the shared requirement of a positive-definite Hilbert space (unitarity).

**Wheeler Propagator Bridge — Novelty CONFIRMED:** Six targeted literature searches found zero papers connecting Belenchia et al.'s causal set result (Wheeler propagator for unstable modes) to Anselmi's fakeon prescription. The tree-level identity G_fakeon = G_Wheeler, combined with its independent emergence from causal set dynamics, is a genuinely novel observation absent from the literature. This is the strongest concrete finding across all explorations.

**Unique contributions:** Causal order uniquely explains WHY the fakeon propagator specifically (constructive SJ derivation). Indivisibility uniquely explains WHY analyticity must be sacrificed (irreducible multi-time correlations). Entanglement uniquely places the fakeon within the emergent gravity paradigm (MVEH self-consistency).

**Open problems (5 identified):** Loop-level completion (NEAR-FATAL for causal order), spin-2 SJ construction, whether causal set actions produce quadratic gravity (currently evidence is AGAINST), nonlinear MVEH as prescription discriminator, QFT extension of Barandes-Doukas.

## Key Takeaway

Our work is **primarily interpretive, anchored by one genuinely novel mathematical observation** (the Wheeler propagator bridge). Two of three approaches (indivisibility, entanglement) add conceptual framing without new mathematical content. The causal-order approach comes closest to derivation but is fatally limited at loop level. The appropriate output is a perspective/review article, not a research paper — except possibly for the Wheeler bridge alone, which merits a focused short paper connecting the causal set and QG+F programs.
