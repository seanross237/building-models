# Exploration 002 Summary: BH Evaporation Phase Transition — Quantitative Predictions

## Goal
Derive all quantitative predictions from the BH evaporation phase transition in the unified QG+F–AS framework, with numbers and classifications.

## What Was Done
Combined the Bonanno et al. 2025 instability threshold (r_H ≈ 0.876/m₂) with the NGFP ghost mass (m₂ ≈ 1.42 M_P from Exploration 001) and the Schwarzschild relation to derive M_crit, T_crit, and related quantities. Used web research on QCD deconfinement (lattice results, Polyakov loop, latent heat) and CDT phase diagrams to assess the transition order. Computed PBH remnant DM implications.

## Outcome: SUCCEEDED (with honest caveats)

**12 predictions derived and classified:**
- 6 NOVEL (M_crit = 0.31 M_P, T_crit = 0.13 M_P, three-phase picture, transition order, latent heat estimate)
- 4 INHERITED from AS (remnant mass/temperature, heat capacity sign change, PBH DM)
- 1 CONSISTENCY CHECK (M_rem > M_crit ordering)
- 1 DISCRIMINATING (ghost confinement trigger mechanism itself)

## Key Takeaway

**Most BH predictions are INHERITED from Asymptotic Safety alone.** The unified framework's genuine contribution is narrow: a ghost confinement trigger mechanism and specific numerical values (M_crit, T_crit) that are completely unobservable. The endpoint of evaporation (Planck-mass remnant, T → 0) is entirely from AS. The trigger (Schwarzschild instability) is entirely from QG+F. The unified framework combines them but generates no new testable prediction beyond AS.

**Critical finding:** There is a significant convention dependence (m₂ = 1.42 M_P vs m₂ = 1.42 M̄_P) that changes whether the ghost confinement instability is preempted by the AS transition (M_crit < M_rem) or actively drives it (M_crit > M_rem). This is the single largest systematic uncertainty.

## Leads Worth Pursuing

2. **CDT with matter fields** — the prediction that matter softens the transition from first-order to crossover could potentially be tested on the lattice
3. **The ghost propagator prediction from Exploration 001 remains the sharpest discriminator** — it's a theoretical calculation, not an astrophysical observation, but it's actually computable
