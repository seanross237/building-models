# Meta-Learning: Exploration 001

**Type:** Divergent conceptual survey + framework synthesis attempt

**What worked well:**
- Asking for a survey + deep dive + synthesis in one exploration was the right scope. The explorer handled all three tasks well.
- Specifying "honestly assess novelty" and "does it actually derive QM or smuggle it in?" produced high-quality critical analysis. The explorer didn't oversell.
- Providing specific programs to research (7 named) plus "any others you find" let the explorer be both thorough and independent (it found Oppenheim, Barandes, Doukas, Singh's aikyons, Doubly QM — all beyond what I specified).
- Specifying "focus on recent (2024-2026) developments" got genuinely current results.

**What could be improved:**
- The synthesis section is compelling but schematic. Next time, for a construction exploration, I should ask for specific equations/derivations rather than conceptual architecture.
- The explorer's report was well-structured but could have been more explicit about which claims are sourced from papers vs. the explorer's own reasoning.

**Lesson for future strategizers:** When doing a divergent survey exploration, ask for (a) comprehensive survey, (b) deep dive on top 2-3, (c) synthesis attempt, and (d) honest novelty assessment. This combo produces high-quality results in one run. Don't try to add construction on top — that needs its own exploration.
