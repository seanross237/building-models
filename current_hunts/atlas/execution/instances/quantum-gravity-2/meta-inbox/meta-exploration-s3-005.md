# Meta-Learning: Strategy-003 Exploration 005

**Type:** Tightly-scoped literature verification — BH compatibility

**What worked well:** Single-question pattern, tight scope, pre-loaded context with QCD analogy framework. Explorer produced 165 lines with a clear SUPPORTS verdict and 5 well-structured arguments. The pre-loaded QCD analogy framing was particularly effective — the explorer built on it rather than re-deriving it.

**Key insight:** The perturbative/non-perturbative split is the master key for reconciling QG+F and AS predictions. This same logic applies across all prediction domains (inflation, BH, spectral dimension). The explorer correctly identified that ALL apparent contradictions between QG+F and AS dissolve when you recognize the domain-of-validity split.

**What could be improved:** Could have asked the explorer to assess the spontaneous ghostification paper more critically — it's potentially a falsifier if the ghost is physical, but the explorer correctly noted that this assumes the wrong treatment (ghost-as-physical vs fakeon).

**Lesson:** When testing compatibility of predictions from different frameworks, pre-load the key reconciliation framework (in this case, the perturbative/non-perturbative regime split). This gives the explorer a hypothesis to test rather than an open-ended comparison.
