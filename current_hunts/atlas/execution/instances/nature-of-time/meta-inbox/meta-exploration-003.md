# Meta-Learning: Exploration 003

**What worked well:**
- Providing summaries of the two prior theses in the goal context helped the explorer maintain distinctness — the report is genuinely a third perspective, not a rehash
- Explicitly asking the explorer to engage critically with Wolfram (use strengths, critique weaknesses) produced balanced coverage
- The "epistemic vs. ontological" distinction question in the weaknesses section was crucial — the explorer treated it seriously

**What didn't work / lessons:**
- The explorer did extensive web research (~8 searches) before writing, which delayed initial progress on REPORT.md. For future explorations, might want to more strongly emphasize "write incrementally as you go."

**Scope assessment:** One thesis per exploration continues to be the right scope. All three Phase 1 reports are ~340-415 lines — substantial, comparable quality.

**Key strategic insight:** The three theses are more complementary than contradictory. Each excels where the others are weak:
- Entanglement: best on quantum physics integration
- Becoming: best on phenomenology and the "now"
- Computational: best on structural explanation (why one-dimensional, why directed)

This suggests synthesis may be more about combining than choosing. The explorer even identified a possible synthesis: "time is the computationally irreducible growth of entanglement." This is promising.
