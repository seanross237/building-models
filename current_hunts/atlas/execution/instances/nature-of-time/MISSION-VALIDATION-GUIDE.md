# Mission Validation Guide

The only real criterion: **does the interpretation hold up to rigorous logical critique?**

## Tier 1: Internal Consistency
- No contradictions between claims
- If it makes assumptions, it states them
- If it speculates, it knows it's speculating

## Tier 2: Logical Rigor
- Arguments follow from their premises
- Doesn't hand-wave past hard questions — engages with them directly
- Can survive a devil's advocate attack without collapsing

## Tier 3: Explanatory Power
- Actually explains something — doesn't just redescribe the problem in fancier language
- Addresses why time feels the way it does, not just what it "is" abstractly
- A thoughtful non-expert walks away understanding something new

## Tier 4: Boldness with Honesty
- Takes real positions, not "on the one hand / on the other hand"
- Explores ideas that might seem out there — but tests them seriously
- Earns its conclusions through argument, not assertion

## Tier 5: Completeness
- Doesn't dodge the hardest questions (why does time flow? why can't we go back? what is "now"? does time exist without observers?)
- Engages with the strongest counterarguments to its own position
- The final interpretation feels like a whole — not a collection of disconnected observations
