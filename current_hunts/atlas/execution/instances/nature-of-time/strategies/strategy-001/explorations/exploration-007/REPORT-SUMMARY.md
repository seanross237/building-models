# Exploration 007 — Report Summary

The refined synthesis ("Time is the computationally irreducible processing of quantum correlations, as encountered from within a relational perspective") was subjected to five targeted attacks. **Scorecard:** Attack 1 (LBB without Past Hypothesis) partially succeeds — the LBB triad explains the *mechanism* of irreversibility but still needs non-equilibrium conditions for the *direction*; the "no Past Hypothesis needed" claim must be weakened. Attack 2 (QRF doing real work) partially succeeds — the QRF framework is a genuine framework-clearing move but not a substantive ontological claim; the wave-particle analogy is too flattering and should be replaced with gauge equivalence. Attack 3 (factorization problem) **succeeds** — the most serious vulnerability; the synthesis presupposes subsystem decomposition that in quantum gravity is emergent, creating a vicious circularity; scope must be limited to "within the regime of well-defined subsystems." Attack 4 (the "now") partially succeeds — structural past/future asymmetry via Landauer is real, but the "computational frontier" is circular or perspectival; the "now" must be acknowledged as the weakest point. Attack 5 (simpler alternatives) is **repelled** — the synthesis is genuinely more explanatory than the thermodynamic arrow, Rovelli's relational time, and the block universe, explaining features (one-dimensionality, flow, open future) that no competitor addresses.

**Overall verdict:** The synthesis survives stress-testing as valuable and genuinely novel, requiring four mandatory modifications (weaken arrow claim, reframe Layer 1 as dissolution not ontology, acknowledge factorization scope limitation, acknowledge "now" weakness). Core strengths — the computational account of one-dimensionality, flow, the open future, and the LBB mechanism — survive all attacks unchallenged. The synthesis is ready for final articulation with these modifications.
