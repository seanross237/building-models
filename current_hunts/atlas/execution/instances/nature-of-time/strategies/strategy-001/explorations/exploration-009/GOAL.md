## The Interpretation

**"Time is the computationally irreducible processing of quantum correlations, as encountered from within a relational perspective."**

### What this means, layer by layer:

**Layer 1 — Framework Dissolution:** The old question "Is the universe really static or really evolving?" is like asking "Is electromagnetism really a field or really a potential?" — it's a choice of mathematical description, not a fact about nature. Hoehn's quantum reference frame program proves that "static" and "evolving" descriptions of constraint-satisfying quantum systems are mathematically equivalent, related by physical transformations. This dissolves the false binary that trapped prior approaches and frees us to ask the productive question: not "does time exist?" but "why does time have the specific properties it has?"

**Layer 2 — Computational Structure (the core):** Time's properties are structural consequences of information processing in a finite universe:
- **One-dimensional:** The Margolus-Levitin theorem bounds the rate of orthogonal state transitions to 2E/h. Processing is sequential — one step at a time. This is why time has one dimension while space has three.
- **Irreversible (mechanism):** The Landauer-Bennett-Bekenstein triad: finite storage (Bekenstein) forces erasure (Bennett), erasure is physically irreversible (Landauer: erasing 1 bit costs at least kT ln 2 energy). This is the ENGINE of irreversibility — it explains WHY time cannot be reversed (information destroyed, not merely redistributed).
- **Directed (arrow):** The LBB engine explains the mechanism, but the direction still requires the universe to be far from equilibrium. (A universe in thermal equilibrium satisfies all LBB conditions but has no arrow.) The Past Hypothesis provides the fuel for the engine.
- **Unshortcutable (open future):** The universe is computationally irreducible — there is no faster way to determine the future state than to run the computation step by step. This is not merely epistemic ("we can't predict") but structural (no subsystem can contain a model of its embedding universe that runs faster than the universe itself — Wolpert's self-prediction impossibility theorem).
- **Gravitationally dilated:** The ML bound sets the computational rate. In gravitational fields, energy available for local computation is redshifted, reducing the rate. This doesn't independently derive dilation (GR already tells us this), but it provides a computational interpretation: near massive objects, the universe computes more slowly.
- **Flowing:** The experience of temporal flow IS computational irreducibility from inside. A subsystem traversing an irreducible computation cannot skip ahead, cannot look ahead, cannot shortcut to the end. It must take each step sequentially. This is flow — not an illusion, not a brute given, but the structural consequence of being inside an irreducible computation.

**Layer 3 — Phenomenology:** Becoming is what this computational processing is like from within a bounded subsystem. Past/future asymmetry is physical: the past is partially erased (Landauer), the future is inaccessible (CI). The "now" is the least well-explained aspect — the synthesis provides structural past/future asymmetry at any point but doesn't fully explain why any particular point IS the present. This may be where physics reaches the boundary of what it can explain about temporal experience.

### The Honest Limits

1. **The "now":** The interpretation provides structural past/future asymmetry but does not explain what makes any particular point "the present." This may be an irreducible aspect of temporal experience.
2. **The factorization problem:** The interpretation presupposes that the universe admits a decomposition into subsystems. In our universe (atoms, molecules, observers), this is well-justified. In fundamental quantum gravity, the decomposition may be emergent. The interpretation describes time's properties *within the regime of well-defined subsystems*.
3. **The direction needs the Past Hypothesis:** The LBB engine explains WHY time is irreversible but not WHY the arrow points the way it does. The direction requires non-equilibrium initial conditions.
4. **No novel predictions:** The interpretation does not make empirical predictions that differ from standard QM + GR. Its value is explanatory unification, not prediction.
5. **The flow claim is interpretive:** "CI IS temporal flow" is a philosophical identification, not a deduction. It is the most well-motivated such identification available, but it cannot be proven.

## How to Write This

**Audience:** A thoughtful, intelligent reader. Not necessarily a physicist. Not dumbed down. Someone who reads serious non-fiction and wants to understand the world.

**Tone:** Direct, confident where warranted, honest about limits. Not academic hedging, not pop-science breathlessness. The tone of someone who has thought deeply about something and is sharing what they found.

**Structure:** You decide the best structure for maximum impact. Some possibilities:
- Start with the question ("What is time?"), then the answer, then the argument
- Start with what we all know about time (it flows, it has an arrow, we can't go back), then explain each feature
- Start with the most striking claim and work outward

**Length:** As long as it needs to be, no longer. Aim for thoroughness without bloat. Every paragraph should earn its place.

**Key writing principles:**
- Use concrete examples and thought experiments to make abstract ideas vivid
- When you use a technical term, explain it — but don't patronize
- Be direct about what's established, what's plausible, and what's speculative
- Don't hide weaknesses — stating them honestly is a strength
- Make the argument build — each section should make the reader want to read the next

## Success Criteria

- The interpretation is clearly stated and the argument follows logically
- The honest limits are acknowledged without apologizing
- The comparison with alternatives is fair and accurate
