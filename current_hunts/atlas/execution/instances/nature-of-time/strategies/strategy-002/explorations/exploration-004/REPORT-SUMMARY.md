# Exploration 004 Summary: Head-to-Head Tournament — CI vs. CP

## Goal
Run a fair, rigorous head-to-head tournament between the Computational Interpretation (CI) and the Causal-Processual Interpretation (CP) of time, scoring each on 13 criteria across six temporal features, four hard questions, and three meta-criteria.

## What Was Done
Evaluated both interpretations as an impartial judge, presenting each side's best case on every criterion and justifying every score (1-5 scale). Read CI's definitive articulation (strategy-001/exploration-009), CP's construction (exploration-002), and CP's adversarial results (exploration-003) as source material.

## Outcome: Near-Draw — CP wins 48.5 to 47.5

CP edges ahead by 1.0 point out of 65 possible. The margin is within the range where different weights would flip the result.

**CP's winning factors:** Directionality/arrow (+1.5, intrinsic arrow needing no Past Hypothesis), "What is now?" (+1.5, ontological distinction between past/present/future where CI acknowledges weakness), and explanatory depth (+1.0, deeper metaphysical engagement with what time IS).

**CI's strongest factors:** Physics grounding (+2.5, the single largest gap — every CI ingredient is established or proven), "Why can't we go back?" (+1.0, the LBB engine is the tournament's most compelling individual argument), and open future (+0.5, rigorous results from Wolfram + Wolpert).

## Key Takeaway

The most important finding is not who wins — it's that CI and CP converge on a shared insight: **time is constituted by an irreversible, one-way process that generates novelty and cannot be shortcut.** Whether you call this "computation" or "actualization," the underlying structure is remarkably similar. CI is the safer bet (established physics, no speculative ingredients); CP is the deeper exploration (genuine answers to "what is now?" and "why does the arrow point?"). A thoughtful person would benefit from knowing both. The question of which name is right may matter less than the convergence.

## Leads Worth Pursuing
- The convergence between CI and CP could be formalized — is there a meta-framework that subsumes both?
- CP's directionality advantage (no Past Hypothesis) deserves deeper scrutiny — is "minimal initial actuality" really different from the Past Hypothesis?
- CI's "now" weakness could potentially be addressed using elements of CP's ontological framework without buying the full package.
