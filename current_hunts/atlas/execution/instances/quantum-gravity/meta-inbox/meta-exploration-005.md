# Meta-Learning: Exploration 005

**What worked well:**
- Pursuing an independent direction (entanglement-first vs. spectral dimension) was the right call — it tested whether our main result could be independently confirmed
- The clear negative answer (entanglement is a framework, not a selector) is itself valuable and saves future effort
- The novel observation connecting spectral dimension and entanglement entropy is a genuinely useful finding

**What didn't work:**
- Explorer stalled briefly mid-report (185 lines for a while). May need to nudge more aggressively.

**Lessons:**
1. Not every direction leads to a new theory — some lead to "this approach can't do what we need, but here's what it CAN do." That's equally valuable.
2. The classification (constructive / selective / confirmatory) is a useful framework for understanding different types of constraints
3. Negative results should be reported clearly and without apology — they narrow the search space
