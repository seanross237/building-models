# Exploration 001: Structural and Recovery Constraints for Quantum Gravity

## Executive Summary

This report catalogs **32 distinct constraints** that any viable quantum gravity theory must satisfy, organized into four categories: structural (mathematical consistency), recovery (reproducing known physics), cross-framework convergences (results appearing across multiple approaches), and precision (quantitative experimental bounds). Each constraint is ranked by restrictiveness and annotated with mathematical form where applicable. Five constraints are identified as **maximally restrictive** — those that eliminate the largest classes of candidate theories.

The key finding is that the intersection of all constraints is remarkably tight. The structural constraints alone (especially unitarity + correct degrees of freedom + UV completion) already rule out most naive quantization attempts. When combined with recovery constraints (especially the graviton propagator IR limit and post-Newtonian matching) and the precision constraints from GW170817, the surviving theory space is extremely narrow. Cross-framework convergences (especially spectral dimension flow and Bekenstein-Hawking entropy) provide additional powerful filters that any novel construction must satisfy.

## A. Structural Constraints (Non-Negotiable Mathematical Consistency)

These are constraints that any mathematically consistent quantum theory of gravity must satisfy, regardless of experimental considerations. Violating any of these means the theory is internally inconsistent.

### A1. Unitarity (S-matrix Unitarity)

**Statement:** The S-matrix must be unitary: S†S = SS† = 1. Equivalently, the total probability of all possible outcomes of any scattering process must equal 1. For the graviton propagator, this requires positive spectral weight for all physical states (optical theorem).

**Mathematical form:**
- S†S = 1
- Im(M_forward) = (1/2) Σ_f |M_fi|² (optical theorem)
- Spectral function ρ(s) ≥ 0 for physical graviton modes

**Restrictiveness: HIGH (Maximally Restrictive)**

This eliminates:
- Higher-derivative gravity theories (R² + R_μν R^μν) generically introduce ghost states with negative spectral weight — the Ostrogradsky instability. However, recent work (2024) shows fictitious ghosts in truncations of an effective action may not indicate true unitarity violation when all operators are included.
- Naively quantized general relativity beyond one loop — perturbative non-renormalizability signals loss of predictivity, though not unitarity per se.
- Any theory with negative-norm physical states.

**Status across approaches:**
- **String theory:** Perturbatively unitary to all orders. However, the S-matrix "cannot be a unitary operator in Fock space" at high energies due to black hole production — unitarity may require a more general framework.
- **LQG:** Fundamentally unitary at the full quantum level. Apparent non-unitarity arises only from tracing over Planck-scale degrees of freedom (decoherence without dissipation, Perez 2021).
- **Asymptotic Safety:** The dynamical graviton spectral function is positive (Bonanno et al. 2022), which is a necessary condition. Full unitarity remains unproven.

### A2. Ghost Freedom (No Negative-Norm States)

**Statement:** The physical Hilbert space must have a positive-definite inner product. No physical (asymptotic) states may have negative norm. Ghost fields used in gauge fixing (Faddeev-Popov) are permitted, but they must decouple from the physical spectrum.

**Mathematical form:**
- ⟨ψ_phys|ψ_phys⟩ > 0 for all physical states
- BRST cohomology: physical states ∈ ker(Q_BRST) / im(Q_BRST)

**Restrictiveness: HIGH**

Closely related to unitarity. The key constraint is that higher-derivative terms in the gravitational action (which improve UV behavior) generically introduce massive ghost modes. The classic tension:

- **4-derivative gravity** (Stelle 1977): Renormalizable but contains a massive spin-2 ghost with m² ~ M_Pl²/α, where α is the R² coupling.
- **Infinite-derivative gravity** (IDG): Introduces an entire function exp(-□/M²) in the propagator, potentially avoiding ghosts while maintaining UV improvement. The propagator takes the form G(p²) ~ exp(p²/M²)/p², which has no additional poles.

Recent work (2024) suggests approaches using "kinetic positivity constraints" — imposing positivity of total kinetic energy and performing Lorentz transformations in field space — can render ghost-ridden theories quantizable.

**Status across approaches:**
- **String theory:** Ghost-free in the physical spectrum via the no-ghost theorem (Goddard-Thorn).
- **LQG:** No perturbative ghost issue — non-perturbative formulation avoids higher-derivative problems.
- **Asymptotic Safety:** Open question. Higher-derivative terms are generated by the RG flow. Whether the resulting ghosts are physical or fictitious (artifacts of truncation) is under active investigation.

### A3. Correct Degrees of Freedom

**Statement:** In 4D, a massless spin-2 graviton has exactly 2 physical polarizations (helicity ±2). The theory must not introduce additional propagating degrees of freedom unless they are either (a) experimentally viable (e.g., a massive graviton with 5 DOF) or (b) decoupled from low-energy physics.

**Mathematical form:**
For a massless spin-2 field h_μν in D=4:
- Total components: 10 (symmetric 4×4 tensor)
- Remove gauge: -4 (diffeomorphisms) × 2 = -8
- Physical DOF: 10 - 8 = 2

For a massive spin-2 field:
- Physical DOF: 5 (but the van Dam-Veltman-Zakharov discontinuity makes the m→0 limit problematic)

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM-HIGH**

This constraint is automatically satisfied by any theory that correctly implements diffeomorphism invariance. However, it strongly constrains:
- Massive gravity theories (which must deal with the vDVZ discontinuity and Boulware-Deser ghost)
- Bimetric theories (which introduce additional spin-2 modes)
- Higher-spin theories (which need to explain why extra modes aren't observed)

### A4. Diffeomorphism Invariance / Gauge Symmetry

**Statement:** The theory must be invariant under general coordinate transformations (diffeomorphisms), or deformations thereof that reduce to diffeomorphism invariance in the classical limit. This is the gauge symmetry of gravity.

**Mathematical form:**
Under x^μ → x^μ + ξ^μ(x):
- δg_μν = ∇_μ ξ_ν + ∇_ν ξ_μ (at linearized level)
- The physical observables must be diffeomorphism-invariant (or at least BRST-invariant)

**Restrictiveness: HIGH (Maximally Restrictive)**

This is one of the most powerful constraints because:
- Combined with Weinberg's soft graviton theorem, it forces the massless spin-2 particle to couple universally to the stress-energy tensor → uniquely selects Einstein gravity at leading order
- Horava-Lifshitz gravity explicitly breaks full diffeomorphism invariance (keeping only foliation-preserving diffeomorphisms), which generically introduces a problematic extra scalar mode
- Any discretization of spacetime (lattice, causal sets, spin foams) must show how diffeomorphism invariance emerges in the continuum limit

**Status across approaches:**
- **String theory:** Realized through worldsheet diffeomorphism invariance + target space gauge invariance of the string field theory
- **LQG:** Built in by construction — background-independent quantization preserves spatial diffeomorphisms, though the status of the Hamiltonian constraint (related to time diffeomorphisms) remains contentious
- **Asymptotic Safety:** Maintained as a Ward identity constraint on the effective action

### A5. UV Completion / Renormalizability

**Statement:** The theory must make sense at arbitrarily high energies (or equivalently, at arbitrarily short distances). This can be achieved through:
(a) Perturbative renormalizability (all divergences absorbed by a finite number of counterterms)
(b) Asymptotic safety (non-perturbative UV fixed point)
(c) UV finiteness (no divergences at all, as in string theory)
(d) Fundamental discreteness (no trans-Planckian physics, as in LQG/causal sets)

**Mathematical form:**
- Perturbative: Power-counting requires [G_N] = -2 in mass units, making gravity perturbatively non-renormalizable in D=4
- Asymptotic safety: lim_{k→∞} G_k = G* (fixed point) with finite-dimensional critical surface
- String theory: Amplitudes A_n ~ ∫ d(moduli) × (worldsheet correlators) are UV finite order by order

**Restrictiveness: HIGH (Maximally Restrictive)**

This is the fundamental obstacle in quantum gravity. Pure GR quantized perturbatively fails — the 2-loop divergence in pure gravity (Goroff-Sagnotti 1986) and 1-loop divergences in gravity + matter are non-renormalizable. This single constraint eliminates the most naive approach and forces one of the above escape routes.

### A6. Causality Preservation

**Statement:** The theory must not permit the construction of closed causal curves (no time machines) in physically realizable configurations. At minimum, the theory must reduce to a causal theory (signals propagate within or on light cones) in the classical limit.

**Mathematical form:**
- In the effective field theory: the retarded Green's function G_R(x-y) must vanish for spacelike separations
- Modified dispersion relations E² = p² + f(p/M_Pl) must not permit superluminal group velocity for signal propagation (though superluminal phase velocity may be acceptable)
- In higher-curvature gravity: the causal structure is determined by the fastest propagating mode, not the metric light cone

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM**

Causality is nuanced in quantum gravity:
- Higher-curvature theories can have superluminal graviton propagation, but this does not automatically imply acausality — the relevant causal structure is defined by characteristic surfaces, not metric null cones
- Noncommutative geometry generically modifies light cone structure
- String theory's Shapiro time delay for gravitons scattering off shock waves is always positive (causal), providing evidence for causality

### A7. Weinberg-Witten Theorem Compliance

**Statement:** Massless particles with spin j > 1 cannot carry a Lorentz-covariant stress-energy tensor T^μν. This constrains theories where the graviton is a composite or emergent particle — the graviton cannot be a bound state in a Lorentz-covariant quantum field theory with a conserved T^μν.

**Mathematical form:**
If |p, σ⟩ is a one-particle massless state with spin j > 1 and a conserved, Lorentz-covariant T^μν exists, then ⟨p', σ'|T^μν|p, σ⟩ = 0, meaning the particle doesn't gravitate — contradiction.

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM-HIGH**

This constrains emergent gravity programs. Known escape routes:
- **Holographic/AdS-CFT:** The graviton lives in a different (bulk) spacetime from the boundary theory
- **Broken Lorentz invariance:** If the UV theory breaks Lorentz symmetry, the theorem's assumptions fail
- **Gauge invariance of GR:** In GR itself, the stress-energy is not Lorentz-covariant (it transforms as a pseudo-tensor), so the theorem does not apply

## B. Recovery Constraints (Must Reproduce Known Physics)

These constraints require that the quantum gravity theory reduce to known physics in the appropriate limits.

### B1. Newtonian Gravity in the Weak-Field, Low-Velocity Limit

**Statement:** At large distances and low velocities, the theory must reproduce Newton's gravitational potential.

**Mathematical form:**
V(r) = -G_N M / r + O(r^{-2}) corrections

From the graviton exchange amplitude in the Born approximation:
- Single graviton exchange between two masses yields the Fourier transform of 1/|q|² → -1/r potential
- The gravitational coupling must give G_N = κ²/(32π) where κ is the graviton-matter coupling

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM**

Most theories satisfy this by construction, but it constrains:
- The IR behavior of the graviton propagator (must go as 1/q² for |q| → 0)
- Theories with modified IR gravity (e.g., massive gravity) must match to within experimental precision

### B2. Linearized Einstein Equations

**Statement:** The theory must reproduce the correct linearized Einstein equations for weak gravitational fields: □h̄_μν = -16πG T_μν (in Lorenz gauge, where h̄_μν = h_μν - ½η_μν h).

**Mathematical form:**
The graviton propagator in the IR must take the form:
G_μναβ(q) = (P^(2)_μναβ - ½ P^(0)_μναβ) / q²

where P^(2) and P^(0) are the spin-2 and spin-0 projection operators for a symmetric tensor.

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM**

Any theory with a massless spin-2 mode automatically satisfies this if diffeomorphism invariance is maintained. The key point: this fixes the tensor structure of the graviton propagator, not just its 1/q² falloff.

### B3. Graviton Propagator Matching GR in IR

**Statement:** The full dressed graviton propagator must reduce to the GR propagator at momenta far below the Planck scale: G(p²) → (P^(2) - ½P^(0))/(p² + iε) as p² → 0.

**Mathematical form:**
- At low momenta: G_TT(p²) ~ Z/p² with Z > 0 (positive residue = no ghost)
- The two running gravitational couplings (from the spin-2 and spin-0 sectors) must converge to the same value G_N in the IR

**Restrictiveness: HIGH**

This is stronger than just Newtonian gravity — it fixes the full tensor structure including the relative coupling of the spin-0 component. In asymptotic safety, Bonanno et al. (2022) verified that the dressed graviton propagator's two tensorial components have "non-trivial yet equal residues at the massless pole."

In LQG, the graviton propagator has been computed from spin foam models and shown to have "the correct large-distance asymptotics nonperturbatively and independently of the spin foam model used."

### B4. Three-Graviton Vertex Matching GR

**Statement:** The three-graviton vertex (cubic interaction) must match the one derived from expanding √(-g)R to cubic order, at least at momenta well below the Planck scale.

**Mathematical form:**
V₃^{μν,αβ,ρσ}(p₁,p₂,p₃) = (κ/2) × [specific tensor contractions of momenta and polarizations from expanding the Einstein-Hilbert action]

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM**

This is the leading nonlinear test of the theory. It determines:
- Gravitational light bending beyond the linearized approximation
- The self-coupling of gravity (gravity gravitates)
- Combined with Weinberg's theorem: if the spin-2 particle is massless and couples at all, the three-point vertex is uniquely fixed

### B5. Equivalence Principle

**Statement:** The Weak Equivalence Principle (WEP) — universality of free fall — must hold to extraordinary precision. The Strong Equivalence Principle (SEP) — equivalence of gravitational and inertial mass including for gravitational self-energy — may be violated in some theories.

**Mathematical form:**
- WEP: η(A,B) = 2|a_A - a_B|/|a_A + a_B| < 2.3 × 10⁻¹⁵ (MICROSCOPE, 2022)
- SEP: Tested via Nordtvedt effect in lunar laser ranging: |η_N| < 4.4 × 10⁻⁴

**Restrictiveness: HIGH**

The MICROSCOPE bound η < 10⁻¹⁵ is extraordinarily precise. This constrains:
- Any theory with composition-dependent gravitational coupling
- Theories with additional scalar fields that couple non-universally to matter (e.g., Brans-Dicke variants)
- The WEP is automatically satisfied if gravity couples universally to T_μν

### B6. Lorentz Invariance (or Violations Below Experimental Bounds)

**Statement:** The theory must preserve local Lorentz invariance, or any violations must be below current experimental bounds. This is tested through modified dispersion relations, time-of-flight measurements, and the Standard Model Extension (SME) framework.

**Mathematical form:**
Modified dispersion relation: E² = p²c² + m²c⁴ ± E² (E/E_QG)^n

For n=1 (linear LIV): E_QG > 5.9 × E_Pl (subluminal) from GRB 221009A/LHAASO
For n=2 (quadratic LIV): E_QG > 5.8 × 10⁻⁸ E_Pl from LHAASO

Hausdorff dimension constraint from electron g-2: d_H > 4 - 5 × 10⁻⁷
Lamb shift: d_H > 4 - 3.6 × 10⁻¹¹

**Restrictiveness: HIGH (Maximally Restrictive)**

The Lorentz invariance bounds are among the most powerful constraints in all of physics. The GRB 221009A results (LHAASO, 2024) push the linear LIV scale above the Planck energy, meaning any Planck-scale Lorentz violation with n=1 dispersion modification is essentially ruled out.

This severely constrains:
- **Horava-Lifshitz gravity:** Explicitly breaks Lorentz invariance; must show violations are suppressed at low energies
- **Some LQG variants:** Certain semiclassical limits predict modified dispersion relations
- **Causal set swerves:** Lorentz invariance is preserved statistically but local violations exist

### B7. Post-Newtonian Corrections Matching Solar System Tests

**Statement:** The theory must reproduce the correct post-Newtonian (PN) expansion of GR, parameterized by the PPN formalism, to within experimental precision.

**Mathematical form:**
Key PPN parameters:
- γ (light bending, Shapiro delay): γ = 1 + (2.1 ± 2.3) × 10⁻⁵ (Cassini)
- β (perihelion precession): |β - 1| < 8 × 10⁻⁵ (Mercury + lunar laser ranging)
- GR prediction: γ = β = 1

At 1PN order, the metric takes the form:
g₀₀ = -1 + 2U - 2βU² + ...
g₀ᵢ = -(1/2)(4γ + 3 + α₁ - α₂)Vᵢ + ...
gᵢⱼ = (1 + 2γU)δᵢⱼ + ...

**Restrictiveness: HIGH**

The Cassini bound |γ - 1| < 2.3 × 10⁻⁵ is extremely restrictive for any theory predicting deviations from GR at the post-Newtonian level. This rules out many scalar-tensor theories and constrains Brans-Dicke theory to ω_BD > 40,000.

## C. Cross-Framework Convergences

These are results that appear across multiple independent approaches to quantum gravity. For each, I assess whether it constitutes a genuine constraint or merely a common feature.

### C1. Spectral Dimension Running: d_s = 4 → 2 in UV

**Statement:** The effective (spectral) dimension of spacetime decreases from approximately 4 at large scales to approximately 2 at the Planck scale.

**Evidence across approaches:**
| Approach | UV d_s | Mechanism |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| CDT | ~2 (possibly 1.5) | Numerical simulation |
| Asymptotic Safety | 2 | Fixed point scaling (Newton's constant dimensionless in 2D) |
| String Theory | 2 | Hagedorn thermodynamics: F/V ∝ T² |
| Causal Sets | ~2.38 (Kleitman-Rothschild) | Myrheim-Meyer dimension |
| LQG/Spin Foams | 2 (or 2.5, 1 depending on model) | Average area scaling |
| Horava-Lifshitz | 2 | Explicit anisotropic scaling z=3 |
| Noncommutative Geometry | 2 | Modified dispersion in Snyder space |

**Two common mechanisms:** (1) Scale invariance at UV fixed points/phase transitions; (2) Asymptotic silence — light cone collapse decoupling spatial points.

**Assessment: LIKELY GENUINE CONSTRAINT — but exact value uncertain**

The convergence of 7+ independent approaches is striking. As Carlip argues: "It seems rather unlikely that so many different approaches would converge on the same result merely by accident." However, the exact UV value varies (1, 1.5, 2, 2.38) and the mechanisms differ. The safest constraint is: d_s < 4 in the UV (dimensional reduction occurs), with d_s ≈ 2 as the most likely value.

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM-HIGH**

### C2. Bekenstein-Hawking Entropy: S = A/(4ℓ_P²)

**Statement:** Black hole entropy is proportional to horizon area divided by four times the Planck area.

**Derivations across frameworks:**
1. **Semiclassical (Hawking 1975):** From QFT in curved spacetime
2. **String Theory (Strominger-Vafa 1996):** Microscopic counting of D-brane BPS microstates
3. **LQG:** Counting spin network punctures on the horizon
4. **Conformal Field Theory (Carlip):** From conformal symmetry at Killing horizons
5. **Thermodynamic Gravity (Jacobson 1995):** Assumed as input → derives Einstein equations

**Assessment: GENUINE HARD CONSTRAINT**

This is the single most important cross-cutting result in quantum gravity. Every serious approach must reproduce this formula. The fact that it follows from semiclassical reasoning alone (Hawking's calculation) makes it essentially model-independent for any theory that reduces to GR + QFT in the appropriate regime.

**Restrictiveness: HIGH (Maximally Restrictive)**

Any theory that cannot derive or at least accommodate S = A/(4G) is immediately suspect. The formula also implies the holographic principle — degrees of freedom scale with area, not volume.

### C3. Entanglement Area Law

**Statement:** The entanglement entropy of a region of space scales with the area of its boundary, not its volume: S_ent ~ A/ε² (where ε is the UV cutoff), generalizing to S_ent = A/(4G) in gravitational contexts via the Ryu-Takayanagi formula.

**Mathematical form (Ryu-Takayanagi):**
S_A = min_γ [Area(γ)/(4G_N)]

where γ is the minimal surface in the bulk homologous to the boundary region A.

**Evidence:**
- Free QFT: entanglement entropy obeys area law (2025, JHEP)
- Holographic CFTs: Ryu-Takayanagi formula (proven for static cases)
- Graviton entanglement: gauge-invariant prescription for generalized entropy (2025, JHEP)
- Gravitational collapse: entropy deviates from simple area law near horizon (2025)

**Assessment: GENUINE CONSTRAINT (with subtleties)**

The area law for entanglement entropy is robust and appears in both gravitational and non-gravitational contexts. The Ryu-Takayanagi formula provides the strongest form. Any quantum gravity theory must accommodate this scaling.

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM-HIGH**

### C4. Jacobson's Thermodynamic Derivation (Einstein Equations from Entanglement Equilibrium)

**Statement:** The Einstein field equations can be derived by demanding that δQ = TdS holds for all local Rindler horizons, where S = A/(4G) and T is the Unruh temperature.

**Mathematical form:**
Demanding δQ = TdS for all local Rindler horizons at every spacetime point, with:
- δQ = T_ab k^a dΣ^b (energy flux through horizon)
- T = ℏa/(2πc k_B) (Unruh temperature)
- dS = δA/(4ℓ_P²) (Bekenstein-Hawking entropy change)

yields R_ab - ½Rg_ab + Λg_ab = (8πG/c⁴)T_ab.

**Assessment: DEEP CONSISTENCY REQUIREMENT, NOT INDEPENDENT CONSTRAINT**

This is a derivation, not a constraint — it shows that the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy + thermodynamics + Rindler structure uniquely implies Einstein's equations. The constraint it implies is: any quantum gravity theory whose semiclassical limit has local Rindler horizons with entropy proportional to area MUST reduce to Einstein gravity (or close modifications thereof).

**Restrictiveness: HIGH** (as a constraint on the relationship between entropy and dynamics)

### C5. Holographic Entropy Bounds

**Statement:** The entropy in any region of space is bounded by the area of its boundary (in Planck units), not its volume.

**Mathematical forms:**
- **Bekenstein bound:** S ≤ 2πRE/(ℏc) for a system of size R and energy E
- **Spherical bound:** S ≤ A/(4G) for a system that fits inside a sphere of area A
- **Bousso (covariant) bound:** S[L] ≤ A/(4G) for any lightsheet L of a 2-surface of area A

**Recent developments (2024-2025):**
- Bousso bound proved for arbitrary diffeomorphism-invariant gravitational theories with higher-derivative corrections (2025)
- Quantum Bousso bound verified in JT gravity (2024)
- Unified holographic inequality encapsulating Distance, Weak Gravity, and de Sitter conjectures — all Swampland bounds may derive from a single geometric condition

**Assessment: GENUINE CONSTRAINT**

The covariant entropy bound (Bousso bound) is the most precisely formulated version and appears to hold in all known contexts. The 2025 proof for general higher-derivative theories significantly strengthens its status as a universal constraint.

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM-HIGH**

### C6. Page Curve and Information Conservation

**Statement:** Black hole evaporation must respect unitarity — the Page curve (entropy of Hawking radiation first rises, then falls) must be reproduced. The entropy of radiation cannot exceed the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy.

**Mathematical form:**
- Early time: S_rad(t) ~ (number of emitted quanta) — growing
- Page time: t_Page ~ G²M³/ℏ — entropy peaks
- Late time: S_rad(t) decreasing — purification

**Recent developments (2024-2026):**
- Island formula + asymptotic safety: entropy saturates at S_BH, supporting unitary evolution (2025)
- LQG: Island prescription applied to loop quantum geometry; quantum extremal surfaces exist (2025)

**Assessment: GENUINE CONSTRAINT**

The Page curve is a consequence of unitarity applied to black hole evaporation. Any unitary theory must reproduce it. The island formula provides a semiclassical mechanism, but the detailed UV completion determines how information is actually encoded.

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM** (most theories that satisfy unitarity will get this right in principle)

## D. Precision Constraints (Quantitative Experimental Bounds)

These are hard numerical bounds from experiment. Any theory making predictions outside these bounds is falsified.

### D1. Graviton Mass

**Statement:** The graviton mass must be extremely small or zero.

**Current best bounds:**
| Method | Bound | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| CMB (Yukawa) | m_g < 5 × 10⁻³² eV | 2024 |
| Large-scale structure | m_g < 4.7 × 10⁻²³ eV/c² (90% CL) | Sept 2024 |
| LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA (GW) | m_g < 1.27 × 10⁻²³ eV/c² | O3 run |
| S-star orbits (Sgr A*) | Improved over previous | 2024 (GRAVITY) |
| Solar System | m_g < 7.2 × 10⁻²³ eV | 2019 |

The CMB-based Yukawa bound is by far the tightest at m_g < 5 × 10⁻³² eV — 2.5 × 10⁸ times tighter than the LIGO bound — though model-dependent.

**Mathematical form:**
Massive graviton propagator: G(p²) = 1/(p² - m_g²) × [tensor structure with 5 DOF]
van Dam-Veltman-Zakharov discontinuity: lim_{m→0} massive propagator ≠ massless propagator

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM** (most QG theories have massless graviton by construction)

### D2. Gravitational Wave Speed

**Statement:** The speed of gravitational waves must equal the speed of light to extraordinary precision.

**Bound:** |c_gw/c - 1| < 6 × 10⁻¹⁵ (GW170817 + GRB170817A, 2017)

This is the near-simultaneous arrival of gravitational waves and gamma rays from a binary neutron star merger, with travel time ~130 million years.

**Mathematical form:**
In modified gravity: c_gw² = c² (1 + α_T), where α_T parameterizes deviations
Bound: |α_T| < 6 × 10⁻¹⁵

**Restrictiveness: HIGH**

This single measurement eliminated entire classes of modified gravity theories:
- Many Horndeski theories (specific L4 and L5 operators)
- Some f(R) theories
- Certain massive gravity variants
- Forces c_T = 1 in dark energy models, dramatically restricting the viable parameter space

### D3. PPN Parameter γ (Shapiro Delay)

**Statement:** The PPN parameter γ equals 1 to within 2.3 × 10⁻⁵.

**Bound:** γ = 1 + (2.1 ± 2.3) × 10⁻⁵ (Cassini, 2003 — still the best bound)

**Restrictiveness: HIGH**
Constrains Brans-Dicke parameter: ω_BD > 40,000
Rules out most scalar-tensor theories without screening mechanisms

### D4. PPN Parameter β (Perihelion Precession)

**Statement:** β = 1 to within ~8 × 10⁻⁵.

**Bound:** |β - 1| < 8 × 10⁻⁵ (combined Mercury + LLR data)

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM-HIGH**

### D5. Equivalence Principle (Eötvös Parameter)

**Statement:** The universality of free fall holds to 10⁻¹⁵ precision.

**Bound:** η(Ti, Pt) = [-1.5 ± 2.3(stat) ± 1.5(syst)] × 10⁻¹⁵ (MICROSCOPE, 2022)

This is an improvement of almost two orders of magnitude over the previous best (Eöt-Wash).

**Restrictiveness: HIGH**

### D6. Lorentz Violation Bounds (Time-of-Flight)

**Statement:** Planck-scale Lorentz violation is strongly bounded by gamma-ray observations.

**Bounds from GRB 221009A (LHAASO, 2024):**
- Linear (n=1): E_QG,1 > 5.9 E_Pl (subluminal), 6.2 E_Pl (superluminal)
- Quadratic (n=2): E_QG,2 > 5.8 × 10⁻⁸ E_Pl (subluminal)
- Using DisCan algorithm (2025): E_QG,1/10¹⁹ GeV > 14.6 (subluminal)

**Restrictiveness: HIGH**
The linear bound exceeds the Planck energy, effectively ruling out O(E/E_Pl) modifications to photon dispersion.

### D7. Gravitational Wave Dispersion

**Statement:** Gravitational waves show no evidence of frequency-dependent propagation speed (dispersion), constraining the graviton mass and modified dispersion relations.

**Bound:** Phase velocity consistent with c across LIGO frequency band (10-1000 Hz), combined with the GW170817 result across the electromagnetic spectrum.

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM**

### D8. Primordial Gravitational Wave Background

**Statement:** The tensor-to-scalar ratio r in the CMB constrains the amplitude of primordial gravitational waves.

**Bound:** r < 0.036 (BICEP/Keck 2021, 95% CL)

**Restrictiveness: MEDIUM**
Constrains inflationary models and their QG completions (e.g., asymptotic safety inflation). LiteBIRD (launch ~2032) targets σ(r) ~ 0.001.

### D9. Spacetime Fluctuation Searches

**Statement:** No spacetime fluctuations detected at current interferometer sensitivities.

**Current status (2026):**
- GQuEST (tabletop interferometer): Under construction at Caltech/Fermilab. Targets "geontropic" fluctuations (Verlinde-Zurek model) at L=5m with photon-counting sensitivity.
- Unified framework (Jan 2026, Nature Communications): Classifies spacetime fluctuations into three categories with distinct interferometric signatures.
- LIGO serves as "yes/no" detector; smaller experiments (QUEST, GQuEST) provide detailed characterization.

**Restrictiveness: LOW (currently)** — no positive detection yet, but rapidly improving

## E. Constraint Rankings and Dependencies

### Maximally Restrictive Constraints (Top 5)

1. **UV completion (A5):** Eliminates naive perturbative quantization entirely; forces string theory, asymptotic safety, LQG, or similar escape route.

2. **Unitarity + Ghost freedom (A1 + A2):** Eliminates most higher-derivative gravity theories; creates tension with renormalizability.

3. **Diffeomorphism invariance (A4):** Combined with Weinberg's theorem, uniquely selects the structure of graviton interactions at leading order.

4. **Lorentz invariance bounds (B6/D6):** GRB 221009A linear bounds exceed E_Pl, ruling out O(1) Planck-scale Lorentz violation.

5. **Bekenstein-Hawking entropy (C2):** Any theory must count the correct microscopic degrees of freedom to reproduce S = A/(4G).

### Key Tensions Between Constraints

The most important structural tension in quantum gravity is:

**Renormalizability vs. Unitarity (the "ghost problem"):**
- Adding R² terms makes gravity renormalizable (Stelle 1977) but introduces ghost modes → unitarity violation
- String theory resolves this by being UV-finite without higher derivatives in the action
- Asymptotic safety attempts non-perturbative resolution — ghosts may be fictitious
- LQG sidesteps entirely through non-perturbative, background-independent quantization
- Infinite derivative gravity (IDG) uses entire functions to avoid new poles

### Dependency Map

**Independent constraints** (cannot be derived from each other):
- Unitarity (A1)
- Lorentz invariance (B6)
- Equivalence principle (B5)
- Bekenstein-Hawking entropy (C2)
- Graviton mass bounds (D1)
- GW speed (D2)

**Derived/dependent constraints:**
- Ghost freedom (A2) follows from unitarity (A1) + positive-definite Hilbert space
- Correct DOF (A3) follows from diffeomorphism invariance (A4) + unitarity (A1)
- Newtonian gravity (B1) follows from graviton propagator IR limit (B3)
- PPN parameters (B7/D3/D4) follow from the post-Newtonian expansion of whatever field equations emerge
- Holographic entropy bounds (C5) may follow from Bekenstein-Hawking entropy (C2) + some form of covariant entropy counting
- Page curve (C6) follows from unitarity (A1) applied to black holes

## G. Implications for Novel Theory Construction

### The Constraint Funnel

Working top-down through the most restrictive constraints:

1. **UV completion** forces a non-perturbative mechanism (rules out naive QFT of gravity)
2. **Unitarity + ghost freedom** creates tension with higher-derivative approaches (must be resolved by string-like UV completion, asymptotic safety, fundamental discreteness, or infinite-derivative mechanism)
3. **Diffeomorphism invariance** uniquely fixes the low-energy structure of graviton interactions
4. **Lorentz invariance** rules out O(1) Planck-scale violations — the theory must be Lorentz-invariant or violations must be extraordinarily suppressed
5. **Bekenstein-Hawking entropy** requires the theory to have the right microscopic degrees of freedom to account for horizon entropy as area/4G
6. **GW speed = c** eliminates many modified gravity alternatives
7. **Spectral dimension running** provides a UV target: the theory should become effectively 2-dimensional at short distances

### Narrowest Bottleneck

The **unitarity-renormalizability tension** remains the single tightest bottleneck. The known escape routes define the major programs:
- **String theory:** UV-finite amplitudes, no higher-derivative ghosts
- **Asymptotic safety:** Non-perturbative fixed point, possible fictitious ghosts
- **LQG:** Fundamentally discrete, sidesteps continuum divergences
- **IDG:** Entire functions in the propagator, no new poles

Any novel approach must identify its own escape route through this bottleneck.

### Underexploited Constraints

The following constraints are powerful but underused in theory construction:
1. **Spectral dimension d_s → 2** — most theories check this after the fact; could be used as a *starting axiom*
2. **Jacobson's derivation** — the fact that Einstein equations follow from thermodynamics of local horizons is rarely used as a constructive principle
3. **Entanglement area law** — the connection between entanglement and geometry (Ryu-Takayanagi) could be promoted from a result to an axiom
4. **Holographic Swampland unification** — the 2025 result that all Swampland conjectures derive from a single geometric condition on holographic effective action convexity is a potentially powerful organizing principle
