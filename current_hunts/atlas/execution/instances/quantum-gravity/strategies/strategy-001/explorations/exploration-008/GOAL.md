# Exploration 008: Cosmological Constant, Matter Content, and Parameter Determination

## Mission Context

We've established that spectral dimension d_s = 4 → 2 uniquely selects quadratic gravity + fakeon, which is the perturbative face of asymptotic safety. The theory passes all 7 validation tests. It has 2 free parameters: M₂ (fakeon mass) and M₀ (scalar mass).

Key open questions remain:
1. Can either free parameter be determined by additional constraints?
2. Does the theory address the cosmological constant problem?
3. What matter content is required for UV completeness (f₀ needs a UV fixed point)?

## Your Goal

Investigate the following three interconnected questions. Write incrementally.

### Q1: Can the free parameters be fixed?

The theory has 2 new parameters beyond GR: M₂ and M₀. Currently they are free. Investigate whether any of the following can fix them:
- **Inflationary constraints:** The R² term drives Starobinsky inflation. Does matching CMB data (n_s, r) constrain M₀?
- **Asymptotic freedom conditions:** f₂ is asymptotically free. What about f₀? Does requiring f₀ to also reach a UV fixed point (with appropriate matter content) fix the ratio M₂/M₀?
- **The hierarchy problem:** Salvio-Strumia's "agravity" scenario has M₂ ~ 10¹¹ GeV to solve the hierarchy problem. Is this consistent with our constraint-driven derivation?
- **Spectral dimension matching:** If the flow d_s(E) must match the specific profiles seen in CDT or LQG numerical simulations, does this constrain the masses?

### Q2: The cosmological constant problem

Can quadratic gravity + fakeon address the 120-order-of-magnitude discrepancy?
- **Unimodular extension:** Salvio (2024) showed unimodular quadratic gravity solves the "old" CC problem. What remains?
- **Running vacuum energy:** In asymptotic safety, the cosmological constant runs. What does this predict for the observed value?
- **Causal set comparison:** CST's "everpresent lambda" predicted the right order of magnitude. Can quadratic gravity do the same?

### Q3: Matter content constraints

The f₀ coupling is NOT asymptotically free in pure gravity. With Standard Model matter, does it become so? What matter content is needed for full UV completion? This constrains beyond-Standard-Model physics.

## Success Criteria
- Clear assessment of whether any constraint fixes M₂ or M₀
- Status of the CC problem in this framework
- Required matter content for UV completion
- At least one concrete numerical result
