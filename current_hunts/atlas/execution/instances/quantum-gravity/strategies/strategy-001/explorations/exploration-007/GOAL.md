# Exploration 007: Experimental Signatures — GQuEST, LIGO, and Observable Predictions

## Mission Context

Our theory (quadratic gravity + fakeon, selected by d_s = 4 → 2) has one established quantitative prediction: tensor-to-scalar ratio r ∈ [0.0004, 0.0035]. But we need MORE testable predictions to reach Tier 4 of the validation guide.

The experimental landscape (2025-2026) includes:
- **GQuEST** (Caltech/Fermilab): tabletop interferometer targeting spacetime fluctuations
- **QUEST** (Cardiff): similar concept
- **LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA**: GW observations, spacetime fluctuation searches
- **Unified framework** (Nature Comms Jan 2026): classifies fluctuation signatures into 3 categories
- **LiteBIRD** (JAXA): CMB B-modes, late 2020s
- **CMB-S4**: ground-based CMB, early 2030s
- **Gravity-induced entanglement**: tabletop experiments becoming feasible

## Your Goal

Calculate or estimate the specific experimental signatures that quadratic gravity + fakeon makes for current/near-future experiments. We need NUMBERS, not vague claims.

**IMPORTANT: Write incrementally, section by section.**

### Specific Questions

1. **Spacetime fluctuation spectrum:** In the unified framework (Nature Comms 2026), what category of spacetime fluctuations does quadratic gravity + fakeon produce? What is the predicted amplitude? Can GQuEST or LIGO detect it?

2. **Additional gravitational wave polarizations:** The scalar mode (mass M₀) produces a breathing mode. Could next-generation detectors (Einstein Telescope, Cosmic Explorer) detect this? What frequency range?

3. **Primordial gravitational wave predictions beyond r:** The full tensor spectrum, including blue/red tilt, running of spectral index, non-Gaussianity. Are there distinctive signatures beyond just the value of r?

4. **Modified GW waveforms:** Do the R² and C² corrections modify binary inspiral/merger waveforms? At what post-Newtonian order? Could LISA or ET detect this?

5. **Gravity-induced entanglement:** Does the theory predict different rates for gravity-induced entanglement compared to GR? The fakeon prescription means the massive spin-2 mode doesn't mediate forces — does this affect the entanglement rate?

6. **Most promising near-term test:** Which experiment is most likely to test this theory first? Give a specific timeline and sensitivity requirement.

## Success Criteria
- At least 2 quantitative predictions with specific numbers
- Assessment of detectability for each prediction
- Identification of the most promising near-term experimental test
- Clear distinction between predictions that are unique to this theory vs. shared with other QG approaches
