# Exploration 006 Summary: Cosmological Constant Problem in QG+F and the Everpresent-Λ Mechanism

## Goal
Investigate whether causal set theory's everpresent-Λ mechanism can be imported into QG+F, and assess alternative approaches to the cosmological constant problem within QG+F.

## What Was Tried
- Reconstructed the full everpresent-Λ mechanism (Sorkin 1987, Ahmed et al. 2004): unimodular gravity conjugacy (δΛ·δV ~ ℏ) + causal set Poisson fluctuations (δV ~ √V) → δΛ ~ H² ~ 10⁻¹²² M_P⁴
- Analyzed latest observational tests (arXiv:2307.13743, Oct 2024 revision): 90,000 seeds tested against Pantheon+SH0ES and Planck CMB
- Assessed whether AS fractal structure or continuum path-integral fluctuations could replace discrete Poisson statistics
- Analyzed unimodular QG+F (Salvio 2024), Kaloper-Padilla sequestering, AS predictions for Λ, and pre-geometric gravity (Addazi & Meluccio 2025)
- Investigated whether QG+F's known parameters or six-derivative extension could predict Λ

## Outcome: SUCCEEDED — Clear negative result with constructive recommendations

### Key Finding 1: Everpresent-Λ cannot be imported into QG+F
The mechanism requires Poisson fluctuations from counting discrete spacetime atoms (δV ~ √V). No known continuum mechanism reproduces this scaling. Perturbative path-integral fluctuations give δV ~ V·(ℓ_P/L)² ≪ √V. AS fractal structure describes propagator behavior, not discrete counting. The conceptual gap is fundamental.

### Key Finding 2: Everpresent-Λ has serious observational problems anyway
Only 0.017% of random seeds fit SN Ia data. CMB fits are significantly worse than ΛCDM. The model cannot simultaneously satisfy high-z (CMB) and low-z (SN) constraints. The ISW effect kills most realizations.

### Key Finding 3: Unimodular QG+F solves the old CC problem
Salvio (2024) showed the unimodular constraint is compatible with quadratic gravity + fakeon prescription. Vacuum energy doesn't gravitate; Λ is a radiatively stable integration constant. However, there is an active debate about whether this genuinely solves the problem or merely reformulates fine-tuning as initial conditions.

### Key Finding 4: No approach within QG+F predicts Λ ~ 10⁻¹²²
- AS: Λ_IR is a free parameter on the RG trajectory
- Kaloper-Padilla: Untested with QG+F, has non-locality concerns
- Six-derivative extension: New scalars have mass ~ 10¹⁵ GeV, far too heavy for late-time effects
- No natural combination of QG+F parameters (G, M₂, M₀) gives 10⁻¹²²

## Key Takeaway
**The CC problem is the Achilles heel of ALL continuum QFT approaches to quantum gravity, not just QG+F.** The best QG+F can do is adopt the unimodular formulation to solve the old CC problem and accept Λ as a free integration constant. The everpresent-Λ mechanism is intellectually impressive but incompatible with continuum physics and observationally challenged. The most honest assessment: explaining Λ ~ 10⁻¹²² likely requires either spacetime discreteness, anthropic selection, or a yet-undiscovered mechanism.

## Leads Worth Pursuing
1. **Adopt unimodular QG+F as the default formulation** — costs nothing, solves old CC problem, makes testable prediction (isocurvature mode B decouples)
2. **Investigate Kaloper-Padilla sequestering + QG+F compatibility** — most promising untested direction for the new CC problem
3. **Watch the pre-geometric gravity program** (Addazi & Meluccio 2025) — novel topological quantization of Λ, but very new and unvalidated
4. **The nonlinear QM approach** to unimodular gravity (arXiv:2511.12897) — proposes dynamical redshifting of the CC, worth scrutinizing
5. **Don't over-invest in the CC problem** — it's arguably the hardest problem in physics, and QG+F's inability to solve it puts it on equal footing with every other approach
