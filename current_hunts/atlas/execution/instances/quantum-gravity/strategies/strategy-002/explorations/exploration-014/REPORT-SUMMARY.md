# Exploration 014 Summary: GW and Non-CMB Experimental Signatures of QG+F

## Goal
Identify and assess experimental signatures of QG+F beyond the CMB sector — gravitational waves, table-top experiments (GQuEST, BMV, short-range gravity), cosmological signatures (21-cm, LSS, stochastic GW background, dark matter), microcausality violation, and scattering signatures.

## What Was Tried
Systematic investigation of 19 potential non-CMB signatures across five categories: (1) GW signatures (scalar mode, modified dispersion, extra polarizations, BH ringdown QNMs, six-derivative extension); (2) table-top experiments (GQuEST, BMV entanglement, Eöt-Wash Yukawa bounds); (3) cosmological signatures (21-cm, matter power spectrum, stochastic GW background, dark matter candidates); (4) microcausality violation (scattering cross sections, missing resonance, indirect channels); (5) experimental timeline and priority ranking.

## Outcome: SUCCEEDED — Comprehensive negative result

**Every non-CMB signature of QG+F is undetectable with current or foreseeable technology.** The fundamental barrier is the ~15 orders of magnitude gap between the highest energies we can probe (~10⁴ GeV at LHC, ~10² Hz at LIGO) and the QG+F mass scales (M₂, M₀ ~ 10¹³–10¹⁹ GeV). Specific findings:

- **GW signatures:** All Planck-suppressed. Massive spin-2 QNMs have been computed (arXiv:2412.15037) and first LVK ringdown constraints obtained (arXiv:2506.14695), but these constrain M₂ > 5×10⁻¹² eV — 40 orders of magnitude weaker than QG+F's expected M₂. Modified dispersion suppressed by (E/M_Planck)² ~ 10⁻⁸⁰. No extra polarizations at observable frequencies.

- **Table-top experiments:** GQuEST tests holographic spacetime fluctuations (Verlinde-Zurek geontropic model), NOT QG+F. BMV tests whether gravity is quantum generically — not discriminating between QG theories. Yukawa bounds 25 orders of magnitude too weak.

- **Cosmological signatures:** All modifications to Friedmann equations, matter power spectrum, and 21-cm signal suppressed by (H/M)² ~ 10⁻¹¹⁰. Scalaron too heavy (M₀ ~ 10¹³ GeV) for DM. Stochastic GW from Planck-scale transitions at f ~ 10⁸ Hz (undetectable).

- **Microcausality violation:** Anselmi himself showed (2019, 2020) the violation is "short range for all practical purposes" — does NOT propagate via light cones or GWs. "The universe conspires to make the effect disappear." The "missing resonance" scattering signature requires collider energy ~10¹⁹ GeV.

## Key Takeaway
**QG+F is effectively a one-prediction theory: the tensor-to-scalar ratio r.** The CMB is the only sector where Planck-scale physics gets amplified to observable scales (via inflation). All other signatures are suppressed by insurmountable energy gaps. This is not unique to QG+F — it is the fundamental predicament of ALL perturbative quantum gravity theories. The r measurement by LiteBIRD (~2036-2037) remains the only game in town. The most interesting non-CMB direction is the model-dependent agravity DM candidate (right-handed neutrinos), but this is not a generic QG+F prediction.

## Leads Worth Pursuing
1. The agravity framework (Salvio) with right-handed neutrino DM is the most concrete non-CMB testable extension — worth a deeper investigation of its predictions and constraints
2. If M₂ is allowed to be much lower than M_Planck (which some QG+F variants permit), QNM ringdown constraints from next-gen detectors (ET, CE) could become relevant — worth investigating the lower bound on M₂ from consistency
3. The theoretical question of WHY the microcausality violation doesn't propagate deserves deeper analysis — it might connect to fundamental features of the fakeon prescription
