# Exploration 011: Black Hole Predictions in QG+F and Six-Derivative Extension

## Mission Context

QG+F passes all validation tiers but has limited predictions: r and n_s from inflation. Black holes are the other major arena where quantum gravity makes predictions. We need to know what QG+F predicts for black holes — singularity resolution, Hawking radiation, information paradox, remnants.

## Your Specific Goal

### Task 1: Black Hole Solutions in QG+F
- What are the static, spherically symmetric BH solutions in quadratic gravity?
- The Stelle potential gives V(r) = -GM/r[1 + (1/3)e^{-M₀r} - (4/3)e^{-M₂r}]. What happens at r → 0?
- Does QG+F resolve the BH singularity? (V(r) → 0 as r → 0 from the Stelle potential — but this is the Newtonian limit, not the full nonlinear solution)
- What are the full nonlinear BH solutions? (Lü, Perkins, Pope, Stelle 2015 and subsequent work)
- What is the difference between the fakeon BH and the standard QG BH?

### Task 2: BH Entropy in QG+F
- Wald entropy formula for quadratic gravity: S = A/(4G) + corrections from R² and C² terms
- What are the Wald entropy corrections explicitly?
- At what BH mass do corrections become significant?
- BH entropy in the six-derivative extension?

### Task 3: Hawking Radiation and Information
- How does the fakeon prescription affect Hawking radiation?
- The fakeon violates microcausality at Δt ~ 1/M₂. Does this affect the information paradox?
- Exploration 007 found: AS predicts Planck-mass remnants (T→0). Does perturbative QG+F also predict remnants?
- Is information preserved in QG+F? Through the fakeon mechanism?

### Task 4: Novel BH Predictions
- What predictions does QG+F make that differ from GR for BHs?
- Any observational signatures? (BH shadows, quasi-normal modes, BH mergers, BH evaporation endpoint)
- Can LISA, ET (Einstein Telescope), or gravitational wave observations test QG+F BH predictions?
- What about the six-derivative extension — different BH predictions?

## Success Criteria
- Explicit BH predictions (Wald entropy, singularity resolution, remnant prediction)
- Comparison to GR and to AS predictions
- Assessment of testability
