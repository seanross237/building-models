## Task 1: QG+F + Standard Model Coupling and Constraints

### How QG+F Couples to the Standard Model

The QG+F action coupled to matter takes the form:

S = S_QG + S_matter

where the gravitational sector is:

S_QG = ∫d⁴x √g [-Λ_bare + (M_P²/2)R + (1/2f₂²)C²_μνρσ - (1/6f₀²)R²]

and the matter sector includes all renormalizable SM interactions plus **non-minimal couplings** ξ_a φ_a² R between scalar fields and the Ricci scalar. These non-minimal couplings are **required by renormalizability** — they are generated by quantum corrections even if set to zero classically (Salvio 2018 review, Frontiers in Physics).

The complete matter Lagrangian includes:
- Gauge fields with standard Yang-Mills kinetic terms
- Weyl fermions with standard kinetic terms and Yukawa couplings
- Scalar fields with quartic self-interactions
- Non-minimal scalar-gravity couplings ξ_a φ_a² R

### Beta Functions for Gravitational Couplings

The one-loop beta functions for the gravitational couplings (in the standard/off-shell MS-bar scheme) are known from Fradkin-Tseytlin (1982), corrected by Avramidi-Barvinsky (1985), and extended to include matter by Salvio-Strumia (2014).

**For f₂ (Weyl-squared coupling, written as λ = f₂²/2):**

The one-loop pure-gravity beta function (Kawai-Ohta 2024, arXiv:2405.05706):

β_λ^standard = -(1/(4π)²) × (133/10)λ²

This is ALWAYS negative — f₂ is **asymptotically free** in the pure gravity sector.

With matter (N_S real scalars, N_f Weyl fermions, N_V vectors), the matter contribution to β_f₂ adds terms proportional to:
- -(N_V/5) f₂⁴  [vectors]
- -(N_f/20) f₂⁴  [fermions]
- -(N_S/60) f₂⁴  [scalars]

All with the **same negative sign**. Every matter species contributes with the same sign as the gravitational self-interactions. This means:

**f₂ is always asymptotically free regardless of matter content.**

More matter = MORE asymptotically free. There is no upper bound on the number of species from f₂ asymptotic freedom.

For the SM: N_V = 12, N_f = 45 (or 48 with right-handed neutrinos), N_S = 4.

**For f₀ (R² coupling, written as ξ = f₀²/6):**

β_ξ^standard = -(1/(4π)²) × 5(72λ² − 36λξ + ξ²)/36

f₀ is **NOT asymptotically free** in general. The coupling grows at high energies. The resolution in agravity (Salvio-Strumia 2017, EPJC 78, 124): f₀ can grow to large values where the conformal mode of the graviton decouples, and the theory flows to conformal gravity at infinite energy. This requires:
- All scalars be asymptotically conformally coupled (ξ_a → -1/6)
- All other couplings approach fixed points

**Physical Beta Functions (Branchina et al. 2024, PRL 133):**

The **physical** (on-shell, gauge-invariant) beta functions differ from the standard MS-bar ones:

β_λ^phys = -(1/(4π)²) × (1617λ − 20ξ)λ/90

β_ξ^phys = -(1/(4π)²) × (ξ² − 36λξ − 2520λ²)/36

Asymptotic freedom requires ξ > 0.5487λ > 0 (tachyon-free condition automatically satisfied).

### Does Asymptotic Freedom Constrain the Number of Matter Fields?

**Answer: No, for f₂.** All spin species contribute with the same negative sign. QG+F does not constrain the number of matter fields through f₂ asymptotic freedom.

**For f₀, the situation is subtler.** The f₀ coupling is not AF and its UV behavior depends on the full coupled system. Total Asymptotic Freedom (TAF) — the requirement that ALL couplings flow to zero — does constrain the matter sector, but primarily through matter self-couplings (Yukawas, gauge couplings, quartics), not primarily through gravity.

### Does QG+F Predict the SM Gauge Group SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1)?

**No.** The gravitational sector is compatible with ANY gauge group and ANY matter representation. Gravity couples universally to the stress-energy tensor and does not distinguish between gauge groups. This was confirmed most recently by Anselmi (2025, JHEP 07, 175, arXiv:2504.05900): "strict renormalizability" serves as a **selection principle among already-specified theories**, not as a predictor of particle content or gauge structure. It constrains which interactions are allowed given a particle content and symmetry, but does not derive the gauge group. Gauge anomaly cancellation restricts the number and type of fields, but this is a SM constraint, not a gravitational one.

However, the **Total Asymptotic Freedom (TAF) condition** (Giudice, Isidori, Salvio, Strumia, JHEP 2015) does constrain SM extensions. The pure SM is NOT TAF-compatible because:
- The U(1)_Y coupling g₁ is not asymptotically free (it runs to a Landau pole)
- The top Yukawa coupling y_t is not asymptotically free
- The Higgs quartic λ_H is not asymptotically free

TAF requires extending the SM with additional matter that makes ALL couplings AF. This is a strong constraint that significantly limits viable BSM physics, but it selects **extensions** of the SM, not the SM gauge group itself.

## Task 2: The Higgs Mass Connection (Shaposhnikov-Wetterich)

### The Prediction

In 2009 (published 2010), Shaposhnikov and Wetterich predicted:

**m_H = 126 GeV ± few GeV**

This was **before the Higgs was discovered at 125.1 GeV in July 2012**.

Reference: arXiv:0912.0208, Phys. Lett. B 683, 196 (2010)

### The Mechanism in Detail

**Assumptions:**
1. Gravity is asymptotically safe (AS) — there exists a non-perturbative UV fixed point
2. The SM + gravity is valid up to arbitrarily high energies ("big desert" — no new physics between the Fermi and Planck scales)
3. No intermediate mass scales

**The key mechanism:**

At scales above the Planck mass, gravity contributes an anomalous dimension A_λ to the running of the Higgs quartic coupling λ_H. The modified beta function becomes:

β_λ = β_λ^SM + A_λ · λ

where A_λ is the gravity-induced anomalous dimension.

**Case A_λ > 0 (favored by explicit AS calculations):**
- The fixed point at λ = 0 becomes UV-attractive
- λ is driven to zero at the UV fixed point: λ(μ → ∞) → 0
- This provides a **boundary condition** at the Planck scale: λ(M_Pl) ≈ 0
- Running λ down from M_Pl to the electroweak scale using SM RG equations uniquely determines:

**m_H = m_min ≈ 126 GeV**

**Case A_λ < 0:**
- The window opens: 126 GeV < m_H < 174 GeV
- Less predictive

**Why 126 GeV specifically:**
The condition λ(M_Pl) ≈ 0 corresponds to the **minimum** Higgs mass consistent with vacuum stability. Below this mass, the EW vacuum would be absolutely unstable. At exactly 126 GeV, the vacuum is metastable — on the boundary of stability. This is the observed "near-criticality" of the SM vacuum, and this prediction explains it.

### Robustness

The prediction is remarkably robust:
- Independent of details of UV running (only the sign of A_λ matters)
- Holds for a "wide class of extensions of the SM"
- Only requires the big desert assumption (no new physics between Fermi and Planck)

**Limitations:**
- If there IS new physics (SUSY, extra dimensions, new fermions), the prediction can shift or break
- The prediction assumes the SM is the complete low-energy theory
- Uncertainty of a few GeV comes from M_top uncertainty

### Connection to QG+F

**Critical question: Does QG+F reproduce this prediction?**

The answer is nuanced:
- QG+F is the **perturbative face** of asymptotic safety (established in explorations 001-009)
- The Shaposhnikov-Wetterich prediction uses the **non-perturbative** AS fixed point
- In perturbative QG+F, one-loop gravity corrections to λ_H exist but are suppressed by (μ/M_Pl)² — far too small to drive λ → 0

**Verdict:** The Higgs mass prediction is a **non-perturbative** AS result. QG+F is consistent with it (same theory, different regime) but cannot derive it perturbatively. This is analogous to how perturbative QCD cannot derive proton mass — it requires non-perturbative confinement dynamics.

### Other Particle Physics Predictions from AS Boundary Conditions

Eichhorn, Held, Wetterich, and collaborators have extended this approach:

1. **Top quark mass:** AS boundary conditions on top Yukawa (y_t → y_t* at UV FP) can constrain M_top (Eichhorn & Held 2017, arXiv:1707.01107)

2. **Fine structure constant:** For grand unified models, the balance of gravity and matter fluctuations at the UV FP determines a fixed-point value α* of the unified gauge coupling. Running down to low energies predicts α_em (Eichhorn, Held & Wetterich 2018, Phys. Lett. B 782). The predicted value depends on the GUT matter content and gravitational fluctuation strength — current uncertainties are too large for a sharp prediction, but the mechanism is calculable in principle.

3. **Gauge coupling unification:** In asymptotically safe grand unification, all quartic couplings of the GUT scalar potential become computable predictions from the UV FP (Eichhorn & Held 2020, JHEP). This is a dramatic increase in predictivity compared to standard GUTs.

## Task 3: Agravity (Salvio-Strumia)

### What is Agravity?

"Agravity" = "Adimensional gravity." The core idea: **the fundamental theory contains no dimensionful parameters at all.** The action is:

S_agravity = ∫d⁴x √g [R²/(6f₀²) + C²_μνρσ/(2f₂²) + L_SM(dimensionless)]

where L_SM contains the SM fields with ALL couplings dimensionless. No Einstein-Hilbert term (M_P² R), no cosmological constant (Λ), no mass terms. All are generated by quantum effects (dimensional transmutation).

This IS QG+F + SM, but with the specific philosophical stance that dimensionful parameters (M_P, M_W, Λ) are derived quantities, not fundamental inputs.

Key papers:
- Salvio & Strumia, "Agravity", JHEP 06 (2014) 080 (arXiv:1403.4226)
- Salvio & Strumia, "Agravity up to infinite energy", EPJC 78 (2018) 124 (arXiv:1705.03896)

### The Hierarchy Problem in Agravity

**The "old" hierarchy problem:** Why is M_W ≪ M_P? (Why is the Higgs mass so much lighter than the Planck mass?)

**Agravity's answer:** In a classically scale-invariant theory, there is no hierarchy problem because there are no fundamental mass scales to compare. ALL masses are generated by dimensional transmutation:

1. **Planck mass generation:** A new strong gauge interaction (analogous to QCD) becomes non-perturbative at high energies, generating a condensate that breaks scale invariance and produces M_P.

2. **Weak scale generation:** The Higgs mass is generated through radiative corrections (Coleman-Weinberg mechanism) at a much lower scale.

3. **Naturalness:** The gravitational couplings f₀, f₂ must be very small (≲ 10⁻⁸) for the Higgs mass not to receive large corrections. This is technically natural because small f₀, f₂ enhance the symmetry (conformal symmetry in gravity sector).

**Two scenarios:**
- **Scenario A (f₀ ≪ 1 at Planck scale):** Planck mass induced by a new confining gauge group. Accidental SUSY suppresses the CC.
- **Scenario B (f₀ ≫ 1 at Planck scale):** Conformal mode strongly coupled. The theory flows to conformal gravity at high energies.

### Agravity Predictions

**Cosmology:**
- Inflation driven by the "Higgs of gravity" (the R² scalar/scalaron): n_s ≈ 0.967, r ≈ 0.13 (original prediction — now r is too large, ruled out by BICEP/Keck)
- Updated with fakeon: r ∈ [0.0004, 0.0035], compatible with data

**Particle physics:**
- SM is NOT TAF-compatible — requires extensions. Salvio (2016, Phys. Rev. D 94, 096007) showed that adding **3 right-handed neutrinos with masses below the electroweak scale** can:
  - Account for neutrino oscillations (via seesaw or inverse seesaw)
  - Provide a dark matter candidate (lightest sterile neutrino)
  - Generate baryon asymmetry (leptogenesis/baryogenesis)
  - Keep the QCD θ-term naturally small (strong CP)
  - Preserve the softened gravity solution to the hierarchy problem

**Key scale:** The gravity softening scale (where quadratic gravity effects become important) is ≲ 10¹¹ GeV.

### The Cosmological Constant in Agravity

In a classically scale-invariant theory, the CC is zero at tree level (no dimensionful parameters). Quantum corrections generate a CC, but:
- In unimodular agravity (Salvio 2024, Phys. Lett. B 856, 138920): the old CC problem is solved — vacuum energy does not gravitate, Λ appears as an integration constant
- The new CC problem (why Λ ~ 10⁻¹²²M_P⁴) is NOT solved — Salvio invokes anthropic reasoning within a "multiverse of eras"

### Current Status of the Agravity Program

The agravity program is essentially QG+F with the additional philosophical commitment to classical scale invariance. Status:

**Successes:**
- Renormalizable and unitary (via fakeon prescription)
- Naturally addresses the hierarchy problem through technical naturalness of small f₀, f₂
- Compatible with inflation (Starobinsky-like)
- Framework for generating all mass scales from dimensional transmutation

**Open problems:**
- The original r ≈ 0.13 prediction was ruled out (fixed by fakeon prescription)
- f₀ is not asymptotically free → requires special UV behavior (flow to conformal gravity)
- The mechanism for generating M_P from a new confining group is speculative — no explicit model
- The CC remains unexplained (same as all other approaches)
- TAF requirement forces BSM extensions but doesn't uniquely determine them

## Task 4: Fakeon Phenomenology at Colliders

### The Massive Spin-2 Fakeon

QG+F propagates three gravitational degrees of freedom:
1. **Massless spin-2 graviton** (standard gravity)
2. **Massive spin-0 scalar** (the scalaron/inflaton, mass m_0 ~ 3×10¹³ GeV)
3. **Massive spin-2 fakeon** (mass m_2, unknown but expected ~ M_Pl or sub-Planckian)

The fakeon is **purely virtual** — it mediates interactions but NEVER appears as an external (on-shell) particle. This has profound consequences for collider phenomenology.

### Peak Uncertainty (Anselmi 2022, JHEP 06, 058)

The dressed propagator of a fakeon near its mass shell exhibits "peak uncertainty":

ΔE ≳ ΔE_min ≈ Γ_f/2

where Γ_f is the fakeon width. This means:
- You cannot probe the fakeon resonance as sharply as a normal resonance
- The Breit-Wigner peak is **smeared** by an irreducible uncertainty of order Γ_f/2
- This is a fundamental quantum gravity prediction, not an experimental limitation

Two regimes:
- **Large Γ_f** (relevant for collider physics): The fakeon peak is broad and hard to distinguish from background
- **Small Γ_f** (relevant for quantum gravity): The peak is narrow but fundamentally fuzzy

### How Fakeons Differ from Normal Particles Experimentally

| Property | Normal particle | Fakeon |
|----------|----------------|--------|
| On-shell production | Yes | **No** |
| Missing energy signature | Yes | **No** |
| Resonance peak | Sharp Breit-Wigner | **Smeared by Γ_f/2** |
| Loop contributions | Standard | Modified (different from ghost or normal) |
| Z-pole constraints | Apply | **Evaded** |

Key consequence: **Fakeons evade most direct detection bounds.** They cannot be produced on-shell, so they don't contribute to missing energy, don't produce resonance bumps, and avoid Z-pole precision constraints. Their effects appear only at loop level.

### The Fake Inert Doublet Model (Anselmi et al. 2021)

Paper: arXiv:2104.02071, JHEP 10 (2021) 132

A concrete collider model: take the SM and add a second scalar doublet (like the Inert Doublet Model) but treat it as a **fakeon** rather than a physical particle.

**Observable effects:**
1. **Modified h→γγ decay:** Loop contributions from the fake doublet modify the Higgs to diphoton decay width
2. **Modified Higgs trilinear coupling:** The fake doublet shifts the Higgs self-coupling
3. **Vacuum stability:** The fake doublet can stabilize the electroweak vacuum
4. **NO missing energy:** Unlike the standard IDM, the fake version produces no dark matter signatures at colliders

The fake doublet can have mass **below the electroweak scale** without violating Z-pole constraints — a unique feature impossible for normal particles.

### Fake Doublet and the Muon g-2 (Anselmi et al. 2021)

Paper: arXiv:2104.03249

A fake scalar doublet coupling to the muon can explain the muon anomalous magnetic moment discrepancy. The key advantage: fakeon masses below the EW scale are allowed because they evade direct production bounds.

### Production Cross Sections

**For the spin-2 gravitational fakeon:** The mass is expected to be near the Planck scale (m_2 ~ M_Pl), making direct production at the LHC impossible. Even at next-generation colliders (100 TeV FCC-hh), the production rate would be negligible due to the Planck-suppressed coupling.

**For fake scalar doublets:** The production cross section is **zero by definition** — fakeons cannot be produced on-shell. Their effects are purely virtual (loop-level). The observable effects scale as:
- Δ(h→γγ) ~ (m_h²/m_fake²) × coupling² for m_fake > m_h
- For m_fake < m_h, the corrections can be larger but still loop-suppressed

### Constraints on the Fakeon Mass

**Spin-2 gravitational fakeon:** No direct collider constraints. The mass enters inflationary predictions — from r ∈ [0.0004, 0.0035], the ratio m_2/m_0 is constrained. Given m_0 ~ 3×10¹³ GeV, this puts m_2 in the range ~10¹³-10¹⁸ GeV. Far beyond collider reach.

**Fake scalar doublet (if it exists):** Tests of gravity exclude extra gravitational components below 0.05 eV. The fake doublet mass is constrained by precision electroweak data, but much less stringently than normal doublets.

## Task 5: Novel Connections

### 5.1 Does the Fakeon Prescription Have Implications for the Strong CP Problem?

**Direct answer: Not directly, but indirectly via agravity.**

The strong CP problem asks why the QCD vacuum angle θ_QCD is tiny (< 10⁻¹⁰). In the agravity framework (Salvio 2016):
- Classical scale invariance means no bare θ-term at the fundamental level
- θ is generated by quantum corrections but remains naturally small
- Three right-handed neutrinos with sub-EW masses preserve this naturalness

A separate but related approach (Karananas, Shaposhnikov 2024): Weyl-invariant Einstein-Cartan gravity (which shares features with agravity) contains an axion-like particle that can solve the strong CP problem. The smallness of this particle's mass is protected by the gauge structure.

**The fakeon prescription itself** does not directly address strong CP. But the broader framework (classically scale-invariant quadratic gravity) provides a natural setting where θ_QCD is suppressed.

### 5.2 Implications for Grand Unification

**This is one of the most interesting connections.**

Asymptotic safety (the non-perturbative face of QG+F) has striking implications for GUTs:

1. **Eichhorn & Held (2018):** AS gravity can predict the gauge coupling at the Planck scale for GUT models. The UV fixed point determines α_GUT. Running down from M_Pl to low energies then predicts α_em ≈ 1/137. Current computational uncertainties prevent a sharp prediction, but the mechanism is remarkable — quantum gravity could explain why α_em has its observed value.

2. **Predictive power of AS-GUTs (Eichhorn & Held 2020, JHEP 08, 111):** In asymptotically safe grand unification, the UV FP determines all quartic couplings of the GUT scalar potential. This converts several free parameters of standard GUTs into predictions. The GUT scale, proton lifetime, and scalar spectrum become calculable.

3. **Asymptotic grand unification (2025, JHEP 08, 042):** Recent work on asymptotic grand unification in SO(10) with one extra dimension shows that gauge couplings can reach a non-trivial UV fixed point, providing an alternative to traditional grand unification.

**From the perturbative QG+F side:**
- TAF (Total Asymptotic Freedom) constrains which GUT models are viable
- The requirement that all couplings go to zero at infinite energy severely limits the GUT particle content
- Specifically, SU(5) and SO(10) models with particular scalar sectors can be TAF-compatible

### 5.3 Can the Scalaron Play a Role in Baryogenesis?

**Yes, through several mechanisms:**

1. **Scalaron decay and leptogenesis:** The scalaron (mass ~3×10¹³ GeV) decays during reheating. If right-handed neutrinos exist with M_N < m_scalaron, the scalaron decays into right-handed neutrinos, which then generate lepton asymmetry → baryon asymmetry via sphalerons. This is the standard thermal leptogenesis scenario naturally embedded in QG+F/Starobinsky inflation.

2. **Gravitational baryogenesis:** The coupling between the derivative of the Ricci scalar ∂_μR and the baryon current J^μ_B can generate baryon asymmetry during the transition from inflation to radiation domination. This is a purely gravitational mechanism requiring no new particles beyond the scalaron.

3. **Reheating temperature:** Starobinsky inflation gives T_reheat ~ 10⁸-10⁹ GeV (Salvio 2016, recent 2024 calculations refine this). This is high enough for standard thermal leptogenesis (requires T > 10⁹ GeV for lightest right-handed neutrino mass ~10⁹ GeV), though it may require optimization.

### 5.4 Connection to Dark Matter

**Several potential connections:**

1. **Scalaron as dark matter mediator:** The scalaron's gravitational production during reheating can produce dark matter particles. Recent work (2021-2024) shows that purely gravitational production of dark matter from Starobinsky inflation is viable for a range of dark matter masses, with constraints from isocurvature perturbations and Lyman-α data.

2. **Right-handed neutrinos as dark matter (agravity framework):** Salvio (2016) showed that one of three right-handed neutrinos (with sub-EW mass) can be a dark matter candidate — this is the standard sterile neutrino dark matter scenario, naturally embedded in the agravity framework.

3. **Planck-mass BH remnants (non-perturbative):** From the QCD analogy (exploration 007), non-perturbative gravitational confinement could produce stable Planck-mass black hole remnants that serve as dark matter.

4. **The fakeon itself is NOT dark matter:** Fakeons are purely virtual — they cannot be produced on-shell and cannot constitute dark matter. This is a key distinction from ghost-based approaches.

5. **Fake scalar doublet and vacuum stability:** While the fake doublet doesn't provide dark matter directly (no on-shell production), it can stabilize the electroweak vacuum, which affects dark matter model building.

### 5.5 Additional Novel Connection: Near-Criticality of the SM

Perhaps the most profound connection between QG+F and the Standard Model is the **near-criticality** observation:

The measured values of the Higgs mass (125.1 GeV) and top mass (172.7 GeV) place the SM vacuum almost exactly at the boundary between stability and instability. This "near-criticality" is:
- **Explained** by the Shaposhnikov-Wetterich AS boundary condition (λ → 0 at UV FP)
- **Consistent** with agravity's dimensional transmutation (scale invariance naturally produces criticality)
- **A prediction** of the entire QG+F/AS framework, not an accident

This is arguably the strongest piece of evidence connecting quantum gravity to particle physics: the Higgs boson mass, measured to be 125.1 ± 0.1 GeV, is within 1 GeV of the Shaposhnikov-Wetterich prediction made 3 years before discovery.

### Key Takeaways

1. **QG+F/AS provides the ONLY quantum gravity framework with a confirmed particle physics prediction** (Higgs mass). No other approach to quantum gravity has achieved this.

2. **The perturbative QG+F framework is not very constraining on the SM** — f₂ asymptotic freedom accepts any matter content, and gravity couples universally. The strong constraints come from the non-perturbative AS regime (boundary conditions at the UV FP).

3. **Agravity is the most phenomenologically developed version of QG+F**, addressing hierarchy, DM, baryogenesis, and neutrino masses through a minimal extension (3 right-handed neutrinos). It's not a new theory but a specific realization of QG+F with particular philosophical commitments.

4. **The fakeon's most important particle physics role is what it DOESN'T do** — it doesn't produce ghosts, it doesn't create missing energy, it doesn't appear on-shell. The fakeon prescription is primarily about making the theory consistent, not about producing new observable particles.

5. **Grand unification within AS/QG+F is a high-potential direction** that could convert free GUT parameters into predictions. This is actively being developed (Eichhorn, Held et al.) and represents the most promising path toward connecting QG+F to particle physics beyond the Higgs mass prediction.
