# Exploration 008 Summary: Full Validation of Six-Derivative QG+F (R³ Extension)

## Goal
Run the six-derivative extension of QG+F (adding R³ curvature corrections) through comprehensive Tier 1-4 validation, assessing whether it's a viable and novel theory.

## What Was Tried
Systematic validation across four tiers: (1) structural sanity (ghosts, unitarity, renormalizability), (2) known physics recovery (GR limit, Newtonian potential, PPN, GW speed), (3) precision tests (BH entropy, spectral dimension, graviton mass), and (4) novel predictions (inflationary observables, scattering amplitudes, new modes). Also assessed novelty and research activity.

## Outcome: SUCCEEDED — Complete validation with clear verdicts

### All Four Tiers Pass

**Tier 1 (Structural):** The six-derivative propagator has 14 DOF (vs 8 in QG+F): massless graviton + 2 massive spin-2 + 2 massive spin-0. Ghosts are present but resolved via Lee-Wick complex-mass pairs (preferred) or fakeon prescription. Unitarity proven (Piva 2023, Anselmi et al. 2025). Theory is **super-renormalizable** — divergences only at one loop (huge improvement over QG+F). Asymptotically free.

**Tier 2 (Known Physics):** GR recovered at low energies; all corrections Planck-suppressed. PPN parameters γ=β=1. GW speed = c for massless graviton.

**Tier 3 (Precision):** Wald BH entropy well-defined. Spectral dimension d_s = 4/3 in UV (vs 2 for QG+F, 4 for GR). Stelle potential modified with additional Yukawa terms, all negligible.

**Tier 4 (Predictions):** n_s ≈ 0.974, r ≈ 0.0045, α_s ≈ −0.0008 (testable by LiteBIRD/CMB-S4 ~2032). Non-inflationary predictions (microcausality violation, Lee-Wick bound states) at Planck scale — not testable.

### Key Finding: NOT a Novel Theory — A Natural Extension

Six-derivative QG+F is best described as the **super-renormalizable extension** of QG+F, not a new theory. It's the next term in the EFT expansion, adding one new inflationary parameter (δ₃ ≈ −10⁻⁴). The new mass scale Λ₆ ~ 3×10¹⁵ GeV is between the inflation scale and Planck scale — moderately natural. The framework (fakeon/Lee-Wick + higher derivatives) is unchanged from QG+F.

## Key Takeaway
**The six-derivative extension passes full validation and fixes QG+F's only observational tension (n_s), but it's not a novel theory — it's QG+F's natural next-order correction.** The real question is whether δ₃ ~ 10⁻⁴ is predicted by the theory's RG structure or must be fit to data. If the former, it's a genuine prediction; if the latter, it's a one-parameter fit.

## Leads Worth Pursuing
1. **Can the RG flow of four-derivative couplings constrain δ₃?** The six-derivative couplings don't run, but the four-derivative ones do — does their flow at one loop predict the relationship between M₀, M₂, and the R³ coefficient?
2. **Lee-Wick bound states in gravity** — Can the complex-mass spin-2 pairs form physical composite states? This is being explored in scalar toy models.
3. **The tensor spectrum** — C□C modifies the primordial tensor power spectrum differently from C² alone. What is the exact prediction for the tensor spectral tilt n_T?
4. **Regular black holes** — 2025 paper found exact BH solutions in six-derivative gravity with extreme horizons. Are any of these singularity-free?
