# Devil's Advocate Review: QG+F Uniqueness Claims

**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Role:** Hostile external reviewer. Perspective of researchers whose programs (string theory, LQG, causal sets, asymptotic safety beyond perturbation theory) are threatened by the "uniqueness" claim.

**Overall verdict:** The system has done competent literature synthesis, but the central "uniqueness" claim rests on axiom choices that are far more contestable than the reports acknowledge. The claim is not "QG+F is the unique quantum gravity." The honest claim is "QG+F is the unique theory that satisfies five axioms we chose." The entire weight of the argument shifts to whether those five axioms are justified — and the reports spend almost no time defending them.

---

## Attack 1: Perturbative Renormalizability Is Not a Requirement

This is the single most devastating objection. The minimal axiom set is {Lorentz invariance, diffeomorphism invariance, locality, perturbative renormalizability, unitarity}. Drop "perturbative renormalizability" and the entire uniqueness proof collapses.

**Why this axiom is suspect:**

- General relativity itself is not perturbatively renormalizable. If we had demanded perturbative renormalizability in 1915, we would have rejected GR. The axiom is historically contingent — it reflects mid-20th-century particle physics methodology, not a law of nature.

- QCD is perturbatively renormalizable, but its most important physics (confinement, chiral symmetry breaking, the hadron spectrum) is non-perturbative. The perturbative sector of QCD would never have told you about protons. If gravity is like QCD — which the reports themselves argue via the QG+F/AS analogy — then demanding perturbative renormalizability is demanding you can see the full theory through a keyhole.

- Asymptotic safety (non-perturbative) is explicitly allowed by the reports as a "different regime of the same theory," but this is an unproven conjecture. If AS is NOT the same theory as QG+F, then it is a separate, non-perturbatively renormalizable quantum gravity that violates the uniqueness claim. The reports wave this away with a QCD analogy, but the analogy is not a proof. In QCD we can verify perturbative and non-perturbative regimes describe the same theory via lattice calculations. No such verification exists for QG+F/AS.

- The Weinberg asymptotic safety program is fundamentally non-perturbative. Calling QG+F the "perturbative sector" of AS is like calling phi^4 theory the "perturbative sector" of the Ising model. They live in the same universality class, but that does not make them the same theory, and the non-perturbative physics can be radically different.

**Damage assessment:** If you drop perturbative renormalizability, you admit asymptotic safety (in its genuinely non-perturbative form), causal dynamical triangulations, and potentially many other approaches as viable quantum gravities. The uniqueness claim becomes "QG+F is the unique perturbative quantum gravity" — a much weaker statement that most quantum gravity researchers would greet with a shrug, since they never believed the answer was perturbative in the first place.

**The reports' own admission:** Strategy-002 explicitly says the "most interesting option" for future work is "dropping 'perturbative' entirely and working in a genuinely non-perturbative framework (which may not even look like QFT)." This is a confession that the authors themselves believe the axiom is potentially wrong.

## Attack 2: Fakeons Are Not Physics

The fakeon prescription (Anselmi-Piva 2018) is presented as established physics. It is not. It is a proposal that is less than a decade old, has a small author community, and faces serious conceptual objections.

**The circularity problem:**

The ghost problem in quadratic gravity is this: the massive spin-2 mode has negative norm, which violates unitarity. The fakeon "solution" is to declare that this mode is "purely virtual" — it can never appear as an external state, only inside Feynman diagrams. Unitarity is then recovered because the problematic states are excluded by definition.

But this is precisely the circularity critics allege: the theory is unitary because you defined the ghost out of the physical spectrum. This is not deriving unitarity from the theory — it is imposing unitarity on the theory by restricting which states you allow. One could equally "solve" the ghost problem in any higher-derivative theory by declaring all ghosts to be fakeons. The prescription is not falsifiable at the level of the prescription itself.

**The microcausality cost:**

The reports note, almost in passing, that the fakeon prescription violates microcausality at the Planck scale (Delta_t ~ 10^{-43} s). This is listed as a "cost" in a table. But microcausality is not a minor technical property — it is a foundational axiom of quantum field theory, tied to the spin-statistics theorem, the CPT theorem, and the entire axiomatic QFT framework (Wightman axioms, Haag-Kastler axioms). If you violate microcausality, you are not doing standard QFT anymore. The reports cannot simultaneously claim the theory is "standard QFT" and acknowledge it violates one of the defining axioms of QFT.

**Physical interpretation:**

What IS a fakeon physically? It is a particle that "exists" inside loops but can never be produced or detected. This is uncomfortably close to the medieval concept of epicycles — mathematical devices with no physical referent. At minimum, the reports should engage seriously with the question: what would it mean for a degree of freedom to exist mathematically but not physically? How is this different from simply having the wrong theory?

**The competition:**

Lee-Wick quantization is dismissed because it "violates unitarity" (Kubo-Kugo 2023). But the Lee-Wick community could equally point to the fakeon's microcausality violation and say "your prescription violates causality." The choice between unitarity-violation and causality-violation is a CHOICE, not a derivation. Different physicists will make it differently, and the reports present only one side.

**Damage assessment:** If fakeons are rejected, then quadratic gravity has ghosts, unitarity fails, and the entire program collapses. The uniqueness claim becomes "there is no viable perturbative quantum gravity" — which is exactly what string theorists have been saying for 40 years.

## Attack 3: You Missed My Theory

The reports claim to have mapped the "entire landscape" of perturbative quantum gravity. The list of examined alternatives is remarkably short. Here are approaches that were either not examined or dismissed too quickly:

**1. Asymptotically safe gravity beyond perturbation theory.** The reports treat AS as "probably the same theory as QG+F." This is an assumption, not a result. The functional renormalization group (Wetterich, Reuter) operates in a fundamentally different mathematical framework than perturbative QFT. The fixed-point structure, the relevant operators, and the physical predictions can differ from QG+F. The reports cite Sen-Wetterich-Yamada (2022) as evidence for equivalence, but that paper found both fixed points in the SAME truncation — it did not prove they describe the same physics. Truncation artifacts in the FRGE are a known and serious problem.

**2. Causal Dynamical Triangulations (CDT).** CDT is mentioned only as evidence for d_s = 2. But CDT is itself a candidate quantum gravity — one that is non-perturbative, background-independent, and produces 4D spacetime dynamically. The reports never evaluate whether CDT satisfies the axiom stack. It arguably satisfies Lorentz invariance (emergently), diffeomorphism invariance (by construction), locality (at macroscopic scales), and unitarity (by construction as a Euclidean path integral). It is not "perturbatively renormalizable" because it is not perturbative. If the reports drop that axiom (as they admit may be necessary), CDT is a live candidate that was never tested.

**3. Tensor models and group field theory.** These are combinatorial/algebraic approaches to quantum gravity that have produced significant results (Gurau's 1/N expansion, colored tensor models). They were not examined.

**4. Non-commutative geometry (Connes).** The spectral action principle produces the Standard Model coupled to gravity from purely geometric axioms. The reports never examine whether this approach survives the constraint stack. Connes' approach is one of the few that actually DERIVES the Standard Model gauge group — something the reports list as an unsolved problem for QG+F.

**5. Twistor string theory (Witten, 2003).** Produces perturbative gravity amplitudes in a radically different framework. Not examined.

**6. Amplitudes program.** The modern S-matrix / on-shell methods approach to gravity (BCFW recursion, double copy, color-kinematics duality) constructs gravitational amplitudes without ever writing down a Lagrangian. This framework might produce a consistent quantum gravity that looks nothing like QG+F at the Lagrangian level but agrees at the amplitude level. The reports never consider this possibility.

**7. Causal sets (Sorkin).** Mentioned only in passing. Causal set theory is a well-developed program that reproduces the cosmological constant from first principles (the Sorkin prediction Lambda ~ 1/sqrt(N), which roughly matches observation). This is more than QG+F can say about Lambda. The reports dismiss causal sets because they break Lorentz invariance — but the causal set community argues Lorentz invariance is emergent and exactly recovered in the continuum limit (Bombelli-Henson-Sorkin swerves notwithstanding).

**Damage assessment:** The "we examined all alternatives" claim is clearly false. The reports examined alternatives within a very specific framework (perturbative QFT with a local Lagrangian) and found QG+F is unique within that framework. This is unsurprising. It is like searching for the best Italian restaurant in a single city block and declaring you have found the world's best restaurant.

## Attack 4: The Higgs Mass Prediction Is Not What You Think

The reports repeatedly cite "Higgs mass predicted at ~126 GeV" as a confirmed prediction, calling it "the ONLY QG framework with a confirmed particle physics prediction." This claim deserves scrutiny.

**The Shaposhnikov-Wetterich (2010) paper:**

- The prediction requires assuming that the Standard Model is valid all the way up to the Planck scale with no new physics. This is itself a strong assumption that many physicists reject (the hierarchy problem exists precisely because this assumption seems unnatural).

- The prediction depends on the boundary condition that all quartic couplings vanish at the Planck scale (asymptotic safety boundary condition). This is motivated by AS but not derived from QG+F. The connection between QG+F (a perturbative theory) and AS boundary conditions is the very conjecture the reports admit is unproven.

- The "prediction" used known values of the top quark mass and strong coupling constant as inputs. With different (but allowed) values of these parameters, the predicted Higgs mass shifts. The 2010 paper gave a range of 126 +/- 3 GeV, which is a 6 GeV window. Given that the Higgs mass was already constrained by LEP to be above 114.4 GeV and by electroweak precision data to be below ~180 GeV, the prediction narrowed the window from ~65 GeV to ~6 GeV — significant but not miraculous.

- Most importantly: was this a prediction or a postdiction? The paper appeared in 2010, two years before the Higgs discovery. This IS a genuine prediction in the temporal sense. However, the theoretical framework (asymptotic safety) is not the same as QG+F. Claiming QG+F "predicted" the Higgs mass because AS did, and QG+F might be the perturbative sector of AS, is a chain of inference with an unproven link in the middle.

**The deeper problem:**

If QG+F and AS are truly the same theory, then the Higgs mass prediction belongs to QG+F. If they are not the same theory, then QG+F has NO confirmed predictions at all. The reports cannot have it both ways — they cannot claim QG+F and AS are the same theory when it helps (Higgs mass prediction) and acknowledge they might be different theories when pressed (non-perturbative completion is "unproven"). This is a serious logical inconsistency in the argument.

**Damage assessment:** The Higgs mass "prediction" should be listed with a prominent asterisk: "*Predicted by asymptotic safety, which may or may not be the same theory as QG+F." Without this prediction, QG+F has zero confirmed predictions and one untested prediction (r) that requires waiting until 2037.

## Attack 5: Spectral Dimension Is Not a Physical Observable

The spectral dimension d_s is the linchpin of the entire strategy-001 derivation. The no-go theorem depends on d_s = 2. The "universality explanation" depends on d_s = 2. The action selection depends on d_s = 2. If d_s is not physical, the foundation is sand.

**Why d_s might not be physical:**

- Spectral dimension is defined via a fictitious diffusion process on spacetime. There is no physical particle that performs this random walk. The "return probability" that defines d_s is a mathematical operation on the heat kernel, not an observable quantity.

- d_s is scheme-dependent. Different definitions of the diffusion process (momentum-space vs. position-space, Laplacian vs. d'Alembertian) give different results. The reports do not specify which definition they use or demonstrate that their results are scheme-independent.

- The "universality" of d_s = 2 across different programs might be an artifact of shared mathematical structure rather than shared physics. All these programs involve some form of UV modification of the propagator. The return probability integral is a crude measure of propagator falloff. Saying "they all see d_s = 2" might be saying nothing more than "they all modify the propagator in the UV" — which is trivially true by construction.

- String theory's d_s = 2 has a completely different interpretation (worldsheet dimensionality, Hagedorn thermodynamics) from the diffusion-process d_s = 2 in CDT or AS. Calling them "the same" is misleading. They are different mathematical quantities that happen to give the same number.

**The constructive axiom problem:**

The reports use d_s = 2 as a "constructive axiom" — an input that selects the theory. But axioms should be physically motivated. What is the physical motivation for d_s = 2? "Multiple programs see it" is circular if those programs are all approximating the same theory (which is the conclusion being derived from the axiom). The logic is: "d_s = 2 is true because multiple programs see it; multiple programs see it because they are all approximating QG+F; QG+F is the right theory because d_s = 2 selects it." This is a closed logical loop.

**Damage assessment:** If d_s is not a reliable physical quantity, then the spectral dimension derivation path is invalidated, the no-go theorem loses its physical significance (it becomes a statement about a mathematical quantity with no operational definition), and the "universality explanation" becomes an exercise in circular reasoning. The remaining derivation paths (entanglement bootstrap, renormalizability counting) would have to carry the full weight.

## Attack 6: The No-Go Theorem Has Loopholes

The no-go theorem states: ghost-free Lorentz-invariant theories cannot produce d_s = 2. The proof uses Hadamard's factorization theorem and the Kallen-Lehmann spectral representation. Both have assumptions that can be challenged.

**Hadamard's theorem loophole:**

Hadamard's factorization applies to entire functions of finite order. The proof assumes the propagator modification f(p^2) is an entire function. But:
- Why must f(p^2) be entire? A propagator with a branch cut in the complex p^2 plane (which is generic in interacting QFTs) would not be entire. The proof would not apply.
- The theorem requires finite order. Functions of infinite order (which grow faster than any polynomial of log|z|) are not covered. Some non-local gravity proposals involve functions of infinite order.
- The conclusion that entire-function modifications "grow exponentially" conflates two distinct mathematical properties. The Hadamard genus determines growth along the real axis, but the propagator behavior on the real p^2 axis (which determines d_s) is not directly controlled by the Hadamard genus in general.

**Kallen-Lehmann loophole:**

The Kallen-Lehmann representation assumes:
- Lorentz invariance (OK, this is an axiom)
- Existence of a complete set of states with positive-definite norm
- The spectral density rho(mu^2) >= 0

But: in theories with confinement (like QCD), the spectral representation for confined degrees of freedom (gluons, quarks) does not have positive spectral density. The gluon propagator violates the Kallen-Lehmann representation. If gravity has a "confining" phase (which the QCD analogy the reports themselves promote would suggest), then the Kallen-Lehmann argument does not apply to the UV degrees of freedom.

**Non-local gravity is not dead:**

The no-go eliminates ghost-free non-local gravity from producing d_s = 2. But:
- Infinite derivative gravity (IDG) proponents would argue that d_s is not the right quantity to track. Their theory resolves singularities and produces a UV-finite theory without ghosts. Whether d_s = 2 or d_s = 0 is irrelevant if d_s is not a physical observable (see Attack 5).
- The no-go forces a "hard choice" between ghosts, Lorentz violation, or d_s != 2. IDG proponents simply choose d_s != 2 and move on. The no-go only constrains theories that WANT d_s = 2 — it does not eliminate IDG as a candidate quantum gravity.

**Damage assessment:** The no-go theorem is mathematically interesting but physically narrow. It constrains a specific subspace of theories (ghost-free, Lorentz-invariant, wanting d_s = 2) and has technical assumptions (entire-function propagators, positive spectral density) that can be questioned. It does not eliminate non-local gravity, asymptotic safety, or any non-perturbative approach.

## Attack 7: One Testable Prediction in 2037 Is Not Enough

The reports acknowledge this problem but do not adequately confront its implications for the scientific status of the theory.

**The falsifiability problem:**

Karl Popper would have something to say about a theory whose make-or-break test is a single number to be measured in 11 years by a single experiment (LiteBIRD). Specifically:

- CMB-S4 was cancelled (July 2025). LiteBIRD is the only remaining experiment with sufficient sensitivity. If LiteBIRD is also delayed, cancelled, or suffers systematic issues, the theory becomes unfalsifiable in practice for decades.

- The r prediction is a RANGE: r in [0.0004, 0.0045]. This is more than an order of magnitude. A range this wide is difficult to falsify — you would need r > 0.005 or r exactly 0 to rule out the theory. And even r > 0.005 does not rule out QG+F; it "favors non-perturbative AS over perturbative QG+F" — which the reports say is probably the same theory anyway.

- If r falls in the predicted range, does that confirm QG+F? No — many other theories predict r in this range (Starobinsky inflation, various string inflation models). The prediction is not unique to QG+F.

**The "15+ orders of magnitude" problem:**

Every non-CMB prediction is suppressed by at least 15 orders of magnitude beyond current experimental reach. This means:
- Modified Newtonian potential at 10^{-35} m: inaccessible
- Microcausality violation at 10^{-43} s: inaccessible
- Yukawa corrections: inaccessible
- BH modifications: suppressed by 10^{-76}

A theory that makes only one accessible prediction and whose other predictions are all 15-76 orders of magnitude beyond reach is, from a practical standpoint, not very different from a theory that makes no predictions at all.

**The string theory comparison:**

The reports implicitly position QG+F as superior to string theory by virtue of having "testable predictions." But string theory at least has a rich mathematical structure that produces concrete results in other areas (AdS/CFT, black hole entropy counting, topological invariants). QG+F has fewer mathematical spinoffs, fewer confirmed predictions, and approximately the same number of near-term testable predictions as string theory (roughly one: the swampland conjectures predict no stable de Sitter vacua, which is comparably difficult to test).

**Damage assessment:** The theory is in a similar evidential situation to many approaches it claims to supersede. It is testable in principle but barely testable in practice. This does not make it wrong, but it undermines the triumphalist tone of the reports.

## Attack 8: Additional Vulnerabilities

### 8a. The Convergence of 8 Paths Is Less Impressive Than It Sounds

The "8 independent derivation paths" that all converge on QG+F are presented as overwhelming evidence. But examine them:

1. Spectral dimension -> QG+F (novel, but d_s is questionable; see Attack 5)
2. Entanglement bootstrap -> QG+F (novel, but depends on MVEH which is itself unproven)
3. Most general renormalizable gravitational action (Stelle 1977) -- this is trivially true by CONSTRUCTION. The space of renormalizable 4D gravity actions is tiny. This is not a "derivation path," it is a classification result.
4. Asymptotic freedom requirement (Fradkin-Tseytlin 1982) -- asymptotic freedom requires 4-derivative gravity. Same as path 3 with extra constraint.
5. Lee-Wick gravity collapses to QG+F -- this is not an "independent path." It is a result about a different quantization prescription for the SAME action.
6. Asymptotic safety perturbative sector -- this ASSUMES QG+F = AS, which is the unproven conjecture.
7. Agravity / classical scale invariance -- this adds SM fields and a specific symmetry. It selects QG+F because it starts from the same action.
8. Weinberg's AS program (perturbative realization) -- same as path 6 with different emphasis.

Most of these "independent paths" are not independent. They are different framings of the same mathematical fact: the space of 4-derivative, diffeo-invariant, renormalizable Lagrangians in 4D is two-dimensional (R^2 and C^2). Once you require 4 derivatives (for whatever reason), you get quadratic gravity. The ghost then forces you to either accept it (Lee-Wick, fails), define it away (fakeon), or go non-perturbative (AS). "Eight convergent paths" is a rhetorical flourish on what is essentially one mathematical fact viewed from eight angles.

### 8b. The n_s Tension Was "Resolved" by Adding a Parameter

The 2.3-sigma tension with ACT+DESI data (n_s = 0.974 vs. predicted 0.967) was resolved by adding a six-derivative R^3 term with a tuned coefficient delta_3 = -1.19 x 10^{-4}. This is adding a free parameter to fit data. Every theory can resolve tensions by adding parameters. The honest statement is: "base QG+F predicts n_s = 0.967, which is in 2.3-sigma tension with data. We can fix this by going to the next order in the EFT expansion." But this is exactly what you would say about any EFT — you can always add higher-order operators. This "resolution" provides no evidence for or against QG+F specifically.

Worse: if you allow six-derivative corrections, why not eight-derivative? Ten-derivative? Each order adds parameters. The EFT expansion never converges on a unique prediction — it produces a family of predictions parameterized by Wilson coefficients. The "quantum-correction-proof" claim about QG+F is then undercut by the admission that classical higher-derivative corrections are needed to match data.

### 8c. The QCD Analogy Is Strained

The reports lean heavily on the analogy: "QG+F is to AS as perturbative QCD is to non-perturbative QCD." This analogy is attractive but misleading:

- In QCD, we can verify the connection between perturbative and non-perturbative regimes via lattice calculations. For QG+F/AS, no such verification exists.
- In QCD, asymptotic freedom was proven rigorously (Gross-Wilczek-Politzer, Nobel 2004). For QG+F, asymptotic freedom in f_2 has been shown, but asymptotic freedom is not asymptotic safety. The Reuter fixed point is an interacting UV fixed point, not a free one. These are fundamentally different UV completions.
- In QCD, the perturbative and non-perturbative sectors make distinct, testable predictions (jet cross-sections vs. hadron masses). For QG+F/AS, the non-perturbative predictions (BH remnants, modified G running) are all inaccessible.

### 8d. The "Quantum Lovelock's Theorem" Analogy Is Misleading

Lovelock's theorem is a rigorous mathematical result: in 4D, the unique tensor that is (a) symmetric, (b) divergence-free, (c) depends only on the metric and its first two derivatives, is the Einstein tensor (plus cosmological constant). The axioms are clean, physical, and few.

The "quantum Lovelock's theorem" has five axioms, one of which (perturbative renormalizability) is heavily contested, one of which (unitarity) is only achieved via the controversial fakeon prescription, and one of which (locality) is violated by the theory itself at the Planck scale (microcausality violation). The analogy flatters the result by associating it with a theorem that has much cleaner foundations.

### 8e. What About UV/IR Mixing?

Several quantum gravity approaches predict UV/IR mixing — the idea that ultraviolet and infrared physics are connected in ways that perturbative QFT cannot capture. Non-commutative field theories exhibit this. String theory exhibits this (via the stringy uncertainty principle). If UV/IR mixing is a genuine feature of quantum gravity, then perturbative renormalizability (which assumes clean UV/IR separation) is not just unnecessary but actively misleading. The reports never address UV/IR mixing.

### 8f. The Theory Has ~50 Parameters

Strategy-002 notes that the "complete theory" (QG+F + six-derivative + unimodular + agravity-SM) has ~50 parameters. For context, the Standard Model has ~19 parameters (or ~26 with neutrino masses). A theory with 50 parameters that cannot explain the cosmological constant, cannot derive the gauge group, and makes only one near-term testable prediction is not parsimonious. The reports present the ~50 parameters as if the number is acceptable, but never compare it to the explanatory power achieved per parameter.

## Summary of Verdicts

| Claim | Verdict |
|-------|---------|
| QG+F is the unique quantum gravity | **Overstated.** It is the unique PERTURBATIVE quantum gravity satisfying 5 contestable axioms. |
| The no-go theorem eliminates alternatives | **Narrow.** It eliminates ghost-free theories wanting d_s = 2. Most alternatives do not fall in this category. |
| 8 convergent derivation paths | **Inflated.** Most paths are restatements of one mathematical fact: 4-derivative diffeo-invariant gravity in 4D is unique. |
| Higgs mass confirmed prediction | **Borrowed.** Belongs to asymptotic safety, whose connection to QG+F is conjectural. |
| Spectral dimension universality explained | **Circular.** Uses d_s = 2 to select QG+F, then uses QG+F to explain d_s = 2. |
| Fakeons solve the ghost problem | **Contested.** May be definitional rather than physical. Violates microcausality. |
| The theory is testable | **Barely.** One prediction (r) in 2037, one experiment (LiteBIRD), wide range. All other predictions inaccessible by 15-76 orders of magnitude. |
| n_s tension resolved | **Parameter fitting.** Added a 6-derivative operator to match data. Any EFT can do this. |
| Alternatives eliminated | **Within a narrow framework.** CDT, tensor models, NCG, causal sets, amplitudes program, and genuinely non-perturbative AS were not seriously examined. |

## What Would Change My Mind

To be clear: none of the above proves QG+F is wrong. The attacks identify weaknesses in the CLAIMS, not necessarily in the theory. Here is what would strengthen the case:

1. **Prove QG+F = AS.** If the perturbative/non-perturbative equivalence were established rigorously (e.g., via lattice gravity), the uniqueness claim would be enormously strengthened, the Higgs mass prediction would be genuinely confirmed, and the theory would gain non-perturbative predictions.

2. **Observe r in the predicted range AND rule out Starobinsky inflation independently.** If r ~ 0.003 and it could be shown this specifically favors the QG+F mechanism over other R^2-inflation models, that would be powerful.

3. **Derive something unexpected.** All current predictions are either known from other contexts (Starobinsky inflation) or inaccessible. A genuinely novel, unexpected prediction that is subsequently confirmed would transform the program.

4. **Give fakeons a physical interpretation.** If the fakeon prescription could be derived from a deeper principle (not just unitarity by construction, but e.g., from a path integral measure or an information-theoretic argument that is independently motivated), the conceptual objections would lose force. The entanglement bootstrap in exploration 009 gestures toward this but does not fully deliver.

5. **Address the theories you did not examine.** Seriously evaluate CDT, Connes' NCG, causal sets, and the amplitudes program against the same constraint stack. If they all fail, that strengthens uniqueness. If any survives, the uniqueness claim must be modified.
