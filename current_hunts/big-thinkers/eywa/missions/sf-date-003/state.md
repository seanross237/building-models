# State — sf-date-003

## What I decided and why
Executed directly. The task is a single-domain creative design problem — building a date itinerary around a specific person profile and city. No sub-problems warranted independent investigation. The quality of the result depends on holistic design sense (how the pieces flow together), which is lost if you decompose into parallel research streams.

## What I considered and rejected
- **Plan: parallel research on cliff spots, nerdy venues, and sunset locations, then synthesis.** Rejected because the individual research tasks are trivial (I know SF well enough) and the real work is in the design — how the stops connect, how timing works, how the arc builds. Synthesis after independent research would produce a list of places, not a date.
- **Marin Headlands alternative.** Point Bonita Lighthouse is more dramatic than Lands End (suspension bridge over ocean, working lighthouse), but it requires driving across the Golden Gate Bridge, and dinner options on that side are sparse. A date that ends with a 30-minute drive back into the city to find food loses its momentum. Lands End keeps everything in one corridor.
- **Adding indoor rock climbing (Planet Granite Presidio) as an opener.** Considered as a sporty component, but it would make the date too long and front-load the most artificial segment. The Lands End trail is plenty sporty, and cliff hiking is more aligned with "likes cliffs" than an indoor gym.
- **Musée Mécanique (antique arcade) as the nerdy component.** It's at Fisherman's Wharf, way off the corridor. Sutro Baths history is more naturally integrated and higher quality for a nerd — real engineering, real archaeology.

## Hunches
- The Interval might be a stretch. It's 20 minutes from the Outer Sunset and the date might have natural energy to end at dinner. But for the right kind of nerd, it's an unforgettable spot. Left it as explicitly optional.
- Spring timing (late March) is ideal for this date. Summer fog could obscure the sunset entirely. Fall is the best weather but sunset is earlier, compressing the schedule.

## What surprised me
Nothing surprising — this was a straightforward execution. The strongest insight was that the Lands End → Sutro Baths corridor is almost purpose-built for this person profile: it serves cliffs, sport, nerdiness, and sunsets without any awkward transitions.

## Uncertainty
- Restaurant hours and availability are not verified. Outerlands and Pizzetta 211 could have changed operations since my training data.
- The stone labyrinth at Eagle's Point is community-maintained and occasionally gets dismantled/rebuilt. It's usually there but not guaranteed.
- The Interval at Long Now — need to verify it's still operating at Fort Mason.
