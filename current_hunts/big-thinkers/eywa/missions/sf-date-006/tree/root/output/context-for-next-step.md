# Context for Step 4: Synthesize Meta-Analysis

Three date designs are complete. Below are the structural patterns worth comparing — not the full outputs (the synthesis agent should read those directly), but the key design choices that differ across archetypes.

## Date 1: Adventurer — "Earned Views & Shared Heights"

**Core thread:** Every view is earned through physical effort. No passive consumption.
**Structure:** Progressive physical intensity (stairs → climbing → biking/hiking), with recovery meals between activity blocks.
**Pacing:** Escalation all day, peak late afternoon (Golden Gate + Marin Headlands), wind-down dinner.
**Bonding mechanism:** Shared physical vulnerability — belaying each other, being tired together, literal trust.
**Key design choice:** The date *is* the activity. Food is recovery fuel, not an event. Meals are the least specified part.
**Weakness:** Meal stops are placeholders ("nearby café"), multiple "or" options instead of commits.

## Date 2: Intellectual — "Discovery Through Conversation"

**Core thread:** Threshold moments — stepping together into layered spaces that reward curiosity.
**Structure:** Knowledge spaces in sequence (rare books → bookstore → historic library → hidden park → dinner), each deepening the conversation layer.
**Pacing:** Slow burn. Morning is high-knowledge (Bancroft rare books), midday is canon (City Lights), afternoon is obscure (Mechanics' Library), evening is reflection.
**Bonding mechanism:** Shared discovery — reading to each other, asking questions, finding hidden things together. Conversation is the main event.
**Key design choice:** Spectacle is deliberately minimized. Every venue has historical weight and rewards depth over photos. Conversation prompts are built into each stop.
**Weakness:** Opens in Berkeley (not SF), dinner gives two options, ~7 stops exceeds the 3-5 target.

## Date 3: Foodie Artist — "Layers of Light and Taste"

**Core thread:** Visual palette progression — white/natural light → warm ochre → candlelight/jewel tones → deep blue → streetlight cool tones.
**Structure:** Sensory escalation (coffee → light snack → pure landscape → full multi-course meal → simple dessert).
**Pacing:** Movement → stillness → immersion → movement → simplicity. Each space has one sensory job.
**Bonding mechanism:** Shared noticing — attention to texture, light, sound, aroma. The date trains you to perceive together.
**Key design choice:** The date itself is an art piece with a deliberate tonal palette. The coherence section explicitly maps visual through-line, sensory escalation, and pacing.
**Weakness:** Several venues likely fabricated or closed (Vespertine is in LA, not SF). Two "or" options for dessert.

## Cross-cutting observations for synthesis

1. **All three dates have a single sentence that captures the emotional thread** — but they're very different kinds of threads (physical accomplishment vs. intellectual discovery vs. sensory attention).
2. **Pacing differs radically by archetype** — adventurer escalates then crashes; intellectual slow-burns; artist oscillates between movement and stillness.
3. **Food plays a completely different role in each** — fuel (adventurer), context for conversation (intellectual), the main event (artist).
4. **All three share the "or" flaw** — giving alternatives instead of committing. Worth noting as an anti-pattern.
5. **All three are more verbose than requested** — each added psychology/philosophy sections beyond the spec. The synthesis step can note whether this helps or hurts.
