## What I decided and why

**2-step linear plan**: per-itinerary analysis → comparative synthesis + final document.

The task is clearly structured: read three itineraries, analyze each on its own terms, compare across dimensions, extract principles, compile the full doc. I chose to split this into exactly two steps because:

1. **Step 1 (analysis)** isolates the close-reading work — an executor focuses entirely on understanding each itinerary on its own terms, with a structured five-dimension framework. This produces clean, comparable inputs for step 2.

2. **Step 2 (synthesis + assembly)** takes clean inputs and does the harder conceptual work: finding the cross-cutting patterns, extracting generalizable principles, and assembling the final document. Bundling comparison + principles + assembly into one step is fine because these flow naturally from the same analytical act — they're not independently decomposable without creating awkward hand-offs.

## Alternatives considered and rejected

**3-step plan (independent per-archetype analyses)**: I considered three independent steps (one per archetype) before the synthesis. Rejected because: (a) the itineraries are short (~78–109 lines each), so one executor can easily handle all three; (b) splitting 3 ways creates coordination overhead without proportionate benefit; (c) the five-dimension framework makes the per-archetype analysis tractable in a single pass.

**4-step plan (analysis → comparison → principles → assembly)**: Too granular. Comparison and principle extraction happen in the same cognitive act — you notice what differs, which surfaces what matters, which becomes the principle. Separating them would force an artificial hand-off mid-thought.

**Single executor step**: Tempting given the modest input size (~300 lines total). Rejected because the analysis work (what made each date work) is conceptually distinct from the synthesis work (what does that reveal about date design in general). Separating them ensures step 1 doesn't overfit to the synthesis question and produces genuinely independent per-archetype assessments.
