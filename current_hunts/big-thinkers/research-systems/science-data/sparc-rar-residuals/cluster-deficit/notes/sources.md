# Key Sources

- Chan & Del Popolo 2020, "The radial acceleration relation in galaxy clusters"
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.06141
  Note: 52 non-cool-core clusters; finds large scatter in cluster RAR and argues the galaxy RAR is unlikely to be universal on cluster scales.

- Tian et al. 2024, "A distinct radial acceleration relation across the brightest cluster galaxies and galaxy clusters"
  Link: https://astroweb.case.edu/ssm/papers/aa47868-23.pdf
  Note: argues for a tight cluster/BCG RAR with acceleration scale g‡ about 17 times the galaxy value; quotes intrinsic scatter about 15 percent for the CLASH RAR and a MOND missing-mass ratio Q about 2.2 at median cluster baryonic acceleration.

- Kelleher & Lelli 2024, "Galaxy clusters in Milgromian dynamics: Missing matter, hydrostatic bias, and the external field effect"
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.08557
  Note: five X-COP clusters with explicit per-cluster MOND missing-mass proxies; strong relaxed/merging split and active-neutrino phase-space argument requiring 2-5 eV active neutrinos, hence ruled out.

- Famaey, Pizzuti, Saltas 2024/2025, "On the nature of the missing mass of galaxy clusters in MOND: the view from gravitational lensing"
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.02612
  Note: CLASH lensing sample; finds remarkable inner uniformity in missing-to-hot-gas density ratio of order 10 and exponential cutoff radius about 400 kpc.

- Mistele et al. 2025, "Mass models of galaxy clusters from a non-parametric weak-lensing reconstruction"
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.13716
  Note: CLASH weak-lensing mass profiles; baryonic fractions vary significantly cluster to cluster and the cluster offset from the galaxy BTFR/RAR depends strongly on gas-mass extrapolation.

- Zhang, Hasani Zonoozi, Kroupa 2026, "Revisiting the missing mass problem in MOND for nearby galaxy clusters"
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06082
  Note: 46 nearby clusters; with IGIMF-based stellar remnants, total baryons reach at least 88 percent of MOND dynamical mass on average. Useful as a recent caveat, but model-dependent and later than the papers requested in the prompt.

- Famaey & McGaugh 2012 review
  Link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.12942/lrr-2012-10
  Note: standard review of MOND successes and the classical factor-of-few residual cluster deficit.

- KATRIN 2025 result
  Link: https://www.katrin.kit.edu/1271.php
  Note: direct electron-neutrino mass limit tightened to m_nu < 0.45 eV at 90 percent CL.

- KATRIN Science paper metadata
  Link: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40208982/
  Note: "Direct neutrino-mass measurement based on 259 days of KATRIN data", Science 388 (2025), 180-185.

- Planck 2018 cosmological parameters
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1807.06209
  Note: standard LCDM benchmark behind the often-quoted Sigma m_nu < 0.12 eV type bounds when combined with BAO; these bounds are model-dependent and do not port directly to MOND cosmologies.
