# Notes and Source Pointers

- **Mistele et al. 2024, JCAP, _Radial acceleration relation of galaxies with joint kinematic and weak-lensing data_**  
  Joint kinematic + weak-lensing RAR. Key result: weak-lensing RAR smoothly extends the kinematic RAR by about 2.5 dex, and early/late types align once isolation and baryonic masses are handled consistently.  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.15248

- **Mistele et al. 2024, ApJL, _Indefinitely Flat Circular Velocities and the Baryonic Tully-Fisher Relation from Weak Lensing_**  
  Weak lensing implies flat circular velocities to nearly 1 Mpc and a BTFR consistent with the kinematic relation.  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.09685

- **Lelli et al. 2024 review, _The empirical laws of galaxy dynamics: from gas kinematics to weak lensing_**  
  Short overview connecting weak-lensing results to the flat-VC, BTFR, and RAR empirical laws.  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.02499

- **Brouwer et al. 2021, A&A**  
  Earlier KiDS-1000 lensing RAR paper; found good overall agreement with modified gravity predictions but also an ETG/LTG difference that later work argues is not robust after improved isolation and mass accounting.  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.11677

- **Milgrom 2013, PRL**  
  Earlier galaxy-galaxy lensing test; reported MOND agreement for blue/spiral and red/elliptical subsamples with baryonic mass-to-light ratios, versus much larger Newtonian-inferred M/L.  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/1305.3516

- **Bekenstein 2004**  
  Canonical relativistic MOND completion (TeVeS). Key weak-field statement: gravitational light deflection is governed by the same potential responsible for particle dynamics.  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/astro-ph/0403694

- **Famaey & McGaugh 2012 review**  
  Useful for the conceptual split between modified inertia and modified gravity, and for the long-standing cluster missing-mass problem in MOND.  
  https://link.springer.com/article/10.12942/lrr-2012-10

- **Mistele, McGaugh, Hossenfelder 2023**  
  Superfluid-dark-matter variant reproducing MOND-like dynamics but not the same lensing is in tension with weak-lensing data.  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.08560

- **Famaey et al. 2024, _On the nature of the missing mass of galaxy clusters in MOND: the view from gravitational lensing_**  
  CLASH strong+weak lensing; residual MOND missing component remains, with inner missing-to-hot-gas density ratio around 10.  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.02612

- **Mistele et al. 2025, _Mass models of galaxy clusters from a non-parametric weak-lensing reconstruction_**  
  Clusters deviate from the galaxy BTFR and RAR unless an additional positive baryonic component is added.  
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.13716
