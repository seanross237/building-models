# Literature Notes

This is a compact source list for the main empirical relations and extension attempts relevant to the search.

## Koide and direct follow-ups

- Yoshio Koide, "New View of Quark and Lepton Mass Hierarchy," Phys. Lett. B 120 (1983) 161-165.
  Link: https://www.osti.gov/biblio/5835500
  Relevance: classic charged-lepton Koide paper.

- R. Foot, "A note on Koide's lepton mass relation," 1994.
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/9402242
  Relevance: early short follow-up; gives the geometric interpretation of the charged-lepton relation.

- Alejandro Rivero and Andre Gsponer, "The strange formula of Dr. Koide," 2005.
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/0505220
  Relevance: historical review of Koide phenomenology and extensions to quarks and neutrinos.

## Quark and neutrino extensions

- Nan Li and Bo-Qiang Ma, "Estimate of neutrino masses from Koide's relation," 2005.
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/0505028
  Relevance: introduces deviation parameters for neutrinos and quarks.

- Nan Li and Bo-Qiang Ma, "Energy scale independence of Koide's relation for quark and lepton masses," 2006.
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/0601031
  Relevance: studies running-mass behavior and quark-sector Koide-like parameters.

- Nan Li and Bo-Qiang Ma, "On the Koide-like Relations for the Running Masses of Charged Leptons, Neutrinos and Quarks," 2006.
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/0602134
  Relevance: explicit running-mass Koide extensions across fermion sectors.

- A. Kartavtsev, "A remark on the Koide relation for quarks," 2011.
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1111.0480
  Relevance: quark-sector generalization using all six quark masses.

## Theory attempts to protect or explain Koide

- Y. Sumino, "Family Gauge Symmetry and Koide's Mass Formula," 2008.
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/0812.2090
  Relevance: proposes a family-gauge mechanism that cancels QED corrections.

- Y. Sumino, "Family Gauge Symmetry as an Origin of Koide's Mass Formula and Charged Lepton Spectrum," 2008.
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/0812.2103
  Relevance: expanded model-building implementation of the Sumino mechanism.

## Related mass and mixing numerology

- R. Gatto, G. Sartori, M. Tonin, "Weak self-masses, Cabibbo angle, and broken SU(2) x SU(2)," 1968.
  Link: https://www.osti.gov/biblio/4810496-weak-self-masses-cabibbo-angle-broken-su-sub-su
  Relevance: classic Gatto-Sartori-Tonin relation behind sin(theta_C) ~ sqrt(m_d / m_s).

- H. Georgi and C. Jarlskog, "A new lepton-quark mass relation in a unified theory," 1979.
  Link: https://cds.cern.ch/record/142456
  Relevance: classic Georgi-Jarlskog relation linking charged-lepton and down-type quark masses at the GUT scale.

- S. F. King, S. Morisi, E. Peinado, J. W. F. Valle, "Quark-Lepton Mass Relation in a Realistic A4 Extension of the Standard Model," 2013.
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1301.7065
  Relevance: "golden mass relation" style charged-lepton/down-quark relation without GUT unification.

- Mu-Chun Chen, Stephen F. King, Omar Medina, Jose W. F. Valle, "Quark-lepton mass relations from modular flavor symmetry," 2023.
  Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.09255
  Relevance: modern flavor-model example where experimentally testable quark-lepton mass relations emerge.

## Statistical-significance note

I did not find a standard primary-source paper that cleanly quantifies the look-elsewhere significance of Koide-like searches across broad Standard Model parameter spaces. That gap is why the main deliverable here uses an explicit 10,000-set Monte Carlo null rather than citing a literature estimate.
