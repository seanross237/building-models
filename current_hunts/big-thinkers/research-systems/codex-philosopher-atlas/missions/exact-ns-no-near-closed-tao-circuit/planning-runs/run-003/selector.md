# Selector Verdict - exact-ns-no-near-closed-tao-circuit / run-003

## Selected Portfolio

### 1. Chain 02 - Admissibility Versus Exact-Closure Separation

Selected.

This is the strongest lead branch because it uses the Step `008` negative most
directly and asks the sharpest downstream question now on the table:
whether repaired `G_tmpl`, repaired `G_leak`, or their conjunction implies any
honest finite exact closure statement on the frozen ledger.

It also has the best floor in the set. Even if the strongest separation claim
fails, the branch can still return an exact closure-predicate memo, a witness
relation matrix, or a minimal-repair implication statement that clarifies which
mission objects are theorem-grade and which are only screens.

### 2. Chain 03 - Support-Level One-Bridge No-Finite-Closure Theorem

Selected.

This is the portfolio moonshot. It repairs the precise support-ledger gap that
Step `008` exposed and has the highest obstruction-side ceiling:
an audited impossibility theorem, a forced-support lower bound, or a sharply
defined loophole realization.

Its failure value remains strong. Even without a full theorem, the branch can
still produce the finite realization ledger and forced-triad table that the
mission currently lacks, or else cleanly hand off an explicit loophole packet
to later constructive work.

### 3. Chain 01 - Exact `F_SL(rho)` Template Frontier

Selected.

This is the cleanest positive calibration branch left after the `F_SS(1/12)`
collapse. It determines exactly what repaired `G_tmpl` earns on the carried
`F_SL(rho)` family and, just as importantly, what that family-local theorem
does not license about honest exact closure.

It belongs in the top three because it adds a different kind of value from the
two obstruction-facing branches:
an exact admissible-interval theorem, a corrected endpoint memo, or a bounded
family-level negative in the same repaired Step-6 currency.

## Full Ranking

1. Chain 02 - Admissibility Versus Exact-Closure Separation
2. Chain 03 - Support-Level One-Bridge No-Finite-Closure Theorem

4. Chain 04 - Adversarial Exact Near-Circuit Construction Beyond `F_SS`

## Why This Is The Strongest Three-Chain Portfolio

- It maximizes diversity across relation testing, support-level obstruction,
  and family-local positive theorem work.
- It preserves a genuine high-upside path through Chain `03` while keeping two
  branches with stronger presentable floors than the broader constructive
  search.
- Every selected branch still yields useful output on failure:
  Chain `02` can return a precise separation or repair memo,
  Chain `03` can still return an audited support ledger or loophole packet,
  and Chain `01` can still return an exact endpoint correction or bounded
  family-level negative.
- It keeps one honest positive-control branch in the portfolio, which matters
  after Step `008` so the mission does not overgeneralize from the
  `F_SS(1/12)` failure to claims about the whole carried `F_SL` family.

## Why Chain 04 Was Not Selected

Chain `04` is not weak.
It is the strongest fresh-construction kill route in the set.
But under a three-slot budget it is the easiest cut:

- much of its setup overlaps with Chain `03` at the support-ledger and
  forced-triad level;
- its floor is lower and more search-contingent than Chain `01`'s family-local
  theorem program;
- and its best failure product, a constructive failure atlas, is less decisive
  for this replan than Chain `02`'s implication verdict or Chain `01`'s sharp
  template frontier.

If Chain `03` exposes a real loophole or the selected portfolio later proves
that a broader constructive sweep is the missing next move, Chain `04` is the
natural reserve branch to reactivate.
