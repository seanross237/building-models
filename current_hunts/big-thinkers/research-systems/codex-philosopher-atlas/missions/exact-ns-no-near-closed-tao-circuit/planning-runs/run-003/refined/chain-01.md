# Chain 01 - Record-Relative Admissibility Versus Exact-Closure Demotion

## Central Premise

The real bottleneck is now frozen sharply enough to change the branch's task.
Step 6 promoted repaired
`G_tmpl`
and repaired
`G_leak`
as screening objects. Step 7 froze the single carried witness
`F_SS(1/12)`
on one canonical same-currency ledger. Step 8 then stopped at classwise exact
closure burden rather than one finite exact closed subsystem on that same
ledger.

So the honest next move is not to prove a clean separation theorem and not to
reopen extra witnesses. It is to write the strongest exact relation statement
the current record actually supports:
on the frozen ledger, neither
`G_tmpl`
nor
`G_leak`,
alone or jointly, is currently licensed as implying finite honest exact
closure. They remain screening tools unless a closure-level object is frozen
separately.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a scoped relation memo stating that, on the frozen Step-7 / Step-8 record,
  no admissibility-to-closure implication is theorem-ready for repaired
  `G_tmpl`,
  repaired `G_leak`,
  or their conjunction;
- a corrected implication memo naming one additional same-ledger premise that
  is already independently formulable and is not closure in disguise; or
- a bounded negative memo stating that the closure target itself is still
  unfrozen, so the branch may conclude only screening status and explicit
  non-claims.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is not family-facing and not witness-search-facing. It does not try
to find a new packet, and it does not try to rescue a theorem by broadening the
scope. Its only job is to convert the Step-6 to Step-8 record into one exact
statement about what relation language is and is not currently earned for the
promoted gate objects.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze The Claim Type, Domain, And Ledger

Depends on: none.

Action: freeze the domain to the current frozen record for the single carried
witness
`F_SS(1/12)`
on the Step-7 canonical same-currency ledger.

Action: write the implication sheet in record-relative language:
what, if anything, may the repository honestly claim now about finite exact
closure from pass of repaired
`G_tmpl`,
repaired `G_leak`,
or their conjunction?

Action: state explicitly that this branch is **not** testing a family-level
theorem over all packets and is **not** claiming a mathematical non-implication
theorem unless stronger same-ledger evidence appears.

Expected output: one frozen implication sheet with exact subject, target, and
claim type.

Kill condition: if the branch slides between witness-local, ledger-level, and
theorem-level meanings of "implies," stop.

### Step 2 - Freeze The Closure Target Without Inventing A New One

one finite exact closed subsystem on the same frozen ledger.
