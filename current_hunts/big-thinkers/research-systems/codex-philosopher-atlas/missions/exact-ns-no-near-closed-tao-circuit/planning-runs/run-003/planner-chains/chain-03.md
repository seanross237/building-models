# Chain 03 - Support-Level One-Bridge No-Finite-Closure Theorem

## Central Premise

Step `008` stopped because the current record never froze one explicit
wavevector/helicity support ledger for the one-bridge packet.
The highest-upside obstruction route is to repair exactly that gap and then ask
a theorem-shaped question:

can any explicit exact realization of the Tao-like five-channel one-bridge core
close finitely without extra support or arbitrary truncation?

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a support-level theorem or lower bound showing that the one-bridge Tao-like
  core forces extra exact support beyond any finite closed packet of the frozen
  type;
- a precise lower bound on unavoidable forced extra modes, shell spill, or
  truncation defect for every realization in the audited class; or
- an explicit loophole realization showing that the intended obstruction is
  false in that realization class.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is support-level and theorem-facing.
It does not stay at classwise budgets and it does not search heuristically for
a packet.
It tries to convert the Step-8 witness-local negative into a general exact
obstruction or a clean explicit loophole.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze A Finite Exact Realization Class

Depends on: none.

Action: write an explicit parameterized wavevector/helicity realization class
for the canonical one-bridge packet
`P_n = (A_n, B_n, C_n, D_n, E_n)`,
including mandatory conjugates, desired role incidences, and the sign/order
constraints needed for Tao-like behavior.

Action: keep the realization class small enough that exact triad enumeration is
finite and the later theorem does not hide behind an unconstrained geometry.

Expected output: one finite realization ledger with named geometric and
helicity parameters.

Kill condition: if the audited realization class cannot be reduced to a finite
exact parameter sheet without effectively reopening the whole mission, stop.

### Step 2 - Enumerate All Forced Triads And Support Completion

Depends on: Step 1.

Action: enumerate every exact triad touching the packet support in the chosen
class, including desired core channels, mirror companions, same-scale
companions, feedback terms, and cross-scale spill.

Action: compute or symbolically record the helical/Leray coefficient sheet for
those triads so the later obstruction is genuinely exact.

Expected output: one support-level interaction table plus one minimal forced
support-completion ledger.

Kill condition: if the branch still cannot turn the class into a finite exact
interaction table, it has not repaired the Step-8 gap and should stop.

### Step 3 - Derive The No-Finite-Closure Obstruction Or Forced-Support Bound

Depends on: Step 2.

Action: prove the strongest exact claim supported by the table:
no finite exact closed subsystem in the audited class,
or a lower bound on the number, size, or burden of unavoidable extra support
terms needed for closure.

Action: keep the theorem in exact support language, not only in classwise
leakage language, so it genuinely strengthens the current record.

Expected output: one theorem candidate or lower-bound memo on forced support.

Kill condition: if explicit geometry choices can tune away the forced extras in
the audited class, stop and promote that loophole instead of overstating an
obstruction.

### Step 4 - Validate Scope And Package Either Theorem Or Loophole

Depends on: Step 3.

Action: compare the theorem or loophole against the recorded `F_SS(1/12)`
negative and the carried Step-6 objects, making clear whether the new result
explains Step-8, strengthens it, or contradicts it.

Action: if a loophole realization exists, package it cleanly as the handoff to
the constructive branch rather than as a half-proved obstruction failure.

Expected output: one scoped impossibility memo, forced-support theorem, or
explicit loophole packet sheet.

Kill condition: if the final claim outruns the audited realization class or
quietly conflates classwise admissibility with support-level closure, the
branch fails.
