# Attack on Selected Chain 01 - Joint Feasibility Frontier On The Canonical One-Bridge Class

`selected/chain-01.md` is the text of original Chain 03. That relabeling is
already a small traceability hazard, and this chain has a larger version of
the same problem: it treats several things as "frozen" that the local record
has not actually stabilized enough for a joint-frontier program.

## Bottom line

This is a real chain, not empty rhetoric. It aims at a genuine unresolved
question: whether the two Step-6 promoted gates interact in a meaningful way
or merely co-orient on the current witnesses.

But as written it is premature and structurally over-ambitious. Step 6 did not
hand off "solve the whole joint frontier on the full class." It handed off two
smaller first exact tests:

- `G_tmpl` on `F_SL(rho)` for `0 < rho <= 1/16`
- `G_leak` on `{F_SS(1/12), F_SL(1/16)}`

and it explicitly warns against claiming an implication/equivalence between the
gates on the current record. This chain jumps over that discipline and asks for
the hardest synthesis result first. That is bad dependency order.

The most likely honest output of this branch, if run now, is not a frontier
theorem or strongest survivor. It is a memo saying the joint question is still
underdetermined until the object-local ledgers are closed cleanly.

## Step-by-step critique

### Step 1 - Freeze The Full One-Bridge Parameter Ledger

This step looks innocent and is actually the first major gamble.

- The chain assumes the "full canonical one-bridge packet class" can be turned
  into a finite exact parameter ledger. The repo has frozen packet semantics
  and canonicalization policy, but it has not earned a finite classwise
  parameterization of the whole admissible one-bridge space. Those are not the
  same thing.
- If this finite ledger exists only after a new wavevector/helicity
  classification, then Step 1 is quietly reopening object-definition work
  under a nicer name.
- The thresholds are not as frozen as the chain pretends. The local record has
  a real inconsistency on the leakage side: `steps/step-005/explorations/
  exploration-001/REPORT.md` leaves `Lambda_cross = 1/12`, while
  `steps/step-005/RESULTS.md` and `steps/step-006/RESULTS.md` use
  `L_cross <= 1/24`. A joint feasibility frontier built on a sheet that is
  still internally drifting is not a clean theorem object.
- The kill condition is miscalibrated. If the class does not reduce to a
  finite exact family, the chain says to "send the mission back." That is too
  dramatic. The Step-6 handoff already specifies narrower next tests. The
  honest fallback is to do those, not to pretend the mission lost its footing.

Failure mode: Step 1 either duplicates prior freeze work or manufactures a
"finite ledger" whose finiteness comes from hidden modeling choices rather than
from the record.

### Step 2 - Express Both Gates In One Exact Variable System

This is the chain's central promise, and the local record does not yet support
it.

- Step 6 says the next `G_tmpl` work still needs one explicit `rho`-dependent
  formula or rigorously checked ledger for `Delta_tmpl` and `Delta_spec` on
  `F_SL(rho)`. If even that family-local formula is still missing, jumping to a
  common exact variable system on the full one-bridge class is upside-down.
- Step 6 also says the next `G_leak` work still needs one exact closed-form or
  rigorously checked classwise ratio ledger for the carried friendly stress
  set, plus sharpness proofs. Again: even the object-local sheet is not closed.
- The hostile side is thinner than the chain admits. The record still lacks an
  exact hostile-side numerical separation for `Delta_tmpl`, and it still does
  not land a hostile cross-only gap above `1/24`. So the "same currency" is
  partly symbolic exactly where the chain wants exact joint geometry.
- There is a hidden assumption that writing both gates in one parameter system
  will expose a meaningful relation between them. It may just expose that they
  are two different observables on the same class. Shared coordinates do not
  imply shared structure.

Failure mode: Step 2 produces a joint worksheet full of placeholders and
symbolic remainder terms, then later steps treat that worksheet as if it were
an exact frontier object.

### Step 3 - Map The Joint Frontier With Known Witnesses And Anti-Witnesses

This is where the branch starts pretending the current dossier is cleaner than
it is.

- `F_SS(1/12)` is a real explicit friendly witness. `F_SL(1/16)` is a real
  boundary witness. But the family-level theorem on `F_SL(rho)` is not yet
  proved, and the anti-family `F_DT(delta, eta)` still carries symbolic pieces
  on the template side. That is not one homogeneous witness ledger.
- The chain talks about placing `F_SS`, `F_SL`, and `F_DT` in the interior,
  on the boundary, or outside the admissible region. That sounds geometric and
  precise, but the ingredients mix explicit witnesses, one carried family, and
  a partly symbolic hostile comparator. The resulting "frontier map" is likely
  to be a collage, not a theorem.
- "Search the remaining one-bridge parameter space" is exactly the point where
  this branch can turn into an open-ended sweep. The kill condition notices the
  risk but points to the wrong redirect. If the joint map becomes a loose
  parameter hunt, the natural fallback is object-local theorem work from
  Chains 01 and 02, not Chain 04's constructive moonshot.
- This step duplicates prior-art inside the same planning run. It redoes the
  `F_SL`/`G_tmpl` side of Chain 01 and the friendly-stress-set/`G_leak` side of
  Chain 02 before either of those narrower programs has been closed.

Failure mode: Step 3 spends serious effort on a "joint frontier" whose only
honest content is replotting already-known witnesses while the real missing
work remains object-local.

### Step 4 - Decide Complementarity, Redundancy, Or Separation

This step asks for a logic verdict before the record has earned the right to
one.

- The Step-6 handoff explicitly says: do not assign a theorem claiming
  equivalence between `G_tmpl` and `G_leak`; no such implication is earned on
  the current record. This step is basically a polite version of doing exactly
  that too early.
- "Complementary screens," "effectively redundant," and "separated by explicit
  packets" are not the same kind of claim. One is about search behavior, one is
  about logical redundancy, and one is about explicit examples. The step
  bundles them together as if one analysis pipeline decides all three.
- A clean separation witness would indeed be valuable, but the chain does not
  say where it expects that witness to come from. If it comes from outside the
  carried families, the branch is reopening broad search. If it comes from the
  carried families, the current record already suggests co-orientation rather
  than separation.
- The step also confuses "no proved dominance relation yet" with "the most
  urgent next theorem is to settle dominance." That does not follow. Sometimes
  the right response to non-equivalence is to strengthen each object on its own
  honest domain first.

Failure mode: Step 4 returns vague language like "related but distinct" or a
weak separation anecdote and tries to package that as decisive structure.

### Step 5 - Package The Frontier Verdict

This step compresses three very different endpoint types into one fake choice
set.

- A joint frontier theorem, an explicit strongest survivor, and a clean
  separation memo have very different burdens of proof. The chain does not
  commit early to which burden it is actually trying to satisfy, so the earlier
  steps can drift.
- "Strongest exact one-bridge survivor on disk" is undefined. Strongest by
  what order: smallest template defect, smallest leakage vector, best joint
  slack margin, closest itinerary mimic, or something else? Without a declared
  order, the endpoint is vulnerable to arbitrary winner-picking.
- The final kill condition only rejects a purely qualitative picture. That is
  too weak. A branch can still fail honestly and yet write a polished memo that
  sounds exact while resting on unresolved formulas, unresolved hostile
  margins, or an under-specified ranking rule.
- Because the chain keeps theorem, construction, and separation outputs alive
  until the end, it never forces the burden to narrow soon enough.

Failure mode: Step 5 packages whichever partial result landed first and calls
it a "frontier verdict" even if the underlying question was never made exact.

## Structural weaknesses of the whole chain

### 1. It ignores the actual Step-6 handoff

The local record did not hand off "solve the full joint frontier." It handed
off two smaller first exact tests and warned against implication/equivalence
claims. This chain is therefore not the natural continuation of Step 6. It is
an attempted synthesis branch that should come later.

### 2. It depends on record stability that the repo does not fully have

The chain treats the promoted gates and thresholds as stable inputs. But the
record still contains material drift:

- leakage cross-threshold disagreement (`1/12` vs `1/24`)
- lingering symbolic hostile template margin for `Delta_tmpl`
- no hostile cross-only overload gap above `1/24`

A frontier theorem built on that footing is brittle.

### 3. It overestimates what "same packet class" buys

Both gates live on the same canonical one-bridge class. Fine. That does not
mean there is a finite global optimization problem, a useful frontier geometry,
or a near-equivalence theorem waiting to be read off. Shared ambient class is a
starting point, not a synthesis result.

### 4. It has bad dependency order

The branch wants the joint theorem before the object-local ledgers are fully
closed. That is backwards. The honest order is:

1. close the `F_SL` theorem-facing `G_tmpl` formulas,
2. close the friendly-sharpness `G_leak` ledger,
3. reconcile the hostile exact sheet and threshold drift,
4. only then ask whether a meaningful joint frontier exists.

### 5. It is more redundant with Chains 01 and 02 than advertised

Once you strip away the frontier rhetoric, much of the branch is simply doing a
weaker, broader, less disciplined version of the two narrower object-local
programs. If it collapses honestly, its output is likely to be "do Chains 01
and 02 first."

### 6. Its kill conditions are badly staged

The early steps are too tolerant of unresolved exact formulas and unresolved
sheet inconsistencies. The late steps demand decisive packaging. That is the
wrong asymmetry. The branch should fail early on missing exact ledgers, not
late after a large amount of synthetic interpretation.

### 7. The file-label mismatch hints at a workflow weakness

This selected chain lives at `selected/chain-01.md` but is titled `Chain 03`.
That is minor compared with the mathematical issues, but it signals sloppy
traceability in a branch that is already vulnerable to packaging drift.

## What is genuinely strong

- It targets a real unresolved question: whether the two promoted objects do
  more together than they do separately.
- A true separation witness on one explicit packet would be valuable.
- It keeps the proposed claims inside the audited one-bridge class instead of
  bluffing about global exact-NS conclusions.
- If downgraded to a later synthesis branch, it could become useful after the
  narrower object-local theorems are finished.

## Fair overall judgment

This chain is not bad because it is too negative or too abstract. It is bad
because it tries to synthesize before the ingredients are ready.

As a later-stage integration branch, it is plausible. As the first-priority
continuation after Step 6, it is misordered. The current record more naturally
supports:

- one theorem-facing `G_tmpl` program on `F_SL(rho)`,
- one obstruction-facing `G_leak` program on `{F_SS(1/12), F_SL(1/16)}`,
- and only after those are closed, a smaller joint question about separation or
  partial interaction.

So the ruthless but fair verdict is:

- strong question,
- wrong timing,
- too much hidden dependence on unresolved exact ledgers,
- and too much room to repackage object-local incompleteness as a "frontier"
  insight.

The honest near-term value of this branch is therefore mostly adversarial: it
shows exactly why the repo should not yet pretend it has a joint-frontier
theorem program.
