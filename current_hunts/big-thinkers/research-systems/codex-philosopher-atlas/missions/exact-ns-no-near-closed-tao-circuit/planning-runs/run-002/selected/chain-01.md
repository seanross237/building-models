# Chain 03 - Joint Feasibility Frontier On The Canonical One-Bridge Class

## Central Premise

After Step `006`, the main uncertainty is no longer what the objects are. It
is what the intersection of the two promoted gates looks like on the full
frozen one-bridge packet class.

Treat
`G_tmpl`
as the Tao-shape screen and
`G_leak`
as the isolation screen, parameterize the whole canonical one-bridge class,
and determine whether the joint admissible region is empty, narrow, or
contains a strong explicit survivor.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a frontier theorem showing that the canonical one-bridge class cannot realize
  a stronger near-circuit than the currently recorded friendly witnesses under
  the joint gate pair; or
- one explicit frontier packet that passes both promoted gates and becomes the
  strongest exact one-bridge survivor on disk; or
- a separation memo proving that the two gates remain genuinely non-equivalent
  even on the frozen one-bridge class.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This is the structured follow-on that uses both promoted objects together. It
is not a single-observable theorem and not a direct constructive search beyond
the audited packet class.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze The Full One-Bridge Parameter Ledger

Depends on: none.

Action: describe the entire canonical one-bridge packet class still allowed
after Step-6 canonicalization:
geometry variables,
helicity/sign choices,
phase choices,
amplitude parameters,
and any fixed normalization constraints.

Action: freeze both promoted observables and thresholds on that same class:
`G_tmpl`
with
`(1/4, 49/256)`
and
`G_leak`
with
`(1/4, 1/12, 1/12, 1/16, 1/24)`.

Expected output: a finite parameter ledger for the joint feasibility problem.

Kill condition: if the one-bridge class cannot be reduced to a finite exact
parameter family without reopening object-definition work, stop and send the
mission back to narrower family-level branches.

### Step 2 - Express Both Gates In One Exact Variable System

Depends on: Step 1.

Action: derive the common exact variables that feed both
`v_tmpl`
and
`v_leak`.
Make the coupling identities, conjugate-completion rules, and spectator classes
explicit in one parameter system.

Action: identify which constraints are structural,
which are threshold inequalities,
and which are merely witness-family choices from earlier steps.

Expected output: one joint inequality or optimization sheet for the full
one-bridge class.

Kill condition: if the two gates cannot be written in the same exact currency
without changing packet semantics or normalization, the branch loses its whole
reason for existing.

### Step 3 - Map The Joint Frontier With Known Witnesses And Anti-Witnesses

Depends on: Step 2.

Action: place the recorded families
`F_SS`,
`F_SL`,
and
`F_DT`
on the joint feasibility map and identify whether each sits in the interior,
on a boundary, or outside the admissible region.

Action: search the remaining one-bridge parameter space for packets that
improve simultaneously on the recorded witnesses, or prove that the frontier is
already attained by the known friendly families.

Expected output: a frontier map plus a shortlist of explicit candidate
extremizers or a proof strategy that the frontier collapses.

Kill condition: if this stage degenerates into an open-ended parameter sweep
with no exact frontier statement, stop and redirect to Chain 04.

### Step 4 - Decide Complementarity, Redundancy, Or Separation

Depends on: Step 3.

Action: determine whether the two promoted gates are:
complementary screens with a meaningful joint frontier,
effectively redundant on the one-bridge class,
or separated by explicit packets that pass one gate and fail the other.

Action: if a clean partial implication exists under additional exact
hypotheses, state it. If not, state the strongest explicit separation result
earned on the same frozen class.

Expected output: one exact relation memo for the gate pair on the canonical
one-bridge class.

Kill condition: if the branch can only say
"they seem related"
without one proved implication, extremizer, or separation witness, it failed.

### Step 5 - Package The Frontier Verdict

Depends on: Step 4.

Action: package one of three honest endpoints:
a joint frontier theorem,
an explicit strongest one-bridge survivor,
or a clean separation memo showing that later theorem and construction work
must remain split.

Action: state explicitly what the result does and does not say beyond the
canonical one-bridge class.

Expected output: a theorem candidate, survivor packet memo, or separation memo.

Kill condition: if the final output is only a qualitative picture or a
scatterplot intuition without an exact frontier statement, the chain failed.
