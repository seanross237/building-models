# Exploration 002 Goal - Audit Realization Authority, Provenance Burden, And Scope

## Objective

Audit whether the repository actually contains one explicit audited realization
or one explicitly parameterized audited family that can be promoted on the same
currency for the new run-003 chain.

The audit must decide:

- whether explicit wavevector/helicity realization data already exist on disk;
- whether exact coefficient identities and later-critical realization data have
  promoted same-currency derivation or citation paths;
- whether any coefficient-zero or degeneracy subvarieties are explicitly
  tracked rather than silently assumed away;
- whether one honest finite-closure convention is already frozen; and
- what maximum claim scope is actually licensed.

## Success Criteria

- One realization/provenance memo lands.
- The memo states clearly whether Step 9 can freeze:
  one single audited realization,
  one audited family,
  or only a source-basis / no-canonical-finite-reduction memo.
- The memo separates promoted authority from helpers, draft arithmetic,
  runtime logs, and non-authoritative notes.
- The memo states the correct scope consequence for repaired `G_tmpl` and
  repaired `G_leak` at this stage.
- The memo gives a decisive Step-2 readiness verdict for the new chain.

## Failure Criteria

- The report promotes `F_SS(mu)` or any other family without same-currency
  realization authority.
- The report treats classwise Step-4 or Step-8 screening values as if they
  were one explicit wavevector/helicity realization or one exact coefficient
  ledger.
- The report licenses a scope broader than the audited object actually on disk.
