# Exploration 001 Goal - Freeze The Surviving Objects On The Repaired Ledger

## Objective

Using the controlling Step-5 shortlist and the inherited Step-1 through Step-4
freezes, determine whether repaired
`Template-Defect Near-Closure`
and repaired
`Windowed Spectator-Leakage Budget`
should be promoted as two distinct downstream objects, narrowed into one
smaller promoted object, or rejected at Step 6.

This exploration must treat
`missions/exact-ns-no-near-closed-tao-circuit/steps/step-005/RESULTS.md`
and the Step-6 goal as the controlling authority for the repaired thresholds.

## Required Output

Produce a report that does all of the following:

1. Restates, for each survivor, the exact criterion, repaired thresholds,
   packet semantics, canonicalization policy, fixed window, carried-friendly
   witness or stress set, hostile comparator, and invariant downstream gate.
2. Decides whether the local record supports:
   two distinct promoted objects,
   one narrower promoted object,
   or
   no promoted object at all.
3. If the objects stay distinct, explains why they are not merely duplicate
   packaging on the frozen ledger.
4. If one candidate must be narrowed or rejected, states the exact local
   reason in the allowed Step-6 language:
   `precision`,
   `robustness`,
   or
   `usefulness`.
5. Records the final status of the discarded itinerary route as inherited
   background:
   `pre-trigger delay filter` ->
   `not useful for the target theorem or counterexample question`,
   `next-stage transfer-start filter` ->
   `not well-defined`.
6. States one no-overclaim guardrail for each promoted object:
   what the current record permits and what it still does not justify.

## Success Criteria

- The report stays on the frozen packet class, spectator partition, sign /
  phase sheet, and fixed window.
- Any promoted object keeps one concrete invariant gate on the same audited
  ledger.
- The report does not force a single global notion unless the local record
  actually proves one survivor subsumes the other.

## Failure Criteria

- The exploration fails if it silently treats the two survivors as equivalent
  without identifying one exact dominance relation on the current record.
- It fails if it promotes a survivor by changing packet semantics, event
  language, or repaired thresholds.
- It fails if it upgrades either object beyond the audited packet class or the
  carried-friendly witnesses already on record.
