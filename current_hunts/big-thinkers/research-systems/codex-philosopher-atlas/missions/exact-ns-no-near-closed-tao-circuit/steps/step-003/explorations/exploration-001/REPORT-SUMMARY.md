# Exploration 001 Summary - Freeze The Step-3 Robustness Taxonomy

## Goal

Freeze one source-grounded three-part transformation taxonomy for the Step-3
robustness audit of:

- `Template-Defect Near-Closure`
- `Windowed Spectator-Leakage Budget`
- `Delayed-Threshold Itinerary`

and state which parts of their packet / criterion / gate sheets must be
symmetry-invariant, which are acceptable only because Step 1 and Step 2 froze
them up front, and which changes count as a different model.

## Outcome

- Outcome: `succeeded`

## One Key Takeaway

- `[INFERRED]` The local record is strongest on what is **not** cosmetic, so
  the honest exact-symmetry tier must stay narrow.
  Most Step-3 robustness work is therefore about guarding the boundary between
  frozen canonicalization and genuine model drift, not about demanding a large
  coordinate-free invariance class.

## Final Taxonomy In Compact Form

### 1. True exact symmetries

- `[INFERRED]` Freeze this class narrowly to exact NS symmetries that still
  preserve the whole role-labeled one-bridge packet family.
- `[INFERRED]` Admit role-preserving rigid rotation of the whole packet
  geometry.
- `[INFERRED]` Admit full coupled dilation / NS scaling only when the entire
  packet / normalization / time sheet is transported together.
- `[PROPOSED]` Do not admit broader moves such as phase rotations,
  helical-basis changes, conjugate-representative changes, packet regroupings,
  sign-sheet swaps, or Tao's averaged multiplier freedoms.

### 2. Fixed canonicalization choices

- `[VERIFIED]` Mandatory conjugate completion belongs here as canonical
  real-valued bookkeeping, not primary packet identity.
- `[INFERRED]` The frozen sheet includes:
  conjugate representative,
  helical basis,
  role-label ordering,
  amplitude normalization anchor,
  phase anchor / gauge,
  and consistent bookkeeping for one already declared sign sheet.
- `[VERIFIED]` Dependence on these choices is acceptable only because Step 1
  and Step 2 froze them up front.

### 3. Substantive model changes

- `[VERIFIED]` Packet regrouping.
- `[VERIFIED]` Support-semantics changes.
- `[VERIFIED]` Making conjugate completion optional.
- `[VERIFIED]` Threshold retuning.
- `[VERIFIED]` Time-window changes.
- `[VERIFIED]` Changing the helical sign sheet or relative phase sheet.
- `[INFERRED]` Changing spectator partitions.
- `[INFERRED]` Importing Tao's averaged freedoms by hand.

## Candidate-Facing Freeze

- `[VERIFIED]` All three candidates share:
  one canonical one-bridge role-labeled helical packet family,
  mandatory conjugate completion,
  one shared five-channel core,
  and one named downstream gate.
- `[INFERRED]` For each candidate, what must be symmetry-invariant is the
  packet-level truth value of the candidate and its downstream gate after
  transporting the whole candidate sheet through the symmetry.
- `[INFERRED]` What may depend on canonicalization is the common frozen packet
  sheet:
  sign / amplitude / phase anchor,
  conjugate representative,
  helical basis,
  role order.
- `[VERIFIED]` What counts as a different model rather than a rewrite is any
  change to packet semantics, packet grouping, spectator partition, thresholds,
  windows, sign sheet, or Tao-style averaged freedoms.
