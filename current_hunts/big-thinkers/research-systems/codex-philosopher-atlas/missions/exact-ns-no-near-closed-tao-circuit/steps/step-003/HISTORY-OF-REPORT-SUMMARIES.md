- Goal:

  three promoted Step-2 candidates.
- What was checked:
  the Step-1 packet-language freeze, the Step-2 candidate sheets and
  downstream gates, the run-001 chain judgment and attack packet, and the
  filed library notes on support objects, canonicalization, and substantive
  model drift.
- Outcome:
  `[VERIFIED]` the required three-tier taxonomy is sharp enough for Step 3:
  true exact symmetries, frozen canonicalization choices, and substantive
  model changes.
  `[VERIFIED]` the canonicalization tier does real work and should not by
  itself kill a candidate.
  `[VERIFIED]` packet regrouping, support changes, spectator-partition
  changes, threshold retuning, time-window changes, sign-sheet changes, and
  averaged imports are substantive model changes rather than harmless rewrites.
- Key takeaway:
  Step 3 should test whether candidates survive true exact symmetries and
  remain stable once the already-frozen canonical packet sheet is held fixed,
  while rejecting any apparent survivor that only works by changing the packet
  model.
- Compact taxonomy:
  `[INFERRED]` true exact symmetries are only whole-packet exact-NS symmetries
  that preserve the same internal five-role interaction ledger and are then
  read back on the same frozen packet sheet;
  `[VERIFIED]` canonicalization includes conjugate representative, helical
  basis, role order, amplitude anchor, phase anchor or gauge, and bookkeeping
  on one frozen sign sheet;
  `[VERIFIED]` substantive model changes include packet regrouping, support
  changes, optional conjugate completion, spectator-partition changes,
  threshold retuning, time-window changes, sign-sheet changes, or importing
  Tao's averaged mechanism by hand.
- Audit readiness:
  `[VERIFIED]` the taxonomy is sharp enough for candidate-by-candidate audit.
