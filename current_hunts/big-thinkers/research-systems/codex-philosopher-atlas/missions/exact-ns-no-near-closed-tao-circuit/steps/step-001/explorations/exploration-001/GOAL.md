## Objective

Use the inherited Atlas mechanism reconstruction to separate Tao-circuit
features that are genuinely load-bearing for this mission branch from features
that are merely convenient packaging.

This exploration should build the Step-1 ledger for at least:

- stage order
- delayed-threshold behavior
- pump / amplifier / rotor / next-shell role hierarchy
- amplitude hierarchy
- admissible leakage
- time-scale separation
- phase sensitivity
- helical-sign sensitivity
- support semantics

It must also propose a sharp Tao-likeness discriminator for later candidate
definitions, without yet writing those candidate definitions.

## Success Criteria

- Produce a source-grounded ledger classifying each feature as:
  `essential`, `dispensable`, or `open but load-bearing`.
- Distinguish what is part of Tao's mechanism itself from what is only the new
  exact-NS branch's testing framework, especially for leakage language.
- State what later steps must preserve for a packet family to count as
  Tao-like rather than merely sparse, multiscale, or triad-engineered.
- Tag all major conclusions as `[VERIFIED]`, `[INFERRED]`, or `[PROPOSED]`.
- Name exact local source files for every major ledger claim.

## Failure Criteria

- Repeats the five-mode story without deciding what is genuinely essential.
- Treats generic "interaction entanglement" rhetoric as if it were already a
  discriminator.
- Smuggles in candidate near-closure formulas that belong to Step 2.
- Fails to separate Tao's internal mechanism from the branch's later leakage
  testing policy.
