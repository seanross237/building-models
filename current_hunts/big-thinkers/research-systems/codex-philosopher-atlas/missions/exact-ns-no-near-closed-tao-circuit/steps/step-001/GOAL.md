# Step 1 Goal: Chain Step 1 Extract the Essential Tao-Circuit Feature Ledger

## Mission Context

**Mission:** Exact NS No-Near-Closed Tao Circuit

**Active chain:**
`Canonical Winning Chain - Co-Evolving Definition Freeze and Exact Interaction Audit With Invariant Downstream Gates`

**Why this step exists now:** this is a bootstrap step after planning run
`run-001`. Mission control selected the current chain because the mission's
main bottleneck is no longer broad motivation. It is object stability. Before
any leakage theorem, exact interaction table, or adversarial construction can
be trusted, the mission must first say what features make a packet genuinely
Tao-like, what packet language later steps are allowed to use, and what scope
locks downstream candidate definitions must inherit.

## This Step Covers

This strategizer execution covers **Chain Step 1 only**.

You must do all of the following, and only the following:

- extract from the inherited Atlas source packet the Tao-circuit features that
  are genuinely essential versus merely convenient or disposable;
- decide what `support` means for this branch:
  single Fourier modes,
  conjugate-complete mode pairs,
  or helical packet objects;
- record the amplitude hierarchy, phase information, helical-sign structure,
  stage order, delayed-threshold role, admissible leakage role, and time-scale
  separation data that later exact-NS testing will need;
- freeze the canonical representation policy that later robustness checks will
  use;
- freeze one scope-lock sheet stating:
  - the packet semantics for later steps;
  - the allowed form of leakage and/or behavioral thresholds;
  - the rule that every promoted notion in Chain Step 2 must name one
    downstream gate;
  - and the rule that final negative conclusions must land in exactly one
    bucket:
    `not well-defined`,
    `not robust after canonicalization`,
    or `not useful for the target theorem or counterexample question`.

Do **not** write full candidate near-closure definitions yet. That belongs to
Chain Step 2. Do **not** compute full exact interaction templates yet except
where a minimal sketch is needed to justify why a feature is essential.

## Required Deliverables

Produce all of the following inside `RESULTS.md`:

1. **Essential-feature ledger**
   - one itemized ledger covering at least:
     - stage order;
     - delayed-threshold behavior;
     - pump/amplifier or role hierarchy;
     - admissible leakage;
     - time-scale separation;
     - phase sensitivity;
     - helical-sign sensitivity;
     - and support semantics;
   - classify each item as:
     - `essential`
     - `dispensable`
     - or `open but load-bearing`
   - give a source-grounded justification for each classification.

2. **Canonical packet-language memo**
   - state exactly what the branch will count as a packet object;
   - state whether real-valuedness forces conjugate completion at the object
     level or only at bookkeeping level;
   - state the canonical representation policy later robustness audits must use;
   - state what later steps may treat as cosmetic representation changes versus
     substantive modeling choices.

3. **Scope-lock sheet**
   - freeze the packet semantics and representation commitments;
   - freeze the allowed language for later leakage or behavioral thresholds;
   - freeze the rule that each promoted notion must name one downstream gate;
   - freeze the allowed final negative-failure buckets.

4. **Tao-likeness discriminator**
   - write a short but explicit statement of what later steps must preserve for
     a packet family to count as Tao-like rather than merely sparse,
     multiscale, or triad-engineered;
   - this discriminator may be a structured checklist, not yet a formula, but
     it must be sharp enough that Step 2 can test candidate notions against it.

5. **Step verdict**
   - state whether Chain Step 2 is now well-posed;
   - if yes, name the exact frozen commitments Chain Step 2 must inherit;
   - if no, state plainly that the branch should stop with an
     under-specified-object or definition-level failure.

  well-posed.

  verdict with no vague middle ground.

## Kill Conditions

Trigger an early negative conclusion for this step if any of the following
happens:

- the work cannot state what makes a packet Tao-like rather than merely sparse,
  multiscale, or triad-engineered;
- packet semantics, support semantics, or representation policy remain too
  ambiguous for later robustness testing;
- the only way to stabilize the object is to hard-code Tao's averaged
  construction back into exact-NS language;
- the scope-lock items can be stated only through threshold gerrymandering,
  representation cherry-picking, or other circular moves.

If any of those occur, report the sharpest earned failure verdict and recommend
that the branch stop before Chain Step 2.
