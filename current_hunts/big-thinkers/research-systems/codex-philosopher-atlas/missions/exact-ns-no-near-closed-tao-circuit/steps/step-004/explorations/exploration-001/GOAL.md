# Exploration 001 Goal - Freeze Normalized Packet Families And Pass/Fail Sheets

## Objective

Freeze the Step-4 burden currency before reading off any outcomes for the three
surviving candidates:

- `Template-Defect Near-Closure`
- `Windowed Spectator-Leakage Budget`
- `Delayed-Threshold Itinerary`

The exploration must choose explicit normalized exact packets or explicitly
parameterized packet families on the frozen one-bridge role-labeled helical
packet object, and it must predeclare the admissibility filters, thresholds,
diagnostic vectors, and no-retuning discipline that later dossiers must obey.

## Required Output

Produce a report that does all of the following:

1. Names the anti-circuit packet families and pro-circuit packet families to be
   used in Step 4.
2. States one normalization sheet that all later dossiers inherit:
   amplitude anchor,
   phase anchor,
   sign-sheet convention,
   conjugate-completion rule,
   finite activation window,
   and interaction currency.
3. States one hard admissibility filter set that blocks vacuous wins such as
   zero-activity or post hoc packet drift.
4. States the exact predeclared pass/fail sheet for each surviving candidate.
5. States the non-aggregated diagnostic vector to retain for each candidate on
   each tested family.
6. Maps each chosen family to the adverse or favorable case(s) it is meant to
   probe.

## Family Menu To Freeze

The output should choose from the following concrete Step-4 family slots and
either keep them or replace them with a sharper equivalent from the local
record:

- Anti-circuit family `F_BM`:
  Beltrami-cancellation / mirror-complete packet family.
- Anti-circuit family `F_DT`:
  nearly-degenerate-triad / tiny-trigger-stress family.
- Pro-circuit family `F_SS`:
  engineered-helical-sign / sparse-triad family.
- Pro-circuit family `F_SL`:
  scale-separated family with leakage that may be dynamically negligible on the
  frozen window.

Any replacement must still cover all four required adverse cases and all four
required favorable cases, and it must remain inside the frozen packet-sheet
discipline from Steps 1 through 3.

## Success Criteria

- Later outcomes can be read without threshold retuning or packet-family drift.
- Each family comes with one exact role/spectator ledger written in the same
  burden currency.
- The later anti-circuit and pro-circuit dossiers can compare outcomes without
  changing packet semantics, spectator classes, sign sheet after the fact, or
  time window.

## Failure Criteria

- The exploration fails if it needs a different normalization or threshold
  sheet for different families.
- It fails if any chosen family can only be described rhetorically rather than
  as one explicit packet or one explicit parameter family on the frozen packet
  object.
- It fails if the pass/fail rules would still permit post hoc rescue by
  retuning thresholds, moving the window, or redefining spectator classes.
