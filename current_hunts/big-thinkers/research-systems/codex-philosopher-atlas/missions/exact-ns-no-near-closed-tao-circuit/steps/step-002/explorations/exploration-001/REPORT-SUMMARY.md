# REPORT-SUMMARY

## Goal

Promote a bounded family of exact candidate notions of `near-closed Tao
circuit` that inherit the Step-1 freezes on packet semantics,
canonicalization, and Tao-likeness.

## What I Tried

- Read the Step-1 freeze outputs, packet-language memo, Tao mechanism anatomy,
  exact-NS intervention map, chain attack / repair stack, downstream leakage and
  search chain briefs, and the Step-2 receptionist search note.
- Screened possible candidate styles against the Step-1 requirements:
  finite role-labeled helical packets,
  mandatory conjugate completion,
  one declared sign / amplitude / phase sheet,
  one fixed finite window,
  and predeclared threshold language.
- Kept only candidates that become real test objects and explicitly rejected
  graph-only, mechanism-only, and threshold-tuned slogan versions.

## Outcome

`succeeded`

Three candidates were promoted:

- `Template-Defect Near-Closure`
- `Windowed Spectator-Leakage Budget`
- `Delayed-Threshold Itinerary`

## One Key Takeaway

The best exact Step-2 shortlist is a three-way split, not one canonical object:
one template-defect notion, one leakage-budget notion, and one behavioral
itinerary notion.

## Leads Worth Pursuing

- Attach one exact interaction-template sheet and one downstream gate to each
  promoted candidate in Exploration 002.
- Run the later robustness audit with special attention to whether Candidate 1
  becomes too hierarchy-tunable and whether Candidate 3 remains stable after
  canonicalization.
- Use Candidate 2 as the clean entry point for the leakage-obstruction chain and
  Candidate 3 as the clean entry point for pro-near-circuit packet search.

## Unexpected Findings

- The local record supports a bounded split family more strongly than a search
  for one universal definition.
- A graph-closure notion fails earlier than expected because the Step-1 freeze
  made phase, helical sign, and amplitude hierarchy non-optional.
- A genuinely pro-near-circuit-friendly candidate does survive without
  importing Tao's literal averaged coefficients: the behavioral
  `Delayed-Threshold Itinerary`.
