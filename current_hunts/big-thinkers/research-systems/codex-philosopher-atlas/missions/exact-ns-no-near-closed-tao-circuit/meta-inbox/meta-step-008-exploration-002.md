# Meta Note - Step 8 Exploration 002

## What Worked

- Treating Step-8 as a symmetry test between positive and negative outcomes
  kept the verdict sharp:
  if finite closure is not earned, the branch must stop before dynamics rather
  than soften the gap into
  `more bookkeeping later`.
- Using the run-002 judgment and controller memo as control sources helped
  separate
  `packet growth is plausible`
  from
  `packet growth is already proved`.

## Reusable Lesson

- When the record proves forced interaction classes but does not prove one
  finite closed support ledger, the right constructive verdict is often
  non-isolability or arbitrary-truncation requirement, not an overclaimed class
  exit or infinite-growth theorem.

## Caution

- A branch can be blocked before any dynamics are run even when its witness
  passes all inherited static screens.
  Static screening success does not entitle the next step to assume a finite
  exact ODE.
