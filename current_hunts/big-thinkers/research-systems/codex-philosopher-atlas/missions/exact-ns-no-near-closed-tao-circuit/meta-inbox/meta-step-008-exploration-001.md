# Meta Note - Step 8 Exploration 001

## What Worked

- Splitting the Step-8 closure question into
  classwise burden first and finite support closure second prevented the branch
  from smuggling a finite reduced system out of Step-4 screening totals.
- Reusing the Step-2 interaction-template freeze together with the Step-7
  witness freeze made it possible to say exactly which interaction classes are
  already known to be forced:
  mirror,
  same-scale companion,
  feedback,
  and cross-scale burden.

## Reusable Lesson

- When a branch inherits only classwise screening data, treat that as a
  closure-burden ledger, not as a finite exact closed packet.
  A later step must still show one explicit support-level closure convention
  before exact reduced dynamics are honest.

## Caution

- The phrase
  `one primary mode per role plus closure-forced companions`
  is not a finite support description by itself.
  If the exact companion list is not frozen explicitly, later reduction steps
  can drift into post hoc truncation without noticing.
