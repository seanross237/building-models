## Candidate Audit

### 1. Template-Defect Near-Closure

- `[INFERRED]` **True-symmetry tier.**
  No local evidence says the candidate fails under the minimal exact-symmetry
  tier.
  The observable pair
  `(Delta_tmpl, Delta_spec)`
  is defined from the same role-projected packet data on the same one-bridge
  packet object and should not change merely because the same exact packet is
  realized in a symmetry-related frame. Sources:
  `steps/step-002/RESULTS.md`,
  `steps/step-003/explorations/exploration-001/REPORT.md`.
- `[VERIFIED]` **Canonicalization tier.**
  The candidate does depend on one frozen role order, conjugate
  representative, helical basis, amplitude anchor, phase anchor, and sign
  sheet.
  That dependence is acceptable here because Step 2 wrote those inputs into the
  candidate sheet explicitly instead of hiding them. Sources:
  `steps/step-002/RESULTS.md`,
  `library/factual/exact-ns-tao-circuit-near-closure-screening/template-defect-near-closure-compares-one-canonical-packet-to-one-fixed-five-channel-tao-template-on-one-window.md`,
  `library/factual/exact-ns-tao-circuit-near-closure-screening/step-2-candidate-notions-must-freeze-packet-class-window-threshold-language-and-packet-sheet.md`.
- `[VERIFIED]` **Substantive model-drift risks.**
  The candidate would become fatal if it were rescued by:
  changing the role packet,
  changing the template hierarchy,
  retuning `lambda_tmpl` or `lambda_spec`,
  changing the finite window,
  or replacing exact projected channel data with Tao's averaged freedoms.
  Those are all substantive changes rather than harmless rewrites. Sources:
  `planning-runs/run-001/attacks/chain-01.md`,
  `library/factual/tao-circuit-feature-ledger/destroyed-by-averaging-means-loss-under-averaged-circuit-freedoms-not-under-canonical-relabeling.md`,
  `steps/step-003/explorations/exploration-001/REPORT.md`.
- `[VERIFIED]` **Step-1 / Step-2 commitments doing real work.**
  packet-level support,
  mandatory conjugate completion,
  explicit sign / phase bookkeeping,
  one fixed activation window,
  and one invariant defect observable.
- `[INFERRED]` **Robustness classification.**
  `stable after canonicalization`.
- `[INFERRED]` **Reason.**
  The candidate is not representation-free, but its dependence is concentrated
  in the already-frozen canonical packet sheet rather than in post hoc packet
  rewrites or threshold gerrymandering.
- `[INFERRED]` **Downstream-gate stability.**
  The Step-2 gate
  `G_tmpl(P_n; I) = (Delta_tmpl, Delta_spec)`
  remains meaningful after the robustness audit.
  Step 4 needs:
  one explicit anti-circuit packet family,
  one explicit pro-circuit packet family,
  the exact helical coefficient/sign ledger on the frozen sheet,
  and predeclared tolerances for the defect screen on those same packet
  families.

### 2. Windowed Spectator-Leakage Budget

- `[INFERRED]` **True-symmetry tier.**
  No local evidence says the leakage candidate fails under the minimal
  exact-symmetry tier.
  The relevant ratios compare on-template and off-template forcing on the same
  closed packet ledger, so a symmetry-related realization of the same packet
  should not change whether the same leakage sheet passes. Sources:
  `steps/step-002/RESULTS.md`,
  `steps/step-003/explorations/exploration-001/REPORT.md`.
- `[VERIFIED]` **Canonicalization tier.**
  The candidate depends on one frozen normalization and one frozen spectator
  partition into
  `mirror`,
  `companion`,
  `feedback`,
  and
  `cross`.
  That dependence is acceptable only if the partition itself is stable under
  the declared canonicalization policy. Sources:
  `steps/step-002/RESULTS.md`,
  `library-inbox/step-002-exploration-002-interaction-templates-and-gates.md`,
  `library/factual/exact-ns-tao-circuit-near-closure-screening/windowed-spectator-leakage-budget-compares-off-template-mass-to-the-desired-five-channels-class-by-class.md`.
- `[VERIFIED]` **Substantive model-drift risks.**
  The candidate would become fatal if it were rescued by:
  redefining the spectator classes,
  changing the interaction currency,
  changing the finite window,
  or adjusting the class budgets after outcomes were inspected.
  Those are model changes, not cosmetic cleanup. Sources:
  `planning-runs/run-001/attacks/chain-01.md`,
  `steps/step-003/explorations/exploration-001/REPORT.md`,
  `library/meta/obstruction-screening/demand-estimate-level-action-on-the-live-obstruction.md`.
- `[VERIFIED]` **Step-1 / Step-2 commitments doing real work.**
  packet-level support,
  forced spectator classes,
  one fixed finite window,
  one interaction currency,
  and one hard admissibility sheet.
- `[INFERRED]` **Robustness classification.**
  `stable after canonicalization`.
- `[INFERRED]` **Reason.**
  The candidate's only serious robustness burden is whether the spectator
  partition and normalization are canonical enough; it does not need an
  after-the-fact rewrite of the packet object.
- `[INFERRED]` **Downstream-gate stability.**
  The Step-2 gate
  `G_leak(P_n; I) = (L_tot, L_mirror, L_companion, L_feedback, L_cross)`
  remains meaningful after the robustness audit and is the cleanest obstruction
  gate of the three.
  Step 4 needs:
  one explicit closed interaction ledger on the normalized packet families,
  one anti-circuit family,
  one pro-circuit family,
  and the classwise leakage ratios computed on the fixed window without
  changing the partition or budgets.

### 3. Delayed-Threshold Itinerary

- `[INFERRED]` **True-symmetry tier.**
  No local evidence says the candidate fails under the minimal exact-symmetry
  tier.
  What matters is whether the same canonically frozen packet exhibits the same
  ordered event logic; the candidate is not killed merely because it lives on a
  finite window or because it is behavior-based. Sources:
  `judgments/chain-01.md`,
  `steps/step-002/RESULTS.md`.
- `[VERIFIED]` **Canonicalization tier.**
  The candidate openly depends on one amplitude anchor, one phase anchor, one
  sign sheet, and one predeclared finite activation window.
  Step 2 made those dependencies explicit, which keeps them from being hidden
  representation drift. Sources:
  `steps/step-002/RESULTS.md`,
  `library/factual/exact-ns-tao-circuit-near-closure-screening/delayed-threshold-itinerary-fixes-one-exact-ordered-activation-sequence-with-predeclared-spectator-budgets.md`.
- `[VERIFIED]` **Substantive model-drift risks.**
  The candidate would become fatal if it were rescued by:
  retuning the event thresholds,
  changing the window,
  changing the sign sheet,
  changing the phase gauge,
  weakening the spectator screen after outcomes,
  or importing Tao's averaged one-way transfer behavior by hand.
  Sources:
  `planning-runs/run-001/attacks/chain-01.md`,
  `steps/step-003/explorations/exploration-001/REPORT.md`,
  `library/factual/tao-circuit-feature-ledger/destroyed-by-averaging-means-loss-under-averaged-circuit-freedoms-not-under-canonical-relabeling.md`.
- `[VERIFIED]` **Step-1 / Step-2 commitments doing real work.**
  stage order,
  delayed-threshold logic,
  tiny-trigger centrality,
  explicit phase / sign / normalization sheet,
  and the ordered-threshold admissibility gate.
- `[INFERRED]` **Robustness classification.**
  `use-case-limited but honest`.
- `[INFERRED]` **Reason.**
  The candidate is honest only on a narrow finite-window, sheet-dependent
  admission-filter problem.
  That dependence is real, but it is already frozen and therefore does not yet
  amount to fatal arbitrariness.
- `[VERIFIED]` **Downstream-gate stability.**
  The Step-2 gate
  `G_itin(P_n; I) = (t_clk, t_trig, t_rot, t_next; sign sheet; spectator pass/fail)`
  remains meaningful after the robustness audit, but only as a construction-
  facing admission filter rather than as a coercive theorem observable. Sources:
  `library/meta/obstruction-screening/a-tao-screen-can-be-operational-on-an-exact-but-noncoercive-ledger-if-it-is-only-used-as-an-admission-filter.md`.
- `[INFERRED]` **Step-4 data needed.**
  one explicit pro-circuit packet family,
  one explicit anti-circuit packet family,
  event-time traces on the fixed window,
  frozen threshold values,
  and a no-retuning discipline for the sign / phase / spectator screen.
