# Meta Note - Step 004 Exploration 001

## What Worked

- Reducing the current rung to abstract connected three-edge support graphs made
  the catalog decisive:
  once the rung is interpreted as a **single** repeated orbit, only the
  seven-orbit three-arm star survives.
- Treating smaller-support three-triad pictures as lower-budget artifacts or
  multi-repeated outside-budget pictures prevented disguised rung-2 cores from
  re-entering as fake third-budget families.

## Reusable Lesson

- When a ladder rung is defined by a singular support feature like
  `one repeated orbit`, classify the abstract support graph first.
  The degree sequence can eliminate whole families before any helical closure
  work starts.

## Caution

- A three-triad picture with six or fewer wavevector orbits is not
  automatically a better seed.
  At this rung it usually means a hidden second repeated orbit or a collapse
  back into an already exhausted lower budget.
