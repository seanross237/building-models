# Exploration 003 Goal - Metric Sheet And Step-2 Readiness Verdict

## Objective

Using the intrinsic-object and search-class freezes as input, define the
quantitative delayed-transfer metrics for this branch and issue a sharp verdict
on whether Chain Step 2 is now well-posed.

The report must answer:

- what counts as delayed transfer relative to the support's own turnover time;
- what counts as delayed transfer relative to the support's viscous decay time;
- what spectator-burden threshold is allowed during the same window;
- what robustness standard later steps must meet:
  single trajectory,
  open parameter regime,
  exact invariant family,
  or another explicitly justified level; and
- what exact frozen commitments Step 2 must inherit if the verdict is yes.

## Success Criteria

- The metrics are quantitative rather than narrative.
- Spectator burden and robustness are explicit enough to kill weak later
  successes.
- The verdict is a clean `Step 2 can start` or a clean methodology failure.

## Failure Criteria

- Delayed transfer is still defined only by rhetoric.
- Spectator burden has no numeric threshold.
- Robustness is left ambiguous enough that a single lucky trajectory could
  count by default.
