# Exploration 001 Goal - Intrinsic Candidate Family And Admissibility Screen

## Objective

Identify a very small candidate family of exact phase/coherence objects built
from exact Fourier or helical interaction data, and classify each candidate as
`intrinsic`,
`canonically representable but non-intrinsic`,
or
`inadmissible`
under an explicit equivalence and admissibility test.

The report must answer:

- what the raw definition of each candidate is, with no desired-channel labels,
  packet sheets, or frozen transfer annotations in the raw formula;
- which transformations count as true equivalences:
  conjugation,
  real-valued conjugate completion,
  helical-sign relabeling,
  gauge conventions,
  normalization changes,
  and representation changes;
- why each candidate does or does not remain meaningful after those quotient
  operations; and
- which candidate, if any, survives as the branch's best intrinsic object for
  later exact support testing.

## Success Criteria

- No more than three candidate classes are proposed.
- Each candidate gets one explicit raw definition and one explicit status.
- The decisive reason for each status is sharp enough that Step 2 can reject
  non-intrinsic objects immediately.

## Failure Criteria

- Every candidate depends essentially on desired-channel labels, packet sheets,
  or frozen transfer annotations.
- The equivalence relation is too vague for a third party to apply.
- The report blurs intrinsicity with mere canonical representability.
