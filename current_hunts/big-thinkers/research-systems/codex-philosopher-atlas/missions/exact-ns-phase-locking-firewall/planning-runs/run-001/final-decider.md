## Why Chain 01 Wins

### 1. It best matches the mission's required ambition

This mission is explicitly moonshot-biased. When forced to spend a single next
chain, the right choice is the branch that touches exact delayed transfer
itself rather than only auditing encodings or admissibility classes around it.
Chain 01 keeps the one result that would matter most if true:
an honest exact subsystem that survives exact accounting and still realizes the
needed delayed-transfer behavior.

### 2. Its failure floor is now good enough to justify the risk

The judgment fixed the original overreach. The refined version no longer treats
small-support failure as a firewall theorem, no longer hard-bakes Tao's
five-role template, and no longer postpones honesty checks. As rewritten, it
can still end presentably through:

- a class-limited obstruction memo,
- a bounded escalation memo,
- or a sharply named failure atlas:
  recursive closure explosion,
  phase incompatibility,
  coefficient rigidity,
  spectator overload,
  or absence of a robust delayed window.

That is a materially better useful floor than the original branch had.

### 3. It has the highest novelty ceiling without becoming irresponsible

Chain 03 has the cleanest scoped-audit floor, but as a standalone winner it
risks spending the whole slot on another definition-level closure memo. That is
valuable as a guardrail, but too safe for this mission if used alone.

Chain 02 is disciplined and useful, but it is structurally more likely to prove
only that one finite phase-only encoding is weak, noncanonical, or
encoding-dependent. That is informative, yet it still sits one layer away from
the actual firewall.

Chain 01 is the only route that can both kill the firewall directly and still
produce a bounded negative that meaningfully advances the program.

### 4. It is the best fit after merging the strongest protections from the losers

The winning version of Chain 01 should not be the raw refined text unchanged.
It should absorb the best constraints from the losing chains:

- From **Chain 03**:
  front-load a formal intrinsicity screen for the candidate phase/coherence
  object, with an explicit equivalence test and admissibility statuses.
  This prevents the search from drifting back into desired-channel labels,
  packet sheets, or frozen transfer annotations.
- From **Chain 02**:
  front-load canonical encoding discipline, admissible enlargement checks, and
  explicit separation between genuine phase constraints and auxiliary
  amplitude/time hypotheses.
  This prevents overreading a fragile finite reduction.

Once those are imported, Chain 01 keeps the highest ceiling while shedding its
largest credibility risks.

## Why The Other Two Do Not Win

### Chain 03

Best raw probability of a clean memo, and the strongest guardrail branch.
Not chosen because as the single next chain it undershoots the mission's
required ambition. On this mission, a scoped intrinsicity audit is better used
as a gate inside the construction route than as the whole route.

### Chain 02

Best pre-screen branch and a useful reserve if Chain 01 reaches a real finite
candidate that needs a tighter compatibility classification.
Not chosen because its strongest honest outputs are still encoding-level or
family-level, and the judgment correctly showed that the phase-only reduction
is often too weak to bear mission-level claims by itself.
