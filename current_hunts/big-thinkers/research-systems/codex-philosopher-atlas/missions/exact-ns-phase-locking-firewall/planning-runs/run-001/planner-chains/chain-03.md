# Chain 03 - Finite Phase-Frustration Compatibility Theorem

## Central premise

The obstruction may already be present before any long-time dynamics are
studied. To sustain Tao-like delayed transfer, a finite exact interaction
network must satisfy a collection of phase-sign requirements on desired
channels while simultaneously suppressing mirror and spectator channels. Exact
NS may make those requirements algebraically incompatible once conjugate
completion and helical sign relations are imposed.

## Verifiable result target

Either:

- a finite incompatibility theorem showing that no exact assignment of phases,
  helicity signs, and transfer directions can realize the required network; or
- an explicit compatible assignment, which then becomes a target for the
  construction chain; or
- a bounded negative memo explaining why the compatibility problem itself is not
  intrinsic.

## Prior context to carry forward

- Exact NS interactions are rigid triad sums with compulsory mirror and
  conjugate structure.
- Tao requires a specific ordered transfer logic, not merely sparse support.
- The predecessor mission already showed that packet-level leakage objects are
  meaningful but not yet intrinsic enough; here they can only appear as
  secondary checks on a finite exact network.

## Ordered steps

### Step 1 - Encode the minimal exact interaction network

- Depends on: none.
- Actions:
  - choose the smallest exact network that could realize the Tao role chain plus
    the immediately forced companion channels;
  - define the phase variables modulo global gauge and conjugation;
  - record all exact sign conventions and target-mode transfer directions.
- Expected output:
  - one finite compatibility graph or hypergraph,
  - one list of independent phase variables.
- Kill condition:
  - if the network definition already depends on arbitrary packet labeling with
    no intrinsic closure rule, stop.

### Step 2 - Translate transfer and suppression demands into phase constraints

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Actions:
  - express desired positive transfer, delayed activation, and spectator
    suppression as sign/interval constraints on the phase combinations attached
    to each triad;
  - separate hard exact constraints from soft modeling preferences.
- Expected output:
  - a finite ledger of equalities, inequalities, and allowed phase windows.
- Kill condition:
  - if the transfer objectives do not generate any sharp phase constraints, the
    chain has no theorem target and should close.

### Step 3 - Close cycles using exact NS identities

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Actions:
  - impose conjugate completion, helical sign relations, conservation
    constraints, and any cycle identities forced by repeated modes across
    triads;
  - identify overdetermined phase cycles or frustration patterns.
- Expected output:
  - one reduced constraint system,
  - either explicit inconsistent cycles or a surviving compatibility class.
- Kill condition:
  - if exact NS adds no nontrivial cycle closure beyond the original desired
    graph, the route is too weak to justify further work.

### Step 4 - Solve or classify the finite compatibility problem

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Actions:
  - solve the reduced constraint system symbolically or classify the feasible
    geometry/helicity regimes;
  - if solutions exist, record the smallest surviving compatible family exactly.
- Expected output:
  - either an incompatibility theorem statement,
  - or an explicit compatible assignment with parameter conditions.
- Kill condition:
  - if the problem only yields a large undifferentiated feasible region with no
    obstruction and no concrete survivor, the chain should stop.

### Step 5 - Promote the result to a mission decision

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Actions:
  - if incompatible, state the firewall as a finite exact compatibility
    obstruction;
  - if compatible, hand the survivor to Chain 02 as a phase-locking candidate;
  - if the route fails on intrinsicity grounds, write that sharply instead of
    blurring it into "complicated geometry."
- Expected output:
  - one decision memo:
    obstruction,
    construction handoff,
    or definition-level failure.
- Kill condition:
  - if the final claim is merely "there are many constraints" without a solved
    finite problem, it does not count.

## Why this chain is meaningfully different

This chain looks for a finite algebraic contradiction, not a dynamical lower
bound and not a constructed subsystem. Its promise is speed: if the needed
phase windows are already incompatible on a closed exact network, the firewall
can be stated much earlier and more cleanly.
