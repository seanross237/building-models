# Chain 01 - Calibrated Tao Differential Audit With Exact Theorem-Floor Freeze And Mandatory Evidence Gate

## Central Premise

Do not spend serious mission budget on any exact Navier-Stokes mechanism merely because it sounds non-generic or Tao-sensitive. A candidate stays live only if it is both:

- genuinely damaged by Tao-style averaging at a fixed comparison level; and
- operational on one exact theorem-facing bottleneck, directly on the chosen floor or through a bridge clearly cheaper than the target theorem.

If no candidate clears that bar, the correct output is a bounded obstruction atlas or reserve/handoff memo, not a forced ranking.

## Verifiable Result Target

One of the following:

- one or more live candidates, each with a precise Tao differential, a fixed theorem floor, one exact comparator theorem, one named bottleneck, and one hard same-currency artifact;
- a bounded obstruction atlas showing why the audited candidate families fail;
- a reserve or hybrid-handoff memo showing that a candidate is structurally interesting but not yet operational on the chosen floor.

## Why This Chain Wins

This chain keeps the best property of the original selection logic: it protects budget across the whole exact-NS field instead of overcommitting early to one family. But it also absorbs the strongest critiques from the geometry and harmonic-tail branches:

- every survivor must freeze one exact theorem-facing target and one exact comparator theorem;
- theorem-floor definability and bridge debt are tested before narrative momentum accumulates;
- anti-fake-locality and prior-art absorption screens fire early;
- no final promotion is allowed without a hard insertion-level artifact in the same endpoint currency.

## Scope Lock

Before the chain is active, freeze all of the following:

- one exact Navier-Stokes formulation and one exact Tao averaged comparator;
- one comparison level:
  identity-level,
  coercivity-level,
  localization-level,
  propagation-level,
  or blowup-scenario-level;
- one intended theorem floor:
  suitable-weak / LEI by default, or one named proxy class with explicit bridge debt;
- one primary endpoint family for the audit:
  criterion insertion,
  stretching control,
  harmonic-tail control,
  helical or packet interaction control,
  or one fully specified named alternative;
- four separate ledgers:
  Tao distinctiveness,
  theorem-floor admissibility,
  endpoint contact,
  and bridge debt;
- one passing example and one failing example for the Tao-differential rule.

If these cannot be frozen concretely, stop with an under-specified comparison memo.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Calibrate the Tao differential contract

- Depends on: none.
- Action: state exactly what counts as "destroyed by averaging" at the chosen comparison level.
- Action: distinguish at least these statuses:
  exact identity lost,
  formally preserved but no longer coercive,
  preserved globally but broken by localization,
  preserved statically but not dynamically propagated,
  or preserved.
- Action: write one candidate that clearly passes the Tao filter and one that clearly fails.
- Expected output: a calibrated comparison memo with non-rhetorical pass/fail examples.
- Kill condition: if the memo cannot distinguish a clear pass from a clear fail, stop with an under-specified Tao-filter obstruction.

### Step 2 - Build typed candidate cards from mission context, with one wildcard slot

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: build a bounded register of serious candidates already on the mission board:
  strain-vorticity alignment,
  local Beltrami deficit,
  tube geometry,
  deterministic deformation/stretching,
  far-field pressure harmonicity,
  exact helical interaction geometry,
  and closely related exact-NS candidates.
- Action: allow at most one wildcard candidate that is not merely a relabeling of an existing family.
- Action: for each candidate card, record:
  exact object or statement,
  comparison level,
  intended theorem-facing target,
  exact comparator theorem or criterion family,
  nearest prior-art family,
  prior-run downgrade status,
  overlap with other cards,
  and why it is not merely generic harmonic analysis or packaging.
- Action: reject cards that are slogans, generic machinery, duplicate labels, or candidates whose claimed gain cannot even be stated against a theorem-facing comparator.
- Expected output: a typed candidate ledger with overlap accounting and theorem-facing intent.
- Kill condition: if no candidate survives as a distinct exact-NS feature, stop with a no-live-candidate obstruction.

### Step 3 - Run the Tao differential test candidate by candidate

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: for each card, identify the concrete witness of distinctiveness appropriate to its type:
  lost sign or identity,
  lost triad geometry,
  lost localization law,
  lost propagation mechanism,
  or lost strain-geometry coupling.
- Action: classify each card using the richer table:
  `destroyed`,
  `formally preserved but decoupled`,
  `preserved but non-coercive`,
  `localization-sensitive`,
  `unclear`,
  or `preserved`.
- Action: merge or demote cards that are the same structural fact in different language.
- Expected output: a Tao-differential table that tracks distinctiveness and overlap honestly.
- Kill condition: if every surviving candidate is either preserved or only vaguely `unclear`, stop with a Tao-filter failure memo.

### Step 4 - Freeze one exact theorem-facing route per survivor

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: for each Tao-surviving candidate, assign one primary proof architecture and, if necessary, one secondary interpretation only.
- Action: freeze one exact theorem-facing target, one exact endpoint currency, and one exact comparator theorem for that candidate.
- Action: state the literal claimed improvement margin over the comparator in the same currency.
- Action: if the candidate is not directly definable on the chosen floor, name one proxy class only and write explicit bridge debt that must be clearly cheaper than the target theorem.
- Expected output: a candidate-to-architecture map with exact target, exact comparator, endpoint ledger, and bridge debt notes.
- Kill condition: if a candidate has no exact theorem-facing target, no literal comparator gain, or only a theorem-scale bridge, demote it immediately.

### Step 5 - Run early admissibility, anti-fake-locality, and prior-art gates

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: test whether each surviving candidate is definable, stable, and meaningful in the chosen solution class and localization regime.
- Action: record whether the candidate is:
  directly admissible,
  admissible only after architecture change,
  or dead on contact.
- Action: for pressure or elliptic routes, require an explicit reason the candidate is cheaper than standard local pressure theory or generic interior regularity in the same theorem currency.
- Action: record the closest prior-art absorption threat and whether Tao-compatible averaging already appears to preserve the relevant mechanism.
- Expected output: an admissibility memo that eliminates candidates before package rhetoric accumulates.
- Kill condition: if every candidate is dead on contact, absorbed by prior art, fake-local after honest charging, or survives only after a theorem-scale architecture switch, stop with an admissibility obstruction.

### Step 6 - Produce one hard same-currency artifact immediately

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: for each still-live candidate, produce one hard artifact:
  a prototype estimate,
  a representative insertion into the named bottleneck,
  a localized identity with all error terms exposed,
  or a failure sheet showing exactly where the hoped-for gain is spent.
- Action: require all gains and all debts to appear in the same ledger as the claimed endpoint gain.
- Action: for geometry candidates, charge localization, frame, cutoff, pressure, and nonlocal remainder in the artifact itself.
- Action: for harmonic-tail candidates, charge split radius, gauge, recombination, and near-field compensation inside the artifact itself.
- Expected output: an evidence packet for each live or failed route.
- Kill condition: if every candidate remains a well-described story rather than a same-currency artifact, stop with a documentary-success obstruction.

### Step 7 - Run the survivor audit on persistence, recombination, and absorption

- Depends on: Step 6.
- Action: for geometry candidates, test persistence, full-stretching coverage, threshold drift, frame retuning, and prior-art absorption as one joint audit.
- Action: for pressure candidates, recombine near and far contributions and test whether the net gain still beats both standard local pressure technology and the actual endpoint threshold.
- Action: for all candidates, decide whether the surviving claim is genuinely stronger than the exact comparator theorem, merely equivalent, or absorbed after honest charging.
- Expected output: a survivor audit separating real gains from package-internal gains.
- Kill condition: if the candidate survives only in a hand-picked coherent regime, only improves an auxiliary quantity, collapses under recombination, or is absorbed by the named comparator after honest charging, demote it to bounded obstruction.

### Step 8 - Rank only where comparison is earned

- Depends on: Step 7.
- Action: rank candidates only if they share the same primary architecture, theorem floor, and gain currency.
- Action: otherwise classify outcomes into buckets:
  live lead,
  reserve or hybrid handoff,
  bounded obstruction,
  or unresolved non-promotion.
- Action: nominate at most two primary leads only if the evidence actually supports that level of discrimination.
- Expected output: either a narrow ranked shortlist inside one comparable class or a bucketed result.
- Kill condition: if the branch needs apples-to-oranges comparison to produce a clean ranking, replace ranking with buckets.

### Step 9 - Produce the canonical atlas with evidence grades

- Depends on: Step 8.
- Action: write the final atlas naming each audited candidate, its Tao status, theorem-floor status, exact comparator, bottleneck contact, hard artifact, and current disposition.
- Action: state explicitly whether each negative is:
  a hard mathematical death,
  a current-budget closure,
  an architecture-specific closure,
  prior-art absorption,
  fake locality,
  recombination collapse,
  or a reserve-only route.
- Action: record whether exact helical structure remains demoted, returns as reserve, or re-enters the live set only as a consequence of the evidence packet.
- Expected output: a reusable property atlas with evidence grades rather than prose-only closures.
- Kill condition: if the atlas says more than the evidence packet earned, narrow the claims until the mismatch disappears.

## Probability Assessment

**Probability that this chain yields a presentable result: 0.76**

The most likely presentable output is a sharper obstruction atlas or reserve/handoff memo. The live-upside case remains real because the chain still allows a survivor to win, but only after exact theorem contact and a same-currency artifact.
