# Judgment on Selected Chain 02

## Overall Assessment

The attack is directionally strong, but not uniformly correct. Its best points are that the original chain compresses too many distinct failure modes, understates how central the viscous and nonlocal obstacles are, and ties success too tightly to an inherited LEI / CKN-style proof interface. Its weakest move is to treat the whole chain as little more than a disguised demolition protocol. The original chain does contain real discipline: early admissibility gating, an explicit coercivity check, and a demand for a hard artifact are all good constraints.

The right judgment is therefore: keep the chain alive, but refine it so that it separates admissibility from stability from usefulness, ranks the actual bottlenecks before claiming leverage, and distinguishes mechanism failure from theorem-interface mismatch.

### Step 1 critique

Status: Partially valid.

Valid points:
- The original chain treats "admits" too loosely for deformation-based observables on the suitable weak / LEI floor.
- The Step 1 kill condition is too compressed; measurable definition, renormalized definition, approximation stability, and LEI-compatible compactness should be separated.
- The endpoint choice needs more justification than "local concentration exclusion by default."

Overreach:
- It is too strong to say Step 1 is mostly a kill switch. An early admissibility gate is exactly the right discipline for this branch. The problem is not that the gate exists, but that it is too coarse and too tied to one downstream interface.

Judgment:
- Refine Step 1 so it freezes one observable, one native framework, one theorem interface, and one novelty bet, while splitting admissibility failure into named subcases.

### Step 2 critique

Status: Valid.

The attack is right that "exact transport or deformation identity" overstates what is likely available on the admissible floor. It is also right that the viscous term and nonlocal strain cannot be treated as mild residual bookkeeping. For this branch, they are part of the mechanism question itself.

The attack is also correct that Step 2 needs finer failure labels, including:
- smooth-only identity,
- admissible identity only after mollification and commutators,
- defect ledger larger than the principal term,
- nonlocal term inseparable from the candidate gain.

Judgment:
- Replace the demand for an exact identity with the strongest admissible identity available, and force an explicit exact / mollified / renormalized / formal separation.

### Step 3 critique

Status: Partially valid.

Valid points:
- The original "one named bad term" language is too vague.
- A branch should not pass this gate by controlling a secondary burden while the dominant obstruction remains untouched.
- The chain should allow the possibility that partial leverage on an intermediate quantity matters if there is a credible compound mechanism.

Overreach:
- The criticism goes too far when it implies the coercivity gate itself is misplaced. The gate is useful. A transport-first branch still needs some defensible theorem-facing leverage test before spending effort on localization.

Judgment:
- Keep the coercivity gate, but precede it with a ranked burden ledger and allow a compound-mechanism path when the controlled quantity is not the final bottleneck but plausibly feeds one.

### Step 4 critique

Status: Valid.

This is one of the attack's strongest points. The original chain does not just test whether the observable has value; it tests whether it can be inserted into one inherited LEI / CKN architecture. That is too narrow. Failure against that architecture is evidence, but not automatic proof that the mechanism itself is dead.

The attack is also right that trajectory-level or semi-global structure should not be dismissed merely because it does not immediately fit the inherited ledger.

Judgment:
- Step 4 should still perform a hard localization-and-insertion test.
- It must distinguish gain destroyed by admissible localization from gain that survives in a form suggesting a different theorem interface is needed.

### Step 5 critique

Status: Partially valid.

Valid points:
- The artifact menu is too heterogeneous. A prototype estimate, failed lemma, and toy scenario do not carry the same evidentiary weight.
- The Tao-sensitivity check needs a concrete target rather than a rhetorical gesture.

Not valid:
- The sequencing complaint is not decisive. It is reasonable to delay the Tao-style stress test until there is an actual artifact worth stress-testing.

Judgment:
- Keep the hard-artifact requirement, but rank artifacts by evidentiary value and define the post-artifact sensitivity check in concrete terms.

### Step 6 critique

Status: Partially valid.

The attack is right that Step 6 mostly packages what earlier gates have earned, and that its failure taxonomy should be more finely split. That is a worthwhile revision. But there is nothing wrong with having a final formatting-and-classification step; the issue is only the granularity of the categories.

Judgment:
- Keep the closing step, but expand the obstruction taxonomy so it does not collapse localization failure, topology mismatch, exponent weakness, and interface mismatch into one bucket.

### Structural critique 1: the chain is too endpoint-inherited

Status: Valid.

This is correct. The original chain is transport-first in rhetoric, but its decisive tests are largely phrased in terms of insertion into an inherited proof ledger. That is narrower than the full mechanism question.

Judgment:
- The refined chain should still test inherited insertion, but only as one interface test, not as the only success criterion.

### Structural critique 2: it confuses admissibility with usefulness

Status: Valid.

This is correct and important. Definability on the admissible floor, stability under approximation/localization, and actual theorem leverage are separate gates. The refined chain should keep them separate.

### Structural critique 3: the route is biased toward obstruction-production

Status: Partially valid.

The original chain is indeed obstruction-heavy and optimized for early stopping. That is not automatically a flaw in a planning run, but the attack is right that the chain leaves too little room for hybridization, interface redesign, or partial salvage.

Judgment:
- Preserve early-stop discipline, but require that each stop produce a high-resolution obstruction, not just a generic negative verdict.

### Structural critique 4: the nonlocal difficulty is under-described

Status: Valid.

Correct. Pressure, strain nonlocality, and viscous defect are not generic "debts." The refined chain should require one explicit nonlocal obstruction schema rather than a single ledger label.

### Structural critique 5: hidden prior-art overlap

Status: Partially valid.

The underlying warning is sound: this branch should say what novelty class it is betting on, or else it may simply repackage a familiar dead end. But the attack states prior-art overlap without actually identifying a concrete overlap. So the critique is right as a planning demand, not as an established substantive defeat.

Judgment:
- Add a novelty declaration early in the chain: new observable, new sign structure, new localization device, or new theorem interface.

### Structural critique 6: kill conditions are not calibrated for information gain

Status: Valid.

Correct. The original chain's kill conditions are better at early stopping than at producing fine-grained learning. In a wide-funnel planning loop, higher-resolution negatives are more valuable.

Judgment:
- Every kill condition in the refined chain should force a named obstruction class and a short note on what downstream branch that obstruction favors or rules out.

## Net Decision

The attack succeeds on the core methodological revisions:
- separate admissibility, stability, and usefulness,
- treat viscous and nonlocal obstacles as central rather than residual,
- rank the true bottlenecks before claiming leverage,
- avoid treating inherited LEI / CKN insertion as the only legitimate theorem interface,
- upgrade kill conditions so they produce high-resolution obstructions.

The attack does not succeed in collapsing the whole branch into an invalid or purely negative exercise. The original chain's insistence on early discipline and hard artifacts is sound and should be preserved.
