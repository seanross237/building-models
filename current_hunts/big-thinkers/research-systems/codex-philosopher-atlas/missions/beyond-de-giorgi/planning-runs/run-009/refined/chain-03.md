# Chain 03 - Obstruction-First Packet-Complete Helical Route With Early Endpoint-Exchange And Full-Restoration Gates

## Central Premise

Exact helical packet structure is worth keeping alive only as a tightly constrained obstruction-first program. The branch is live only if one exact Leray-projected packet feature can be attached to one fixed theorem-facing bad term, written as an explicit candidate inequality in one fixed burden currency, and shown to survive mixed-helicity completion, packet-family rerouting, shell summation, localization, pressure restoration, and exchange into the inherited LEI / CKN ledger.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- one proposition-scale candidate route naming one exact packet mechanism, one theorem-facing bad term, one explicit inequality, one quantified net gain after full reconstruction, and the first narrower missing lemma; or
- one sharp obstruction memo stating exactly where the branch fails:
  endpoint-selection failure,
  prior-art redundancy,
  endpoint-exchange failure,
  mixed-helicity collapse,
  thin-sector or rerouting irrelevance,
  shell-summation collapse,
  localization or pressure collapse,
  or exact-but-non-coercive packet structure.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This remains the exact-symbol and helical-packet branch, but it no longer treats packet completeness as progress by itself. Its purpose is to determine whether any genuinely exact Navier-Stokes helical effect survives the full equation and still cashes out on the actual theorem floor, not to produce a prettier triad ledger.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze the endpoint, bad term, burden currency, representation, and comparator before symbolic work

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one inherited theorem-facing endpoint on the suitable-weak / LEI floor, normally one specific contradiction or insertion line in the CKN / Lin package.
- Action: choose one bad transfer pattern only if it can be written as the concrete term or burden that matters for that endpoint.
- Action: choose one burden currency inside that target line and one packet representation only; do not permit later switching of endpoint, currency, or representation for convenience.
- Action: record the closest prior-art comparator at the mechanism level, not just the vocabulary level: Waleffe-type triad geometry, same-helicity suppression, helical decimation, or another exact nearby baseline.
- Action: write one anti-target-shopping rule: no threshold, gain metric, or packet family may be chosen because it is merely the most symbol-friendly formulation.
- Expected output: a setup memo naming one endpoint line, one bad term, one burden currency, one packet representation, and one prior-art comparator.
- Kill condition: if the route cannot write one fixed theorem-facing target in one fixed currency before packet analysis begins, stop and record an endpoint-selection obstruction.

### Step 2 - Build the exact packet ledger and the endpoint-exchange memo immediately

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: derive the exact packet-complete identity sheet, including conjugate completion, spectator leakage, and full mixed-helicity bookkeeping.
- Action: separate exact structure from folklore by recording which features are strict identities and which are only heuristic sector preferences.
- Action: write an endpoint-exchange memo showing how a packet-level gain would have to translate into the Step 1 burden currency, and what exchange losses are already visible before any promotion.
- Action: estimate the maximal plausible pre-reconstruction effect size each exact packet feature could have on the chosen bad term.
- Expected output: one exact packet ledger plus one endpoint-exchange memo.
- Kill condition: if the route obtains only a packet-native description with no credible exchange path to the chosen endpoint currency, or if every exact feature already looks too small or too mismatched to matter, stop and record an endpoint-exchange obstruction.

### Step 3 - Run prior-art and full-system coercivity screening before promoting any mechanism

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: compare each candidate packet effect against the Step 1 comparator and reject anything that only restates known same-helicity, decimated-sector, or filtered-family advantages.
- Action: restore full mixed-helicity completion immediately and reject any gain that survives only on a restricted decimated, same-helicity, or otherwise pre-filtered sector.
- Action: promote at most one mechanism, allowing it to be either one exact packet feature or one explicitly quantified aggregate bias, but not a loose pile of suggestive effects.
- Action: require the promoted mechanism to come with a provisional burden-share estimate relative to the full theorem-facing bad term, not merely a visually favorable subledger.
- Expected output: at most one promoted mechanism with a prior-art verdict and provisional burden-share estimate.
- Kill condition: if all candidate gains collapse into known sector folklore, die under full mixed-helicity completion, or lack a burden-share estimate, stop and record a redundancy or non-coercivity obstruction.

### Step 4 - Write the explicit candidate inequality and run the rerouting test

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: write the exact candidate inequality, corrected transfer estimate, or contradiction-line modification where the promoted mechanism enters.
- Action: quantify what portion of the relevant shell sum, packet population, or bad-transfer mass is actually controlled by the promoted mechanism.
- Action: test whether the uncontrolled packet families can simply reroute the bad transfer around the favorable sector.
- Action: reject mechanisms that are exact but live only on one coherent packet family, one geometry class, or one polarized subpopulation without quantified exclusion of rerouting.
- Expected output: one theorem-gap memo containing the explicit inequality, claimed gain, controlled burden share, and rerouting verdict.
- Kill condition: if no explicit inequality can be written, or if the gain depends on a thin sector that does not block rerouting of the full bad transfer, stop and record a relevance obstruction.

### Step 5 - Charge the full reconstruction and localization bill

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: restore full shell summation, angular multiplicity, all non-favorable completed interactions, and packet-to-physical reconstruction costs.
- Action: if the endpoint needs localization, add cutoff, commutator, LEI, pressure-restoration, and local-to-global bookkeeping losses explicitly.
- Action: compare the net post-reconstruction gain directly against the Step 1 baseline criterion in the same burden currency.
- Action: allow stacked failure labels when appropriate rather than forcing a falsely single-cause diagnosis.
- Expected output: one full reconstruction ledger with a net margin or a stacked failure diagnosis.
- Kill condition: if the gain evaporates, becomes negligible, or is paid away by exchange, summation, localization, or pressure debt, terminate with an obstruction memo rather than narrowing the claim into theater.

### Step 6 - Demand a hard artifact and use Tao sensitivity only as a secondary discriminator

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: require one hard artifact:
  an explicit inequality that survives Step 5,
  an exact counterexample family,
  or a failed lemma isolating the first fatal obstruction.
- Action: benchmark that artifact against the Step 1 prior-art comparator so the route does not overcredit a more exact restatement of a known favorable sector.
- Action: ask whether the specific artifact, not merely the packet language, would be destroyed by Tao-style averaging or by replacing the exact Navier-Stokes symbol with an averaged surrogate.
- Expected output: one artifact memo with a prior-art verdict and a Tao-sensitivity verdict.
- Kill condition: if the branch remains exact and interesting but artifact-free, or Tao-sensitive only in a purely decorative sense, stop and record an exact-but-useless obstruction.

### Step 7 - State the narrowest earned claim

- Depends on: Step 6.
- Action: if positive, write one proposition-scale route naming the exact packet mechanism, endpoint line, explicit inequality, quantified post-reconstruction gain, admissible regime, and first narrower missing lemma.
- Action: if negative, write one obstruction statement naming the exact packet feature that was tested and the precise stage or stack of stages where its leverage failed.
- Action: reject any final claim that smuggles the hard problem into hypotheses such as persistent packet coherence, favorable helical composition, or suppressed cross-family leakage unless those have already been earned inside the chain.
- Expected output: a final-comparison-ready proposition blueprint or obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the final claim cannot be written without hidden regime assumptions or packet-specific theater, downgrade it to obstruction immediately.
