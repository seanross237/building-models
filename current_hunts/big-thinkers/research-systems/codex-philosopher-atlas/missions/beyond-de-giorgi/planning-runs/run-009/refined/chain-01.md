# Refined Chain 01 - Quantified Reformulation Audit With Anti-Repackaging and Endpoint Budgeting

## Central Premise

This branch stays live only if an exact Navier-Stokes reformulation does more than rename the quadratic burden. A candidate is genuinely live only if, after localization, pressure recovery, reconstruction, and admissibility debt are charged in the same ledger, it yields a net theorem-facing gain at one inherited LEI / CKN bottleneck.

The motivating bet comes from the SQG-style lesson that mechanism matters. But this is only a heuristic. The chain must allow the honest outcome that no reformulation improves on the inherited old-variable burden ledger.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- one candidate packet containing an exact equation sheet, localized debt ledger, prior-art delta, bottleneck insertion point, and a hard artifact showing net gain; or
- one obstruction memo showing that every audited reformulation collapses by equivalence, prior-art absorption, non-robust gain, endpoint-transfer failure, or net-negative debt; or
- one hybrid-handoff memo showing that a candidate becomes interesting only after changing endpoint, architecture, or propagated object.

## Why This Refined Chain Is Better

The original chain had the right hostility to fake novelty, but it gave too much room to judgment calls. This refinement keeps the anti-repackaging core while tightening three weak points: early path dependence, vague burden accounting, and under-specified prior-art and endpoint insertion checks.

## Scope Lock

Before the chain is active, it must freeze all of the following:

- one primary endpoint family and one backup endpoint family on the LEI / CKN floor;
- one theorem-facing baseline burden;
- one typed burden ledger whose allowed currencies are explicit:
  derivative count,
  locality,
  cancellation,
  integrability,
  pressure recoverability,
  admissibility,
  compactness input,
  or scaling compatibility;
- one comparability rule:
  same-currency comparison only unless an explicit transfer lemma is named;
- one anti-repackaging classification rule:
  equivalent redistribution,
  ambiguous redistribution,
  or potentially live mechanism change;
- one solution class and localization regime;
- one nearest-prior-art audit format naming the closest known reformulation and the exact claimed mathematical delta.

If these cannot be fixed concretely, stop and write an under-specified-ledger obstruction.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Calibrate the endpoint window and burden ledger

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one primary endpoint and one backup endpoint, not a wide menu and not an artificially rigid single line.
- Action: freeze one baseline burden and one admissible gain notion in the chosen ledger.
- Action: define what counts as a same-currency improvement and what would require an explicit transfer lemma.
- Action: define the anti-repackaging test with the three-way classification rule.
- Expected output: an endpoint-and-ledger calibration memo that constrains later decisions.
- Kill condition: if no stable endpoint window and burden ledger can be fixed, stop with an under-specified-ledger obstruction.

### Step 2 - Build a small comparison slate and derive exact plus localized equations

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: keep at most two active candidate families and one reserve, so the process is not hostage to first choice.
- Action: for each active candidate, derive the full exact evolution and the localized form actually relevant to the endpoint.
- Action: record immediately the pressure re-entry, cutoff commutators, vector reconstruction, and admissibility debt.
- Action: attach the nearest prior-art analogue and the exact claimed delta.
- Expected output: a candidate slate with exact equation sheets and localized debt ledgers.
- Kill condition: if every candidate is either a recycled prior-art package with no mathematical delta or only an identity-level rewrite, stop with a recycled-reformulation obstruction.

### Step 3 - Run the anti-repackaging and prior-art gate

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: classify each candidate as equivalent redistribution, ambiguous redistribution, or potentially live mechanism change.
- Action: reject candidates whose claimed novelty is absorbed by known reformulations at the mechanism level.
- Action: keep ambiguous candidates alive only if they name one exact place where the baseline burden might change in the frozen ledger.
- Expected output: a screened slate with only candidates that cleared both equivalence and overlap checks.
- Kill condition: if no candidate survives this gate, stop with an anti-repackaging or prior-art-absorption obstruction.

### Step 4 - Attach each survivor to one exact bottleneck and one minimum gain margin

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: name one exact bad term, closure gap, compactness obstruction, or contradiction input where the candidate must act.
- Action: specify one minimum meaningful gain margin in the frozen currency.
- Action: test whether the claimed gain survives legitimate inequalities, reconstruction, and localization rather than only pre-absolute-value narration.
- Action: if the candidate improves only a surrogate quantity, require an explicit transfer lemma from surrogate to theorem-facing burden.
- Expected output: a bottleneck sheet with typed gain and minimum margin.
- Kill condition: if no survivor can name one precise bottleneck and one non-cosmetic gain margin, stop with a non-operative-gain obstruction.

### Step 5 - Run a quantified gain-versus-debt insertion test

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: place the candidate into a mini proof skeleton containing local energy, pressure handling, cutoff debt, reconstruction debt, and solution-class admissibility.
- Action: budget gains and debts in the same ledger and mark any uncaptured cross-currency step as unresolved rather than silently favorable.
- Action: reject candidates whose local improvement disappears once the full endpoint budget is restored.
- Expected output: an endpoint-budget memo showing net gain, net loss, or unresolved transfer.
- Kill condition: if every candidate loses its net gain after endpoint budgeting, stop with an endpoint-transfer obstruction.

### Step 6 - Demand a hard artifact with evidence tiers

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: require one evidence packet per live candidate and classify it by tier:
  Tier A, prototype insertion or inequality in the live endpoint ledger;
  Tier B, toy model or reduced localization test with an explicit transfer map;
  Tier C, failed lemma that sharply identifies the obstruction.
- Action: allow Tier C to support only obstruction, not positive promotion.
- Action: allow positive promotion only from Tier A or Tier B with a credible transfer map.
- Expected output: a tiered artifact packet for each surviving candidate.
- Kill condition: if every candidate remains descriptive or only has weak analogical evidence, stop with a documentary-success obstruction.

### Step 7 - Use Tao comparison only as a secondary discriminator and close narrowly

- Depends on: Step 6.
- Action: ask whether the feature supporting the surviving artifact is erased by Tao-style averaging, but do not count that as positive evidence by itself.
- Action: compare candidates only if they share endpoint family, burden ledger, and evidence tier.
- Action: otherwise output buckets:
  live lead,
  hybrid handoff,
  bounded obstruction,
  or no reformulation advantage over the inherited ledger.
- Action: tie every final label to a concrete failed or successful term, estimate, or transfer step.
- Expected output: one final-comparison-ready packet or one sharply labeled obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the conclusion outruns the hard artifact and endpoint budget, narrow it until it does not.

## Probability Assessment

**Probability that this refined chain yields a presentable result: 0.77**

The likeliest presentable output is a disciplined obstruction memo showing that no audited reformulation beats the inherited endpoint ledger after full debt accounting. A positive route is possible, but only if one candidate survives exact derivation, prior-art screening, same-ledger gain tests, and a hard insertion artifact.
