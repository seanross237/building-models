# Chain 04 - Stochastic Circulation And Martingale Transport Route

## Central Premise

Viscosity does not destroy transport structure completely; it replaces deterministic Kelvin/Cauchy laws with stochastic transport identities. This chain asks whether one exact stochastic circulation or back-to-label object yields theorem-relevant control that Tao's averaged model would not preserve.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- one conditional route using a specific stochastic transport observable to feed a continuation criterion, scenario exclusion, or stretching-control statement; or
- a calibrated obstruction memo showing that the route dies first by expectation-only control,
  pathwise non-coercivity,
  solution-class mismatch,
  or localization collapse.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is stochastic and representation-based. It is not the deterministic material-stretching route, not the old tube-geometry reboot, and not a broad transport-topology bucket.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Fix one exact stochastic object and one admissible framework

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one primary object, such as:
  a Constantin-Iyer representation term,
  a stochastic circulation identity,
  a stochastic back-to-label observable,
  or another exact martingale transport statement tied to viscous NS.
- Action: choose the weakest admissible solution framework in which the object is still meaningful.
- Action: reject broad candidate menus and keep at most one backup object.
- Expected output: a setup memo naming the stochastic object, framework, and target endpoint family.
- Kill condition: if the object is already undefined or unusable at any realistic weak or suitable-solution level, stop and write a compatibility obstruction.

### Step 2 - State the exact Tao discriminator and theorem endpoint

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: specify exactly what the averaged model should fail to preserve:
  adapted stochastic transport,
  circulation martingale structure,
  or one exact representation identity beyond harmonic-analysis closure.
- Action: choose one endpoint where that structure would have to matter:
  a continuation criterion,
  a stretching-control step,
  or a blowup-scenario exclusion.
- Action: reject routes whose only claim is that the representation looks more physical.
- Expected output: a Tao-and-endpoint memo naming one exact destroyed feature and one insertion site.
- Kill condition: if the Tao distinction or the endpoint remains rhetorical, terminate the chain negatively.

### Step 3 - Test whether the stochastic structure yields real gain

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: determine whether the object yields pathwise control, conditional-expectation control, cancellation, or only a reformulation.
- Action: require one explicit analytic role:
  monotonicity,
  improved integrability,
  averaged depletion,
  or exclusion of one bad scenario.
- Action: reject expectation-level statements that do not feed the chosen endpoint.
- Expected output: a gain memo stating exactly what kind of control the stochastic object can and cannot provide.
- Kill condition: if the route never rises above representation-level exactness, stop and write a non-coercive-structure obstruction.

### Step 4 - Charge pathwise, localization, and reconstruction debt

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: restore pathwise variability, localization, pressure coupling, and weak-solution bookkeeping to see whether the claimed gain survives.
- Action: test whether any benefit exists only after averaging over noise in a way that is useless for the endpoint.
- Action: identify the first missing lemma precisely.
- Expected output: a reconstruction ledger showing whether the stochastic gain survives in the actual regularity task.
- Kill condition: if the gain disappears once pathwise or localized control is required, close the chain negatively.

### Step 5 - Close only on an earned stochastic route

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: if a survivor remains, write a proposition-scale target naming the stochastic object, the endpoint, the explicit gain, and the first missing lemma.
- Action: if not, state the narrowest honest obstruction:
  expectation-only control,
  pathwise non-coercivity,
  framework mismatch,
  or localization collapse.
- Expected output: either a stochastic-transport attack path or a sharply typed obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the final claim reduces to "stochastic formulations look special," downgrade it to obstruction.
