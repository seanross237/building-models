# Judgment on Selected Chain 01

## Overall verdict

The attacker is mostly right about the central weakness. The selected chain has a strong governing idea: compare exact Navier-Stokes structure against Tao's averaged model before spending serious budget on any one mechanism. But as written, it turns that good filter into a premature ranking machine. The main repair is to separate the comparison axes, tighten the meaning of "destroyed by averaging," and require a hard theorem-facing artifact before any final promotion.

The chain should be kept, but only as a calibrated triage program. It should not be trusted as a reliable attackability ranking unless it earns that status with an explicit insertion, prototype estimate, or failure sheet.

## Major critique assessments

### 1. The comparison contract is under-specified

**Verdict: Valid.**

This is the strongest critique. The original Step 1 never fixes which averaged equation is being used as comparator, which preserved structures matter, or what level of failure counts as "destroyed": exact identity loss, loss of coercivity, loss after localization, loss of transport propagation, or loss of canonical definition. Without that, the Tao filter can be made to favor whatever conclusion the memo already wants.

The attacker is also right that collapsing `Tao destruction + theorem-floor admissibility + endpoint contact` into one scoring currency is too coarse. Those are distinct tests and should remain distinct until there is a reason to compare candidates inside one shared class.

### 2. Step 2 is too anchored to the mission's existing shortlist

**Verdict: Partially valid.**

There is real selection-bias risk here. Limiting the register to current serious candidates is efficient, but it makes the branch a portfolio triage of known families rather than a neutral census of all exact-NS structure. That should be stated honestly.

Still, the critique overstates the problem. A bounded shortlist is appropriate for this mission, and the chain does not need to reopen the entire space. The fix is not to remove the cap. The fix is to type the candidates more carefully, mark overlap explicitly, and allow a narrow wildcard slot if something exact-NS-specific falls outside the current labels.

### 3. The Tao differential test is too coarse and too row-wise

**Verdict: Valid.**

The original `destroyed / unclear / preserved` table is not expressive enough. A property may survive formally but lose the localization, coercivity, or dynamic coupling that mattered. That difference is theorem-relevant and should not be hidden inside `unclear`.

The attacker is also right that some candidates are not single identities and cannot be judged fairly by the same witness requirement. The refined chain should demand a concrete witness, but allow different witness types for algebraic, geometric, and propagation-based candidates, and it should track overlap so the same structural fact is not counted three times under different names.

### 4. Step 4 creates false negatives by freezing architecture and endpoint too early

**Verdict: Partially valid.**

The critique is correct that the original chain is too rigid when it demands exactly one architecture and one endpoint before any prototype work exists. A live candidate may need a primary route and one secondary formulation before the correct insertion point becomes visible.

But some early forcing is still a strength. The chain is right to reject candidates that never touch a theorem-facing floor. The repair is to permit one primary architecture plus, at most, one secondary interpretation, with explicit bridge debt, rather than reopening a menu of hopes.

### 5. The chain ranks candidates before inserting hard evidence

**Verdict: Valid.**

This is the central structural criticism, and it should be adopted without dilution. The original chain goes from descriptive filtering to ranking without an evidence gate that asks for one prototype estimate, localized identity, insertion into a bottleneck, or explicit failure sheet on the claimed floor.

Without that gate, the final ranking is too vulnerable to polished rhetoric. This is the key refinement the chain needs.

### 6. The final atlas and obstruction notes risk becoming prose closures

**Verdict: Valid.**

The attacker is right that several kill conditions are articulation tests rather than mathematical tests, and that one-page obstruction notes can be misread as deeper closures than they really are.

The fix is to separate outcomes cleanly:

- earned mathematical negatives;
- current-budget or architecture-specific negatives;
- unresolved non-promotions.

The final atlas should package evidence already earned, not substitute for it.

### 7. The whole chain conflates Tao distinctiveness with theorem relevance

**Verdict: Valid.**

This is a real structural issue. Being destroyed by Tao averaging is necessary for this mission's filter, but it is not remotely sufficient for usefulness in a regularity attack. The refined chain should therefore use Tao sensitivity only as an admission screen. Promotion requires separate theorem-floor compatibility and bottleneck contact.

### 8. The chain is biased toward obstruction as the respectable output

**Verdict: Partially valid.**

There is some asymmetry here. Negative outputs are more clearly specified than positive ones, so the branch is safer at saying no than at promoting a survivor. That is a real bias risk.

Still, that conservatism is not itself a flaw for this mission. The problem is not that obstruction is allowed; the problem is that positive promotion was underspecified. Once the evidence gate and comparability rules are added, the asymmetry becomes acceptable.

## What the refined chain must change

1. Define the comparison contract at one fixed level and distinguish identity loss, coercivity loss, localization loss, and propagation loss.
2. Replace the one-number scoring currency with separate ledgers for Tao distinctiveness, theorem-floor admissibility, endpoint contact, and bridge debt.
3. Keep the candidate set bounded, but use typed candidate cards, overlap notes, and one optional wildcard slot.
4. Replace the coarse Tao table with richer statuses such as `destroyed`, `formally preserved but decoupled`, `preserved but non-coercive`, `localization-sensitive`, and `preserved`.
5. Allow one primary architecture and one secondary interpretation when needed, but force both onto the same endpoint ledger.
6. Insert a mandatory hard-artifact gate before ranking: prototype estimate, explicit insertion, or failure sheet.
7. Rank only genuinely comparable survivors; otherwise report buckets such as `live lead`, `reserve/hybrid handoff`, `bounded obstruction`, or `unresolved non-promotion`.
8. State obstruction notes as budget- or architecture-bounded unless a stronger closure was actually earned.

## Probability assessment

**Probability that the refined chain yields a presentable result: 0.78**

The most likely presentable result is a disciplined obstruction or reserve/handoff memo rather than a fully convincing positive survivor. That is still a good outcome for this branch. With the evidence gate added, the branch should either isolate one genuinely theorem-facing exact-NS lead or close several attractive-but-soft candidates in a way that is sharper than the original chain could support.
