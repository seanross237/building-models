# Chain 03 - Deterministic Stretching Observable With Suitable-Weak Admission Gate

## Central Premise

The stochastic transport route failed on admissibility, not because every transport-side structure is dead. This chain is live only if one deterministic or coarse-grained stretching observable can be defined on the inherited theorem floor, or on one explicitly bridged proxy class, and then yield one-sided leverage on a frozen host bad term such as cumulative stretching or local concentration feed.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This is the only transport-first branch. It does not rely on geometric package changes, harmonic pressure structure, or compactness architecture. Its core bet is that exact NS deformation or stretching clocks may survive Tao filtering in a way the stochastic packaging obscured.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze one observable, one admissible framework, and one endpoint

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one observable only: cumulative positive strain along coarse-grained trajectories, mollified deformation growth, or another equally explicit deterministic stretching clock.
- Action: state exactly where it lives: suitable-weak / LEI if possible, otherwise one stronger proxy class with a named bridge burden.
- Action: freeze one endpoint only: local concentration exclusion by default, or a continuation criterion if the observable already behaves like norm control.
- Expected output: a scope-lock memo naming the observable, framework, bridge burden, and endpoint.
- Kill condition: if the observable still depends on smooth-flow debt or an unnamed proxy bridge, stop with a solution-class obstruction.

### Step 2 - Derive the exact or coarse-grained evolution sheet

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: write the evolution identity for the observable in the chosen framework.
- Action: separate deterministic transport content from viscous defect, commutator debt, temporal-window debt, and nonlocal strain freedom.
- Action: mark which pieces are exact and which already assume extra regularity.
- Expected output: an evolution sheet plus a defect ledger.
- Kill condition: if the identity is exact only for classical trajectories or if the defects already dominate the observable, stop with a definability obstruction.

### Step 3 - Freeze one host bad term and test coercivity before localization

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: choose one theorem-facing host bad term only, such as positive stretching production or one fixed local-concentration feed line.
- Action: test whether the observable gives one-sided control, an exclusion rule, or a monotonicity surrogate on that exact term.
- Action: compare the proposed gain to one baseline burden in the same currency.
- Expected output: a coercivity memo with one promoted gain or one clean failure.
- Kill condition: if the observable is exact but analytically non-coercive on the host bad term, stop with a transport-without-leverage obstruction.

### Step 4 - Insert the gain into one theorem-facing line and pay all debt

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: place the gain into one inherited LEI / CKN concentration-exclusion line, or into a continuation reserve only if Step 1 justified that endpoint.
- Action: charge cutoff loss, pressure debt, nonlocal strain freedom, and proxy-transfer debt before any promotion.
- Action: reject trajectory-level surrogates that never cash out on the theorem ledger.
- Expected output: a localization-and-insertion memo.
- Kill condition: if localization or transfer erases the gain, stop with an endpoint-transfer obstruction.

### Step 5 - Demand one hard artifact and only then ask the Tao question

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: produce one hard artifact: prototype inequality, reduced deterministic scenario, or a failed lemma isolating the precise obstruction.
- Action: ask whether the surviving feature is plausibly destroyed by Tao averaging only after the artifact exists.
- Expected output: an evidence packet plus a Tao-comparator note.
- Kill condition: if the branch still relies on transport language with no artifact, stop with a documentary obstruction.

### Step 6 - State the narrowest earned claim

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: if positive, write one conditional route naming the observable, admissible framework, endpoint, insertion point, and first missing lemma.
- Action: if negative, classify failure as solution-class mismatch, viscous-defect collapse, non-coercivity, nonlocal-strain freedom, or endpoint-transfer failure.
- Expected output: a comparison-ready route or obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the missing lemma is just full regularity renamed in transport language, downgrade immediately to obstruction.
