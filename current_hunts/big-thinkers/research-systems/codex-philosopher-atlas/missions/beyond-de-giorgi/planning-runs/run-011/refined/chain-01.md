# Refined Chain 01 - Calibrated Tao Differential Audit With Mandatory Evidence Gate

## Central Premise

Keep the comparison-first instinct, but narrow the claim. This branch is not allowed to rank candidate mechanisms just because they sound exact-NS-specific. Its job is to determine whether any exact Navier-Stokes structure is both genuinely damaged by Tao-style averaging and operational on a named theorem-facing bottleneck.

If no candidate clears that bar, the correct output is a bounded obstruction or reserve/handoff memo, not a polished ranking.

## Verifiable Result Target

One of the following:

- one or more live candidates, each with a precise Tao differential, a fixed theorem floor, one named bottleneck, and one hard artifact;
- a bounded obstruction atlas showing why the audited candidate families fail;
- a reserve or hybrid-handoff memo showing that a candidate is structurally interesting but not yet operational on the chosen floor.

## Why This Refined Chain Is Better

The original chain had the right filter and the right skepticism about generic harmonic analysis. The refined version keeps that discipline but removes the false precision. It separates distinct tests, types the candidates, and forbids final promotion before a concrete theorem-facing artifact exists.

## Scope Lock

Before the chain is active, it must freeze all of the following:

- one exact comparison target:
  the specific 3D Navier-Stokes formulation and the specific Tao averaged equation being compared;
- one comparison level:
  identity-level,
  coercivity-level,
  localization-level,
  propagation-level,
  or blowup-scenario-level;
- one intended theorem floor:
  suitable-weak / LEI by default, or one named proxy class with explicit bridge debt;
- one endpoint family:
  criterion insertion,
  stretching control,
  harmonic-tail control,
  helical or packet interaction control,
  or one fully specified named alternative;
- four separate ledgers:
  Tao distinctiveness,
  theorem-floor admissibility,
  endpoint contact,
  and bridge debt;
- one passing and one failing example for the Tao-differential rule.

If these cannot be frozen concretely, stop with an under-specified comparison memo.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Calibrate the Tao differential contract

- Depends on: none.
- Action: state exactly what counts as "destroyed by averaging" at the chosen comparison level.
- Action: distinguish at least the following failure modes:
  exact identity lost,
  formally preserved but no longer coercive,
  preserved globally but broken by localization,
  preserved statically but not dynamically propagated,
  or preserved.
- Action: write one candidate that clearly passes the Tao filter and one that clearly fails.
- Expected output: a calibrated comparison memo with non-rhetorical pass/fail examples.
- Kill condition: if the memo cannot distinguish a clear pass from a clear fail, stop with an under-specified Tao-filter obstruction.

### Step 2 - Build typed candidate cards from mission context, with one wildcard slot

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: build a bounded register of serious candidates already on the mission board:
  strain-vorticity alignment,
  local Beltrami deficit,
  tube geometry,
  deterministic deformation/stretching,
  far-field pressure harmonicity,
  exact helical interaction geometry,
  and closely related exact-NS candidates.
- Action: allow at most one wildcard candidate that is not just a relabeling of an existing family.
- Action: for each candidate card, record:
  exact object or statement,
  comparison level,
  nearest prior-art family,
  prior-run downgrade status,
  overlap with other cards,
  and why it is not merely generic harmonic analysis.
- Action: reject cards that are only slogans, only generic machinery, or duplicates in different language.
- Expected output: a typed candidate ledger with overlap accounting.
- Kill condition: if no candidate survives as a distinct exact-NS feature, stop with a no-live-candidate obstruction.

### Step 3 - Run the Tao differential test candidate by candidate

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: for each card, identify the concrete witness of distinctiveness appropriate to its type:
  lost sign or identity,
  lost triad geometry,
  lost localization law,
  lost propagation mechanism,
  or lost strain-geometry coupling.
- Action: classify each card using a richer table:
  `destroyed`,
  `formally preserved but decoupled`,
  `preserved but non-coercive`,
  `localization-sensitive`,
  `unclear`,
  or `preserved`.
- Action: merge or demote cards that are just the same structural fact in different costumes.
- Expected output: a Tao-differential table that tracks both distinctiveness and overlap honestly.
- Kill condition: if every surviving candidate is either preserved or only vaguely `unclear`, stop with a Tao-filter failure memo.

### Step 4 - Attach theorem-facing architectures without freezing too early

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: for each candidate that survives the Tao test, assign one primary proof architecture and, if necessary, one secondary interpretation only.
- Action: force both interpretations onto the same theorem-floor and endpoint ledger.
- Action: state one fixed endpoint per candidate and the minimum non-cosmetic gain that would matter there.
- Action: write explicit bridge debt if the candidate lives off the theorem floor.
- Expected output: a candidate-to-architecture map with endpoint ledger and bridge debt notes.
- Kill condition: if a candidate has no theorem-facing endpoint, or the bridge debt is already theorem-scale, demote it immediately.

### Step 5 - Run early admissibility and localization gates

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: test whether each surviving candidate is definable, stable, and meaningful in the chosen solution class and localization regime.
- Action: record whether the candidate is:
  directly admissible,
  admissible only after architecture change,
  or dead on contact.
- Action: note when a candidate survives formally but loses the specific localization or propagation property needed by the endpoint.
- Expected output: an admissibility memo that eliminates candidates before narrative momentum accumulates.
- Kill condition: if every candidate is dead on contact or only survives after a theorem-scale architecture switch, stop with an admissibility obstruction.

### Step 6 - Force a hard artifact before any ranking

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: for each still-live candidate, produce one hard artifact:
  a prototype estimate,
  a representative insertion into the named bottleneck,
  a localized identity with all error terms exposed,
  or a failure sheet showing exactly where the hoped-for gain is spent.
- Action: require all gains and defects to appear in the same ledger as the claimed endpoint gain.
- Action: reject candidates that remain well-described stories but produce no artifact.
- Expected output: an evidence packet for each live or failed route.
- Kill condition: if every candidate remains documentary after this step, stop with a documentary-success obstruction.

### Step 7 - Rank only where comparison is earned

- Depends on: Step 6.
- Action: rank candidates only if they share the same primary architecture, theorem floor, and gain currency.
- Action: otherwise classify outcomes into buckets:
  live lead,
  reserve or hybrid handoff,
  bounded obstruction,
  or unresolved non-promotion.
- Action: nominate at most two primary leads only if the evidence actually supports that level of discrimination.
- Expected output: either a narrow ranked shortlist inside one comparable class or a bucketed result.
- Kill condition: if the branch needs apples-to-oranges comparison to produce a clean ranking, replace ranking with buckets.

### Step 8 - Produce the canonical atlas with evidence grades

- Depends on: Step 7.
- Action: write the final atlas naming each audited candidate, its Tao status, theorem-floor status, bottleneck contact, hard artifact, and current disposition.
- Action: state explicitly whether each negative is:
  a hard mathematical death,
  a current-budget closure,
  an architecture-specific closure,
  or a reserve-only route.
- Action: record whether exact helical structure remains demoted, returns as reserve, or re-enters the live set, but only as a consequence of the evidence packet.
- Expected output: a reusable property atlas with evidence grades instead of prose-only closures.
- Kill condition: if the atlas says more than the evidence packet earned, narrow the claims until the mismatch disappears.

## Probability Assessment

**Probability that this refined chain yields a presentable result: 0.75**

The most likely presentable output is a sharper obstruction atlas or reserve/handoff memo. A genuine live lead is possible, but only if at least one candidate survives the artifact gate with real theorem-facing contact.
