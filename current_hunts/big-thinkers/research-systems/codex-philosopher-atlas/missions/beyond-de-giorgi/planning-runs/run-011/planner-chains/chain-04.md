# Chain 04 - Harmonic Far-Field Tail Route Via Local Harmonic Norms

## Central Premise

The old pressure route died on the global `H^1` ledger, but one untested lead remains: after splitting pressure into near and far pieces, the far-field component is harmonic on the local cylinder. This chain is live only if one genuinely local harmonic observable is strictly cheaper than the old global pairing currency and can enter a theorem-facing line with all split-scale, cutoff, and recombination debt charged.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This is the only branch centered on exact local harmonicity of a nonlocal NS quantity. It is not a generic "better pressure estimate" search, not a geometry route, not a transport observable route, and not a compactness architecture. Its distinctive bet is that harmonic continuation of the far-field tail may create a local control mechanism that Tao-compatible harmonic-analysis packaging misses.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze one decomposition, one theorem endpoint, and one admissibility floor

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose the exact near/far pressure split and one theorem-facing endpoint only: local concentration exclusion, criterion input, or one fixed LEI insertion line.
- Action: state the admissibility floor and what pressure objects are actually defined there.
- Action: write the old failed global pairing as the comparator ledger.
- Expected output: a split-and-endpoint memo with one comparator.
- Kill condition: if the branch cannot say how the local harmonic observable will be compared against the old global ledger, stop with a comparator obstruction.

### Step 2 - Choose one local harmonic observable and one gain currency

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: choose one observable only: local `H^1` norm on `Q_k`, harmonic oscillation / Campanato quantity, non-tangential maximal control, or another fully specified harmonic norm.
- Action: freeze one gain currency only: pairing coefficient, localized oscillation decay, or criterion input.
- Action: reject observables that are merely renamed versions of the old global `H^1` bound.
- Expected output: an observable-and-currency memo.
- Kill condition: if the observable is not visibly local or not clearly cheaper than the old global quantity, stop with a fake-locality obstruction.

### Step 3 - Derive the exact harmonic control sheet

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: use harmonicity on the local cylinder to derive the relevant local control mechanism: Harnack decay, mean-value propagation, Caccioppoli-type local gradient control, Poisson-kernel representation, or another exact harmonic tool.
- Action: separate exact harmonic gains from the debt created by the scale split and by the near-field source term.
- Action: state where exponential or geometric decay is real and where it is only packaging.
- Expected output: a harmonic control sheet with exact-versus-debt separation.
- Kill condition: if harmonicity only improves a package-internal quantity and not the frozen gain currency, stop with a harmonic-packaging obstruction.

### Step 4 - Insert the gain into one theorem-facing line and charge recombination debt

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: place the local harmonic gain into the frozen LEI / criterion line.
- Action: charge split-scale choice, cutoff loss, near-field compensation, and recombination of near plus far pressure before claiming progress.
- Action: compare the net effect directly against the old failed global coefficient.
- Expected output: an insertion-and-recombination memo.
- Kill condition: if the net coefficient or endpoint ledger is unchanged after recombination, stop with a no-net-gain obstruction.

### Step 5 - Run the Tao filter on the exact harmonic mechanism, not on pressure rhetoric

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: ask whether the precise harmonic-tail feature used here depends on the exact NS pressure law in a way Tao averaging destroys, or whether the averaged system preserves enough of the same mechanism to neutralize the route.
- Action: distinguish `exact-NS harmonic continuation` from generic elliptic regularity language.
- Expected output: a Tao-sensitivity note tied to the actual observable.
- Kill condition: if the branch turns out to rely only on Tao-compatible elliptic regularity, downgrade it immediately to obstruction.

### Step 6 - Produce one hard artifact and close narrowly

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: produce one hard artifact: prototype local inequality, explicit split-scale ledger, or a failed lemma isolating why local harmonicity does not beat the old global pairing.
- Action: if positive, write the narrowest live claim naming the decomposition, local harmonic observable, gain currency, endpoint insertion, and first missing lemma.
- Action: if negative, classify failure as fake locality, recombination collapse, Tao-compatible elliptic structure, or real-but-too-weak gain.
- Expected output: a comparison-ready route or obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if no hard artifact exists, stop with a documentary obstruction.
