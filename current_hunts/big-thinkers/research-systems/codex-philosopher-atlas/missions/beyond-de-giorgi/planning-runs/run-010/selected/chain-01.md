# Chain 02 - Adapted-Package Geometric Vorticity Route From Local Beltrami Deficit To Criterion Scale

## Central Premise

Geometry remains the most mission-facing candidate mechanism, but the old neutral-Eulerian package is already weak. This chain is live only if one openly declared package change turns local Beltrami deficit, direction coherence, or tube persistence into a theorem-facing bridge to a Constantin-Fefferman-type criterion or to full-stretching reduction, without hiding the gain inside the localization itself.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This is the only geometry-first branch in the portfolio. It differs from Chain 01 by centering geometric observables rather than deterministic transport clocks, from Chain 03 by avoiding packet-symbol language, and from Chain 04 by searching for a mechanism rather than consuming one.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Declare one package change, one comparator, and one claimed advantage

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one adapted package only: tube-following localization, high-vorticity tracking, or another equally explicit geometry-aware regime.
- Action: name the killed neutral-Eulerian package as the mandatory comparator.
- Action: state exactly what the new package is supposed to buy: persistence, criterion compatibility, or control of the full stretching mechanism.
- Expected output: a package-change memo with one comparator and one claimed advantage.
- Kill condition: if the package change cannot say what it buys beyond hidden normalization or relabeling, stop with a package-theater obstruction.

### Step 2 - Freeze one geometric observable family, one scenario class, and one endpoint

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: choose one promoted observable family only: local Beltrami deficit, direction coherence, Beltrami-plus-concentration, or another comparably sharp family.
- Action: choose one scenario class in advance, such as vortex-tube concentration or sheet-to-filament transition.
- Action: choose one endpoint family only: a Constantin-Fefferman-type criterion by default, or one fixed full-stretching target if criterion scale is not the right first cash-out.
- Expected output: an observable-and-endpoint table.
- Kill condition: if the branch needs several loosely connected observables or scenario classes to sound plausible, stop with an under-specified geometry obstruction.

### Step 3 - Run the persistence and admissibility screen under the adapted package

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: ask what actually persists under the chosen package and what derivative, tracking, or threshold debt is introduced.
- Action: charge hidden `nabla xi` debt, boundary leakage, tube-identification ambiguity, and any relocalization cost immediately.
- Action: reject observables that only look strong after the geometry is retrofitted to the observed structure.
- Expected output: a persistence ledger with at most one promoted survivor.
- Kill condition: if persistence requires hidden axis fitting, repeated retuning, or stronger derivative control than the route admits, stop with a persistence obstruction.

### Step 4 - Test the promoted observable on the full stretching mechanism

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: freeze one stretching representation and test whether the observable acts on the full mechanism rather than only on local Lamb-vector depletion or another descriptive fragment.
- Action: distinguish near-field gain from exterior or nonlocal remainder and from localization-evolution debt.
- Action: require a declared burden share of the full stretching term, not just a favorable subregion.
- Expected output: a full-stretching audit memo.
- Kill condition: if the observable controls only a local depletion effect while the decisive remainder remains free, stop with a full-stretching obstruction.

### Step 5 - Bridge to criterion scale or contradiction scale with a hard artifact

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: write one explicit criterion-scale proposition or contradiction line using the promoted observable and the chosen package.
- Action: benchmark it against the neutral-Eulerian comparator and the closest Constantin-Fefferman-type prior art.
- Action: require one hard artifact: prototype estimate, toy geometry scenario, or failed lemma sharply isolating the obstruction.
- Expected output: a theorem-facing bridge memo plus an evidence packet.
- Kill condition: if the gain never rises above descriptive geometry or prior-art direction regularity, stop with a prior-art-collapse obstruction.

### Step 6 - Use Tao sensitivity as secondary evidence and state the narrowest earned claim

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: ask whether the feature supporting the artifact is plausibly erased by Tao-style averaging, but only after criterion-scale relevance is established.
- Action: if positive, write one conditional route naming the package, observable, scenario class, endpoint, and first narrower missing lemma.
- Action: if negative, classify failure as package theater, persistence collapse, hidden derivative debt, full-stretching failure, or prior-art collapse.
- Expected output: a final-comparison-ready geometry route or obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the final claim depends on silently preserving the adapted package at every step without paying that cost, downgrade to obstruction.
