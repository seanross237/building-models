# Chain 02 - Obstruction-First Helical-Triad Route With Explicit Inequality Test

## Central Premise

Exact helical-triad geometry is worth pursuing only against one fixed theorem-level bottleneck and under a presumption of obstruction. The branch is live only if one exact Leray-symbol feature can be written as an explicit gain in one named estimate or contradiction step and still survive dyadic summation, mixed-helicity restoration, and any required localization.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a proposition-scale candidate inequality naming one interaction class, one theorem-level bottleneck term, one gain currency, the full reconstruction bill, and the first missing lemma; or
- a sharp obstruction memo stating exactly where helical-symbol structure dies: endpoint mismatch, effect-size insufficiency, shell or angular summation collapse, mixed-helicity collapse, localization collapse, or regime-only irrelevance.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain stays at the exact Fourier-symbol level, but it no longer treats elegant triad structure as evidence by itself. It is designed mainly to determine whether any Tao-sensitive helical feature produces theorem-grade leverage after honest reconstruction of the full Navier-Stokes interaction.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Fix the theorem endpoint, framework, representation, and gain currency now

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one exact theorem-level target before any symbolic exploration: for example one flux estimate, one stretching bound, one enstrophy-production inequality, or one contradiction point in a fixed solution framework.
- Action: choose one representation only for execution purposes: helical decomposition with explicit Leray-projected triad coefficients, or an exactly equivalent symbol form, but do not permit later switching of bottleneck or proof architecture.
- Action: define one admissible gain currency inside the chosen estimate: sign, size reduction, summability improvement, derivative saving, or another metric that is commensurable with that estimate.
- Action: write the target in near-inequality form, including the term where a helical-symbol feature would have to enter.
- Expected output: a setup sheet naming the theorem endpoint, framework, explicit target term, representation, and gain currency.
- Kill condition: if the branch cannot write one fixed target inequality or contradiction point at the outset, stop and record an endpoint-selection obstruction.

### Step 2 - Build an operational symbol ledger and bound the maximal plausible triad effect

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: decompose the exact Navier-Stokes nonlinearity into the chosen helical-triad variables and record which sign, helicity, resonance, and Leray-projector features are exact.
- Action: define the comparison class to Tao-style averaging operationally relative to the chosen target: what averaging is being compared, at what frequency resolution, and with what notion of preserved control.
- Action: for each candidate feature, estimate the largest plausible contribution it could make to the chosen target term before full shell summation and localization.
- Action: reject features that are exact but merely classificatory, or that change notation without changing the sign, size, or summability of the target term.
- Expected output: a triad ledger with columns for exact structure, averaged-model sensitivity, target-term relevance, and maximal plausible pre-reconstruction effect size.
- Kill condition: if no candidate feature changes the chosen target term in a quantitatively meaningful way even before reconstruction, terminate with a symbol-level obstruction.

### Step 3 - Promote one mechanism and write the candidate inequality explicitly

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: promote at most one mechanism, allowing it to be either one exact feature or one explicitly quantified aggregate bias, but not a loose collection of suggestive effects.
- Action: write the exact candidate inequality, corrected transfer bound, or contradiction line where the mechanism enters, and state what improvement is claimed over the baseline estimate.
- Action: if the mechanism uses a restricted interaction class, quantify what proportion of the relevant transfer, shell sum, or interaction measure that class captures.
- Action: identify the nearest prior-art comparison so the route does not relabel standard same-helicity or near-resonant folklore as a new mechanism.
- Expected output: one theorem-gap note containing the explicit inequality, the promoted mechanism, the relevant interaction class, and the claimed gain.
- Kill condition: if the mechanism cannot be written as an explicit candidate inequality, or if it only lives on an unquantified thin triad set, stop and record a relevance obstruction.

### Step 4 - Charge the full reconstruction bill

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: restore full dyadic shell summation, angular multiplicity, and all mixed-helicity interactions, and calculate whether the Step 3 gain survives with positive margin.
- Action: if the theorem endpoint needs localization, add the corresponding cutoff, commutator, local-energy, or profile-decomposition losses and test whether the mechanism is stable under that reconstruction.
- Action: perturb away from exactly favorable helicity or resonance classes and determine whether the gain reflects true structure or only a narrow regime artifact.
- Action: classify failure precisely as effect-size insufficiency, shell-summation collapse, angular-multiplicity collapse, mixed-helicity collapse, localization collapse, or regime-only signal.
- Expected output: a reconstruction ledger showing net gain or precise failure mode after honest restoration of the full equation.
- Kill condition: if the gain is annihilated or becomes negligible after reconstruction, terminate with an obstruction memo rather than promoting a positive route.

### Step 5 - State the narrowest earned claim in proposition or obstruction form

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: if one mechanism survives, state a proposition-like claim naming the exact interaction class, theorem endpoint, explicit gain, known inputs, and first missing lemma.
- Action: if nothing survives, state the sharpest negative result earned: exactly what the helical-symbol structure controls at single-triad level and exactly where that control is lost in the full equation.
- Action: keep the final claim tied to the single fixed theorem endpoint and the single promoted mechanism. Remove all unevaluated alternatives.
- Expected output: a final-comparison-ready proposition blueprint or a precise obstruction statement.
- Kill condition: if the final claim cannot be written without vague references to favorable triads, special cancellations, or heuristic transfer preferences, the chain has not earned promotion.
