# Chain 03 - Critical-Element Audit with Explicit Tao Screen and Solution-Class Discipline

## Central Premise

If local mechanism hunts are exhausted, a compactness-and-rigidity route should
remain alive only if it can first support a non-circular Navier-Stokes minimal-
element setup in a specific critical framework. The refined chain therefore
decouples threshold formulation from rigidity and treats the Tao barrier as an
operational test, not a slogan.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a narrowly scoped critical-element program in one named phase space and one
  named solution class, with one rigidity route whose missing input is
  materially smaller than a disguised global-regularity theorem; or
- a calibrated obstruction map showing exactly where the route fails:
  threshold definition, compactness modulo symmetries, solution-class
  incompatibility, missing coercive backbone, or absence of a limit-stable
  NS-specific rigidity mechanism.

## Why This Chain Is Still Worth Keeping

This is not a novelty-driven branch. It is worth keeping because it directly
audits the global compactness-and-rigidity route and can still produce a useful
negative result if the route collapses. Its value is disciplined calibration.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Fix candidate spaces by threshold feasibility, not premature rigidity

- Depends on: none.
- Action: compare `L^3`, `\dot H^{1/2}`, and at most one weaker candidate such
  as `BMO^{-1}` only if an explicit replacement compactness package is stated.
- Action: for each candidate, specify the minimized quantity, solution class,
  continuation criterion, perturbative input, symmetry group, and the topology
  in which compactness modulo symmetries would even be formulated.
- Action: keep rigidity prospects separate at this stage; do not require a
  plausible rigidity lever yet.
- Expected output: a candidate-space memo ranking spaces by threshold-
  formulation feasibility, with a separate note on downstream rigidity
  prospects.
- Kill condition: terminate only if no candidate space supports a clean
  threshold setup with plausible compactness modulo its relevant symmetries.

### Step 2 - Build the minimal-element package with explicit theorem-hypothesis compatibility

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: in the top-ranked space, define the threshold functional, maximal-
  lifespan solution class, symmetry normalization, pressure treatment, and the
  exact meaning of "almost periodic modulo symmetries" or its replacement.
- Action: distinguish clearly between formal setup, known literature, and the
  actual missing compactness or stability theorem.
- Action: check compatibility across theorem hypotheses so that mild-solution
  inputs, weak-solution tools, and candidate rigidity regimes are not silently
  mixed.
- Expected output: a threshold dossier listing what is genuinely available,
  what is missing, and which missing statement is first-order.
- Kill condition: if the required compactness or stability input is effectively
  as hard as solving regularity in that framework, stop and record a
  foundational obstruction.

### Step 3 - Audit the coercive backbone and supercritical gap before testing rigidity

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: identify what scale-invariant coercive quantity, monotonicity
  principle, sign structure, or substitute control would keep a threshold
  element from being only a formal object.
- Action: test whether the chosen framework has anything stronger than generic
  energy control plus harmonic analysis.
- Action: record whether the supercritical gap shows up as lack of coercivity,
  lack of limit-stable decay, or inability to propagate normalization data.
- Expected output: a coercive-backbone memo naming the first indispensable
  missing control and why it is structurally necessary.
- Kill condition: if no credible backbone exists even for a hypothetical
  threshold element, conclude that the branch is obstruction-only and stop.

### Step 4 - Run an operational Tao screen on representative rigidity routes

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: test at least two representative rigidity routes, chosen for
  different mechanisms rather than from a vague menu. One may be analytic, such
  as backward uniqueness or ancient-solution exclusion. One may be geometric,
  self-similar, or Liouville-type.
- Action: for each route, state:
  - the exact NS-specific ingredient claimed to do work;
  - why Tao-style averaged models would not obviously preserve it;
  - whether the ingredient is stable under the compactness limit and solution
    class from Steps 1 and 2;
  - and what extra regularity, decay, or topology the route actually needs.
- Expected output: a rigidity audit table with route, solution class,
  NS-specific ingredient, Tao discriminator, limit stability, and first missing
  theorem.
- Kill condition: if every route either relies on Tao-preserved structure,
  needs stronger assumptions than the threshold object provides, or fails to
  survive the chosen limit process, stop with a route-specific obstruction
  memo.

### Step 5 - Promote only a non-circular survivor into a contradiction blueprint

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: only if one route survives the Step 4 audit, write the contradiction
  program in one fixed phase space and one fixed solution class.
- Action: benchmark the bottleneck theorem against prior Navier-Stokes and
  adjacent critical-PDE literature to avoid relabeling a familiar dead end as a
  new route.
- Action: test explicitly whether the bottleneck is materially narrower than
  the original regularity problem.
- Expected output: a contradiction blueprint with one threshold object, one
  rigidity route, one bottleneck theorem, and one explicit next execution
  target.
- Kill condition: if the bottleneck is equivalent in difficulty to ruling out
  critical blowup outright, downgrade the result back to obstruction.

### Step 6 - Close with a calibrated claim tied to audited coverage

- Depends on: Step 5, or directly on Step 4 or Step 3 if the chain dies
  earlier.
- Action: make the final claim no broader than the spaces, solution classes,
  and rigidity routes actually tested.
- Action: include an explicit prior-art comparison so the output distinguishes a
  new obstruction diagnosis from a rediscovered known failure mode.
- Expected output: either a limited positive program in one framework, or an
  obstruction map specifying whether failure came from threshold setup,
  solution-class mismatch, missing coercive structure, or lack of a limit-
  stable NS-specific rigidity mechanism.
- Kill condition: if the conclusion generalizes beyond the audited coverage
  without a real meta-argument, narrow it.

## Probability Assessment

- Probability this refined chain yields a presentable result: **0.76**
- Most likely presentable result: a calibrated obstruction map.
- Less likely presentable result: a genuinely actionable contradiction program.
