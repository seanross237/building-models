# Chain 02 - Calibrated Geometry Route

## Central Premise

NS-specific vortex geometry is still worth testing, but only under a protocol that prevents premature defeat. The right question is not whether a vague geometric feature "looks helpful." It is whether a sharply defined local or hybrid geometric observable, attached to a fixed localization protocol and scenario class, can control the full stretching mechanism `S omega . omega` in a way that is both scale-credible and dynamically plausible under Navier-Stokes.

## Verifiable Result Target

Produce one of the following:

- a new implication from a sharply defined geometric or hybrid observable to a credible regularity mechanism, together with a plausible persistence route;
- a calibrated obstruction result showing that a specific observable or hybrid observable fails to control the full stretching mechanism even after fixing localization and representation;
- or a benchmarked hybrid-observable shortlist that is strong enough to compare against other chains.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain remains the geometry-first route, but it is no longer allowed to win by vague intuition or lose by decomposition artifact. It is organized around:

- prior-art calibration before novelty claims,
- joint definition of observables and localization,
- early dynamic screening,
- and kernel-level analysis of the full stretching term.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Calibrate against prior art and the Tao filter

- Depends on: none.
- Action: benchmark the route against the main geometric regularity mechanisms already in play, including Constantin-Fefferman-Majda style direction coherence, later vorticity-direction coherence work, and near-Beltrami or geometric depletion ideas.
- Action: split candidate NS-specific ingredients into separate buckets:
  - transport or propagation structure,
  - exact algebraic structure,
  - multiscale coherence or concentration structure.
- Action: for each bucket, state what an averaged-model analogue would preserve or destroy.
- Expected output: a benchmark memo saying which ingredients are already covered by prior art, which are genuinely new, and what would count as an NS-specific gain rather than generic singular-integral geometry.
- Kill condition: terminate only if every candidate ingredient collapses to generic static Calderon-Zygmund structure with no additional NS-specific dynamic content after benchmarking.

### Step 2 - Fix the localization protocol and scenario class before defining observables

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: choose one or two concrete singular-scenario classes to test, for example filament concentration or sheet/pancake concentration.
- Action: fix the localization protocol:
  - intense-vorticity threshold,
  - spatial scale,
  - time window,
  - and whether localization is Eulerian, Lagrangian, or tube-adapted.
- Action: define observables only after that protocol is fixed. Allow both single observables and hybrid observables, such as:
  - local Beltrami deficit,
  - vorticity-direction regularity,
  - tube-family coherence,
  - Beltrami deficit plus concentration,
  - Beltrami deficit plus anisotropy,
  - direction coherence plus tube persistence.
- Expected output: a table listing each observable or hybrid observable, its localization data, its scaling, the scenario class it is meant to address, and its first plausible route to leverage.
- Kill condition: terminate a specific observable only if its formulation is unstable under reasonable localization choices or if its apparent strength comes entirely from hidden normalization choices.

### Step 3 - Run an early dynamic plausibility screen

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: for each shortlisted observable, ask immediately what quantity is transported, approximately propagated, or lost under localization.
- Action: record the best available transport identity, commutator structure, diffusion loss, and localization-evolution cost.
- Action: rank observables by dynamic plausibility before investing in full stretching analysis.
- Expected output: a shortlist with three labels:
  - dynamically plausible,
  - informative but dynamically weak,
  - static-only and not worth further investment.
- Kill condition: terminate a route only if it has no credible propagation mechanism even at a heuristic level and no reason to think the chosen scenario class preserves it.

### Step 4 - Fix one primary kernel-level representation of stretching

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: choose an explicit Biot-Savart-level representation for `S omega . omega` and one primary localization protocol for decomposing it, for example near/far relative to the chosen intense region.
- Action: define every remainder term inside that fixed representation.
- Action: where possible, cross-check the conclusion against one secondary reasonable decomposition to detect decomposition artifacts.
- Expected output: a representation memo with the exact formula, the localization split, and a glossary of terms that will be treated as controlled, partially controlled, or uncontrolled.
- Kill condition: do not declare failure from a surviving "remainder" unless that remainder is stable across reasonable reformulations and is clearly tied to the fixed representation.

### Step 5 - Test control of the full stretching mechanism

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: evaluate whether each shortlisted observable controls:
  - the near-field contribution,
  - the exterior contribution,
  - and any interaction terms created by the localization protocol.
- Action: distinguish three cases:
  - full control of the chosen stretching representation,
  - partial control of only one portion,
  - no operational control despite geometric descriptiveness.
- Expected output: a lemma sheet or obstruction memo stating exactly what is controlled and what remains free, without switching representations midstream.
- Kill condition: terminate a positive route if it only improves a descriptive local piece while leaving the scale-critical stretching mechanism uncontrolled in the fixed representation.

### Step 6 - Promote only the routes that survive both stretching and dynamics

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: promote a route to a serious final candidate only if it has:
  - a sharply defined observable or hybrid observable,
  - a scale-credible hypothesis,
  - control of the full stretching mechanism in the chosen representation,
  - and a plausible persistence story for the chosen scenario class.
- Action: otherwise classify the output into one of three presentable forms:
  - conditional proposition,
  - calibrated obstruction result,
  - or hybrid-observable follow-up target.
- Expected output: a final mission-ready note that is explicit about whether the geometry is operational, merely descriptive, or promising only in hybrid form.
- Kill condition: classify the chain as negative only if the failure persists after the observable, scenario class, and stretching representation have all been fixed.
