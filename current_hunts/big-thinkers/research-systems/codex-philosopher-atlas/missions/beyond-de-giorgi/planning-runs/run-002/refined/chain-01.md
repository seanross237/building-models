# Refined Chain 01 - Architecture-Fixed Obstruction Test for Exact NS Reformulations

## Central Premise

Do not treat this branch as a broad search for the mission's hidden NS-specific mechanism. Treat it as a bounded falsification program:

Within one explicitly fixed inherited estimate architecture, can a tightly defined family of exact NS reformulations, or one predeclared paired auxiliary quantity, produce an estimate-level gain on the named bottleneck term that

- survives localization, projection, and commutator losses,
- is not merely a geometry-only alignment effect,
- and depends on a feature not preserved by Tao's averaged model?

If not, the correct endpoint is a sharp obstruction memo, not a longer survey of attractive identities.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a concrete reformulation or paired auxiliary route that improves one named localized/truncated NS bottleneck estimate and comes with an explicit Tao discriminator, or
- a bounded negative result showing that, within a fixed architecture and specified candidate family, no estimate-level gain survives except possibly through geometry-dominated or architecture-changing effects outside this chain.

## Why This Refined Chain Is Worth Running

This branch remains worth running because the "maybe an exact NS rewrite helps" loophole is still live enough to deserve a disciplined test. What it should not claim to be is a general discovery branch. Its proper role is to close or sharpen one narrow possibility cleanly.

## Scope Discipline

Before substantive testing begins, the chain must fix all of the following:

- one inherited architecture:
  - De Giorgi truncation bottleneck, or
  - local-energy flux/localization bottleneck, or
  - one other specifically named inherited estimate architecture;
- one exact bad term inside that architecture;
- one bookkeeping notion of gain:
  - derivative reduction,
  - integrability improvement,
  - coefficient shrinkage,
  - or a clearly stated closure improvement in that same architecture;
- one explicit NS-versus-averaged discriminator;
- one perturbation metric for identifying geometry/alignment dependence.

If any of these remain vague, the branch downgrades immediately to a negative-result memo about under-specification.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Fix the architecture, bad term, and Tao discriminator

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose exactly one inherited estimate architecture and name the precise localized or truncated interaction that must improve.
- Action: define the single currency of gain that all candidates will be judged against.
- Action: predeclare a tightly bounded candidate family:
  - at most 2-3 direct reformulations of the exact NS nonlinearity,
  - plus at most one paired identity or auxiliary propagated quantity if it still targets the same bad term.
- Action: state explicitly what feature of the exact NS interaction is supposed to fail in Tao's averaged model, and where that distinction should enter the estimate.
- Expected output: a screening memo containing the named architecture, bad term, gain metric, candidate family, Tao discriminator, and geometry perturbation metric.
- Kill condition: if the architecture, gain metric, or Tao discriminator cannot be made concrete, close the chain as an under-specified obstruction branch.

### Step 2 - Derive candidate estimates with full loss accounting

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: for each candidate, derive the actual localized/truncated estimate that would replace or improve the named bad term.
- Action: record explicitly, not implicitly, the losses from:
  - localization,
  - Leray projection or Calderon-Zygmund structure,
  - commutators,
  - and any shell or frequency interaction bookkeeping.
- Action: retest standard Lamb-vector, projected, or vorticity forms only if the candidate estimate differs from prior known negatives at the level of these explicit losses.
- Expected output: an estimate table stating, for each candidate, the rewritten term, the full loss mechanism, and whether the gain is real, cosmetic, or merely architecture-changing.
- Kill condition: if every candidate returns the same effective closure cost in the fixed architecture, and the auxiliary route does not improve that verdict, stop and write the obstruction memo.

### Step 3 - Run the geometry/robustness split early, not as a late purity test

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: test any apparent gain against the predeclared perturbation metric immediately.
- Action: classify the signal as one of:
  - generic,
  - geometry-entangled but still carrying an algebraic remainder,
  - or geometry-only.
- Action: do not treat near-Beltrami behavior as positive evidence by itself, but do not assume algebra and geometry separate cleanly either.
- Expected output: a robustness memo stating whether the candidate survives outside special alignment classes, survives only with explicit geometric dependence, or dies completely.
- Kill condition: if the only surviving effect is geometry-only and leaves no estimate-level remainder outside that regime, hand off to Chain 02 and close this branch negatively.

### Step 4 - Convert the surviving signal into either a usable proposition or a sharp obstruction

- Depends on: Step 3, or directly on Step 2 if the branch dies there.
- Action: if a candidate survives, translate it into a proposition that feeds the same fixed architecture, or state explicitly that it requires changing architecture and therefore exits this branch.
- Action: quantify "materially improves the mechanism" by one of:
  - shrinking the bad coefficient,
  - improving the integrability or exponent at the bottleneck,
  - or replacing the bad term by a strictly weaker hypothesis in the same proof setup.
- Action: if no positive route survives, write the sharpest obstruction statement supported by the tested family.
- Expected output: either a draft proposition with explicit quantitative gain and first missing lemma, or a bounded obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the surviving signal only produces a polished reformulation with no quantified gain in the fixed architecture, close negatively.

### Step 5 - Close with the narrowest earned claim

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: state exactly which architecture and candidate family were tested, and exactly what the Tao discriminator showed.
- Expected output: one of three endpoints:
  - a live estimate-level lead within the fixed architecture,
  - a bounded negative claim on the specified candidate family,
  - or a handoff note that any live mechanism is geometry-dominated or architecture-changing and therefore belongs elsewhere.
- Kill condition: if the final claim extends beyond the tested architecture or candidate family, narrow it until it is fully earned.
