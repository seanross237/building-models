## Why These Three Form The Strongest Portfolio

- Chain 01 provides the highest-floor common screen. Even a negative result improves all downstream work by turning vague exact-NS hopes into a ranked admission protocol or a mission-wide obstruction map.
- Chain 02 is the cleanest theorem-facing attempt to exploit the mission's explicit SQG lesson. It tests whether a changed variable package can produce a genuine commutator gain instead of another pressure or LEI repackaging.
- Chain 04 preserves the one high-upside branch that the portfolio should not discard. It probes stochastic transport and circulation structure that is plausibly exact-NS-specific and meaningfully different from both the atlas branch and the scalarization branch.
- The three selected branches maximize diversity across methodologies: comparison-first, reformulation-first, and stochastic-transport-first.
- All three still yield useful output on failure. Each has a built-in obstruction memo that would narrow future work rather than merely ending in a dead proof sketch.
- Chain 03 is credible, but as a portfolio choice it is less differentiating than Chain 04 and does not preserve the same upside. Chain 05 is explicitly downstream of a surviving firewall and is therefore premature for this slot.
