# Meta-Learning: Step 1, Exploration 001 (Far-Field Obstruction Reconstruction)

## What Worked

- Requiring an explicit formula sheet forced the exploration to pin down the live term rather than paraphrase the pressure obstruction.
- Asking separately for "modes already killed" versus "modes still live" exposed a real quotienting issue: only constants are automatically annihilated, so many apparent harmonic gains are cosmetic.
- Anchoring the goal to a small source list prevented broad re-derivation of pressure background and kept the report tied to the copied mission evidence.

## What Could Be Improved

- The sign and normalization conventions for the pressure pairing vary across copied notes. Future goals of this type should ask the explorer to normalize notation up front.
- If a schematic bound and a concrete operative bound both appear in the source trail, tell the explorer explicitly to decide which one the chain is actually using.

## Generalizable Lesson

When a branch depends on "quotienting out killed modes," make the explorer compute exactly which modes the test structure annihilates. Otherwise the branch will overcount fake progress on constants or other already-killed components.
