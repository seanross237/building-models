# Meta-Learning: Step 2, Exploration 001 (Geometry Prior-Art Benchmark)

## What Worked

- Treating the local mission/planning corpus as a benchmark memo, rather than pretending it was a full literature packet, kept the report honest.
- Forcing each candidate family to state a "forbid novelty claim" rule made the overlap with prior art operational instead of rhetorical.
- Reusing the step-001 obstruction result as a negative guardrail prevented static singular-integral or alignment-language repackaging from slipping through as fake novelty.

## What Could Be Improved

- A dedicated factual-library packet on the geometry literature would make later benchmark steps sharper and less dependent on planning artifacts.
- The benchmark would have been easier to operationalize if the local corpus had recorded theorem-level details for the direction-coherence route, not just the headline criterion.

## Generalizable Lesson

When a branch sits near a large prior-art literature, benchmark it by writing explicit "forbid novelty claim" rules for each family. That is more useful than a vague similarity memo.
