# When Forced Interaction Classes Are Known But No Finite Support Ledger Is Frozen, Label The Gap Exact Non-Isolability

When a branch has already frozen one witness and identified the forced
interaction classes around it, the next positive target is one finite exact
closed support ledger.

If the record still does not freeze that finite ledger, do not overclaim a
class-exit theorem or an infinite packet-growth theorem just because larger
closure burdens are plausible.

The constructive negative label is exact non-isolability on the frozen
ledger, equivalently that honest reduced dynamics would require a new
finite-closure convention or arbitrary truncation before the next step can
even be posed.

Keep the negative verdict symmetric with the positive target:
if the step was supposed to earn finite exact closure, then failure to earn it
should stop the branch before dynamics rather than be softened into
`more bookkeeping later`.
