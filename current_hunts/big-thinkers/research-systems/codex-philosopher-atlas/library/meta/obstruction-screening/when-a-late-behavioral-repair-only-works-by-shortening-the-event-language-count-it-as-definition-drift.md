# When A Late Behavioral Repair Only Works By Shortening The Event Language, Count It As Definition Drift

When a behavior-based candidate is ambiguous only at the late stage, first
split the early exact filter from the late transfer requirement on the same
frozen window, threshold language, and event sheet.

That often separates two different failure types cleanly.
The early piece may remain exact but too weak to justify a downstream object
freeze, while the late piece may fail because the decisive event trace was
never uniformly fixed on the frozen record.

Do not call the late split an honest repair if it only works by deleting the
final threshold event, weakening it to an earlier onset condition, or
otherwise shortening the required event language after outcomes are seen.
That is a definition change, not a survivor.

In that situation, discard the late split as `not well-defined` and keep the
bottleneck labeled as event-language or event-trace rigidity rather than
drifting back into leakage bookkeeping.
