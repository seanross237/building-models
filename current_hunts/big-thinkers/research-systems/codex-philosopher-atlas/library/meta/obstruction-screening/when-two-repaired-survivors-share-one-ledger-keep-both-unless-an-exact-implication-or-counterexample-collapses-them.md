# When Two Repaired Survivors Share One Ledger, Keep Both Unless An Exact Implication Or Counterexample Collapses Them

When a repair pass leaves two exact survivors on the same frozen packet
ledger, do not collapse them just because the shortlist is already small.
Keep both objects if they act on different invariant quantities or different
downstream burdens, unless the local record actually proves one exact
implication or lands one exact counterexample that forces a merge or a
rejection.

Shared friendly and hostile outcomes are not enough.
Parallel pass/fail behavior on the current dossier may show co-orientation,
but it does not prove that one gate subsumes the other or that a conjunction
object is the honest packaging.

This matters especially when one survivor is theorem-facing and the other is
obstruction-facing:
collapsing them early can overclaim an equivalence theorem that the record
has not earned.
