# Transfer-Weighted Phase-Coherence Scores Are Canonical Diagnostics Not Intrinsic Objects

Status: `INFERRED` aggregation-status rule with `VERIFIED` exact-core support

Transfer-weighted scalar coherence scores obtained by averaging `Re z_tau`
against the exact interaction weights
`|C_tau| |a_p| |a_q| |a_k|` are honest summaries of the exact triad-phase
ledger, but they are not themselves intrinsic objects.

The exact content lies in the orbit-level `z_tau` data.
The choice to collapse that ledger to one scalar, the choice of weights, and
the choice among equally natural alternatives such as unweighted averages,
entropy scores, median defects, sign counts, or window thresholds are
aggregation policy rather than symmetry-forced mathematics.

So later steps may use scalar coherence scores as diagnostics on a fixed
canonical sheet, but they should not promote them as theorem-facing intrinsic
objects unless the aggregation law itself becomes forced.
The same logic demotes a bare phase-locking window or threshold score unless
it is explicitly anchored to the exact triad-phase ledger.
