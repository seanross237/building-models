# Final Synthesis Plan: Navier-Stokes Independent Domain Plans

## Core judgment

No honest prize-facing main route is currently justified.

That is the central conclusion of the synthesis, and it is stronger than any one
planner's local caution. All five plans reject a generic direct route to the
Millennium problem, and the authoritative status file supports them: the
classical pressure / epsilon / `H^1` / far-field lanes are structurally blocked,
the Tao-adjacent packet family is still blocked at theorem-object stage, the
critical-space route lacks a canonical extraction object, and the `e_2`-alignment
headline route is structurally dead for unconditional regularity.

The justified merged plan is therefore not "pick one main proof route anyway."
It is a bounded decision program with two active campaigns and a small number of
strictly conditional reopenings.

## Where the five plans agree

They agree on the following nontrivial points:

1. No existing domain currently supports an honest direct prize mainline.
2. The deepest shared bottleneck is theorem-object admissibility and canonicity,
   not lack of raw estimates alone.
3. Non-canonical packetization, post hoc role labels, desired-channel
   bookkeeping, and tiny finite-support Tao analogues should not be treated as a
   lead route.
4. Pressure / geometry work is only live at the exact borderline obstruction:
   the core-scale nonparallel tube interaction / anisotropic pressure debt.
5. If a lane cannot produce either a true subcritical gain or a family-level
   no-go theorem, it should be demoted quickly.

## Actual disagreements

The plans do not disagree about the closures above. They disagree about what the
best bounded use of time is now.

1. The critical-spaces plan wants `L^3` canonical extraction as the decisive
   prerequisite.
2. The proof-architecture plan wants a broader no-canonical-finite-reduction
   theorem first.
3. The triad-dynamics plan prefers an intrinsic shell-interface recursive-spill
   theorem before more abstract no-go work.
4. The harmonic-analysis plan treats the tensor-level borderline pressure debt
   as the only remaining analytic place where a real prize-relevant gain could
   still appear.
5. The geometric plan agrees on the same frontier in physical language and wants
   a reduction from tube-encounter geometry to CF/DHY-type regularity.

The synthesis does not average these. It ranks them by what is most justified by
the current repository record.

## Active bounded campaigns

### Campaign A: theorem-object decision campaign

This is the top-ranked campaign.

Why it is ranked first:

1. Three of the five plans converge here directly.
2. The status file repeatedly identifies canonicity / extraction / finite
   reduction as the actual failure mode of the strongest Tao-adjacent and
   critical-element branches.
3. A decision theorem here would clarify multiple lanes at once instead of
   advancing only one local formalism.

The campaign has two accepted outputs:

1. a family-level no-go theorem closing finite canonical Tao-style reduction
   architectures; or
2. a canonical extraction lemma that survives symmetry, refinement,
   compactness, and same-currency bookkeeping.

The campaign should be pursued in this order:

1. Define the admissible finite theorem-object class precisely enough to include
   the already-failed packet, anti-circuit, sign-ledger, and tiny-support phase
   families.
2. Prove family-level no-go lemmas for:
   - canonicity versus post hoc target/role selection;
   - finite closure versus recursive spill / arbitrary truncation;
   - same-currency fidelity versus refinement stability.
3. If those lemmas leave a genuine escape class, isolate the narrowest surviving
   extraction problem and try to prove a canonical extraction lemma there.

Default output if the positive extraction step fails:

- promote the no-go theorem and formally demote finite canonical Tao-style
  reduction as a prize route for now.

### Campaign B: borderline geometry-pressure decision campaign

This is the second-ranked campaign.

It merges the harmonic-analysis and geometric plans because both are attacking
the same structural frontier in different language:

- tensorially: the anisotropic pressure/strain derivative debt;
- geometrically: the nonparallel core-scale tube-encounter debt.

These are not two independent mainlines anymore. They are one bounded decision
campaign.

The campaign has two accepted outputs:

1. a real logarithmic or power gain beyond the critical `delta = 1/2`
   borderline, strong enough to feed a recognized regularity mechanism
   (CF/DHY-type or equivalent); or
2. a sharp no-go theorem proving that the current pressure / tube-geometry
   toolkit cannot beat the borderline in the exact dangerous regime.

The campaign should be pursued in this order:

1. Prove the cleanest possible exact decomposition localizing the dangerous
   contribution to the core-scale nonparallel encounter / anisotropic tensor
   term.
2. Show that all single-tube or lower-order pieces are genuinely subcritical.
3. Test the remaining bad term for one of two theorem outcomes:
   - explicit logarithmic or subcritical gain;
   - exact saturation / sharpness theorem.
4. If a gain appears, convert it immediately into a recognized regularity
   criterion rather than leaving it as a local estimate.

Default output if no gain appears:

- promote the saturation theorem and formally demote alignment-only or
  classical pressure/CZ strategies.

## Conditional routes

These are not active mainlines now. They reopen only if a prior decision
campaign breaks positively.

### Conditional Route 1: `L^3` minimal-element hybrid

Reopen only if Campaign A produces a genuinely canonical extraction rule for a
critical element.

Then the route becomes:

`minimal element => canonical concentration/ledger object => rigidity or
splitting contradiction`.

Without the extraction lemma, this route remains parked.

### Conditional Route 2: intrinsic shell-interface spill firewall

Reopen only if Campaign A isolates an intrinsic shell-interface object that does
not depend on packet boundaries, Tao-role imports, or desired-channel
annotation.

Then the next theorem target is:

`constructive forward shell transfer => depth-2 recursive spill lower bound =>
no delayed Tao-like window`.

Without the intrinsic object, this route remains bounded support work, not a
main route.

### Conditional Route 3: geometry-pressure regularity route

Reopen only if Campaign B produces a true gain beyond the critical borderline
and converts it into CF/DHY-type control or another accepted regularity
criterion.

Without that gain, this lane should be treated as sharp-obstruction work only.

## Hard stops

Stop immediately if any route falls into one of the following:

1. The object still depends on non-intrinsic packetization, desired-channel
   annotation, target-edge labeling, or coordinate-specific tie-breaking.
2. The best result is only a constant improvement with unchanged critical
   homogeneity.
3. The route quietly reopens closures already marked structural in the status
   file:
   - enstrophy/BKM-circle regularity;
   - De Giorgi / Vasseur pressure improvement toward full regularity;
   - standard epsilon-regularity retries;
   - `H^1` pressure repair;
   - harmonic far-field pressure rescue;
   - `e_2`-alignment or GMT as unconditional regularity routes.
4. The theorem candidate needs assumptions essentially equivalent to already
   known regularity criteria.
5. The supposed Tao-adjacent progress is still only a frozen-ledger local fact
   with no extraction or family-level impossibility bridge.
6. The pressure / geometry lane cannot isolate the bad term more sharply than
   the already-known core-scale nonparallel encounter picture.

## What counts as genuine progress

Only the following count as real progress now:

1. A theorem closing a whole admissible family of finite canonical Tao-style
   reductions.
2. A canonical extraction lemma stable under symmetry, refinement, compactness,
   and same-currency bookkeeping.
3. An intrinsic shell-interface spill theorem or a no-finite-carrier theorem for
   exact helical shell-crossing supports.
4. A theorem reducing the geometry/pressure problem to one explicit bad tensor /
   encounter functional with all other contributions shown subcritical.
5. Any logarithmic or power gain beyond `delta = 1/2` in the actual dangerous
   regime, together with immediate conversion to a recognized regularity
   criterion.
6. Failing that, a sharp saturation theorem proving the current borderline is
   exact for the available toolkit.

The following do not count:

1. new packet examples or witness engineering;
2. nicer reformulations with unchanged scaling;
3. DNS-style plausibility arguments without theorem objects;
4. broad "critical spaces should work" narratives without a canonical
   descriptor;
5. local ledger algebra that does not feed either extraction or impossibility.

## What the merged plan refuses to spend time on

The merged plan refuses to spend time on:

1. enstrophy or BKM-bypass regularity programs as a lead route;
2. De Giorgi / Vasseur / epsilon-regularity retries under new notation;
3. `H^1` pressure, Hardy-BMO, compensated-compactness, or harmonic far-field
   retries as main lines;
4. packet, hypergraph, or sign-defect routes that still rely on non-canonical
   target sets or representative choices;
5. renewed tiny finite-support phase-locking searches already killed by
   recursive spill;
6. unconditional regularity claims from `e_2`-alignment, `s_2 <= 0`, GMT, or
   pressure-Hessian self-damping stories already closed by the status file;
7. open-ended explicit support engineering unless it directly serves a bounded
   no-go theorem.

## Final route ranking

1. Campaign A: theorem-object decision campaign.
2. Campaign B: borderline geometry-pressure decision campaign.
3. Conditional reopening of `L^3` minimal-element rigidity only after canonical
   extraction exists.
4. Conditional reopening of intrinsic shell-interface spill only after a truly
   intrinsic object exists.

## Bottom line

The honest merged plan is not a prize attempt in disguise.

It is:

1. decide whether the finite canonical reduction paradigm is structurally
   impossible;
2. decide whether the exact `delta = 1/2` geometry-pressure borderline can be
   beaten or is sharp;
3. reopen a prize-facing route only if one of those decisions breaks
   positively.

Until then, the correct synthesis statement is explicit:

> no prize-facing main route is currently justified, and bounded decision
> theorems are the right use of effort.
