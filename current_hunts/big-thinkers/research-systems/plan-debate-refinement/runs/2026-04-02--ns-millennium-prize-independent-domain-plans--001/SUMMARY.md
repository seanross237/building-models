# Summary of the Five Independent Plans

## Shared conclusion

All five plans agree on the main synthesis fact:

> no honest prize-facing main route is currently justified.

This is not superficial agreement. The plans come from different lenses and
still converge on the same diagnosis:

1. the classical pressure / epsilon / `H^1` / far-field routes are structurally
   blocked;
2. the Tao-adjacent route is blocked primarily at theorem-object and canonicity
   stage;
3. the critical-space route is blocked at canonical extraction stage;
4. the `e_2`-alignment headline route is blocked for unconditional regularity;
5. any surviving lane must now produce either a sharp new theorem object or a
   family-level no-go theorem.

## What each plan contributes

### 1. Critical spaces / compactness-rigidity

Main claim:

- there is no serious compactness-rigidity route unless one first proves a
  canonical `L^3` concentration extraction theorem for a minimal blowup element.

Best contribution:

- it sharply identifies the missing compactness object and gives a clean
  fallback no-go target if no finite canonical reduction survives symmetry,
  subsequences, and refinement.

Main limitation:

- it does not yet explain why the broader finite-reduction problem should be
  attacked at the critical-element level rather than at the theorem-object class
  level.

### 2. Harmonic analysis / pressure

Main claim:

- the only live analytic frontier is the exact borderline anisotropic
  pressure/strain derivative debt; everything broader in the pressure lane is
  already closed.

Best contribution:

- it gives the cleanest narrow theorem target for a possible genuine analytic
  gain: beat the critical pressure/CZ borderline or prove it sharp.

Main limitation:

- by itself it risks sounding more independent than it really is; the status
  file and the geometric plan show that this is the same frontier as the
  tube-encounter obstruction.

### 3. Triad dynamics

Main claim:

- the only Tao-adjacent route worth naming is an intrinsic shell-interface
  recursive-spill theorem, with a finite-carrier no-go theorem as fallback.

Best contribution:

- it gives the strongest intrinsically formulated Tao-adjacent target and
  correctly refuses more packet engineering.

Main limitation:

- it is still downstream of the theorem-object issue: if the shell-interface
  observable cannot be made intrinsic and stable, the route collapses back into
  the same admissibility bottleneck identified by the proof-architecture plan.

### 4. Geometric regularity

Main claim:

- `e_2`-alignment alone is dead; the only live geometric frontier is whether
  high-vorticity tube-encounter geometry can force a CF/DHY-type gain beyond
  the critical borderline.

Best contribution:

- it translates the remaining analytic barrier into a sharp physical-space
  theorem agenda and makes clear that single-tube geometry is not the problem.

Main limitation:

- like the harmonic plan, it does not by itself justify promotion to a mainline
  until an actual gain appears.

### 5. Proof architecture

Main claim:

- the repository-wide bottleneck is theorem-object admissibility, so the first
  serious task is a family-level no-canonical-finite-reduction theorem or a
  genuine extraction lemma.

Best contribution:

- it best matches the strongest repeated repository failure mode and gives the
  broadest decision-quality theorem target.

Main limitation:

- if pursued too abstractly, it could drift away from the specific exact NS
  structures that made the failures informative in the first place.

## Major disagreements

The major disagreements are about ranking, not about already-closed routes.

### Disagreement 1: what should be ranked first?

- The proof-architecture plan ranks family-level no-go highest.
- The critical-spaces plan ranks canonical `L^3` extraction highest.
- The triad-dynamics plan ranks intrinsic shell-interface spill highest.
- The harmonic and geometric plans rank the `delta = 1/2` borderline analytic
  obstruction highest.

Synthesis judgment:

- the theorem-object decision campaign ranks first, because it absorbs the
  critical-space and Tao-adjacent failures more directly than either of their
  local formulations alone.

### Disagreement 2: are harmonic and geometric routes distinct?

- The harmonic plan treats the pressure tensor debt as the key object.
- The geometric plan treats tube-encounter geometry and CF/DHY reduction as the
  key object.

Synthesis judgment:

- these are one merged campaign, not two separate mainlines. The status file
  already identifies the same core-scale nonparallel interaction as the
  obstruction in both languages.

### Disagreement 3: should Tao-adjacent work stay live?

- The triad plan keeps an intrinsic spill theorem as the best Tao-adjacent
  moonshot.
- The proof-architecture plan is more skeptical and wants family-level
  impossibility decided first.

Synthesis judgment:

- Tao-adjacent work stays alive only conditionally. It is not promoted as a
  main route until an intrinsic object survives the admissibility tests.

### Disagreement 4: should critical-element work stay live?

- The critical-spaces plan keeps it alive conditionally via canonical
  extraction.
- The proof-architecture plan treats it as premature until the extraction class
  is understood more generally.

Synthesis judgment:

- the critical-element route is parked behind the theorem-object campaign. It is
  a valid conditional reopening, not an active mainline.

## Why the final synthesis chose its route ranking

### Rank 1: theorem-object decision campaign

Chosen first because:

1. it is the strongest point of overlap among the critical-space,
   proof-architecture, and triad plans;
2. it is the repository's most repeated failure mode;
3. a result here would decisively clarify multiple routes at once.

This is why the final synthesis prefers:

- family-level no-go theorems for finite canonical Tao-style reductions;
- or a genuinely canonical extraction lemma if such an object survives.

### Rank 2: merged geometry-pressure borderline campaign

Chosen second because:

1. it is the only place where harmonic and geometric plans both still see a
   plausible theorem-shaped chance of a real analytic gain;
2. the status file localizes the remaining obstruction sharply enough to support
   a bounded decision campaign;
3. even a negative result here would be valuable, because it would close one of
   the last analytically respectable escape hatches.

It is not ranked first because no actual gain is currently in hand, while the
status record on theorem-object failure is much firmer.

### Parked but conditional: critical-element rigidity and intrinsic shell spill

Both remain logically possible, but neither is justified as active first-line
work yet.

- Critical-element rigidity depends on canonical extraction.
- Intrinsic shell spill depends on an intrinsic observable surviving the same
  admissibility pressures that killed earlier packet and small-support objects.

## Final synthesis statement

The five plans do not support choosing a current prize-facing proof route.

They support a narrower and more defensible ranking:

1. decide the theorem-object / canonicity question at family level;
2. decide the exact `delta = 1/2` geometry-pressure borderline;
3. reopen prize-facing work only if one of those decisions breaks positively.

That is why the final merged plan explicitly says that no prize-facing main
route is currently justified.
