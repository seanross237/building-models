# Mission

The ultimate goal of this mission is to produce novel, defensible results — findings rigorous enough that we could present them to the scientific community with confidence. Everything below serves that goal. A fascinating exploration that doesn't produce a citable, stress-tested result has not yet succeeded.

---

Investigate whether the amplituhedron framework (and related positive geometry approaches to scattering amplitudes) is merely a computational tool for QFT, or reveals deeper physical structure — and find a concrete case where the two frameworks diverge or where the amplituhedron makes predictions QFT cannot easily reach.

Background: Arkani-Hamed and Trnka's amplituhedron computes scattering amplitudes in N=4 super Yang-Mills without reference to spacetime, fields, or Feynman diagrams. The amplitudes emerge from the geometry of a mathematical object in a higher-dimensional space. Related geometric structures (associahedron, cosmological polytopes) extend this to other theories. Locality and unitarity are not inputs — they emerge as outputs.

The question is whether this is just a better calculator for the same physics, or whether the geometric formulation is more fundamental — and if so, whether that has physical consequences. Concretely, pursue:
- Compute specific scattering amplitudes using both the amplituhedron and standard Feynman diagram methods — verify agreement where both apply
- Identify regimes where the amplituhedron gives results more naturally or where Feynman diagrams become intractable — are there predictions the amplituhedron can reach that QFT practically cannot?
- Investigate whether the amplituhedron framework extends beyond N=4 SYM — what happens for realistic gauge theories? What breaks?
- Explore whether the absence of locality and unitarity as inputs has physical implications — are there "amplituhedron-allowed" amplitudes that violate standard QFT assumptions?
- Examine the cosmological polytope program — do geometric approaches to cosmological correlators predict anything standard perturbation theory misses?
- Search for a concrete observable where the geometric and field-theoretic approaches disagree, even in principle

If the frameworks are provably equivalent, characterize exactly what "equivalent" means and whether the equivalence extends beyond N=4 SYM. If they're not equivalent, identify the discrepancy precisely. Either result, if rigorous, is a novel contribution.
