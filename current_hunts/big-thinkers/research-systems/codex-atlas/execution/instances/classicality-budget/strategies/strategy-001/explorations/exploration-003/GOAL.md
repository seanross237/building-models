# Exploration 003: Prior Art Search — Has the Classicality Budget Been Derived Before?

## Goal

Systematically search the scientific literature to determine whether the "classicality budget" — the trade-off between redundancy (observer-agreement) and information content (richness of classical reality) imposed by entropy bounds — has been derived before, even if under a different name or implicitly within a broader framework.

The candidate formula is: **R_δ ≤ (S_max / S_T) − 1**

This combines quantum Darwinism's redundancy measure R_δ with the Bekenstein/holographic entropy bound S_max.

## What to Search For

### A. Direct Searches

Search for the following terms and concepts explicitly:
- "classicality budget"
- "objectivity bound" + quantum
- "redundancy bound" + decoherence OR quantum Darwinism
- "intersubjective information limit"
- "classical information capacity" + bounded region
- "Bekenstein bound" + "quantum Darwinism"
- "holographic bound" + redundancy
- "entropy bound" + objectivity OR intersubjectivity
- "information-theoretic limit" + classicality
- "objectivity" + "entropy bound"

### B. Key Authors and Papers to Check

These authors have worked at the intersection of quantum Darwinism and information theory. Check their most relevant papers for any bound resembling R_δ ≤ S_max/S_T:

1. **Wojciech Zurek** — The originator of quantum Darwinism. Key papers:
   - Zurek (2003) "Environment as a witness" (Rev. Mod. Phys. 75, 715)
   - Zurek (2009) "Quantum Darwinism" (Nature Physics 5, 181)
   - Zurek (2000) "Einselection and decoherence from an information theory perspective"
   - Check ALL Zurek papers 2000-2026 for any mention of entropy bounds limiting redundancy

2. **Robin Blume-Kohout & Zurek** — Early quantum Darwinism formalism:
   - Blume-Kohout & Zurek (2005) "A simple example of 'quantum Darwinism'"
   - Blume-Kohout & Zurek (2006) "Quantum Darwinism: Entanglement, branches, and the emergent classical world"

3. **Jess Riedel, Zurek et al.** — Formalization of redundancy:
   - Riedel & Zurek (2010) "Quantum Darwinism in an everyday environment: Huge redundancy in scattered photons"
   - Riedel, Zurek, Zwolak (2012) "The rise and fall of redundancy in decoherence and quantum Darwinism"
   - Check for any discussion of entropy limits on redundancy

4. **Fernando Brandão, Marco Piani, Pawel Horodecki et al.** — Rigorous information-theoretic quantum Darwinism:
   - Brandão, Piani, Horodecki (2015) "Generic emergence of classical features in quantum Darwinism" (Nature Communications)
   - This paper proves quantum Darwinism is generic — CHECK whether they derive any bound on R_δ from total system size or entropy

5. **Jarosław Korbicz et al.** — Spectrum broadcast structures:
   - Korbicz, Horodecki, Horodecki (2014) "Objectivity in a noisy photonic environment"
   - Korbicz (2021) "Roads to objectivity: quantum Darwinism, spectrum broadcast structures, and strong quantum Darwinism"
   - These formalize objectivity more rigorously — check for entropy bounds

6. **Raphael Bousso** — Holographic bounds and communication:
   - Bousso (2017) "Universal limit on communication" (arXiv:1611.05816)
   - This derives holographic limits on channel capacity — VERY likely relevant. Does it implicitly contain the classicality budget?
   - Bousso, Casini, Fisher, Maldacena (2014) on entropy bounds
   - Check for any connection to environmental information / observer agreement

7. **Michael Zwolak** — Quantum Darwinism quantitative studies:
   - Zwolak, Quan, Zurek (2009, 2014) — numerical studies of redundancy
   - Check for any mention of upper bounds on redundancy from system size

8. **C. Jess Riedel** — Solo papers on quantum Darwinism and the classical world:
   - Riedel (2017, 2019) — check for any discussion of information limits on classicality

### C. Conceptual Neighbors

Even if no one has derived R_δ ≤ S_max/S_T exactly, someone may have derived something closely related. Check for:

1. **Holographic limits on quantum channel capacity** — Bousso (2017) bounds the rate at which information can be communicated through a region. If the environment IS a channel, this might BE the classicality budget.

2. **Decoherence rate bounds** — Are there entropy-based bounds on how fast or completely decoherence can occur?

3. **Quantum error correction capacity bounds** — QEC requires redundancy. There are well-known bounds (quantum Singleton bound, quantum Hamming bound). Do any of these, applied to the "environment as error-correcting code" picture, give the classicality budget?

4. **Objectivity measures in quantum information theory** — Horodecki et al. developed "spectrum broadcast structures." Do these come with entropy-limited bounds?

5. **Channel capacity of the environment** — If we model the system-environment interaction as a quantum broadcast channel (one sender, many receivers = environmental fragments), quantum broadcast channel capacity theorems might already contain this bound.

6. **Quantum information bottleneck** — Information bottleneck methods applied to quantum systems might have produced similar trade-offs.

### D. Recent Work (2022-2026)

Search specifically for recent developments:
- New papers on quantum Darwinism, especially any connecting it to holographic bounds
- Any papers combining Bekenstein bound with decoherence or quantum-to-classical transition
- Recent reviews of quantum Darwinism (check for bounds we might have missed)
