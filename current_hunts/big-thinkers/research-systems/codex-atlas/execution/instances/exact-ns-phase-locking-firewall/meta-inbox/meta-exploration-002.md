# Meta Note: Exploration 002

## What worked

- The smallest-support audit was framed correctly as a **model-level go/no-go
  test**, not as a larger theorem campaign. That forced the route to reveal its
  weakness quickly.
- Reusing the exact helical coefficient machinery from the old singleton-support
  mission saved time and kept the audit on exact NS rather than on packet
  surrogates.
- The right negative test was two-step: first show the observable is trivial on
  one triad, then show it is arbitrarily weak on the first nontrivial cluster.
  Either side alone would have been less persuasive than the pair.

## What to carry forward

- For phase-burden missions, the first honest model-level audit should ask both:
  "is the observable forced in the smallest setting?" and "does it immediately
  become weak once the setting is large enough to be nontrivial?"
- Cancellation-ratio observables are especially vulnerable. If the numerator is
  the net transfer and the denominator is absolute transfer mass, a two-channel
  cluster can often kill the burden before any delayed-threshold mechanism is
  studied.
- When the minimal exact cluster already admits positive receiver gain with
  arbitrarily small coherence, stop. Adding more modes will only add more
  cancellation freedom unless a new structural invariant is introduced.

## Execution note

- The launched math-explorer was left running in the background, but manual
  recovery closed the question faster than waiting because the decisive
  computation was small and explicit: a five-mode exact-helical cluster plus one
  short script.
