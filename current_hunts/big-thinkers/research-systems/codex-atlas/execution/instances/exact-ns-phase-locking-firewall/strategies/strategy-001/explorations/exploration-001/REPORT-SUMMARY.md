# Exploration 001 Summary

## Goal

Decide whether this mission admits one preferred intrinsic exact-helical
phase/coherence observable, built from exact helical triad interactions and
pairable with the already available delayed-transfer event `E_flux`, or whether
every honest candidate still collapses into non-canonical bookkeeping,
representative-choice dependence, or a non-faithful transfer restatement.

## What I tried

- Reused the exact helical triad law and the earlier exact-support audit to fix
  the single-triad phase variable precisely.
- Used primary-source helical references to verify the exact triad law and
  checked the translation / gauge / conjugation invariances of the phase
  combination `arg(C_tau u_k u_p u_q)`.
- Tested two candidate object shapes:
  - an oriented circular phase-concentration observable on the exact triad
    population,
  - a conjugation-even transfer-efficiency ratio based on `cos(Phi_tau)`.
- Tried to pair the surviving conjugation-even backup with the fixed delayed
  transfer event `E_flux`.

## Outcome

Succeeded: the gate decision is stable, and the correct verdict is
`fail: definition-level failure`.

## One Key Takeaway

The exact helical triad phase is canonical at the single-triad level, but no
preferred population-level observable survives both harmless-change invariance
and faithful pairing to `E_flux`.

## Leads Worth Pursuing

  smallest-support audit on a fake preferred object.
