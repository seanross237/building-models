## Goal
Assess Barandes' Feb 2026 paper cluster for physical content, test three value propositions (selection principle, computational advantage, structural insight), and answer the "what does it buy me?" question.

## Outcome: COMPLETED — MIXED VERDICT

**Test A (Selection Principle):** NEGATIVE. ISP covers all finite-dim QM; it characterizes which stochastic theories ARE quantum (indivisible), but excludes no quantum systems. Not a new result (equivalent to non-Markovian quantum channels in QI).

**Test B (Computational Advantage):** NEGATIVE. ISP erases phases/coherences; every quantum calculation is harder or equivalent. Zero computational benefit for any standard physics problem.

**Test C (Structural Insight):** POSITIVE in three ways:
1. CHSH/Tsirelson (2512.18105): causal local ISP → exactly Tsirelson bound, not just ≤ 4. This identifies WHICH postulate (causal locality of underlying dynamics, not just observable statistics) explains the bound. No-signaling allows PR boxes; causal local ISP does not.
2. Deflationary Account (2602.01043): complex numbers necessary because for N>2, unistochastic matrices require complex (not real orthogonal) amplitudes. Structural derivation of a QM axiom.
3. Phase freedom = realm selection (E003b): still the sharpest novel finding, not yet developed by Barandes.

## Key Takeaway
**Barandes adds genuine structural insight — explaining known facts from new starting points — but zero operational value.** The framework is a principled conceptual architecture for QM foundationalism, not a tool for computing or predicting. The "what does it buy me?" answer for a working physicist: one genuine explanatory result (the Tsirelson bound characterization) and a structural story about complex numbers, plus fundamental limitations on entanglement, dynamics, and QFT that make the framework irrelevant to most physics.

The amplituhedron comparison is instructive: the amplituhedron adds OPERATIONAL value (EFT-hedron inequalities, new calculations). Barandes adds only CONCEPTUAL value. Both are valid; only one advances the frontier.

## Leads Worth Pursuing
1. **CHSH/Tsirelson proof strength:** The 2512.18105 derivation uses amplitude matrices Θ (quantum objects). Does the paper derive Θ from ISP axioms, or assume it? If the latter, the argument is partially circular. A careful read of the proof's assumptions would sharpen the verdict.
2. **Phase freedom = realm selection formalization:** E003b identified this as novel but unproven. A computation verifying that the dimension of Schur-Hadamard gauge orbit equals the dimension of the realm-selection ambiguity for finite N would be the natural next step. A 50-line numpy computation might work for N=2,3.
3. **Real-vs-complex in ISP for N=2:** The deflationary account claims real orthogonal matrices suffice for N=2. Renou et al. (2021) showed real QM fails entanglement tests. Do these results clash? If ISP says N=2 needs no complex numbers, but Renou says real N=2 QM makes different predictions, there may be a tension worth exploring.

## Unexpected Findings
- **The "pseudo-quaternion" claim (2602.01043):** Barandes states Hilbert spaces in QM are actually defined over pseudo-quaternions (generated by i, K, iK with all squares = 1), not just complex numbers. This is a structural observation about the algebra underlying QM that is not widely noted in the foundations literature. If true, it means the standard "QM uses complex numbers" claim understates the structure.
- **Pilot-wave paper makes a real contribution:** The HMM paper (2602.10569) does something beyond ISP: it resolves the ontological-nomological fork for Bohmian mechanics using the latent-variable framework. This is a genuine contribution to Bohmian mechanics foundations. It's not part of the main ISP program but extends the deflationary methodology in a different direction.
- **The deflationary critique papers (Weak Values, ABL Rule) are consistent:** Barandes is building a comprehensive deflationary program: reinterpreting weak values, post-selection, pilot waves, and quantum theory itself as either mathematical artifacts or tools without deep ontological significance. This is a coherent philosophical program, even if it adds no new predictions.
