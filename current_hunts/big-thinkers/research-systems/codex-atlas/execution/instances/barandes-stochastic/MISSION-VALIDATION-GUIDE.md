# Mission Validation Guide — Barandes Indivisible Stochastic Dynamics

## What Success Looks Like

A concrete finding about whether the Barandes stochastic reformulation of QM has physical content beyond standard QM — either a case where it reveals new structure/predictions, a proof of equivalence with precise scope, or identification of where the framework breaks. The finding must involve actual analysis, not just philosophical argument.

## Validation Tiers

### Tier 1: Framework Understanding
- The indivisible stochastic dynamics framework is correctly stated with all axioms explicit
- The equivalence theorem (stochastic dynamics ↔ Hilbert space QM) is precisely characterized — what is proved, what is assumed, what is the scope
- The relationship to at least 3 prior stochastic/reformulation programs is mapped (SED, Nelson, consistent histories, 't Hooft)
- Barandes' claims about the measurement problem are stated precisely enough to evaluate

### Tier 2: Scope and Limits
- The exact domain of equivalence is characterized — for which systems, in which regimes, under what assumptions
- At least one case is computed explicitly in both frameworks (stochastic and standard QM) to verify agreement
- Edge cases are identified — where might equivalence break? Infinite-dimensional systems, QFT, gravity, mixed states, open systems?
- The comparison to SED is rigorous — why does Barandes avoid the ω³ feedback failure?

### Tier 3: Physical Content
- The question "is this just notation?" is addressed with evidence, not philosophy
- If physically equivalent: the equivalence is characterized precisely and honestly (for which theories, in which regimes, with what caveats)
- If not equivalent: the divergence is identified concretely (specific system, specific observable, specific regime)
- The measurement problem claim is evaluated — does it genuinely resolve, partially address, or merely reframe?
- At least one concrete insight or computation is identified where the stochastic lens adds value (even if predictions are identical)

### Tier 4: Novelty and Robustness
- Prior art search covers Barandes, Nelson, Griffiths, Omnes, Gell-Mann, Hartle, 't Hooft, Adler, and related stochastic QM literature
- The finding is new — not a restatement of known equivalences or known limitations
- If a new insight is found, it is cross-verified
- The strongest objection is addressed
- Connection to prior Atlas findings (SED failure, thermal time, classicality budget) is explored

### Tier 5: Implications
- What the results mean for the foundations of quantum mechanics
- Whether the stochastic framework suggests new physics or experimental tests
- Whether it changes what's computationally tractable
- Clear statement of what remains open vs. what has been settled

## What "Exhausted" Looks Like

A strategy is exhausted when:
- The equivalence/non-equivalence question has been answered for the accessible regime
- The measurement problem claim has been evaluated
- The comparison to SED and other stochastic programs is complete
- Extensions to QFT/gravity have been attempted or characterized as out of scope
