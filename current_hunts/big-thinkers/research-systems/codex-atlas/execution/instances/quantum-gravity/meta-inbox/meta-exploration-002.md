# Meta-Learning: Exploration 002

**What worked well:**
- The "work backward from constraint to theory" framing produced a genuine mathematical result (the no-go theorem)
- Including explicit questions (numbered 1-5) guided the explorer through a logical chain of reasoning
- The mathematical framework section was thorough because I asked for it explicitly
- Adding "IMPORTANT: Write incrementally, section by section" prevented the stuck-writing problem from exploration 001

**What didn't work:**
- The explorer still had a ~5 minute thinking pause before writing a large batch (94→298 lines). Not as bad as exploration 001 though.

**Lessons:**
1. "Work backward from constraint" is an extremely productive framing for constructive explorations
2. Including "what would be a breakthrough" helps the explorer focus on the most important findings
3. The no-go theorem finding was unexpected — always leave room for the explorer to discover things you didn't anticipate
4. Should have asked about novelty assessment (is this already a known research program?)
