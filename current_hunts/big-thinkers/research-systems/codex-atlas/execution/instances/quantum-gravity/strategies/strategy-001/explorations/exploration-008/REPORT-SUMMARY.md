# Exploration 008 Summary: Parameters, Cosmological Constant, and Matter Content

## Goal
Determine whether the 2 free parameters (M₀, M₂) of quadratic gravity + fakeon can be fixed, whether the theory addresses the cosmological constant problem, and what matter content is needed for UV completion.

## What Was Tried
Investigated inflationary constraints on M₀ from CMB data, the Anselmi-Piva mass bound on M₂, the unique asymptotically free trajectory from physical beta functions, the agravity hierarchy problem scenario, unimodular quadratic gravity for the CC problem, running vacuum energy from asymptotic safety and lattice QG, the causal set everpresent-Λ comparison, and beta function analysis for f₀ and f₂ with Standard Model and BSM matter content.

## Outcome: SUCCEEDED — with important results

### Key Finding 1: The theory has effectively 1 free parameter, not 2
M₀ (scalar/inflaton mass) = 3 × 10¹³ GeV is **already fixed** by the CMB power spectrum amplitude A_s. It was never truly free. M₂ (fakeon mass) is bounded from below (M₂ > M₀/4) and constrained by a unique asymptotically free trajectory (ξ/λ ≈ -3.53). M₂ will be fully determined once r (tensor-to-scalar ratio) is measured by LiteBIRD (~2031) or CMB-S4 (~2035).

### Key Finding 2: The CC problem is partially solved
Unimodular quadratic gravity (Salvio 2024) cleanly solves the "old" CC problem — vacuum energy doesn't gravitate because the CC becomes an integration constant. But the "new" CC problem (why Λ_obs ≈ 10⁻¹²² M_P⁴) is NOT solved. The causal set everpresent-Λ (δΛ ~ H²) remains the only approach that predicted the right order of magnitude. This is a genuine gap in the QG+fakeon framework.

### Key Finding 3: No BSM matter strictly needed for UV completion
The f₀ coupling is NOT asymptotically free (positive one-loop beta function regardless of matter content), but this is resolved non-perturbatively by the Reuter fixed point of asymptotic safety. For Total Asymptotic Freedom, SU(3)³ trinification with extra fermions is the simplest option, predicting TeV-scale BSM particles.

## Key Takeaway
**The theory is more predictive than previously appreciated: 1 free parameter (not 2), determinable by a single measurement (r).** The agravity hierarchy solution is incompatible with the CMB-derived M₀ in the standard parameterization. The CC problem is the main remaining weakness — solved for the "old" problem but not the "new" one.
