# Exploration 011 Summary: Black Hole Predictions in QG+F and Six-Derivative Extension

## Goal
Determine what QG+F predicts for black holes — singularity resolution, Wald entropy, Hawking radiation, information paradox, remnants, QNMs, and observational signatures. Compare to GR and AS. Assess testability.

## What Was Tried
Systematically investigated: (1) static BH solutions in quadratic gravity (Stelle potential, Lü-Perkins-Pope-Stelle nonlinear solutions, fakeon vs ghost-as-physical branches, singularity resolution); (2) Wald entropy formula for quadratic and six-derivative gravity with explicit corrections; (3) Hawking radiation modifications, microcausality violation, information paradox, and remnant predictions; (4) observational signatures including QNMs, BH shadows (EHT), gravitational waves (LISA/ET), and the six-derivative extension.

## Outcome: SUCCEEDED — Complete catalog of BH predictions with a stark conclusion

### The central finding: QG+F makes NO testable black hole predictions for astrophysical BHs.

All corrections are suppressed by (l_P/r_H)² ~ (M_P/M_BH)², ranging from 10⁻⁷⁶ (solar mass) to 10⁻⁹⁶ (SMBH). This is not a deficiency of the theory — it's a fundamental consequence of the fakeon prescription selecting Schwarzschild as the unique classical solution with corrections entering only at the quantum level.

**Specific findings:**

1. **Static BHs are Schwarzschild** under the fakeon prescription. The non-Schwarzschild branches (Yukawa attractive/repulsive) require nonzero ghost field profiles and are excluded by the fakeon's purely virtual nature.

2. **Wald entropy = A/(4G) + O(l_P²/r_H²)** — the R² term drops out entirely for static BHs (R = 0 by the Lichnerowicz theorem), leaving only tiny C² corrections. Six-derivative corrections enter at O(l_P⁴/r_H⁴) — even more suppressed.

3. **QNMs include massive spin-2 modes** that exist in the perturbation spectrum even around Schwarzschild, but under the fakeon prescription these are purely virtual — they cannot be excited as real propagating modes. Their indirect effects are negligible.

4. **BH shadow deviations are exponentially suppressed** — literally exp(-10⁵⁰) for M87* — as demonstrated by Held, Gold & Sen (2023) using ~10,000 numerical solutions.

5. **Singularity NOT resolved** perturbatively. The ghost-as-physical treatment allows conformal cores (Bonanno 2025) and spontaneous ghostification to naked singularities (Bonanno 2025), but these are excluded under the fakeon prescription. AS resolves singularities non-perturbatively.

6. **Microcausality violation at Δt ~ 10⁻⁴⁴ s** provides a potential information leakage channel but no quantitative resolution of the paradox exists.

7. **No remnant prediction** from perturbative QG+F. AS predicts Planck-mass remnants (T → 0); ghost-as-physical treatment predicts naked singularities.

## Key Takeaway
**Black holes are the weakest sector for QG+F predictions.** The fakeon prescription's great strength — eliminating the ghost from the physical spectrum — simultaneously eliminates all classical modifications to BH physics. Everything reduces to GR + unobservably small quantum corrections. The BH sector powerfully illustrates why the non-perturbative completion (AS) matters: it makes qualitatively different predictions (singularity resolution, remnants, modified entropy) that perturbative QG+F cannot access. The theory's testable predictions remain entirely in the inflationary/CMB sector (r < 0.005).

## Leads Worth Pursuing
1. The ghost-induced phase transition / spontaneous ghostification (Bonanno 2025) deserves scrutiny — does the fakeon prescription truly suppress this at the non-perturbative level, or could it occur?
2. A quantitative calculation of information leakage through fakeon microcausality violation would be valuable — no one has done this.
3. The Anselmi "new type of uncertainty" from non-perturbative effects in fakeon theories is directly relevant to BH physics and remains poorly understood.
4. EMRI observations with LISA could probe near-horizon geometry — worth checking whether the massive scalar (spin-0) modifies EMRI dynamics even under the fakeon prescription.
