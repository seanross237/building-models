## Task 1: Black Hole Solutions in QG+F

### 1.1 The Stelle Potential and Linearized Solutions

The linearized (Newtonian limit) gravitational potential in quadratic gravity was first computed by Stelle (1978). The action is:

S = ∫ d⁴x √(-g) [ R/(16πG) + α R² + β C_μνρσ C^μνρσ ]

where α is the R² coupling and β is the Weyl² (C²) coupling. The theory propagates three degrees of freedom:
- The massless graviton (spin-2, from GR)
- A massive scalar (spin-0) from the R² term, mass m₀² = 1/(6α) for α > 0
- A massive spin-2 ghost from the C² term, mass m₂² = -1/(2β) for β < 0

The static gravitational potential between two point masses:

**V(r) = -(GM/r) [ 1 + (1/3) e^{-m₀ r} - (4/3) e^{-m₂ r} ]**

Key features:
- At **r >> 1/m₂, 1/m₀**: Reduces to the Newtonian potential -GM/r (Yukawa corrections exponentially suppressed)
- At **r → 0**: V(r) → 0, because 1 + 1/3 - 4/3 = 0. The gravitational potential vanishes at the origin!
- The **spin-2 ghost contribution** (-4/3 term) is what regularizes the potential. It is the stronger of the two corrections.

The linearized metric includes a Yukawa-type correction:

h₀₀(r) = 1 - 2M/r + 2 S₂⁻ e^{-m₂ r}/r

where S₂⁻ is the "Yukawa charge." Its sign determines attractive (S₂⁻ < 0) or repulsive (S₂⁻ > 0) correction.

**Important caveat:** V(r) → 0 at r = 0 is a linearized/weak-field result. The full nonlinear BH solution may behave very differently near horizons and singularities.

### 1.2 Full Nonlinear BH Solutions (Lü-Perkins-Pope-Stelle)

**Key paper: Lü, Perkins, Pope, Stelle (2015), PRL 114, 171601 [arXiv:1502.01028]**

This foundational paper established critical results for static, spherically symmetric BH solutions in Einstein-Weyl gravity (the C² sector, since R drops out):

**Lichnerowicz-type theorem:** All static BH solutions in quadratic gravity must have vanishing Ricci scalar (R = 0). This is a profound simplification — the R² term drops out entirely for static BHs, and only the C² (Weyl²) sector contributes to modifications. The static BH problem reduces to Einstein-Weyl gravity.

**Three families of solutions:**

1. **Schwarzschild branch:** The standard GR Schwarzschild solution with zero Yukawa charge (S₂⁻ = 0). These exist for all masses and are identical to GR.

2. **Non-Schwarzschild "Yukawa attractive" branch:** BHs with negative Yukawa charge (S₂⁻ < 0). Arbitrarily large horizon radii and temperatures. Gravity is *stronger* near the horizon. Unbounded parameter space.

3. **Non-Schwarzschild "Yukawa repulsive" branch:** BHs with positive Yukawa charge (S₂⁻ > 0). Exist only for narrow parameter ranges. Small horizon radii and temperatures. Gravity is *weaker* near the horizon.

**The crossing/transition point:** The branches intersect at a critical dimensionless horizon radius:

r_H^cross ≈ 0.876 / m₂

In physical units (with the ghost mass at or near the Planck mass, m₂ ~ M_P):
- Transition radius: ~0.876 × l_P (sub-Planckian!)
- Transition mass: ~0.4 M_P
- Transition temperature: ~0.5 T_P

For the Starobinsky-motivated value α ~ 10⁸ (Planck units), the crossover could be at ~10⁻⁸ M_☉ — relevant only for primordial BHs in final evaporation stages.

**All solutions obey the first law of thermodynamics** with appropriately defined Wald entropy.

**Phase diagram (Silveravalle & Zuccotti 2023, PRD 107, 064029):** A comprehensive phase diagram of Einstein-Weyl gravity maps all static, spherically symmetric solutions as functions of mass and Yukawa charge strength. The rich solution space includes not just BHs but also naked singularities and wormholes. All solutions are "essentially identical at coordinate distances r ≳ 2M, with significant deviations appearing close to and below r = 2M only."

### 1.3 Fakeon BH vs Standard QG BH

**This is a crucial distinction for QG+F specifically.**

In the standard treatment (Lee-Wick or ghost-as-physical), the massive spin-2 field can form classical configurations — leading to the non-Schwarzschild branches described above.

Under the **fakeon prescription (Anselmi):**

1. **The ghost is purely virtual.** It never appears as an asymptotic state, never forms a classical field profile. Therefore the Yukawa charge S₂⁻ must vanish for any solution generated by physical sources.

2. **Static BHs in QG+F are Schwarzschild.** The non-Schwarzschild branches are excluded because they require nonzero ghost field profiles. This is a clean, sharp prediction: **at the classical level, QG+F BHs are identical to GR BHs.**

3. **Quantum corrections through classicization.** The true classical limit of QG+F is not the standard classical limit — it's obtained by quantizing first, then "classicizing" (integrating out the fakeon at tree level). The classicized field equations:
   - Include nonlocal corrections from the ghost averaging
   - Take the form ⟨F⟩ = ma, where ⟨F⟩ includes "a little bit of future" (Anselmi 2018, CQG 36, 065010)
   - Are inherently perturbative — no complete "exact" classical field equations exist
   - Generate corrections at O(1/m₂²) to the Schwarzschild geometry

4. **Microcausality violation.** The fakeon introduces causality violation at Δt ~ 1/m₂ ~ 1/M_P ~ 10⁻⁴³ s. This is "short range for all practical purposes" (Anselmi & Piva 2019, CQG 37, 095003). It does **not** propagate along light cones or via gravitational waves. "The universe even conspires to make the effect disappear."

5. **Non-perturbative uncertainty.** Anselmi acknowledges (JHEP 01 (2026) 104, arXiv:2510.05276) that the classicized equations are inherently perturbative. "Nonperturbative effects play crucial roles" in certain regimes, introducing a "new type of uncertainty." This is particularly relevant for BH interiors where the geometry is strongly curved.

**Key implication:** QG+F makes **no modifications** to BH physics at the classical, macroscopic level. All modifications are quantum corrections suppressed by powers of (l_P/r_H)², which are negligible for astrophysical BHs but become important at the Planck scale.

### 1.4 Singularity Resolution

**Status in QG+F: NOT RESOLVED at the perturbative level.**

| Approach | Singularity Status |
|----------|-------------------|
| GR | Singular (r = 0 curvature divergence) |
| QG (ghost as physical) | Modified — conformal cores possible (Bonanno et al. 2025) |
| QG+F (fakeon) | Classical = Schwarzschild (singular). Quantum corrections perturbative. |
| AS (non-perturbative) | Resolved — G(r) → 0 in UV gives regular core (Bonanno-Reuter) |

**Detailed analysis:**

1. **Linearized potential:** V(r) → 0 at r = 0 (Section 1.1) — but this is the weak-field limit, not applicable near the singularity.

2. **Conformal cores (Bonanno et al. 2025, PRD 111, 044031):** In the ghost-as-physical treatment of quadratic gravity, new exact power-law solutions exist under spherical symmetry. These "conformal cores" are regions near the BH center where the geometry becomes conformally flat. This is suggestive of singularity modification but is **not available under the fakeon prescription** (because it requires a nonzero ghost field profile).

3. **Spontaneous ghostification (Bonanno et al., May 2025, arXiv:2505.20360; 2505.05027):** A dramatic recent proposal — the massive spin-2 ghost destabilizes the Schwarzschild solution at late evaporation stages, triggering a "phase transition" to a **stable, exotic naked singularity**. The naked singularity is a "massive triple point" where all BH families coincide.

   - This is NOT singularity resolution — it's singularity *exposure*
   - Occurs only when BH mass drops below the crossing point ~0.876/m₂
   - The naked singularity "circumvents the inconsistencies associated with classical naked singularities" through quantum effects
   - "Renders the information paradox moot as it merges with the singularity issue"
   - **Under the fakeon prescription, spontaneous ghostification would be suppressed** because the ghost cannot destabilize the Schwarzschild solution classically. However, non-perturbative fakeon effects might still allow this transition — this is an open question.

4. **AS singularity resolution (Bonanno-Reuter):** From exploration 007, AS predicts G(k) → 0 in the UV, which gives a regular BH core with de Sitter interior. The metric becomes ds² ~ -(1 - Λ_eff r²/3) dt² + ... near r = 0, with Λ_eff set by the UV fixed point. **This is the cleanest singularity resolution and requires non-perturbative physics.**

**Bottom line:** Perturbative QG+F does **not** resolve the BH singularity. Singularity resolution requires non-perturbative completion (AS/CDT). The ghost-as-physical interpretation allows more exotic possibilities (conformal cores, ghostification to naked singularity) but these are excluded by the fakeon prescription at the perturbative level.

## Task 2: BH Entropy in QG+F

### 2.1 Wald Entropy Formula for Quadratic Gravity

The Wald entropy formula (Wald 1993, Iyer-Wald 1994) gives the BH entropy for any diffeomorphism-invariant gravity theory as:

**S_Wald = -2π ∮_Σ (∂L/∂R_abcd) ε_ab ε_cd dA**

where Σ is the bifurcation surface of the horizon, ε_ab is the binormal to Σ, and L is the gravitational Lagrangian density.

For the quadratic gravity action:

L = (1/16πG) R + α R² + β C_μνρσ C^μνρσ

we need:

∂L/∂R_abcd = (1/16πG) g^{a[c} g^{d]b} + 2α R g^{a[c} g^{d]b} + 4β C^{abcd}

The Wald entropy evaluates to:

**S_Wald = A/(4G) + 32πα R_H + 64πβ (C_abcd ε^{ab} ε^{cd})_H**

where the subscript H means evaluated at the horizon.

**For the Schwarzschild solution specifically:**

The Schwarzschild solution has:
- R = 0 everywhere (it's Ricci-flat)
- C_μνρσ ≠ 0 (the Weyl tensor is nonzero)

However, the specific contraction C_abcd ε^{ab} ε^{cd} evaluated at the Schwarzschild horizon gives a term proportional to the Kretschner scalar-related quantity. For Schwarzschild:

C_abcd ε^{ab} ε^{cd}|_H = (specific tensor contraction at r = r_H)

The key result from the Lü-Perkins-Pope-Stelle analysis and subsequent work on BH thermodynamics in Einstein-Weyl gravity (the relevant sector since R = 0 for static BHs):

**S_BH = 16π²γ (r_H² + 2/m₂² (1 - f₁ r_H))**

where:
- γ relates to the coupling constant (γ = 1/(16πG) in appropriate normalization)
- r_H is the horizon radius
- f₁ is a near-horizon parameter related to the metric expansion
- m₂ is the ghost mass

For the **Schwarzschild solution** (f₁ takes its Schwarzschild value):

**S_Schw = A/(4G) + (correction term proportional to 1/m₂²)**

The correction is:

**ΔS = O(l_P²/r_H²) × A/(4G)**

This is a *fractional* correction of order (l_P/r_H)², which is:
- For M87* (r_H ~ 10¹³ m): ΔS/S ~ 10⁻⁹⁶ — utterly negligible
- For a solar-mass BH (r_H ~ 3 km): ΔS/S ~ 10⁻⁷⁶ — utterly negligible
- For a Planck-mass BH (r_H ~ l_P): ΔS/S ~ O(1) — corrections are order unity

**For non-Schwarzschild BHs** (ghost-as-physical only, not QG+F):
The entropy takes the more general form above with f₁ ≠ f₁^Schw. These solutions have modified entropy-mass relations that differ qualitatively from Schwarzschild.

### 2.2 Entropy Corrections at What Mass Scale?

The Wald entropy corrections in QG+F become significant when:

**(l_P/r_H)² ~ O(1)**, i.e., **M_BH ~ M_P ~ 2.2 × 10⁻⁸ kg**

| BH Mass | r_H | ΔS/S | Significance |
|---------|-----|------|-------------|
| 10⁶ M_☉ (SMBH) | ~10¹⁰ m | ~10⁻⁹⁰ | Completely negligible |
| M_☉ (stellar) | ~3 km | ~10⁻⁷⁶ | Completely negligible |
| 10¹⁵ g (PBH) | ~10⁻¹³ m | ~10⁻⁴⁰ | Negligible |
| 10⁻⁵ g (micro-BH) | ~10⁻³³ m | ~O(1) | Corrections dominant |
| M_P | ~l_P | ~O(1) | Full QG regime |

**Key insight:** The entropy corrections in QG+F are relevant ONLY for Planck-scale BHs. For any astrophysically observable BH, the entropy is identical to the Bekenstein-Hawking result A/(4G) to extraordinary precision.

This is in stark contrast to AS, where the running G(k) modifies the entropy at larger scales through the Bonanno-Reuter modification of the metric.

### 2.3 Six-Derivative Extension Entropy

The six-derivative extension of QG+F (Anselmi & Piva 2018, studied in detail by exploration 004) adds cubic curvature terms:

L₆ = L₄ + γ₁ R³ + γ₂ R C² + γ₃ C³ + ...

where L₄ is the quadratic (four-derivative) action.

For the Wald entropy of the six-derivative theory:

**S₆ = S₄ + (cubic curvature corrections at the horizon)**

The cubic corrections enter at order **(l_P/r_H)⁴** relative to the Bekenstein-Hawking term (since each additional derivative adds two powers of 1/r). These are even more suppressed than the quadratic corrections.

For static BHs with R = 0, the R³ and RC² terms vanish identically. Only the C³ term (pure Weyl cubic) survives:

**ΔS₆ ~ β_cubic × (l_P/r_H)⁴ × A/(4G)**

This is negligible for all conceivable BH observations. The six-derivative extension does not change the BH entropy predictions in any observable way.

**A subtlety for rotating BHs:** For Kerr BHs (R = 0 but more complex geometry), the cubic terms could in principle produce different corrections. However, these are still suppressed by (l_P/r_H)⁴ and remain unobservable.

## Task 3: Hawking Radiation and Information

### 3.1 Fakeon Prescription and Hawking Radiation

Hawking radiation is a semiclassical effect — quantum fields on a classical BH background. In QG+F, there are two modifications to consider:

**1. Modified particle spectrum:** QG+F contains three fields — the graviton, the massive scalar (mass m₀), and the massive spin-2 fakeon (mass m₂). The Hawking radiation spectrum includes:
- Standard model particles (as in GR)
- Massive scalars (emitted when T_Hawking > m₀)
- The fakeon is **not emitted** — it cannot appear as an asymptotic state

The scalar emission provides an additional channel that slightly modifies the evaporation rate when T_H > m₀. Since m₀ ~ M_P for natural values of α, this only matters for Planck-scale BHs.

**2. Classicized corrections to the background:** The BH metric receives fakeon-averaged corrections of order (l_P/r_H)². These modify the Hawking temperature:

T_H = T_H^GR × [1 + O(l_P²/r_H²)]

Again negligible for astrophysical BHs but potentially significant for micro-BHs near the Planck mass.

**3. Ghost instability at late stages:** In the ghost-as-physical treatment, the massive spin-2 field drives an instability at late evaporation stages (Bonanno et al. 2025). This "ghost-induced phase transition" fundamentally alters the evaporation endpoint. Under the fakeon prescription, this instability is suppressed at the perturbative level — but non-perturbative effects may still trigger it.

**Key prediction:** QG+F Hawking radiation is indistinguishable from GR Hawking radiation for any macroscopic BH. Modifications appear only in the final Planck-scale stages of evaporation.

### 3.2 Microcausality Violation and Information

**The fakeon microcausality violation (Anselmi & Piva 2018, 2019):**

The fakeon prescription violates microcausality — strict locality of commutators — at timescales:

**Δt ~ 1/m₂ ~ 1/M_P ~ 5.4 × 10⁻⁴⁴ s**

and distance scales:

**Δx ~ 1/m₂ ~ l_P ~ 1.6 × 10⁻³⁵ m**

**Key results from Anselmi & Piva (2019, CQG 37, 095003):**

1. The microcausality violation is "short range for all practical purposes"
2. It does **not** propagate along light cones
3. It does **not** propagate via gravitational waves
4. "The universe even conspires to make the effect disappear" — the positivity of the Hubble constant helps suppress the violation
5. The violation is a fundamental prediction of QG+F but is experimentally undetectable with foreseeable technology

**Implications for the information paradox:**

The information paradox hinges on the incompatibility of: (a) unitarity of quantum mechanics, (b) locality/causality, (c) semiclassical BH geometry.

QG+F modifies (b) — it sacrifices strict microcausality at Planck scales. This could in principle provide a channel for information to leak out during Hawking evaporation at a rate set by the microcausality violation scale. However:

- The information leakage rate would be ~ (l_P/r_H)² per emission event
- For a solar-mass BH, this is utterly negligible per Hawking quantum
- Over the BH lifetime (~10⁶⁷ years), the cumulative leakage could potentially account for information recovery — but no detailed calculation exists
- Anselmi has not published a specific resolution of the information paradox using the fakeon mechanism

**The non-perturbative uncertainty (JHEP 01 (2026) 104):** Anselmi acknowledges that the classicized equations miss non-perturbative effects, introducing a "new type of uncertainty." For BHs, this means the perturbative QG+F framework cannot fully address the information paradox — the resolution likely requires non-perturbative physics.

**Ghost-induced naked singularity alternative (Bonanno et al. 2025):** The proposal that evaporating BHs transition to stable naked singularities "renders the information paradox moot as it merges with the singularity issue." If the BH becomes a naked singularity, there is no horizon and no information trapping. This is an elegant (if radical) resolution, but applies only to the ghost-as-physical treatment, not the fakeon.

### 3.3 Remnant Predictions

**QG+F does not predict stable BH remnants at the perturbative level.**

The reasoning:
1. At the perturbative level, QG+F BHs are Schwarzschild with small corrections
2. Hawking evaporation proceeds as in GR, with the BH temperature increasing as mass decreases
3. No mechanism within perturbative QG+F halts the evaporation

**However, three scenarios for Planck-scale endpoints exist:**

| Scenario | Source | Mechanism | Remnant? |
|----------|--------|-----------|----------|
| Standard evaporation | Perturbative QG+F | No modification to late-stage evaporation | No prediction — perturbative expansion breaks down |
| Ghost phase transition | Ghost-as-physical QG | Ghost instability → naked singularity | Stable naked singularity (not a traditional remnant) |
| AS completion | Non-perturbative | G(k) → 0 in UV → T → 0 | Yes — Planck-mass remnant with T → 0 |

**The AS remnant (from Exploration 007):** If QG+F is the perturbative manifestation of AS, then the non-perturbative completion predicts:
- Hawking temperature T → 0 as M → M_remnant ~ M_P
- A stable Planck-mass remnant
- Potential dark matter candidate
- Entropy ~O(1) — the remnant stores approximately one bit of information

**The GUP remnant (Carr et al. 2020, EPJC 80, 478):** In quadratic gravity combined with the Generalized Uncertainty Principle, stable mini-BH remnants can exist when the BH luminosity vanishes for appropriate parameter combinations. The remnant mass is set by the GUP parameter and gravitational couplings.

**Bottom line for QG+F:** The perturbative theory cannot predict the evaporation endpoint. The non-perturbative completion (AS) predicts Planck-mass remnants. The ghost-as-physical treatment predicts naked singularities. This is a fundamental open question.

For **astrophysical BHs** (M >> M_P), QG+F makes essentially **no detectable modifications** to GR. This is a precise and honest assessment:

| Observable | GR | QG+F (fakeon) | Difference |

| BH entropy | A/(4G) | A/(4G) + O(l_P²/r_H²) | Negligible |
| BH shadow | Standard GR | Same to exponential precision | Undetectable |

The modifications are always suppressed by **(l_P/r_H)² ~ (M_P/M_BH)²**, which for astrophysical BHs ranges from 10⁻⁷⁶ (solar mass) to 10⁻⁹⁶ (SMBH).

**However, QG+F makes one qualitatively new prediction:** the existence of **massive spin-2 quasi-normal modes** in the BH ringdown spectrum.

### 4.2 Quasi-Normal Modes

**Key paper: Tattersall & Silveravalle (2024), arXiv:2412.15037**

This is the most promising observational signature of quadratic gravity and the most significant finding of this exploration.

**The QNM spectrum in quadratic gravity (Einstein-Weyl sector) contains:**

1. **Standard GR modes:** The Schwarzschild solution admits the same QNMs as in GR — the familiar graviton modes with ω ∝ 1/M.

2. **NEW massive spin-2 modes:** Additional QNM families corresponding to the massive spin-2 degree of freedom. These modes have:
   - Mass scale: μ² = 1/(2α_W) where α_W is the Weyl² coupling
   - Propagation: behave as massive spin-2 particles at infinity
   - Excitation: "Both classes of modes can be excited in physical processes"
   - Damping: different damping timescales from GR modes

**Critical insight:** Even when the stationary BH solution is Schwarzschild (which it is in QG+F), the massive spin-2 modes exist in the perturbation spectrum. The ghost/fakeon nature affects whether these modes are *excited* in a given process, but the linear perturbation spectrum still contains them.

**Under the fakeon prescription specifically:**
- The massive spin-2 modes are purely virtual — they cannot carry away energy to infinity as real particles
- However, they can be excited as **virtual intermediate states** in the ringdown, modifying the waveform
- The effect would appear as a correction to the GR ringdown amplitudes, not as new independent modes
- The correction is suppressed by ~ (l_P/r_H)² for the coupling to external gravitational waves

**For the non-Schwarzschild (hairy) BHs** (ghost-as-physical only):
- ALL QNM frequencies are shifted from GR values
- The hairy branch exists only for horizon parameters p ≈ 0.876-1.143 (in units of 1/m₂)
- These are Planck-scale BHs and not astrophysically relevant

**Observational prospects for QNMs:** The massive spin-2 QNMs have frequencies ~ m₂ ~ M_P, corresponding to gravitational wave frequencies ~ 10⁴³ Hz — utterly beyond any conceivable detector. The indirect effects on standard QNM frequencies are suppressed by (l_P/r_H)² and equally undetectable.

### 4.3 BH Shadows and Gravitational Wave Signatures

**BH Shadows (EHT observations):**

**Key paper: Held, Gold & Sen (2023), A&A 674, A190 — "Probing quadratic gravity with the EHT"**

This paper systematically studied ~10,000 static, spherically symmetric solutions of Einstein-Weyl gravity against EHT observations:

1. **Shadow size:** For BHs with Planck-scale massive modes, the deviation from GR is:

   ζ^{M87} = 2 exp(-6.5 × 10⁵⁰) S₂⁻

   This is **exponentially suppressed** — literally e^{-10⁵⁰} — meaning the shadow radius cannot discriminate between quadratic gravity and GR for any astrophysical BH.

2. **Interior brightness:** Some non-Schwarzschild solutions (Type II naked singularities) show enhanced brightness within the shadow, with intensity ratios η ≈ O(10) compared to Schwarzschild. These are **observationally excluded** by EHT data.

3. **Wormhole solutions:** Some parameter space regions produce wormhole-like geometries (Type III), which can be partially constrained.

4. **Net result for QG+F:** Since the fakeon prescription selects only the Schwarzschild solution, the EHT prediction is **identical to GR** to exponential precision. No BH shadow observation can test QG+F.

**Gravitational Wave Signatures:**

For BH binary mergers and ringdowns:

1. **Inspiral phase:** The gravitational waveform is determined by the BH metric, which in QG+F is Schwarzschild/Kerr + O(l_P²/r²). The corrections to the inspiral waveform are:
   - Suppressed by (l_P/r)² at each PN order
   - Include the effect of the massive scalar (m₀) as a fifth force
   - Completely undetectable by LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA, LISA, or ET

2. **Merger phase:** Requires full numerical relativity in quadratic gravity — very few results exist. The corrections are again (l_P/r_H)².

3. **Ringdown phase:** As discussed in Section 4.2, the QNM spectrum includes massive spin-2 modes. A recent analysis (arXiv:2411.12428) argues that "causality constraints on the on-shell graviton three-point couplings" dictate that the QNM spectrum and interactions "are dictated solely by Einstein's gravity" under weak coupling assumptions. This means **even the QNM corrections from quadratic gravity may vanish** in the weakly-coupled regime relevant for astrophysical BHs.

### 4.4 Six-Derivative Extension BH Predictions

The six-derivative extension (adding cubic curvature terms R³, RC², C³) produces:

1. **Static BHs:** Still R = 0 for static solutions (by the Lichnerowicz theorem, which generalizes). The C³ term provides additional corrections:
   - To the metric: O(l_P⁴/r⁴) relative to Schwarzschild
   - To the entropy: O(l_P⁴/r_H⁴) relative to BH area
   - To Hawking temperature: O(l_P⁴/r_H⁴)

   All even more suppressed than the four-derivative corrections.

2. **Additional massive particles:** The six-derivative extension introduces new massive particles with masses set by the cubic couplings. These could in principle modify the QNM spectrum with even heavier modes.

3. **Inflationary predictions (for comparison):** While the six-derivative extension shifts n_s to ~0.974 and r to ~0.0045 (from Exploration 010), its BH predictions are indistinguishable from the four-derivative theory for any observable BH.

**Bottom line for six-derivative extension:** No additional observable BH predictions. The cubic corrections are doubly suppressed compared to the already-undetectable quadratic corrections.

### Key Conclusions

1. **QG+F makes no testable BH predictions for astrophysical black holes.** All corrections are suppressed by (l_P/r_H)² ~ (M_P/M_BH)², ranging from 10⁻⁷⁶ to 10⁻⁹⁶ for known BHs. This is a fundamental limitation of any perturbative quantum gravity theory — the corrections are simply too small.

2. **The only novel QNM prediction — massive spin-2 modes — is virtual under the fakeon prescription.** These modes exist in the perturbation spectrum but cannot be excited as real propagating degrees of freedom. Their indirect effects on the standard GR QNM frequencies are negligible.

3. **Singularity resolution requires non-perturbative physics.** The fakeon prescription preserves the Schwarzschild singularity at the perturbative level. True resolution comes from the AS sector (G → 0 in UV) or possibly from ghost confinement dynamics.

4. **The evaporation endpoint and remnant question are open.** Perturbative QG+F cannot address this because the expansion breaks down at the Planck scale. The AS completion predicts Planck-mass remnants; the ghost-as-physical treatment predicts stable naked singularities. These are genuinely different predictions that depend on the correct non-perturbative completion.

5. **The information paradox gets a possible but incomplete resolution.** Microcausality violation at the Planck scale provides a channel for information leakage, but no quantitative calculation exists. The non-perturbative "new uncertainty" acknowledged by Anselmi suggests the perturbative framework is insufficient.

6. **BH physics highlights the tension between QG+F and AS interpretations.** The perturbative QG+F framework predicts essentially GR BHs, while the AS non-perturbative completion predicts qualitatively modified BHs (regular cores, remnants, modified entropy). This reinforces the importance of determining whether QG+F and AS are the same theory in different regimes — a question the BH sector makes particularly sharp.

7. **The sharpest BH-related test remains the CMB tensor-to-scalar ratio r,** which tests the inflationary sector where QG+F and AS make distinguishable predictions (r < 0.005 vs r up to 0.01). No direct BH observation can competitively test QG+F.
