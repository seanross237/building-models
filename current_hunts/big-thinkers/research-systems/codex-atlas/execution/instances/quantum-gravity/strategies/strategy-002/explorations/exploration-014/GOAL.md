# Exploration 014: GW and Experimental Signatures Beyond CMB

## Mission Context

QG+F's testable predictions are concentrated in the CMB sector (r, n_s). BH predictions are unobservable. We need to find experimental signatures in OTHER sectors — gravitational waves (LIGO/ET/LISA), table-top experiments (GQuEST), or cosmological observations beyond CMB.

## Your Specific Goal

### Task 1: Gravitational Wave Signatures
- The scalar mode (spin-0 from R²) in QG+F — can it produce detectable GW signals?
- Modified dispersion relations at high energy — any effect on GW propagation?
- Scalar GW polarization — can LIGO/ET/LISA detect a scalar GW mode?
- BH merger ringdown: the massive spin-2 and spin-0 modes appear in the QNM spectrum. Under fakeon prescription these are virtual — can they still leave imprints?
- What about the six-derivative extension — any new GW signatures?

### Task 2: GQuEST and Table-Top Experiments
- GQuEST (Gravity from Quantum Entanglement of Space-Time) — what does it measure?
- Can it detect signatures of the fakeon or the R² scalaron?
- Gravity-induced entanglement experiments (Bose-Marletto-Vedral) — do they distinguish QG+F from other QG theories?
- Cavendish-type short-range gravity tests — the Stelle potential has Yukawa corrections at r ~ 1/M₂, 1/M₀. What are the current bounds on M₂ and M₀ from these experiments?

### Task 3: Cosmological Signatures Beyond CMB
- 21-cm cosmology — any QG+F imprints on the hydrogen signal?
- Large-scale structure — any modifications to the matter power spectrum?
- Gravitational lensing — any QG+F corrections?
- Stochastic GW background — any prediction from QG+F phase transitions?
- Dark matter: the scalaron or Planck-mass remnants as candidates

### Task 4: The Microcausality Violation Signature
- QG+F violates microcausality at Δt ~ 1/M₂ ~ 10⁻⁴³ s
- Can this be detected INDIRECTLY? Through cosmological correlations, BH information leakage, or scattering signatures?
- Anselmi has computed tree-level cross sections with fakeons — any bump or feature in scattering amplitudes?
- What about the "pair of bumps" signature at E ~ M₂?

### Task 5: Experimental Timeline and Priority
- For each potential signature: what experiment, what timeline, what sensitivity?
- Rank by likelihood of detection in the next 20 years
- What is the single most promising non-CMB test?

## Success Criteria
- At least 3 non-CMB experimental signatures identified
- Assessment of each: detectable or not?
- Priority ranking with timeline
