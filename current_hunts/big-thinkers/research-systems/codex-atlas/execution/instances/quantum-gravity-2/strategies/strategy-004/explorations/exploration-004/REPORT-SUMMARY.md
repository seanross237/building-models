# Exploration 004 Summary: Higgs Mass Consistency Under Unification

## Goal
Determine whether the fakeon prescription for the C² ghost changes the Shaposhnikov-Wetterich Higgs mass prediction (m_H ≈ 126 GeV) under the unified QG+F–AS framework.

## What Was Tried
Reconstructed the SW mechanism (λ(M_Pl) ≈ 0 from NGFP → m_H ≈ 126 GeV via SM RG running). Analyzed three channels through which the fakeon could affect the prediction: (A) perturbative beta functions, (B) the NGFP boundary condition itself, (C) threshold corrections at the ghost mass M₂ ~ M_Pl. Searched the literature on gravitational contributions to SM running (Eichhorn-Held 2017, Dona-Eichhorn-Percacci 2014, Salvio-Strumia 2014) and on fakeon loop properties (Anselmi 2022).

## Outcome: SUCCEEDED — Classification: CONSISTENCY CHECK

The fakeon prescription does NOT change the SW Higgs mass prediction. Three independent arguments:

1. **Beta functions are prescription-independent.** UV divergences (which determine counterterms and beta functions) come from the real part of loop integrals. The fakeon modifies only the imaginary/absorptive part. Confirmed by Anselmi: quadratic gravity has the same counterterms under fakeon and standard quantization.

2. **The NGFP is Euclidean.** The FRG computes in Euclidean signature where there is no i-epsilon prescription. The fakeon is a Lorentzian concept. The NGFP fixed-point values and the gravitational anomalous dimension A_λ are identical regardless of Lorentzian quantization choice.

3. **Threshold absorptive parts vanish.** At the ghost mass threshold M₂ ~ M_Pl, the matching condition's imaginary part (the only part affected by the fakeon) is proportional to θ(p² − 4M₂²), which is zero for all Higgs physics at the EW scale.

**Upper bound: |Δm_H| < 10⁻⁷ GeV** — six orders of magnitude below experimental uncertainty (±0.17 GeV), seven below theoretical uncertainty from m_top (±3 GeV).

## Key Takeaway

The unified framework inherits the SW Higgs mass prediction unchanged. This is a **CONSISTENCY CHECK**, not a novel prediction. The fakeon is invisible to the mechanism because: (a) it doesn't affect UV divergences, (b) the NGFP lives in Euclidean space, and (c) the ghost is far too heavy to affect EW-scale physics. The dominant uncertainties remain m_top and α_s — entirely within the SM sector.

## Leads Worth Pursuing

- Computing A_λ in the C²-extended FRG truncation would test whether the NGFP structure (not the fakeon, but the higher-derivative truncation itself) preserves the sign of A_λ. This is independent of the fakeon question but relevant for the robustness of the SW prediction in higher-derivative gravity.
