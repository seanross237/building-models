# Exploration 009 — Summary

## Goal
Find predictions that DISCRIMINATE the unified QG+F–AS framework from standalone theories and from the "compatible-but-separate" null hypothesis. Focus on computationally testable predictions.

## What Was Tried
Investigated five areas: (1) the spin-2 ghost propagator in C²-extended FRG, (2) the Bonanno-Platania b parameter from NGFP critical exponents, (3) applying the fakeon average continuation to AS's Euclidean effective action, (4) near-term experimental tests before 2030, (5) explicit development of the "compatible-but-separate" null hypothesis. Surveyed recent literature including Knorr-Saueressig spectral reconstruction (2022), Draper et al. complex pole towers (2020), Antoniou-Gualtieri-Pani QNMs in quadratic gravity (2024), Baldazzi-D'Angelo-Knorr foliated Lorentzian AS (2025), and Platania-Wetterich fictitious ghosts (2020).

## Outcome: PARTIALLY SUCCEEDED

Found **one strong discriminating prediction** and **one moderate one**, but **zero near-term experimental discriminators**.

**Strong discriminator:** The spin-2 ghost propagator in the (R + R² + C²) FRG truncation. The unified framework predicts the ghost pole dissolves (into complex towers or via mass divergence). The compatible-but-separate view has no mechanism for this — AS dynamics shouldn't affect QG+F's ghost if the theories are separate. Nobody has done this calculation, but the methodology exists (Knorr-Saueressig spectral reconstruction + Falls/SWY beta functions). Estimated: 1-2 years for an expert group.

**Moderate discriminator:** Applying the fakeon average continuation to the Wetterich equation to extract Lorentzian physics. Predicted by unified framework, unnecessary if separate. But may be rendered moot if Lorentzian AS (D'Angelo et al. 2024) works independently.

**Not discriminating:** The b parameter (consistency check, not unique to unification), near-term CMB (r ~ 0.003 right at BICEP threshold), LIGO QNMs (suppressed by M_P/M_BH), and the n_s tension (resolvable by standalone AS too).

## Key Takeaway
**The unified QG+F–AS framework has exactly ONE genuinely discriminating prediction: the ghost pole dissolution in the C²-extended FRG.** Everything else is either a consistency check (could be explained by compatible-but-separate) or experimentally unreachable before ~2037. The framework is falsifiable in principle via computation, not via experiment in the near term. The compatible-but-separate interpretation remains the simpler (Occam) alternative until the ghost propagator computation is performed.

## Leads Worth Pursuing
- The ghost propagator computation is the make-or-break test. The Saueressig, Knorr, or Platania groups have the technical capability.
- Investigating whether the Shaposhnikov-Wetterich Higgs mass prediction requires the fakeon prescription could open a matter-sector discriminator — unexplored territory.
- If the AF→NGFP trajectory is computed and yields a specific b → specific r, this becomes experimentally testable by LiteBIRD (~2037).
