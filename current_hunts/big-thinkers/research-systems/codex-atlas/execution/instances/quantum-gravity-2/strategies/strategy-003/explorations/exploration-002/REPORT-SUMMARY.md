# Exploration 002 — Summary

## Goal
Determine whether the published literature establishes an RG flow trajectory connecting the asymptotically free (AF) fixed point of quadratic gravity to the Reuter non-Gaussian fixed point (NGFP) of asymptotic safety.

## What Was Tried
Searched for and analyzed ~15 papers across the AS and higher-derivative gravity literature, focusing on SWY (2022, 2023), Codello-Percacci (2006), Niedermaier (2009), Ohta-Percacci (2014), Falls et al. (2023), and QQG claims from Salvio et al.

## Outcome: INCONCLUSIVE

No paper has explicitly computed an RG trajectory from AF → NGFP. The closest result is SWY (2022), who find both fixed points in the same fourth-order truncation and state: "There may exist a critical trajectory between the two fixed points, starting in the extreme ultraviolet from asymptotic freedom." The language is hedged — conjectured, not proven.

## Key Takeaway

There are **two competing interpretations** in the literature, and neither is settled:

1. **Same fixed point, different approximations**: Codello-Percacci (2006) found that non-perturbative RG turns the perturbative AF Gaussian fixed point into an NGFP. If true, AF and AS are the same UV completion seen at different levels of approximation, and the "connection" question is moot.

2. **Distinct fixed points, possible connection**: SWY (2022) resolves two separate fixed points with different critical exponents in their more refined truncation. A critical trajectory "may exist" but isn't computed. They frame AF as an *alternative* to AS.

The tension is truncation-dependent: coarser truncations see one fixed point; finer ones (SWY) resolve two. No universality class analysis or explicit flow computation bridges the gap.

## Leads Worth Pursuing
- The SWY (2023) scaling solutions paper (arXiv:2211.05508) computes AF → IR flows but does NOT check whether these pass near the NGFP. This would be a tractable computation that could resolve the question.
- Falls et al. (2023) find a 4D UV critical surface at the NGFP — matching quadratic gravity's coupling count. This dimensional coincidence deserves investigation.
- The QQG claim ("concrete realisation of asymptotic safety") from the Salvio group needs to be traced to its original source paper to understand its precise technical content.
