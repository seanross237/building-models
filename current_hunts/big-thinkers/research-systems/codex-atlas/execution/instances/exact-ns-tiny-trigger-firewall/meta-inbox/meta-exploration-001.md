# Meta Note: Exploration 001

## What worked

- The right first question was whether the trigger-focused mission genuinely produced a smaller exact object. That exposed quickly that the trigger cannot be made theorem-facing without reintroducing the full clock/amplifier/conduit/handoff chain.
- Reusing the predecessor exact-object results prevented fake progress. Once the preferred trigger object collapsed to the prior five-role hypergraph, the earlier singleton non-embeddability result became immediately relevant and no fresh support campaign was justified.
- Coefficient-weighted amplitude-level bookkeeping remains the right language for trigger-style mechanism missions. Packet-energy and triad-count surrogates are too coarse to say when a tiny trigger becomes dynamically decisive.

## What to carry forward

- Before launching a new mechanism mission that narrows an older object, ask explicitly whether the narrowing actually shrinks the faithful exact support. If it does not, the predecessor negative may already transfer.
- A trigger variable is not a standalone theorem object when its meaning is purely causal. If the trigger needs surrounding clock, amplifier, conduit, and output channels to define "delayed" and "decisive," make that dependence explicit immediately.
- When the preferred exact object is inherited but structurally dead, and the only backup is non-canonical, the right terminal category is often `model-level negative`, not `definition-level failure`.

## Execution note

- The initially launched explorer stalled after writing only placeholder report files. Manual recovery by the strategizer was straightforward because the relevant local evidence had already been narrowed by the receptionist and preloaded before launch.
