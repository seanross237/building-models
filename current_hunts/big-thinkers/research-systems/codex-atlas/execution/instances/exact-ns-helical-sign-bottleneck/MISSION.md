# Mission: Exact NS Helical Sign-Bottleneck

## The Question

Recent `codex-atlas` and `codex-nexus` work has narrowed the Tao-facing frontier for 3D Navier-Stokes:

- the exact singleton Tao-circuit object is impossible
- trigger-only narrowing collapses back to the same dead singleton object
- the first concrete clustered exact-mode packet model failed canonicity
- a physical-space hidden-precursor branch found a real event object, then died because the exact balance law has no backward-memory term

One new clue survived those negatives:

```text
on the existing exact five-role helical singleton audit, the least-leaky
nontrivial sign patterns are the most globally near-homochiral ones, but the
surviving desired activation edge a1 + a3 -> a4 is nonzero only in
heterochiral sign configurations.
```

This mission asks whether that clue persists at a genuinely packet-level exact-NS object:

```text
can one define a canonical helical packet model in exact NS for Tao-like roles
such that low leakage pushes the packet ledger toward global one-sign dominance,
while the load-bearing desired Tao-like edges still require positive
heterochiral dependence?
```

The target is not a full proof of regularity. The target is a theorem-facing firewall object or a clean negative result.

## Why This Direction Is Distinct

This is not:

- another De Giorgi / epsilon-regularity mission
- another pressure-improvement mission
- another exact-rewrite mission
- another broad Tao survey
- another packet mission that treats canonicity as an afterthought

This is a **helical-sign packet mission**. It tests whether helicity creates a one-sided obstruction to Tao-like delayed forward transfer that pure combinatorial leakage minimization missed.

## What Success Looks Like

One of the following:

1. **Positive helical bottleneck**
   A concrete statement of the form:
   - low packet leakage forces low global minority-helicity participation
   - but every Tao-like desired drive window has a positive lower bound on desired-edge heterochirality
   - therefore no near-isolated Tao-like packet cascade can satisfy all constraints simultaneously

2. **Negative kill**
   A canonical or defensibly fixed exact packet model admits a low-leakage Tao-like packet configuration with both:
   - low global sign-defect
   - low desired-edge heterochirality defect

3. **Canonicity failure**
   The packet/sign functionals cannot be fixed canonically enough to support a theorem-facing question.

4. **Model-level failure**
   The sign signal seen at singleton level does not survive any faithful packet lift.

## What Failure Looks Like

Any of these is a valid negative result:

- the mission keeps changing packet definitions to fit desired conclusions
- the sign functionals depend on arbitrary representative choices with no canonical resolution
- the "bottleneck" is only rhetorical and collapses when computed on the packet object
- the packet object silently reduces to the already-killed singleton exact circuit

## Established Results (Do Not Re-Derive)

- De Giorgi `beta = 4/3` is sharp within that framework
- epsilon-regularity family appears structurally capped
- `H^1` pressure route is dead at the `W^{1,3}` wall
- harmonic far-field pressure loophole is closed
- Tao's averaged-NS blowup is mechanistically a five-mode delayed-threshold circuit
- exact singleton Tao-circuit embedding is structurally impossible
- trigger-only narrowing does not produce a new faithful exact object
- the first clustered exact-mode packet model failed canonicity
- physical-space hidden-precursor observability failed despite finding a real exact-NS event object
- the helical singleton sign-defect prototype is only a local clue, not a theorem

This mission starts after all that.

## What Must Be Avoided

- Do **not** reopen closed estimate routes under new names.
- Do **not** treat a packet model as acceptable if its support partition, role graph, or leakage accounting changes under harmless refinements.
- Do **not** optimize an arbitrary scalar `SignDefect` just because it is convenient.
- Do **not** accept “homochiral tends to inverse transfer” as a proof.
- Do **not** let the mission drift into singleton exact-mode work after claiming to study packets.

## Core Mission Structure

### Step 1: Freeze one theorem-facing object

Starting from the best existing packet-level exact object, decide whether there is **one** canonically defensible helical packet model for this mission.

It must fix:

- packet families
- conjugate/sign bookkeeping
- desired Tao-like role graph
- leakage functional
- one global sign-coherence functional
- one desired-edge heterochirality functional

Acceptable sign functionals may resemble the `SD_part` / `SD_target` pair from the prototype sketch, but they must be made canonical, not merely suggestive.

**Deliverable:** one frozen packet/sign object, or a proof-quality explanation of why no such frozen object exists.

**Kill condition:** if the object still moves under admissible packet refinements or representative choices, stop with `canonicity failure`.

### Step 2: Test the singleton clue under packet lift

Compute or tightly analyze whether the singleton signal survives:

- does low leakage correlate with low global minority-helicity participation?
- do the desired Tao-like activation edges still require positive heterochiral dependence?

The result must be quantitative on the chosen packet object, not just verbal.

**Deliverable:** one computed or proved packet-level sign/leakage relationship.

**Kill condition:** if the singleton sign signal disappears under any faithful packet lift, stop with `model-level failure`.

### Step 3: Formulate the sharp bottleneck statement

If Step 2 survives, sharpen it into one theorem candidate such as:

- `Leak(I) <= eps` implies `SD_part(I) <= delta(eps)`
- every Tao-like activation window has `SD_target(I) >= c > 0`
- or the triple `(Leak, SD_part, SD_target)` cannot be simultaneously small

The candidate must be stated on exact NS packet data, not on a toy shell model unless fidelity is explicitly justified.

**Deliverable:** one precise obstruction statement or one explicit counterexample candidate.

### Step 4: Adversarial helical stress test

Search specifically for packet arrangements designed to defeat the bottleneck:

- near-homochiral packet families
- mixed-sign target edges with low spectator leakage
- symmetry choices that might trivialize the sign functionals

**Deliverable:** either a surviving lower bound / impossibility shape or a falsifying packet configuration.

### Step 5: Final report

The mission must end in exactly one of:

1. **positive helical bottleneck**
2. **counterexample: low-leakage low-sign-defect packet realization exists**
3. **canonicity failure**
4. **model-level failure: singleton sign clue does not survive packet lift**

## Budget

4-6 explorations.

Expected shape:

- 1 exploration for object freezing / canonicity
- 1 exploration for packet-level sign functional computation
- 1 exploration for theorem candidate or counterexample
- 1 adversarial helical stress test
