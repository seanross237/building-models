# Surfaceology — Factual Library

Knowledge about the surfaceology program: the reformulation of QFT scattering amplitudes as curve-integral / counting problems on fatgraph surfaces. Covers the foundational framework (2023–2026), its relationship to the amplituhedron, the canonical Yang-Mills integrand (PRL 2025), the full suite of new results across theories, and open problems. **5 findings.**

## Contents

- **surfaceology-framework-and-amplitude-formula.md** — Core definition: fatgraphs, headlight functions (u-variables), amplitude formula, the "first miracle" (all Feynman diagrams from cones in g-vector fan), non-planar extension via Mirzakhani kernel, key mathematical structures (tropical geometry, cluster algebras, Teichmüller space).

- **surfaceology-amplituhedron-distinction.md** — CRITICAL: surfaceology (kinematic space, colored theories, all genera) and the amplituhedron (twistor space, N=4 SYM, planar only) are *parallel* geometric programs, not nested. Unification map showing what surfaceology covers vs. what it doesn't. The mysterious open question of their common origin.

- **surfaceology-yang-mills-integrand.md** — The canonical loop integrand for non-SUSY Yang-Mills, solved via scalar scaffolding (arXiv:2408.11891, PRL 2025). Scalar scaffolding technique, surface gauge invariance, perfect factorization (resolving 1/0 ambiguities), recursion structure, explicit 1-loop all-multiplicity and 2-loop 4-point results.

- **surfaceology-new-results-2023-2026.md** — Survey of all other major results: cut equation recursion (all-loop NLSM), NLSM δ-shift with Adler zero at integrand level, Yukawa fermion extension, string theory connection (worldsheet = fatgraph surface), n/L decoupling, cosmological extension (cosmohedra), Monte Carlo 10-loop sampling, inverse KLT kernel (Feb 2026).

- **surfaceology-open-problems.md** — The central open problem: no bridge from surface kinematics to conventional loop momentum space. Also: higher-loop YM, N=4 SYM connection, Standard Model, gravity, integrated vs. integrand. Field assessment: leading framework for colored amplitudes but not the "final form."
