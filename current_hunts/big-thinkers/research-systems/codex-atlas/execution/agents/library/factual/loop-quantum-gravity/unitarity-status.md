# LQG Unitarity Status and Information Conservation

## Fundamental Unitarity

LQG is fundamentally unitary at the level of its full quantum description. In loop quantum cosmology, the evolution from a contracting universe (before the bounce) to an expanding universe (after) is perfectly unitary.

## Apparent Non-Unitarity via Decoherence

A key result (Perez, arXiv:1912.09750, published in Frontiers in Astronomy and Space Sciences 2021) demonstrates that even when the underlying model is fundamentally unitary, effective descriptions appear non-unitary. When microscopic Planckian degrees of freedom — irrelevant to low-energy observers — are traced over, pure states evolve into mixed states through decoherence with the Planck-scale microstructure.

Crucially, this decoherence occurs without dissipation: the hidden degrees of freedom carry no "energy" in the relevant physical regime. This realizes, in a fully quantum-gravitational context, the idea that information degradation can occur without energy loss.

## Information Conservation

Information is not destroyed but degraded — it becomes encoded in Planck-scale correlations inaccessible to macroscopic observers. At the fundamental level, information is fully conserved; it is simply stored in structures that low-energy physics cannot probe.

## Black Hole Information Paradox

Within the LQG community, the dominant view is that resolving the information paradox requires understanding how the black hole singularity is resolved by quantum gravity effects. The Planckian microstructure of the horizon provides "a large reservoir of q-bits for information to be coded at the end of black hole evaporation." Singularity resolution (replacing the singularity with a quantum bounce or transition region) may modify the causal structure enough to allow information to escape without violating unitarity.
