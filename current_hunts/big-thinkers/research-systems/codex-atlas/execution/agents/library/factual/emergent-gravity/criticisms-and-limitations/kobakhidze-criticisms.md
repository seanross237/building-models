# Criticisms of Entropic Gravity: Kobakhidze's Neutron Experiments

Archil Kobakhidze published two papers (arXiv:1009.5414 and arXiv:1108.4161) arguing that experimental evidence from quantum mechanics directly disproves the entropic origin of gravitation.

## The Argument

Kobakhidze cites two classes of experiments:

1. **Neutron interference experiments**: Neutrons passing through an interferometer in Earth's gravitational field show quantum interference patterns exactly as predicted by the Schrodinger equation with a standard gravitational potential.

2. **Gravitational bound states of neutrons**: Ultra-cold neutrons bouncing above a surface in Earth's gravitational field occupy discrete quantum energy levels, precisely matching predictions of standard quantum mechanics with a Newtonian gravitational potential.

## Why This Challenges Entropic Gravity

If gravity were fundamentally an entropic force (statistical/thermal in nature), quantum coherence effects like interference should be washed out by the thermal/statistical nature of the force. The fact that neutrons display pristine quantum mechanical behavior in gravitational fields suggests gravity is not reducible to a thermal/entropic phenomenon at the quantum level.

## Counterarguments

The criticism is not universally accepted:
- **Chaichian et al.** (arXiv:1104.4650) suggested a potential loophole in the argument in weak gravitational fields such as those in Earth-bound experiments
- **Tsallis entropy approaches**: Some researchers using non-additive entropy frameworks (Tsallis entropy) claimed compatibility between entropic gravity formalism and quantum experiments
- Kobakhidze responded (arXiv:1108.4161) that these criticisms are "shown to be invalid"

## Status

The debate remains unresolved. Kobakhidze's argument is the most cited experimental objection to entropic gravity, but proponents argue it misidentifies the regime in which the entropic description applies.
