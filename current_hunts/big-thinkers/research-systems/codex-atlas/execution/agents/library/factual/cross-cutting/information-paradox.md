# Black Hole Information Paradox: Current Status (2025-2026)

## Island Formula and Asymptotically Safe Quantum Gravity

Researchers from Beijing Institute of Technology employed asymptotically safe quantum gravity to examine the black hole information paradox. By incorporating "entanglement islands" -- regions inside the black hole that contribute to the entropy calculation -- they demonstrated that radiation entropy reaches saturation at the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy value, supporting unitary evolution and quantum information preservation. The study derived both the Page time and scrambling time by equating early- and late-time entropies, establishing when information recovery occurs during black hole evaporation. The framework predicts a minimum mass threshold below which no event horizon forms, suggesting stable black hole remnants rather than complete evaporation.

## Loop Quantum Gravity Resolution

Researchers from Sun Yat-sen University examined black hole behavior through loop quantum gravity, analyzing two distinct solutions to the equations of loop quantum gravity using the island prescription. Solution 1 shows the loop quantum gravity parameter enhances the barrier around the black hole, accelerating evaporation. Solution 2 predicts slower evaporation, potentially yielding a stable remnant or transition to a white hole. The research demonstrates that quantum extremal surfaces exist in Solution 1 geometries, and the island boundary's volume expands in tandem with the interior, offsetting the continued growth of radiation entropy. This preserves unitarity by shifting island boundaries and suppressing entropy growth.

## Unresolved Challenges

As documented in a July 2025 review (arXiv:2507.03778), the theoretical framework of black hole thermodynamics rests on approximations whose validity remains unproven in regimes where quantum gravitational effects become important. Persistent challenges include the trans-Planckian problem, backreaction effects, and the absence of experimental verification.

## QG+F Fakeon and Information

In QG+F, the fakeon prescription violates microcausality at Planck scales (Δt ~ 1/M₂ ~ 10⁻⁴³ s), providing a potential channel for information leakage during Hawking evaporation. The leakage rate would be ~(l_P/r_H)² per emission event — utterly negligible per Hawking quantum for astrophysical BHs, but cumulative leakage over the BH lifetime could potentially account for information recovery. No quantitative calculation exists. ## Cross-Cutting Significance

The information paradox remains the central cross-cutting problem in quantum gravity. The island formula and quantum extremal surface methods, originally developed in the context of AdS/CFT, have now been applied within asymptotic safety and loop quantum gravity, demonstrating that the tools developed in one approach can illuminate problems across the field.
