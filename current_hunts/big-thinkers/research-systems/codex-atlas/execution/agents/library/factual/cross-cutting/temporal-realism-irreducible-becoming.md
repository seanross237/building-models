# Temporal Realism: The Case for Irreducible Becoming

## Summary

Time — understood as the irreversible process of becoming, the passage from potentiality to actuality — may be the most fundamental structure of reality rather than an emergent phenomenon. The "block universe" is a mathematical artifact of a formalism that spatializes time to calculate. The Wheeler-DeWitt equation's "timeless" universe is a confession of the formalism's incompleteness, not a revelation about nature. This is the **counter-thesis** to the [time-from-entanglement synthesis](time-from-entanglement-synthesis.md), which argues time is emergent from static entanglement.

The case rests on four converging pillars: phenomenological (Bergson's durée), metaphysical (Whitehead's process philosophy), physical (Prigogine, causal set theory, QM measurement, cosmological history), and logical (time eliminativism is self-defeating).

## Philosophical Foundations

### Bergson's Durée vs. Temps

Henri Bergson (1859–1941) identified a critical distinction:

- **Durée (duration):** Lived, qualitative, continuous flow of experience. Each moment interpenetrates the next; the past is carried forward. Heterogeneous, indivisible — like a melody whose meaning is in the flow, not individual notes.
- **Temps (clock time):** Spatialized, quantitative, homogeneous medium science uses. Time as a line, moments as points, intervals as interchangeable. A *useful abstraction* but not time itself.

**Core argument:** Science, by its method of isolating variables and measuring repeatable quantities, operates at "the lowest limit of duration" — eliminating the qualitative becoming that makes time *time*. The eliminativist's claim that "physics reveals time is an illusion" is circular: it assumes the spatial model captures the full reality, then concludes that non-spatial features are "merely subjective."

Key works: *Time and Free Will* (1889), *Creative Evolution* (1907), *Duration and Simultaneity* (1922).

### Whitehead's Process Philosophy

Alfred North Whitehead (1861–1947) built a complete ontology in which **becoming is the fundamental category**.

- **Actual occasions:** The basic units of reality — momentary events of experience that come into being through *concrescence* (integrating data from predecessors into a new unity). Not things sitting in time; they *are* processes of becoming.
- **No static substrate:** Becoming is all the way down. No timeless "stuff" underlies change.
- **Creativity as ultimate:** The advance from disjunction (many data) to conjunction (new unity) — inherently temporal.
- **Perishing is real:** Each occasion, once complete, is finished. Cannot be re-entered. Gives time its irreversibility without entropy or statistics.
- **Fallacy of misplaced concreteness:** Treating abstractions (the coordinate "t") as fundamental reality. The block universe commits precisely this fallacy.

Key work: *Process and Reality* (1929).

## Physical Arguments for Temporal Realism

### Prigogine's Fundamental Irreversibility

Ilya Prigogine (1917–2003, Nobel 1977) argued that **irreversibility is fundamental, not statistical**:

- Research into far-from-equilibrium thermodynamics (dissipative structures, self-organization) showed irreversible processes are *constitutive of physical reality*, not artifacts of coarse-graining
- Developed non-unitary transformation theory building irreversibility into fundamental dynamics
- Distinguished **physics of being** (time-reversible, static, deterministic) from **physics of becoming** (time-irreversible, generates novelty and structure)
- Famous quote: "Irreversible processes created us; we did not create them"

**Fair assessment:** Prigogine's technical program remains controversial. Many physicists accept macroscopic irreversibility but argue it derives from time-symmetric microscopic laws plus boundary conditions. The debate is not settled, but the existence of a serious, Nobel-caliber program arguing for fundamental irreversibility gives temporal realism a genuine foothold in physics.

**Contrast with entanglement arrow:** The [entanglement-based arrow of time](arrow-of-time-from-entanglement.md) grounds irreversibility in entanglement growth within a fundamentally static state. Prigogine's program instead grounds irreversibility in the dynamics themselves — a different explanatory strategy.

Key works: *From Being to Becoming* (1980), *The End of Certainty* (1997).

### Causal Set Theory: Order as Fundamental

Causal set theory's sequential growth dynamics (Rideout & Sorkin 1999) is a **physical realization of temporal becoming**:

- The causal set *grows* — new elements are "born" one at a time, each with a definite set of causal ancestors
- Becoming is a physical process, not a pre-given block; the future does not yet exist
- Temporal-causal order is the most basic structure (more basic than spatial geometry): "Order + Number = Geometry" (Sorkin)
- The parallel with Whitehead is structural: actual occasions that prehend predecessors ≅ causal set elements born with ancestors

The CSG dynamics is governed by discrete general covariance and Bell causality, making the growth process compatible with relativistic causality. See [dynamics.md](../causal-set-theory/dynamics.md) for full technical details.

### Quantum Measurement as Becoming

On collapse interpretations (Copenhagen, GRW, Penrose objective reduction), wave function collapse is an instance of **genuine becoming** — the creation of new facts:

- Collapse creates irreversible facts not derived from thermodynamics or initial conditions
- The transition from possible to actual is intrinsically temporal (potentiality → actuality)
- **Penrose's objective reduction:** Superpositions of different spacetime geometries are physically unstable, collapsing at the Planck mass scale — gravity itself drives the process of becoming

**Fair assessment:** This argument depends on the interpretation of QM. Everett (many-worlds) avoids collapse at the cost of a vast static ontology. But the most straightforward reading of the quantum formalism involves genuine creation of new facts.

### Cosmological History as Genuine Temporal Sequence

The entire practice of cosmology presupposes genuine temporal sequence: Big Bang → nucleosynthesis → recombination → structure formation. Cosmologists don't merely describe a static 4D manifold; they explain *how one state gave rise to another*. The explanatory structure is inherently temporal and causal.

## Key Research Programs

### Smolin-Cortês Temporal Naturalism

Lee Smolin (*Time Reborn*, 2013) and Marina Cortês developed **temporal naturalism**:

1. Time, in the sense of the succession of present moments, is real
2. Laws of nature evolve in time
3. Space is emergent; time is fundamental

**Methodological advantage:** Makes the character of physical law itself a subject of scientific explanation rather than brute stipulation. If the block universe is correct and laws are fixed, initial conditions are unexplainable brute facts.

Key works: Smolin, "Temporal Naturalism" (2013, arXiv:1310.8539); Smolin & Unger, *The Singular Universe and the Reality of Time* (2015).

### Ellis's Evolving Block Universe

George Ellis proposes spacetime as a **growing structure**:

- The past is real (it has happened, is definite)
- The present is the edge of becoming (where indefinite future becomes definite)
- The future does not yet exist
- Compatible with relativity via observer-dependent local surfaces of becoming

Key work: Ellis & Cortês, "The Evolving Block Universe and the Meshing Together of Times" (2014, arXiv:1407.7243).

### Maudlin's Directed Topology

Tim Maudlin developed the **Theory of Linear Structures** (*New Foundations for Physical Geometry*, 2014):

- Replaces the foundational notion of standard topology (open sets) with **directed continuous lines**
- Standard topology has no built-in way to distinguish temporal direction; Maudlin's framework makes directionality primitive
- Argues that temporal ordering — not spatial openness — is the more fundamental geometric concept
- The passage and direction of time are fundamental features of reality

## The Self-Defeating Argument Against Eliminativism

Time eliminativism ("time is an illusion") is **performatively self-defeating**:

1. An *illusion* requires temporal process (believing at one time what is false at another)
2. If becoming is not real, *change* is not real — but the claim was never "made" (making a claim is temporal)
3. *Explaining* something requires reasoning, inference, arriving at conclusions — all temporal activities
4. The eliminativist is like someone saying "I am not speaking" — the act negates the content

**Deeper point:** Temporal becoming is a precondition for rational discourse itself. Any physics that eliminates time must explain how time-bound creatures formulate physics at all — inevitably reintroducing temporal structure.

## What the Thesis Explains Well

1. **Why time feels like passage:** Because it *is* passage. No "hard problem of temporal experience."
2. **Why there is an arrow of time:** Becoming is intrinsically directional. You can become but cannot un-become. Not contingent on initial conditions.
3. **Why there is a "now":** The present is the boundary of the real — the growing tip of the causal set, the edge of the evolving block, the moment of concrescence. Metaphysically special.
4. **Why time differs from space:** Time involves becoming; space involves extension. Encoded in the Lorentzian signature (−,+,+,+). You can turn around in space; you cannot turn around in time.

## Genuine Difficulties

### The Relativity of Simultaneity (BIGGEST CHALLENGE)

The Rietdijk-Putnam argument: if different observers disagree about what is "present," there is no objective "now" and thus no objective becoming.

**Three response strategies:**

**(a) Local becoming (Maudlin):** Becoming does not require a global "now." Each worldline has its own proper time with a definite objective temporal order. Passage is local, parameterized by invariant proper time.

**(b) Observer-dependent surfaces (Ellis):** The growing boundary of the evolving block is not a single global hypersurface but a family of local surfaces respecting causal structure.

**(c) Causal structure, not simultaneity (CST):** The partial order is Lorentz-invariant. Sequential growth respects causal structure without privileging any foliation.

**Honest assessment:** These responses are defensible but require giving up a universal "now" in favor of local, relational becoming — more sophisticated but less intuitive.

### The Mechanism Problem

If becoming is fundamental, what determines *what* becomes? Different answers: Whitehead's eternal objects and initial aims (metaphysically rich, scientifically speculative), Smolin's evolving laws (testable in principle), CSG stochastic rules (mathematically precise), Prigogine's dissipative structures. No fully satisfying account yet — but the block universe has its own version (why *this* block?).

### Mathematical Elegance

The block universe is mathematically cleaner (coordinate-free 4D geometry). The temporal realist picture (growing blocks, local becomings) is messier. Counter: mathematical elegance is not a reliable guide to ontology.

### Retrocausation

Some QM interpretations (transactional, two-state vector) involve retrocausal influences. Would challenge irreversible directional becoming if genuine.

## Assessment

**Convergence argument:** The case rests on no single line of evidence but on simultaneous support from phenomenology, process metaphysics, causal set theory, thermodynamic irreversibility, quantum mechanics, cosmological history, and the logical incoherence of the alternative.

**Strongest point:** The convergence itself — each argument can be questioned individually; together they are formidable.

**Weakest point:** The relativity of simultaneity. Requires a local, relational conception of becoming that is philosophically defensible but goes against the intuitive picture of a single advancing present.

**Relation to time-from-entanglement thesis:** These are genuinely competing positions. The entanglement thesis says time is emergent from a fundamentally static entangled state; temporal realism says time (as becoming) is irreducible and the "static state" is a formalism artifact. The debate is not settled — both positions have strong arguments and genuine difficulties.
