## Result

At the Planck scale, the classicality budget is almost zero. S_max ~ 4.5 bits. Classical reality in the quantum Darwinism sense cannot exist for any fact more complex than a few bits.

## Exact Values

| Bound | Nats | Bits |
|-------|------|------|
| Bekenstein | 2*pi (exact) | 9.064 |
| Holographic | pi (exact) | 4.532 |
| Effective (tighter) | pi | **4.532** |

The holographic bound is tighter by exactly a factor of 2 — this is the ONLY system where the holographic bound dominates.

## Classicality Budget at Planck Scale

| S_T (bits) | R_delta (max) | Interpretation |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | 3.53 | Barely classical — ~3 observers max |
| 2.27 | 1.0 | Critical: exactly 1 independent observer |
| 4.53 | 0 | No redundancy at all |
| >= 5 | Negative | **Impossible** — fact cannot exist classically |

## Physical Significance

This is the most physically meaningful prediction of the classicality budget:

1. **Classicality fails naturally** at the Planck scale without any ad hoc assumptions. The failure emerges from the combination of finite entropy + quantum Darwinism information requirements.

2. **Critical fact size = 2.27 bits.** Facts requiring more than ~2 bits have at most 1 observer (no independent confirmation possible).

3. **Facts requiring >= 5 bits cannot exist as classical facts at all** — the information budget is exhausted before a single redundant copy can be made.

4. **Consistency check:** This aligns with the expectation from quantum gravity that spacetime itself loses classical character at the Planck scale. The classicality budget provides a quantitative, information-theoretic derivation of this expectation.

5. **R_delta ~ 3.5 for a minimal (1-bit) fact** is barely above the threshold for classical objectivity (which typically requires R_delta >> 1). Even the simplest possible classical fact can only be verified by ~3 independent observers.
