# The Reuter Fixed Point

The Reuter fixed point is the nontrivial (non-Gaussian) UV fixed point of the gravitational renormalization group flow, first identified by Martin Reuter in 1998 using the functional renormalization group. It is the central object in the asymptotic safety program for quantum gravity.

## Reuter's 1998 Breakthrough

In a paper submitted in May 1996 (arXiv:hep-th/9605030) and published as Physical Review D, volume 57, pages 971-985 (1998), Martin Reuter constructed a scale-dependent effective action for gravity and derived an exact nonperturbative evolution equation governing its renormalization group flow. The evolution equation is invariant under general coordinate transformations and satisfies modified BRS Ward-Identities.

## Key Findings

- In 2+epsilon dimensions, nonperturbative corrections to the beta-function of Newton's constant were derived and its dependence on the cosmological constant was investigated.
- In 4 dimensions, Einstein gravity was found to be "antiscreening" -- Newton's constant increases at large distances.
- The work identified a nontrivial fixed point in four-dimensional quantum gravity within truncated functional renormalization group equations.

## Fixed Point Values (Einstein-Hilbert Truncation)

The fixed point is characterized by dimensionless couplings g* (Newton's constant) and lambda* (cosmological constant). Values depend on the computational scheme:

- **Euclidean (standard):** g* ~ 0.34, lambda* ~ 0.3
- **Lorentzian (covariant):** g* ~ 1.15, lambda* ~ 0.42
- **ADM formalism:** g* ~ 0.21, lambda* ~ 0.3
- **Typical range across truncations:** g* ~ 0.1-1.2, lambda* ~ 0.1-0.4

Both g* and lambda* are positive at the fixed point.

## Critical Exponents

The critical exponents theta characterize the RG flow near the fixed point. They come as a complex conjugate pair, indicating a spiraling approach to the fixed point:

- **Euclidean:** theta = 1.55 +/- 3.83i
- **Lorentzian:** theta = 5.11 +/- 11.59i
- **ADM:** theta = 0.94 +/- 3.1i

The positive real part (Re(theta) > 0) of both eigenvalues means there are exactly two UV-relevant directions, corresponding to Newton's constant and the cosmological constant.

## Properties of the Reuter Fixed Point

The Reuter fixed point has the following established properties:

- It features "non-vanishing interactions" that enable gravity to remain interacting at the smallest length scales
- It provides scale invariance, eliminating divergences that plague conventional quantum gravity approaches
- Positive, finite fixed point couplings with no blow-up or pathological behavior in the flow
- Robustness against truncation scheme modifications
- It has been confirmed across multiple independent computational frameworks

## Stability

The fixed point has been found consistently across increasingly sophisticated truncations, from the Einstein-Hilbert truncation to truncations including R^2, R_mu_nu R^mu_nu, and higher-order operators. The product g* x lambda* is approximately gauge-independent.

## Significance

This work can be considered the essential breakthrough in asymptotic safety related studies on quantum gravity as it provides for the first time the possibility of nonperturbative computations for arbitrary spacetime dimensions. The Reuter fixed point may provide a unified description of all forces of nature and have profound consequences for our understanding of physics inside black holes.
