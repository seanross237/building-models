# Cosmological Constant Problem: Current Status (2025-2026)

## The Core Problem

The cosmological constant problem represents the most severe discrepancy in theoretical physics. The minimum disagreement is approximately 55 orders of magnitude (considering only the Standard Model), extending to over 120 orders of magnitude when accounting for Grand Unified Theories or Planck-scale contributions. For context, the Higgs boson alone contributes roughly 244 trillion GeV^4 to vacuum energy, yet observations show rho_vac approximately 10^{-47} GeV^4. Resolving this requires adjustment "with such fantastic precision...to within an accuracy of 55 digits."

Source: https://cosmoversetensions.eu/learn-cosmology/quantum-vacuum-the-cosmological-constant-problem/

## Running Vacuum Model

The most promising approach involves recognizing that the cosmological constant is not actually constant. Through proper renormalization of quantum field theory in curved spacetime, when calculating vacuum energy between two cosmic epochs with different Hubble rates, the problematic m^4 terms from both the classical Lambda term and quantum zero-point energy cancel. What remains is a smooth function rho_vac(H) proportional to H-squared. This framework eliminates fine-tuning because "the offending quartic powers just disappeared from the final result." The Running Vacuum Model predicts that Lambda varies dynamically across cosmic history and that the equation of state parameter w_vac becomes dynamical rather than fixed at -1.

Source: https://cosmoversetensions.eu/learn-cosmology/quantum-vacuum-the-cosmological-constant-problem/

A presentation at Perimeter Institute (April 2025) proposed that a calculation of the vacuum energy reproduces the observed value and points to a completely new understanding of quantum gravity as "gravitized quantum theory." ## Spacetime Foam and Planck-Scale Inhomogeneity

Recent work (Nesterov, 2024) suggests that accounting for Planck-scale inhomogeneities may hide vacuum energy effects through local curvature fluctuations, potentially explaining the observed small value through "exponential suppression, not miraculous cancellation."

Source: https://www.emergentmind.com/topics/cosmological-constant-problem

## Algebraic Reformulations

Modern approaches eliminate fundamental spacetime backgrounds, interpreting the cosmological constant as "a parameter labeling the underlying symmetry algebra, not as a dynamical energy density."

## QG+F / Agravity Analysis

**Old CC problem:** In agravity (classically scale-invariant QG+F), dimensional transmutation via the Coleman-Weinberg mechanism gives Λ = 0 at tree level. Combined with unimodular gravity (Salvio 2024, PLB 855, 138920), the old CC problem is genuinely solved — vacuum energy does not gravitate, Λ is an integration constant.

**New CC problem:** NOT solved in QG+F. One-loop corrections give Λ ~ f₂⁴M_Pl⁴/(16π²) ~ 10⁻³⁴ M_Pl⁴, still ~10⁸⁸ times too large. Cascading dimensional transmutation was explored (multiple CW-like stages to produce M_Pl/Λ^{1/4} ~ 10³⁰) but **fails physically**: QG+F has no IR-active coupling that confines at meV scales. Salvio invokes anthropic reasoning. See `../quadratic-gravity-fakeon/standard-model-and-agravity.md` for full analysis.

**Key theoretical results:** Kugo (2020) proved classical scale invariance is necessary but insufficient for the CC. Weinberg's no-go generalizes: no continuous symmetry in a gravitational theory can protect the CC — including scale invariance.

Sources: Salvio (2024), PLB 855, 138920; Kugo (2020), arXiv:2004.01868; Maggiore et al. (2015), arXiv:1506.06217

## Cross-Cutting Significance

The cosmological constant problem sits at the intersection of quantum field theory, general relativity, and every approach to quantum gravity. String theory addresses it through the landscape of vacua (10^{500} possible solutions). Loop quantum gravity and causal sets each offer distinct mechanisms. Causal set theory notably predicted a small, fluctuating cosmological constant before the 1998 discovery of accelerating expansion. The problem forces every quantum gravity program to confront how quantum vacuum energy gravitates, making it one of the most demanding cross-cutting tests of any proposed theory.
