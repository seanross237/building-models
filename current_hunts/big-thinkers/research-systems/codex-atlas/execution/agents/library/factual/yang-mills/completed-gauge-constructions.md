## Why These Matter

These are the only complete constructions of interacting gauge theories satisfying Osterwalder-Schrader axioms. They demonstrate that the constructive program CAN work for simpler models, providing both proof of concept and methodological templates.

## Brydges-Fröhlich-Seiler: 2D Abelian Higgs Model (1979-1981)

Three-part series:
- Part I: General results (Ann. Phys. 121, 1979)
- Part II: Convergence of lattice approximation (CMP 71, 1980)
- Part III: 2D abelian Higgs model without cutoffs (CMP 79, 1981)

**This is the ONLY complete construction of an interacting gauge theory satisfying OS axioms.** Mass gap proved separately by Balaban-Brydges-Imbrie-Jaffe (Ann. Phys. 158, 1984).

## King: 3D Higgs Model Continuum Limit (1986-1988)

Established existence of the continuum limit of the three-dimensional Higgs model using phase cell renormalization. Notable success showing the full program works for simpler models.

## Gross / Driver: Abelian Yang-Mills

- Gross (1983): Continuum limit of U(1) Yang-Mills
- Driver (1989): Extended to 4D U(1) lattice gauge theory

**Limitation:** The abelian case lacks the key difficulties of the non-abelian theory (no asymptotic freedom, no mass gap problem).
