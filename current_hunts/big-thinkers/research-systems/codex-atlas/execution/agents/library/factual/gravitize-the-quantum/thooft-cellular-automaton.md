# 't Hooft's Cellular Automaton Interpretation (CAI)

## Core Idea

Quantum mechanics is not fundamental but is a mathematical tool for describing an underlying deterministic system. At the Planck scale, physics is governed by a classical cellular automaton — a discrete, deterministic dynamical system on a lattice. QM emerges as the effective description when coarse-graining over Planck-scale details.

## Mechanism

1. The universe is a cellular automaton: lattice of cells with discrete states evolving via deterministic local rules
2. These deterministic systems can be exactly mapped into quantum Hilbert spaces using "beables" — ontological states forming an orthonormal basis
3. The quantum Hamiltonian generating time evolution is explicitly constructed and produces standard QM for beable states
4. Superpositions are "non-ontic" — mathematical but not physical. Only beable states are physically real
5. Information loss at the Planck scale generates equivalence classes that give rise to quantum behavior at larger scales

## How It Handles Bell's Theorem

Requires **superdeterminism**: measurement settings are correlated with hidden variables because both trace to common causal ancestors in the initial conditions. 't Hooft argues this is natural for deterministic dynamics. Critics (Zeilinger, Bell) argue it destroys scientific methodology.

## Status (2025)

**Van Berkel et al. (2025, *Quantum*)** demonstrated computationally that deterministic dynamics can reproduce all observed quantum correlations. This removes the empirical objection (deterministic models CAN match Bell test data) but not the philosophical objection about superdeterminism.

## Does It "Gravitize the Quantum"?

**No.** The CAI derives QM from generic determinism, not gravity/spacetime specifically. Gravity enters only speculatively as a possible Planck-scale automaton structure. The framework is "determinism → QM" not "gravity → QM."
