- **comparison-exploration-pattern.md** — When two approaches seem related, a direct comparison exploration with a multi-dimensional framework and forced verdict ("same, complementary, or distinct") is highly productive. *Updated: added Verlinde vs. T_U/T_dS comparison (E005) — detailed comparison revealed RAR failure and deep-MOND-only limitation not visible in the initial survey.*
- **math-explorer-dimensional-analysis.md** — Math Explorer + dimensional analysis is an excellent combination for "is this dimensionally possible?" questions; produces decisive quantitative verdicts with plots.
- **decisive-negative-pivot.md** — When dimensional analysis produces discrepancies of >10 orders of magnitude, it's a hard kill; pivot immediately to what CAN work at the right scale.
- **scale-spread-in-numerical-surveys.md** — In numerical survey explorations, the spread of scales (Planck to cosmological) matters more than many systems at similar scales; reveals where bounds are tight vs. trivial.
- **gap-finding-in-existing-programs.md** — Identifying open problems and gaps in existing research programs is as valuable as inventing new theories; the "what's already been computed" comparison table format makes gaps immediately visible.
- **multi-ansatz-sweep-pattern.md** — When a problem has 2–4 mathematically similar candidate ansatze, test all of them in one exploration; rules out N-1 candidates in a single pass and produces a diagnostic comparison table. *Updated: Dense Fine Sweep variant (RH s002-E005): for non-monotone parameter behavior, add a fine grid around the suspected extremum — coarse sweep missed the true maximum (p=809 vs p=997) and underestimated neighbor fluctuations (±0.4).*
- **dual-mechanism-robustness.md** — When two independent physical mechanisms produce the same phenomenological result (via different assumptions and mathematics), that is the strongest form of robustness evidence; worth a dedicated confirmation exploration.
- **sequence-computation-approaches.md** — When the goal is to compute something by two methods, sequence them: verify Method 1 against an independent baseline first, then compare Method 2 against the verified result. Simultaneous attempts can't cross-validate each other.
- **standard-explorer-for-literature-surveys.md** — Use standard explorers (not math explorers) for literature surveys: ~20 min vs. 50+ min, equivalent depth. Reserve math explorers for computation, dimensional analysis, code execution.
- **investigate-why-on-discrepancies.md** — When results disagree with theoretical predictions, investigate WHY rather than just reporting the discrepancy. The explanation is often the most interesting finding (e.g., SED O(β) vs O(β²) gap → Langevin vs ALD distinction became the central result).
- **adversarial-check-between-phases.md** — Run a lightweight adversarial check after Phase 2 (before Phase 3 computation begins); early reframing is more valuable than corrections after major compute is committed. *Updated (E008): upgraded to full standard-test adversarial exploration early.* **UPDATED (vasseur-pressure E009): timing refinement — run adversarial AFTER key mechanism identified/tested but BEFORE extended tests. UPDATED (vasseur-pressure E010): adversarial -> targeted follow-up variant — adversarial identifies weakest link, then a single targeted exploration definitively tests it (E010 confirmed E009's weakest-claim assessment via perturbed-ABC sweep).**
- **check-algebra-before-multi-approach.md** — Before designing multiple exploration approaches to derive a "missing factor" from a ratio formula, pre-screen algebraically: if both numerator and denominator share the same structural factor (e.g., 2π), that factor cancels identically and no internal mechanism can restore it. Don't send 4 approaches when algebra predicts failure — spend the time on the one external lead that might work.
- **sign-flip-diagnostic-for-critical-thresholds.md** — NEW (SED E007): When two variants bracket a qualitative threshold (one undershoots, one overshoots), the sign reversal locates the critical parameter via linear interpolation. n=2 (negative Δe) + n=3 (positive Δe) → n*≈2.61. Reusable: design variants to straddle the expected threshold; treat the crossover value as the primary finding.
- **staged-computation-goals.md** — NEW (amplituhedron E006): Structure math explorer computation goals as 4-stage sequential pipelines where each stage verifies before the next builds on it. Enables independent debuggability, physics sanity checks per stage, and emergent insights not in the original goal.
- **parallel-math-explorer-explorations.md** — NEW (amplituhedron E005+E006): When two math explorer computations are logically independent, run them simultaneously. Each takes ~20 min; parallel execution gives ~2× wall-clock saving with no quality cost.
- **verify-unexpected-findings-immediately.md** — NEW (yang-mills s002 E005-006): When a computation produces an unexpected major finding, design an immediate stress-test exploration targeting the most plausible failure mode (different dimension, adversarial configs). Include a direct comparison table; treat outcome as decisive. E006 verified E005's 45× Hessian slack in 4D (138× confirmed) and adversarially (≥176× across all search strategies).
- **work-backward-from-constraint.md** — NEW (thermal-time strategy-001 meta-exploration-002): For constructive explorations, frame the task as "what theory/value satisfies this constraint?" rather than forward-computing predictions. Produces definitive answers anchored to constraints and unexpected byproducts (e.g., no-go theorems, uniqueness conditions). Contrast: forward framing generates correct calculations that may not resolve the key question; backward framing forces a definitive derivation. Structure: (1) identify the constraint, (2) derive what satisfies it, (3) check consistency, (4) allow unexpected results.
- **include-trivial-control-checks.md** — NEW (thermal-time meta-003), UPDATED (vasseur-pressure E007): Every computation exploration should include one "should equal X by construction" check (different code path, limit case, or symmetry). The thermal-time E003 control check C_global = C_full (at machine zero for all λ) confirmed that the 82.7% discrepancy was physical, not a bug. Cost: near zero. Payoff: immediate trust or bug detection. **NEW variants (E007):** known-answer validation at exact-solution conditions (t=0 Beltrami sanity check caught sign error propagated from prior code), and verify mathematical identity before building measurements (div(u_below) != 0 invalidated prescribed Lamb decomposition).
- **structural-vs-quantitative-discrepancy.md** — NEW (thermal-time meta-003): When two predictions disagree, diagnose whether the discrepancy is structural (different signal topology — wrong number of peaks, different symmetry class) or quantitative (scale factor, coefficient). Parameter tuning cannot fix structural discrepancies — they require a different model. In E003, C_full had two FFT peaks and C_local had one — no value of ω_eff can make a single sinusoid match a beating signal. Ask in goals: "Is the discrepancy structural or quantitative?"
- **require-trend-tabulation-for-negative-results.md** — NEW (RH s003-meta-004): When testing a scaling law or universality claim, require a table of the metric at each parameter value + direct test for monotone trend. Makes negative results decisive: "β_max = 1.32, 1.15, 1.02 (decreasing)" is a clean refutation; "results vary" is not.
- **split-search-from-synthesis.md** — NEW (RH s002-meta-008): For explorations requiring both iterative literature searches AND structured writeup, split into two: (1) search exploration (produce raw dump, write as you go), (2) synthesis exploration (read dump, write structured report, no new searches). Proposed architectural fix for the research-buffering stall pattern (3 consecutive failures: RH E006-E008). Not yet confirmed by successful split execution.
- **useful-failure-extracts-secondary-results.md** — NEW (YM s003-meta-s003-007): When an exploration's main goal fails (e.g., prove X when X is false), the exploration often produces valuable secondary results: algebraic invariants (B_□ B_□^T = 4I₃), analytical proofs for special cases (Δ = 14(cosε−1) ≤ 0), structural constants (Haar average E[M(Q)] = 6I). Design goals to capture secondary output. Complements decisive-negative-pivot and allow-explorer-synthesis.
- **gradient-ascent-on-projected-quantity.md** — NEW (YM s003-meta-006): When testing whether a bound holds on a specific eigenspace, run gradient ascent on the projected quantity (e.g., λ_max(P^T R P)) rather than the full matrix. More informative (shows margin from violation), computationally cheaper, and tests the correct target. YM s003-E006: gradient ascent on 9×9 P^T R P stayed at −8 to −11 (margin 8–11 from 0), cleaner than full 192×192 λ_max which gave margin 1.6.
- **lattice-convergence-fixed-frequency.md** — NEW (thermal-time s002-meta-001): For lattice QFT convergence tests, use fixed physical frequency (not fixed mode index) across N values; mode-0 convergence gives IR artifacts (omega_0 -> 0 as N -> infinity). Multi-angle strategy: test both fixed-mode and fixed-frequency to distinguish genuine physics from artifacts. Thermal-time E003: mode-0 showed fake convergence N^{-0.46}, fixed-freq showed true divergence N^{+0.33}.
- **verify-identity-generalization-before-extending.md** — NEW (yang-mills-conjecture meta-E008): When extending a proof to a larger space, FIRST check whether the key identities (trace identity, budget identity, etc.) still hold. The E006 staggered proof relied on a trace identity (c + Tr(P) = 64) that fails for general spatial patterns (varies by factor ~3). A quick test at the outset would have ruled out the approach immediately.
- **consider-sdp-sos-for-constrained-proofs.md** — NEW (yang-mills-conjecture meta-E008): For matrix-inequality proofs over constrained spaces (λ_max(M|_V) ≤ c), consider SDP/SOS formulations from the start. The constraint structure (essential for the bound to hold) is naturally handled by SDP relaxations, while algebraic approaches struggle to exploit constraints. E008: unconstrained eigenvalue reaches ~21 but bound is 16 on constrained space; enormous numerical margin (best adversarial: 15.997) suggests certificate exists. *Updated (S002-E003): tight-bound variant — when infimum = 0, loose inequalities (Cauchy-Schwarz, AM-GM) are ruled out; prefer SDP/SOS or exact algebraic decomposition.*
- **budget-adversarial-for-high-dim.md** — NEW (yang-mills-conjecture S002-meta-E003): Budget adversarial optimization from the start for verification in high-dimensional parameter spaces (≥20D). Random uniform sampling has poor coverage: 200K random tests on SO(3)^10 (30D) missed a counterexample with min eigenvalue = -2.13 that 20 adversarial runs found immediately. Always pair random testing with adversarial optimization (≥10 independent starts). *Updated (S003-E004): domain-specific structured starts variant — include physics-motivated extremal configuration classes (e.g., anti-instantons for Hessian eigenvalue bounds) as GD seeds; anti-instanton starts found 63% more negative eigenvalue than random GD.*
- **special-case-proof-guides-generalization.md** — NEW (yang-mills-conjecture S002-meta-E004): When a proof works for a special case, analyze WHY (the structural property that made it easy), then frame the general case as "restore that property + bound the deviation." E004: sum_S proof on rotation axes worked because u=v (bilinear → quadratic); deviation u−v = −(I−R^T)T has same structure as baseline f(R,T) terms, suggesting the natural generalization route.
- **check-affine-structure-before-bounding.md** — NEW (yang-mills-conjecture S002-meta-E005): Before attempting bounds, check whether the quantity is linear/affine in some parameters. Affine dependence enables per-parameter independent minimization with closed-form bounds. E005: M9 affine in D (verified 3.5e-15) decomposed 18D minimization into 6 independent per-pair Cauchy-Schwarz problems, unlocking the sum_S ≥ 0 proof after 7 prior approaches failed.
- **verify-counterexample-before-investigating.md** — NEW (yang-mills-conjecture S002-meta-E007): When finding a potential counterexample, the FIRST priority is verifying it against the exact problem statement — not investigating consequences. Check that the mathematical object being computed matches the conjecture's definition (representation, convention, normalization). A false counterexample can derail strategy. Protocol: stop → re-read exact statement → verify computation matches → resolve ambiguity → only then investigate.
- **decompose-correction-into-psd-and-indefinite.md** — NEW (yang-mills-validation S002-meta-E002): When analyzing a matrix inequality C = B − A ≥ 0, decompose C into PSD + indefinite components (C = C₊ + C₋). Reveals the mechanism by which the inequality holds (or fails), quantifies margins, and guides proof strategy. Protocol: identify sign-definite components, verify decomposition, check at relevant eigenspace, use ratio C₊/|C₋| to assess margin.
- **definition-extraction-gates-computation.md** — NEW (vasseur-pressure E001), UPDATED (anatomy-of-averaged-ns-blowup-firewall meta-001, exact-ns-no-near-closed-tao-circuit meta-001, exact-ns-hidden-precursor-firewall meta-001, exact-ns-phase-locking-firewall meta-001): Before computation phases, run a definition-extraction exploration ("Phase 0") reading primary sources to verify the team's understanding of the target quantity, event, or theorem object. Prevents the most wasteful failure: computing the wrong thing due to incorrect mental model. Vasseur-pressure: team assumed beta = pressure integrability; Phase 0 revealed beta = De Giorgi recurrence exponent. **Updates:** if Phase 0 only needs the mechanism architecture, partial primary-source access can still be sufficient; if architecture is already known locally, one exact law from primary sources can be the missing layer that closes the definition gate; if exact-PDE post-processing plus observability conventions already defines the object, prefer that over inventing a reduced model; if a predecessor scaffold exists, reuse only the parts that preserve the new exact ledger and rank surviving candidates by coupling to the load-bearing ledger rather than raw concentration.
- **test-cancellation-ratio-observables-on-two-channel-clusters.md** — For observables of the form `(signed net transfer)/(absolute transfer mass)`, test the first exact support with two inputs feeding the same receiver mode. Opposite-phase tuning can keep the target transfer positive while driving the ratio to zero, so larger-support or delayed-threshold campaigns are unjustified unless a new invariant appears.
- **coefficient-weighted-amplitude-level-leakage.md** — **[NEW, exact-ns-no-near-closed-tao-circuit meta-001]** For exact-support mechanism audits, define leakage using exact coefficient-amplitude contributions, not triad counts or packet energies. Use packet coarse-graining only as a backup summary after the amplitude-level definition exists.
- **sufficient-ic-diversity-for-outliers.md** — NEW (vasseur-pressure E002): For measurement campaigns, use 5+ ICs covering different structural classes (symmetric/random, 2D-like/3D, special algebraic structure, low/high intermittency). Fewer ICs risk missing qualitatively different behavior. ABC (Beltrami) was the 5th IC and produced the most important finding (beta_eff = 1.01 vs 0.35-0.73 for others). Distinct from anticipate-symmetry-degeneracies (avoiding bad ICs) and require-independent-verification-baseline (checking same answer).
- **reconcile-notation-before-falsification.md** — NEW (far-field-pressure-harmonic-loophole meta-001): When a surviving lead is inherited across multiple vocabularies or decompositions, reconcile notation before falsification. Prevents testing the wrong object. Example: Vasseur's harmonic `P_{k1}` vs bad local `P_{k21}` vs later Wolf-style far/near split. Distinct from decomposition-audit-before-attacking-barrier (one proof's chain) and complementary to preload-context-from-prior-work (load context + verify compatibility).
- **decomposition-audit-before-attacking-barrier.md** — NEW (vasseur-pressure S2-E001): Always run a line-by-line proof reading + sharpness classification before attacking a mathematical barrier. Understanding the proof structure first prevents wasting budget on provably closed directions. S2-E001: narrowed 5 open Track B directions to 1, saving 3+ explorations. Deliver: step-by-step chain, sharpness classification, sensitivity table, free parameter catalog, attack surface narrowing. Distinct from definition-extraction-gates-computation (right quantity vs. right step) and gap-finding (literature landscape vs. single proof structure).
- **test-generalization-before-concluding.md** — NEW (vasseur-pressure E010): When a mechanism is demonstrated for an exact special case (eigenstate, symmetry, exact solution), always test whether it extends to perturbations before treating it as a strategy conclusion. "Mechanism works for exact X" does NOT imply "mechanism works for near-X" — eigenfunction properties can be structurally fragile. E010: Beltrami B_k = O(2^{-k}) destroyed by 1% perturbation. Design a perturbation sweep (eps = 0.01 to 0.50) before concluding. Distinct from verify-identity-generalization (proof identities, not empirical mechanisms) and adversarial-check (diagnostic, not treatment).
- **model-pde-comparison-for-mechanism-identification.md** — NEW (vasseur-pressure S2-E003): When investigating a mathematical barrier in a specific PDE, compare the barrier across structurally related model PDEs (different dimensions, dissipation orders, nonlinearities). Reveals whether the barrier is universal (same formula across PDEs — attack the mechanism) or PDE-specific (study what the successful PDE has that the target lacks). S2-E003: comparing beta across Burgers/2DNS/SQG/MHD/fractional NS produced two major insights in one exploration — universal formula beta = 1 + s/n AND SQG exception mechanism (drift structure, not Chebyshev). Protocol: identify barrier, list 4-6 model PDEs, compute barrier for each, tabulate, identify universal formula or systematic exceptions. Distinct from comparison-exploration-pattern (two approaches to one problem, not one approach across problems) and gap-finding (within one framework, not across).
- **toy-subsystem-isolation-inside-exact-network.md** — NEW (anatomy-of-averaged-ns-blowup-firewall meta-002): In toy-mechanism vs exact-PDE comparisons, the decisive test is whether the reduced subsystem stays isolated once the full exact interaction network is restored. Protocol: write the literal channel graph, restore the exact interaction law, enumerate forced spectators, separate standalone candidates from enforcement mechanisms, and test genuine small-parameter dominance.
- **test-improvability-before-pursuing-variations.md** — NEW (vasseur-pressure S2-E003): When a decomposition audit identifies a single target step as the potential weak link, the FIRST analytical exploration should test whether that target is independently improvable (i.e., improvement is possible without solving the entire original problem). If circular with the main problem, pivot immediately. S2-E003: 6 Chebyshev improvement routes all ruled out or circular in one exploration; improving L^{10/3} to L^4 Chebyshev requires H^{5/4} regularity (the Lions threshold = the original problem). Saved 3+ explorations on Lorentz/interpolation/support tricks. Protocol: enumerate improvement routes, classify each as (a) impossible, (b) insufficient, (c) circular, or (d) open; if all (a)/(b)/(c), target is not independently improvable — pivot. Distinct from decomposition-audit (identifies target, not viability) and decisive-negative-pivot (dimensional analysis, not circularity detection).
- **extract-precise-obstruction-from-failed-route.md** — NEW (vasseur-pressure S2-E004): When a promising route fails, extract the PRECISE obstruction — not just "it doesn't work." The precise failure point immediately suggests the next direction. S2-E004: three independent obstructions from the commutator route (no div-curl, remainder dominance, CRW vacuous) each generated specific next leads (frequency-localized De Giorgi, div-free approximation, unbounded-but-BMO multiplier). Protocol: identify all independent obstruction layers, state precise mathematical condition that fails, state what would need to change, rank resulting leads. Distinct from decisive-negative-pivot (decision to pivot vs. information extraction) and useful-failure-extracts-secondary-results (accidental findings vs. deliberate obstruction analysis).
- **numerical-spectral-dns-diagnostic.md** — NEW (vasseur-pressure S2-E004): Numerical spectral analysis (frequency-resolved energy decomposition) on DNS data is a powerful diagnostic for mathematical PDE questions. Reveals whether obstructions are high-frequency or low-frequency — information invisible to analytical norms. S2-E004: commutator part had faster spectral decay but remainder dominated high frequencies by 18x at k=20, immediately identifying the analytical bottleneck. Protocol: compute decomposition on DNS, measure L^2 norms per component, compute spectral energy distribution, compare decay rates, report dominant component at high frequencies. Distinct from sufficient-ic-diversity (IC selection vs. frequency analysis) and include-trivial-control-checks (consistency vs. diagnostic).
- **distinguish-constant-from-scaling-slack.md** — NEW (vasseur-pressure S2-E002): When measuring "tightness" of an inequality in a De Giorgi or similar iterative proof, the key question is NOT "is there slack?" but "does the slack scale with U_{k-1}^{-delta}?" Constant multiplicative slack (ratio independent of k and U) only affects C in the recurrence, NOT beta. Only slack scaling as U_{k-1}^{-delta} can improve beta. S2-E002: De Giorgi Chebyshev tightness ratios ~3-5x and k-independent — correctly interpreted as non-transformative, but this distinction should have been in the exploration goal from the start. Protocol: measure ratio vs k, test correlation with U_{k-1}, classify as constant or scaling. Distinct from test-improvability-before-pursuing-variations (circularity of proof step, not measurement interpretation) and decomposition-audit-before-attacking-barrier (identifies target, not interprets data).
- **bernstein-lp-poison-pill-3d.md** — NEW (vasseur-pressure S2-E005): The Bernstein inequality exchange rate 2^{3j/5} per LP block in 3D is fixed by dimensional analysis (Sobolev embedding cost L^2_{3/10} -> L^{10/3}) and destroys any frequency-localized improvement attempt for De Giorgi iterations. General lesson for LP-based approaches to 3D PDE barriers: the conversion cost from regularity (Sobolev/Besov) to integrability (L^p) is exactly what CZ theory already accounts for. Additionally, the k-dependent character change (resonance at low k -> paraproduct at high k) means no single analytical technique handles all De Giorgi levels optimally, which may explain the 4/3 barrier's robustness. When to apply: check if Bernstein exchange rate 2^{dj(1/2-1/p)} is comparable to spectral decay; if yes, LP cannot help. Distinct from extract-precise-obstruction-from-failed-route (information extraction vs. structural recognition), numerical-spectral-dns-diagnostic (diagnostic tool vs. interpretation), and distinguish-constant-from-scaling-slack (measurement interpretation vs. tool selection).
- **check-bypass-not-just-improve-bottleneck.md** — NEW (vasseur-pressure S2-E005): When a bottleneck tool limits progress, always ask "can we avoid this tool entirely?" before "can we improve it?" Protocol: (1) Can we avoid using this tool at all? (2) Is there a fundamentally different tool for the same job? (3) Can we improve the tool's output? S2-E001 through S2-E005 spent 5 explorations on question 3 (improving CZ or working around it); the strategizer should have asked question 1 after S2-E001 showed all CZ-based routes are tight. Distinct from ask-what-replaces-the-bottleneck (what new bottleneck after reformulation vs. whether to use the tool at all), decomposition-audit-before-attacking-barrier (which step is tight vs. whether the framework is right), and decisive-negative-pivot (when to stop vs. what direction to go).
- **tool-independence-stronger-than-single-failure.md** — NEW (vasseur-pressure S2-E006): When multiple independent analytical routes to the same exponent/barrier are computed, the result upgrades from "method X doesn't work" to "the barrier is intrinsic to the problem structure." Always compute at least two alternative routes when closing a direction. S2-E006: IBP (beta=1), H^1/BMO (beta=4/3), CRW (beta<=1) — the H^1/BMO match via different mechanism establishes tool-independence of 4/3. Distinct from extract-precise-obstruction (ONE failed route), check-bypass-not-just-improve-bottleneck (whether to avoid the tool), ask-what-replaces-the-bottleneck (new bottleneck after reformulation).
- **quantitative-comparison-reveals-why-standard-is-standard.md** — NEW (vasseur-pressure S2-E006): When closing a direction, compute the QUANTITATIVE gap between the closed route and the standard route. The magnitude and mechanism of the gap reveal why the standard method became standard, constraining future improvement attempts. S2-E006: IBP beta=1 vs CZ beta=4/3 — gap of exactly 1/3 = CZ consolidation gain (bilinear product mapped to single L^p function enables Chebyshev extraction). Shows CZ consolidation is ESSENTIAL, not just convenient. Distinct from extract-precise-obstruction (what fails vs. why standard is better), distinguish-constant-from-scaling-slack (slack type vs. inter-route gap mechanism), tool-independence-stronger-than-single-failure (structural conclusion vs. mechanism explanation).
