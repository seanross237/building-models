## Lesson

When an exploration reveals a connection between two frameworks that have not been connected in the existing literature (e.g., geometric regularity criteria + De Giorgi iteration), flag this explicitly in future goals. Such connections are potential novel territory because the individual frameworks are well-studied but their intersection may be uncharted.

## Evidence

- **vasseur-pressure strategy-001 exploration 006** — The exploration connected geometric regularity criteria (Constantin-Fefferman 1993, Beirao da Veiga-Berselli 2002) with Vasseur's De Giorgi iteration framework. No existing paper formulates a conditional regularity result in the specific form: "If the Beltrami deficit B(u) < epsilon_0, then the De Giorgi exponent satisfies beta > 3/2." This cross-framework connection was identified as potential novel territory precisely because both frameworks are well-established but have not been combined.

## When to apply

Whenever a literature survey or analysis identifies that two independently well-studied frameworks could be combined to produce a result that neither yields alone. The goal should explicitly state: "Determine whether the connection between Framework A and Framework B appears in the literature" and "If not, formulate what the combined result would look like."
