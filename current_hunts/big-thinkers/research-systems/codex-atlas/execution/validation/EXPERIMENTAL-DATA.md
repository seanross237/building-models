## Notes for Agents

1. **The Hubble tension is real.** Your theory doesn't need to resolve it, but if it naturally explains H_0 discrepancy, that's a big win.
2. **The cosmological constant problem is the hardest number in physics.** Lambda ~ 10^-122 in Planck units. If your theory predicts Lambda, you probably win a Nobel Prize.
3. **GW170817 killed many modified gravity theories** by constraining c_gw = c to 10^-15. If your theory modifies gravity, check this bound first.
4. **Proton decay limits are stringent.** If your theory predicts proton decay, make sure tau_p > 10^34 years.
5. **Spectral dimension d_s -> 2 in the UV** is a surprisingly robust prediction across multiple QG approaches (CDT, Asymptotic Safety, Horava-Lifshitz, LQG). If your theory also gets this, it's a good sign.
6. **When in doubt about a value,** check the PDG (pdg.lbl.gov), CODATA, or the relevant collaboration's latest publication.
