# Meta-Learning Note — Exploration 004

## What Worked Well
- **Standard explorer again excellent** for deep literature analysis. 362 lines, 16 cited papers, completed in ~25 min.
- **"Be brutally honest" instruction worked perfectly.** The explorer explicitly labeled claims as REFORMULATION, ESTABLISHED, SUPPORTED, SPECULATIVE, or REFUTED. The assessment table was excellent.
- **Found cutting-edge result** (March 2025 arXiv:2503.03805) that was highly relevant. The standard explorer's web search capability is valuable for finding recent papers.
- **The structured question format** (mechanism → deformations → consequences → assessment) produced a well-organized, logical report.

## What Didn't Work Well
- Minor: one arXiv number was incomplete ("arXiv:1901.xxxxx"). The explorer filled in the rest of the citation but couldn't find the exact arXiv ID for that paper.

## Lessons for Future Explorations
1. **"Be brutally honest" is the most effective framing for assessment tasks.** It produces much more useful output than neutral framing.
2. **Assessment tables (Claim | Evidence For | Evidence Against | Status) are excellent.** Should use in every exploration that involves evaluating claims.
3. **The standard explorer handles both survey AND analysis well** — the 4-part structure (mechanism, deformations, consequences, assessment) worked as a single exploration.
4. **The most valuable output was the SYNTHESIS** — the three genuine physical contributions, clearly separated from reformulation claims. This is what the mission needs.
