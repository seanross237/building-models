# Mission Validation Guide

## Tier 1: Novelty
- The theory is not a restatement of an existing approach (QG+F, string theory, LQG, etc.)
- If it shares elements with existing theories, it combines or extends them in a genuinely new way
- A domain expert would recognize this as a fresh contribution, not a literature summary

## Tier 2: Logical Consistency
- Internally consistent — no contradictions between claims
- Arguments follow from their premises
- Survives a devil's advocate attack without collapsing
- Assumptions are stated explicitly

## Tier 3: Explanatory Power
- Explains why gravity behaves the way it does — not just how
- Offers at least one explanation for an observed phenomenon that is clearer or more intuitive than existing accounts
- A thoughtful non-physicist can understand the core idea without a physics degree

## Tier 4: Compatibility with Reality
- Does not contradict established experimental results (gravitational waves travel at c, Newton's law at large scales, equivalence principle, etc.)
- Addresses what happens at extreme scales (Planck scale, black holes, Big Bang)
- Makes at least one prediction — even speculative — that could in principle distinguish it from existing theories
- When a prediction depends on a computation, **do the computation** rather than reasoning about what the result "should" be. Explorers have full shell access and can write code. A numerically computed result is stronger evidence than a dimensional analysis estimate.

## Tier 5: Depth
- The theory has internal structure — it's not just a single idea but a framework that can be explored further
- It handles edge cases and hard questions rather than dodging them
- It suggests new questions worth investigating
