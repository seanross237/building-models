# Strategy 002 Final Report: Focused Theory from QG+F's Explanatory Gaps

## Executive Summary

Over 10 explorations using a "gap-mine → concept-sprint → deep-build → harden" methodology, this strategy attempted to build a focused novel theory around QG+F's most fertile explanatory gap. The result is **not a complete theory**, but a set of genuinely novel observations about the physical interpretation of QG+F's fakeon prescription, anchored by one publishable mathematical finding.

**The main finding:** The fakeon propagator equals the Wheeler propagator at tree level, and causal set dynamics independently produce the Wheeler propagator for unstable modes (Belenchia, Benincasa & Liberati, JHEP 2015). This connection is absent from the literature — nobody has bridged the causal set and QG+F research programs through this mathematical identity. This is the **Wheeler propagator bridge**, the strategy's most concrete novel contribution.

**Honest verdict:** The strategy **barely meets its minimum success criterion** (a focused result that explains something more intuitively than QG+F) but does **not** meet the good success criterion (testable predictions) or breakthrough criterion. The Wheeler bridge is a genuine observation worth a short paper, but it is interpretive, not predictive.

## The Novel Findings

### 1. The Wheeler Propagator Bridge (Publishable)

The fakeon propagator G_fakeon = P.V.(1/(p² - m²)) equals (G_retarded + G_advanced)/2, which is the Wheeler propagator. Independently, Belenchia, Benincasa & Liberati (2015) showed that causal set dynamics produce the Wheeler propagator for unstable modes, automatically excluding them from asymptotic states.

**What's new:** Nobody has connected these findings. The causal set program and the QG+F program are completely disconnected communities, yet they independently arrive at the same mathematical object for ghost-like modes. This bridge observation suggests a deeper connection between causal discreteness and the fakeon prescription.

**Limitation:** This holds at tree level only. Anselmi (2020) showed the Wheeler propagator fails at loop level — the full fakeon prescription requires additional structure (threshold-dropping) that has no causal set derivation.

### 2. Three-Way Interpretive Convergence

Three independent approaches all motivate the fakeon from different angles:
- **Causal order:** The ghost's anti-causal energy flow is incompatible with the causal partial order → Wheeler/fakeon prescription
- **Indivisibility:** The ghost can't become real because that would make the process divisible → explains analyticity sacrifice (irreducible multi-time memory)
- **Entanglement:** The ghost must be purely virtual for consistent entanglement entropy → modular unitarity requires the fakeon

The convergence is non-trivial in mechanism, even though it's guaranteed at a deep level by the shared requirement of unitarity (positive-definite Hilbert space).

### 3. The 23-Debt Catalog (Reference Value)

The comprehensive ranked catalog of QG+F's explanatory debts (Exploration 001) is a useful reference. Key distinctions: 8 debts are QG+F-specific (no other approach shares them), 12 are partially shared, 3 are universal hard problems.

## Assessment Against Strategy Criteria

**Minimum success** ("passes Tiers 1-2, partially Tier 3"):
- **BARELY MET.** The Wheeler bridge and CFT framework are genuinely novel (Tier 1) and internally consistent at tree level (Tier 2). The three-way convergence provides more intuitive explanation of the fakeon than QG+F alone (partial Tier 3).

**Good success** ("passes Tier 3 fully, at least one prediction"):
- **NOT MET.** Zero novel predictions. All "predictions" are reformulations of existing QG+F or causal set results.

**Breakthrough:**
- **NOT MET.** The work is interpretive, not predictive.

**Strategy exhaustion:**
- **YES, for the fakeon interpretation direction.** The structural obstacle (classical → quantum gap) applies to ALL interpretive approaches. Further exploration within the interpretive paradigm faces diminishing returns. New mathematical frameworks (not reinterpretation) are needed.

## What Didn't Work and Why

1. **The "build a focused theory around one gap" methodology** produced an observation, not a theory. The gap (fakeon interpretation) turned out to be more amenable to interpretation than construction. A theory needs predictions; the fakeon interpretation doesn't generate any.

2. **All three concept sprints faced the same obstacle.** The "classical → quantum gap" blocked all attempts to derive the fakeon from pre-quantum structures. This was identified in Strategy-001 (cost function negative result) but we hoped causal order, indivisibility, or entanglement might circumvent it. They didn't.

3. **The deep build (CFT) was premature.** The concept sprint scores (viability 4-6/10) warned that none of the concepts was ready for full theory construction. Building CFT and then immediately attacking it confirmed this in 2 explorations — efficient, but the attempt itself may have been avoidable.

## Directions for the Next Strategy

### Top Recommendation: QG+F ↔ AS Connection (Direction D from Exploration 010)

The question "are QG+F and asymptotic safety the same theory?" is:
- **Sharp** — they make different predictions (r < 0.005 vs r up to 0.01; Schwarzschild BHs vs modified BHs)
- **Tractable** — functional RG tools exist; lattice simulations are improving
- **High-impact** — the answer reshapes the field

A strategy focused on this could produce genuine new results: computing the RG flow that connects (or doesn't connect) the asymptotically free fixed point to the Reuter fixed point.

### Secondary Recommendations

2. **Non-perturbative QG+F** — ghost confinement, bound states, mass gap. No computational path exists yet, but a strategy focused on designing the right simulation (lattice QG+F) would be pioneering.

3. **Observable predictions** — combine with LiteBIRD preparation (launch ~2032). Sharpen the r prediction, compute the exact crossover between QG+F and six-derivative extension, prepare the definitive discriminator.

4. **The Wheeler bridge paper** — worth writing up regardless of the next strategy. Connect Belenchia et al.'s causal set result to Anselmi's fakeon. One short paper.

### What NOT to Do Next

- Don't try another interpretation of the fakeon. The interpretive space is exhausted (3 approaches all face the same gap).
- Don't try to build a full alternative to QG+F. Strategy-001 showed this is too ambitious.
- Don't repeat the "synthesis of existing programs" approach. It produces packaging, not physics.

## Methodology Assessment

### What Worked

- **Gap-mining then concept sprint** is excellent for exploring interpretive directions. The ranked debt catalog (exploration 001) directly determined the concept sprint targets.
- **Parallel concept sprints** saved enormous time. Three independent explorations in parallel, each with self-assessment scores, made selection easy.
- **Immediate devil's advocate** (applied from Strategy-001's lesson) correctly identified CFT's fatal weaknesses in 1 exploration.
- **One task per exploration** — zero timeouts across 10 explorations.

### What Didn't Work

- **Interpretive targets don't produce theories.** The methodology assumed the selected gap would be amenable to theory-building. The fakeon interpretation gap is amenable to interpretation, not construction. A future strategy should distinguish "gaps that can be filled with new physics" from "gaps that can only be filled with new understanding."
- **Explorer stalls on synthesis tasks.** Explorations involving synthesizing 5+ large documents stall frequently. The strategizer should handle synthesis directly.
- **The "focused theory" criterion is unclear.** What counts as a "theory" vs. an "observation" vs. an "interpretation"? The strategy criteria should specify: does the result need to make a NEW prediction?

---

## The Big Picture

Strategy-002 mapped the territory around QG+F's deepest conceptual mystery — the fakeon — and found that it is robust against all interpretive attacks. The fakeon cannot be derived from causal order, indivisibility, or entanglement in a way that produces new physics. It CAN be motivated from all three, which provides conceptual depth but not predictive power.

The one concrete finding — the Wheeler propagator bridge between causal sets and QG+F — is a genuine contribution to the literature, connecting two independent research communities through a previously unrecognized mathematical identity.

The main lesson for the next strategy: **stop interpreting QG+F and start computing with it.** The most productive directions involve new calculations (QG+F ↔ AS flow, parameter constraints, non-perturbative structure), not new interpretations.
