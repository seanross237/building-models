# Exploration 007 Summary: Devil's Advocate — Attack CFT

## Goal
Ruthlessly attack Causal Fakeon Theory (CFT) across 6 vectors. Rate severity. Give overall verdict.

## What Was Tried
Attacked CFT on: (1) loop-level gap, (2) SJ construction limitations, (3) circularity, (4) whether it actually derives the fakeon, (5) whether novel predictions are real, (6) comparison to existing programs. Used literature searches to ground each attack in evidence.

## Outcome: PIVOT RECOMMENDED

**Ratings:** Loop-Level Gap = NEAR-FATAL. SJ Limitations = SERIOUS. Circularity = MODERATE. Derivation question = SERIOUS. Novel Predictions = SERIOUS. Comparison = MODERATE.

**Key finding:** CFT is an interpretive bridge observation, not a theory. The tree-level identity G_fakeon = G_Wheeler is real and worth noting, but: (a) the loop-level extension is provably wrong in its naive form and requires nonexistent technology in its sophisticated form; (b) the entire argument rests on scalar analogies — the spin-2 SJ construction doesn't exist; (c) CFT doesn't derive the fakeon, it selects it post-hoc given continuum input; (d) zero genuinely novel predictions; (e) the "structural prediction" that causal sets should produce quadratic gravity is currently unsupported (Eichhorn et al. 2023 get R²−2□R, not Stelle's action).

## Key Takeaway
CFT was worth constructing (Exploration 006 did good work) but is not worth further development cycles. It's a nice motivational story for the fakeon at tree level. File it as an observation; pivot to more productive directions — non-perturbative QG+F, observable predictions, or Anselmi's own classicized dynamics program.
