## Verdicts

### 1. Barandes vs. SED: CATEGORY ERROR (distinct programs, incompatible problem statements)
SED is a *physical theory* (reductionist: classical EM + ω³ ZPF → QM emerges). Barandes is a *mathematical reformulation* (QM re-expressed in stochastic language, no physical mechanism proposed). The comparison is a category error: SED fails as a physical theory (wrong predictions for nonlinear systems). Barandes cannot fail in the same way because it makes no physical predictions beyond QM — the Born rule is a definitional algebraic identity, not a testable approximation. There is no ω³ spectral density, no ALD equation, no radiation reaction — nothing that could create the positive feedback loop that breaks SED. The comparison reveals Barandes' essential character: it gains freedom from failure by sacrificing physical specificity entirely.

### 2. Barandes vs. Nelson: DISTINCT (non-overlapping programs)
Barandes himself (2507.21192) identifies the core distinction: Nelson used Markovian dynamics (Brownian motion); Barandes uses non-Markovian/indivisible dynamics. Structurally: Nelson = continuous Markovian diffusion on ℝⁿ with fixed diffusion constant ℏ/2m and real particle trajectories. Barandes = discrete-space non-Markovian transition kernels on finite N with no specified dynamics. Nelson is ontologically committed (real particle trajectories); Barandes is ontologically agnostic (the multi-time realizer is "potentially unknowable"). A critical asymmetry in the multi-time problem: Nelson over-specifies (unique path measure → wrong multi-time correlations without adding collapse back in); Barandes under-specifies (any Kolmogorov tower consistent with Γ is valid → multi-time structure is underdetermined). Both fail to independently derive QM multi-time correlations, but for opposite structural reasons. Neither subsumes the other.

### 3. Barandes vs. Consistent Histories: COMPLEMENTARY (different aspects of same foundational problem)
CH addresses: "when can probabilities be assigned to sequences of events?" (interpretational). Barandes addresses: "what is the stochastic structure of quantum dynamics?" (representational). Both use decoherence for measurement. Both adopt ontological agnosticism. CH handles multi-time correlations correctly (built around histories); Barandes treats multi-time structure as underdetermined. Neither subsumes the other. Notably, Barandes never mentions consistent histories in any of his papers — a significant omission given the structural parallels.

## Key Takeaway
The central insight: stochastic approaches to QM fall into three levels. **Level 1** (physical/reductionist): SED and Nelson — try to derive QM from specific physical stochastic mechanisms; both fail to capture QM multi-time structure without importing QM itself. **Level 2** (formal/representational): Barandes — re-expresses QM in stochastic language, makes no new predictions, succeeds by being physically vacuous. **Level 3** (interpretational/extensional): Consistent Histories — works within QM formalism, adds consistency rules for classical descriptions, handles multi-time correctly.

Barandes is correctly Level 2. The SED comparison is a category error (Level 1 vs. Level 2). The Nelson comparison is between a failed Level 1 program and a successful Level 2 program (where Nelson's Markovian assumption is the technical reason for failure). The CH comparison is between Level 2 and Level 3 — complementary, not redundant.

## Unexpected Findings

**Finding 1 (important):** Barandes' phase freedom (multiple Θ matrices give valid ISPs for the same QM) and CH's realm selection problem (multiple consistent families give valid classical descriptions) appear to be structurally analogous — both express the non-uniqueness of embedding a quantum density matrix into classical probability space. If formalized, this would mean Barandes' "gauge invariance" IS CH's realm selection problem in stochastic kernel language. This connection does not appear in the literature.

**Finding 2:** The multi-time problem has *opposite character* in Nelson vs. Barandes. Nelson: over-specified (unique paths → wrong multi-time predictions). Barandes: under-specified (multiple consistent towers → no unique multi-time prediction). The correct middle is CH, which directly encodes multi-time structure.

**Finding 3:** Barandes never addresses consistent histories in any of his papers. Given the structural parallels at the measurement problem and the realm-selection/phase-freedom connection, this is a notable gap in Barandes' literature engagement.

**Finding 4 (SED reinterpreted):** Through Barandes' lens, SED's failure becomes a negative existence theorem: no physical stochastic mechanism with ω³ spectral density can recover the quantum phase structure (encoded in complex Θ). This sharpens: any physical mechanism that correctly specifies the phases must be equivalent to QM itself — circular.
