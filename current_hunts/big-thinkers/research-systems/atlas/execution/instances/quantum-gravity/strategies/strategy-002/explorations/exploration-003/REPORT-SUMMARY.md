# Exploration 003 Summary: Lee-Wick Quantum Gravity — Full Viability Assessment

## Goal
Assess Lee-Wick quantum gravity as a complete, viable theory of quantum gravity and compare it to QG+F (Anselmi's fakeon approach).

## What Was Tried
Systematic literature review of 12+ key papers (2015-2026) including the founding papers by Modesto and Shapiro, Anselmi's fakeon development, Kubo & Kugo's unitarity analysis, and the definitive 2025 comparison paper co-authored by both Anselmi AND Modesto.

## Outcome: FAILED — Lee-Wick QG is not a viable independent theory

### The Unitarity Verdict (CLEAR)
- **Lee-Wick/CLOP prescription: UNITARITY VIOLATED** above a threshold energy (Kubo & Kugo, PTEP 2023). Complex ghosts are actually created, contradicting Lee & Wick's original claim.
- **Additionally: CLOP breaks Lorentz invariance** at the quantum level (Nakanishi 1971, confirmed definitively by Anselmi+Modesto 2025).
- **Fakeon prescription: UNITARITY PROVEN** to all perturbative orders (Anselmi & Piva 2017-2018).
- **Modesto himself now agrees** — he co-authored the 2025 paper (JHEP05(2025)145) concluding "only the fakeon prescription is physically viable."

### The Identity Problem
Lee-Wick QG and QG+F share the SAME action (quadratic gravity: R + R_μν R^μν + R² or equivalently R + C² + R²). The ONLY difference is the quantization prescription for the massive spin-2 pole. Since the Lee-Wick prescription fails and the fakeon prescription succeeds, "Lee-Wick QG with correct prescription" IS QG+F. There is no independent Lee-Wick quantum gravity program.

### The Super-Renormalizable Variant
Modesto's six-derivative version is genuinely different (super-renormalizable vs merely renormalizable) but still requires the fakeon prescription. It's a known extension of QG+F with more free parameters, not a novel independent theory.

## Key Takeaway
**Lee-Wick QG is eliminated as our construction target.** It either collapses onto QG+F (with fakeon prescription) or violates unitarity (with LW prescription). The search for a genuinely novel quantum gravity theory must look elsewhere. The next candidate should be something that is NOT simply a variant of quadratic gravity with a different prescription — it needs a fundamentally different mechanism.

## Leads Worth Pursuing
- The ghost confinement idea (Asorey et al. 2025) is interesting conceptually but doesn't restore unitarity
- The six-derivative super-renormalizable QG with fakeon is a valid theory but is essentially "QG+F with better UV behavior" — not novel enough
- We should look for theories with genuinely different propagator structures, not just different prescriptions for the same poles
