# Mission Validation Guide — Quantum Gravity

## What Success Looks Like

A breakthrough: a novel theory of quantum gravity that produces new explanations for observed phenomena, new mathematical connections, or new testable predictions that go beyond what existing approaches have achieved.

Passing validation tests is necessary but not sufficient. The tests keep the work grounded in real physics. The goal is discovery.

## How to Measure Progress

Progress is measured by two axes:

1. **Validation tier reached** — How far has the current best theory progressed through the tiers below? Moving up tiers means the theory is structurally sound and consistent with known physics.
2. **Novelty produced** — Has the work generated genuinely new insights, connections, or predictions? This can happen at any tier. A novel insight at Tier 1 (e.g., a new way to construct the action that naturally avoids ghosts) is valuable even before higher tiers are reached.

The ultimate target is Tier 4 — novel, quantitative, testable predictions. But unexpected discoveries at any tier are the real prize.

## Validation Tiers

### Tier 1: Structural Sanity
Does the theory hold together as a mathematical object?

- Well-defined action or partition function
- Correct degrees of freedom (2 for massless graviton in 4D)
- Gauge symmetry consistent with diffeomorphisms
- Ghost freedom (no negative-norm states)
- Unitarity (S-matrix is unitary)
- Dimensional consistency

If a theory fails Tier 1, it has foundational issues. These are not subjective — they either pass or fail.

### Tier 2: Known Physics Recovery
Does the theory reproduce what we already know works?

- Graviton propagator matches GR in appropriate limit
- Newton's law recovery (V(r) = -GM/r at large distances)
- Three-graviton vertex matches GR (or deviations within observational bounds)
- Lorentz invariance preserved (or violations below experimental bounds)
- Equivalence principle satisfied
- Linearized equations of motion match GR

A theory that passes Tier 2 is at least as good as GR in the regimes we can test. Failing specific tests here isn't necessarily fatal — it may indicate the theory modifies gravity in a specific regime, which is interesting if the modification is consistent with data.

### Tier 3: Quantitative Checks
Does the theory match specific measured numbers?

- Graviton mass bound (m_g < 1.2 x 10^-22 eV)
- Gravitational wave speed (|c_gw/c - 1| < 5 x 10^-16)
- Lorentz violation parameters within SME bounds
- Black hole entropy reproduces Bekenstein-Hawking (S = A/4G)
- Spectral dimension running (d_s ~ 4 in IR, d_s ~ 2 in UV)
- Post-Newtonian parameters within bounds (|gamma - 1| < 2.3 x 10^-5)

These are the hard quantitative tests. Passing them means the theory survives contact with precision data. For detailed test procedures and experimental bounds, see `../../validation/VALIDATION-TESTS.md`.

### Tier 4: Novel Predictions
Does the theory predict something new?

- At least one observable that standard GR does not predict
- The prediction is quantitative (a number or range, not a vague claim)
- The prediction is testable with current or near-future experiments
- The prediction is distinguishable from competing approaches

This is the breakthrough tier. A theory that reaches here with a genuine, testable prediction is a major success.

### Tier 5: Unification (Aspirational)
Does the theory explain deeper questions?

- Standard Model gauge group emerges naturally
- Fermion content (three generations of quarks and leptons)
- Cosmological constant explained or constrained
- Dark matter candidate
- Hierarchy problem addressed

Most theories won't reach Tier 5. Progress here is extraordinary.

## What "Exhausted" Looks Like

A strategy is exhausted when:
- Repeated explorations are not advancing the theory to higher tiers
- The theory is stuck at a specific test with no clear path forward
- Multiple independent attempts to resolve a failure have all converged on the same obstacle
