# Mission: Concavity of Spectral Functions on Matrix Groups

## Question

What techniques exist for proving that the maximum eigenvalue of a matrix-valued function on a compact Lie group is globally concave (or convex)? Specifically: if H(Q) is a smooth Hermitian-matrix-valued function on SU(N)^E, when can we prove that λ_max(H(Q)) is concave on the group manifold?

## Context

This arises from lattice Yang-Mills theory. We have numerical evidence that λ_max(HessS(Q)) achieves its global maximum at flat connections (Q=I) and that all gradient ascent trajectories converge there. The second variation is negative-definite at flat. If λ_max is concave on SU(2)^E, the local max is automatically global — giving a proof of the Hessian eigenvalue bound.

1. **Survey existing results** on concavity/convexity of eigenvalue functions: Davis-Lewis theorem, Ando's theorem, results on geodesically convex functions on Riemannian manifolds.
2. **Identify applicable techniques** — what conditions on H(Q) guarantee concavity of λ_max(H(Q))? Does the sum-of-plaquettes structure help?
3. **Assess feasibility** — is this a known-hard problem, or are there clean paths?
