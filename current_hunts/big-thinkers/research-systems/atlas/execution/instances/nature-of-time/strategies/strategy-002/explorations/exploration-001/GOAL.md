# Exploration 001: Survey and Select the Challenger

## Mission Context

We are running a "Challenger Tournament" to test whether a radically different interpretation of time can beat the computational interpretation developed in a prior strategy. The computational interpretation says:

> "Time is the computationally irreducible processing of quantum correlations, as encountered from within a relational perspective."

It explains six features of time (one-dimensionality, irreversibility, directionality, open future, flow, dilation) from a unified framework grounded in the Landauer-Bennett-Bekenstein triad, Margolus-Levitin bound, computational irreducibility, and Wolpert's impossibility theorem. It passes all five validation tiers (consistency, rigor, explanatory power, boldness with honesty, completeness).

**Its five acknowledged weaknesses:**
1. **The "now"** — provides structural past/future asymmetry but not why any point IS the present. Acknowledged as weakest point.
2. **Factorization problem** — presupposes well-defined subsystems. Doesn't explain how they emerge.
3. **Arrow direction needs the Past Hypothesis** — LBB explains mechanism of irreversibility but not why it points the direction it does.
4. **No novel predictions** — interpretive framework, not a new theory.
5. **Flow claim is interpretive** — "CI IS flow" is a philosophical identification, not a deduction.

## Your Task

**Survey 4+ candidate non-computational interpretations of time, score them rigorously, and select the one with the strongest potential to beat the computational interpretation.** Then begin an initial sketch of the selected challenger.

### Candidates to assess (plus any you discover):

**A. Causal fundamentalism** — Time IS the causal ordering of events. Not emergent from anything deeper — causation is the bedrock. (Maudlin's directed topology, causal set theory's sequential growth dynamics, interventionist causation.)

**B. Thermodynamic realism** — Time is the real, irreducible process of entropy production. Not statistical but fundamental. (Prigogine, non-equilibrium thermodynamics, dissipative structures.)

**C. Experience-first** — Time is fundamentally a feature of experience/consciousness, and physics describes the structure of that experience. (Phenomenology, IIT/Tononi, the hard problem of consciousness applied to time.)

**D. Causal-processual hybrid** — Time is the irreversible causal process of becoming. Combines causal order (Maudlin/CST) with process metaphysics (Whitehead) and physical irreversibility (Prigogine).

**E. Whatever else you find** — There may be framings not listed here. Smolin's temporal naturalism, Cortees-Smolin energetic causal sets, retrocausation approaches, the two-state vector formalism, or something entirely novel.

### For each candidate, assess:

1. **Explanatory coverage** — How well does it explain each of the six features of time? (one-dimensionality, irreversibility, directionality, open future, flow, dilation). Score 1-5 for each.
2. **Physics grounding** — Is it anchored in established physics, or is it pure philosophy?
3. **Strength against computational interpretation's weaknesses** — Does it offer better answers to the five acknowledged weaknesses?
4. **Vulnerability profile** — What are its own weaknesses? Can they be repaired?
5. **Novelty** — Does it offer genuinely different insights, or is it the computational interpretation in different language?

### Then select one and begin building:

Choose the candidate with the strongest overall profile — the one most likely to genuinely challenge the computational interpretation. Justify the selection explicitly.

For the selected candidate, provide:
- Core claim (one sentence)
- Initial sketch of how it explains each of the six features
- Its strongest advantages over the computational interpretation
- Its honest weaknesses
- What further development it needs (this will be Exploration 002)

## Success Criteria

- All candidates assessed with explicit scoring
- Selection justified with clear reasoning
- Selected candidate has a genuine shot at competing — not a straw man
- The initial sketch is substantive enough to build on in Exploration 002

## Failure Criteria

- Assessment is superficial (no scoring, no engagement with actual physics)
- The selected candidate is obviously weaker than the computational interpretation on every dimension
- Only existing literature is reviewed without constructive development

## Output

Write your findings to `explorations/exploration-001/REPORT.md` (detailed) and `explorations/exploration-001/REPORT-SUMMARY.md` (concise summary). Write incrementally — get content into REPORT.md as you go, don't wait until the end.
