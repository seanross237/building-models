# Meta-Learning: Exploration 010

**What happened:** Explorer crashed (likely rate limit) before writing any substantive content. Only a skeleton with placeholder sections was produced.

**Lesson:** For the final exploration in a strategy — especially one that's more survey/assessment than deep research — consider writing the content directly (as the strategizer) rather than delegating to an explorer. The strategizer has all the accumulated context and can produce a competent assessment without needing a fresh agent to do web research.

**Alternative approach that would have worked:** Instead of a full explorer, use a simpler goal: "Based on the GOAL.md, write your initial assessment of each direction WITHOUT doing web research first, then do targeted research only on the most promising ones." This front-loads the output and makes crashes less costly.
