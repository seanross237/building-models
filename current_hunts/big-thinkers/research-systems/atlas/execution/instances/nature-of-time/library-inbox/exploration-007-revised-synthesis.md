## 1. The Revised Core Claim

> **Time is irreversible determination — the ongoing, physically constrained process by which the indeterminate becomes determinate, as encountered from within.**

This sentence is unchanged from v1. What changes is how we understand and present it.

### What "Determination" Is — and What It Isn't

"Determination" is a **bridging concept** — a proposed theoretical identification, not a discovered primitive. We propose that the information-processing described by the Computational Interpretation (CI) and the ontological becoming described by the Causal-Processual Interpretation (CP) are two descriptions of the same process. We call this process "determination" — the generation of the determinate from the indeterminate.

The evidence for this identification is:

1. **Structural isomorphism.** The tournament (Exploration 004) documented a near-total structural correspondence between CI and CP concepts: potential→actual maps to input→output, concrescence maps to computation, Landauer's principle maps to irreversibility of actualization, the ML bound maps to serial actualization, and so on. This convergence was not manufactured — CI and CP were developed independently by different intellectual traditions.

2. **The Landauer-possibility link.** Landauer's principle (information erasure costs kT ln 2) can be read as the thermodynamic cost of extinguishing a real possibility. If potentialities are ontologically real, then information destruction IS possibility-extinguishing. This is a substantive philosophical claim, not a pun — but it is an identification claim that could be wrong.

3. **Unified explanatory power.** The combined account explains more than either parent alone: CI provides the physical constraints (LBB engine, ML bound, Bekenstein bound, computational irreducibility, Wolpert); CP provides the ontological character (what the process consists of, why it has a direction, what the "now" is). Together, they answer both "how does time work?" and "what is time?"

We are explicit: "determination" does not name a newly discovered fundamental entity. It names a proposed unification. The unification earns its keep if and only if the combined account is genuinely more explanatory than its parts — which we argue it is, but modestly.

### The Process-Primary Position

The v1 synthesis contained an unresolved tension: it claimed both that becoming is ontologically real AND that the timeless Wheeler-DeWitt description is fundamental (via the QRF dissolution). These pull in opposite directions. If the timeless description is fundamental, becoming is perspectival. If becoming is fundamental, the timeless description is a mathematical tool.

**The revised synthesis takes the process-primary position.** Becoming — the irreversible determination of the indeterminate — is ontologically real, not merely perspectival. The Wheeler-DeWitt equation captures constraints on the determination process; it does not describe a timeless reality from which time merely appears. The QRF program demonstrates that "static" and "evolving" descriptions are *mathematically equivalent perspectives* — but mathematical equivalence does not settle ontological priority. We take the metaphysical position that the evolving, processual description is ontologically primary, and the timeless constraint description is a powerful abstraction from it.

**What this costs:** The QRF dissolution no longer serves as a circularity-breaker in the deepest sense. We cannot say "the temporal description emerges from a fundamentally timeless formalism." Instead, we say: the temporal process is fundamental, and the timeless formalism captures its constraints from a God's-eye view that no physical observer actually occupies.

**What this buys:** Internal coherence. The synthesis's most distinctive contributions — the ontologically real "now," the intrinsic direction of the arrow, the grounding of irreversibility in genuine possibility-extinguishing — all require that becoming is real, not perspectival. Taking the process-primary position makes these contributions honest rather than equivocal.

**Regarding circularity:** Yes, this means the synthesis describes time using a temporal process. Every processual account of time faces this. The synthesis's response is the same as the heat-kinetic energy analogy: "Heat is molecular kinetic energy" uses the concept of motion (which presupposes time), but it is not viciously circular — it identifies heat with something specific and structurally illuminating. Similarly, "time is irreversible determination" identifies temporal passage with something specific (the generation of facts from possibilities, constrained by LBB, ML, and Bekenstein) rather than leaving it as an unexplained primitive. The circularity is virtuous, not vicious: it adds structural detail rather than merely restating what it claims to explain.

## 2. Quantum Mechanics Compatibility Matrix

The synthesis requires a specific commitment about quantum mechanics: that quantum potentialities are ontologically real. This is not a minor footnote — it determines whether the synthesis is even available as a live option for a given reader. We put this front-and-center.

### Compatible QM Interpretations

| Interpretation | Compatibility | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Heisenberg's potentia** | ✅ Full | The synthesis directly inherits this: quantum states represent real possibilities that are genuinely extinguished upon measurement. |
| **Kastner's Transactional Interpretation** | ✅ Full | Offer waves and confirmation waves describe real potentialities that are resolved in transactions. The determination process maps directly to the transaction. |
| **Objective collapse theories** (GRW, Penrose, Diósi) | ✅ Full | Physical collapse IS the determination event. The stochastic/gravitational collapse mechanism provides a physical trigger for possibility-extinguishing. |
| **Some forms of Relational QM** | ✅ Partial | Rovelli's relational QM treats quantum states as relational facts. If "facts relative to a system" are read as genuine determinations, the frameworks are compatible. But Rovelli himself avoids strong ontological commitments about potentiality. |

### Incompatible QM Interpretations

| Interpretation | Compatibility | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Everettian many-worlds** | ❌ Incompatible | All possibilities are realized in different branches. No possibility is ever "extinguished." The synthesis's core mechanism — possibility-extinguishing as the engine of irreversibility and directionality — has no analogue. |
| **Pilot wave / Bohmian mechanics** | ❌ Incompatible | The particle always has a definite trajectory. There is no genuine indeterminacy, hence no genuine determination. Time in Bohm's theory is the unfolding of a deterministic dynamics, not the resolution of real potentialities. |
| **Strict instrumentalist Copenhagen** | ❌ Incompatible | The wavefunction is a calculational tool, not a description of anything real. If potentialities aren't real, they can't be extinguished, and the synthesis's ontological additions collapse. |

### Unclear

| Interpretation | Compatibility | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **QBism** | ❓ Unclear | QBism and the synthesis share a rejection of a single determinate reality prior to measurement. Both treat measurement as genuinely creating something new. But QBism locates this in the agent's beliefs, not in ontological possibilities. Whether QBist "belief updating" can ground "determination" is an open question. The synthesis is closer to QBism than to many-worlds, but the fit is imperfect. |

### What This Means

The synthesis is available to physicists and philosophers who already accept (or are open to) the reality of quantum potentialities — roughly, those in the Heisenberg/Kastner/objective-collapse tradition. It is NOT available to committed Everettians or Bohmians. This is a genuine limitation on the synthesis's audience and should be weighed honestly. For those who find the potentiality commitment unacceptable, CI (Strategy 001) remains a fully coherent interpretation with no metaphysical commitments, scoring ~47.5/65.

## 3. The Architecture: CI with an Ontological Upgrade

The stress test (Exploration 006, Attack 4) showed that every improvement the synthesis offers over CI traces to a single philosophical commitment: the ontological reality of quantum potentialities. Remove this commitment, and the synthesis IS CI. This is not a defect — it reveals the synthesis's actual architecture, which we now present honestly.

### Ground Floor: The Computational Interpretation (CI)

CI provides the established physics with zero metaphysical commitments:

- **Margolus-Levitin theorem** — maximum rate of state transitions: 2E/πℏ
- **Landauer's principle** — information erasure costs kT ln 2 (experimentally confirmed)
- **Bekenstein bound** — finite information storage in finite regions
- **Bennett's reversibility theorem** — reversible computation requires complete records
- **The LBB engine** — finite storage + mandatory erasure = irreversible processing
- **Computational irreducibility** — some computations have no shortcut
- **Wolpert's impossibility theorem** — no embedded system can predict all future states
- **QRF mathematical equivalence** — "static" and "evolving" descriptions are formally equivalent

This ground floor is load-bearing and uncontroversial. It stands regardless of one's QM interpretation.

### Upper Floor: The Ontological Upgrade

The potentiality commitment adds:

- **An account of what time IS** — not just constraints on a process, but the process identified: the resolution of real possibilities into actualities
- **An intrinsic direction for the arrow** — from possibility to actuality, built into the nature of determination, not dependent on the Past Hypothesis
- **An ontologically real "now"** — the locus of active determination, where possibilities are being resolved, ontologically distinct from the settled past and the open future
- **Deeper grounding for irreversibility** — information is destroyed because real possibilities are extinguished; Landauer's cost is the thermodynamic signature of possibility-extinguishing
- **Phenomenological structure for flow** — retention (inherited past), primal impression (active determination), protention (open future)

This upper floor provides genuine explanatory gains. But it is **optional**. Someone who finds the potentiality commitment too costly can stay on the ground floor (CI) and lose nothing that CI already provides.

### The Upgrade Is Not Free

The upper floor costs:

- **Physics grounding** — drops from 5.0 to 4.0. The synthesis introduces a metaphysical commitment that is not derivable from established physics.
- **QM compatibility** — incompatible with Everettian QM, pilot wave, and strict instrumentalism. This limits the audience.
- **Philosophical exposure** — the synthesis is now vulnerable to attacks that CI is immune to (the substitution test, the circularity objection, the "real potentialities" debate).

The upgrade is worth it IF the explanatory gains matter to you. The synthesis does not claim these gains are free.

## 4. The Six Features Explained

### 4.1 One-Dimensionality

**Why does time have exactly one dimension?**

**From CI (constraints):** The Margolus-Levitin theorem establishes a maximum rate of distinguishable state transitions: 2E/πℏ. For any given subsystem, state transitions proceed one step at a time — serial processing.

**From CP (ontology):** Each determination event must integrate all of its causal past into a single determinate outcome. You cannot "determine in two directions at once" because a fact must be a single consistent resolution. The causal structure of the world (a partial order) naturally has one-dimensional maximal chains — this is Malament's theorem: causal structure determines conformal geometry.

**The synthesis:** Time is one-dimensional because determination is serial: each act of determination produces one fact from many possibilities, and the ML bound is the physical expression of this seriality. Space is multi-dimensional because independent subsystems can undergo determination in parallel; time is one-dimensional because any given determination event is a single resolution.

**Honest caveat (from stress test):** The argument that "determination is serial" is somewhat circular — seriality already presupposes one-dimensionality. The ML bound and chain-length arguments do independent work, but the "determination" wrapper doesn't add substantial explanatory power beyond what CI and CP individually provide for this particular feature.

**Score: 3.5/5** — Both parents' arguments are available and complement each other. The combination is incrementally better than either alone, but this is the feature where the synthesis adds the least over its parents.

### 4.2 Irreversibility

**Why can't processes run backward?**

**From CI (mechanism):** The Landauer-Bennett-Bekenstein engine: (1) The universe processes information. (2) Reversible processing requires complete records (Bennett). (3) Storage capacity is finite (Bekenstein). (4) Therefore information must be erased. (5) Erasure is physically irreversible — it dissipates kT ln 2 per bit (Landauer, experimentally confirmed).

**From CP (ontology):** Actualization is inherently one-way. When possibilities become one actuality, the other possibilities are genuinely extinguished — not hidden, not recoverable, but ontologically gone.

**The synthesis proposes an identification:** The LBB engine is the information-theoretic expression of ontological irreversibility. We propose that information is "destroyed" by erasure *because* the possibilities it encoded have been genuinely extinguished by determination. Landauer's kT ln 2 is the thermodynamic cost of extinguishing a real possibility. This is a substantive identification claim — it could be wrong, but it is not merely a relabeling. It provides a deeper answer to *why* information destruction is irreversible: not just because of thermodynamics, but because the possibilities that the information described are ontologically gone.

**Score: 4.5/5** — The LBB mechanism (CI's strength) is preserved in full precision. The proposed ontological grounding adds philosophical depth. But the identification (Landauer erasure = possibility-extinguishing) is an assertion, not a derivation, which prevents a perfect score.

### 4.3 Directionality (The Arrow)

**Why does time have a preferred direction?**

**From CI (mechanism + gap):** The LBB engine explains why individual processes are irreversible. But for a global arrow — entropy increasing in one direction — CI needs the Past Hypothesis: the universe began in a low-entropy state. This initial condition is assumed, not derived.

**From CP (direction + gap):** The direction of time IS the direction of actualization: from potential to actual. This direction is intrinsic. But CP lacks CI's precise physical mechanism.

**The synthesis:**

1. **The mechanism** is the LBB engine (from CI). Precise, physical, established.

2. **The direction** is the direction of determination: from indeterminate to determinate (from CP). This is intrinsic to the process — the asymmetry between "possible" and "actual" provides a direction that doesn't require external explanation. You cannot un-determine what has been determined because the possibilities that were extinguished are gone.

3. **The Past Hypothesis remains an initial condition.** The v1 synthesis claimed the PH was "reframed as natural." The stress test (Attack 3) showed this was substantially verbal — calling an assumption "natural" is rhetorical, not explanatory. The revised position: the Past Hypothesis must be assumed, not derived. Within the synthesis's framework, it has a specific characterization — the determination process began with a maximally open state — but whether this makes the PH less mysterious is a philosophical judgment, not a physical result.

**The synthesis's genuine contribution to directionality is the intrinsic direction of determination** (possibility → actuality), which CI lacks. It is NOT the reframing of the initial condition.

**Score: 4.0/5** — The intrinsic direction is a genuine improvement over CI (which needs the PH for direction and scores 3.0). The LBB mechanism provides precision. But the initial condition question remains open, and the directionality score must reflect this honestly.

### 4.4 Open Future

**Why is the future undetermined?**

**From CI (negative results):** Computational irreducibility — some computations have no shortcut. Wolpert's impossibility theorem — no embedded system can predict all properties of its universe's future states. Together: the future is not merely unknown but *not yet computed*.

**From CP (positive ontology):** The future is genuinely open because determination has not yet occurred. The future is not a hidden pattern waiting to be discovered; it is a space of real potentialities not yet resolved.

**The synthesis:** The future is open for two complementary reasons:

1. **Ontologically:** The potentialities that will be resolved into facts are currently real but unresolved. There is genuinely nothing there to predict. (CP's contribution)

2. **Informationally:** Even if you wanted to access the future, you couldn't. Computational irreducibility ensures no shortcut exists. Wolpert's theorem ensures no embedded system can predict it. The ML bound ensures you can't run the determination process faster than the universe itself. (CI's contribution)

CI shows you *can't* access the future; CP shows why there's *nothing there to access*. These reinforce each other.

**Score: 4.5/5** — This criterion survived all five stress-test attacks unscathed. CI's rigorous negative results are established. CP's positive account of genuine indeterminacy completes the picture. The combination is stronger than either parent.

### 4.5 Flow

**Why does time pass?**

**From CI (structural identification):** Flow IS computational irreducibility as experienced from inside. Sequential states, inaccessible future, no skipping, genuinely happening.

**From CP (phenomenological structure):** Flow IS actualization as encountered by an actualizing subject. Husserl's retention-primal impression-protention structure maps onto the structure of determination.

**The synthesis (process-primary):** Flow is what irreversible determination is like from the inside. Because we take the process-primary position, becoming is ontologically real — not merely perspectival. This gives the phenomenological structure genuine ontological grounding:

- **Retention** (trailing awareness of the just-past) — the phenomenological expression of inheritance. Each determination event carries forward results of prior determinations. This is both ontological (the causal past is inherited) and informational (the input state contains results of prior computations).

- **Primal impression** (the vivid "now") — the phenomenological expression of active determination. Where the LBB engine is running, where possibilities are being resolved into fact.

- **Protention** (anticipatory openness) — the phenomenological expression of the open future. Real but unresolved potentialities that will be determined next.

The synthesis does not require panexperientialism. It operates at the moderate commitment: complex determination processes in sufficiently integrated biological systems produce temporal experience because their functional architecture maps onto the structure of determination itself.

**Honest caveat:** This still faces the hard problem of consciousness. The synthesis explains the *structure* of temporal experience (why it has retention-impression-protention) but not *why* this structure is accompanied by experience at all.

**Score: 3.5/5** — Richer than CI alone (3.0) because the phenomenological structure is included. The process-primary position gives the account internal coherence that v1 lacked (no more QRF-process tension). But the hard problem remains, and the account's depth depends entirely on the potentiality commitment.

### 4.6 Gravitational Time Dilation

**Why do clocks tick slower near massive objects?**

**From CI (quantitative):** The ML theorem says maximum computation rate is proportional to energy. Gravitational redshift reduces effective energy near a massive object by √(1 − 2GM/rc²). Therefore the ML-bounded processing rate is reduced.

**From CP (structural):** In causal set theory, proper time along a worldline is proportional to the longest chain length. Near a massive object, worldlines traverse fewer causal set elements — fewer determination events — less proper time.

**The synthesis:** Time dilation is the variation in the rate of determination along different worldlines. The ML bound provides the quantitative constraint; the chain-length picture provides the structural interpretation. Both are restatements of GR in different vocabularies — neither derives dilation from first principles — but together they offer complementary physical and structural readings.

**Score: 4.0/5** — Both readings are available. Neither parent alone scored above 3.5. The combination is incrementally stronger. This criterion survived all stress-test attacks.

## 5. The Four Hardest Questions

### Question 1: Why does time flow?

Time flows because you are a bounded subsystem embedded in an irreversible determination process that cannot be shortcut. Each moment, the world around you and within you is determining itself — resolving possibilities into facts, erasing information that can never be recovered, generating new conditions for the next determination. You experience this as flow because:

1. You pass through states sequentially (ML bound)
2. You cannot access the future (computational irreducibility + Wolpert)
3. Each determination must complete before the next begins
4. The outcome at each step is genuinely new — not pre-computed, not pre-determined
5. Your experience has the structure of determination: inheriting the past (retention), actively resolving the present (primal impression), open to the future (protention)

Taking the process-primary position: this is not an illusion projected onto a static reality. The determination process is ontologically real. Flow is what this real process is like from the inside. A world where everything was already settled — where the "future" was just the past you haven't seen yet — would have succession but not flow. Our world has genuine determination, and flow is its experiential signature.

**Score: 3.5/5** — Richer than CI alone (3.0) because the process-primary position gives ontological grounding to the phenomenological structure. But the hard problem of consciousness remains (why does this process feel like anything?), and the account's strength depends on the potentiality commitment.

### Question 2: Why can't we go back?

You can't go back because:

1. **The information has been destroyed** (CI). The LBB engine: finite storage forces erasure; erasure is physically irreversible. The information that constituted the unbroken egg has been dissipated into a Bekenstein-bounded environment. Physically, irrecoverably gone.

2. **The possibilities have been extinguished** (CP). When the egg broke, the possibility of it remaining whole was genuinely extinguished — not hidden elsewhere, but ontologically gone. To reverse the process, you would need to resurrect possibilities that no longer exist.

We propose these are not two different reasons but the same reason at two levels. Information is destroyed BECAUSE possibilities are extinguished. The kT ln 2 of Landauer's principle is the thermodynamic cost of extinguishing a real possibility. This is the synthesis's identification claim: irreversibility is not merely about information — it is about the fact that determination has happened, and determination cannot be undone.

**Score: 4.5/5** — The LBB engine (CI's strongest argument) is preserved in full. The ontological grounding adds depth. But the identification (Landauer = possibility-extinguishing) is proposed, not proved, so a half-point less than the v1 score of 5.0.

### Question 3: What is "now"?

"Now" is where determination is happening.

Taking the process-primary position, the present has genuine ontological standing — it is not merely a label on a slice of a completed block. Past, present, and future have different modes of being:

- **The past** is settled: fully determined, partially erased (the LBB engine has destroyed some of its information). It is the domain of accomplished facts.
- **The future** is open: not yet determined, inaccessible (computational irreducibility ensures no shortcut). It is the domain of real but unresolved potentialities.
- **The present** is active: the locus where the LBB engine is running, where the ML bound is constraining the rate of determination, where real possibilities are being resolved into fact. It is the frontier between the settled and the open.

This account is **local and perspectival**: there is no global "now." Each determination event is its own present. Different worldlines have different presents. This is compatible with special relativity because the present is defined by causal structure (Lorentz-invariant), not by simultaneity surfaces (frame-dependent).

**Why THIS now?** Because "now" is not a property of a timeless moment but a feature of determination. You are always at the determination event you are currently undergoing. The question "why is this moment special?" presupposes a view from outside the determination process. From inside, there is no mystery: the present is wherever determination is happening, and you are always at yours.

**Score: 3.5/5** — The process-primary position resolves the QRF-process tension that plagued v1 (where the "now" was simultaneously claimed to be ontologically real and structurally perspectival without resolving the conflict). The account is now internally coherent. But it comes at a cost: without the QRF dissolution carrying full philosophical weight, the "now" account depends more heavily on the potentiality commitment. For someone who rejects real potentialities, the "now" reverts to CI's computational frontier (2.5/5).

### Question 4: Does time exist without observers?

Yes. Unequivocally.

Time is the process of irreversible determination. This process occurs wherever anything becomes determinate — in quantum measurements, in causal interactions, in decoherence events, in the formation of correlations between subsystems. None of this requires observers. The universe was determining itself for 13.8 billion years before any observer existed.

What observers add is not time itself but a *perspective on* time — a particular reference frame within which the determination process is experienced as temporal evolution. The QRF framework captures this precisely: the constraint equation and the relational evolution are mathematically equivalent perspectives on the same physics. An observer provides a specific reference frame; the determination process is the same whether observed or not.

An empty universe with energy would still have ML-bounded determination events. A finite universe without observers would still have the LBB engine running.

**Score: 4.0/5** — Both parents give strong answers, and the synthesis preserves both. This criterion survived all stress-test attacks.

## 6. Honest Limits

### 1. The Hard Problem of Consciousness Remains

The synthesis explains the *structure* of temporal experience (retention-impression-protention maps onto inheritance-determination-openness) but not *why* this structure is accompanied by experience at all. This is a limit shared with every interpretation. The "now" is explained structurally, but its *vividness* — why the present feels so much more real than memory or anticipation — remains at the boundary of what any physical interpretation can explain.

### 2. The Potentiality Commitment Is Genuine and Costly

The claim that quantum potentialities are ontologically real is the synthesis's single most important philosophical move. It is supported by the Heisenberg interpretation and by the work of Kastner and Kauffman, but it is not derivable from physics alone. It is incompatible with Everettian QM, pilot wave theory, and strict instrumentalist Copenhagen (see §2 above). Someone who rejects this commitment loses every improvement the synthesis offers over CI. This is not a bug — it is the synthesis's architecture — but it means the synthesis's audience is limited to those willing to make this commitment.

### 3. The Factorization Problem Persists

The synthesis presupposes that the universe can be decomposed into subsystems. In fundamental quantum gravity, this decomposition may itself be emergent. The synthesis, like both parents, describes time's properties within the regime of well-defined subsystems but does not explain the origin of subsystem structure itself.

### 4. No Novel Predictions

The synthesis generates no experimental predictions beyond what CI and CP individually offer. CI makes none. CP makes predictions through causal set theory (spacetime discreteness, swerves) that are constrained but not confirmed. The synthesis inherits CP's predictions through its CST component but adds none of its own. Its value is explanatory, not predictive.

### 5. The Past Hypothesis Is Not Resolved

The Past Hypothesis remains an initial condition that must be assumed, not derived. Within the synthesis, it has a specific characterization — the determination process began with a maximally open state — but whether this characterization makes the PH less mysterious is a philosophical judgment, not a physical result. The synthesis's genuine contribution to the arrow is the intrinsic direction of determination (possibility → actuality). It does not explain why the universe's initial state had the specific character it did.

### 6. "Determination" as Bridging Concept Is Vulnerable

The stress test showed that "determination" can sometimes be replaced by "irreversible process" without apparent loss (the substitution test, Attack 1). The synthesis argues that "determination" picks out a *specific kind* of irreversible process — one that generates facts from possibilities — and that this specificity does genuine work. But the case for "determination" as more than a unifying label rests on the identification claim (Landauer = possibility-extinguishing), which is proposed, not proved.

### 7. The Circularity Is Acknowledged

Describing time as a temporal process is inherently circular. The synthesis argues this circularity is virtuous (adds structural detail, analogous to "heat = molecular kinetic energy") rather than vicious (merely restates the explanandum). The process-primary position makes this circularity more visible than the v1 version, which partially obscured it via the QRF dissolution. We own the circularity and argue it is the price of any non-primitivist, non-eliminativist account of time.

### Honest Summary

The synthesis is **CI with an ontological upgrade**. CI provides the ground floor — established physics, zero metaphysical commitments, a solid interpretation scoring 47.5/65. The potentiality commitment provides the upper floor — ontological depth, an account of what time IS, a richer "now," a deeper grounding for irreversibility.

The upgrade is **genuine**: the explanatory gains are real, not merely verbal. The two-level explanation (constraints + ontology) is deeper than CI's single-level account. The intrinsic direction is a genuine improvement over CI's dependence on the Past Hypothesis.

The upgrade is **optional**: CI stands on its own. Someone who finds the potentiality cost too high — whether because they prefer Everettian QM, or because they want zero metaphysical commitments, or because they find the identification claim (Landauer = possibility-extinguishing) unpersuasive — can stay on the ground floor and lose nothing that CI already provides.

The upgrade is **costly**: it introduces a metaphysical commitment, limits QM compatibility, and exposes the interpretation to philosophical attacks that CI is immune to.

## 9. The Final Statement (Revised)

> **Time is irreversible determination — the ongoing, physically constrained process by which the indeterminate becomes determinate, as encountered from within.**

Unpacked (revised):

- **"Irreversible"** — each determination event erases information (LBB engine) and, we propose, extinguishes real possibilities. The process cannot be reversed because the information is physically destroyed and the possibilities are ontologically gone.

- **"Determination"** — a bridging concept: the proposed identification of CI's "computation" and CP's "actualization" as two aspects of one process. The evidence: structural isomorphism, the Landauer-possibility link, and unified explanatory power. The identification could be wrong. If it is, CI and CP remain as strong, separate interpretations.

- **"Ongoing"** — time doesn't stop. Determination keeps happening. Each event inherits results of prior determinations and generates conditions for future ones. Taking the process-primary position: this ongoing character is ontologically real, not perspectival.

- **"Physically constrained"** — determination operates within established physics: ML bound (rate), Bekenstein bound (storage), Landauer's principle (cost of erasure). These explain why time has its specific quantitative properties.

- **"By which the indeterminate becomes determinate"** — the ontological heart. Possibilities that are genuinely real are resolved into facts. This requires the potentiality commitment. Without it, you have CI's account of information processing — still valuable, but without the ontological identification.

- **"As encountered from within"** — time as experienced is the determination process from a particular reference frame within it. The QRF framework shows the mathematical equivalence of "static" and "evolving" descriptions. We take the process-primary position: the evolving description is ontologically primary, the static description is a mathematical abstraction.
