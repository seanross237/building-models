# Adversarial Review: TTH Claims Across All Explorations

**Explorer:** Exploration 002, Strategy 003
**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Goal:** Three-part adversarial review — (A) prior art search for each claim, (B) three conceptual attacks, (C) null hypothesis testing

---

## Executive Summary

After systematic prior art searches across arXiv, JHEP, PhilSci Archive, and related sources, and after seriously engaging with all three conceptual attacks:

- **Claims 1 and 4** are mathematically confirmed but partially known in structure. Their novelty lies in the explicit computation, spectral analysis, and the "preparation-history" framing — not in the algebraic fact that K = −log(ρ).
- **Claim 2** (product-state identity) is very likely a known result; it adds value as a diagnostic but should not be presented as novel.
- **Claim 3** (excited-state modular flow at wrong frequencies, growing with N) survives as the strongest claim. The closest prior art (Lashkari-Liu-Rajagopal 2021) computes modular flow operators for excited states but does NOT compare correlator dynamics to physical time evolution. The N^{0.33–0.44} growing discrepancy has no known precedent.
- **The "preparation-history time" central interpretation** does not appear to have been stated in the reviewed literature. It is a novel physical framing of a known mathematical fact.
- **Attack 1** ("TTH was never meant for non-equilibrium states") is factually incorrect: Connes-Rovelli 1994 define thermal time for ANY faithful state, not just KMS states. This attack misrepresents TTH. The correct framing is that TTH defines a time for all states, and the question is whether that time matches physical time — which is exactly what we tested.
- **Attack 2** (modular flow defines "correct" time for each state) is the strongest conceptual challenge and partially succeeds: it reveals an interpretive ambiguity in TTH that the community has not resolved.
- **Attack 3** (lattice ≠ type III) is partially valid as a caveat but is *weakened* by the convergence data, since the discrepancy grows with N, making the continuum prediction worse, not better.

## Part A: Prior Art Search — Claim by Claim

### A.1 Claim 1: Post-Quench Modular Flow Generates Pre-Quench Dynamics

**Search terms used:**
- "modular flow non-equilibrium post-quench" (arXiv)
- "modular Hamiltonian quench entanglement spectrum correlator dynamics"
- "entanglement Hamiltonian quantum quench time evolution" (JHEP, arXiv)
- "thermal time hypothesis non-equilibrium KMS Connes Rovelli"

**Papers examined:**

**(a) Cardy & Tonni (2016/2018) — arXiv:1608.01283**
Title: "Entanglement Hamiltonians in two-dimensional conformal field theory"
Content: Enumerates cases in 2D CFT where the modular Hamiltonian can be written as an integral over the stress tensor, including local and global quench scenarios. The paper computes the *form* of K_A after quenches in CFT.
**What it does NOT do:** It does not compute correlator dynamics generated by K_A and compare them to physical dynamics C_QM(t). It does not test TTH. It does not address whether modular flow generates pre-quench or post-quench dynamics.
**Gap relative to Claim 1:** Our claim is specifically that the *dynamics generated by* K compares to *pre-quench* Hamiltonian, not just that K has a certain form.

**(b) Cardy et al. (2018) — arXiv:1807.04440**
Title: "Entanglement Hamiltonian evolution during thermalization in conformal field theory"
Content: Studies how K_A itself evolves in time after a quench (i.e., how the entanglement Hamiltonian changes as the system thermalizes). Finds K_A → thermal K_A exponentially. Mentions modular flows for visualization of entanglement propagation.
**What it does NOT do:** Does not compute C_mod(τ) = ⟨x(e^{iKτ} x e^{-iKτ})⟩ or compare it to C_QM(t). The modular flow is used qualitatively; no spectral comparison is made.
**Gap:** We are computing dynamics *generated by* a fixed K; Cardy et al. study how K itself changes over time.

**(c) Hollands & Sanders (2017) — arXiv:1702.04924**
Title: "Entanglement measures and their properties in quantum field theory"
Content: Mathematical treatment of entanglement measures in QFT. Discusses modular Hamiltonians in the context of nuclearity conditions. Does not compute correlator dynamics for non-equilibrium states.
**Gap:** Theoretical framework, not a test of dynamics.

**(d) Standard algebraic QFT: Takesaki, Bratteli-Robinson**
The fact that K = −log(ρ_0) = βH_0 + const for a Gibbs state ρ_0 = e^{−βH_0}/Z is textbook. This means modular flow generates H_0 dynamics — tautologically.
**What's new in Claim 1:** The post-quench setup makes this non-trivial because H₁ ≠ H₀. The spectral content is explicitly computed: C_QM contains ω_± = √(ω² ± λ), C_global contains only ω. The asymptotic discrepancy √3 for any λ > 0 is a concrete quantitative result. The "preparation-history" framing is new.

**Prior art verdict for Claim 1:**
- **(a) Closest prior art:** Standard modular theory (K = -log ρ) implicitly contains the algebraic fact. Cardy-Tonni and Hollands-Sanders are the closest explicit references but neither computes correlator dynamics or tests TTH.
- **(b) What's genuinely new:** The explicit spectral comparison (disjoint peaks), the √3 asymptotic result, and the "modular time = pre-quench time" interpretation.
- **(c) What an AQFT specialist would say:** "The fact that K_0 generates H_0 dynamics is a corollary of K_0 = βH_0. This is known. What is new is that you're calling this a 'test of TTH' and providing the explicit correlator computation."
- **Novelty rating: 2.5/5** — Algebraically expected, but the explicit computation, spectral analysis, and TTH framing are new. The √3 asymptotic result is clean and new.

### A.2 Claim 2: Product-State Identity

**Search terms used:**
- "Takesaki tensor product modular operator product state"
- "modular Hamiltonian product state local observable commutes"
- "global local modular flow product state equal"

**Papers examined:**

**(a) Takesaki (Vol. II) — Theorem IX.4.2**
The tensor product structure of modular operators is standard: for ρ = ρ_A ⊗ ρ_B, the modular operator Δ_{ρ_A ⊗ ρ_B} = Δ_{ρ_A} ⊗ Δ_{ρ_B}. This implies K_AB = K_A ⊗ I + I ⊗ K_B, and [K_B ⊗ I, x_A ⊗ I_B] = 0 is immediate.

**(b) Bratteli-Robinson, Tomita-Takesaki theory**
This result is part of standard modular theory for tensor product algebras.

**Prior art verdict for Claim 2:**
- **(a) Closest prior art:** Takesaki Vol. II, Theorem IX.4.2. The tensor product structure of modular operators is known.
- **(b) What's genuinely new:** Essentially nothing algebraically. The numerical confirmation to 10^{-16} is useful as a pipeline check.
- **(c) What an AQFT specialist would say:** "This is a straightforward corollary of the tensor product structure of modular operators. It is known."
- **Novelty rating: 1/5** — Known result. Useful as a verification but should not be presented as a novel claim.

### A.3 Claim 3: Excited-State Modular Flow Has Structurally Wrong Frequency Content (STRONGEST CLAIM)

**Search terms used:**

Title: "Modular flow of excited states"
Content: Develops formalism for modular and relative modular flows of excited states. Expresses modular operators for excited states in terms of the vacuum modular operator. Illustrates with coherent and squeezed states in generalized free field theory.
**Critical assessment:** This paper computes the *modular flow operator* σ_s(O) = e^{iKs} O e^{-iKs} for excited states. The key question is whether they compute two-point correlators C_mod(τ) = ⟨Ψ|σ_s(x) x|Ψ⟩ and compare them to physical correlators C_QM(t). From the abstract and available content, the paper focuses on the structure of the modular operator and relative entropies, illustrated with squeezed/coherent state examples. It does NOT appear to compare modular flow correlators to physical Hamiltonian evolution or test TTH.
**Gap relative to Claim 3:** Our claim is specifically that (1) modular flow response has ZERO spectral weight at the physical frequency ω_m, and (2) this discrepancy GROWS as N^{0.33–0.44} in the continuum limit. Neither result appears in Lashkari et al.

**(b) Lashkari (2016) — arXiv:1508.03506**
Title: "Modular Hamiltonian of Excited States in Conformal Field Theory"
Content: Computes modular Hamiltonian matrix elements for excited states in 2D CFT using a replica trick. Focuses on relative entropy computation. Does not compute correlator dynamics or frequency content of modular flow.

**(c) Casini & Huerta (2009) — arXiv:0903.5284**
Title: "Reduced density matrix and internal dynamics for multicomponent regions"
Content: Computes modular Hamiltonians for free scalar fields on lattices. Provides the Williamson decomposition and tools for bosonic modular theory. Does not study excited states or compare modular flow to physical time evolution.

**(d) Faulkner (2015–2016) — arXiv:1605.08072**
Title: "Modular Hamiltonians for Deformed Half-Spaces and the ANEC"
Content: Studies modular Hamiltonians for deformed half-spaces in relativistic QFT. Not about excited states. Not about correlator dynamics comparison.

**(e) Bisognano-Wichmann extensions to excited states**
Several papers (e.g., arXiv:2002.04637 "Modular Hamiltonian for holographic excited states") compute the modular Hamiltonian for specific excited states in holographic settings. None found to compute the frequency content of modular flow correlators.

**(f) Growing discrepancy with N:**
No paper found that studies the continuum limit of modular flow discrepancy (the N^{+0.33} or N^{+0.44} scaling). This result appears entirely new.

**What an AQFT specialist would say about Claim 3:**
"The modular Hamiltonian of an excited state differs from the vacuum modular Hamiltonian. The modular flow therefore generates dynamics at modular (entanglement) frequencies, not physical frequencies. This is expected — the modular flow is not the physical time evolution, and for a non-KMS state, there's no reason to expect agreement. However, the quantitative analysis — zero spectral weight at the physical frequency, the explicit N-scaling of the discrepancy, and the lattice-to-continuum extrapolation — is new and nontrivial."

**Prior art verdict for Claim 3:**
- **(a) Closest prior art:** Lashkari-Liu-Rajagopal 2021 (modular flow of excited states, includes coherent/squeezed in GFF), but they compute the modular operator structure, not correlator dynamics vs. physical time evolution.
- **(b) What's genuinely new:** Zero spectral weight at physical frequency, N^{0.33–0.44} divergence in continuum limit, domain map across multiple state types, explicit TTH test framing.
- **Novelty rating: 4/5** — The specific computational result (zero spectral weight, growing discrepancy) and the TTH testing framing are new. The general statement "K_excited ≠ β H" is known.

### A.5 Central Interpretation: "Modular Time Is Preparation-History Time"

**Search terms used:**
- "modular time preparation history state encodes dynamics"
- "thermal time tracks preparation history" (no results)
- "modular automorphism preparation" (no results for this interpretation)
- Checked: Rovelli (gr-qc/9406019, 0903.3832), Martinetti, Connes, Swanson (2020), Chua (2024), Paetz thesis, 1807.04651

**What Connes-Rovelli 1994 say:**
Connes-Rovelli define thermal time for ANY faithful state using Tomita-Takesaki, not just KMS states. Their claim is: "given any faithful state, the flow of time is defined by the state-dependent modular automorphism group." They say the system is "always in equilibrium with respect to its own thermal time." They do NOT frame this as "modular time = preparation-history time" or discuss what happens for post-quench states.

**What critics say:**
- Swanson (2020) critiques TTH for non-equilibrium states but does not provide the "preparation-history" interpretation.
- Chua (2024, arXiv:2407.18948) critiques the circularity of TTH but does not articulate that thermal time tracks preparation rather than current dynamics.
- Paetz thesis (Göttingen) has a section on "state-dependence of thermal time" (section 7.17) and "tests of the thermal time hypothesis" but from what's visible, does not articulate the preparation-history mechanism.
- The 2018 paper arXiv:1807.04651 argues thermal time and physical time are "not compatible" but doesn't frame it as preparation-history.

**Conclusion on central interpretation:**
The "preparation-history time" framing — that modular time generates dynamics under the Hamiltonian that *created* the state, not the Hamiltonian *currently governing* the dynamics — does NOT appear to have been stated in the reviewed literature. It is a genuinely novel physical framing.

**Closest prior art for the interpretation:** Connes-Rovelli 1994 implicitly contain it (K = −log ρ encodes the preparation), but they don't frame it this way and don't draw the TTH-failure conclusion.

**Novelty of interpretation: 4/5** — The mechanism is implicit in K = −log ρ, but the explicit articulation as "preparation-history time" and its use to explain the pattern of TTH failures is new.

## Part B: Conceptual Attack Engagement

### B.1 Attack 1: "TTH Was Never Meant for Non-Equilibrium States"

**Steelman version:** Connes and Rovelli designed TTH to recover the KMS condition and explain why Gibbs states yield the physically correct time flow. The whole point was that thermal equilibrium *is* the condition for TTH to work. Testing TTH on post-quench or excited states is like testing a thermometer on a turbulent plasma — it's out of scope. Claims 1, 3, and 4 are therefore not "falsifications" of TTH but "demonstrations of domain limitations," which is less interesting.

**Response:**

This attack is **factually incorrect** about the scope of TTH. Connes-Rovelli 1994 explicitly define thermal time for ANY faithful state via the Tomita-Takesaki theorem — not just KMS states. The key quote from Connes-Rovelli is: "given any faithful state ω, the flow of time is defined by the state-dependent modular automorphism group." They specifically present TTH as a *general* definition of time, not one restricted to equilibrium. The equilibrium case (where modular time = physical time) is presented as a *consequence* (verification) of TTH, not its prerequisite.

Several later commentators (Chua 2024, Swanson 2020) note that the extension of TTH to non-equilibrium states generates conceptual problems, but this is a *critique* of Connes-Rovelli, not their intention.

**Partial concession to the attack:** There is a legitimate weakened version of this attack: if one interprets TTH as a program for *quantum gravity* (where there's no background time and any state defines a time), then the "mismatch with physical time" is arguably not a criticism but a feature — thermal time defines a *different* time than the nonrelativistic QM Hamiltonian, and maybe that's fine in quantum gravity contexts. This is Attack 2's territory (see below).

**Verdict on Attack 1:** The attack fails in its literal form (TTH IS defined for all states). The weakened version — that TTH is most interesting for quantum gravity, not nonrelativistic QM — has some merit but doesn't invalidate the claims, which are about what TTH *predicts* for physical dynamics.

**Impact on claims:** The claims are NOT weakened by Attack 1. They are correctly framed as tests of what TTH predicts, not violations of TTH's intended scope.

### B.2 Attack 2: "Modular Flow of Excited State Should Differ from Boost — That's a Feature, Not a Bug"

**Steelman version:** A sophisticated TTH proponent would argue: for an observer in an excited state |1_m⟩, the *correct* physical time is defined by the modular flow of that state. The boost is only "more physical" because it's what an accelerating detector measures — but that's a particular physical setup. For a different observer (one who prepared the excited state), the modular time *is* the natural time. The "mismatch" is a matter of choosing which observer's time you call "physical." Both times are valid; they just differ.

This has real force: modular flow is intrinsically state-dependent, and in quantum gravity without a background spacetime, there's no external standard of "physical time" against which to compare.

**Response:**

The attack has genuine merit in the quantum gravity context but fails in the specific test cases we studied, for two reasons:

**(1) There IS an independent physical standard in the Rindler case.** For the Rindler wedge vacuum, the physical time for a uniformly accelerating observer is the Rindler/boost time, fixed by operational considerations (acceleration, proper time). The BW theorem tells us modular flow ≡ boost time — they agree. For an excited state |1_m⟩ in the same Rindler context, the *same accelerating observer* would measure Rindler time (boost), but TTH predicts modular flow at entanglement frequencies ε_k/(2π) instead. This is not a choice of observer — the observer is fixed, and TTH gives the wrong answer for that observer.

**(2) For the post-quench case, there's an unambiguous physical standard.** The coupled oscillator after a quench is governed by H_{AB}(λ). The correlator C_QM(t) = ⟨x_A(t) x_A(0)⟩ under H_{AB}(λ) is what an experimentalist would measure. The global modular flow predicts C_global(τ) at frequency ω (pre-quench), not ω_± (post-quench). There's no interpretive freedom here — one is what the system does, the other is what TTH predicts.

**(3) The "preparation-history" interpretation makes the attack into a different claim.** If the TTH proponent says "modular time correctly tracks preparation history, so it SHOULD generate H_0 dynamics," they're essentially accepting our interpretation. The dispute becomes: is preparation-history time a useful notion of time? We say no (for most physical purposes), they say maybe (for quantum gravity). This is a legitimate scientific disagreement.

**Partial concession to the attack:** In the quantum gravity context (where the Hamiltonian is a constraint and there's no background time), one genuinely cannot say "the Hamiltonian generates the correct time." In that context, the attack has real force. However, in the non-relativistic QM and QFT settings we tested, the physical time is unambiguously defined, and TTH disagrees with it for non-equilibrium/excited states.

**Verdict on Attack 2:** The attack partially succeeds in identifying a genuine interpretive ambiguity for quantum gravity applications. It fails for the specific test cases (Rindler wedge, coupled oscillators) where physical time has an independent operational definition. The claims are weakened slightly in their quantum gravity implications but remain valid as explicit tests of TTH predictions.

**Impact on claims:** The numerical claims (spectral content, discrepancy values) are unaffected. The interpretation ("TTH fails for non-equilibrium states") should be qualified: "fails to match the physically measured dynamics in operationally well-defined settings."

### B.3 Attack 3: "The Lattice Is Not Type III"

**Steelman version:** The Rindler wedge algebra is type III₁ — this is where BW applies and modular flow has its non-trivial content. A half-lattice algebra with N sites is type I (it's just M_{2^N}(ℂ)), not type III₁. The two are qualitatively different: in type III₁, modular flow is outer (genuinely non-trivial), while in type I, it's inner (can be implemented by a matrix). The Claim 3 result shows discrepancy growing as N^{0.33–0.44} — but this might just be the signature of the type I → type III₁ transition, not evidence that TTH fails in the continuum. Perhaps in the true type III₁ limit, modular flow does something completely different, and the lattice results are unreliable predictors.

**Response:**

The attack correctly identifies that finite-lattice computations use type I algebras. However, the convergence data does more than just exist — it provides the direction of the limit:

**(1) The discrepancy GROWS with N.** If the lattice were converging to a regime where TTH works, we'd expect the discrepancy to *shrink* with N. Instead, δ_disc ~ N^{+0.33} (one-particle) and N^{+0.44} (squeezed). This means the mismatch gets *worse* in the continuum limit, not better. The attack's scenario — "maybe TTH works in type III" — would require the discrepancy to reverse its trend, which contradicts the data.

**(2) The structural mismatch is qualitative, not quantitative.** The modular flow has ZERO spectral weight at the physical frequency ω_m (at 0.01% of expected). If there were a type I → type III transition that fixes this, it would need to somehow introduce spectral weight at a frequency that currently has none. This would require the modular spectrum to change qualitatively, not just quantitatively.

**(3) Lattice modular Hamiltonians are known to converge.** Papers on lattice entanglement Hamiltonians (Casini-Huerta 2009, Eisler-Peschel 2009, arXiv:1807.01322) show that the Bisognano-Wichmann profile is well-reproduced on the lattice within a few lattice spacings from the cut. The continuum limit is physically meaningful and the lattice approach is standard.

**(4) The Gaussian approximation (one-particle case) is a separate caveat.** For the squeezed state result (Strategy-003 Exploration-001), the modular Hamiltonian is exact (no Gaussian approximation). The growing discrepancy therefore cannot be attributed to approximation errors.

**Partial concession to the attack:** The type I → type III transition is real, and in the strict continuum limit, the modular flow might have qualitatively different properties than on any finite lattice. However, the data strongly suggests the discrepancy diverges in this limit rather than healing.

**Verdict on Attack 3:** The attack identifies a real conceptual issue (type I vs. type III) but is *weakened* rather than strengthened by the convergence data. Growing discrepancy with N is the worst possible result for the lattice defense — it means the continuum prediction is even more discrepant than the lattice result.

**Impact on claims:** Claim 3 should be stated as "the discrepancy grows to the continuum limit" rather than "the discrepancy converges to a finite value," but the qualitative conclusion (structural mismatch) stands.

## Part C: Null Hypothesis Tests

The null hypothesis is: these observations can be explained by standard QM without TTH — i.e., they are either trivially known or artifactual.

### C.1 "Post-quench modular flow = pre-quench dynamics" — Is this trivially K = βH_0?

**Null hypothesis:** For the post-quench state ρ_0 = e^{-βH_0}/Z, K_AB = βH_0 + log Z. Modular flow therefore generates H_0 dynamics. The result C_global(t) = A·cos(ωt) is immediate from K = βH_0 — there's no additional content.

**Assessment:** The null hypothesis is **partially correct.** The algebraic fact is indeed trivial: K = -log ρ = βH_0 for this specific state, so modular flow = H_0 evolution tautologically. An algebraist would say: "You computed what you were already told."

**What the computation adds beyond the trivial observation:**
1. **Explicit spectral comparison.** The quantitative comparison C_QM(t) vs C_global(t) shows disjoint spectral content. This makes the mismatch concrete and measurable, not just "different operators."
2. **Asymptotic discrepancy = √3.** This clean result comes from Parseval + disjoint spectra. It's not a trivial consequence of K = βH_0; it requires computing both correlators and applying the norm formula.
3. **Parameter dependence.** The discrepancy grows with λ: 9.6% at λ=0.1, 102% at λ=0.3, 175% at λ=0.5. This systematic study adds value.
4. **TTH framing.** No prior paper has framed the K = βH_0 = H_0 = pre-quench fact as a test of TTH showing "modular time tracks preparation history."

**Novelty verdict for Claim 1 under null hypothesis:** The core algebraic observation is LOW novelty (the tautology). The explicit computation and TTH framing raise this to MEDIUM novelty. The asymptotic √3 result is a clean new result.

**Rate the claim:** Not falsified. The null hypothesis explains *why* the result holds, but not the explicit computation or its TTH implications.

---

### C.2 "Modular flow at wrong frequencies for excited states" — Is this trivially K_excited ≠ H?

**Null hypothesis:** For a one-particle state |1_m⟩, the modular Hamiltonian K_excited differs from the physical Hamiltonian H. Therefore modular flow generates different dynamics. This is trivially obvious — why is this a claim at all?

**Assessment:** The null hypothesis is **partially correct** at the qualitative level: of course K_excited ≠ H, so they generate different dynamics. An algebraist would say "this follows immediately from the definition."

**What the computation adds beyond the trivial observation:**
1. **Zero spectral weight at ω_m.** The modular flow response has essentially ZERO power at the physical frequency (0.01% of expected). This is not "different dynamics" — this is "completely wrong dynamics." The specific quantitative statement about spectral power is new.
2. **Growing discrepancy N^{0.33}.** The scaling of the mismatch with lattice size is entirely new. The continuum extrapolation is new.
3. **Domain map.** The systematic comparison across 8 physical modes and multiple N values establishes this is not an artifact.
4. **Squeezed state (exact).** The same result holds for the Gaussian-exact squeezed state, eliminating the non-Gaussian approximation as an explanation.

**Novelty verdict for Claim 3 under null hypothesis:** The qualitative observation (K ≠ H → different dynamics) is LOW novelty. The quantitative findings (zero spectral weight, N-scaling, squeezed-state confirmation) are MEDIUM-HIGH novelty. The continuum divergence (N^{0.44}) is the strongest result and appears entirely new.

**Rate the claim:** Not falsified. The null hypothesis identifies the mechanism but doesn't account for the specific quantitative findings.

---

### C.3 "Squeezed states have quantitative not structural discrepancy" — Is this trivially K_sq ≈ βH + O(r)?

**Null hypothesis:** For a squeezed Gibbs state ρ_sq = S·ρ_Gibbs·S†, K_sq = S(βH)S† = S e^{-βH} S† ... wait, actually K_sq = -log(S ρ_Gibbs S†) = S(-log ρ_Gibbs)S† = S(βH)S†. By the BCH formula, S(βH)S† = βH + β[r(H + ε),...] + ... ≈ βH + O(r). So modular flow ≈ H evolution + O(r) corrections.

**Assessment:** The null hypothesis is **correct as a structural argument.** The observation that squeezed states have approximate agreement with physical dynamics follows from K_sq ≈ βH + O(r) — this is a straightforward operator identity.

**What the computation adds beyond the trivial observation:**
1. **Explicit numbers.** 7.8% global discrepancy vs. 102% for post-quench is a concrete, publishable comparison.
2. **"Correct frequencies, wrong amplitudes" characterization.** The FFT shows peaks at ω_± for the squeezed state (matching C_QM), not at ω (as for post-quench). This clarifies the nature of the agreement.
3. **Structural vs. quantitative taxonomy.** The classification of discrepancies into "structural" (wrong frequencies) vs. "quantitative" (right frequencies, wrong amplitudes) is a new conceptual framework.

**Novelty verdict for Claim 4 under null hypothesis:** The algebraic origin of the result is LOW novelty (K_sq ≈ βH is known). The explicit computation and taxonomy are MEDIUM novelty. The claim is a single data point and needs systematic study.

**Rate the claim:** Not falsified. The null hypothesis explains the pattern; the computation adds quantitative specificity and a useful conceptual framework.

## Part D: Conclusion-Level Prior Art — The "Preparation-History Time" Interpretation

**The most important prior art check:** Has any researcher previously stated that "modular time is preparation-history time" — i.e., that thermal time generates evolution under the Hamiltonian that *created* the state, not the current Hamiltonian?

**Searched:** Connes-Rovelli 1994 (gr-qc/9406019), Rovelli "Forget time" (arXiv:0903.3832), Martinetti-Rovelli "Diamond's temperature," Rovelli-Smerlak thermal time (arXiv:1005.2985), Swanson 2020, Chua 2024 (arXiv:2407.18948), Paetz thesis (Göttingen), "Physical time and thermal clocks" (arXiv:1807.04651), all search results for "modular time preparation history."

**What was found:**
- Connes-Rovelli 1994 define time for any faithful state. They say modular time defines the state's "equilibrium time." No mention of "preparation history."
- Rovelli later papers discuss the state-dependence of time but frame it as a feature, not an explanation of failures.
- No paper found using "preparation history" as a characterization of thermal time.
- No paper found explaining the TTH success/failure pattern via "preparation Hamiltonian = current Hamiltonian iff TTH works."

**Assessment:** The preparation-history interpretation appears to be NOVEL as an explicit statement and as an explanatory framework for the TTH success/failure pattern.

**Qualification:** The interpretation is implicit in K = -log ρ (the density matrix encodes how the state was prepared), but this implication has not been stated in the reviewed literature. The novelty is in the *articulation* and *application* as an explanatory principle.

## Final Assessment

### What Survives Adversarial Review

**Claim 3** is the strongest result and survives all attacks. The specific findings — zero spectral weight at physical frequency, discrepancy growing as N^{0.44} for squeezed states — are not in the literature. The continuum extrapolation (if anything it gets worse) is the most striking result.

**The central interpretation** ("modular time = preparation-history time") survives as a novel framing. It's implicit in K = -log ρ but has not been articulated. It provides a unifying explanation for all six+ regimes tested.

### What Is Weakened

**Claim 1** is mathematically sound but its algebraic content is low novelty. An AQFT specialist would say the spectral consequences are implied by the algebra. The value is the explicit computation and TTH framing, not the algebraic fact.

**Claim 4** is a single data point. The taxonomy is clean but needs systematic study.

### What Should Be Dropped or Demoted

**Claim 2** (product-state identity) should be presented as a verification/diagnostic, not a novel claim. It's a textbook consequence of tensor product modular theory.

### Verdict on Each Conceptual Attack

| Attack | Result |
|--------|--------|
| "TTH not for non-equilibrium" | **Fails** — TTH is explicitly defined for all faithful states in Connes-Rovelli 1994 |
| "Modular flow of excited state IS the correct time" | **Partially succeeds** for quantum gravity context; fails for operationally defined physical times |
| "Lattice ≠ type III, results untrustworthy" | **Fails** — growing discrepancy with N strengthens continuum conclusion rather than weakening it |
