## Lesson

Explorers behave differently when told "this is a writing/synthesis task, not a research task." Without this instruction, they default to web searches even when all needed information is in the provided context. Explicitly state "do NOT search, only read and synthesize" for construction and synthesis explorations.

## Evidence

- **strategy-001 exploration 003** — Telling the explorer "this is a construction task, not a survey" kept it focused on synthesis rather than additional research.
- **strategy-003 exploration s3-007** — Providing source files + "use prosecution verdicts as building blocks" produced the best result of the strategy. Explorer used existing evidence rather than re-researching.
- **strategy-004 exploration s4-007** — "Do NOT search, only read and synthesize" produced a focused synthesis document from three specific source files. The explorer stayed on task without drifting into research.

## When to apply

Any exploration where the inputs are already available (construction, synthesis, final documents, comparisons of existing results). The distinction is: if the explorer needs to FIND information, it's research mode. If the explorer needs to ORGANIZE or SYNTHESIZE information, tell it explicitly and provide all inputs.
