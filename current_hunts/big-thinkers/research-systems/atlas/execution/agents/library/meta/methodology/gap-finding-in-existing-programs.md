## Lesson

Identifying open problems and gaps in existing research programs is as strategically valuable as constructing new theories. When a prior art search reveals that a result partially exists, the key deliverable shifts from "here is our new theory" to "here is the gap in the existing program, and here is why filling it matters."

The output format that enables this: a "what's already been computed" comparison table that maps existing results against desired results, making gaps immediately visible.

## Evidence

- **classicality-budget strategy-001 exploration 003** — The prior art search revealed Tank (2025) had the structural form of the classicality budget bound, but the physical grounding via Bekenstein was absent. The paper-by-paper comparison table (15 papers, each checked for the specific formula) made the gap between QD community and entropy bounds community immediately visible — and the gap itself became the core novelty claim.

- **stochastic-electrodynamics strategy-001 exploration 003** — Cataloging what had already been computed in the SED anharmonic oscillator literature (Pesquera-Claverie 1982 analytical result; no numerical verification) made the gap immediately obvious. The "what's already been computed" table format was the key tool for identifying that the O(beta) Langevin regime had never been tested numerically, which became the core contribution.

## Variant: "Landscape of Attempts" Deliverable

**NEW (vasseur-pressure E005):** Include a "what has the community tried and failed at?" deliverable in literature surveys. Requesting a table of all post-X papers and their relationship to a specific problem produces decisive strategic intelligence. In E005, the table of post-2007 De Giorgi papers revealed that NO paper had attempted to improve beta beyond 4/3 — the community moved orthogonally. This confirmed the problem is hard AND that any improvement would be genuinely novel. The table format (year, authors, result, relation to target) forces completeness and makes negative results (absence of attempts) immediately visible.

Distinct from the "what's been computed" gap: the landscape-of-attempts variant focuses on "what has been TRIED" (including failures and orthogonal moves), not just "what's been achieved."

## When to apply

After any novelty assessment that returns "partially known" or "structural precursor exists." The strategic move is to characterize the gap precisely: what exactly is missing, why hasn't anyone filled it, and what value does filling it provide?

Also: whenever investigating a specific bottleneck or open problem, include a landscape-of-attempts deliverable to determine whether the community has been actively working on it or has moved away.
