# The Benincasa-Dowker Action and Scalar Curvature

## The Discrete d'Alembertian

Sorkin first observed that a particular alternating linear combination of scalar field values at elements of a causal set approximates the d'Alembertian (wave operator) of 2-dimensional Minkowski space. Benincasa and Dowker (2010) generalized this to 4 dimensions, introducing "a one parameter family of retarded linear operators on scalar fields on causal sets" that are Lorentz invariant but nonlocal, parametrized by the scale of the nonlocality.

These operators approximate the continuum scalar d'Alembertian when acting on fields that vary slowly on the nonlocality scale. When applied to causal sets well-approximated by curved spacetimes, they approximate the operator Box - (1/2)R, where R is the Ricci scalar curvature. This connection to curvature enables the construction of "an approximately local action functional for causal sets."

## The Benincasa-Dowker-Glaser Action

The resulting action is the causal set equivalent of the Einstein-Hilbert action of general relativity. In the continuum limit, the mean discrete action converges to the integral of the Ricci scalar (the Einstein-Hilbert action) plus boundary terms.

Machet and Wang (2021) demonstrated that when a causal set is randomly distributed on a small causal diamond with arbitrary spacetime curvature, "the action admits a finite limit" composed of:

1. **Bulk term**: An Einstein-Hilbert action component, confirming the Benincasa-Dowker conjecture.
2. **Boundary term**: A contribution "exactly proportional to the codimension-two joint volume."

## Generalization to Arbitrary Dimensions

The original BD action was formulated for 2 and 4 dimensions. Dowker and Glaser later generalized it to arbitrary spacetime dimensions, with Glaser deriving closed-form expressions for the action coefficients in all dimensions.

## Generalized d'Alembertian Operators

Aslanbeigi, Saravani, and Sorkin introduced a family of "Generalized Causet Box (GCB) operators" in D-dimensional Minkowski spacetimes that are manifestly Lorentz-invariant, retarded, and non-local. These generalize the original Benincasa-Dowker operators and provide an infinite family of discrete wave operators for scalar field propagation on causal sets.
