## Mission Comparison

**Mission statement:** "Develop a novel theory of quantum gravity. Not a rediscovery of existing theories — something genuinely new. Prioritize originality and explanatory clarity over mathematical formalism."

**Assessment against stated goals:**

1. **"Something genuinely new"** — The framework is a synthesis of QG+F (Stelle/Anselmi) and AS (Reuter), not a new theory. Closer to rediscovery-with-synthesis. **Shortfall.**
2. **"Novel explanations for observed phenomena"** — Explains why two formalisms are compatible, not why gravity exists. Meta-theoretical, not physical. **Shortfall.**
3. **"Prioritize originality and explanatory clarity over mathematical formalism"** — Heavy on mathematical formalism (actions, fixed points, beta functions), light on physical intuition. Non-specialists can grasp the "two maps" metaphor but the content is technical QFT. **Partial shortfall.**
4. **"Predictions that differ from existing approaches"** — Zero novel discriminating predictions survived adversarial assessment. Every testable prediction is inherited from one standalone component. **Shortfall.**

**Verdict: Partially satisfies mission goals.** Legitimate intellectual contribution, but falls short of the mission's core demand for a genuinely novel theory of quantum gravity.

Most valuable contribution is negative: a clear demonstration that QG+F–AS unification produces zero novel discriminating predictions with current tools, plus a precise specification of the three computations that could change this. The program reached the boundary of what can be achieved by reasoning about existing theories without performing original calculations.
