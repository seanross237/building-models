# Entanglement Entropy in Causal Set Theory

## The Challenge

Defining entanglement entropy on a causal set is nontrivial because the standard definition relies on canonical hypersurface data (a spatial slice and its quantum state), which is not available in the fundamentally Lorentzian, discrete framework of causal sets. There is no natural notion of a spatial hypersurface in a causal set.

## The Sorkin-Yazdi Approach

Sorkin and Yazdi (2018) developed an approach using a global spacetime expression for entanglement entropy based on a Gaussian scalar field's correlation function (its Wightman function) within a spacetime region. This yields "an entropy which is both finite and of a Lorentz invariant nature."

## Volume Law vs. Area Law

A surprising initial result: numerical calculations for regions in 1+1 dimensional causal sets produced entanglement entropy that follows a spacetime volume law rather than the spatial area law expected from continuum quantum field theory.

However, the authors discovered that the expected area law can be recovered by truncating the commutator function (Pauli-Jordan operator) and the Wightman function -- specifically by "projecting out the eigenmodes whose eigenvalues are too close to zero according to a geometrical criterion." This truncation procedure filters out UV modes that are artifacts of the discreteness.

## Universality of Coarse-Graining Entropy

The researchers also studied "entropy of coarse-graining" when randomly thinning elements from a causal set. They found identical universal behavior between thinned causal sets and similarly thinned harmonic oscillator chains, suggesting a deep connection between the entropy of coarse-graining and the underlying discrete structure.
