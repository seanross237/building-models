# Black Hole Entropy in Causal Set Theory

## Horizon Molecules and Boltzmannian State Counting

Homsak and Veroni (2024) presented the first numerical study of black hole thermodynamics in causal set theory, examining the entropy of a Schwarzschild black hole through the distribution of proposed "horizon molecules."

Their key results:

1. The horizon molecules model is consistent with the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy formula S = A/(4 l_P^2) "up to a dimensionless constant that can be interpreted as the fundamental discreteness scale in the order of a Planck length."

2. The molecules are "found to straddle the horizon of the black hole to within a few Planck lengths," confirming that "entropy lives on the surface of the black hole."

3. The study suggests potential resolutions to the black hole information paradox, including possibilities for a finite temperature cutoff or prevention of complete black hole evaporation.

## Computational Framework

The researchers developed a highly parallelized C++ framework enabling generation of causal sets with over one million points -- "the largest causal sets in a non-conformally flat spacetime to date."

## Earlier Work: Causal Links as Horizon Atoms

Earlier work by Dou and Sorkin modeled a black hole spacetime as a causal set and counted the number of causal links crossing the horizon in proximity to a spacelike or null hypersurface. They found this number is proportional to the horizon's area, supporting the interpretation of the links as the "horizon atoms" that account for entropy.
