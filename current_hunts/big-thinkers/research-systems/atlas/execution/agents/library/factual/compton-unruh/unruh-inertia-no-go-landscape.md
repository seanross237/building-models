## Finding

There is NO single theorem ruling out all Unruh-based inertia modification. However, the landscape separates cleanly: thermal-detection mechanisms are fatally excluded, while vacuum-structure mechanisms (mode counting, entanglement entropy) survive. The most dangerous remaining objection is the freely-falling observer paradox.

## What IS Fatally Ruled Out

1. **Thermal-detection mechanisms** — Any mechanism requiring a particle to detect Unruh radiation at T ~ 10⁻³¹ K against CMB at 2.7 K. Energy density ratio ~10¹²³. Fatal for all "detector-based" approaches.

2. **Direct Compton-Unruh resonance** — Ruled out by 43 orders of magnitude. See `dimensional-inconsistency.md`.

3. **HRP/SED vacuum inertia** — Levin (2009) showed the relevant Lorentz force is zero relativistically. See `hrp-sed-inertia-dead.md`.

4. **McCulloch's QI as literally formulated** — Core equation gives m_i/m ≈ −0.70 at MOND-relevant accelerations; Renda (2019) identified additional derivation errors; a₀ predicted to vary with cosmic epoch (conflicts with observations showing a₀ constant to z ~ 1). See `mcculloch-qi-negative-mass.md`.

## What Is NOT Ruled Out

1. **Vacuum mode-counting mechanisms** — The Hubble horizon as a Unruh mode cutoff is physically motivated (modes with λ > Hubble radius are unphysical). This is McCulloch's conceptual insight separated from his failed implementation. NOT subject to the temperature argument.

2. **Entanglement entropy mechanisms** (Verlinde-type) — Competition between area-law and volume-law entropy in de Sitter naturally transitions at a ~ cH₀ without requiring thermal radiation detection. Derives a₀ = cH₀/6 ≈ 1.1 × 10⁻¹⁰ m/s². See `verlinde-emergent-gravity-a0.md`.

3. **The de Sitter crossover** — Model-independent physical transition from acceleration-dominated to Gibbons-Hawking-dominated thermal environment at a ~ cH₀. The T_U/T_dS = μ_MOND algebraic identity connects this crossover directly to the empirical MOND formula. See `tu-tds-mond-identity.md`.

## Key Surviving Objections Any Proposal Must Address

**1. The Freely-Falling Observer Paradox (MOST IMPORTANT)**

The Unruh effect requires proper acceleration (felt acceleration). Stars in galaxies are in free fall — zero proper acceleration. Any Unruh-based mechanism must specify what "acceleration" enters the formula for an orbiting star.

Possible resolution: the relevant quantity is deviation from a de Sitter geodesic rather than a Minkowski geodesic. In de Sitter space, cosmic expansion defines a preferred rest frame and "acceleration" relative to the Hubble flow may be meaningful even for freely-falling objects.

**2. Super-Hubble Wavelengths**

At a = a₀, the Wien-peak Unruh wavelength is ~44 × the Hubble radius. These modes do not fit within the observable universe. Mode-counting arguments must explain what it means to truncate modes that don't exist in the first place.

**3. Galaxy Cluster Residual**

MOND-like proposals fail for clusters: ~2× more mass than baryons provide. Any proposal inheriting MOND phenomenology inherits this problem.

**4. CMB and Large-Scale Structure**

MOND-type modifications struggle to reproduce CMB acoustic peaks and large-scale structure formation. ΛCDM succeeds here precisely because dark matter provides gravitational scaffolding.

**5. Mathematical Rigor**

Both McCulloch and Verlinde have faced serious technical criticisms. Any new proposal needs a rigorous, relativistically consistent formulation.

## Most Promising Surviving Mechanism

Verlinde's de Sitter entropy approach is the most theoretically grounded: derives a₀ = cH₀/6 (within 8%), avoids thermal detection, connects to holography. The T_U/T_dS = μ_MOND identity (see `tu-tds-mond-identity.md`) offers a concrete but unjustified ansatz within this framework. The open problem is converting the algebraic identity into a rigorous physical derivation.
