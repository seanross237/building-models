## Finding

As of early 2026, surfaceology is the **current leading framework for colored amplitude theory** but is explicitly not the "final form." The central technical gap is the missing bridge from surface kinematics to conventional loop momentum space.

## The Central Open Problem: Momentum-Space Bridge

The loop-level results (1-loop YM integrand, hidden zeros at loop level) are expressed in "surface kinematics" — variables X_{i,j} that are distinct from standard loop momenta ℓ. The missing link:

**How do we go from surface kinematics back to conventional loop momentum space?**

Surface kinematic variables include standard Mandelstam invariants AND additional loop-sector variables (X_{i,p} where p labels the puncture) without direct momentum-space counterparts. Computing actual S-matrix elements / cross-sections requires returning to conventional kinematics. This is explicitly acknowledged as the central technical challenge in arXiv:2408.11891 and arXiv:2503.03805.

**Consequence:** All surfaceology results are for *integrands*, not *integrated amplitudes*. Computing actual cross-sections from the surface formula remains an open problem, even for φ³ at 1-loop.

## Other Open Problems

### Yang-Mills at Higher Loops

The 2-loop YM integrand is known (2-loop 4-point, arXiv:2408.11891). Full all-loop all-multiplicity YM recursion in surface kinematics is not yet explicit — the cut equation exists but explicit formulas at ≥3-loop order remain open.

### N=4 SYM Connection

Does surfaceology have a sector that recovers N=4 SYM / amplituhedron results? Unclear. The massive spin-2 ghost of non-SUSY YM (absent in SUSY) might mean the supersymmetric completion requires new structure. The question of whether surfaceology and the amplituhedron point to a common deeper framework is the most important open *conceptual* question.

### Standard Model / Uncolored Theories

All surfaceology results are for colored theories (large-N / planar limit makes sense). Extensions needed:
- Uncolored particles (QED, electroweak) — tree works, loops require color
- Full electroweak sector (massive W/Z, Higgs)
- Full Standard Model at loop level

### Gravity

Double copy (BCJ/KLT) connects gauge and gravity amplitudes, but direct "gravity surfaceology" has not been constructed. The inverse KLT kernel (arXiv:2602.15102, Feb 2026) is a step in this direction but not a complete solution.

### Integrated Amplitudes

Performing loop integrals in the surface formulation — even for φ³ at 1-loop — has not been fully worked out in general kinematics. The Schwinger representation is standard, but surface kinematic variables introduce complications for dimensional regularization.

## Assessment: Is This the "Final Form"?

**What surfaceology achieves:**
1. Unifies colored scalar, NLSM, Yang-Mills, Yukawa, and string amplitudes under one framework
2. Solves canonical YM loop integrand (PRL 2025)
3. Handles non-planar amplitudes at all loops (advantage over amplituhedron)
4. Makes new structure manifest (hidden zeros, surface soft theorem, n/L decoupling)
5. Works at all loop orders for colored scalar theories

**What it does NOT achieve:**
1. Does not cover N=4 SYM — amplituhedron still preferred there
2. No bridge from surface kinematics to conventional loop-momentum space
3. Gravity not included
4. Non-colored (electroweak) theories not handled at loop level
5. Integrand formula, not integrated amplitudes

**Verdict (as of Mar 2026):** Not the final form, but clearly the current leading framework for colored amplitude theory. The missing momentum-space bridge may be filled in the next few years given the pace of progress (~15 papers in 2.5 years). The conceptual relation between surfaceology and the amplituhedron remains mysterious and is the most important open question.
