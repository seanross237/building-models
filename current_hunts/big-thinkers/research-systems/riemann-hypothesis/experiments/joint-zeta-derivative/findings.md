# Joint Distribution of (zeta, zeta') at sigma > 1/2

**Date:** 2026-04-04
**Status:** In progress
**Predecessor:** Four-Fold Architecture (which identified the joint (zeta, zeta') reformulation as the most promising next step)

## Executive Summary

We investigate the JOINT distribution of (zeta(sigma+it), zeta'(sigma+it)) for sigma > 1/2, motivated by the reformulation from the Four-Fold Architecture experiment:

**Reformulation:** RH is equivalent to the statement that the joint distribution of (zeta(sigma+it), zeta'(sigma+it)) as t varies never realizes (0, w) for any w satisfying |w| = |chi(sigma+it)| * |zeta'((1-sigma)+it)| at the same t.

The functional equation forces: at an off-line zero rho = sigma_0 + it_0 with sigma_0 > 1/2, the derivative |zeta'(rho)| must be vanishingly small, specifically decaying as (t_0/2pi)^{1/2-sigma_0}. We ask: does the Euler product structure prevent the joint distribution from accessing these (0, small-derivative) points?

## Key Questions

1. What does the joint (|zeta|, |zeta'|) distribution look like at sigma > 1/2?
2. What is the conditional distribution of |zeta'| given |zeta| < epsilon?
3. Does the Euler product create a lower bound on |zeta'| when |zeta| is small?
4. How does the Davenport-Heilbronn function differ (it HAS off-line zeros)?
5. Can the mutual information I(zeta; zeta') quantify the exclusion?
