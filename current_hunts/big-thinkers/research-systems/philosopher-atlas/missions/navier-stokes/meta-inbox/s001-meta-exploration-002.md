# Meta-Learning: Step 1, Exploration 002 (Lin 1998)

## What Worked
- Asking the explorer to compare with CKN on each item (not just in a summary section) produced a much richer analysis than "describe Lin's proof"
- The explorer identified a subtle structural insight (C(r) alone gives dim <= 2; dim <= 1 requires E(r)) that was not mentioned in the goal — good sign of deep engagement

## What Could Be Improved
- The compactness argument description could have been shorter — the explorer spent significant context explaining blow-up mechanics that are standard
- A "what is genuinely new vs. what is repackaged" framing might have been more efficient than "extract all 5 items and then compare"

## Generalizable Lesson
**Ask "what does this change?" not just "what does this do?"** For comparison explorations, framing each item as "how does this differ from the baseline?" produces sharper insights than "describe this independently."
