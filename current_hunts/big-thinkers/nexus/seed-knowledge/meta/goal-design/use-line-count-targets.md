## Lesson

Specify a line count target in the exploration goal. This prevents explorers from either writing too little (stalling) or expanding into unnecessary detail. Different task types have different sweet spots.

## Evidence

- **strategy-001 exploration 007** — "Keep report 200-400 lines" prevented expansion into unnecessary detail. Clean success vs. the unbounded exploration 006 which timed out.
- **strategy-001 exploration 008** — "200-400 lines" produced a focused 386-line report.
- **strategy-003 exploration s3-003** — 150-300 line target confirmed as right for single-question literature verifications.
- **strategy-004 exploration s4-005** — "For each domain, write 50-100 lines. Total: 200-300 lines" prevents one section from dominating in multi-domain explorations.
- **strategy-004 exploration s4-007** — Document outline with per-section line counts (total 500-800) prevented scope creep in synthesis tasks.

## Recommended targets

- Literature verification (single question): 150-300 lines
- Repair/revision task: 200-400 lines
- Comparison/assessment: 200-400 lines
- Theory construction: 300-500 lines
- Full synthesis document: 500-800 lines with per-section guidance

## When to apply

Every exploration. Always specify a total target. For multi-section tasks, also specify per-section targets.
