## Lesson

For theory repair/revision tasks, use this structure: (1) ONE repair per exploration, (2) specify a line count target, (3) use a structured format -- what changed, what survived, what's fixed, what's broken, verdict, (4) include a scorecard comparing before/after severity ratings.

## Evidence

- **strategy-001 exploration 006** — Tried to combine "research new approach + rewrite theory + address 4+ flaws." Timed out.
- **strategy-001 exploration 007** — Same topic as 006, narrowed to ONE repair with a 200-400 line target and 5-section structure (axioms, chain survival, what's fixed, what's broken, assessment). Succeeded in under 10 minutes. The scorecard (v1.0 severity vs v2.0 severity) produced the clearest possible output.

## When to apply

After a devil's advocate exploration identifies specific flaws. Each flaw should be repaired in its own exploration. The repair exploration should receive: (1) the specific flaw description, (2) the original framework, (3) any suggested repair approach. If the repair approach itself is novel or uncertain, research it in a separate exploration first.
