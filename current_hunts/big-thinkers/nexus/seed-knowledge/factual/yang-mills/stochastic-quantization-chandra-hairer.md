## Result

**"Stochastic quantisation of Yang-Mills-Higgs in 3D"** — Chandra, Hairer, Shen, Chevyrev (Inventiones Mathematicae 237, 541-696, 2024):

- Constructs a state space and Markov process for the stochastic YMH flow in 3D
- Uses regularity structures (Hairer's framework) to prove local-in-time solutions
- Shows unique renormalization counterterms exist making the solution gauge covariant in law

## Fundamental Difference from Balaban

This is a fundamentally different approach: constructs the **dynamics** (stochastic flow) rather than the **measure** directly. For the Millennium Problem, one would need to show the flow has an invariant measure satisfying the OS axioms. This is incomplete.

## Assessment

Represents a promising new direction, especially for 3D. The regularity structures framework is powerful but the gap between local-in-time solutions and a complete construction (invariant measure, OS axioms, mass gap) remains large. Currently limited to 3D — the 4D case would require additional analytical innovations.
