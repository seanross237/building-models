# Mission Validation Guide — Stochastic Electrodynamics

## What Success Looks Like

A novel, quantitative finding about SED's relationship to QFT — either SED works somewhere unexpected, or fails for a reason nobody has clearly articulated, or a minimal modification bridges the gap. The finding must be backed by explicit computation, survive adversarial scrutiny, and be genuinely new (not already in Boyer, de la Peña, Cetto, or the standard SED literature).

## Validation Tiers

### Tier 1: Reproduction
- At least one known SED result (Casimir, blackbody, harmonic oscillator) reproduced from first principles with explicit calculation
- Computation is reproducible — code provided, steps explicit
- Result matches published SED literature

### Tier 2: Extension
- SED pushed into a domain where results are not well-established in the literature
- Calculation is complete (not a sketch or dimensional analysis)
- Result compared quantitatively against QED/QFT prediction for the same observable
- Discrepancy (or agreement) is stated with numerical precision

### Tier 3: Boundary Identification
- The exact regime or observable where SED diverges from QED is identified
- The divergence is characterized — does SED give a wrong number, a qualitatively wrong behavior, or no prediction at all?
- The physical reason for the failure (or unexpected success) is identified
- Multiple independent calculations confirm the boundary

### Tier 4: Novel Insight
- A finding that changes understanding of SED's scope — either it works somewhere unexpected, or fails for a surprising reason
- The insight is new — thorough prior art search confirms it is not in the existing SED literature
- If SED fails, the minimal modification needed is identified and its implications analyzed
- If SED succeeds unexpectedly, the result is cross-verified by independent computation
- The strongest counterargument has been identified and addressed
- The result would be defensible in front of a knowledgeable physicist

### Tier 5: Implications
- The findings have implications beyond SED itself — for foundations of QM, the necessity of field quantization, or the interpretation of specific experiments
- A clear statement of what the results mean for the question "is field quantization necessary?"
- Connections to other programs (pilot-wave theory, direct-action theories, information-theoretic reconstructions) are explicit

## What "Exhausted" Looks Like

A strategy is exhausted when:
- Repeated explorations are producing diminishing returns on the same calculation
- A hard obstacle is hit (e.g., SED's framework cannot express the needed quantity) with no clear workaround
- The boundary between SED and QED has been characterized as precisely as the available tools allow
