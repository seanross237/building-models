### Claim A: The SED Tunneling Rate Formula (E001 + E005)

```
ln(Γ_SED/Γ_exact) = ln(A) + (S_WKB − V_barrier/E_zpf)
```
with A ≈ 1.075, slope ≈ 1.049, R² = 0.9998 across 7 data points (λ ∈ [0.05, 0.30]).

**Where E_zpf = ħ√2/2 (universal for V = -½x² + ¼λx⁴, ω₀=1).**
**Where S_WKB is the WKB action for the quantum tunneling problem.**

**Adversarial challenges to pursue:**

**(A1) Kramers escape rate overlap:** The Kramers escape rate for thermal noise over a barrier gives Γ_Kramers ∝ exp(−V_barrier/k_BT). The ZPF acts like a "zero-temperature thermal bath" with E_zpf = effective "temperature." Has Γ_SED/Γ_WKB formula already been derived as Γ_Kramers/Γ_WKB? Search for:
- "Kramers rate quantum tunneling comparison" and "Arrhenius WKB crossover"
- "classical vs quantum rate theory zero-temperature limit"
- "Caldeira-Leggett quantum tunneling Ohmic bath" — does their formula reduce to this at T=0?

**(A2) Slope 1.049 significance:** The slope = 1.049 ± 0.007 deviates from 1.0 at 7σ. This means the formula is NOT exactly Γ_SED/Γ_exact ∝ exp(S_WKB - V_b/E_zpf) — there's a systematic correction. What is it?
- Is slope > 1 just because S_WKB was computed incorrectly (outer-wall contamination issue from E005 bugs)?
- Or does it indicate a genuine physical correction (e.g., prefactor from Gaussian fluctuation correction)?
- Search for "WKB prefactor correction" and "fluctuation-corrected tunneling rate"

**(A3) ω_max dependence:** E005 reports A changes by ~50% when ω_max changes. This means the absolute value of Γ_SED is UV-sensitive. Argue: "The UV-dependence of A means the formula only holds relative to a fixed ω_max, making it a less fundamental result." Is there a physical choice of ω_max that makes A universal?

### Claim B: C_xx(d) = cos(ω₀d/c) and Bell S ≤ 2 (E002)

**Adversarial challenges to pursue:**

**(B1) Boyer precedent for correlation formula:** Boyer (1973) computed the van der Waals interaction energy between two SED oscillators. This requires computing ⟨x₁x₂⟩ as a function of separation. Does Boyer's formula reduce to cos(ω₀d/c) in the 1D case? Search for:
- Boyer, T.H. (1973). "Retarded van der Waals forces arising from cosmological zero-point radiation." Phys. Rev. A 7, 1832.
- Any Boyer paper with two-point ZPF correlator: ⟨E(r₁,t)E(r₂,t)⟩

**(B2) Bell S ≤ 2 trivial:** For uncoupled oscillators in the QM vacuum (separable state), Bell S ≤ 2 in QM as well. Is our SED Bell S ≤ 2 result trivial (not novel) because: (a) we're comparing SED to a separable QM state, or (b) SED is a local realistic theory by construction, so S ≤ 2 is a tautology? Argue this strongly.

**(B3) de la Peña LSED criticism:** The E004 explorer said LSED doesn't fix nonlinear failures. But is the claim that C_xx(d) = cos(ω₀d/c) already present in LSED? Search de la Peña papers for this formula.
