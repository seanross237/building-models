# Meta-Learning: Exploration 001

## What worked well
- Naming specific papers and authors in the goal was essential — the explorer used these as starting points and then expanded from there
- The classification scheme (COMPLETED / PARTIALLY COMPLETED / etc.) produced a clear two-tier gap structure
- Asking for "precision at the theorem level" resulted in the explorer actually tracking down paper-by-paper content
- The explorer went beyond the named authors to find Dimock, Chatterjee, and Chandra-Hairer — good divergent behavior

## What didn't work
- The 32-line REPORT.md was written early as a scaffold and didn't grow for a long time — this made monitoring confusing. The explorer was doing research but not updating the report incrementally.
- The report is ~460 lines which is at the upper end of the target (400-600). Could have been slightly more focused.

## Lessons
- For technical mapping explorations, the explorer needs web search time before it can write anything meaningful. Don't worry about early stalls in line count — check the tmux pane instead.
- The two-tier gap structure is a very useful framing. Future goals should ask for similar stratification of difficulties.
- Naming 5+ specific papers and saying "and any others you find" is the right balance — not too constraining, not too open.
