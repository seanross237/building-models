# Meta-Learning: Exploration 004

## What worked well
- The "numerical vs rigorous" classification scheme produced very clear output. Every major result was tagged.
- Asking for "proof strategies with specific bottlenecks" got exactly that — 5 strategies, each with a clear bottleneck.
- The explorer discovered very recent results (Adhikari-Cao 2025, Chatterjee 2024) that significantly update the picture.
- The 7-step gap structure is even more precise than the 2-tier structure from exploration 001.

## What didn't work
- Nothing major failed. This was the cleanest exploration so far.

## Lessons
- When probing a well-studied area, asking for "the precise gap between numerical and rigorous" forces the explorer to be honest and specific. This should be a standard prompt pattern.
- Recent (2020-2025) results changed the landscape significantly — always ask explorers to check for work in the last 5 years specifically.
- The "finite → continuous gauge group" barrier emerged as the key bottleneck — this wasn't obvious before the exploration and is a strong candidate for computational attack.
