# Exploration 010: What Comes Next — Future Directions and Open Problems

## Context

After nine explorations, we've developed a complete interpretation of time:

**"Time is the computationally irreducible processing of quantum correlations, as encountered from within a relational perspective."**

This interpretation:
- Explains six features of time (one-dimensionality, irreversibility, directionality, open future, flow, gravitational dilation) from a unified computational framework
- Is grounded in established physics (Landauer, Bennett, Bekenstein, Margolus-Levitin, Wolpert, Hoehn)
- Has been stress-tested through multiple adversarial attacks
- Honestly acknowledges its limits (factorization problem, needs Past Hypothesis for direction, weakest on "now," no novel predictions, flow claim is interpretive)

## Your Mission

**Identify the most promising research directions this interpretation opens.** Think forward: if you had unlimited time and resources, what would you investigate next? What could strengthen the interpretation, test it, extend it, or connect it to other domains?

For each direction, provide:
1. What the research question is
2. Why it matters (what would success achieve?)
3. What the approach would be
4. Feasibility assessment (easy, hard, possibly impossible)
5. Expected payoff (revolutionary, significant, incremental)

## Directions to Investigate

### 1. The Factorization Problem
The interpretation presupposes subsystem decomposition. In quantum gravity, this may be emergent. Carroll's "quantum mereology" asks: given a Hilbert space and Hamiltonian, what's the best factorization?

**Questions:** Can quantum mereology provide the subsystem decomposition the interpretation needs? Does the "best" factorization depend on the dynamics (and hence on time)? Is there a way to make the subsystem decomposition and the temporal structure co-emerge without circularity?

### 2. The Unruh-ML Connection
The Margolus-Levitin bound sets the computation rate as E/h. In an accelerating frame, the Unruh effect introduces a temperature T = ℏa/2πck. Does this modify the ML bound? Does the Unruh-ML connection give a computational account of the equivalence principle?

**Questions:** Can we derive the equivalence principle from computational bounds? Is there a "Landauer-Unruh" connection — does the Unruh temperature set a minimum computational cost for information processing in an accelerating frame?

### 3. Testing Computational Irreducibility in Physical Systems
The interpretation claims the universe is computationally irreducible. Cubitt et al. (2015) showed the spectral gap problem is undecidable. Are there other physical manifestations of CI?

**Questions:** Are there systems where computational irreducibility has measurable consequences? Does the spectral gap undecidability have practical implications? Can quantum simulation experiments distinguish between CI and mere complexity?

### 4. Consciousness and Temporal Experience
The interpretation says becoming is what CI "feels like from inside." But it doesn't explain the "hard problem" of temporal experience — WHY there is a subjective "now."

**Questions:** Does the computational interpretation connect to theories of consciousness (IIT, Global Workspace)? Does CI play a role in conscious experience of time? Is the "now" problem fundamentally about consciousness rather than physics?

### 5. Implications for Quantum Gravity
The interpretation is built from QG-adjacent concepts (QRF, entanglement, holography). Does it constrain which QG approaches are viable?

**Questions:** Which QG programs are most compatible with the computational interpretation? Does the interpretation favor causal set theory (which has a built-in temporal ordering) over string theory (which treats time as a background parameter)? Can the interpretation distinguish between QG approaches?

### 6. The Arrow Without the Past Hypothesis
The biggest remaining weakness: the LBB mechanism explains the engine but not the fuel. Can the fuel be explained?

**Questions:** Can the Barbour-Koslowski-Mercati "Janus point" approach (arrow from Newtonian gravity without Past Hypothesis) be integrated with LBB? Does cosmological expansion naturally provide non-equilibrium conditions? Is there a way to get the direction from CI + gravity without ad hoc initial conditions?

### 7. Novel Predictions
The interpretation currently makes no novel empirical predictions. Can this change?

**Questions:** Does the ML bound at Planck energy (Planck time) have observable consequences? Are there quantum information experiments that could distinguish the computational interpretation from standard QM? Does the LBB mechanism make different predictions from the standard thermodynamic arrow in any regime?

## What You Should Produce

1. For each of the 7 directions above: the assessment requested (question, why it matters, approach, feasibility, payoff)
2. A ranking: which directions are most promising?
3. A recommendation: if a follow-up strategy had 10 more explorations, what should it focus on?
