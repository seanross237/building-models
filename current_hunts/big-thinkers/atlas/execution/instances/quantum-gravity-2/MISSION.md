# Mission

Develop a novel theory of quantum gravity. Not a rediscovery of existing theories — something genuinely new. The theory should be explainable in plain language to a thoughtful non-physicist at its core, even if the technical details require expertise. Start from scratch. Do not assume any particular existing framework is correct.

The theory should offer novel explanations for observed phenomena — why gravity behaves the way it does, what spacetime actually is, what happens at extreme scales. It should make predictions, even speculative ones, that differ from what existing approaches predict.

Prioritize originality and explanatory clarity over mathematical formalism. A theory that explains something in a new way that makes intuitive sense is more valuable here than one that reproduces known results through conventional methods. Be bold. Test your ideas honestly.
