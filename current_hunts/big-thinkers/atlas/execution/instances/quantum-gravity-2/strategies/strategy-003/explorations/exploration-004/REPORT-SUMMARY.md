# Exploration 004 Summary — Inflationary Prediction Reconciliation

**Goal:** Determine whether QG+F and AS inflationary predictions (r, n_s) can be reconciled, focusing on pinning down what AS specifically predicts for r.

**What was tried:** Systematically reviewed all major AS inflation models (Bonanno-Reuter 2002, Bonanno-Platania 2015/2018, Codello et al. 2014, Gubitosi et al. 2018, Pawlowski et al. 2024) and cross-checked against QG+F predictions (Anselmi-Piva 2020, Bianchi-Gamonal 2025).

**Outcome:** **SUPPORTS** (moderate confidence) — the predictions are reconcilable.

**Key finding:** The pre-loaded framing was misleading. The claim that "AS predicts r up to ~0.01" comes from one specific model (Bonanno-Platania with maximal logarithmic correction parameter b ~ 10⁻²). The majority of AS inflation papers — Codello et al. (2014), Gubitosi et al. (2018), and others — show that AS naturally **produces Starobinsky inflation** via RG flow, with r ≈ 0.003. This falls squarely within the QG+F prediction window of r ∈ [0.0004, 0.0035]. Both theories predict Starobinsky inflation as their inflationary mechanism; they are not rival mechanisms producing different r values, but rather different UV completions of the same inflationary model.

**Key takeaway:** r is NOT the clean discriminator between QG+F and AS that the library entry suggested. The two overlap at r ≈ 0.003. The missing calculation that would sharpen this is AS inflation in the full R² + C² (fourth-order) truncation rather than just f(R).

**Leads worth pursuing:**
- The Bianchi-Gamonal (2025) formula r ≈ 3(1−β/6α)(n_s−1)² gives a clean prediction for how the W² coupling modifies r — connecting this to AS beta functions for the W² coupling could produce a unified prediction
- The six-derivative QG+F extension (r ≈ 0.0045, n_s ≈ 0.974) may overlap with the emergent potential results from Pawlowski et al. (r ≈ 0.005)
- Tensor sound speed ratio c_t/c_s could provide discrimination beyond r alone
