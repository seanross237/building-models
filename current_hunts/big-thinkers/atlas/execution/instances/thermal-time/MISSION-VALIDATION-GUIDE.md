# Mission Validation Guide — Thermal Time Decoherence

## What Success Looks Like

Either a novel, quantitative prediction that distinguishes the thermal time hypothesis from standard quantum mechanics, or a rigorous demonstration of empirical equivalence. Either outcome, if properly derived and defended, is a publishable contribution to the foundations of physics.

## Validation Tiers

### Tier 1: Formalization
- The physical setup is precisely defined — systems, states, algebras, boundary
- The modular Hamiltonians are written down explicitly for at least one concrete system
- The thermal time flow is computed, not just described
- The standard QM prediction for the same system is computed for comparison
- Tomita-Takesaki machinery is applied correctly (modular operator, modular conjugation, KMS condition)

### Tier 2: Derivation
- The predicted observable (decoherence rate or other) is derived from the thermal time hypothesis
- Steps are explicit enough to be checked by someone with operator algebra background
- Computation is provided where analytical results are claimed
- Intermediate results are verified against known cases (e.g., Rindler wedge modular Hamiltonian, Bisognano-Wichmann theorem)

### Tier 3: Discrimination
- The thermal time prediction is compared quantitatively against the standard QM prediction
- Either a difference is found (with magnitude and parameter dependence), or equivalence is demonstrated
- If equivalent for one observable, alternative observables have been explored
- The result is not an artifact of a specific system choice — checked for at least 2 different setups

### Tier 4: Novelty and Robustness
- Prior art search: checked Connes-Rovelli (1994), Martinetti-Rovelli, Haggard-Rovelli on thermal time, Borchers on modular theory, and the broader algebraic QFT / quantum thermodynamics literature
- The specific prediction (or proof of equivalence) is new
- Assumptions are identified and the result is tested under alternative assumptions
- Strongest objection identified and addressed
- The result would be defensible in front of an algebraic QFT specialist

### Tier 5: Measurability
- If a discriminating prediction exists, an experimental scenario is identified (even in principle)
- The required precision, temperature, and conditions are specified
- Connections to Unruh effect, Rindler observers, and black hole thermodynamics are explicit
- The result is situated in the broader "is time fundamental?" debate with clear implications

## What "Exhausted" Looks Like

A strategy is exhausted when:
- The modular theory calculations are complete for the systems considered and no new systems are likely to change the conclusion
- Either a discriminating prediction has been found and verified, or all plausible observables have been checked without finding one
- The prior art search has converged
