# Exploration 004 Summary: Asymptotic Safety ↔ Quadratic Gravity

## Goal
Determine whether asymptotic safety (AS) and quadratic gravity with fakeon quantization (QG+F) are the same theory, complementary, or distinct.

## What Was Tried
Systematic comparison across six dimensions: UV propagator structure, fixed point vs. asymptotic freedom, exact RG flow for the quadratic gravity action, ghost/fakeon resolution, spectral dimension, and recent (2024-2026) literature review. Analyzed key papers by Sen-Wetterich-Yamada, Bonanno-Pawlowski et al., Platania-Wetterich, Buccio-Donoghue-Menezes-Percacci, Salvio-Strumia, Buoninfante, and others.

## Outcome: SUCCEEDED — Strong Convergence Found

**QG+F and AS are two faces of the same theory** — specifically, QG+F is the perturbative sector and AS is the non-perturbative completion. The evidence:

1. **Identical UV propagator:** Both give G(p²) ~ 1/p⁴ — via explicit R²/C² terms (QG) or anomalous dimension η_N = -2 (AS). Knorr et al. showed that reconstructing the AS propagator non-perturbatively reproduces the quadratic-curvature form factors.

2. **Two fixed points in the same theory space:** Sen, Wetterich & Yamada (JHEP 2022) found that the functional RG applied to higher-derivative gravity reveals both an asymptotically free fixed point (= quadratic gravity) and the Reuter fixed point (= asymptotic safety), connected by a critical RG trajectory. They are not competing but complementary — different fixed points of the same theory. Their 2023 follow-up confirmed scaling solutions connecting the AF fixed point to the IR.

3. **Ghost resolution converges from ALL directions:** The fakeon prescription (perturbative), the fictitious ghost mechanism (non-perturbative, Platania-Wetterich 2020), AND the perturbative resummation (Buccio-Donoghue-Menezes-Percacci 2025) all converge on the same answer: the Stelle ghost has **complex conjugate poles**, not a real pole, and no negative-norm states appear in the physical spectrum. The non-perturbative AS spectral function (Bonanno et al. 2022) confirms the same pole structure.

4. **Spectral dimension bridge:** Both predict d_s = 4 → 2, and this flow uniquely requires a 1/p⁴ propagator, which is the defining feature of both approaches.

5. **Physical beta functions (NEW, 2024):** Buccio et al. showed that the physical running of couplings (as opposed to scheme-dependent MS-bar beta functions) confirms asymptotic freedom is compatible with the absence of tachyons, strengthening the QG side.

## Key Takeaway

**The theory selected by the spectral dimension constraint (quadratic gravity + fakeon) is the perturbative face of asymptotic safety.** This is analogous to QCD: perturbative QCD (asymptotic freedom) and non-perturbative QCD (confinement) are the same theory. Similarly, quadratic gravity (asymptotic freedom in f₂) and asymptotic safety (Reuter fixed point) are the same quantum gravity. Neither is complete without the other: QG needs AS for the f₀ UV fixed point and non-perturbative ghost resolution; AS needs QG for perturbative computability and unitarity.

This greatly strengthens the case: the theory passes both perturbative tests (renormalizability, asymptotic freedom, unitarity via fakeon) AND non-perturbative tests (UV fixed point, ghost resolution, positive spectral function).

## Leads Worth Pursuing
- The f₀ coupling needs a UV fixed point (not asymptotically free) — this constrains matter content beyond the Standard Model. Worth investigating what matter is required.
- Compare Buccio et al.'s physical beta functions to the non-perturbative AS beta functions — if they match, this is the most direct equivalence proof.
- The IHO approach (2026 paper, arXiv:2603.07150) offers a third ghost resolution with testable CMB predictions — worth tracking.
- Torsion extension (JHEP 08 2025) preserves asymptotic freedom — explore whether AS in Palatini formulation gives the same fixed-point structure.
- The exact mapping between fakeon prescription and non-perturbative ghost resolution is not yet constructed — this would be a novel contribution.
