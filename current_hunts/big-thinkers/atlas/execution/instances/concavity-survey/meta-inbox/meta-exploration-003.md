# Meta-Learning: Exploration 003

## What Worked
- The synthesis format (cross-reference + rank + critical path + failure modes) produced genuinely new insight — the matrix domination approach emerged from combining the two surveys, not from either alone.
- Pre-loading detailed findings from both prior explorations directly into the GOAL.md gave the explorer all the context it needed without any library lookups.
- Asking for "connections between surveys" as an explicit success criterion forced the explorer to do synthesis rather than just juxtaposing.

## What Didn't Work / Could Improve
- The goal was long (~200 lines). For a synthesis task this was appropriate, but for other tasks I'd aim for <100 lines.
- Could have explicitly asked "what approach do the prior surveys NOT name that the synthesis reveals?" to more directly prompt for emergent ideas like matrix domination.

## Key Lesson
Synthesis explorations are highly valuable when fed with detailed prior findings and asked for connections and emergent approaches. The best finding of the entire mission (matrix domination) came from the synthesis, not from either survey alone. Budget at least one exploration for synthesis in any multi-survey mission.
