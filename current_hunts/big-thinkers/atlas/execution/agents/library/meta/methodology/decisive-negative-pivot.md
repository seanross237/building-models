## Lesson

When dimensional analysis produces a discrepancy of many orders of magnitude (e.g., 43 orders), it is a **hard kill** — the literal hypothesis cannot be rescued by small corrections or refinements. Do not spend further explorations trying to save it. Instead, immediately pivot to: what DOES work at the right scale?

## Evidence

- **compton-unruh strategy-001 exploration 001** — The Compton-Unruh resonance hypothesis failed by 43 orders of magnitude. This is not a "maybe with corrections" situation — it's a fundamental dimensional mismatch. The strategizer correctly identified the Gibbons-Hawking crossover (which IS at the right scale) as the pivot point for future work.

## When to apply

After any exploration that performs dimensional analysis or order-of-magnitude estimation. If the discrepancy is:
- < 2 orders of magnitude: potentially rescuable, investigate corrections
- 2-5 orders: unlikely but not impossible, do one more exploration to check
- > 10 orders: hard kill, pivot immediately
- > 40 orders: absolute kill, don't even consider rescue

## Extended: Negative Results That Eliminate Directions Are High-Value Phase 3 Outputs

Beyond the dimensional analysis context, negative results that **eliminate an entire tightening direction** are among the most valuable Phase 3 outputs. They save the exploration budget that would otherwise be spent pursuing the dead direction.

- **navier-stokes strategy-001 exploration-006** — Proved that spectral support alone CANNOT improve the sharp Ladyzhenskaya constant as a worst-case bound (LP cross terms dominate at 63%, adversarial phases always achieve near-sharp constants). This eliminates the "spectral Ladyzhenskaya proof" direction entirely, redirecting future work toward flatness bounds or structural constraints from NS dynamics.

**Implication for strategizers:** When a negative result eliminates a direction, explicitly mark it as such in the strategy state and redirect budget. One exploration that kills a direction is worth more than three that make incremental progress.

## Strategic implication

Budget exactly one exploration for dimensional analysis of any new quantitative hypothesis. If it fails by > 10 orders, redirect the strategy toward the nearest physically viable feature identified during the analysis.
