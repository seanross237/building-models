## Finding

There IS a genuine physical feature at a ~ cH_0: a **crossover from acceleration-dominated to cosmological-horizon-dominated thermal effects**. This occurs at the right acceleration scale (a ~ cH_0 ~ a_0) but does NOT constitute a resonance and provides no mechanism for inertia modification.

## The De Sitter Modified Temperature

Standard result (Deser & Levin 1997):

    T_dS(a, H) = (hbar / (2*pi*c*k_B)) * sqrt(a^2 + c^2*H^2)

This interpolates between:
- **Flat Unruh** (a >> cH): T_dS -> T_U = hbar*a/(2*pi*c*k_B)
- **Static de Sitter** (a = 0): T_dS -> T_GH = hbar*H/(2*pi*k_B)
- **At a = cH_0**: T_dS = sqrt(2) * T_GH ~ 3.78 x 10^-30 K

## Key Properties

1. **T_GH acts as a temperature floor**: As acceleration decreases below cH_0, the temperature levels off at T_GH ~ 2.67 x 10^-30 K rather than continuing to zero.
2. **The crossover is smooth**: No discontinuity, no peak — a gradual transition.
3. **The crossover scale matches a_0**: a_0/cH_0 = 0.182, so a_0 ~ cH_0/5.5. Within a factor of 6.
4. **T_U(cH_0) = T_GH identically**: This is an algebraic identity, not a coincidence. T_U(a) evaluated at a = cH_0 gives hbar*cH_0/(2*pi*c*k_B) = hbar*H_0/(2*pi*k_B) = T_GH.

## What This Crossover Does NOT Do

- Does NOT involve the Compton frequency of any particle
- Does NOT produce a resonance (no pole, peak, or non-analyticity)
- Does NOT provide a mechanism for modifying inertia
- Does NOT explain MOND phenomenology

## Significance

The crossover means that below a ~ cH_0, a particle's thermal environment is dominated by the cosmological horizon rather than its own acceleration. Whether this has physical consequences for inertia is a separate question from the "Compton-Unruh resonance" and would require an entirely different theoretical framework.

## Scale Reference

The Gibbons-Hawking floor T_GH ~ 2.7 x 10^-30 K is 42 orders of magnitude below the proton Compton "temperature" T_C = m_p*c^2/k_B ~ 1.09 x 10^13 K.

The Unruh wavelength at a_0 is lambda_U(a_0) ~ 2.96 x 10^28 m, roughly 217 times the Hubble radius R_H ~ 1.36 x 10^26 m.
