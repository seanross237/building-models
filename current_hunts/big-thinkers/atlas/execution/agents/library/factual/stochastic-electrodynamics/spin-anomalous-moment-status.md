## Pure SED: No Natural Framework for Spin

SED does not naturally incorporate spin. The electron is a classical point charge with no intrinsic angular momentum, making g-2 formulation fundamentally problematic.

## de la Pena & Cetto (1981) -- Phenomenological Treatment

Proposed that ZPF-induced zitterbewegung accounts for spin. By selecting subensemble of ZPF with given circular polarization, argued for a "satisfactory account" of spin projection and anomalous magnetic moment. Assessment: qualitative, not first-principles. Assumes connection between ZPF circular polarization and spin rather than deriving it.

## Cavalleri et al. (2010) -- SEDS (SED with Spin)

Extended electron model: center of charge in circular motion around center of mass at zitterbewegung frequency. Claims mass and charge corrections "without logarithmic divergence terms" and anomalous magnetic moment accurate to 9 decimal places.

**This claim should be viewed with extreme skepticism:**
1. Not pure SED -- adds spin as fundamental modification
2. Extended electron model with additional assumptions
3. 9-decimal precision from a classical theory would rival QED's Schwinger result
4. Published in Frontiers of Physics in China, not a standard foundations journal
5. Not independently verified by any other group
6. Companion Lamb shift claim uses hand-imposed cutoff

## Boyer (1988) -- Lamb Shift Environmental Effects

More rigorous treatment of how environmental modifications of the EM vacuum affect the Lamb shift. Results coincide with QED for the *modification* by boundaries, but does not address the Lamb shift itself.

## Status: NOT A PRODUCTIVE DIRECTION

Pure SED has no spin; SEDS claims are from a small, non-mainstream group with unverified results. Theoretical framework too uncertain for productive exploration.
