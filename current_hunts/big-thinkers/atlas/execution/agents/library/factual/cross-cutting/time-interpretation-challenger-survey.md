# Time Interpretation Challenger Survey: Systematic Comparative Assessment

## Summary

Systematic scored comparison of five non-computational interpretations of time against a six-feature benchmark, to select the strongest challenger to the computational interpretation ("time is the computationally irreducible processing of quantum correlations"). The **Causal-Processual Hybrid** was selected as the strongest challenger (36/40), narrowly beating Causal Fundamentalism (35/40). See [causal-processual-interpretation.md](causal-processual-interpretation.md) for the detailed sketch of the selected challenger.

## The Six-Feature Benchmark

Any complete interpretation of time must explain these six features:

| # | Feature | Description |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | **One-dimensionality** | Why time has exactly one dimension |
| 2 | **Irreversibility** | Why processes can't run backward |
| 3 | **Directionality** | Why time has a preferred direction (arrow) |
| 4 | **Open future** | Why the future is not predetermined |
| 5 | **Flow** | Why time seems to pass/move |
| 6 | **Dilation** | Why time runs at different rates in different frames |

The computational interpretation (benchmark) scores these via a three-layer architecture (QRF dissolution, computational structure, phenomenology). Its five acknowledged weaknesses: (1) the "now," (2) factorization problem, (3) arrow direction needs Past Hypothesis, (4) no novel predictions, (5) flow claim is interpretive. See [computational-irreducibility-thesis.md](computational-irreducibility-thesis.md) for full details.

## Candidate Assessments

### A. Causal Fundamentalism (Maudlin / Sorkin / CST)

**Core claim:** Time IS the causal ordering of events. Causation is constitutive of temporal structure. Direction, passage, and topology of time are primitive features of causal order.

**Key versions:** Maudlin's directed topology (primitive directedness, linear structures replacing open sets); Rideout-Sorkin sequential growth dynamics (elements "born" one at a time, stochastic, respecting general covariance).

**Scores:** One-dimensionality: 4, Irreversibility: 3, Directionality: **5** (strongest suit — direction IS the causal order, no Past Hypothesis), Open future: 3, Flow: 2 (primitive passage is assertion, not explanation), Dilation: 3. **Feature total: 20/30.**

**Meta-criteria:** Physics grounding: 5 (CST is serious QG program, Malament's "order + number = geometry" theorem). Strength vs CI weaknesses: 3 (much better on arrow, no better on "now" or flow). Vulnerability: 3. Novelty vs CI: 4 (genuinely different — causal vs computational). **Meta total: 15/20.**

**Key vulnerabilities:** (1) Circularity risk ("time is causal order" + "causal order is temporal" may be definitional). (2) Maudlin's primitive passage is assertive but explanatorily thin. (3) CST continuum limit recovery incomplete. (4) Doesn't address phenomenology. (5) One-dimensionality answer may reduce to "that's how partial orders work."

**Why not selected:** Close second (35/40). Strongest physics grounding. But inability to address flow (score: 2) is a major liability — CI at least offers "computational irreducibility from inside."

### B. Thermodynamic Realism (Prigogine)

**Core claim:** Time IS the real, irreducible process of entropy production and dissipative self-organization. Irreversibility is fundamental, not statistical.

**Scores:** One-dimensionality: 1 (nothing to say), Irreversibility: **5** (centerpiece — Poincare resonances, non-unitary transformations, constructive role of dissipation), Directionality: 4, Open future: 3, Flow: 2, Dilation: 1 (no account). **Feature total: 16/30.**

**Meta-criteria:** Physics grounding: 4 (Nobel Prize for dissipative structures, but fundamental irreversibility claim controversial). Strength vs CI weaknesses: 2. Vulnerability: 2. Novelty vs CI: 2 (overlaps via Landauer-Bennett-Bekenstein triad). **Meta total: 10/20. Grand total: 26/40.**

**Key vulnerabilities:** (1) Mainstream objection: second law IS statistical (majority physics view). (2) No account of spacetime structure (1D, dilation). (3) Scope too narrow. (4) Poincare resonance argument technically contested.

### C. Experience-First / Consciousness-Based (Bergson / Husserl / IIT)

**Core claim:** Time is fundamentally a feature of experience/consciousness. The "hard problem of time" — why there is felt passage, lived present, experienced duration — is the primary datum.

**Key contributions not elsewhere in library:**
- **Husserl's temporal phenomenology:** Every consciousness-moment has three-part structure: primal impression (now), retention (just-past still present), protention (anticipation of about-to-come). The "now" is not a point but a thick structure with temporal breadth (specious present).
- **IIT's temporal structure:** Temporal experience is a structural feature of the system's cause-effect structure, not produced by processing in clock time. Phenomenal distinctions are directed and related via inclusion, connection, and fusion.

**Scores:** One-dimensionality: 2, Irreversibility: 3, Directionality: 3, Open future: 4, Flow: **5** (raison d'etre — Husserl's analysis of continuous retentional modification is the most detailed philosophical account of temporal flow), Dilation: 1. **Feature total: 18/30.**

**Meta-criteria:** Physics grounding: 2 (Bergson is pure philosophy; Husserl rigorous but non-physical; IIT controversial). Strength vs CI weaknesses: 3 (MUCH better on "now" via specious present and primal impression). Vulnerability: 2. Novelty vs CI: 5 (opposite approach — phenomenological vs physicalist). **Meta total: 12/20. Grand total: 30/40.**

**Key vulnerabilities:** (1) No physics — can't explain 1D, dilation, physical irreversibility. (2) Idealism risk: what happens to time without experiencers? (3) Explanatory direction unclear. (4) Inherits the hard problem of consciousness.

### D. Causal-Processual Hybrid (Selected Challenger)

See [causal-processual-interpretation.md](causal-processual-interpretation.md) for full details. See [cp-adversarial-assessment.md](cp-adversarial-assessment.md) for post-attack assessment.

**Initial scores (E001):** One-dimensionality: 4, Irreversibility: 4, Directionality: **5** (triple-grounded), Open future: 4, Flow: 3, Dilation: 3. **Feature total: 23/30.**

**Revised scores (E002 — full construction with actualization as unifying principle, Husserlian flow, CST chain-length dilation, perspectival "now"):** One-dimensionality: 4, Irreversibility: **5**, Directionality: **5**, Open future: 4, Flow: **4**, Dilation: **4**. **Feature total: 26/30.**

**Post-attack scores (E003 — adversarial assessment):** One-dimensionality: 4, Irreversibility: **4.5** (-0.5: Prigogine dependency), Directionality: **5** (survived all attacks), Open future: 4, Flow: **3.5** (-0.5: panexperientialism dependency), Dilation: **4**. **Feature total: 25/30.**

**Meta-criteria:** Physics grounding: 3 (attacked: thin physics, 1 unconfirmed framework + 3 philosophies). Strength vs CI weaknesses: 4. Vulnerability: 2.5 (increased: near-total CI structural isomorphism + panexperientialism trap). Novelty vs CI: 3.5 (decreased: convergence deeper than claimed, precise forks are narrower). **Meta total: 13/20. Grand total: 38/40 (post-attack).**

**Tournament result (E004 — formal head-to-head):** In a 13-criterion tournament with impartial judge, CP won 48.5 to CI's 47.5 — a 1.0-point near-draw out of 65. CP's advantages: directionality (+1.5), "what is now?" (+1.5), explanatory depth (+1.0). CI's advantages: physics grounding (+2.5), irreversibility mechanism (+1.0). Verdict: margin is fragile (different weighting could flip), CI more likely correct but CP more illuminating. See [ci-vs-cp-tournament.md](ci-vs-cp-tournament.md).

### E. Additional Candidates (Brief Assessments)

**E1. Smolin's Temporal Naturalism (standalone):** Time is real, laws evolve, cosmological natural selection. Philosophically powerful but underdeveloped physically. Scores: 16/30 features, 27/40 grand total.

**E2. Retrocausal / Two-State Vector:** Time is fundamentally symmetric; asymmetry is perspectival. Wrong direction for challenger — WORSE at explaining temporal asymmetry, flow, etc. Open future explicitly denied. Scores: 9/30 features. **Not viable.**

**E3. Relational / Rovellian Time:** Time emerges from relations between physical systems. Already close to CI's Layer 1 (QRF dissolution). Would duplicate CI rather than challenge it. **Not a genuine alternative.**

## Selection Rationale

The Causal-Processual Hybrid wins on: (1) best feature coverage (only candidate scoring 3+ on ALL six features), (2) strongest against CI's key weakness (#3 arrow — triple grounding without Past Hypothesis), (3) genuinely different metaphysics (processual/causal vs informational/computational), (4) high development potential (pieces individually well-developed, integration is the work).

**Critical concern:** The hybrid risks being a Frankenstein — four traditions that weren't designed to fit together. Must find a unifying principle.
