# Gravitational Wave Speed (Constraint D2)

**Statement:** The speed of gravitational waves must equal the speed of light to extraordinary precision.

**Bound:** |c_gw/c - 1| < 6 × 10⁻¹⁵ (GW170817 + GRB170817A, 2017)

This is from the near-simultaneous arrival of gravitational waves and gamma rays from a binary neutron star merger, with travel time ~130 million years.

**Mathematical form:**
In modified gravity: c_gw² = c² (1 + α_T), where α_T parameterizes deviations.
Bound: |α_T| < 6 × 10⁻¹⁵

**Restrictiveness: HIGH**

This single measurement eliminated entire classes of modified gravity theories in one stroke:
- Many Horndeski theories (specific L4 and L5 operators)
- Some f(R) theories
- Certain massive gravity variants
- Forces c_T = 1 in dark energy models, dramatically restricting the viable parameter space

## Significance

GW170817 is arguably the single most impactful experimental result for constraining modified gravity. Before this measurement, the theoretical landscape for gravitational alternatives to dark energy was much broader. Afterward, only theories predicting c_gw = c survive.
