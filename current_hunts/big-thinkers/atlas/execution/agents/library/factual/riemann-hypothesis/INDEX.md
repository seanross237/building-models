## Trace Formula Limitations

- **trace-formula-gibbs-phenomenon.md** — The explicit prime sum for N_osc(T) converges to the midpoint at step discontinuities (Gibbs phenomenon), producing an irreducible ½ offset at every zero independent of how many primes are included; residual converges as P_max^{−0.13}; to reach residual 0.01 requires ~30 million primes. Correct formula: N_osc ∝ sin(T·ln p) — no ln(p) factor in numerator.
- **prime-corrections-statistical-partial-success.md** — Prime corrections improve bulk statistics (number variance: 75.5% improvement) but destroy local correlations (level repulsion β collapses from 2.32 to 0.03). The trace formula captures spectral density but not spectral correlations.
- **individual-zero-reconstruction-impossible.md** — Individual zeta zero positions cannot be reconstructed from the explicit prime formula — a mathematical impossibility. N_osc at smooth zeros is always exactly ±0.5; linearized corrections get direction right but magnitude wrong (variance explained = −489%). More primes make reconstruction worse, not better.
