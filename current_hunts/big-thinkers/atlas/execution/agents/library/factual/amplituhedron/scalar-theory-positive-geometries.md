## Finding

The most successful extension of positive geometry beyond N=4 SYM is to **scalar theories**. These are genuine (not toy model) positive geometry constructions that work at tree level with no SUSY and no Yangian symmetry required.

## Bi-Adjoint ϕ³ — The ABHY Associahedron (arXiv:1711.09102)

For bi-adjoint scalar ϕ³ theory (scalars in the adjoint of two gauge groups): partial amplitudes m(α|β) are exactly the **canonical form** of an (n-3)-dimensional associahedron embedded in kinematic space. The associahedron is defined by requiring all planar Mandelstam variables sᵢⱼ ≥ 0 in the ordering.

No SUSY, no Yangian, no dual conformal — pure kinematic space geometry. **This is the cleanest extension**: non-supersymmetric, non-gauge, and it works perfectly. The color structure exactly matches associahedron boundary combinatorics. Works because the theory has no UV divergences at tree level.

**Key insight from Section 9**: The relative weights of different ϕ³ Feynman diagrams are fixed by the geometry — you can recover Feynman rules from the associahedron alone, which is conceptually beyond what standard methods provide.

## ϕ⁴ Theory — Stokes Polytopes (arXiv:1811.05904, JHEP 2019)

Banerjee, Laddha, Raman: Planar ϕ⁴ partial amplitudes correspond to canonical forms of **Stokes polytopes** (a different class of polytopes). Multiple Stokes polytopes must be summed with specific weights for the full amplitude — messier than ϕ³ but still a valid positive geometry. Follow-up (arXiv:1911.06008): Stokes polytopes embed into ABHY associahedra as lower-dimensional faces.

## Multi-Scalar — Accordiohedra (arXiv:2104.04915, JHEP 2022)

Jagadale & Laddha: For multi-scalar interactions (mixed ϕ₁³ + ϕ₁²ϕ₂ terms), **accordiohedra** — polytopes generalizing associahedra — provide the positive geometry. In decoupling limits, projects onto accordiohedra for ϕ³ or ϕ³ + gϕ⁴ theories.

## The Associahedron as Universal Scaffold

The ABHY associahedron is NOT a universal positive geometry — but it is a universal **scaffold**. Other positive geometries for different theories are obtained by:
1. Embedding/projecting onto faces (Stokes polytopes)
2. Decorating the canonical form with polynomial factors (corolla polynomial for YM)
3. Deforming the geometry (accordiohedra, Stokes polytopes)

## Stringy Canonical Forms (arXiv:1912.08707, JHEP 2021)

Arkani-Hamed, He et al.: Replace the canonical form of associahedron P by ∫_P e^{−X/α'} dX. Applied to the ABHY associahedron in kinematic space, this produces the **Koba-Nielsen string integral** (open string n-point amplitude) — no worldsheet physics needed, just geometry. Direct connection from bi-adjoint scalar positive geometry to open string theory.

## Associahedral Grid (arXiv:2508.20161, 2025)

Bartsch, Kampf, Podivin, Stalknecht: Introduces the **associahedral grid** — a positive geometry capturing full α'-dependence of string amplitudes, including the **inverse KLT kernel**. The double-copy structure of gravity (gravity = YM²) has a geometric home for the first time via this grid.

## What Limits Scalar Extensions

- Works only at tree level (bi-adjoint ϕ³ in 6D is UV-divergent; no positive geometry for its loop amplitudes)
- Planarity / color-ordering required (non-planar scalar theories unresolved)
- Only scalar/bi-adjoint matter content — quarks and spin-1 fields require additional machinery
