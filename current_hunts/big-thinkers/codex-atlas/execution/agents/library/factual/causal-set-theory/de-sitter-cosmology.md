# De Sitter Spacetime from Causal Set Sequential Growth

Ahmed and Rideout (2009) demonstrated that a large class of classical sequential growth (CSG) dynamical laws for causal sets naturally produce a cyclic universe with "expansion and contraction phases separated by origin elements." Immediately following each origin element -- a special singular point marking the boundary between cycles -- the effective dynamics yields "an initial period of de Sitter like exponential expansion."

## How de Sitter Space Emerges

Within the CSG framework, elements are added to the causal set one at a time according to stochastic rules governed by the Rideout-Sorkin coupling constants. The origin elements act as "big bang" events from which new cycles begin. The growth process immediately after these origin points naturally generates an exponentially expanding spacetime geometry matching de Sitter space, without any fine-tuning of parameters.

## Cosmological Significance

This result is significant because de Sitter spacetime describes a universe undergoing exponential expansion, which is precisely the behavior observed during cosmological inflation in the early universe. The fact that de Sitter-like expansion emerges naturally from the causal set dynamics -- rather than requiring an inflaton field or other ad hoc mechanism -- suggests that CST may offer a fundamental explanation for why the early universe inflated.

The resulting cyclic picture "has many attractive features as a model of the early universe, with the potential to solve some of the standard model puzzles" in cosmology.
