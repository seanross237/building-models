# Steelman of the Barandes Program and Paths to Level 3

Adversarial review (E005) constructed the strongest possible case FOR the Barandes program and graded it honestly. The verdict: "Level 2+" — slightly stronger than the E001-E004 "Level 2 reformulation" classification, but still no operational novelty. Four steelman arguments evaluated, three concrete transition conditions identified.

## Steelman Argument 1: Measurement Problem Dissolution (SUBSTANTIVE)

Standard QM leaves the measurement problem open. ISP postulates that configurations ARE the physical reality and division events (decoherence) replace collapse — no extra postulates, no extra dynamics, no observer-dependence. This is genuinely different from Copenhagen (not realist), many-worlds (extra structure), GRW (new dynamics), and pilot wave (non-local).

**Honest grade:** More than Level 2. The dissolution without new postulates is substantive. But it doesn't produce new predictions for known physics. The measurement problem is also a technical obstacle for quantum gravity (decoherence when spacetime is quantum) — if ISP's configuration-space ontology extends to QG, it could enable new physics indirectly.

## Steelman Argument 2: Lagrangian Analogy (APT BUT INCOMPLETE)

Lagrangian mechanics was "just a reformulation" for ~150 years (1750-1900), then enabled variational calculus, Noether's theorem, gauge theories, QFT, and path integrals. The ISP analog would be a stochastic action principle: a functional S[p(qt|q0)] whose extremization gives quantum stochastic dynamics.

**Honest grade:** The analogy is apt but incomplete. The Lagrangian had the action principle from day one; ISP doesn't have its analog yet. The framework is young (2022-2025) and this takes decades to emerge.

## Steelman Argument 3: Open Systems Advantage (MOST PLAUSIBLE PATH)

Quantum information, computing, and thermodynamics all involve open quantum systems. Standard tools (Lindblad, CPTP maps) are awkward. ISP naturally accommodates non-unistochastic processes = CPTP maps (Stinespring dilation). Lindblad evolution = continuous limit of small division events. The Milz et al. framework (refs [22,23]) does exactly this work — ISP is the natural language for quantum non-Markovian systems.

**Honest grade:** The most plausible path to operational value. Not demonstrated yet, but the connection to quantum process theory is real. If ISP provides a new approach to quantum channel capacities or error-correction thresholds, that would be genuinely useful.

## Steelman Argument 4: Causal Locality as New Principle (PHILOSOPHICALLY STRONGEST)

The stochastic causal locality condition p(qt|q0,r0) = p(qt|q0) is more than no-signaling restated. It constrains the dynamical laws themselves and could be applied to systems not yet understood (quantum gravity), modifications of QM, or classical stochastic processes. The PR-box exclusion by causally local ISPs characterizes QM correlations as exactly the causally local ISP regime.

**Honest grade:** Philosophically valuable and best-justified of the four. The characterization "QM correlations = causally local ISP correlations" (assuming unistochastic) is a genuine insight. Whether it translates to anything operational remains to be seen.

## Level 2 → Level 3 Transition Conditions

The steelman reveals three specific requirements for the framework to move beyond reformulation:

1. **Non-unistochastic proof:** Prove that causally local general ISPs (not just unistochastic) also satisfy Tsirelson. This would make the Tsirelson result genuinely not assuming QM. (Removes the circularity identified in E005.)
2. **Stochastic variational principle:** Find a functional S[p] whose extremization gives quantum dynamics as equations of motion. This would be ISP's "Lagrangian moment."
3. **Operational quantum channel result:** Derive a result on channel capacities, error correction thresholds, or non-Markovian bounds from the stochastic language that is not more easily obtained from standard QM.

**None of these exist as of March 2026.**
