# AdS/CFT Correspondence

The Anti-de Sitter/Conformal Field Theory correspondence, proposed by Juan Maldacena in 1997, is the most concrete realization of the holographic principle and provides a non-perturbative definition of quantum gravity in certain spacetimes. It is the most cited paper in high-energy physics history (over 20,000 citations).

## The Duality Statement

The correspondence states that quantum gravity on (d+1)-dimensional asymptotically anti-de Sitter spacetime is exactly equivalent to a conformal field theory living on its d-dimensional boundary. The best-studied example is:

- **Bulk:** Type IIB superstring theory on AdS_5 x S^5 (with N units of five-form flux)
- **Boundary:** N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory in 4 dimensions with gauge group SU(N)

Both sides preserve 32 supercharges, enabling rigorous checks. The geometry arises as the near-horizon limit of N coincident D3-branes.

## How the Duality Works

The correspondence bridges two previously incompatible theoretical frameworks:

- **AdS side (the "bulk")**: Quantum gravity in anti-de Sitter space, which exhibits negative curvature everywhere -- distinguishing it from flat Minkowski space or positively-curved de Sitter space (which better describes our actual universe).
- **CFT side (the "boundary")**: Conformal field theory -- field theories preserving angles but not distances under conformal transformations -- living on the boundary of the AdS space, in one fewer dimension.

The elegance lies in problem-solving flexibility. Complex mathematical problems intractable on one side become tractable when translated to the other side.

## The 't Hooft Limit

The correspondence is most tractable in the 't Hooft limit: N -> infinity with the 't Hooft coupling lambda = g_YM^2 * N held fixed. In this regime, the boundary theory becomes planar (dominated by connected diagrams) while the bulk gravity becomes classical. Crucially, strong coupling on one side maps to weak coupling on the other -- perturbation theory on one side corresponds to non-perturbative physics on the other.

## Applications to Quantum Gravity

AdS/CFT provides a non-perturbative definition of quantum gravity: the gravitational path integral in the bulk is defined via the boundary CFT partition function and correlation functions. The Ryu-Takayanagi formula relates boundary entanglement entropy to bulk minimal surfaces, embedding information-theoretic principles into spacetime geometry. Black hole microstates in the bulk are identified with specific boundary CFT states, providing a mechanism for information preservation.

## The Holographic Principle

The AdS/CFT correspondence provides a concrete realization of the holographic principle, proposed by Gerardus 't Hooft and developed by Leonard Susskind. Information encoding occurs on an infinitely distant surface, describing all observable phenomena.

## Key Limitation: Our Universe Is Not AdS

A fundamental limitation is that our universe has a positive cosmological constant (approximately de Sitter), not negative (anti-de Sitter). De Sitter space has no timelike boundary at spatial infinity where a dual CFT could naturally live. Strominger proposed a dS/CFT correspondence in 2001, but it remains far less developed -- the putative dual CFT would be non-unitary (imaginary central charge), a major departure from the well-understood AdS case.
