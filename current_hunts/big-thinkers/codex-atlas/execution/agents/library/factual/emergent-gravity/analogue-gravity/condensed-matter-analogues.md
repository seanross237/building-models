# Condensed Matter Analogue Gravity Models

Analogue gravity is a research program investigating analogues of general relativistic gravitational fields within other physical systems, typically condensed matter systems, to gain insight into gravitational phenomena and emergent spacetime.

## Key Systems

1. **Acoustic/Sonic Black Holes ("Dumb Holes")**: William Unruh showed in 1981 that sound waves in a moving fluid experience an effective curved spacetime metric. A supersonic fluid flow creates an acoustic event horizon from which phonons cannot escape, directly analogous to a gravitational black hole. Even though the underlying fluid dynamics is Newtonian, nonrelativistic, and takes place in flat space-plus-time, the fluctuations (sound waves) are governed by a curved (3+1)-dimensional Lorentzian spacetime geometry.

2. **Bose-Einstein Condensates (BECs)**: BEC systems allow tuning of the scattering length to modify effective gravitational properties. In an appropriate regime, a modified non-relativistic gravitational theory appears as the effective description of coupled condensate/quasi-particle dynamics.

3. **Superfluids**: The irrotational condition is automatically satisfied for superfluid components, making them natural candidates. Superfluid 3He has been specifically studied.

4. **Optical Systems**: Light in moving dielectrics can mimic aspects of general relativity.

## Key Experimental Results

**Hawking Radiation Analogue (Steinhauer, 2014-2020)**: Jeff Steinhauer at Technion, Israel, conducted a series of landmark experiments using BECs of rubidium atoms at nano-Kelvin temperatures:
- 2014: Demonstrated stimulated Hawking radiation in a sonic black hole laser
- 2016: Showed spontaneous Hawking radiation from a sonic black hole
- 2020: Confirmed that spontaneous Hawking radiation is stationary by observing the system at six different times (Nature Physics, doi:10.1038/s41567-020-01076-0)

## Cosmological Simulations

Researchers have simulated Friedmann-Robertson-Walker geometries through "physical explosion" in condensates and rapid variations in the effective speed of light.

## Significance for Emergent Gravity

These models demonstrate that curved Lorentzian spacetime geometry can emerge from fundamentally flat, non-relativistic physics -- providing concrete proof-of-concept that gravity-like phenomena can be emergent. However, the analogy has limits: the analogue systems do not produce full dynamical gravity (Einstein's equations) but only the kinematic aspects (curved geometry for wave propagation).
