# "Gravitize the Quantum" — Landscape Overview

## The Reversed Arrow

Most quantum gravity research takes quantum mechanics as given and tries to apply it to gravity ("quantize gravity"). The "gravitize the quantum" direction reverses the arrow: take gravity/spacetime as more fundamental and derive quantum mechanics from it.

## What Programs Actually Do

A careful survey reveals that most programs advertised as "gravitize the quantum" don't literally derive QM from gravity. They fall into four categories:

### Category 1: Derive QM from Something Else (Not Gravity)
- **'t Hooft CAI:** QM from determinism (not gravity-specific)
- **Adler trace dynamics:** QM from statistical mechanics of non-commutative matrices
- **Barandes:** QM from indivisible stochastic processes

### Category 2: Modify QM With Gravity
- **Diósi-Penrose:** Takes QM as given, adds gravity-induced collapse
- **Oppenheim:** Takes classical gravity as given, couples to quantum matter stochastically

### Category 3: Derive Gravity From QM (Wrong Direction)
- **Jacobson, Verlinde, Bianconi:** Derive Einstein equations from quantum thermodynamics/entanglement

### Category 4: QM and Gravity From Common Substratum
- **Adler-Singh (full program):** Both QM and classical gravity emerge from pre-quantum matrix dynamics
- **Barandes-Verlinde:** Both QM and gravity from underlying stochastic causality

## The Most Promising Directions

**Ranked by experimental testability:**
1. **Oppenheim postquantum classical gravity** — decoherence-diffusion trade-off actively being constrained; could be ruled out/confirmed within a decade; pure gravity sector formally renormalizable (2024)
2. **Diósi-Penrose gravitational collapse** — specific collapse timescale τ = ℏ/E_G testable via massive superposition experiments
3. **Adler-Singh trace dynamics** — deepest mathematical derivation of QM from something more fundamental; testable via Singh's Standard Model predictions and collapse phenomenology, but at very high energies

**Ranked by theoretical depth:**
1. **Adler-Singh trace dynamics** — derives canonical commutation relations [q,p] = iℏ from equipartition theorem of non-commutative matrix thermodynamics. This is a genuine derivation, not repackaging.
2. **Barandes-Verlinde** — mathematical stochastic-quantum correspondence is rigorous; physical interpretation is deep but gravity connection remains conceptual
3. **Oppenheim** — well-developed framework but doesn't derive QM — it takes QM as given and constrains how gravity couples to it

## Key Open Question

**Is there a specific mechanism by which stochastic spacetime geometry produces the exact noise kernel needed for QM to emerge?** Answering this would turn "gravitize the quantum" from a research direction into a theory. A synthesis pipeline (stochastic spacetime → Nelson diffusion → Barandes lifting → QM; averaging → Einstein equations → GR; self-interaction → Diósi-Penrose collapse) has been sketched but lacks concrete mathematical development.
