## Lesson

The proven exploration sequence for developing a theoretical framework is: (1) extract/construct predictions, (2) attack with devil's advocate, (3) synthesize into final document. This order is critical -- the synthesis benefits from having attacks already mounted, and the attacks benefit from having concrete predictions to target.

## Evidence

- **strategy-003** — The s3-007 (construct) -> s3-008 (attack) -> s3-009 (sharpen) sequence produced the clearest assessment of framework strength. Construction gave material; attack found weaknesses; sharpening separated discriminators from consistency checks.
- **strategy-004** — The s4-001 through s4-005 (predict) -> s4-006 (attack) -> s4-007 (synthesize) -> s4-008 (validate) sequence was the most productive strategy cycle. Each step built cleanly on the previous.

## When to apply

Any strategy cycle that aims to produce a final assessment of a framework or theory. The validate step (s4-008) should use a fresh agent, not the one that wrote the synthesis -- the same agent will tend to defend its own work.
