## Lesson

When designing a numerical survey exploration (computing a quantity across multiple physical systems), the spread of scales is more important than having many systems at similar scales. A set of 6-7 systems spanning the full dynamic range (e.g., Planck to cosmological) tells a clear story about where a bound/formula is constraining and where it is trivial.

## Evidence

- **classicality-budget strategy-001 exploration 002** — Computing the classicality budget across 7 systems (Planck-scale, quantum computer, lab, brain, near-BH, solar-mass BH, observable universe) revealed that the budget spans 122 orders of magnitude and is only constraining at the Planck scale. This key insight — that classical reality has a finite but astronomically large budget except at the Planck scale — would not have emerged from computing many systems all at everyday scales.

## Pattern

For numerical surveys: choose systems that sample the extremes of the relevant parameter space, plus a few in between. The minimum useful set typically includes:
- The smallest/tightest case (where the bound is most constraining)
- The largest/loosest case (where the bound is trivially satisfied)
- A "human-scale" reference case
- An intermediate case that tests transitions

## When to apply

Any exploration that involves computing a quantity across multiple physical systems or parameter values. Especially important when the goal is to understand where a bound becomes tight vs. trivial.
