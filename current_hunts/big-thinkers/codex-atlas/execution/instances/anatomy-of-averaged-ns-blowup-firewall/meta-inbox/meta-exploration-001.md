# Meta Note: Exploration 001

## What worked

- The paper-first mechanism reconstruction was the right opening move. Local Atlas notes were enough to recover Tao's averaged operator, but not the actual circuit internals; forcing the exploration to separate operator, circuit, and blowup layers prevented drift.
- Preloading the Tao-filter notes from the far-field mission helped anchor the operator-level formulas without wasting time on already-known pressure observations.
- Treating Section 5's gates as the actual conceptual core of the blowup made the Section 6 shell system much easier to parse.

## What to carry forward

- For Tao-style barrier missions, do not stop at "generic harmonic-analysis structure survives." The decisive question is often whether the mechanism requires independently engineered couplings that exact NS cannot realize.
- When a paper builds a toy mechanism and then embeds it into the PDE, require the exploration to separate those layers explicitly. Otherwise later firewall analysis will target the wrong object.
- Tiny trigger variables can be more mechanism-relevant than the main energy carrier. Future intervention-map explorations should track dynamically decisive but energetically negligible variables explicitly.

## Execution note

- Primary-source reconstruction through AMS search snippets was sufficient for Phase 0, even without a locally downloadable PDF, because the mission needed mechanism architecture rather than every auxiliary estimate.
