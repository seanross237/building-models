# Mission Validation Guide — Anatomy of the Averaged-NS Blowup

## Tier 1: Mechanism Fidelity

- Tao's construction is analyzed at the level of the actual cascade, not only introductory slogans.
- The report distinguishes the averaged bilinear operator, the cascade model, and the final blowup mechanism.
- Claims about what averaging destroys are tied to explicit parts of Tao's construction.

## Tier 2: Firewall Precision

- Every proposed real-NS firewall is stated as a concrete mathematical object, not a vibe.
- The object is specific enough to imagine proving or falsifying.
- "Real NS has more structure" is not accepted unless the structure is written down explicitly.

## Tier 3: Causal Relevance

- The candidate firewall is shown to intervene in the actual Tao cascade mechanism, not just to be absent from the averaged model.
- The analysis identifies where the cascade would break, deform, or become unprovable in real NS.
- Cosmetic differences are separated from load-bearing ones.

## Tier 4: Prior-Work Discipline

- Closed estimate-level routes are cited rather than re-run.
- Host-space compactness and standard rewrite frameworks are not quietly reintroduced.
- The report explicitly checks whether the candidate firewall was already exhausted by prior missions or known literature.

## Tier 5: Outcome Sharpness

- A positive result names one strongest firewall candidate and one plausible host framework.
- A negative result says exactly why no candidate survives: vague, non-dynamical, already closed, or mechanism-not-reconstructable.
- Every substantial claim is tagged `[VERIFIED]`, `[COMPUTED]`, `[CHECKED]`, or `[CONJECTURED]`.
- The final implication is unambiguous: concrete lead, closed lead, or reconstruction failure.
