# Exploration 006: Stress-Test the Meta-Interpretation

## The Interpretation Under Test

> "Time is irreversible determination — the ongoing, physically constrained process by which the indeterminate becomes determinate, as encountered from within."

This synthesis combines CI's physics (LBB engine, ML bound, CI+Wolpert, QRF dissolution) with CP's ontology (real potentialities, intrinsic direction of determination, perspectival "now," Husserlian flow structure). Self-scored at 57/65. See `explorations/exploration-005/REPORT.md` for the full construction.

## Your Task

**Hit this interpretation as hard as you can.** Five attacks, each targeting a different vulnerability. State each at maximum strength, give the interpretation's best response, and deliver a verdict.

### Attack 1: Is "determination" a real primitive or a synonym?

"Determination" is supposed to subsume both "computation" (CI) and "actualization" (CP). But does it actually DO anything that these words don't? Is it a genuine concept or just a verbal umbrella that lets you say "CI handles X and CP handles Y" without the awkwardness of admitting you're cherry-picking?

**Test:** Remove the word "determination" and replace it everywhere with "irreversible process." Does the interpretation lose anything? If not, "determination" is decorative, not load-bearing.

### Attack 2: The 57/65 score is inflated

Any synthesis that combines the best features of two parents will score higher on a feature-by-feature basis. This is trivially true and doesn't mean the synthesis is genuinely stronger. You could "synthesize" any two interpretations and get a higher score by cherry-picking each one's strengths.

**Test:** Is there any criterion where the synthesis scores LOWER than both parents? If not, the scoring methodology is biased in favor of synthesis. A genuine synthesis should have its own weaknesses that neither parent has.

### Attack 3: The "natural initial condition" is the Past Hypothesis renamed

The synthesis claims the Past Hypothesis is reframed as "minimal initial determination — the universe began with very few facts settled." But this IS the Past Hypothesis. "Very few facts settled" = "low entropy." "Minimal determination" = "far from equilibrium." You've changed the name without changing the content. The direction of time still depends on this initial condition — you've just given it a more palatable name.

### Attack 4: Real potentialities are doing all the philosophical heavy lifting

The synthesis's improvements over CI come almost entirely from accepting that quantum potentialities are ontologically real. Without this commitment: (a) the "now" collapses back to CI's weakness, (b) the intrinsic direction is lost, (c) the ontological grounding of irreversibility disappears. In other words: without real potentialities, the synthesis IS CI. With real potentialities, it's CI + one controversial philosophical commitment. Is that really a "synthesis" or is it "CI plus optional metaphysics"?

### Attack 5: The circularity problem

Every interpretation of time faces a circularity objection. The synthesis says "time IS irreversible determination." But determination is described as "ongoing" — which presupposes time. The LBB engine operates "step by step" — which presupposes sequence. The "now" is defined as "where determination is actively occurring" — which presupposes a present tense. Has the synthesis actually explained time, or has it described time in fancier language?

## For Each Attack

1. State the attack at full strength
2. Give the synthesis's best possible response
3. Verdict: REPELLED / PARTIALLY SURVIVED / WOUNDED / FATAL
4. If wounded, propose a specific repair
5. If any attack is fatal, say so honestly
