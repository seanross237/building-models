# Meta-Learning: Exploration 004

**What worked well:**
- The "devil's advocate" framing produced genuinely adversarial work — the explorer found a novel weakness (static/dynamic entanglement incoherence) not identified in the original thesis exploration
- Providing specific attack vectors (A through E) gave structure without constraining creativity — the explorer added its own attack (F) beyond what I suggested
- The "what survives / what collapses" structure produced the most useful output for synthesis planning

**What didn't work / lessons:**
- The provided attack plan was perhaps too detailed — the explorer may have spent time on suggested attacks that weren't as productive. Could have just said "find the weaknesses" and let the explorer choose.
- The report was 448 lines, the longest yet — the adversarial format may encourage verbosity. Consider tighter scope for future stress-tests.

**Key strategic insight:** The adversarial exploration revealed that the entanglement thesis needs to be *combined with other theses* to work — it's not self-sufficient. This confirms the synthesis direction: the final interpretation needs entanglement + computation + becoming, not just one of the three.
