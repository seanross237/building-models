# Meta-Learning: Exploration 002

**What worked well:**
- The dialectical framing (explicitly positioning this thesis AGAINST Exploration 001) gave the explorer a clear adversary and sharpened the argument
- Requiring engagement with physics (not just philosophy) produced a much stronger report than a pure philosophy exploration would have
- The four-pillar structure (phenomenological, metaphysical, physical, logical) emerged naturally and was effective
- Explorer completed in good time (~8 minutes)

**What didn't work / lessons:**
- I suggested specific thinkers (Bergson, Whitehead, Prigogine, Smolin, Maudlin) in the goal, which was both helpful (gave the explorer concrete starting points) and potentially narrowing. The explorer found all the right additional material (Cortês, Ellis, causal set sequential growth) on its own, so the suggestions were useful starting points without being limiting.

**Scope assessment:** Again, one thesis per exploration is the right scope for Phase 1. Both explorations so far have been ~350-415 lines — substantial but not bloated.

**Key insight for future:** The most productive synthesis material comes from the honest weaknesses sections. Both theses identified their weak points clearly, which makes Phase 2 stress-testing easier to design.
