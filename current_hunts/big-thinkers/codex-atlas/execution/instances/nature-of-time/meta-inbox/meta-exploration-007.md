# Meta-Learning: Exploration 007

**What worked well:**
- Highly structured attack plan (five specific attacks, each with clear targets) produced the most rigorous stress-test yet
- The "for each attack: state it, strongest version, can synthesis respond, verdict" format forced disciplined evaluation
- The equilibrium counterexample (Prong 4 of Attack 1) is brilliant — it decisively refutes the "no Past Hypothesis" claim by showing the LBB triad holds in a universe with no arrow
- Comparison with alternatives (Attack 5) was the most productive section — the table showing what each approach explains vs. doesn't is extremely useful for the final report

**What didn't work:**
- Explorer crashed (rate limit) before writing REPORT-SUMMARY.md. The full report was complete (523 lines). Strategizer wrote the summary manually. For future explorations with large, multi-section goals, consider breaking into smaller chunks or being more explicit about writing the summary early.

**Key insight:** The factorization problem (Attack 3) is genuinely the most damaging — it limits the synthesis's scope to "within the regime of well-defined subsystems." But this is actually FINE for the final interpretation — we inhabit a universe of well-defined subsystems, and explaining time's properties within that regime is still genuinely valuable. The honest acknowledgment of scope actually makes the interpretation more credible, not less.

**Scope assessment:** Five attacks in one exploration was ambitious but the explorer handled it well. Each attack got full treatment. The ~523 line report was the longest yet.
