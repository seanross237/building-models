# Mission

The ultimate goal of this mission is to produce novel, defensible results — findings rigorous enough that we could present them to the scientific community with confidence. Everything below serves that goal. A fascinating exploration that doesn't produce a citable, stress-tested result has not yet succeeded.

---

Determine the limits of Stochastic Electrodynamics (SED) as an alternative to quantum field theory. SED posits that quantum behavior arises from classical particles immersed in a real, classical zero-point radiation field — no field quantization required.

SED already reproduces several QFT results: the Casimir effect, van der Waals forces, the blackbody spectrum, and the harmonic oscillator ground state. The question is: how far does it go, and where exactly does it break?

Concretely, pursue one or more of:
- Compute the hydrogen energy spectrum in SED and compare against QED predictions — identify the exact point of divergence
- Attempt to derive Bell inequality violations within SED (most physicists assume this fails, but the literature is not settled)
- Calculate the anomalous magnetic moment of the electron (g-2) in SED and compare against QED's 12-decimal-place prediction
- Push SED into multi-particle systems and determine whether entanglement-like correlations emerge
- Identify the minimal modification to SED that would recover full QM predictions, if pure SED fails

Explorers have full shell access and should compute rather than speculate. When a claimed SED result exists in the literature, reproduce it numerically. When SED is claimed to fail, verify the failure explicitly. A computed disagreement between SED and QED at the 5th decimal place is worth more than a philosophical argument about why SED "should" fail.

The deliverable is one or more novel claims about SED's scope — where it works, where it breaks, and why — backed by explicit calculation, with prior art thoroughly searched, and the strongest objections addressed.
