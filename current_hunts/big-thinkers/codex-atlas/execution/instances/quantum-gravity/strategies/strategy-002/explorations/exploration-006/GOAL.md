# Exploration 006: The Cosmological Constant Problem in QG+F — Can Causal Set Discreteness Help?

## Mission Context

We are developing novel quantum gravity theory. The six-derivative extension of QG+F resolves the n_s tension but doesn't address the biggest open problem: why Λ ≈ 10⁻¹²² M_P⁴. Causal set theory's "everpresent-Λ" is the only approach that PREDICTED the right order of magnitude BEFORE observation. Can this mechanism be imported into the QG+F framework?

## Your Specific Goal

Investigate whether the causal set everpresent-Λ mechanism can be combined with or imported into the continuum QG+F framework, and assess alternative approaches to Λ within QG+F.

### Task 1: Deep Dive on the Everpresent-Λ Mechanism
- Find and reconstruct the everpresent-Λ mechanism (Sorkin 1990/1997, Ahmed-Dodelson-Greene-Sorkin 2004)
- How exactly does it work? Key ingredients: unimodular gravity + Poisson fluctuations of spacetime volume
- What are ΔΛ ~ 1/√V where V is the 4-volume of the past? How does this give Λ ~ H₀²?
- What are the latest tests against cosmological data (2023-2025)?
- What are its weaknesses/criticisms?

### Task 2: Can Everpresent-Λ Work in Continuum QG+F?
- The key ingredient is spacetime discreteness (Poisson sprinkling at the Planck scale)
- QG+F is a continuum theory. Can it accommodate discreteness?
- The QG+F ↔ AS duality suggests a non-perturbative sector. Could AS provide effective discreteness at Planck scale?
- Is there a continuum analogue of Poisson fluctuations? (e.g., path-integral measure fluctuations)
- What about unimodular QG+F? (Salvio 2024 showed this is possible)
- If we combine unimodular QG+F with some form of discreteness, do we get everpresent-Λ?

### Task 3: Alternative Approaches to Λ Within QG+F
- **Unimodular gravity:** Makes Λ a constant of integration. Does it solve the "old" CC problem?
- **Radiative stability:** Is the cosmological constant radiatively stable in QG+F? (Technical naturalness)
- **Sequestering mechanisms:** Kaloper-Padilla vacuum energy sequestering — is it compatible with QG+F?
- **Asymptotic safety:** Does the AS non-perturbative sector constrain Λ? What does the Reuter fixed point predict?
- **Poincaré recurrence / quantum fluctuations:** Any mechanism from the non-perturbative sector?

### Task 4: The Deep Question — Why 10⁻¹²²?
- Even if Λ is an integration constant (unimodular), why does it have THIS value?
- The anthropic argument: selection effects in a multiverse. Is this the only option?
- Non-anthropic approaches: (a) causal set fluctuations, (b) radiative stability arguments, (c) symmetry-based arguments (e.g., supersymmetry), (d) holographic arguments
- What would it take for QG+F (or its extension) to actually PREDICT Λ ≈ 10⁻¹²²?

### Task 5: Synthesis — A Novel Approach?
Based on Tasks 1-4, is there a novel approach to Λ that:
- Is compatible with QG+F's successful framework
- Goes beyond what existing programs offer
- Makes a testable prediction about dark energy dynamics
- Does not require fine-tuning or anthropics

If yes, describe it concretely. If no, explain what's missing.

## Relevant Context

**QG+F and Λ:**
- QG+F has two free parameters beyond GR: M₂ (fakeon mass) and M₀ (scalar mass, set by CMB)
- Λ is a separate input — QG+F doesn't predict it
- Unimodular extension possible (Salvio 2024) — makes Λ an integration constant
- The AS non-perturbative sector might constrain Λ, but no concrete prediction exists

**Causal set everpresent-Λ:**
- Combines unimodular gravity (Λ conjugate to 4-volume, ΔΛ × ΔV ~ 1) with causal set discreteness (Poisson fluctuations ΔN ~ √N)
- Predicts Λ ~ 1/√V ~ H₀² ~ 10⁻¹²² M_P⁴
- Made ~decade before 1998 supernova observations
- 2023-2024 data fits as good as ΛCDM, alleviates H₀ and BAO tensions
