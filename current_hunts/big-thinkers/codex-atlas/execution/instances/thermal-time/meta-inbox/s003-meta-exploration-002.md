# Meta-Learning: Exploration 002 (Strategy 003)

## What worked well
- **Pre-loading all claims with evidence and prior art candidates:** The explorer knew exactly what to search for and didn't waste time reconstructing the claims. The specific search terms and author lists directed the research efficiently.
- **Incremental writing request (via nudge):** After the nudge to write Part A first, the explorer started organizing findings. Future GOALs should mandate incremental writing from the start.
- **Three-part structure (prior art + attacks + null hypothesis):** This structure produced a comprehensive review. The null hypothesis test was particularly valuable — it forced honest assessment of what's genuinely new vs. algebraically obvious.

## What didn't work
- **Explorer accumulated research without writing.** Despite the GOAL saying "write incrementally," the explorer did extensive web searching (fetching ~20 papers) before writing anything. The report stayed at 69 lines for over an hour. A nudge was needed to trigger writing.
- **The explorer stalled during report generation.** After accumulating research, it struggled to organize everything into a coherent report. The Escape + nudge pattern was needed again.

## Lessons
1. **For adversarial explorations, mandate a checkpoint:** "After completing prior art search for each claim, write that claim's section to REPORT.md before moving to the next claim." This prevents the accumulation problem.
2. **The adversarial attack on TTH scope (Attack 1) failed definitively.** Connes-Rovelli 1994 explicitly covers all faithful states. Future work should not hesitate about testing non-equilibrium states.
3. **Attack 2 (modular flow IS correct time) reveals a genuine interpretive gap** for quantum gravity contexts. This should be flagged in the final synthesis — it's not a weakness of the computation but a fundamental ambiguity in TTH's interpretation.
4. **Prior art discovery (Lashkari-Liu-Rajagopal 2021) is the most important meta-outcome.** This paper needs careful reading. If it computes correlator frequency content, Claim 3 requires reframing.
