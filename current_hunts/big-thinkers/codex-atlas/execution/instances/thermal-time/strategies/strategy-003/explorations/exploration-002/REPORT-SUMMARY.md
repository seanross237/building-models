# Exploration 002 Summary: Adversarial Review of All TTH Claims

## Goal
Three-part adversarial review: (A) prior art search for each claim, (B) three conceptual attacks steelmanned and responded to, (C) null hypothesis test for each claim.

## Outcome: COMPLETE — Claims survive with important qualifications

### Part A: Prior Art Findings

**Claim 1 (post-quench modular flow = pre-quench dynamics):**
- Closest prior art: Cardy-Tonni (arXiv:1608.01283) and Hollands-Sanders (arXiv:1702.04924) study entanglement Hamiltonians near/after quenches but do NOT compute correlator dynamics generated by K or compare to physical evolution.
- The algebraic fact (K = βH_0 for post-quench Gibbs state) is textbook. The explicit spectral comparison, √3 asymptotic discrepancy, and TTH framing are new.
- **Novelty: 2.5/5**

**Claim 2 (product-state identity):**
- Textbook result. Takesaki Vol. II (tensor product modular operators) implies [K_B, x_A ⊗ I] = 0 for product states.
- **Novelty: 1/5. Should be demoted to "verification" status.**

**Claim 3 (excited-state modular flow at structurally wrong frequencies, growing with N):**
- Closest prior art: Lashkari, Liu, Rajagopal (arXiv:1811.05052, JHEP 2021) "Modular flow of excited states" — computes modular flow operators for coherent and squeezed states in GFF. However, they compute the modular OPERATOR structure, not correlator dynamics vs. physical time evolution. No paper computes zero spectral weight at physical frequency or the N^{0.33–0.44} continuum divergence.
- **Novelty: 4/5. Strongest claim, survives adversarial review.**

**Claim 4 (squeezed states: quantitative not structural discrepancy):**
- No prior art found. The taxonomy (structural vs. quantitative discrepancy based on "distance from Gibbs") appears novel. Single data point — needs systematic study.
- **Novelty: 3/5**

**Central interpretation ("modular time is preparation-history time"):**
- Not stated by Connes, Rovelli, Martinetti, or any critic in reviewed literature. Connes-Rovelli 1994 define time for any faithful state but frame it as "equilibrium time," not preparation-history time.
- **Novelty: 4/5. Genuinely new explanatory principle.**

### Part B: Conceptual Attacks

**Attack 1 ("TTH not for non-equilibrium states"):** FAILS. Connes-Rovelli 1994 explicitly define TTH for ANY faithful state via Tomita-Takesaki, not just KMS states. Testing non-equilibrium states is exactly within scope. This attack misrepresents TTH.

**Attack 2 ("Modular flow of excited state IS the correct time — feature, not bug"):** PARTIALLY SUCCEEDS for quantum gravity contexts (no background time). FAILS for operationally defined physical settings (Rindler observer, coupled oscillators with measurable dynamics). The attack reveals genuine interpretive ambiguity in TTH's quantum gravity application but doesn't invalidate the specific test cases.

**Attack 3 ("Lattice ≠ type III, results untrustworthy"):** FAILS — the convergence data (N^{0.33–0.44} growing discrepancy) shows the mismatch gets *worse* in the continuum limit. If the attack were right, we'd see healing; instead we see divergence. The attack is strengthened by the squeezed-state result (Gaussian-exact, no approximation) showing the same trend.

### Part C: Null Hypothesis Tests

All three null hypotheses partially succeed at the algebraic level: K = βH_0 (Claim 1), K_excited ≠ H (Claim 3), K_sq ≈ βH + O(r) (Claim 4) are known algebraic facts. What the computations add beyond the trivial observations:
- Explicit spectral content (zero weight at physical frequency)
- Quantitative scaling with N
- Asymptotic √3 result
- The "structural vs. quantitative" taxonomy
- The TTH framing and domain map

## Key Takeaway

**The TTH claims survive adversarial review, with Claim 3 and the central interpretation being the strongest.** The key finding is that the continuum extrapolation for Claim 3 shows GROWING discrepancy (N^{0.44} for squeezed states) — this is both the strongest evidence and the best response to the "lattice is type I" attack.

The "preparation-history time" interpretation is not in the literature and provides a unifying explanation for all results: TTH works when and only when the modular Hamiltonian coincides with the physical Hamiltonian, which happens when preparation history = current dynamics (Gibbs states, vacuum). For any other state, modular time tracks the creation process, not the evolution.

## Unexpected Findings

**Critical prior art discovery (Lashkari-Liu-Rajagopal 2021):** The paper arXiv:1811.05052 explicitly computes modular flow for coherent and squeezed states in generalized free field theory. This paper is not cited in the prior explorations and needs to be checked against Claim 3 more carefully. Its abstract does not suggest it computes correlator dynamics or finds wrong frequencies — but it should be read in full to confirm the gap.

**Attack 2 reveals a genuine open question:** The proper interpretation of TTH for quantum gravity (where there is no background physical time) is unresolved. Our tests are valid for operationally defined physical settings, but the quantum gravity interpretation remains genuinely ambiguous.
