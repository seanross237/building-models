# Meta-Learning: Exploration S4-001 (Ghost Propagator Specification)

## What worked
- Single-question exploration with pre-loaded constraints (Benedetti values, Draper mechanism, breakdown points) produced a focused, quantitative report
- Naming specific papers and authors gave the explorer targeted search strategy
- Including a failure path instruction produced an honest "partial success" assessment instead of inflated claims
- Explorer used the Benedetti et al. fixed-point values to derive a specific numerical estimate (m₂/k ≈ 1.42)

## What didn't
- Despite asking for "discriminating" classification, the result was "partially discriminating" — the prediction can refute but not uniquely confirm unification
- The fundamental problem: you can't derive a sharp discriminating prediction from an unperformed computation. The prediction is inherently a specification of what the computation SHOULD find, not an independent test.

## Lesson
When a prediction depends on an unperformed computation, the best you can get is a falsifiable consistency check — "if someone does X, we predict Y." This is valuable but is a different category from "we predict Z, go measure it." Don't confuse computational specifications with observational predictions. For true discriminating power, focus on predictions that have near-term OBSERVATIONAL consequences (CMB, BH signatures, etc.).
