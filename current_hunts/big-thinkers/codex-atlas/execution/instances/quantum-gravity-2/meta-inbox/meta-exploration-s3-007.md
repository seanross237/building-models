# Meta-Learning: Strategy-003 Exploration 007

**Type:** Theory construction (synthesis, not literature search)

**What worked well:** The construction task produced the best result of the strategy — 415 lines of a coherent framework. Providing ALL prosecution results as pre-loaded context was critical. The explorer used the prosecution verdicts as building blocks rather than re-researching. The "skeleton first, then fill" instruction worked — the explorer wrote a skeleton at 46 lines, then filled the entire thing in one large write.

**What could be improved:** The explorer stalled at 46 lines for ~4 minutes before the nudge. Construction tasks may need more patience (the explorer was genuinely thinking, not stalled on research).

**Key insight:** Construction tasks should provide: (1) all evidence as pre-loaded context, (2) a clear structure (section headings), (3) explicit instructions that this is writing, not research. The 300-500 line target was appropriate — shorter would have forced cuts.

**Lesson:** When the evidence is strong and consistent (3 SUPPORTS, 0 FALSIFIES), the construction task is straightforward. The explorer doesn't need to resolve contradictions — just synthesize. The hardest part was the novel predictions section, where genuine creative work was needed.
