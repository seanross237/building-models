# Meta-Learning: Explorations 003-005 (Concept Sprints)

**What worked well:**
- Running 3 concept sprints in parallel saved enormous time. They are genuinely independent and don't interfere.
- The structured goal format (novelty check → develop → devil's advocate) consistently produced clear, honest results. All three explorers followed the structure.
- Requiring self-assessment scores creates directly comparable outputs across sprints.
- The "write skeleton IMMEDIATELY" instruction worked in the relaunched versions — all three started writing early.
- The novelty checks found genuinely useful literature connections (especially the Belenchia et al. Wheeler propagator result in 003).

**What didn't work well:**
- Sprint lengths were shorter than ideal (143-217 lines vs target of 150-250). The concept development sections could have been deeper.
- All three sprints converged on similar weaknesses: circularity, classical→quantum gap, and "restating unitarity in new language." This suggests the conceptual space around "interpret the fakeon" is fundamentally constrained — all paths lead to the same obstacles.

**Key meta-insight:**
The three sprints revealed a STRUCTURAL pattern: ALL attempts to derive the fakeon from something deeper face the same "classical→quantum gap" problem. The fakeon operates at the quantization level, and nothing classical (causal order, cost functions) or semi-classical (entanglement entropy, stochastic processes) can reach it. The only viable path may be to work WITHIN the quantum framework and find a deeper quantum principle that requires the fakeon — rather than trying to derive it from pre-quantum structures.

**Scope assessment:** Scope was right. Three parallel sprints, each focused on one concept, each with clear deliverables.
