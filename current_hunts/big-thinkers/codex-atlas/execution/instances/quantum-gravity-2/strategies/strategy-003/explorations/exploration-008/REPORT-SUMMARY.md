# Exploration 008 — Summary: Devil's Advocate

## Goal
Ruthlessly attack the unified QG+F–AS framework from every angle. Rate attacks FATAL/SERIOUS/MODERATE/MINOR. Perform 5-tier validation (PASS/MARGINAL/FAIL).

## What Was Done
Pursued 7 attack vectors: tautology, QCD analogy breakdown, INCONCLUSIVE-as-failure, unfalsifiable predictions, alternative explanations, circular reasoning in the analyticity bridge, and lack of expert endorsement. Grounded each attack in the prior explorations (001–007), library entries, and web searches for researcher positions.

## Outcome: FRAMEWORK SURVIVES — BARELY

**No FATAL attacks.** Three SERIOUS, four MODERATE. The framework is not killed but is severely weakened.

- **Attack 2 (QCD analogy broken): SERIOUS.** The analogy fails at its most critical joint — the confinement mechanism. No compact gauge group, no quantum number to confine, no lattice evidence. The entire load-bearing structure rests on an analogy that lacks the key ingredient it's supposed to provide.
- **Attack 3 (INCONCLUSIVE → failure): SERIOUS.** Both central conjectures (AF→NGFP trajectory, spin-2 ghost confinement) have been open for ~10 years with no resolution. Mildly negative evidence from absence.
- **Attack 4 (Unfalsifiable predictions): SERIOUS.** No prediction is simultaneously novel to the unified framework, experimentally testable, and numerically specific. The framework cannot currently be distinguished from standalone QG+F or AS.

**5-Tier Validation:** Novel=MARGINAL, Consistent=PASS, Explanatory=MARGINAL, Predictive=FAIL, Testable=MARGINAL.

## Key Takeaway
The unified framework is **a well-constructed conjecture that currently lacks both theoretical proof and experimental discriminability**. It is not wrong (no evidence contradicts it) but also not specifically right (no evidence supports unification over the simpler compatible-but-separate interpretation). Its fate depends entirely on two unperformed calculations: (1) the AF→NGFP connecting trajectory in full FRG, and (2) the spin-2 ghost propagator in C²-extended FRG. Until those are done, treat it as a speculative hypothesis, not an established framework.

## Leads Worth Pursuing
- The spin-2 ghost propagator calculation is the single make-or-break computation. If it shows ghost dissolution → framework strongly supported. If ghost persists → framework is dead.
- The "compatible-but-separate" interpretation deserves explicit development as a competitor hypothesis.
- The Predictive tier FAIL is the most damaging result — the framework needs at least one genuinely novel, computable, specific prediction to achieve scientific credibility.
