# Exploration 006 Summary: Analyticity Reconciliation

## Goal
Can QG+F's sacrifice of S-matrix analyticity be reconciled with AS's Euclidean functional RG framework?

## What Was Tried
Investigated five sub-questions: (1) whether AS requires analyticity, (2) whether AS's own propagator has non-standard analytic structure, (3) whether Lorentzian AS formulations bypass analyticity, (4) how the fakeon's average continuation relates to AS's Wick rotation, (5) Platania-Wetterich's ghost mechanism and analyticity.

## Outcome: SUPPORTS

The apparent tension dissolves completely on examination. Three independent lines of evidence:

1. **AS already has Wick rotation obstructions.** Donoghue (2020) showed the ghost pole sits in the upper right quadrant of the complex q₀ plane, obstructing standard Wick rotation. Draper et al. (2020) found infinite towers of poles at imaginary p². AS itself cannot rely on standard analyticity.

2. **AS doesn't require analyticity.** The Wetterich equation is a computational tool in Euclidean signature. Physical prediction extraction is a separate step. Lorentzian AS (D'Angelo et al. 2024) demonstrated the NGFP directly in Lorentzian signature without any Wick rotation or analytic continuation.

3. **The fakeon may solve a problem AS already has.** QG+F's average continuation provides a systematic nonanalytic method for extracting Lorentzian physics from Euclidean data despite complex poles — exactly the tool AS needs for its own obstructed Wick rotation.

## Key Takeaway
The analyticity sacrifice is NOT a barrier to QG+F = AS. It may be a *feature* — QG+F explicitly acknowledges and handles a non-analyticity that AS already implicitly contains in its complex pole structure. Rather than creating tension, QG+F's average continuation could be the missing prescription AS needs for Euclidean→Lorentzian physics extraction.

## Leads Worth Pursuing
- Can the fakeon average continuation be applied directly to AS effective actions computed via the Wetterich equation? No one has tried this.
- Do the Draper et al. pole towers have exactly the structure needed for the average continuation to work?
- The Platania-Wetterich fictitious ghost mechanism might eliminate complex poles entirely in the full theory, making the analyticity question moot.
