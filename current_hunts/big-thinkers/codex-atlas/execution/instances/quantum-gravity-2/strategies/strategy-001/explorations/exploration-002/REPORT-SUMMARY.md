# Exploration 002 Summary: Computational Spacetime — Circuit Complexity as Geometry

## Goal
Survey the complexity-geometry program (2014–2026) and develop a constructive framework where spacetime geometry IS circuit complexity. Assess genuine novelty vs. repackaging of holographic entanglement.

## What Was Done
Comprehensive survey of the program: CV and CA conjectures, Nielsen's complexity geometry, tensor network models (MERA/HaPPY), quantum error correction and holography (ADH), Lloyd's bound, de Sitter complexity, and 2024-2026 developments (Krylov complexity, singularity theorems from complexity, "complexity = anything"). Then attempted to build a constructive framework with five pillars: complexity as metric, time as complexity growth, gravity as computational cost optimization, cosmological constant as maximum complexity, and black holes as complexity maximizers. Checked against the constraint funnel (graviton propagator, Lorentz invariance, spectral dimension, Bekenstein-Hawking entropy, ghost freedom).

## Outcome: PARTIAL SUCCESS

**The survey is comprehensive.** The program is richer and more advanced than expected — especially Pedraza et al. (2023) who derived the full nonlinear Einstein equations AND higher-derivative gravity from complexity optimization. The 2025 singularity theorem from complexity is remarkable. Krylov complexity provides a concrete calculable bridge to holographic geometry.

**Complexity genuinely extends beyond entanglement.** This is the clearest finding. Entanglement (RT) describes the boundary/near-horizon; complexity (CV/CA) describes the interior. Entanglement saturates at thermalization; complexity grows exponentially longer. The "linearization barrier" of MVEH is overcome by the complexity derivation of full nonlinear equations. This is NOT just entanglement in different language.

**The constructive framework is promising but incomplete.** The proposal that spacetime metric IS complexity geometry passes most constraint checks (BH entropy, Lorentz invariance in continuum limit, plausible spectral dimension reduction). But three critical gaps remain:
1. **Background dependence** — the framework still works within holography, not from scratch
2. **Dimensional reduction** — no mechanism for 4D from exponentially-high-dimensional complexity space
3. **"Complexity = anything"** — many geometric observables have the right qualitative behavior; which one is fundamental?

## Key Takeaway
**Complexity is the most promising direction for going beyond entanglement in quantum gravity**, accessing physics that entanglement cannot reach (black hole interiors, post-thermalization dynamics, full nonlinear + higher-derivative equations). However, it has NOT achieved a fully constructive, background-independent theory. The most intriguing unexplored connection: whether the requirement of a "physically valid" cost function in complexity geometry uniquely selects ghost-free higher-derivative gravity (QG+F). If so, this could provide a computational derivation of the specific UV completion of gravity.

## Lead Worth Pursuing
The Pedraza et al. result that modified cost functions yield higher-derivative gravity should be examined closely. If cost function constraints (positivity, triangle inequality, unitarity) uniquely select the QG+F Lagrangian, that would be a major result connecting computational principles to the specific form of quantum gravity. This is the "narrow gate" that could make computational spacetime genuinely new.
