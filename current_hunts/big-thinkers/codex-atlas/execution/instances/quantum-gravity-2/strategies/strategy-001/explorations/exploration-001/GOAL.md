# Exploration 001: "Gravitize the Quantum" — Conceptual Survey and Framework Development

## Mission Context

We are developing a genuinely novel theory of quantum gravity. The prior research mission proved that within the standard axiom set {Lorentz invariance, diffeomorphism invariance, locality, perturbative renormalizability, unitarity}, the unique theory is QG+F (quadratic gravity with fakeon quantization). We need to escape this by starting from fundamentally different conceptual foundations.

The standard approach is "quantize gravity" — take QM as given, make gravity fit into it. This exploration reverses the arrow: **what if gravity/spacetime structure is more fundamental than quantum mechanics, and QM emerges from it?**

## Specific Goal

Survey and develop the "gravitize the quantum" conceptual direction. Produce a comprehensive assessment of:

1. **Existing programs that derive QM from gravity/spacetime.** Research the following (and any others you find):
   - 't Hooft's cellular automaton interpretation / deterministic QM at Planck scale
   - Penrose's gravitational objective reduction (OR) and the Diosi-Penrose model
   - Stochastic gravity (Hu-Verdaguer framework) — semiclassical gravity with quantum noise
   - Adler's trace dynamics — QM from statistical mechanics of matrix models
   - Nelson's stochastic mechanics — QM from Brownian motion on configuration space
   - "Gravity from the measurement problem" ideas
   - Any 2024-2026 developments in this direction

2. **For the 2-3 most promising programs**, develop them into concrete physical frameworks:
   - What is the core physical picture? (Not just math — what's the intuitive mechanism?)
   - What specific predictions differ from standard QG approaches?
   - What is the status of known objections? Are any fatal?
   - How does it handle the problem of time (QM needs external time; GR has dynamical time)?

3. **Synthesize a novel framework** if you see an opportunity. Can you combine elements from multiple "gravitize the quantum" programs into something new that avoids the known pitfalls of each?

4. **Honestly assess novelty.** For each framework you develop:
   - Is this genuinely novel or secretly a repackaging of existing work?
   - Does it actually derive QM, or does it smuggle QM in through assumptions?
   - Could a domain expert tell this is AI-generated slop vs. a genuine conceptual contribution?

## Success Criteria

- **Success:** A clear picture of 2-3 "gravitize the quantum" frameworks, with honest assessment of strengths, weaknesses, and novelty. At least one framework developed enough to have concrete mechanisms and differing predictions. Clear proposal for which direction is most worth pursuing further.
- **Partial success:** Good survey but no framework developed beyond philosophy.
- **Failure:** Unable to find any viable "gravitize the quantum" program, or all turn out to be known dead ends with no way forward.

## Key Constraint

If you find that all "gravitize the quantum" programs secretly smuggle in QM (i.e., they don't actually derive QM from something deeper), say so clearly. That's a valuable finding. Don't try to paper over it.

## Relevant Context from Prior Work

- The standard axiom set uniquely selects QG+F. Don't try to work within those axioms.
- Jacobson (1995) showed Einstein's equations follow from horizon thermodynamics. He said: "it may be no more appropriate to canonically quantize the Einstein equation than it would be to quantize the wave equation for sound in air." This suggests gravity may be emergent and quantization is the wrong move.
- The entanglement-coupling "Rosetta Stone" (Susskind-Uglum): gravitational couplings are entanglement properties (1/G ~ 1/epsilon^2 of S_EE). Could potentially be read in reverse — gravity determines entanglement determines QM.
- The problem of time is a deep conflict between QM (external time) and GR (dynamical time). A "gravitize the quantum" approach would naturally resolve this by deriving time from spacetime.
- Constraints that still apply regardless of derivation direction: unitarity, Lorentz invariance, diffeomorphism invariance, Bekenstein-Hawking entropy, GW speed = c.
