# Exploration 002: Computational Spacetime — Circuit Complexity as the Foundation of Geometry

## Mission Context

We are developing a genuinely novel theory of quantum gravity. The standard axiom set uniquely selects QG+F (quadratic gravity with fakeon quantization). Starting from entanglement axioms (MVEH) also produces QG+F. We need a genuinely different foundation.

Key insight from prior work: entanglement describes the *boundary* of spacetime (Ryu-Takayanagi), but **complexity** describes the *interior* — the behind-the-horizon, nonlinear, deep-bulk physics that entanglement alone can't reach. This is exactly where the "linearization barrier" prevents MVEH from producing anything beyond linearized equations for higher-derivative gravity.

## Specific Goal

Survey and develop the "computational spacetime" conceptual direction — where spacetime geometry IS quantum circuit complexity, not just correlated with it. Produce:

1. **Survey of the complexity-geometry program (2014-2026):**
   - Susskind's Complexity = Volume (CV) conjecture
   - Brown-Roberts-Susskind-Stanford Complexity = Action (CA) conjecture
   - Nielsen's geometric approach to circuit complexity
   - Tensor network models of spacetime (MERA, HaPPY code, random tensor networks)
   - Quantum error correction and holography (Almheiri-Dong-Harlow)
   - Lloyd's bound on complexity growth rate: dC/dt ≤ 2E/πℏ
   - De Sitter complexity and the "complexity plateau" problem
   - Any 2024-2026 developments (especially anything moving from "correspondence" to "construction")

2. **Develop a constructive framework:** Go beyond "complexity corresponds to geometry" and attempt to BUILD spacetime from complexity:
   - What if circuit complexity IS the metric? (Distance = minimum circuit depth between states)
   - What if time IS complexity growth? (Arrow of time = second law of complexity)
   - What if gravity IS the computational cost function? (Einstein equations = optimization of computational resources)
   - What if the cosmological constant encodes maximum complexity? (CC = 1/S_max, where S_max ~ e^{10^{122}} is the maximum complexity of de Sitter space)
   - What if black holes are complexity maximizers? (Horizon = complexity barrier, Hawking radiation = complexity reset)

3. **Check against constraints:**
   - Does this framework reproduce the correct graviton propagator at low energies?
   - Is it compatible with Lorentz invariance (or does discreteness break it)?
   - What spectral dimension d_s does it predict?
   - Can it reproduce Bekenstein-Hawking entropy (S = A/4G)?
   - Does it avoid ghosts?

4. **Novelty assessment:**
   - How does this compare to existing holographic complexity conjectures? Is it genuinely new or just repackaging CV/CA in constructive language?
   - How does it compare to tensor network approaches (which already use computational structures)?
   - What does it predict that is DIFFERENT from both GR and QG+F?

## Success Criteria

- **Success:** A concrete "computational spacetime" framework with mechanisms (not just philosophy), at least one novel prediction, and honest assessment of viability. Clear picture of whether this can actually produce a theory or is just metaphor.
- **Partial success:** Good survey of complexity-geometry program but no viable constructive framework.
- **Failure:** The computational approach turns out to be equivalent to holographic entanglement (i.e., it just reproduces what RT/MVEH already gives, now in complexity language).

## Key Constraint

If the computational approach collapses back to existing holographic entanglement results (RT, ER=EPR) or to QG+F, say so clearly. The most valuable possible finding is an honest assessment of whether complexity adds anything genuinely new to the theory-construction toolkit, or whether it's just a different language for the same physics.

Also watch out for the "operational vs. fundamental" distinction: complexity might be a useful TOOL for studying QG theories without being the fundamental thing spacetime is made of. If that's the case, say so.

## Relevant Context

- The entanglement-gravity bootstrap (MVEH + renormalizability + unitarity) uniquely produces QG+F. Starting from entanglement tends to converge to QG+F.
- The "linearization barrier" (Bueno et al. 2017): MVEH only gives linearized equations for higher-derivative gravity. Complexity could potentially access the nonlinear physics that entanglement can't reach.
- The constraint funnel: UV completion, unitarity + ghost freedom, diffeomorphism invariance, Lorentz invariance, BH entropy are all non-negotiable.
- The cosmological constant Lambda ~ 10^{-122} M_P^4 — the CC might be related to maximum complexity of de Sitter space.
- Spectral dimension d_s → 2 in the UV is universal across QG approaches. A computational spacetime should reproduce this (or explain why not).
