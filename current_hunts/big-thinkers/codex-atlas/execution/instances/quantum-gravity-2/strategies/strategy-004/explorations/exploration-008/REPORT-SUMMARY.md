### Mission Comparison
The mission asked for "a novel theory of quantum gravity — something genuinely new." The framework is a synthesis of two existing programs (QG+F and AS), not a new theory. It explains why two formalisms are compatible, not why gravity exists. It makes zero novel discriminating predictions. It falls short on the mission's core demands: originality, explanatory clarity about nature, and bold predictions.

### Full Arc
S1 (novel construction from scratch) → SCG v2.0, creative but non-predictive. S2 (QG+F's explanatory gaps) → discovered gaps point to AS. S3 (QG+F–AS unification conjecture) → plausible, consistent, unproven. S4 (prediction extraction + honest assessment) → zero novel predictions survive adversarial scrutiny. The arc converged on an organizationally valuable but empirically vacuous structural conjecture.

### Key Takeaway
The program reached the boundary of what can be achieved by reasoning about existing theories without performing original calculations. The framework's most valuable contribution is specifying exactly what computations would resolve the open questions — but it cannot perform them. Going further requires either computational physics or a genuinely new idea, and the program did not produce the latter.
