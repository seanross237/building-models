# Exploration 004: Higgs Mass Consistency Under Unification

## Mission Context

The Unified QG+F–AS Framework treats QG+F and AS as complementary descriptions of one UV-complete quantum gravity theory. Asymptotic Safety's most celebrated empirical result is the Shaposhnikov-Wetterich (2010) prediction of the Higgs mass: m_H ≈ 126 GeV, made before the 2012 discovery (m_H = 125.25 ± 0.17 GeV). Under unification, the fakeon quantization of the C² term must be compatible with this prediction. The question is: does it CHANGE the prediction?

## Your Task

**One question:** Does the fakeon prescription for the gravitational ghost affect the Shaposhnikov-Wetterich Higgs mass prediction, and if so, how?

## What You Must Deliver

### 1. Reconstruct the SW Mechanism

The Shaposhnikov-Wetterich (SW) prediction comes from demanding that the SM coupled to gravity flows from the NGFP. At the Planck scale, the NGFP constrains the Higgs quartic coupling λ_H via a boundary condition. This boundary condition, evolved down to the electroweak scale via SM RG equations, predicts m_H.

**Reproduce the logic:**
- What is the NGFP boundary condition for λ_H? (λ_H(M_P) ≈ 0 from the requirement that the running coupling passes through the NGFP)
- What SM RG running produces m_H ≈ 126 GeV from this boundary condition?
- What is the sensitivity to the top quark mass m_t and α_s?

### 2. Assess the Fakeon Effect on the Boundary Condition

In standalone AS, the C² term is present but quantized by the standard Wick rotation. Under the unified framework, the ghost in the C² term is quantized as a fakeon. This changes the gravitational sector's contribution to:

a) **The gravitational contribution to the SM RG running near M_P.** The graviton + ghost + scalaron all couple to Higgs via gravity. Does the fakeon prescription change the graviton loop contribution to the running of λ_H?

b) **The NGFP boundary condition itself.** If the C² coupling at the NGFP is different under fakeon quantization (because the ghost doesn't go on shell), does this change the value of λ_H(M_P)?

c) **The threshold correction at M ~ m₂.** The ghost mass m₂ ~ M_P produces a threshold effect. Under the fakeon prescription, the ghost is virtual and doesn't contribute to the spectral function above threshold. Does this change the matching condition?

Search for: Eichhorn & Held (2017, PLB 777, 217) on gravitational contributions to SM couplings; Dona, Eichhorn, Percacci (2014) on SM + gravity RG; Pawlowski, Reichert (2023) on Higgs mass in AS with higher-derivative terms.

### 3. Quantify the Shift (If Any)

If the fakeon changes the boundary condition or running:
- By how much? Express as Δm_H in GeV
- Is Δm_H within the current experimental uncertainty (±0.17 GeV)?
- Is it positive or negative?
- Does this IMPROVE or WORSEN agreement with the measured value?

### 4. Classification

For the Higgs mass result under unification, classify as:
- **CONSISTENCY CHECK if** m_H prediction is unchanged: the unified framework inherits AS's success
- **NOVEL PREDICTION if** m_H shifts to a new specific value: the shift IS the prediction
- **PROBLEM if** m_H shifts AWAY from 125.25 GeV by more than current uncertainty

Also assess: is the original SW prediction robust enough that the C² correction matters? Or are the uncertainties from m_t and α_s already larger than any gravitational correction?
