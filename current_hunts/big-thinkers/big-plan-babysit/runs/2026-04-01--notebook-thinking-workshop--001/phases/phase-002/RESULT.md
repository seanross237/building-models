# Result

## Concept Statement

`Notebook Thinking` is a structured, notebook-only workshop method for converting scattered observations into a shared, written decision memo that clarifies what the team believes, what it needs to decide next, and what workshop should happen afterward.

## One-Sentence Promise

In under `60 minutes`, a facilitator can help a small team turn raw notes into a concrete decision memo that aligns product managers and designers on the next step.

## Why It Matters Internally

It gives internal teams a fast, low-dependency way to move from ambiguity to an explicit artifact they can use to make workflow decisions, reduce alignment drift, and shape the next workshop with more precision.

## Format Implication

The workshop should be tightly timeboxed, highly guided, and output-driven, with simple prompts, visible written synthesis, and a final memo artifact as the main deliverable.
