# Result

## Final Brief

`Notebook Thinking` is a notebook-only workshop method for internal product managers and designers who need to turn scattered notes and observations into a shared decision memo. It is designed for teams that already have raw observations and need a fast, concrete way to decide what they believe, what remains unresolved, and what workshop should happen next.

### Audience

Internal product managers and designers responsible for notebook workflow decisions.

### Promise

In under `60 minutes`, a facilitator can help a small team turn raw notes into a concrete written decision memo that aligns product managers and designers on the next step.

### Workshop Outline

- `0-5 min`: set the decision question
- `5-10 min`: capture notes and observations
- `10-20 min`: sort notes into themes and priorities
- `20-35 min`: shape likely decisions or preferred directions
- `35-50 min`: write the memo
- `50-60 min`: review, confirm, and close

### Memo Structure

- title
- decision question
- context
- key observations
- what we think they mean
- decision or preferred direction
- tradeoffs and risks
- open questions
- next step

### Pitch

`Notebook Thinking` gives teams a simple, low-overhead way to move from scattered notes to a shared decision memo without slides, tools, or extra process. It keeps the work concrete, visible, and fast, so the group leaves with a written artifact that can guide the next workshop design step.

## Plausibility Check

- clarity: the audience, promise, and output are explicit
- feasibility: the `60-minute` spine fits the constraints
- internal fit: the concept stays focused on decision support for product and design teams
