# Calibration

## Cycle 1

- Forecast:
  - 60% chance the local Tao artifacts would sharpen the bottleneck into explicit unresolved choices.
- Actual:
  - Correct.
  - The local artifacts were even more useful than expected because they already contained a packet-level template and a positive obstruction criterion.
- Calibration note:
  - Slight underconfidence on the local packet-definition evidence.

## Cycle 2

- Forecast:
  - 45% chance an external scan would reveal a usable one-sided gain for the packet route.
- Actual:
  - Correct.
  - The strongest helpful signal came from sign-definite helicity and helical triad-class papers.
- Calibration note:
  - I initially treated the external helical literature as mostly descriptive turbulence material; that was too conservative.
  - Update: give more weight to model variants that exhibit extra positive controls, even if they are not the full equation.

## Cycle 3

- Forecast:
  - 55% chance the homochiral bottleneck would become the strongest packet subroute.
- Actual:
  - Correct.
  - The wavelet-first route remained useful, but as an auxiliary extraction layer only.
- Calibration note:
  - Mild overconfidence in wavelet-first canonization as a possible main theorem object.
  - Update: prefer exact-object simplifications over coordinate changes unless the coordinate change removes an actual ambiguity.

## Overall

- Well-calibrated:
  - The local frontier really was packet canonization first.
  - The non-Tao restarts were dominated after comparison.
- Miscalibrated:
  - I initially undervalued sign-definite helicity as a source of one-sided structure.
- Next-time adjustment:
  - When a local frontier is blocked by non-canonical objects, search early for nearby model variants that create positive controls on the same structural variable.
