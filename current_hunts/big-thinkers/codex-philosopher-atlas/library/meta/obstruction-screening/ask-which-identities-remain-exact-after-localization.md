# Ask Which Identities Remain Exact After Localization

In rewrite audits, the first compatibility question is often not "does a weak
solution class exist?" but "what is the native exact equation, which parts of
it stay exact after localization, and what debt appears at that first cutoff
step when they do not?"

That question should be asked directly before candidate comparisons begin.
Freezing the cutoff protocol early is not enough on its own. The audit also
has to say where projector placement, singular-integral reconstruction, and
operator commutation re-enter the estimate once the cutoff is fixed, and it
should charge that debt immediately rather than after narrating a hoped-for
gain in the pre-localized variables.

If an algebraic identity only looks advantageous before those localized debts
are restored, treat that as a package mismatch rather than as provisional
progress.
