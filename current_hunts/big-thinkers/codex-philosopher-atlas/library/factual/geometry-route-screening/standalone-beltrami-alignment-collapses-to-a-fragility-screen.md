# Standalone Beltrami Alignment Collapses To A Fragility Screen

Status: `VERIFIED` with `INFERRED` consequence

The local record already contains the main Beltrami boundary:

- exact Beltrami alignment kills the Lamb-vector / pressure-side CZ loss;
- the effect is extremely fragile under perturbation;
- and local alignment does not by itself control the full stretching mechanism
  `S omega . omega`, especially its exterior or nonlocal part.

Therefore local Beltrami or alignment language is not a primary route for the
geometry branch. At most it remains a fragility screen or a weak hybrid
ingredient, for example when paired with concentration or anisotropy on a fixed
intense set. A route also fails the novelty benchmark if it reaches an existing
direction-coherence criterion only by importing stronger `nabla xi`-type
control under Beltrami-flavored language.

The later dynamic audit on the fixed Step-3 package sharpened the verdict:

- `local Beltrami / alignment` is `static-only`
- `Beltrami deficit plus concentration` is still only
  `informative but dynamically weak`
- `Beltrami deficit plus anisotropy` is still only
  `informative but dynamically weak`

The reasons are now tighter as well:

- local alignment still has no transport law that upgrades a favorable core
  into control of full stretching, especially the exterior or nonlocal part;
- concentration remains hostage to threshold choice and diffuses on the same
  `r^2` window used to define the core;
- anisotropy remains vulnerable to geometry-fitting or preferred-axis choices
  and also diffuses on that same window.

Those variants do not rescue the route unless they name a full-stretching gain
that survives fixed-package localization, including the exterior or nonlocal
contribution.
