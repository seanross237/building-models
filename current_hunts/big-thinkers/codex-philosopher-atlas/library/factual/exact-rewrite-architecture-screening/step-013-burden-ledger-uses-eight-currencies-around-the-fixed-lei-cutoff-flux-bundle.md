# Step-013 Burden Ledger Uses Eight Currencies Around The Fixed LEI Cutoff-Flux Bundle

Status: `INFERRED` ledger rule with mixed `VERIFIED` burden anchors

Step-013 freezes later candidate comparison around the already fixed
theorem-facing object, the full localized LEI cutoff-flux bundle
`I_flux[phi]`, by requiring one typed burden ledger rather than free-form
bookkeeping.

The ledger's eight currencies are:

- `derivative count`: record any claimed derivative reduction and the exact
  step where that reduction returns to the LEI bundle; if it never returns,
  mark it as non-cashable.
- `locality`: record whether the gain survives on the full localized
  `I_flux[phi]` bundle under the shared cutoff, or only on a surrogate local
  piece before exterior or nonlocal terms are restored.
- `cancellation`: record the exact sign, null structure, or commutator
  cancellation and whether it yields a smaller full-bundle coefficient after
  all debt is paid.
- `integrability`: record any improved space or exponent and whether an
  explicit transfer carries it back to a smaller `I_flux[phi]` cost.
- `pressure recoverability`: record every pressure-recovery, Helmholtz,
  Calderon-Zygmund, or nonlocal-pressure step as debt before any gain is
  counted.
- `admissibility`: record any stronger host-class or regularity assumption as
  negative burden unless an explicit admission bridge is supplied.
- `compactness input`: record any extraction, compactness, or rigidity
  machinery outside the frozen endpoint argument as a separate debt rather
  than silent endpoint progress.
- `scaling compatibility`: record whether the gain respects the inherited LEI
  and CKN scaling under the same shared cutoff protocol, rather than only in a
  differently normalized surrogate.

This turns later Step-013 comparisons into a mechanical ledger: candidates
must either post a same-ledger coefficient improvement or identify the exact
debts that keep the claimed gain from cashing out on the live bundle.
