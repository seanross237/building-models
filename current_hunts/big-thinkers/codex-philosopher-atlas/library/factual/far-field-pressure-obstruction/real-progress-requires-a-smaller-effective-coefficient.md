# Real Progress Requires A Smaller Effective Coefficient

Status: `INFERRED`

Any genuine improvement must produce an estimate of the form

`|I_p^far| <= C_far,eff(k) 2^{12k/5} U_k^{6/5}`

where `C_far,eff(k)` is genuinely smaller than
`||u||_{L^2}^2 / r_k^3`, or else becomes small or summable from admissible
data.

Improvements that act only on constants, or only on a partial surrogate of the
pressure term, do not resolve the obstruction. The live target is the full
far-field pairing, with affine-or-higher harmonic content still present.

Exploration 002 sharpened this into a screening rule: a candidate survives only
if it supplies an estimate-level mechanism that makes the live coefficient, or
the full far-field contribution built from it, genuinely smaller.
