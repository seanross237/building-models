- Tao's averaged-NS mechanism has already been narrowed to a five-mode delayed-threshold circuit with one tiny but decisive trigger variable `X3,n`.

## Portfolio Summary

### Chain 01 - Minimal Helical Coefficient-Rigidity Theorem

Central premise: the smallest exact helical packet capable of mimicking Tao's pump / seed / amplifier / rotor logic already fails because the required sign and magnitude hierarchy cannot be realized by one geometry-locked family of triad coefficients.

### Chain 02 - Spectator-Leakage Lower-Bound Ledger

Central premise: even if some desired channels can be matched, exact Fourier/helical closure plus mirror-mode bookkeeping forces enough extra interactions that any honest Tao-like subsystem leaks a quantifiable amount of activity outside the five target roles.

### Chain 03 - Adversarial Near-Circuit Construction Search

Central premise: the obstruction might be false. The fastest honest kill is to search directly for the best exact NS or helical packet that approximates Tao's logic and see whether it survives exact coupling accounting.

### Chain 04 - Definition-Stability Audit for "Near-Closed Tao Circuit"

Central premise: the whole mission may fail at the object-definition level. If every reasonable definition of near-closure is unstable under phase choice, packetization, scaling, or conjugate completion, the right result is a sharp definitional kill rather than a fake theorem program.

## Major Differences Between Chains

- Chain 01 is algebraic and theorem-facing. It targets incompatible coefficient ratios and sign patterns inside a minimal exact helical model.
- Chain 02 is closure- and leakage-facing. It assumes desired channels may exist but asks whether forced spectators can be bounded below.
- Chain 03 is adversarial and constructive. It tries to kill the firewall idea by building the strongest exact near-circuit it can find.
- Chain 04 is meta-definitional. It tests whether the theorem object itself can be made stable enough to support Chains 01-03.
