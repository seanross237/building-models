# Chain 02 - Invariant Leakage Test After Definition Freeze

## Central Premise

This branch is only live after one stable notion of a near-closed Tao circuit has been fixed. Given that frozen object, the question is whether exact Navier-Stokes forces a uniformly nontrivial off-circuit transfer on an explicit packet class, or whether an adversarial family can drive that transfer to zero while preserving the intended Tao-role logic over a stated activation window.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a class-conditional lower bound showing that every exact packet in a specified symmetry-normalized family realizing the target Tao-role channels incurs at least a fixed amount of off-circuit transfer in one stated observable over one stated window; or
- an explicit family in which the same observable tends to zero while the Tao-role interaction logic remains active over that window; or
- a precise obstruction statement showing that no invariant leakage lower bound can be extracted from this route because the candidate observables fail invariance, definability, or coercivity on the chosen class.

## Why This Refined Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain no longer treats leakage as a freely chosen ratio, no longer assumes minimal finite packets are decisive, and no longer jumps from closure bookkeeping to theorem rhetoric. It is a downstream quantitative test on one frozen near-closure definition, one packet class, one observable, and one declared time window.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Import the frozen theorem object and admissible packet class

Depends on: none.

Action: adopt one near-closure definition that is already stable under packetization, normalization, conjugate completion, and basis bookkeeping. If no such object has been supplied by the definition-audit branch, stop immediately.

Action: specify one packet class to test, including allowed role assignment, symmetry completion, redundancy rules, tail rules if any, shell geometry limits, and whether finitely supported packets are only a calibration subclass or the full theorem class.

Expected output: an object sheet fixing the near-closure metric, bookkeeping stack, admissible packet family, and comparison window.

Kill condition: if no stable object sheet exists, stop and report prerequisite failure rather than forcing a leakage theorem on an unstable target.

### Step 2 - Choose one dynamical leakage observable before building any ledger

Depends on: Step 1.

Action: pick one observable tied to dynamics on the mission-relevant window, such as normalized integrated off-circuit energy transfer, unavoidable spectator forcing in a stated norm, or minimum off-graph flux over the activation interval.

Action: define exactly how the observable is normalized across packet size, amplitude scaling, initial spectator amplitudes, and role assignment. Reject observables that can be changed substantially by harmless regrouping or bookkeeping choices.

Expected output: a leakage protocol with formula, normalization, admissible initial data, and time-window specification.

Kill condition: if no observable is both invariant enough and dynamical enough to compare packet realizations honestly, stop and record a leakage-definition obstruction.

### Step 3 - Build a parameterized forced-spectator ledger for the full packet class

Depends on: Step 2.

Action: compute the forced on-class and off-class interactions for the chosen packet family, with geometry, helicity, conjugate completion, and allowed redundancy included from the start rather than as a late stress test.

Action: use minimal packets only as calibration examples. Any claimed obstruction must survive extension to the declared family, including small tails if those are allowed by Step 1.

Expected output: a parameterized ledger identifying which off-circuit channels are structurally forced and which remain movable under geometry or phase choices.

Kill condition: if the packet family itself is not coherent under the chosen bookkeeping stack, stop and report class-definition failure.

### Step 4 - Convert the ledger into a coercivity test on the chosen observable

Depends on: Step 3.

Action: derive lower-bound candidates only through identities that feed the Step 2 observable on the Step 1 time window. Track explicitly where phase cancellation, helical signs, delayed buildup, and pre-existing spectator amplitudes enter.

Action: separate three outcomes cleanly: a uniform positive lower bound on the declared class, decay of the infimum along an explicit adversarial family, or unresolved noncoercive behavior where no class-uniform conclusion survives.

Expected output: a finite inequality or optimization problem in the chosen normalization, or a family construction stated in that same normalization.

Kill condition: if the ledger only proves that extra couplings exist but does not yield sign, coercivity, or a low-leakage family for the chosen observable, downgrade to a route-obstruction result.

### Step 5 - Package the narrowest honest claim

Depends on: Step 4.

Action: if a positive infimum survives, state it with exact hypotheses: packet class, near-closure metric, observable, time window, and bound. Restricted packet-class theorems are acceptable if their scope is explicit.

Action: if the infimum tends to zero, require the witnessing family also to preserve the Tao-role logic over the stated window before calling it serious anti-obstruction evidence.

Action: if neither side is earned, state precisely why the leakage route fails, for example: invariant observable unavailable, cancellations destroy coercivity, or packet-class dependence prevents theorem scope.

Expected output: a theorem-like obstruction, an explicit low-leakage family, or a precise meta-obstruction to leakage-based arguments.

Kill condition: if the final product is still just a list of spectator couplings or a generic "NS is too entangled" narrative, the chain failed.
