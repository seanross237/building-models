# Exploration 001 Report

## Goal

Choose one primary singular-scenario class, and at most one secondary comparator,
for Step 3 of the geometry route, specifically for the narrowed primary Step 2
survivor:

`direction coherence + tube persistence`

This memo is restricted to scenario-class choice and scenario-level failure
modes. It does not define the final localization protocol or bounded observable
table.

## Method

- Start from the required Step 3 and Step 2 repository anchors.
- Identify which singular scenario classes are explicitly supported by the local
  record.
- Compare those classes against the live target: a scenario that fairly tests
  whether the hybrid can still force enough organization toward the
  full-stretching objective.
- Record exclusions if a scenario is weakly sourced, overfitted, or fails to
  stress the hybrid in the right way.

### Why filament or tube concentration is the fairest primary test bed

- [VERIFIED] The surviving Step 2 question is already phrased in tube-family
  language: whether direction coherence can persist `along a coherent tube
  family` strongly enough to make a full stretching contribution, including the
  exterior or inter-scale part, smaller in one fixed representation. Sources:
  - `missions/beyond-de-giorgi/steps/step-002/RESULTS.md`
  - `library/factual/geometry-route-screening/direction-coherence-plus-tube-persistence-is-the-primary-step-2-survivor.md`
- [VERIFIED] The repository does not treat tube persistence as a theorem-level
  criterion by itself; its only live content is dynamic persistence for
  coherent structures across scales and time, tied to a fixed scenario and
  localization protocol, and relevant to the full stretching mechanism. Source:
  - `library/factual/geometry-route-screening/tube-persistence-remains-live-only-as-a-dynamic-or-hybrid-route.md`
- [VERIFIED] Step 3 reasoning explicitly says the repo already leans toward a
  filament or tube concentration scenario for the surviving hybrid. Source:
  - `missions/beyond-de-giorgi/steps/step-003/REASONING.md`
- [INFERRED] Filament or tube concentration is therefore the fairest primary
  scenario because it stresses exactly the feature that kept the hybrid alive
  after Step 2:
  persistent, stretching-facing organization along a coherent tube family,
  rather than bare concentration or static alignment.
- [INFERRED] This scenario is not chosen merely because the word `tube`
  appears in the hybrid. It is chosen because the local Step 2 target is
  already a concrete test about whether tube-family persistence can reduce the
  exterior/inter-scale part of full stretching rather than merely preserve a
  visual tube picture. Sources:
  - `missions/beyond-de-giorgi/steps/step-002/RESULTS.md`

  discipline, not as the repository's favored natural home for the surviving
  hybrid. Source:
  - `missions/beyond-de-giorgi/steps/step-003/REASONING.md`
- [INFERRED] Retaining a sheet/pancake comparator is still useful, because it
  checks whether the hybrid really depends on coherent tube-family persistence
  or whether it secretly collapses into something weaker:
  generic direction regularity, generic concentration, or descriptive intense
  geometry.
- [INFERRED] But sheet/pancake concentration is inferior as the primary test
  bed because the local record does not tie the surviving hybrid to sheet-based
  persistence with comparable specificity. The repository's concrete live
  question is tube-family persistence facing full stretching; the sheet scenario
  enters mainly as a control comparison, not as the main operational target.
- [INFERRED] So sheet/pancake concentration should be kept as one secondary
  comparator, not promoted to co-primary status.

### Why other scenario choices are rejected

- [INFERRED] `Axial tube collapse` is too specific for the present step. The
  local record names it only as one example in an attack on underspecified
  scenario language, without any corresponding Step 2 survivor rationale or
  screening memo.
- [INFERRED] `Filament reconnection-type concentration` is also too weakly tied
  to the survivor set. It appears as an illustrative alternative geometry, but
  no required source connects it to the Step 2 hybrid more strongly than the
  broader filament/tube class already does.
- [INFERRED] Promoting either variant now would overfit the scenario choice
  before the repository has even fixed localization or a stretching
  representation. That would violate the Step 3 instruction to keep the choice
  at the scenario-class level.

### Scenario-level failure modes

- [INFERRED] Failure mode for `filament or tube concentration`:
  the hybrid may improve only core-local or self-induced geometry inside a tube
  family while the exterior/inter-scale contribution to full stretching, or the
  localization-evolution cost, remains uncontrolled. In that case the scenario
  degenerates into descriptive tube language and fails the Tao screen. Sources:
  - `missions/beyond-de-giorgi/planning-runs/run-002/attacks/chain-02.md`
  - `missions/beyond-de-giorgi/steps/step-002/explorations/exploration-002/REPORT.md`
  - `library/factual/geometry-route-screening/tube-persistence-remains-live-only-as-a-dynamic-or-hybrid-route.md`
- [INFERRED] Failure mode for `sheet or pancake concentration`:
  the chosen hybrid may stop looking like a tube-persistence mechanism at all
  and collapse into either a prior-art direction-coherence criterion or a bare
  concentration picture. Then the comparator shows misfit rather than leverage:
  the survivor was over-specialized to tube families and does not transfer to a
  non-tube geometry in a stretching-facing way. Sources:
  - `missions/beyond-de-giorgi/steps/step-002/RESULTS.md`
  - `missions/beyond-de-giorgi/steps/step-002/explorations/exploration-002/REPORT.md`

## Decision Memo

- [VERIFIED] The local repository support is strong enough to make an honest
  scenario-class choice. Step kill is not triggered on source-insufficiency.
- [INFERRED] Primary scenario class:
  `filament or tube concentration`
- [INFERRED] Optional secondary comparator:
  `sheet or pancake concentration`
- [INFERRED] Why this is the right bounded choice:
  the primary hybrid survived Step 2 only as a stretching-facing,
  persistence-heavy test about coherent tube families. Filament/tube
  concentration is the only scenario class in the local record that matches
  that live question directly. Sheet/pancake concentration remains useful only
  as a comparator to expose overfitting or collapse back into generic direction
  or concentration language.
- [INFERRED] Why the rejected alternatives are inferior:
  more specific variants such as axial tube collapse or reconnection-type
  concentration are named too sparsely in the repository to justify adopting
  them as official Step 3 scenario classes.
- [INFERRED] Implication for the next localization step:
  localization should be chosen to test persistence of direction coherence in a
  concentrated tube-family setting, while keeping the sheet/pancake comparator
  available as a stress test against hidden tube-adapted overfitting. This does
  not yet fix the protocol; it only constrains what the protocol must be able
  to discriminate.
