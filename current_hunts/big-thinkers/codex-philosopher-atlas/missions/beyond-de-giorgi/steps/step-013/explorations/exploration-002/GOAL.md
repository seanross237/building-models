# Exploration 002 Goal

## Objective

Freeze the burden ledger for the active reformulation-audit chain:

- choose one theorem-facing baseline burden;
- choose one admissible gain notion in that burden language;
- define the typed burden ledger with explicit currencies:
  derivative count,
  locality,
  cancellation,
  integrability,
  pressure recoverability,
  admissibility,
  compactness input,
  scaling compatibility;
- define the same-currency comparability rule;
- define when a transfer lemma is mandatory for cross-currency claims.

## Success Criteria

- later candidate packets can record gains and debts mechanically rather than
  narratively;
- one baseline burden and one admissible gain notion are frozen in concrete
  theorem-facing language;
- the same-currency rule and transfer-lemma rule are explicit enough to block
  silent cross-currency optimism.

## Failure Criteria

- the baseline burden stays vague or splits into multiple incompatible burdens;
- the gain notion is decorative rather than theorem-facing;
- the ledger currencies are too underspecified for later same-currency
  comparison;
- cross-currency claims are allowed without a named transfer-lemma burden.
