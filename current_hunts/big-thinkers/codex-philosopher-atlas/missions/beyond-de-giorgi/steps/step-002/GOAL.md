# Step 2 Goal: Chain Step 1 Benchmark Prior Art And Operationalize The Tao Screen

## Mission Context

**Mission:** Beyond De Giorgi — What Structural Property Could Break the NS Regularity Barrier?

**Active chain:** `Winning Chain - Calibrated Geometry Route with Explicit Tao Discriminator and Narrow Claim Discipline`

**Why this step exists now:** `step-001` closed the prior harmonic-tail pressure branch negatively, and planning run `run-002` selected the geometry route as the best new mission-live branch. The chain should not jump straight into observable design. It first needs a disciplined benchmark showing which geometric ingredients are genuinely live, which ones are prior-art restatements, and which ones Tao-style averaging would likely preserve.

## This Step Covers

This strategizer execution covers **Chain Step 1 only**:

- benchmark candidate geometry mechanisms against the main existing regularity frameworks;
- separate candidate NS-specific ingredients into the chain's three buckets:
  - transport or propagation structure,
  - exact algebraic structure,
  - multiscale coherence or concentration structure;
- state explicitly what Tao-style averaged or toy models would preserve and what they would destroy for each bucket;
- and decide which ingredients remain live enough to justify Step 2 of the chain.

Do **not** treat this as license to define the final scenario class, localization protocol, or kernel-level stretching representation. Those belong to later chain steps once the benchmark and Tao screen are complete.

## Required Deliverables

Produce all of the following inside `RESULTS.md`:

1. **Prior-art benchmark memo**
   - identify the main geometry-based regularity ideas that materially overlap this branch, including direction coherence, geometric depletion, and near-Beltrami or alignment routes;
   - state what each framework already controls, what claim boundary it earns, and where the present candidate family would merely restate that literature;
   - forbid novelty claims that are only rephrasings of known static singular-integral geometry.

2. **Tao-screen table**
   - bucket each candidate ingredient as propagation, algebraic, or multiscale/coherence;
   - for each bucket, state what Tao-style averaged models plausibly preserve and what they plausibly destroy;
   - classify each candidate as `live`, `weak but informative`, or `collapsed to Tao-robust/static geometry`.

3. **Step-2 readiness recommendation**
   - name the surviving ingredients, if any, that are concrete enough to justify fixing scenario class and localization next;
   - or recommend branch invalidation if every candidate collapses at the benchmark/Tao-screen stage.

## Exploration Tasks

Use **2-4 explorations total** unless a kill condition fires early.

### Exploration A: Benchmark the main geometry frameworks

Tasks:
- Read the most relevant prior-art material already available in the repository context for geometric depletion, direction coherence, vorticity-direction criteria, and local Beltrami or alignment ideas.
- Record what each framework actually buys at the estimate level, not just its motivating language.
- Mark where a proposed candidate would be merely a relabeling of that existing content.

Success standard:
- The benchmark names the real overlap points and the real gaps, not vague similarity claims.

### Exploration B: Operationalize the Tao screen

Tasks:
- For each candidate ingredient family, ask whether Tao-style averaged equations would plausibly preserve the same usable structure.
- Distinguish static Calderon-Zygmund geometry from ingredients that depend on exact NS transport, stretching, or coherence dynamics.
- State the discriminator sharply enough that later steps can reject Tao-compatible candidates without redoing this screen.

Success standard:
- Each candidate family receives a clear verdict with a short reason.

### Exploration C: Synthesize the live ingredient list

Only run this if A and B leave nontrivial survivors.

Tasks:
- Produce a short survivor list with explicit reasons each item remains live after both prior-art calibration and Tao screening.
- For each survivor, state what later Step 2 must still fix before observable claims are allowed: scenario class, localization protocol, and intended leverage point.
- Rank survivors by how likely they are to support full stretching control rather than descriptive local geometry.

Success standard:
- Step 2 can begin from a bounded live list rather than a broad geometry survey.

## Kill Conditions

Trigger an early negative conclusion for this step if either happens:

- every candidate mechanism collapses to prior-art geometry or to generic static Calderon-Zygmund structure with no additional NS-specific dynamic content; or
- every apparent NS-specific ingredient is either Tao-robust in the relevant sense or too vague to support a later observable-level test.

If a kill condition fires, count that as a successful obstruction result for this step and write the strongest honest negative memo. The output should recommend branch invalidation or replanning rather than drifting into Step 2 anyway.
