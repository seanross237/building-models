# Exploration 001 Goal

## Objective

Freeze the Step-11 scope lock for the coupled energy-helicity chain, or say
plainly that the scope lock cannot be fixed concretely enough to justify
continuation into the coercivity gate.

This exploration is responsible for:

- one endpoint burden family;
- one invariant package;
- one Tao-sensitive feature;
- one baseline criterion;
- one minimum quantitative gain threshold;
- one admission rule; and
- one novelty rule.

## Required Questions

Answer all of the following from local repository materials:

1. Which endpoint family is the most theorem-facing and least rhetorical on
   the run-007 record:
   continuation criterion,
   concentration exclusion,
   or stretching/enstrophy bottleneck?
2. Which invariant package should be frozen first:
   coarse-grained local balances,
   dyadic coupled fluxes,
   or another sharply defined ledger already supported on disk?
3. What exact Tao-sensitive feature is concrete enough to test at Step 1,
   rather than backfilled later from whichever decomposition looks best?
4. What frozen baseline criterion should later steps have to beat or
   genuinely sharpen?
5. What minimum gain threshold is still meaningful after localization,
   pressure, viscosity, and mixed-helicity debt are paid?
6. Can the admission rule and novelty rule be written in language that Step 2
   can enforce mechanically?
7. Does the local record support a real scope lock, or only a menu plus
   warnings of collapse?

## Working Hypotheses To Test, Not Assume

- `stretching/enstrophy bottleneck` is the right burden family because the
  local chain repeatedly demands a gain on one named bad term or transfer
  pattern rather than a broad continuation restatement.
- `dyadic coupled fluxes` is the sharpest honest invariant package on the
  current record; balance-law language is too generic unless tied back to the
  same exact ledger.
- The most plausible Tao-sensitive feature is not favorable same-helicity
  folklore but exact role-labeled helical packet coupling that Tao-style
  averaged circuits would wash out.
- The baseline is likely a named full stretching / enstrophy-growth bottleneck
  criterion, but the minimum gain threshold may remain qualitative rather than
  numerically frozen.
- If the baseline or threshold cannot be made concrete enough, the correct
  output is `setup obstruction`, not an optimistic setup memo.

Confirm, refine, or reject these hypotheses from source-backed evidence.

## Success Criteria

- Produce a scope-lock table with columns:
  `item`, `frozen choice`, `status`, `support`, `mechanical enforceability`.
- Label major claims `[VERIFIED]`, `[INFERRED]`, or `[PROPOSED]`.
- End with one of two explicit conclusions:
  `scope lock frozen for Step 2`, or
  `scope lock still obstructed at Step 1`.
- Keep the conclusion narrow enough that Step 2 cannot silently change the
  endpoint family, invariant package, or Tao feature.

## Failure Criteria

- The report leaves multiple endpoint families alive.
- The report treats Tao sensitivity as branding instead of an exact feature.
- The report freezes only slogans for the baseline or gain threshold.

If failure occurs, say so directly and recommend `setup obstruction`.
