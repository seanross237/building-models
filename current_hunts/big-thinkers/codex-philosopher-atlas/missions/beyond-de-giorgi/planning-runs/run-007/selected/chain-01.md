# Chain 01 - Exact-NS Versus Averaged-NS Structural Property Atlas

## Central Premise

The mission may be closer to a publishable obstruction map than to a single proof architecture. The right first branch is therefore a direct comparison between exact Navier-Stokes and Tao's averaged model, updated by all prior run negatives, with promotion only for properties that are both genuinely destroyed by averaging and operational at one named theorem endpoint.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a ranked shortlist of 1-2 exact NS structural properties, each with one exact destroyed-by-averaging statement, one concrete theorem endpoint, one expected gain currency, and one first missing lemma; or
- a calibrated obstruction memo showing that the currently visible NS-only properties fail by one of:
  identity-level exactness only,
  solution-class incompatibility,
  localization collapse,
  prior-art repackaging,
  or endpoint non-operationality.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is not a proof attempt inside one fixed architecture. It is a Tao-filtered property map whose job is to identify which exact NS features still deserve execution budget after the previous runs.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze the comparison protocol and the hard exclusions

- Depends on: none.
- Action: state exactly what Tao's averaged equations preserve for this mission and what counts as evidence that a property is truly destroyed by averaging.
- Action: record the hard exclusions inherited from prior runs:
  De Giorgi tuning,
  harmonic-tail coefficient shrinkage,
  weak-type packaging by itself,
  compactness rhetoric without an extraction package,
  and fixed-protocol exact rewrites of the LEI flux bundle.
- Action: require every later candidate to name one theorem endpoint or one obstruction output before it is called live.
- Expected output: a comparison memo with a preserved/destroyed ledger and an admissibility rule.
- Kill condition: if the comparison standard remains rhetorical rather than operational, stop and write an under-specified-filter memo.

### Step 2 - Build a bounded candidate property ledger

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: shortlist at most five candidate exact-NS properties, with current live candidates expected to include:
  material stretching and deformation,
  joint energy-helicity transfer,
  stochastic circulation or back-to-label structure,
  pressure-Hessian or harmonic-moment tensor structure as a comparator,
  and at most one additional genuinely distinct property if justified.
- Action: reject candidates that survive only as norm-level facts, generic Calderon-Zygmund packaging, or descriptive vortex language.
- Action: attach one-line prior-art notes so a renamed old family is not mistaken for a new route.
- Expected output: a candidate table with Tao verdict, prior-art note, and live/dead status.
- Kill condition: if no candidate survives as more than identity-level specialness, close the branch negatively.

### Step 3 - Attach each survivor to one exact endpoint

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: for each surviving property, choose one exact endpoint:
  a continuation criterion,
  a contradiction inequality,
  a criterion-scale insertion,
  or a mission-worthy obstruction statement.
- Action: name the progress currency for that endpoint:
  coercivity,
  monotonicity,
  cancellation,
  depletion,
  or a quantified reduction of one named remainder.
- Action: demote any candidate that cannot be attached to a concrete bottleneck term or criterion.
- Expected output: an insertion map from property to endpoint, bottleneck, and gain standard.
- Kill condition: if every candidate stalls at "interesting but not inserted," stop and write an endpoint obstruction.

### Step 4 - Run the anti-cosmetic and compatibility screen

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: test the surviving candidates against solution-class requirements, localization debt, and the prior negatives from earlier runs.
- Action: reject routes whose first missing lemma is just the full regularity problem in disguise, or whose supposed gain disappears once the actual equation or actual solution framework is restored.
- Action: require one precise failure label for each demoted candidate.
- Expected output: a reduced shortlist with explicit first missing lemmas and typed failure modes.
- Kill condition: if every survivor is cosmetic, compatibility-blocked, or circular, end with a ranked obstruction memo.

### Step 5 - Close with a portfolio handoff

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: promote at most two properties to follow-on execution chains and explain why they outrank the rest.
- Action: if nothing survives, state the strongest earned conclusion about what NS has that averaged NS does not, and why it still fails to operationalize.
- Expected output: either a mission-ready property ranking or a bounded landscape obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the final ranking claims more than the audited evidence supports, narrow it until each promotion is earned.
