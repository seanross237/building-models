# Chain 03 - Joint Energy-Helicity Flux Constraint Route

## Central Premise

The true 3D Navier-Stokes nonlinearity transports both energy and helicity. The live question is whether this coupled invariant geometry yields a theorem-relevant depletion or no-bad-cascade statement that is invisible to energy-only flux rewrites and stronger than the old symbol-first triad folklore.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- one conditional coupled-flux statement tying a specific energy-helicity transfer observable to a continuation criterion, concentration exclusion, or stretching/enstrophy bottleneck; or
- a calibrated obstruction memo showing that the apparent gain dies first by mixed-helicity restoration,
  localization collapse,
  viscosity,
  or reduction to thin-regime folklore.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is invariant and cascade-facing. It is not another LEI-flux rewrite audit and not another pure helical-triad sign ledger. Its core object is the joint energy-helicity budget of the full equation.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze one endpoint and one admissible invariant package

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one endpoint in advance, such as:
  a no-bad-cascade statement for a blowup scenario,
  a depletion estimate for enstrophy or stretching production,
  or a contradiction line for concentrated helicity-bearing regions.
- Action: choose one exact package for the chain:
  coarse-grained local balance laws,
  dyadic coupled fluxes,
  or another sharply defined joint energy-helicity ledger.
- Action: ban pure energy-flux reformulations and pure same-helicity folklore as standalone success criteria.
- Expected output: a setup memo naming the endpoint, invariant package, and minimum quantitative gain required.
- Kill condition: if the branch cannot say what theorem endpoint the joint invariant would change, stop and write an endpoint obstruction.

### Step 2 - Build the exact coupled-flux identity and Tao comparator

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: write the exact energy and helicity transfer identities in the chosen package, marking which parts are exact NS structure and which are only bookkeeping.
- Action: state what Tao-style averaging should destroy:
  exact coupling of energy and helicity channels,
  sign-sensitive transfer geometry,
  or a sharper scale interaction constraint than energy identity alone.
- Action: reject candidates whose distinction from the averaged model is only a change of decomposition.
- Expected output: a coupled-flux memo with one exact Tao-sensitive feature.
- Kill condition: if the coupled package collapses to energy-only control or a repackaged triad sign table, close the chain negatively.

### Step 3 - Isolate one mechanism beyond thin-regime folklore

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: choose one candidate mechanism, such as:
  a depletion effect tied to near-coherent helicity sign,
  a forbidden simultaneous transfer pattern,
  or a joint-flux imbalance that would reduce one named bottleneck.
- Action: benchmark it against old helical-decimation, Waleffe-type, and same-helicity prior art before promotion.
- Action: require the candidate to control more than a thin favorable interaction class, or else quantify exactly how much of the relevant transfer it captures.
- Expected output: one promoted coupled-flux mechanism with a prior-art note and a quantitative target.
- Kill condition: if the signal only lives on a thin or already-familiar interaction class, stop and write a regime-artifact obstruction.

### Step 4 - Restore the full 3D equation and pay the full bill

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: restore mixed-helicity interactions, low-helicity regions, viscosity, pressure, and any localization losses required by the chosen endpoint.
- Action: measure whether the Step 3 gain survives with positive margin after full restoration.
- Action: distinguish:
  real full-equation leverage,
  partial leverage that is endpoint-irrelevant,
  and exact but non-coercive invariant structure.
- Expected output: a reconstruction ledger stating whether the coupled-flux gain survives the actual equation.
- Kill condition: if the route only works in decimated, shell-model, or artificially filtered sectors, stop and write the obstruction.

### Step 5 - Close with either a coupled-invariant route or a sharp no-go

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: if a survivor remains, write a proposition-scale conditional statement naming the endpoint, the coupled invariant, the explicit gain, and the first missing lemma.
- Action: if not, state the narrowest honest failure:
  energy-helicity coupling is exact but too weak,
  mixed-helicity restoration kills the gain,
  localization kills the gain,
  or the route is only regime folklore.
- Expected output: either a coupled-flux attack path or a sharply typed obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the final claim can be read as "helicity is interesting," the chain has not earned promotion.
