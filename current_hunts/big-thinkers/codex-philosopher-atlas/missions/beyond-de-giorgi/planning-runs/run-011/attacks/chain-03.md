# Attack on `selected/chain-03.md` (original Chain 04)

The chain is not frivolous. It correctly targets the one pressure substructure that prior runs left nominally alive: the far-field piece is harmonic on the local cylinder. It also does better than generic pressure rhetoric by forcing recombination debt and a Tao check.

That said, the route is still structurally weak. Its hidden gamble is that "locally harmonic" means "locally cheaper." For Navier-Stokes pressure tails that is not automatic at all. The far-field pressure is harmonic inside the cylinder, but it is still generated by nonlocal quadratic data outside it. Without a genuinely NS-specific cancellation, the proposed observables risk being only a repackaging of the same exterior debt that already killed the global `H^1` route.

## Step-by-step critique

### Step 1

Freezing one decomposition and one endpoint is correct discipline, but the chain understates how delicate the decomposition itself is. A near/far split is not a neutral bookkeeping move:

- The split radius, cutoff, and normalization of pressure modulo time functions already affect what is even well-defined on the suitable-weak floor.
- The chain says "state what pressure objects are actually defined there," but it does not acknowledge that pointwise harmonic representatives, local `H^1` norms, and oscillation quantities may require subtracting means or choosing gauges before they are meaningful.
- The comparator is also too flattering. The real benchmark is not just the old failed global pairing. It is the best existing local pressure decomposition technology. Beating an internal failed route is not enough.

Failure mode: Step 1 can be "completed" with a memo that looks precise while quietly choosing a decomposition whose admissibility and normalization debt already consumes the alleged gain.

### Step 2

This step gives a menu of observables, but most of the menu hides the same nonlocal dependency in different packaging.

- A local `H^1` norm of `p_far` is only interesting if it is estimated by something strictly better than the exterior source norm that generated `p_far`. Otherwise it is the global route in local clothing.
- Campanato oscillation is not automatically cheaper either. Harmonic oscillation decay is an interior propagation statement, not a free source-to-observable improvement.
- Non-tangential maximal control is especially suspect here because it usually imports boundary-trace or larger-domain information. That is the opposite of genuinely local.

The kill condition "not visibly local or not clearly cheaper" is underpowered. The standard should be: show a same-currency theorem-line gain that is unavailable from ordinary local pressure theory. Anything weaker lets fake locality survive.

### Step 3

This is the most optimistic step in the chain, and also the most likely point of collapse.

- Harnack is for positive harmonic functions; pressure tails are sign-changing unless shifted, and shifting can destroy the quantity relevant to the theorem line.
- Caccioppoli and mean-value estimates usually trade an interior derivative norm for a slightly larger-domain norm. They do not by themselves create a better scale-invariant coefficient.
- Poisson-kernel representation makes the nonlocal dependence more explicit; it does not reduce it.
- "State where exponential or geometric decay is real" is already a warning sign. Harmonic oscillation decays across nested balls, but that decay may only govern a package-internal oscillation quantity, not the pressure term that appears in LEI or concentration exclusion.

The deeper problem is that "exact-versus-debt separation" may be artificial. The far-field harmonic control is only available after a split, and the split debt is not an external surcharge; it is part of the mechanism itself.

### Step 4

This is where the chain needs a miracle and has not earned one.

- The pressure enters theorem-facing lines through very specific pairings. A nice local harmonic norm is useless unless the actual LEI or criterion term can see exactly that norm after the allowed mean subtraction.
- Recombination is not a bookkeeping afterthought. If the near field is handled by standard local pressure estimates and the far field only by harmonic propagation of exterior data, the two pieces may recombine into essentially the same coefficient as before.
- The chain says "compare the net effect directly against the old failed global coefficient," but unchanged coefficient is too weak a failure test. Even a modestly improved coefficient can still be completely irrelevant if it does not cross the threshold needed by the endpoint.

Failure mode: Step 4 may produce a cosmetically improved split ledger that never changes the actual theorem-scale obstacle.

### Step 5

The Tao filter is placed too late, and the chain has not shown that it can survive it.

- Local harmonicity of a far-field tail is generic elliptic structure. Once the source is outside the cylinder, the interior solve is harmonic in many systems, not just exact NS.
- That makes this route look dangerously Tao-compatible from the start. It may depend only on pressure being recovered elliptically from a quadratic forcing, which is exactly the kind of harmonic-analysis structure Tao's averaged obstruction is designed to neutralize.
- The chain asks whether the precise feature is destroyed by averaging, but it never names a candidate feature that sounds plausibly destroyed. "Exact-NS harmonic continuation" is not yet a mechanism; it is just locality of the Poisson solve.

This filter should have been front-loaded. As written, the chain can spend most of its budget refining a route that may already be disqualified on first principles.

### Step 6

Demanding a hard artifact is right, but it comes too late for an estimate-driven branch.

- If the route is real, a prototype inequality should appear by Step 2 or Step 3.
- By postponing the artifact until the end, the chain permits too much pre-artifact optimization of decomposition, observable, and gain currency.
- The failure taxonomy is incomplete. Missing categories include theorem-floor indefinability, pressure-gauge dependence, and prior-art absorption by standard local pressure estimates.

As written, Step 6 risks certifying a highly massaged negative result rather than forcing early contact with the real obstruction.

## Structural weaknesses of the whole chain

1. The chain is built around a likely false inference: local harmonicity does not imply local cheapness. For a far-field pressure tail, the interior harmonic function is still driven by exterior quadratic data, so the route may be nonlocal in substance even if local in syntax.

2. The comparator is too weak. The branch should have to beat existing local pressure decomposition technology, not merely outperform the old failed global `H^1` attempt.

3. The theorem-facing endpoint is frozen too abstractly. "local concentration exclusion," "criterion input," and "one fixed LEI insertion line" are not interchangeable. Different pressure observables naturally speak to different lines, and the chain currently gives itself too much room to choose whichever line flatters the chosen norm.

4. The Tao issue is not a late-stage audit; it is a structural threat to the entire branch. If the only live input is harmonic interior regularity of an elliptically recovered tail, the route is probably dead before any detailed ledger is written.

5. The chain is biased toward survival by menu expansion. "One observable only" sounds strict, but the menu still includes an escape hatch of "another fully specified harmonic norm" and "another exact harmonic tool." That is enough slack to keep renaming the same idea after each failure.

6. The selected file is mislabeled: `selected/chain-03.md` is titled "Chain 04." That is a documentary issue, but it matters because it signals copy-through rather than a clean adversarial handoff. This chain in particular needs exact bookkeeping.

## Fair assessment

What is genuinely strong here:

- It targets the actual residual pressure obstruction rather than reopening dead global `H^1` rhetoric.
- It explicitly requires recombination debt, which many pressure ideas evade.
- It at least notices the Tao hazard instead of pretending all elliptic structure is automatically NS-specific.

What is most likely true:

- The branch dies by fake locality or Tao-compatible elliptic structure, not by a subtle technical glitch.
- If a positive result exists, it will need a very explicit NS-specific cancellation in how the exterior source generates the interior harmonic tail. Nothing in the current chain identifies such a cancellation.

## Verdict

This is a fair reserve attack line, but it is not yet a credible live route. The chain is strongest as a mechanism for proving that local harmonicity of the far-field pressure tail does **not** escape the old obstruction. Unless it can produce an early theorem-line inequality showing that an admissible local harmonic observable beats standard local pressure theory in the same currency, the honest expectation is obstruction, not progress.
