# Refined Chain 02 - Geometry Only Under Exact Criterion-Scale Admissibility

## Central Premise

Geometry has a high novelty ceiling and a high theorem-floor failure rate. This branch stays live only if one explicitly defined geometric observable, attached to one explicit package, is definable on the suitable-weak / LEI floor or via a bridge clearly cheaper than the target theorem, and then yields a criterion-scale gain beyond one exact geometric comparator after all localization, persistence, and nonlocal debts are charged in the same endpoint currency.

## Why This Refined Chain Is Different

This version keeps the original geometry-first ambition but removes its main escape hatches. It no longer allows a vague contradiction target, no longer permits composite observables to hide multiple burdens, and no longer delays the first hard artifact until after package development. Geometry survives here only by early theorem contact or by producing a clean bounded obstruction.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze one exact theorem target, one admissibility gate, and one exact comparator

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one theorem-facing target only, stated as an exact theorem or criterion-scale proposition with named hypothesis class and endpoint currency.
- Action: disallow broad "full-stretching contradiction" ambitions unless they are already translated into the same criterion-scale target currency.
- Action: state whether the observable is definable directly on the suitable-weak / LEI floor. If not, name one proxy class and prove that the bridge burden is strictly cheaper than the target theorem.
- Action: name one exact geometric comparator theorem, including its hypothesis class, conclusion, and the precise improvement margin being claimed.
- Expected output: an admissibility-and-comparator memo with an explicit target statement.
- Kill condition: if theorem-floor definability is absent, if the bridge burden is theorem-scale, or if the claimed gain over the exact comparator cannot be stated literally in the target currency, stop with a theorem-floor or prior-art obstruction.

### Step 2 - Choose one package and one non-composite observable with a literal endpoint map

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: choose one package only: tube-following localization, high-vorticity tracking, or one fully specified alternative.
- Action: choose one primary observable only, and forbid composite definitions that smuggle in extra amplitude, threshold, or persistence structure without charging them separately.
- Action: write the literal map from control of that observable to the chosen endpoint currency, including what norms, scales, and localization operators intervene.
- Action: state all hidden structural assumptions built into the package, including threshold stability, region selection, and frame choice.
- Expected output: a package-and-observable memo with a literal same-currency map.
- Kill condition: if the route needs multiple observables, if the observable-to-endpoint map exists only as package rhetoric, or if the package assumptions already encode most of the theorem burden, stop with an admissibility obstruction.

### Step 3 - Produce one minimal criterion-scale insertion artifact immediately

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: derive the smallest localized evolution, insertion sheet, or representative criterion-scale estimate needed to test the route honestly.
- Action: charge `nabla xi` debt, cutoff and boundary leakage, relocalization cost, pressure contribution, frame dependence, and nonlocal remainder inside the same artifact.
- Action: include one hostile stress test showing whether the artifact survives outside a hand-picked coherent regime.
- Expected output: an insertion artifact packet in the same endpoint currency.
- Kill condition: if the gain is exact only inside the package while every theorem-facing term carries comparable debt, or if the artifact survives only in a specially coherent scenario, stop with a package-theater obstruction.

### Step 4 - Run persistence, full-stretching coverage, and prior-art absorption as one joint audit

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: test whether the observable persists without repeated axis refitting, threshold drift, tube re-identification, or moving frame resets.
- Action: determine what portion of the true stretching ledger is actually controlled after the nonlocal remainder is included, not just what local favorable fragment looks improved.
- Action: compare the surviving statement directly to the exact comparator theorem from Step 1 and decide whether the route is genuinely stronger, merely equivalent, or absorbed by known direction-coherence or depletion language.
- Expected output: a joint persistence-and-absorption audit.
- Kill condition: if persistence requires retuning, if the controlled share is only a favorable local fragment, or if the surviving statement is absorbed by known prior art after honest charging, stop with a persistence or prior-art obstruction.

### Step 5 - Close narrowly, with Tao sensitivity as a secondary diagnostic only

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: classify the route as `live geometry`, `hybrid but weaker than needed`, or `bounded obstruction`.
- Action: state a first missing lemma only if it is genuinely narrower than a disguised restatement of the full regularity problem.
- Action: use Tao sensitivity only after theorem contact to explain whether the surviving geometric content is plausibly erased by averaging; do not treat this as positive evidence by itself.
- Expected output: a comparison-ready final memo.
- Kill condition: if the only surviving claim is that the geometry is NS-specific while still failing to improve criterion-scale control, or if the "missing lemma" is essentially the full problem restated, downgrade to bounded obstruction immediately.
