# Chain 03 - Harmonic Far-Field Tail Route With Early Admissibility and Anti-Fake-Locality Gates

## Central Premise

This branch is live only if, after fixing an admissible near/far pressure decomposition and gauge, one genuinely local observable of the far-field pressure tail yields a theorem-facing gain in the same burden currency, or through an explicit short conversion map, that standard local pressure decomposition technology does not already provide. Local harmonicity is not evidence by itself; the branch must show that harmonic interior structure is cheaper than exterior-source debt after recombination.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This remains the branch focused on exact harmonicity of the far-field pressure tail, not generic pressure improvement rhetoric, not packet geometry, and not a compactness route. Its narrower bet is that exact NS pressure splitting may expose one local harmonic observable whose theorem-facing cost is strictly lower than both the old failed global `H^1` ledger and ordinary local pressure theory. If that cannot be shown early, the branch should close as obstruction.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze one admissible decomposition, one gauge, one endpoint, and the real comparator

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one near/far pressure split only, including split radius, cutoff, local cylinder, and normalization of pressure modulo time functions.
- Action: specify the mean subtraction or gauge choice required to make the chosen local pressure observable well defined on the suitable-weak floor.
- Action: freeze one theorem-facing insertion line only; do not leave "criterion input" or "local concentration exclusion" as interchangeable options.
- Action: name the benchmark local pressure decomposition technology that this branch would need to outperform; keep the old failed global pairing as a secondary comparator only.
- Expected output: an admissibility-and-comparator memo.
- Kill condition: if the decomposition, gauge, or theorem-facing line cannot be made precise on the admissibility floor, stop with an indefinability or calibration obstruction.

### Step 2 - Choose one observable and front-load the anti-fake-locality / Tao screen

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: choose one local observable only and freeze it. If it needs boundary traces or larger-domain data by construction, justify that need explicitly or reject it.
- Action: write one candidate mechanism for why this observable of `p_far` could be cheaper than the exterior source norm and cheaper than standard local pressure theory in the same theorem currency.
- Action: state why the candidate mechanism is not merely generic elliptic interior regularity of an exterior-forced harmonic tail, and record the closest prior-art or Tao-compatible absorption threat.
- Action: forbid fallback menu expansion. No replacement observable or harmonic tool is allowed later unless Step 2 is declared failed and the branch is closed.
- Expected output: an observable-and-mechanism memo with an early Tao/prior-art note.
- Kill condition: if the observable only repackages exterior debt, or the candidate mechanism is already absorbed by standard local pressure theory or generic elliptic/Tao-compatible structure, stop with a fake-locality or absorption obstruction.

### Step 3 - Produce an early prototype inequality in theorem-facing currency

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: derive one prototype inequality linking the chosen observable either directly to the frozen theorem-facing line or to one explicitly named intermediate quantity in a short conversion map.
- Action: charge split radius, cutoff loss, gauge/mean subtraction, and the near-field compensation cost inside this prototype inequality rather than postponing them as later debt.
- Action: use only harmonic tools whose hypotheses fit the chosen quantity. Harnack is allowed only if positivity or harmless shifting is proved; otherwise use oscillation, Campanato, Caccioppoli, mean-value, or Poisson-representation estimates as appropriate.
- Action: compare the prototype against the benchmark from Step 1 in the same burden currency.
- Expected output: a prototype theorem-line inequality or a failed lemma.
- Kill condition: if no prototype inequality can be written, or if it does not beat standard local pressure technology in the same currency, stop with a same-currency or prior-art obstruction.

### Step 4 - Complete the harmonic control ledger without hiding split debt

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: expand the prototype into the exact harmonic control sheet for the chosen observable.
- Action: separate interior propagation gains from exterior-source dependence, but treat split and cutoff debt as intrinsic parts of the mechanism rather than optional later surcharges.
- Action: identify exactly which quantity exhibits decay or oscillation improvement and whether that quantity is the one the theorem line actually sees.
- Action: reject package-internal gains: if the decay only improves an auxiliary harmonic quantity and not the theorem-relevant ledger entry, mark that explicitly as failure.
- Expected output: a complete harmonic ledger with theorem-relevant versus package-internal quantities separated.
- Kill condition: if harmonicity improves only packaging and not the frozen burden currency, stop with a harmonic-packaging obstruction.

### Step 5 - Recombine near and far pressure and test the endpoint threshold

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: insert the surviving gain into the exact frozen theorem-facing line.
- Action: recombine near-field and far-field pressure before claiming success, charging localization, norm conversion, cutoff, and reconstruction debt explicitly.
- Action: compare the net coefficient not only with the old failed global ledger but also with the actual threshold needed by the chosen endpoint.
- Action: record whether the result beats standard local pressure theory after recombination, not before.
- Expected output: a recombination-and-threshold memo.
- Kill condition: if recombination restores essentially the old obstacle, or if the gain is real but still below endpoint relevance, stop with a recombination-collapse or too-weak obstruction.

### Step 6 - Produce one hard artifact and close with a narrow verdict

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: produce one hard artifact only: either an explicit theorem-line inequality, an explicit counterexample pattern, or a failed lemma isolating the obstruction.
- Action: if positive, write the narrowest live claim naming the decomposition, gauge, local observable, candidate mechanism, benchmark beaten, insertion line, net gain, and first missing lemma.
- Action: if negative, classify failure as theorem-floor indefinability, gauge dependence, fake locality, prior-art absorption, Tao-compatible elliptic structure, harmonic-packaging only, recombination collapse, or real-but-too-weak gain.
- Expected output: a comparison-ready route or obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if no hard artifact exists, stop with a documentary obstruction.
