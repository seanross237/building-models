# Refined Chain 01 - Calibrated Tao-Filter Audit with Architecture-Specific Comparison

## Central Premise

Treat this branch primarily as a disciplined screening and falsification chain, not as a free-form discovery ranking exercise.

Question: is there any exact Navier-Stokes feature that

- is genuinely destroyed or washed out by Tao-style averaging at a specified comparison level,
- survives contact with one named localized bottleneck in a concrete proof architecture,
- produces a quantitative gain in a declared comparison currency,
- and is not merely a slogan, a regime-specific artifact, or a repackaging of known non-operative themes?

If not, the correct output is a bounded obstruction memo or a hybrid handoff note, not a forced shortlist.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- one or more genuinely comparable survivors, each with an explicit Tao discriminator, a named bottleneck, a quantitative gain metric, and a first missing lemma; or
- a bounded negative result showing that the audited candidate family fails to operationalize under the fixed admissibility and comparability rules; or
- a hybrid handoff note showing that any apparent signal only becomes live after changing architecture or importing regime-specific structure.

## Why This Refined Chain Is Worth Running

The original chain had the right instinct but too soft a court of evidence. This refined version keeps the Tao filter and the bottleneck discipline, while adding the calibration and comparison rules needed to distinguish real progress from polished ranking theater.

## Scope Lock

Before candidate screening begins, the chain must fix all of the following:

- comparison granularity levels:
  - identity-level,
  - transport-law level,
  - coercive-estimate level,
  - localized bottleneck-interaction level,
  - or blowup-scenario level;
- an admissibility rule saying which levels count as genuinely mission-relevant;
- one worked example that passes the rule and one that fails it;
- intended solution class and localization regime;
- separate lists for:
  - earned technical negatives,
  - methodological exclusions,
  - and style warnings;
- the overlap-audit sources:
  - prior mission findings,
  - internal run history,
  - and the nearest known literature family;
- the policy that candidates are ranked only inside a shared architecture and gain currency.

If these items cannot be fixed concretely, the branch ends with an under-specified-filter memo.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Fix the evidentiary court and calibrate it

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose the comparison granularity level that will govern admissibility.
- Action: state exactly what "destroyed by averaging" means at that level.
- Action: produce one worked passing example and one worked failing example under the chosen rule.
- Action: separate technical negatives, methodological exclusions, and style warnings so later vetoes are not mixed arbitrarily.
- Expected output: a calibration memo defining admissibility, comparison level, and the pass/fail examples.
- Kill condition: if the admissibility rule cannot cleanly separate at least one pass case from one fail case, stop and write the under-specified-filter memo.

### Step 2 - Build a bounded candidate pool with overlap accounting

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: shortlist at most four candidates.
- Action: allow at most one wildcard candidate that is not simply one of the familiar slogan families already preloaded by the chain.
- Action: for each candidate, record a short ledger containing:
  - the exact NS-only feature being claimed,
  - the comparison level where averaging is supposed to destroy it,
  - the proof-relevance hypothesis,
  - and the nearest prior-art overlap.
- Action: reject candidates that are only identity-level, only packaging-level, or already absorbed by known negatives.
- Expected output: a candidate table with short evidence ledgers rather than one-line verdicts.
- Kill condition: if no candidate survives as more than an identity-level or already-absorbed theme, end with a bounded negative memo.

### Step 3 - Run feasibility and comparability checks before ranking

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: assign each surviving candidate one primary proof architecture and one exact bottleneck term.
- Action: allow a coupled secondary route only if the same bottleneck remains the evaluation target.
- Action: declare one quantitative gain currency for that candidate:
  - derivative count,
  - exponent or integrability improvement,
  - cancellation or coercivity gain,
  - localization stability,
  - or propagation time.
- Action: test immediately against intended solution class, localization demands, and an architecture-specific anti-cosmetic criterion.
- Expected output: an insertion memo showing which candidates are actually exposed to a localized bottleneck and what measurable gain would count.
- Kill condition: if no candidate reaches a named bottleneck with a non-cosmetic gain target, stop and close negatively.

### Step 4 - Compute full bottleneck interaction ledgers

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: for each surviving candidate, record where every cost lands:
  - localization losses,
  - projection or Calderon-Zygmund costs,
  - commutator costs,
  - compactness or propagation losses,
  - and any transfer of difficulty rather than real reduction.
- Action: if helpful, reduce the issue to one representative model lemma or subproblem tied to the same bottleneck.
- Action: reject verbal "attachments" that do not survive this loss accounting.
- Expected output: a bottleneck ledger marking each candidate as genuine gain, cosmetic gain, hybrid signal, or full collapse.
- Kill condition: if all apparent gains collapse after full loss accounting, write the bounded obstruction memo.

### Step 5 - Classify survivors as live, hybrid, or blocked

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: test any remaining signal against the declared perturbation or robustness standard.
- Action: classify each candidate as:
  - live within the primary architecture,
  - hybrid and better suited for another branch,
  - or blocked by regime dependence, circularity, or overlap.
- Action: require an explicit first missing lemma for any candidate still counted as live.
- Expected output: a classification memo that avoids forcing comparison across incommensurable routes.
- Kill condition: if all remaining signals are hybrid-only or blocked, do not promote them as live survivors.

### Step 6 - Compare only where comparison is earned

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: rank candidates only if they share the same primary architecture and the same gain currency.
- Action: if they do not, report bucketed outcomes instead of a single preference ordering.
- Action: promote zero, one, or more candidates according to the evidence; do not impose artificial scarcity.
- Expected output: either a narrow ranked shortlist inside one comparison class, or a bucketed result separating live leads, hybrid handoffs, and bounded obstructions.
- Kill condition: if the chain has to force apples-to-oranges comparisons to finish cleanly, replace the ranking with buckets.

### Step 7 - Close with the narrowest earned claim

- Depends on: Step 6.
- Action: state exactly which candidate family, comparison level, architecture, bottleneck, and gain metric were tested.
- Action: state exactly what Tao-discrimination did and did not buy beyond proof relevance.
- Action: if the result is negative, say whether the obstruction belongs to the admissibility rule, the bottleneck interaction, or the comparability standard.
- Expected output: one of three endpoints:
  - a live candidate with a quantitative bottleneck claim,
  - a bounded obstruction memo,
  - or a hybrid handoff note.
- Kill condition: if the final claim reaches beyond the audited family or the fixed comparison class, narrow it until it is fully earned.

## Probability Assessment

**Probability that this refined chain yields a presentable result: 0.74**

The most likely presentable output is a rigorous obstruction or hybrid-handoff memo. A positive survivor is possible, but only if it clears a stricter standard than the original chain required.
