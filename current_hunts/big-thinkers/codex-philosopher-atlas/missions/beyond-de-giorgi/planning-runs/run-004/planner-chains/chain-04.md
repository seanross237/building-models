# Chain 04 - Lagrangian / Stochastic Transport And Topology Route

## Central Premise

The exact NS equations may retain a transport-topological backbone, through circulation, helicity, vortex-line organization, or stochastic Lagrangian flow structure, that Tao-style averaging destroys. If regularity depends on that backbone, neutral Eulerian geometry screens were looking in the wrong place.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- one concrete Lagrangian or stochastic transport mechanism that feeds a stretching-control, persistence, or rigidity argument; or
- a bounded negative result showing that the transport-topological candidates are either viscosity-killed, solution-class-incompatible, or only descriptive.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is organized around transport and topology rather than around Eulerian local estimates, tensor eigenframes, or concentration-compactness.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Fix the admissible object list and solution framework

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose a bounded candidate family such as localized circulation, helicity balance, vortex-line transport, or stochastic Lagrangian representations of viscous NS.
- Action: decide what solution class is admissible for each candidate and reject objects that already require more regularity than the mission can assume.
- Expected output: an admissibility memo with one promoted object and one backup.
- Kill condition: if every candidate is already incompatible with the intended weak-solution setting, stop and write that compatibility obstruction.

### Step 2 - State the exact Tao discriminator

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: explain precisely what part of the transport-topological structure the averaged model should destroy or wash out.
- Action: reject candidates whose supposed specialness is only intuitive vortex language with no exact differential or stochastic statement.
- Expected output: a Tao-screen memo for the promoted object.
- Kill condition: if no concrete discriminator can be stated, terminate the branch negatively.

### Step 3 - Attach the object to one concrete regularity task

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: decide whether the promoted object is supposed to control stretching, enforce persistence of coherent structures, or exclude a critical element.
- Action: fix one bottleneck quantity or theorem gap where it would have to act.
- Expected output: an insertion memo from transport object to proof task and bottleneck.
- Kill condition: if the object cannot be tied to a concrete mathematical task, demote it to descriptive background and stop.

### Step 4 - Stress-test dissipation, localization, and weak-solution defects

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: compute where viscosity, localization, or stochastic representation error enters and whether the hoped-for mechanism survives those costs.
- Expected output: a defect ledger stating whether the route remains live, becomes conditional, or dies.
- Kill condition: if dissipation or localization destroys the only claimed leverage, close the branch with a sharp negative memo.

### Step 5 - Close with either a criterion-scale handoff or a negative diagnosis

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: if the route survives, translate it into a conditional criterion, rigidity ingredient, or scenario-specific persistence statement.
- Action: if not, state exactly whether the failure was Tao-insufficiency, viscosity, localization, or solution-class mismatch.
- Expected output: a concrete handoff note or a bounded obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the final claim relies on vague topology language rather than the audited transport object, narrow it until it is precise.
