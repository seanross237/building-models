# Chain 04 - Critical Element / Concentration-Compactness With NS-Specific Rigidity

## Central Premise

If all local mechanism hunts stall, the right object may be a minimal blowup profile rather than a sharper local estimate. This chain asks whether current NS compactness machinery can isolate a critical element in a workable phase space and whether one rigidity lever uses exact NS structure beyond Tao-preserved harmonic analysis.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- a concrete critical-element program for NS with one viable rigidity lever that is not obviously Tao-preserved; or
- an obstruction map explaining exactly why the supercritical gap or the lack of an NS-specific rigidity mechanism still blocks this route.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This is the only chain that starts from the blowup hypothesis and works globally through compactness, symmetry reduction, and rigidity. It does not depend on localized cutoff gains, triad geometry, or a particular vorticity package.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Choose a critical phase space and pre-screen for rigidity

- Depends on: none.
- Action: rank realistic candidate spaces such as `L^3`, `dot H^{1/2}`, `BMO^{-1}`, or another justified critical setting by extraction feasibility.
- Action: for each candidate, name one plausible rigidity lever that uses more than energy plus generic harmonic analysis.
- Expected output: a phase-space memo ranking extraction packages and naming the first candidate rigidity handle in each.
- Kill condition: if no phase space offers both a realistic extraction route and even one plausible NS-specific rigidity handle, stop immediately.

### Step 2 - Build the minimal-counterexample package

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: formulate the hypothetical minimal blowup object, normalize symmetries, and record what almost-periodicity or concentration properties are needed.
- Action: separate what is already available in the literature from what remains missing.
- Expected output: a critical-element dossier with assumptions, invariances, normalization, and theorem dependencies.
- Kill condition: if the minimal element cannot be formulated cleanly in the chosen space, stop and record that failure precisely.

### Step 3 - Stress-test one rigidity route against Tao's barrier

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: choose one route such as backward uniqueness, Liouville exclusion, self-similar rigidity, geometric concentration law, or another sharply defined mechanism.
- Action: test whether the route genuinely uses exact NS structure rather than only compactness plus harmonic analysis.
- Expected output: a rigidity memo listing required lemmas, known precedents, and the first missing NS-specific ingredient.
- Kill condition: if the chosen route collapses to Tao-preserved structure, stop and write that as the decisive obstruction.

### Step 4 - Organize any surviving rigidity route into a contradiction program

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: if one rigidity route survives, turn it into a stepwise contradiction blueprint from extracted critical element to exclusion.
- Action: keep the dependence on the chosen phase space explicit.
- Expected output: a contradiction blueprint naming the critical element, the rigidity mechanism, the bottleneck lemma, and the next execution target.
- Kill condition: if the route still requires a theorem no sharper than the original regularity problem, narrow the output to an obstruction map and stop.

### Step 5 - Close with a calibrated global claim

- Depends on: Step 4, or directly on Step 3 if the chain dies there.
- Action: state either the strongest viable critical-element path or the sharpest currently supportable reason this route does not yet escape Tao's obstruction.
- Expected output: either a concrete critical-element program with one named rigidity lever, or a polished negative result mapping the exact compactness and rigidity failures.
- Kill condition: if the final statement blurs multiple phase spaces or rigidity routes that were not actually tested, narrow it until each claim is anchored.
