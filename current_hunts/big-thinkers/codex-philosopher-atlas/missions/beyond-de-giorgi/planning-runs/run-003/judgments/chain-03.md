# Judgment on Chain 03

## Overall Verdict

The attacker is correct on the main calibration issue. This branch is still
worth keeping, but chiefly as a disciplined compactness-and-rigidity audit, not
as a likely positive breakthrough route. The original chain identified a real
alternative to local mechanism hunts, yet it bundled too many hard questions
into early screening language and did not operationalize what would count as a
genuinely Navier-Stokes-specific rigidity input.

## Major Critique Assessments

### 1. The selected file is mislabeled as "Chain 04," which signals route slippage

**Verdict: Partially Valid**

The labeling error is minor mathematically, but the attacker is right that this
planning loop needs cleaner route identity. Mislabeling increases the risk of
claim drift when nearby branches are already close conceptually. It is a real
process issue, just not a substantive mathematical objection.

### 2. Step 1 hides the main difficulty by demanding both extraction feasibility and a plausible rigidity lever

**Verdict: Valid**

This is the strongest critique. The original Step 1 effectively asks for the
hardest compactness and rigidity ingredients before the chain has separated
them. That is not a neutral prescreen. It front-loads the core unsolved burden
and makes the branch look cleaner than it is.

### 3. The candidate-space discussion is underspecified, especially around `BMO^{-1}`, solution notion, and threshold definition

**Verdict: Valid**

The critique is right that `L^3`, `\dot H^{1/2}`, and `BMO^{-1}` are not
comparable in any simple scalar sense of "extraction feasibility." The chain
also needs to specify what is being minimized, over which solution class, with
which continuation criterion, before candidate-space ranking means much.

### 4. Step 2 understates the machinery required to formulate a minimal element

**Verdict: Valid**

The original wording makes the threshold package sound like bookkeeping. In
this setting the main burden is exactly the package: profile decomposition,
stability theory, symmetry normalization, pressure handling, and a meaningful
almost-periodic or concentration statement. The attacker is right that these
are not optional refinements.

### 5. Step 3 is too permissive because it tests only one rigidity route from a heterogeneous menu

**Verdict: Partially Valid**

The criticism is fair, but not fatal. A single-route stress test can still be
useful if the conclusion is kept route-specific. The real problem is that the
original chain allowed broad inferences from narrow testing and did not require
a principled reason for the chosen route. The fix is to test representative
routes or sharply narrow the claim.

### 6. "NS-specific rigidity" is not operationalized tightly enough

**Verdict: Valid**

The attacker is right that explicit use of the Navier-Stokes equation is not
the same as obtaining leverage beyond Tao-preserved structure. The chain needs
a concrete discriminator: a candidate rigidity input should identify what exact
NS structure it uses, why Tao-style averaged models would not preserve it, and
why that structure survives the compactness regime of the hypothetical minimal
element.

### 7. The kill conditions are miscalibrated and do not detect disguised equivalence early enough

**Verdict: Valid**

Some original kill conditions were too early, especially in Step 1, while
others arrived too late, especially the warning against requiring a theorem as
hard as the original regularity problem. The refined chain should detect
solution-class mismatch, non-limit-stable assumptions, and disguised
equivalence before any route is promoted.

### 8. The branch is biased toward the Kenig-Merle template and risks rediscovering a familiar dead end

**Verdict: Partially Valid**

This branch is indeed a Kenig-Merle-style audit adapted to Navier-Stokes. That
is not a defect by itself, because the point of the branch is to test that
global route directly. But the attacker is right that prior-art adjacency must
be explicit and novelty claims must be modest.

### 9. Solution-class compatibility is underweighted throughout

**Verdict: Valid**

This matters at every stage. Mild solutions in critical spaces, suitable weak
solutions, local energy solutions, ancient limits, and self-similar objects do
not automatically share the same tools. The refined chain must treat solution
class and topology as first-order constraints rather than cleanup details.

### 10. The chain is more credible as an obstruction map than as a positive attack plan

**Verdict: Valid**

This is the fairest global summary. A positive program should only be credited
if it identifies a route whose missing theorem is materially narrower than a
renamed regularity statement. Otherwise the branch succeeds by locating the
precise failure point.

## Refined Judgment

Chain 03 should survive in narrowed form with four corrections:

1. Separate candidate-space and solution-class feasibility from later rigidity
   hopes.
2. Add an explicit coercive-backbone audit before any contradiction program is
   treated as credible.
3. Replace rhetorical "NS-specificity" with an operational Tao screen and a
   limit-stability requirement.
4. Restrict conclusions to the spaces and rigidity routes actually audited, with
   prior-art comparison built in.

With those changes, the branch becomes a useful decision procedure. It can
still surface a surprisingly viable route, but the most likely good outcome is
an obstruction map saying whether the compactness-and-rigidity program fails at
threshold setup, solution-class compatibility, missing coercive structure, or
lack of a genuine NS-specific rigidity mechanism.

## Probability Assessment

- Probability the refined chain yields a presentable result: **0.76**
- Probability it yields a genuinely actionable contradiction program: **0.12**
- Probability it yields a strong obstruction map or decision memo: **0.64**

The probability is reasonably high only because "presentable result" includes a
clean negative deliverable. The branch is still much more likely to clarify why
the critical-element route stalls than to produce a live exclusion mechanism.
