# Attack Memo - Chain 01

## Bottom line

This chain is credible as a tightly scoped negative-result branch and much less credible as a discovery branch.

Its real strength is discipline. It insists on estimate-level gains, not prettier identities, and it tries to front-load the Tao constraint. That is exactly the right instinct.

Its main weakness is that it quietly assumes any genuinely live beyond-De-Giorgi mechanism should appear as an immediate gain on one inherited localized bottleneck after an exact algebraic rewrite of the same quadratic nonlinearity. That is a very strong assumption, and it biases the chain toward proving that reformulations collapse back to the same obstruction. That outcome may be true, but the chain is structurally tilted toward it before the work begins.

## Structural weaknesses of the whole chain

### 1. The chain is framed as "beyond De Giorgi" but still judges progress in De Giorgi-adjacent currency

The success metrics are derivative reduction, improved integrability, or local closure improvement on a named localized interaction. Those are exactly the currencies inherited from the same estimate architecture the mission context says is exhausted at the harmonic-analysis level.

If the real NS-specific leverage only appears after changing the propagated quantity, changing the contradiction setup, or coupling algebraic structure to geometry or dynamics, this chain will call it irrelevant because it does not immediately improve the chosen bad term.

### 2. It assumes exact reformulation is a serious source of new leverage rather than mostly a change of presentation

For exact rewrites of NS, there is a default expectation: once one restores localization, projection, and commutator bookkeeping, the same quadratic structure reappears. The chain knows this, but still treats reformulation as a live discovery direction instead of treating "collapse back to the same estimate" as the generic baseline that must be defeated by a sharply identified mechanism.

That matters because otherwise Step 2 is not a real test of a broad hypothesis. It is mostly a check of whether a small familiar family of rewrites fails in the familiar way.

### 3. The Tao screen is conceptually right but operationally weak

The mission context is stronger than "the candidate uses an exact NS-looking feature." Tao's result blocks anything resting only on energy identity plus standard harmonic analysis. So the chain should demand a concrete statement of what the averaged model destroys and where that destruction enters the estimate. As written, a candidate can survive Step 1 by sounding algebraically special without showing that the specialness survives localization and actually matters quantitatively.

### 4. The chain treats geometry and tensor structure mainly as contamination, which may be a false separation

Step 3 is built to strip away gains that are "really" geometric or pressure-tensor effects. That is good if the goal is a pure reformulation result. It is bad if the only real NS-specific cancellations are hybrid: exact algebraic form plus alignment, or rewrite plus concentrated tube geometry, or rewrite plus tensor structure. The chain risks discarding the only live mechanisms because they are not pure enough.

### 5. The negative endpoint is much easier to earn than the positive endpoint

To promote a positive route, the chain demands a localized gain, Tao relevance, robustness away from special regimes, and a changed closure mechanism. To reach a negative endpoint, it only needs a small shortlist of natural rewrites to collapse back to familiar structure.

That asymmetry is acceptable if the chain is honestly labeled "cleanup of the exact-rewrite loophole." It is not acceptable if the chain is sold as a serious search for the live beyond-De-Giorgi property.

### 6. Prior-art overlap is larger than the chain admits

The mission context already says the vorticity formulation also lands at the same `beta = 4/3` barrier, and that exact Beltrami cancellation evaporates under tiny perturbation. So some of the most natural Step 2 and Step 3 outputs are already strongly prefigured. The chain is at real risk of spending budget restating known negatives unless it says exactly what new localized estimate is being tested that prior work did not already rule out.

## Step-by-step attack

### Step 1 - Fix the exact bottleneck term and the admissible notion of gain

This step is necessary, but it is under-specified at the exact point where the chain needs rigidity.

Concrete problems:

- It never says which bottleneck is mission-central. Is it the De Giorgi truncated pressure contribution, a local-energy flux term, a vorticity stretching localization, or some projected interaction? Those are not interchangeable. A rewrite that helps one may do nothing for the others.
- The allowed notions of gain are too heterogeneous. Derivative reduction, improved integrability, and "closure improvement" are different units. Without fixing the precise estimate and norm bookkeeping, Step 1 can compare incomparable effects and build a misleading shortlist.
- The shortlist cap of at most three reformulations is efficient but arbitrary. If the screening criteria are vague, the cap mostly encodes prior taste, not mathematical evidence.
- The Tao screen arrives too early and too weakly. Before the localization protocol is fixed, one cannot tell whether the candidate's supposedly NS-specific feature survives the actual estimate rather than only the bare identity.

Fair point: demanding a named bad term and a predeclared meaning of "real gain" is good discipline. The problem is that the chain does not make those declarations sharply enough.

### Step 2 - Test the shortlisted reformulations at the estimate level

This is the strongest step in the chain. It is also where the chain's hidden pessimism becomes almost built in.

Concrete problems:

- The natural test family is too familiar. Lamb-vector, projected form, and vorticity transport are exactly the rewrites one would expect to collapse back to the same structure unless some additional mechanism is injected. The mission context already suggests that at least part of this family has been effectively stress-tested.
- The step still lacks a fixed localization protocol. "Where the localization cost lands" is not a minor detail. It is the whole question. If the cutoff, projector, and commutator placement are not frozen before the test, the candidate estimates are not being compared on equal terms.
- The kill condition is close to tautological. Exact rewrites of the same quadratic nonlinearity generally do return the same Calderon-Zygmund or quadratic estimate once all costs are paid. If the chain only permits exact rewrites and treats that collapse as grounds for termination, then a negative result is the default, not a discovery.
- The estimate table could flatten important distinctions. Two rewrites may share the same headline scaling but differ in where commutator costs, projector losses, or localization errors appear. If those are not itemized explicitly, the table will overstate sameness.
- The step assumes that useful structure must show up at once in a first localized estimate. Some mechanisms only become visible after coupling to a second identity or to a dynamically propagated condition.

Fair point: the ban on identity-level simplification being counted as progress is exactly right.

### Step 3 - Run contamination screens before promoting any gain

This step is sensible as a guardrail, but the chain uses it in a way that may discard the only plausible positives.

Concrete problems:

- It treats geometry, near-Beltrami structure, and tensor effects as things to subtract away before believing a gain. That is too purity-driven for this mission. The live beyond-De-Giorgi mechanism may be hybrid rather than purely algebraic.
- "Survive modest perturbation away from special alignment" is not operationally defined. Perturbation in what norm, on what scale, and relative to which localization? A cancellation can look robust or fragile depending on the metric.
- The contamination screen comes after Step 2 instead of being integrated into it. Given the mission context, fragility to alignment and branch-mixing are not afterthoughts; they are central facts. Waiting until Step 3 means Step 2 may already have overinvested in a mirage.
- The handoff logic is too clean. If a rewrite only helps when combined with local geometry, that may be evidence of the correct mechanism, not evidence that the reformulation chain failed.

Fair point: the step is right to reject special exact solutions or alignment regimes as stand-alone evidence.

### Step 4 - Convert any surviving gain into a concrete non-De-Giorgi attack path

This is the right pressure test, but the chain is too eager to move from a local estimate improvement to a theorem path.

Concrete problems:

- It never specifies which proof architecture the proposition is supposed to feed. "Concrete proposition" is not enough if the proposition has no credible insertion point into a non-De-Giorgi argument.
- The demand that the gain "improve the operative mechanism itself" is correct, but not quantified. Without a benchmark for what counts as materially changing closure, Step 4 reduces to taste.
- The chain assumes that the main valuable positive output would be a closure criterion or theorem target. A sharp obstruction, rigidity statement, or clean no-go theorem for a reformulation family could be more realistic and more valuable.
- There is a risk of laundering a tiny local cancellation into a grand "attack path" with a huge unpriced gap between the estimate and the actual regularity mechanism.

Fair point: the step correctly tries to stop cosmetic positives from being promoted.

### Step 5 - Close with the strongest calibrated claim

This step has the right instinct, but the likely evidence base is too narrow for the likely wording.

Concrete problems:

- If only a short list of familiar rewrites was tested, the negative claim must be very narrow. It cannot honestly become "exact reformulations do not help" without defining the family much more tightly.
- The positive endpoint still sounds too easy: "a specific improved estimate" is not enough unless the memo also states exactly why the estimate uses a feature Tao-style averaging destroys.
- The negative endpoint risks conflating "these few natural rewrites collapse" with "reformulation as a strategy is dead." The chain can support the former far more easily than the latter.
- The final claim is vulnerable to selection bias from Step 1. If the shortlist was narrow or taste-driven, Step 5 inherits that bias.

Fair point: the instruction to narrow the final claim until fully earned is good. It just needs to be enforced ruthlessly.

## Overall verdict

This chain is worth running if it is advertised honestly:

- it can close a specific loophole in the "maybe an exact rewrite creates an SQG-style gain" story;
- it can produce a bounded negative result on a tightly defined family of exact reformulations;
- and it can prevent more time from being spent on cosmetic identity-chasing.

It is not a strong primary search chain for the live beyond-De-Giorgi mechanism because it bakes in two dubious assumptions:

1. that the decisive NS-specific structure should appear as an immediate estimate-level gain on one inherited localized bottleneck;
2. that algebraic reformulation can be cleanly separated from geometry, tensor structure, or dynamics early enough that those can be treated mainly as contamination.

The ruthless but fair summary is:

This is a good chain for proving that a small family of exact Navier-Stokes rewrites does not magically create an SQG-like gain inside the current local estimate architecture. It is a weak chain for discovering new structure, because it searches in a narrow place and judges success in the language of the architecture that has already failed.
