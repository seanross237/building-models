# Chain 04 - Local Beltrami Deficit To Geometric Criterion Bridge

## Central Premise

Exact Beltrami structure annihilates the Lamb vector, but global Beltrami alignment is too rigid to matter. The live geometric question is narrower: can a quantitatively small local Beltrami deficit on intense-vorticity regions be converted into a stretching bound, a Constantin-Fefferman type direction criterion, or another criterion-scale input that survives transport, diffusion, and nonlocal strain?

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- one conditional geometric criterion or mechanism tying local Beltrami deficit on high-`|omega|` sets to a concrete regularity task; or
- a bounded obstruction memo showing exactly where the vortex-core geometry signal dies: transport, localization, nonlocal strain, or criterion mismatch.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This chain is geometry-first and region-specific. It studies coherent high-vorticity structure rather than global compactness, spectral triads, or tensor observables chosen without reference to vortex-core regions.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Fix the geometric package and the audited region class

- Depends on: none.
- Action: define the region class to be audited, for example high-`|omega|` sets, tube-core neighborhoods, or another explicit coherent-structure proxy.
- Action: define one quantitative local Beltrami deficit and one admissible solution framework where it is meaningful.
- Action: decide in advance whether the target output is a stretching bound, a direction-coherence criterion, or a bridge to a known geometric regularity theorem.
- Expected output: a setup memo fixing the region class, the deficit observable, the solution class, and the target criterion family.
- Kill condition: if the deficit observable cannot be defined on a realistic weak or suitable solution framework, stop and write a compatibility obstruction.

### Step 2 - Tie local Beltrami deficit to one concrete regularity bottleneck

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: choose one bottleneck theorem gap, such as control of `S omega . omega`, a Constantin-Fefferman direction regularity hypothesis, or another named geometric criterion.
- Action: state exactly why Tao-style averaging should destroy the promoted geometric feature at that comparison level.
- Action: reject any path whose only claim is descriptive tube language with no criterion-scale target.
- Expected output: an insertion memo from local Beltrami deficit to one bottleneck term or criterion.
- Kill condition: if no criterion-scale insertion can be stated concretely, close the chain negatively.

### Step 3 - Stress-test transport, persistence, and diffusion

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: analyze whether small local Beltrami deficit on the chosen regions has any plausible persistence mechanism under advection, vortex stretching, and viscosity.
- Action: determine whether the route requires tube persistence, directional coherence propagation, or another transport statement stronger than what the framework supplies.
- Action: classify the first missing dynamic ingredient precisely.
- Expected output: a persistence ledger showing whether the geometry signal is instant, propagating, or immediately lost.
- Kill condition: if the deficit signal has no plausible persistence even for the criterion being targeted, stop and write a transport obstruction.

### Step 4 - Charge nonlocal strain and localization debt honestly

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: test whether favorable geometry on the chosen regions can control the full stretching mechanism once nonlocal strain from outside the region is reintroduced.
- Action: account for cutoff costs, small-volume effects, and the possibility that tiny vortex cores do not influence the decisive part of the dynamics.
- Action: classify failure as nonlocal-strain freedom, localization collapse, or measure-insufficient signal.
- Expected output: a debt ledger stating whether local Beltrami structure can affect the full equation rather than only a local caricature.
- Kill condition: if outside-region strain or localization losses erase the claimed gain, terminate with an obstruction memo.

### Step 5 - Convert any survivor into criterion scale

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: if one route survives, state a theorem-like conditional criterion naming the region class, the deficit assumption, the target bottleneck, and the first missing lemma.
- Action: if nothing survives, state the narrowest earned obstruction and say whether the failure was criterion mismatch, transport loss, or nonlocal freedom.
- Expected output: either a criterion-scale geometric route or a sharply typed geometric obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the final claim relies on vague vortex-language rather than the audited deficit observable and region class, the chain has not earned promotion.
