# Judgment for Selected Chain 02

Note: the file at `selected/chain-02.md` is titled `Chain 01 - Deterministic Material Stretching Route With Admissible Proxy Freeze`. This judgment addresses the file contents rather than the internal title.

## Overall Verdict

The attacker is mostly correct. The original chain has good hygiene instincts: it freezes one observable, one endpoint, and asks for a hard artifact. But the critique correctly identifies that the decisive risk is earlier and harsher than the chain admits: theorem-floor admissibility, proxy-bridge inflation, localization/interface mismatch, and prior-art redundancy all need to become gating conditions, not later bookkeeping.

I do not accept the strongest version of the attacker's pessimism. A deterministic stretching route can still yield a presentable result as a bounded obstruction memo, and possibly as a narrow conditional route, if the chain is rewritten to force same-currency endpoint accounting and earlier hard stops.

## Major Critique Assessment

### 1. Step 1 is too permissive about observable choice and proxy fallback

**Verdict: Valid**

The critique is right that the original menu mixes genuinely different object classes, and that "named bridge burden" is too weak. A proxy class is acceptable only if the bridge back to suitable-weak / LEI data is explicitly quantified and clearly cheaper than the target theorem. That stricter admissibility test belongs at Step 1.

### 2. Step 2 risks mistaking formal evolution bookkeeping for analytical control

**Verdict: Valid**

The original Step 2 allows the branch to survive on a polished defect ledger even when the defects are the whole problem. The refined chain should terminate immediately if the key production term is exact only under unavailable regularity, or if commutator, temporal-window, and nonlocal terms are not credibly lower order in the same ledger.

### 3. Step 3 cherry-picks one bad term and tests coercivity before localization

**Verdict: Valid**

This is a real structural flaw. For a concentration-exclusion endpoint, localization and pressure handling are part of the main problem, not a wrapper added later. The refined chain should test one exact theorem-facing bottleneck inside the localized interface, not a pre-localization surrogate.

### 4. Step 4 understates how likely endpoint insertion is to erase the gain

**Verdict: Valid**

The critique is right that the route matters only if the promoted quantity enters an actual LEI / CKN line in the right scale and units. The refined chain should treat insertion as a full gain-versus-debt interface test, with explicit rejection of cross-currency gains lacking a transfer lemma.

### 5. Step 5 asks for a hard artifact too late and overweights Tao comparison

**Verdict: Valid**

The artifact requirement is a strength, but it should appear earlier, immediately after the first endpoint-facing bottleneck and gain claim are fixed. The critique is also right that Tao sensitivity is secondary evidence, not a substitute for endpoint relevance.

### 6. Step 6 is too optimistic about what counts as a narrow remaining lemma

**Verdict: Partially valid**

The warning is fair: for this branch the "missing lemma" can easily be the whole theorem in disguise. The refined chain should add proxy-bridge inflation and prior-art redundancy to the failure taxonomy and should downgrade aggressively when the remaining lemma is not genuinely narrower. I do not accept that a narrow conditional remainder is impossible in principle, only that the original chain did not guard against fake narrowness tightly enough.

### 7. The chain is structurally biased toward rescuing transport language

**Verdict: Partially valid**

The central premise does lean rescue-ward, and the language of "promoted gain" and "insertion" presumes life too early. Still, the original chain already had several kill conditions and was not pure advocacy. The right fix is tonal and procedural: make obstruction the default live outcome until theorem-floor admissibility and endpoint insertion are both earned.

### 8. The chain underestimates the theorem-floor mismatch

**Verdict: Valid**

This is the main substantive criticism. Admissibility on suitable-weak / LEI data, or a quantitatively cheaper proxy descent, is not setup debt. It governs the whole branch and should be fused with endpoint-interface screening near the front of the chain.

### 9. The chain lacks a real prior-art / novelty gate

**Verdict: Valid**

The critique is right. A transport-stretching branch can easily rename known strain criteria, deformation heuristics, or coarse-grained observables without changing the burden ledger. The refined chain should require a prior-art comparator and a concrete claimed delta before continuing.

### 10. The kill conditions are too soft and too late

**Verdict: Valid**

This follows from the earlier points. Earlier hard stops are needed for proxy inflation, defect-ledger non-separation, and endpoint-currency mismatch.

### 11. The original sequential layout hides strong coupling between admissibility, localization, and coercivity

**Verdict: Partially valid**

The attacker is right about the coupling, but a sequential chain can still work if the steps are rewritten so each one explicitly carries forward the localized endpoint ledger and does not allow isolated pre-wins. The issue is not the existence of steps; it is the original step boundaries.

## Refined Judgment

The refined chain should keep the original discipline of one observable and one endpoint, but it must:

- require direct theorem-floor definability or a proxy bridge demonstrably cheaper than the target theorem;
- freeze the endpoint burden ledger before any coercivity talk;
- add an explicit prior-art / anti-repackaging gate;
- test gains only inside the localized theorem-facing interface;
- demand a hard artifact before late-stage comparison language;
- treat obstruction as the base case and a positive route as exceptional.

## Probability Assessment

**Probability that the refined chain yields a presentable result: 0.74**

The most likely presentable output is a disciplined obstruction memo showing that deterministic stretching observables either fail theorem-floor admissibility, fail same-currency endpoint insertion, or repackage known strain criteria without reducing the burden ledger. A positive conditional route is still possible, but only if one observable survives those front-loaded gates with a real localized transfer artifact.
