# Chain 01 - Deterministic Material Stretching Route With Admissible Proxy Freeze

## Central Premise

The stochastic transport branch failed because its objects did not live on the inherited theorem floor. That does not kill transport-side structure altogether. This chain is live only if one deterministic stretching observable can be defined either directly on the suitable-weak / LEI ledger or on one explicitly bridged coarse-grained proxy class, and then yields a theorem-facing gain on cumulative stretching or local concentration without importing smooth-flow or filtration machinery.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This is the only transport-first chain in the portfolio. It is not a geometry-package reboot, not a helical-symbol audit, and not a compactness architecture. Its core bet is that deterministic deformation or stretching clocks may still encode an exact-NS feature that stochastic packaging obscured.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze one admissible stretching observable, one framework, and one endpoint

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one observable only, such as cumulative positive strain along coarse-grained trajectories, a mollified deformation tensor growth quantity, or another equally explicit deterministic stretching clock.
- Action: state exactly where that observable lives: the inherited suitable-weak / LEI floor if possible, otherwise one stronger proxy class with a named bridge burden back to the theorem floor.
- Action: freeze one theorem-facing endpoint: local concentration exclusion by default, continuation only if the observable already behaves like norm control.
- Expected output: a scope-lock memo naming one observable, one admissible framework, one bridge burden if needed, and one endpoint.
- Kill condition: if the observable is not honestly definable without smooth-flow debt or an unnamed proxy bridge, stop with a solution-class setup obstruction.

### Step 2 - Derive the exact or coarse-grained evolution and isolate the defect ledger

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: write the exact or coarse-grained evolution identity for the chosen observable.
- Action: separate deterministic transport content from viscous defect, commutator debt, temporal-window debt, and nonlocal strain freedom.
- Action: mark which pieces are exact on the chosen framework and which already assume extra regularity.
- Expected output: an evolution sheet plus a defect ledger.
- Kill condition: if the observable is exact only for smooth flow maps or if the defect terms already dominate the quantity of interest, stop with a definability obstruction.

### Step 3 - Freeze one host bad term and test coercivity before localization

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: choose one concrete host bad term only, such as positive stretching production or a fixed local-concentration feed line.
- Action: ask whether the observable yields one-sided control, an exclusion principle, or a monotonicity surrogate on that exact bad term.
- Action: compare the proposed gain to one frozen baseline burden rather than to generic transport rhetoric.
- Expected output: a coercivity memo with one promoted gain at most and one failure table.
- Kill condition: if the observable is exact but analytically non-coercive on the frozen bad term, stop with a transport-without-leverage obstruction.

### Step 4 - Insert the promoted gain into one theorem-facing line and charge all debt early

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: place the gain into one inherited LEI / CKN concentration-exclusion line, or into the continuation reserve only if Step 1 already justified that endpoint.
- Action: charge cutoff loss, pressure debt, nonlocal strain freedom, and any proxy-to-theorem transfer debt before claiming progress.
- Action: reject gains that control only a trajectory-level surrogate with no theorem-facing cash-out.
- Expected output: a localization-and-insertion memo.
- Kill condition: if localization or proxy transfer erases the gain, stop with an endpoint-transfer obstruction.

### Step 5 - Demand one hard artifact and only then check Tao sensitivity

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: produce one hard artifact: a prototype inequality, a reduced deterministic scenario, or a failed lemma isolating the precise obstruction.
- Action: ask whether the surviving feature is plausibly destroyed by Tao-style averaging only after the artifact exists.
- Expected output: an evidence packet plus a Tao-comparator note.
- Kill condition: if the branch still relies on transport language with no artifact, stop with a documentary obstruction.

### Step 6 - State the narrowest earned claim

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: if positive, write one conditional route naming the exact observable, admissible framework, theorem endpoint, insertion point, and first narrower missing lemma.
- Action: if negative, classify failure as solution-class mismatch, viscous-defect collapse, non-coercivity, nonlocal-strain freedom, or endpoint-transfer failure.
- Expected output: a final-comparison-ready route or obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the remaining missing lemma is just full regularity renamed in transport language, downgrade immediately to obstruction.
