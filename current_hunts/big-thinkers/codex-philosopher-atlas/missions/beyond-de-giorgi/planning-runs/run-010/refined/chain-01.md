# Refined Chain 01 - Criterion-First Geometric Mechanism Stress Test

## Central Premise

Geometry is not presumed to be the leading route. It is a hypothesis under audit. This branch is live only if one explicit geometry-aware package and one geometric observable can produce theorem-facing leverage beyond the closest live geometric prior art, with the localization and persistence debt paid in the same currency as the claimed gain.

If that cannot be shown, the correct output is a bounded obstruction memo or a hybrid geometry-transport handoff, not a rescued geometry narrative.

## Verifiable Result Target

Either:

- one live geometry packet containing a named endpoint, closest prior-art comparator, explicit package, observable-to-endpoint map, quantitative debt ledger, and prototype insertion or estimate; or
- one bounded obstruction memo showing package theater, endpoint-currency mismatch, persistence collapse, nonlocal remainder dominance, prior-art absorption, or transport dependence; or
- one hybrid handoff memo showing that the apparent gain only becomes live after importing transport structure.

## Why This Refined Chain Is Better

The original chain had the right pressure points but reached them too late. This refined version forces theorem-facing contact earlier, front-loads prior-art comparison, and treats geometry-favoring scenarios as stress tests rather than as admissions evidence.

## Scope Lock

Before the chain is active, it must freeze all of the following:

- one theorem-facing endpoint only:
  a Constantin-Fefferman-type criterion by default, or one named full-stretching contradiction target;
- one closest live geometric prior-art comparator;
- one package only:
  tube-following localization,
  high-vorticity tracking,
  or one fully specified named alternative written out concretely;
- one primary observable family only;
- one gain currency only:
  burden share of stretching,
  derivative count,
  threshold stability,
  criterion input,
  or localization stability;
- one favorable scenario and one hostile scenario for stress testing;
- one burden ledger carrying:
  `nabla xi` debt,
  boundary leakage,
  tube-identification ambiguity,
  relocalization cost,
  and nonlocal remainder cost.

If these cannot be fixed concretely, stop with an under-specified geometry memo.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Freeze the endpoint, prior-art comparator, and candidate proposition first

- Depends on: none.
- Action: choose one endpoint family only and write one candidate proposition, contradiction line, or insertion claim before selecting a flattering package.
- Action: name the closest live geometric prior art as the primary comparator and keep the killed neutral-Eulerian package only as a secondary comparator.
- Action: state one minimum non-cosmetic gain in the chosen currency.
- Expected output: an endpoint-and-comparator memo containing one candidate theorem-facing statement.
- Kill condition: if no candidate statement can be written before package optimization, or if the claimed gain is not clearly stronger than descriptive convenience, stop with an endpoint-free geometry obstruction.

### Step 2 - Choose one explicit package and one observable with a currency map

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: choose one package from the closed menu, or write one fully specified named alternative with no "another geometry-aware regime" escape hatch.
- Action: choose one primary observable family only and explain why it naturally speaks to the frozen endpoint and gain currency.
- Action: choose one favorable scenario and one hostile scenario, but use them only as later stress tests rather than as evidence that the route is live.
- Expected output: a package-and-observable memo with an explicit observable-to-endpoint map.
- Kill condition: if the observable-endpoint map requires an unproven conversion lemma that is already as hard as the original problem, stop with a currency-mismatch obstruction.

### Step 3 - Run a quantitative persistence and debt screen outside the package

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: quantify the burden ledger immediately, including derivative debt, frame-tracking debt, leakage, and relocalization cost.
- Action: test whether the observable persists on the actual theorem floor, not merely inside a continually refitted package.
- Action: allow one auxiliary observable only if its cost is separately charged and the primary observable still carries the route.
- Expected output: a persistence ledger comparing minimum gain against total package debt.
- Kill condition: if persistence depends on repeated retuning, hidden axis fitting, or debt that already exceeds the minimum gain, stop with a persistence-collapse obstruction.

### Step 4 - Audit contact with full stretching in a representation-robust way

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: test the promoted observable against a canonical stretching representation and check that the conclusion survives translation to any alternate representation used for the claim.
- Action: separate near-field gain, nonlocal or exterior remainder, and localization-evolution debt.
- Action: require either a quantitatively meaningful burden share of the full stretching term or a precise explanation of how the controlled share propagates to the endpoint.
- Expected output: a full-stretching audit with unpaid remainder clearly identified.
- Kill condition: if the observable controls only a local depletion fragment, or only works in a friendly representation, stop with a full-stretching obstruction.

### Step 5 - Produce a hard artifact before any promotion

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: produce one hard artifact:
  a prototype estimate,
  a representative insertion into the candidate proposition,
  or a localized stress test that runs both the favorable and hostile scenarios.
- Action: treat a failed lemma as obstruction evidence only, unless the failure still isolates a narrower live theorem-facing insertion point.
- Action: benchmark the artifact against the closest live geometric prior art and the neutral-Eulerian comparator.
- Expected output: an evidence packet showing either genuine gain or sharp failure.
- Kill condition: if the artifact merely repackages direction coherence, Beltrami depletion, or tube persistence without a stronger insertion point than prior art, stop with a prior-art absorption obstruction.

### Step 6 - Use Tao sensitivity and transport dependence as secondary discriminators

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: ask whether the artifact is plausibly erased by Tao-style averaging, but only after theorem-facing contact has already been shown.
- Action: ask whether the claimed gain actually depends on imported quasi-Lagrangian or transport machinery.
- Action: classify the route as:
  live geometry,
  hybrid geometry-transport,
  or bounded obstruction.
- Expected output: a narrow final-classification memo rather than a prestige screen.
- Kill condition: if the only surviving leverage is transport-dependent or Tao-insensitive in a way that erases the claimed distinctiveness, downgrade to hybrid or obstruction.

### Step 7 - State the narrowest earned claim

- Depends on: Step 6.
- Action: state exactly which endpoint, comparator, package, observable, burden currency, and stress tests were audited.
- Action: state exactly what was gained, what debt was paid, and what obstruction remained.
- Action: record the first missing lemma only if the route survived the hard-artifact gate.
- Expected output: one of three endings:
  live geometry packet,
  hybrid handoff memo,
  or bounded obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if the conclusion outruns the artifact or debt ledger, narrow it until it is fully earned.

## Probability Assessment

**Probability that this refined chain yields a presentable result: 0.78**

The likely presentable result is a disciplined obstruction memo rather than a positive geometric mechanism. That is acceptable here because the chain is now designed to test whether geometry actually buys a theorem-facing lever, not to rescue geometry by packaging alone.
