# Chain 03 - Packet-Complete Helical Route With Early Absorption and Reconstruction Gates

## Central Premise

The helical packet branch is live only if an exact packet feature survives three front-loaded tests: it is not already absorbed by known helical or triadic analysis, it has a plausible theorem-facing insertion point before symbolic optimization begins, and it can be converted into a host burden ledger without fake same-currency rhetoric. Only after those gates pass should the branch ask whether full packet completion, mixed-helicity restoration, shell aggregation, and reconstruction leave a quantitatively useful gain.

## Why This Chain Is Meaningfully Different

This remains the exact-symbol and packet-geometry branch in the portfolio, but it is no longer allowed to trade on exactness alone. Its distinctive bet is narrower: a packet-complete NS feature may survive known helical absorption and still yield a theorem-facing gain after explicit conversion and debt charging. If not, the branch should produce a clean obstruction rather than symbolic theater.

## Ordered Steps

### Step 1 - Front-load prior-art absorption and endpoint feasibility

- Depends on: none.
- Action: name one theorem-facing insertion line only.
- Action: name the nearest prior-art family: Waleffe-type triad analysis, helical decimation, same-helicity folklore, or another comparably close helical package.
- Action: write a short non-absorption note stating what exact object, gate, or obstruction is not already handled by that prior art.
- Action: write one early endpoint-feasibility note explaining how a packet quantity could in principle re-enter the named theorem-facing line.
- Expected output: a novelty-and-endpoint memo.
- Kill condition: if the candidate mechanism is already absorbed by known helical analysis or no plausible theorem-facing insertion point can be named before packet work begins, stop with a prior-art or endpoint-feasibility obstruction.

### Step 2 - Freeze one primary host bad term, its coupled debts, and a conversion map

- Depends on: Step 1.
- Action: choose one primary host bad term only.
- Action: list the adjacent coupled debts that could erase any gain on that term, such as pressure restoration, localization loss, transport spillover, or shell-growth debt.
- Action: justify why the primary term is still a fair focal target rather than a convenient ledger entry.
- Action: define the burden currency provisionally and write the explicit conversion map from packet quantities into that host ledger; if a direct same-currency comparison is not yet honest, record the required intermediate conversion stage instead of forcing fake commensurability.
- Action: freeze only a minimal survival floor at this stage, not a fully numeric gain target, until the packet ledger is complete.
- Expected output: a calibrated host-obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if no justified primary host term plus coupled-debt set can be named, or if the conversion map is undefined, stop with a calibration obstruction.

### Step 3 - Write the packet-complete identity sheet and completion criterion

- Depends on: Step 2.
- Action: derive the exact packet identities for the chosen language.
- Action: record conjugate completion, spectator leakage, mixed-helicity interactions, shell aggregation, and all newly introduced cross-terms explicitly.
- Action: state what counts as complete enough to stop: no unnamed interaction classes remain and every term is assigned as favorable, neutral, or debt.
- Action: require either a coercive/sign-definite remainder or an explicit conclusion that the exact ledger is honest but non-improving.
- Expected output: a packet-complete ledger with a completion certificate.
- Kill condition: if the completion keeps moving, if uncontrolled cross-terms erase bookkeeping closure, or if exactness yields no formally improving quantity, stop with a ledger or exact-but-nonimproving obstruction.

### Step 4 - Run mixed-helicity restoration and the quantitative survival gate

- Depends on: Step 3.
- Action: restore full mixed-helicity interactions immediately.
- Action: test whether the surviving effect is one packet mechanism or one tightly coupled bundle of small effects; do not force a single hero mechanism if the ledger says otherwise.
- Action: quantify the survival standard: the effect must persist after shell-count growth and the leading reconstruction and localization losses already visible from the conversion map.
- Action: replace vague Tao-style intuition checks with an explicit averaging-sensitivity comparison on the completed ledger.
- Expected output: a survival memo classifying the candidate as surviving, absorbed, averaging-fragile, or quantitatively too weak.
- Kill condition: if the gain vanishes after restoration, is already absorbed by known averaging or symmetry behavior, or survives only below the stated quantitative floor, stop with a mixed-helicity, absorption, or too-weak obstruction.

### Step 5 - Reconstruct to the theorem-facing line and charge full debt

- Depends on: Step 4.
- Action: insert the surviving packet effect into the frozen theorem-facing line.
- Action: charge shell summation, viscosity, pressure restoration, cutoff and localization loss, commutator debt, temporal integration loss, and norm-conversion loss explicitly.
- Action: compare the net effect against the host burden using the conversion map frozen in Step 2.
- Action: note whether the insertion is compatible with the surrounding argument or is merely an isolated favorable endpoint.
- Expected output: a reconstruction-and-debt memo.
- Kill condition: if reconstruction or conversion consumes the gain, or if the insertion is only cosmetically favorable with no usable surrounding route, stop with a reconstruction-collapse or endpoint-isolation obstruction.

### Step 6 - Demand a hard artifact and enforce a relevance floor

- Depends on: Step 5.
- Action: produce the strongest available artifact: explicit packet inequality preferred, otherwise a counterexample class or a failed lemma isolating the obstruction.
- Action: if positive, write one narrow route naming the packet mechanism or packet bundle, the primary host term, the coupled debts checked, the insertion line, the net quantitative effect, and the first missing lemma.
- Action: if negative, classify failure as calibration failure, prior-art absorption, exact-but-nonimproving ledger, quantitatively too weak, mixed-helicity collapse, reconstruction collapse, or endpoint-isolation.
- Action: enforce a relevance floor: if the claim can be made true only by narrowing below theorem-facing significance, count that as failure rather than success.
- Expected output: a final-comparison-ready route or obstruction memo.
- Kill condition: if no hard artifact exists or the surviving claim falls below the relevance floor, stop with a documentary or irrelevance obstruction.
