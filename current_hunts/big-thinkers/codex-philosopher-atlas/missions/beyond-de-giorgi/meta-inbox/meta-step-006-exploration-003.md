# Meta-Learning: Step 006, Exploration 003

- A good Step-2 Tao screen can succeed by killing a branch cleanly. The right
  synthesis product is a bounded obstruction memo, not a forced survivor list.
- Prior-art calibration was most useful when phrased as:
  "What exact new question did this step test that the earlier De Giorgi,
  pressure, and geometry negatives did not already settle?"
- Once a branch is frozen to one bad term and one gain currency, a final
  continue-or-kill decision can be much sharper than the broad mission-level
  negatives that motivated the branch.
