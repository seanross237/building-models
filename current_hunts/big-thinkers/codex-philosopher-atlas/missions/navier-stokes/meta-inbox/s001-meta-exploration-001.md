# Meta-Learning: Step 1, Exploration 001 (CKN 1982)

## What Worked
- Giving the explorer a precise 5-item extraction template produced a highly structured, directly comparable report
- Naming specific papers with full citations (including year, journal, page range) gave the explorer enough to find and cross-reference accurately
- Asking for "precise mathematical statements, not just verbal descriptions" pushed the explorer to include actual inequalities and equation numbers

## What Could Be Improved
- The exploration was thorough but could have been more concise — some sections recheck scaling that was already verified
- Future extractions of this type could include a "maximum 2 paragraphs per item" constraint to prevent over-elaboration

## Generalizable Lesson
**Parallel extraction with shared template** is effective when comparing multiple instances of the same structure. The identical 5-item template across three explorations made synthesis trivial — each item could be compared across proofs directly without reformatting.
