## 0. The Claim, Precisely Stated

The research program under examination holds that general relativity is not fundamental but emergent — that Einstein's field equations are the thermodynamic/statistical-mechanical equation of state of a deeper, pre-geometric microstructure. Gravity is to this microstructure what pressure and temperature are to molecular motion. The key theoretical results underpinning this program:

| Result | Year | What it showed |
|--------|------|----------------|
| Bekenstein bound | 1973 | Max entropy in a region ~ area, not volume |
| Hawking radiation | 1975 | Black holes radiate thermally; have temperature and entropy |
| Unruh effect | 1976 | Accelerated observers see thermal radiation in vacuum |
| 't Hooft / Susskind holographic principle | 1993-95 | Degrees of freedom of a volume encode on its boundary |
| Jacobson's derivation | 1995 | Einstein's equations follow from dQ = TdS on local causal horizons |
| Ryu-Takayanagi | 2006 | Entanglement entropy = minimal surface area / 4G in AdS/CFT |
| Verlinde's entropic gravity | 2010-11 | Newton's law derived as an entropic force |
| Van Raamsdonk | 2010 | Entanglement builds spacetime connectivity |
| Maldacena-Susskind (ER=EPR) | 2013 | Einstein-Rosen bridges = entangled pairs |
| Verlinde's emergent gravity | 2016 | MOND-like galaxy rotation from emergent gravity without dark matter |
| Padmanabhan's cosmological program | 2002-2017 | Gravity as emergent; derivation of Einstein equations from horizon thermodynamics; cosmic expansion from difference in surface and bulk degrees of freedom |

The question is whether this can move from theoretical derivation to empirical test.

## 1. Experiments Already Proposed or Attempted

### 1.1 Galaxy Rotation Curves (Verlinde 2016)

**What Verlinde predicted:** In his 2016 paper ("Emergent Gravity and the Dark Universe," SciPost Phys. 2, 016), Verlinde argued that in de Sitter space, an emergent gravity framework produces an additional gravitational effect at large distances from a mass — an "apparent dark matter" component. For spherically symmetric, isolated systems, this produces an effective gravitational acceleration that matches the MOND (Modified Newtonian Dynamics) interpolation function at low accelerations, specifically:

g_D(r) = sqrt(g_B(r) * c*H_0 / 6)

where g_D is the apparent dark matter contribution, g_B is the baryonic Newtonian acceleration, H_0 is the Hubble constant, and c is the speed of light.

**Tests performed:**

- **Brouwer et al. (2017)** — "First test of Verlinde's theory of emergent gravity using weak gravitational lensing measurements" (MNRAS 466, 2547). Used 33,613 galaxies from the Galaxy And Mass Assembly survey, measured weak gravitational lensing profiles. **Result: consistent with Verlinde's prediction** for isolated, spherically symmetric galaxies. Also consistent with standard NFW dark matter profiles, so not discriminating.

- **Ettori et al. (2017)** — Tested Verlinde's predictions against X-ray observations of galaxy clusters. **Result: Verlinde's formula underpredicts the observed mass in galaxy clusters by a factor of ~2-3.** This is a significant tension. Verlinde's response is that the formula is strictly valid only for isolated, spherically symmetric systems and galaxy clusters are neither.

- **Hees et al. (2017)** — Tested Verlinde's predictions against observations of the Milky Way's rotation curve and Solar System dynamics. **Result: Solar System constraints are satisfied, but the Milky Way rotation curve fit is poor** in certain regions without additional tuning.

- **Lelli et al. (2017)** — Tested against the radial acceleration relation (RAR) in 153 galaxies from the SPARC database. **Result: Verlinde's prediction is in the right ballpark but does not fit as well as the empirical MOND relation.** The scatter is larger.

- **Tortora et al. (2018)** — Tested with early-type galaxies. **Result: Mixed.** Some tension for massive ellipticals.

**Current status:** The galaxy rotation test is inconclusive. Verlinde's formula works roughly for isolated galaxies but fails for clusters and is not clearly superior to MOND (which itself has problems with clusters). The program needs a more complete framework — not just a formula for the spherically symmetric case — to make predictions for the full range of astrophysical systems.

### 1.2 Neutron Interferometry / Gravity-Quantum Tests

**The idea:** If gravity is entropic (a statistical/thermal effect), it should not be able to create quantum coherence. This was the argument raised by Kobakhidze (2011): "Once more: gravity is not an entropic force." Kobakhidze argued that the COW experiment (Colella, Overhauser, Werner, 1975) — which measured the quantum interference of neutrons in a gravitational field — already disproves entropic gravity. The reasoning: an entropic force is fundamentally statistical/thermal and should decohere quantum superpositions, but the COW experiment shows gravitational phase shifts in a coherent quantum superposition.

**The counterargument (Verlinde, others):** This objection rests on a specific (and arguably narrow) interpretation of "entropic force." Verlinde has argued that his framework is not claiming gravity is like friction (a dissipative entropic force) but rather that it emerges from information-theoretic degrees of freedom that can be fully quantum-coherent. The analogy would be: the elastic restoring force of a polymer is entropic in origin, but polymers can still participate in quantum mechanics. Jacobson's derivation treats the entropy as a state-counting quantity, not as a dissipative/thermal one. The debate is about whether "emergent from information/entropy" necessarily implies "incoherent/thermal/classical."

**Status:** The COW experiment is real data, but whether it refutes entropic gravity depends on what "entropic" means. Most physicists in the emergent gravity program consider this objection based on a misunderstanding of the claim. The objection has not killed the program, but it has forced proponents to be more precise about what they mean.

### 1.3 BMV Experiment (Bose-Marletto-Vedral)

**What it tests:** Whether gravity can mediate quantum entanglement between two masses. Bose et al. (2017) and Marletto & Vedral (2017) independently proposed tabletop experiments where two mesoscopic masses (micro-diamonds, ~10^-14 kg) are put into spatial superpositions and allowed to interact gravitationally. If gravity entangles them, it must be quantum (it must carry quantum information). If it does not entangle them, gravity may be fundamentally classical.

**Relevance to emergent gravity:** This is a critical test. If gravity is emergent/thermodynamic/classical at its core, it should NOT be able to entangle quantum systems. If the BMV experiment detects gravitationally-induced entanglement, that would be strong evidence that gravity has quantum character at the fundamental level — which would be in tension with the "gravity is classical/thermodynamic" reading of the emergent program.

**However:** Proponents like Verlinde and Jacobson can argue that "emergent" does not mean "classical." Spacetime could emerge from quantum entanglement (as in the ER=EPR / Van Raamsdonk program), in which case gravity IS quantum, just not fundamental. The BMV experiment would then be compatible with the emergent program — it would just refute the specific claim that gravity is a classical statistical force.

**Status as of 2026:** Multiple groups worldwide (Bouwmeester at UCSB/Leiden, the groups of Bose, Marletto, Vedral, and others) are working toward this experiment. It has not yet been performed. The technical challenges are severe: you need to maintain quantum coherence of ~10^-14 kg masses for long enough to accumulate a gravitational phase, while shielding from all other interactions (electromagnetic, Casimir, etc.). Estimates suggest it requires mass superposition separations of ~100 micrometers and coherence times of ~1 second for masses of ~10^-14 kg. The most optimistic timelines put this at the late 2020s or 2030s.

### 1.4 Analog Gravity Experiments

**What they are:** Laboratory systems (Bose-Einstein condensates, flowing fluids, optical systems) that reproduce the kinematics of curved spacetime. Notably, Steinhauer (2016) reported observation of stimulated Hawking radiation analogs in a BEC acoustic black hole. Muñoz de Nova et al. (2019) reported observation of the thermal spectrum.

**Relevance:** These experiments confirm that the thermodynamic aspects of horizons (Hawking radiation, Unruh-like effects) are generic features of horizon physics, not specific to gravity. This supports the idea that the thermodynamic character of black holes is about the information structure of horizons, not about the specific dynamics of general relativity. It is consistent with (but does not prove) the emergent gravity program.

**Status:** Analog Hawking radiation has been observed. This supports the thermodynamic-horizon connection but does not test whether gravity itself is emergent.

### 1.5 Gravitational Decoherence Tests

**The idea:** If gravity is fundamentally different from quantum mechanics (classical, or emergent in a way that introduces decoherence), then there should be a gravitational decoherence channel that collapses quantum superpositions. The Diosi-Penrose model quantifies this. Experiments testing matter-wave interferometry with increasingly massive particles (Arndt group, OTIMA, MAQRO satellite proposal) probe whether gravitational self-energy causes decoherence at some mass scale.

**Relevance:** If gravitational decoherence is found at the predicted Diosi-Penrose scale, it would suggest gravity is fundamentally classical — compatible with a thermodynamic/emergent interpretation. If decoherence is NOT found even at high masses, gravity behaves quantum-mechanically, which is compatible with the ER=EPR version of emergent gravity but against the classical-thermodynamic version.

**Status:** Current interferometry reaches masses of ~10^4 AMU (Fein et al., 2019, molecule interferometry). Diosi-Penrose predictions require testing at ~10^9 AMU or above. There is a large gap. The MAQRO space mission proposal could probe this regime but has not been funded.

## 2. Experiments That COULD Distinguish Emergent from Fundamental Gravity

### 2.1 Precision Tests of the Equivalence Principle at Low Accelerations

**The key regime:** The emergent gravity program predicts deviations from standard gravity at very low accelerations, specifically around a_0 ~ cH_0 ~ 10^-10 m/s^2 (the MOND acceleration scale, which Verlinde connects to the de Sitter horizon). Standard GR predicts no special behavior at this scale.

**What to measure:** The gravitational acceleration of test masses in the regime a << a_0. This is technically very difficult on Earth (surface gravity is ~10^10 times a_0) but accessible in:

- **Wide binary star systems:** Stars orbiting each other at separations large enough that their mutual gravitational acceleration is near or below a_0. The relative motion of wide binaries tests gravity in the low-acceleration regime. Pittordis & Sutherland (2023) and Chae (2023, 2024) have published conflicting analyses of Gaia wide binary data — Chae claimed a MOND-like signal, while Banik et al. (2024) and Pittordis & Sutherland found results consistent with Newtonian gravity. This is an active, heated observational debate.

- **Outer Solar System:** The Pioneer anomaly was once interpreted as evidence for modified gravity at low accelerations, but it was resolved as due to anisotropic thermal radiation pressure (Turyshev et al., 2012). Future deep-space missions with extremely precise accelerometers could revisit this. The acceleration of spacecraft at ~50 AU from the Sun is ~10^-7 m/s^2 — still well above a_0, but precision measurements could constrain any transition regime.

- **Galaxy-scale tests:** The radial acceleration relation (RAR) discovered by McGaugh, Lelli, & Schombert (2016) — the tight empirical correlation between observed acceleration and baryonic acceleration in galaxies — is naturally explained by MOND/Verlinde-type modifications and somewhat awkward for dark matter. Ongoing surveys (DESI, Euclid, Rubin/LSST) will provide dramatically better galaxy kinematic data through the late 2020s.

**What different results would mean:**
- **If gravity deviates from Newtonian at a ~ a_0 in a way predicted by Verlinde:** Strong evidence for emergent gravity.
- **If gravity remains Newtonian down to arbitrarily low accelerations:** Does not necessarily kill emergent gravity (the predictions could be wrong while the framework is right), but eliminates the most specific existing prediction.

### 2.2 Gravitational Wave Tests

**What to look for:**

- **Speed of gravitational waves:** GR predicts gravitational waves travel at c. GW170817 (neutron star merger + gamma-ray burst) confirmed c_gw = c to within ~10^-15. This is a severe constraint on any modified gravity theory. Verlinde's framework has not made specific predictions about gravitational wave speed, but any entropic gravity theory that modifies the dispersion relation is already ruled out.

- **Gravitational wave echoes:** If spacetime has a granular/emergent microstructure near horizons, the sharp classical event horizon of GR might be replaced by a "fuzzy" quantum structure (firewall, fuzzball, etc.). This would produce gravitational wave echoes — repeated pulses after the main ringdown of a black hole merger. Abedi, Dykaar, & Afshordi (2017) claimed tentative detection of echoes in LIGO data; subsequent analyses (Westerweck et al., 2018; LIGO/Virgo, 2020) found no statistically significant signal. Next-generation detectors (LISA, Einstein Telescope, Cosmic Explorer) will have much better sensitivity.

- **Modified quasinormal mode spectrum:** If the near-horizon structure is different from GR (as an emergent gravity theory might predict), the quasinormal mode frequencies of merging black holes would deviate from GR predictions. Current LIGO observations are consistent with GR but have large error bars on higher overtones. Future detectors will constrain this much more tightly.

**What different results would mean:**
- **Gravitational wave echoes detected:** Strong evidence for non-classical horizon structure, compatible with emergent gravity.
- **No echoes even with next-gen detectors:** Consistent with classical GR horizons; does not disprove emergent gravity but removes one potential signature.
- **Quasinormal mode deviations from GR:** Would require new physics; could point to emergent structure.

### 2.3 Cosmological Tests

**Dark energy as emergent:** Verlinde's 2016 framework ties the MOND-like modification to the de Sitter (dark energy) component of the universe. He argues that dark energy's entropy — stored on the cosmological horizon — permeates the bulk and modifies gravity at large scales. This predicts:

- **A specific relationship between the dark energy equation of state and the apparent dark matter distribution.** In standard cosmology, dark energy (w ~ -1) and dark matter are independent components. In Verlinde's framework, they are both aspects of emergent gravity, and you cannot independently vary them. Future precise measurements of the dark energy equation of state (from DESI, Euclid, Rubin) combined with dark matter distribution (from weak lensing) could test whether the two are correlated in the way Verlinde predicts.

- **No particle dark matter.** If Verlinde is right, direct dark matter detection experiments (XENONnT, LZ, PandaX, DARWIN) should see nothing. They currently see nothing, which is consistent with Verlinde but also consistent with dark matter particles that are heavier or more weakly interacting than current experiments can probe.

**CMB predictions:** The cosmic microwave background power spectrum is the strongest constraint on any modification to gravity. Standard Lambda-CDM (with cold dark matter particles) fits the CMB power spectrum to extraordinary precision. Any emergent gravity theory must reproduce this. Verlinde's framework has NOT been developed to the point where it can predict the CMB power spectrum. This is a critical gap. Angus (2009) and Skordis & Zlosnik (2021) have shown that MOND-type theories CAN fit the CMB if supplemented with additional fields (e.g., the AeST theory of Skordis & Zlosnik), but Verlinde's framework has not been similarly developed.

**What different results would mean:**
- **Direct dark matter detection succeeds (clear particle signal):** This does NOT kill emergent gravity per se — even if dark matter particles exist, gravity could still be emergent — but it would eliminate the specific Verlinde prediction that apparent dark matter effects are entirely due to emergent gravity modifications.
- **No dark matter particles found AND Verlinde-type predictions match galaxy/cluster data:** Strong circumstantial evidence for emergent gravity.
- **Verlinde's framework can reproduce the CMB power spectrum:** This would be a major advance. Currently it cannot, which is a serious weakness.

### 2.4 Quantum Gravity Phenomenology

**Spacetime discreteness:** If spacetime is emergent from a pre-geometric microstructure, there may be a smallest meaningful length scale (presumably near the Planck length, ~10^-35 m). This could produce:

- **Modified dispersion relations for light:** High-energy photons would travel slightly slower or faster than low-energy photons. Fermi Gamma-Ray Space Telescope observations of gamma-ray bursts (Abdo et al., 2009) have constrained any Lorentz invariance violation at the Planck scale, with limits exceeding the Planck energy for linear modifications. This severely constrains naive models of spacetime discreteness.

- **Fuzzy spacetime signatures in the CMB:** Some quantum gravity models predict modifications to the primordial power spectrum. Current Planck satellite data constrains these but has not detected any.

- **Holographic noise:** Hogan (2009) proposed that the holographic principle implies that spacetime positions have a minimum uncertainty at the scale sqrt(L_P * L), where L_P is the Planck length and L is the size of the apparatus. His Holometer experiment at Fermilab (Chou et al., 2017) searched for this holographic noise. **Result: no signal detected,** ruling out Hogan's specific model. This does NOT rule out holographic gravity in general, as other formulations of the holographic principle do not predict this specific noise signature.

**What different results would mean:**
- **Detection of Planck-scale Lorentz violation:** Strong evidence for discrete spacetime; compatible with emergent gravity.
- **Continued null results at Planck scale:** Consistent with continuous spacetime but does not disprove emergence (the microstructure could be Lorentz-invariant).

### 2.5 Black Hole Information Tests

**The information paradox:** If gravity is emergent from information/thermodynamics, then the black hole information paradox should have a natural resolution: information is never truly lost because the gravitational/geometric description is approximate, and the underlying information-theoretic description is unitary. Recent theoretical progress (the "island formula" — Penington 2019, Almheiri et al. 2019) computes the Page curve from gravitational path integrals and shows unitarity-compatible behavior. This does not yet constitute an experimental test, but:

- **Hawking radiation spectrum:** If information is preserved, the Hawking radiation spectrum should have subtle correlations (not be exactly thermal). These correlations are far beyond any foreseeable measurement capability for astrophysical black holes.

- **Primordial black hole evaporation:** If primordial black holes exist at the right mass scale, their final evaporation stages could be observable. The final burst should encode information about the black hole's formation history if unitarity is preserved. This is speculative and contingent on primordial black holes existing.

This is the critical section. If emergent gravity reproduces GR exactly in all regimes, it is not testable — it is a reinterpretation, not a new theory.

### 3.2 Potential Differences (Predictions Not Yet Made)

These are regimes where the emergent gravity program SHOULD make distinct predictions but has not yet been developed enough to do so:

- **CMB power spectrum:** Lambda-CDM with particle dark matter fits the acoustic peaks to sub-percent precision. Can emergent gravity reproduce this? Unknown — the calculation has not been done.
- **Large-scale structure formation:** Dark matter provides the gravitational scaffolding for structure formation. Can emergent gravity reproduce the observed galaxy distribution, the BAO signal, and the matter power spectrum? Unknown.
- **Gravitational wave waveforms from mergers:** GR predicts these precisely. Does emergent gravity predict any deviations? Unknown.
- **Strong-field gravity (neutron stars, pulsars):** Binary pulsar timing (Hulse-Taylor, double pulsar J0737-3039) confirms GR at the ~0.1% level. Must be reproduced.

The fact that many of these calculations have not been done is itself a significant critique of the program.

## 5. What Would Count as Proof?

**There is no single observation that would conclusively prove gravity is informational rather than fundamental.** This is because the emergent gravity program is a *framework* or *interpretation* of gravity, not (yet) a unique theory with unique predictions. Here is why:

### 5.1 The Underdetermination Problem

If emergent gravity reproduces all of GR's predictions (as Jacobson's derivation suggests it should at the classical level), then at the classical level, it is observationally equivalent to GR. It is a different explanation of the same equations. You cannot experimentally distinguish "the Einstein equations are fundamental" from "the Einstein equations are the thermodynamic limit of something deeper" if both yield the same equations.

### 5.2 What Would Come Closest to Proof

A combination of the following would make the emergent gravity framework far more compelling than fundamental gravity:

1. **Verlinde-type predictions for galaxy dynamics are confirmed without dark matter.** If the rotation curves, cluster masses, CMB, and large-scale structure can all be reproduced from baryonic physics + emergent gravity corrections with fewer free parameters than Lambda-CDM, this would be very strong evidence. Currently nowhere close to this standard.

2. **The BMV experiment shows gravity cannot entangle quantum systems.** This would indicate gravity is fundamentally classical, which is most naturally explained by emergence from a pre-quantum substrate.

3. **Gravitational wave echoes are detected.** This would show the classical event horizon is not exact, suggesting a quantum/informational microstructure.

4. **A UV-complete theory of emergent gravity is constructed** that makes novel, confirmed predictions beyond GR (analogous to how QCD makes predictions beyond the quark model).

5. **A derivation of the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy from a specific microscopic theory** in which spacetime geometry is derived (not assumed). String theory's microstate counting (Strominger-Vafa, 1996) does this for certain extremal black holes within string theory, which itself may or may not describe emergent spacetime.

Any single one of these would be significant but not conclusive. The combination of all five would amount to a paradigm shift.

### 5.3 The Realistic Standard

In practice, emergent gravity would be "proved" the same way any framework in physics is adopted: it would produce a theory that is more predictive, more explanatory, and more parsimonious than the current framework. If someone derives the full content of GR + quantum field theory from an information-theoretic starting point, with fewer assumptions and novel correct predictions, the community would adopt it — not because of a single crucial experiment but because of accumulated theoretical and empirical superiority.

## 6. What Would Count as Disproof?

### 6.1 Things That Would Kill Specific Versions

- **Discovery of the graviton.** Detection of individual gravitons (spin-2 massless particles) with the properties predicted by linearized quantum GR would strongly suggest gravity is quantum and fundamental, not classical and emergent. However: the energy required to detect a single graviton is absurdly beyond current technology (Dyson, 2013, has argued it may be impossible even in principle). And if "emergent" allows "emergent but quantum" (as in ER=EPR), even graviton detection would not kill the program.

- **Discovery of dark matter particles.** If a particle with the right mass and cross-section is detected at XENONnT, LZ, or a collider, and its properties match the dark matter distribution in galaxies and clusters, this eliminates Verlinde's specific claim that apparent dark matter is entirely due to emergent gravity modifications. But it does not eliminate Jacobson's or Padmanabhan's versions, which do not make claims about dark matter.

- **BMV experiment shows gravity DOES entangle quantum systems.** This would strongly constrain "gravity is classical/thermodynamic" versions of emergent gravity. It would not kill "gravity is emergent from quantum entanglement" versions (ER=EPR, Van Raamsdonk).

### 6.2 Things That Would Kill the Entire Program

It is remarkably hard to kill the entire emergent gravity research program because it is not a single falsifiable theory — it is a family of approaches. However:

- **Proof that the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy formula is wrong.** If black holes are shown NOT to have entropy proportional to horizon area, the entire thermodynamic-gravitational connection collapses. This is extremely unlikely given the multiple independent derivations (Hawking's original calculation, string microstate counting, the Ryu-Takayanagi formula, etc.).

- **Proof that Jacobson's derivation is mathematically incorrect.** If a flaw is found in the logic connecting dQ = TdS on local horizons to Einstein's equations, the strongest pillar of the program falls. The derivation has been scrutinized for 30 years and no such flaw has been found. Padmanabhan, Paranjape, and others have extended it in multiple ways.

- **Discovery that gravity violates the holographic bound.** If a physical configuration is found where the entropy exceeds A/4 without forming a black hole, the Bekenstein bound fails and the area-entropy-gravity triangle breaks.

None of these are likely. The honest assessment is: **the emergent gravity program is probably not falsifiable as a whole, only in specific implementations.** This is similar to the status of string theory — you can falsify specific string models but not the general idea that fundamental physics involves extended objects.

## 7. Strongest Theoretical Objections

### 7.1 "It's Not a Theory, It's a Derivation Trick" — The Jacobson Objection Problem

**Who says it:** Various critics, including some who admire Jacobson's result.

**The argument:** Jacobson derived Einstein's equations from thermodynamic assumptions. But this is a *derivation*, not a *theory*. It tells you that IF the entropy-area relation and the Clausius relation hold for all causal horizons, THEN Einstein's equations follow. It does not tell you WHY the entropy-area relation holds, what the microstates are, or what happens when the thermodynamic approximation breaks down. It is like deriving the ideal gas law from the assumption that pressure is proportional to temperature at constant volume — correct, but not a fundamental theory of gases. You still need statistical mechanics (the microstate theory) to have actual predictive power beyond the thermodynamic regime. Jacobson himself has been careful about this distinction.

**Force of the objection:** Significant. The emergent gravity program has the macroscopic equation of state but not the statistical mechanics. Without the microstate theory, it cannot make predictions in regimes where the thermodynamic approximation breaks down (Planck scale, singularities, early universe).

### 7.2 "Entropic Gravity Is Already Falsified by Quantum Coherence" — The Kobakhidze Objection

**Who says it:** Archil Kobakhidze (2011); echoed by others.

**The argument:** An entropic force is a macroscopic, statistical force. It should not be coherent at the quantum level. But gravity is coherent at the quantum level (COW experiment, atom interferometry in gravitational fields). Therefore gravity is not an entropic force.

**Counterargument (Verlinde, others):** This conflates "entropic" with "thermal/dissipative." A polymer's restoring force is entropic (it comes from the statistics of chain configurations) but the polymer can still be in a quantum state. The emergence claimed is statistical, not dissipative. The entropy in Jacobson's derivation is a state-counting quantity (like the logarithm of the number of microstates), not a thermal quantity (like heat divided by temperature in a dissipative process).

**Force of the objection:** Moderate. It has not killed the program, but it highlights a real ambiguity in what "entropic" means. Proponents need to be more precise.

### 7.3 "Where Are the Microstates?" — The Completeness Objection

**Who says it:** Almost everyone who is sympathetic to the general idea but critical of the current state.

**The argument:** If gravity is emergent from a microstructure, what IS that microstructure? What are the fundamental degrees of freedom? In the analogy to thermodynamics, we know the microstates are molecular positions and velocities. For emergent gravity, the microstates are unknown. In AdS/CFT, the microstates are the degrees of freedom of the boundary conformal field theory, but (a) AdS/CFT describes anti-de Sitter space, not the de Sitter space we live in, and (b) it is a specific framework (string theory), not the general information-theoretic picture the emergent gravity program claims.

Without specifying the microstates, the program cannot:
- Make predictions beyond what GR already predicts
- Calculate corrections to GR in specific regimes
- Address the singularity problem
- Be falsified

**Force of the objection:** Very strong. This is the most serious weakness of the entire program.

### 7.4 "It Doesn't Work in de Sitter Space" — The Cosmological Objection

**Who says it:** Multiple critics of Verlinde's 2016 paper.

**The argument:** The holographic principle is well-established in AdS (anti-de Sitter) space through AdS/CFT. Our universe has a positive cosmological constant, making it approximately de Sitter. There is NO well-established holographic duality for de Sitter space. Verlinde's 2016 paper ASSUMES a de Sitter holographic framework to derive his predictions, but this framework does not exist in any rigorous form. He is building on foundations that have not been laid.

**Force of the objection:** Strong. The AdS/CFT ≠ dS reality gap is a fundamental problem for any holographic approach to real-world gravity.

### 7.5 "It Fails for Galaxy Clusters and the Bullet Cluster" — The Empirical Objection

**Who says it:** Ettori et al. (2017), and others.

**The argument:** Verlinde's emergent gravity formula underpredicts the mass in galaxy clusters. The Bullet Cluster shows a spatial separation between the mass (as inferred from lensing) and the baryonic matter (gas), which is naturally explained by collisionless dark matter particles but very difficult to reproduce with any modification of gravity.

**Force of the objection:** Moderate to strong against Verlinde's specific framework. Does not bear on Jacobson or Padmanabhan, who do not make claims about dark matter.

### 7.6 "Gravity Is Already Quantum — We Just Haven't Detected Gravitons Yet" — The Conservatism Objection

**Who says it:** Most mainstream quantum gravity researchers (string theorists, loop quantum gravity proponents).

**The argument:** The default assumption should be that gravity, like all other forces, is quantum at the fundamental level. The success of GR as a low-energy effective field theory of a spin-2 field is well-established. Quantizing this gives a perfectly sensible effective theory valid up to the Planck scale. There is no empirical reason to abandon this and declare gravity emergent. Extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence, and the emergent gravity program has provided none.

**Force of the objection:** The weight of the physics establishment is behind this view. It is not a specific technical objection but a statement about burden of proof. The emergent gravity proponents would counter that Jacobson's derivation IS the extraordinary evidence — deriving Einstein's equations from entropy is extraordinary, and the question is whether you take this as a hint or a coincidence.

### 7.7 "Gravitational Waves Already Confirm Fundamental GR" — The LIGO Objection

**Who says it:** Various, post-2015.

**The argument:** LIGO's detections of gravitational waves match GR's predictions for inspiral, merger, and ringdown of black holes to remarkable precision. This includes strong-field, highly dynamical gravity — exactly the regime where deviations from "fundamental" GR would be most visible. If gravity were emergent/thermodynamic, you'd expect deviations in the strong-field regime (just as thermodynamics breaks down in extreme conditions). The fact that GR works perfectly in strong-field dynamics suggests it is fundamental, not emergent.

**Counterargument:** Thermodynamic equations of state also work in extreme conditions — the ideal gas law breaks down, but the actual equation of state (including corrections) can be precise. The claim is that GR is the exact equation of state, not an approximate one. Jacobson's derivation yields the EXACT Einstein equations, not an approximation. So gravitational waves matching GR exactly is what the emergent program predicts.

**Force of the objection:** Moderate. The counterargument is technically valid, but the objection highlights that emergent gravity has not yet identified ANY regime where it predicts deviations from GR, which limits its testability.

## 8. Overall Assessment

### What the physics supports:
The connection between gravity, thermodynamics, and information is real, deep, and well-established. Jacobson's derivation, the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy, the holographic principle, and AdS/CFT all point to a profound relationship. This is not speculative — it is mainstream theoretical physics.

### What remains unresolved:
Whether this relationship means gravity IS emergent (ontological claim) or merely that gravity CAN BE DESCRIBED in thermodynamic/information-theoretic language (epistemological claim). The physics does not yet distinguish these. It may never distinguish them, in which case the question is metaphysical rather than physical.

### The bottleneck:
The program needs a specific, complete theory — not a framework, not a derivation, not a collection of suggestive results, but a theory with specified microstates, a dynamics, and calculable predictions beyond GR. Without this, it cannot be tested and cannot be falsified. The most promising direction is probably the entanglement-builds-spacetime program (Van Raamsdonk, Maldacena-Susskind, Swingle, etc.), which has the mathematical backing of AdS/CFT but has not yet been extended to de Sitter space or to predictions that differ from GR.

### Verdict on testability:
**Partially testable now, more testable in the next decade, but probably never fully provable or disprovable as a framework.** The specific predictions (Verlinde's galaxy rotation curves, dark matter absence, gravitational wave echoes, BMV entanglement) are testable. The general framework (gravity is emergent from information) is probably not falsifiable in isolation, only through the success or failure of specific implementations.
