## REVISED FRAMEWORK: From Temporal Identity (TII) to Structural Identity (SII)

### What Changed and Why

The adversary and judge exposed that v1's core concept -- "sharing a moment of time" -- fails three tests: it is not Lorentz-invariant as phrased, it becomes circular when applied to entanglement swapping, and when formalized to survive both objections it loses its specifically temporal content.

I accept these rulings. The honest response is not to defend the temporal language but to follow the formal pressure to its conclusion. What survives the adversary's attacks is this: **entangled particles are aspects of a single structure in the 4D spacetime block, and their correlations are features of that structure rather than consequences of information transfer between separate entities.** This is what the temporal identity idea becomes when you make it Lorentz-invariant and apply it to entanglement swapping.

I rename the framework from Temporal Identity Interpretation (TII) to **Structural Identity Interpretation (SII)** to reflect this. The original hypothesis's language of "sharing a moment of time" is preserved as a special case (common-origin entanglement in the center-of-mass frame) but is no longer the general claim.

This is a significant retreat from the original hypothesis. I flag this honestly: the poetic core of the hypothesis -- that entanglement is specifically about *time* -- does not survive rigorous scrutiny. What survives is a claim about *structure*, which is less distinctive but more defensible.

---

### Revised Definitions

**D1.** *Structural identity* -- Two particles possess structural identity if their joint quantum state is a single, non-decomposable element of the global structure of the spacetime block. In the block universe, the entangled state is not "carried" by the particles from their common past into the future; it simply *is* a 4D structural feature connecting the preparation event to the measurement events, with the particles' worldlines as its spatial projections.

**D2.** *Phase ancestry* (Lorentz-invariant replacement for "sharing a moment") -- Two particles share phase ancestry if their quantum phase relationship is entirely determined by a single event (or chain of events) in their shared causal/acausal past, and this phase relationship is preserved as a Lorentz-invariant scalar: the inner product <psi_AB(tau_A, tau_B) | psi_AB(tau_A', tau_B')> is independent of the frame used to parameterize the worldlines.

This is Lorentz-invariant because it refers to proper times along worldlines and inner products of states, both of which are frame-independent. It replaces the notion of "same time" with "same quantum-phase origin," which is physically meaningful and does not require a preferred simultaneity surface.

**D3.** *Structural node* -- The event (or set of events) that determines the structural identity of an entangled pair. For common-origin entanglement, the structural node is the preparation event. For entanglement swapping, the structural node is the Bell-state measurement. For delayed-choice entanglement swapping, the structural node exists in the 4D block regardless of its temporal position relative to the particle detections.

### Revised Premises

*P1-P7 unchanged from v1.*

**P8.** (NEW -- from literature engagement) *Quantum holism is established.* Entangled systems have properties not reducible to properties of their parts (Teller 1986, Howard 1985, Healey 1991, Esfeld 2001). SII builds on this foundation; it does not claim to have discovered holism.

**P9.** (NEW) *Event ontology.* Following Whitehead (1929) and Dieks (2009), quantum states can be interpreted as *events* (occurrences with spatiotemporal extent) rather than as *properties of objects* (attributes attached to particles). SII adopts an event-ontological reading of the quantum state.

### Revised Argument

#### Step 1: The wrong question (revised)

**S1-S2.** *Unchanged from v1.* The question "how does information travel between entangled particles?" presupposes two independent entities.

**S3.** (REVISED) CLAIM: SII proposes that entangled particles are two spatial projections of a single structural feature of the 4D spacetime block.

> **Relation to existing literature (SHOULD FIX -- holism):** This claim extends quantum holism (Teller, Howard, Healey, Esfeld) in a specific direction. Standard holism says: "the entangled pair has properties not reducible to properties of its parts." SII says something more: "the entangled pair is not two parts with an irreducible relation; it is one *event* that has spatial extent." The distinction: holism is compatible with there being two particles that happen to have a non-separable relation. SII denies that the two-particle ontology is fundamental. There is one event; the apparent two-particle structure is how the event projects onto spatially separated measurement devices.
>
> This is closest to Dieks' (2009) event ontology and to Wharton's (2013) spacetime-based approach. SII's specific contribution is the explicit combination of event ontology with the block-universe structural picture applied to all forms of entanglement, including entanglement swapping.

#### Step 2: Structural identity replaces temporal identity

**S4.** (REVISED -- MUST FIX #2, Lorentz invariance) The core claim is no longer "sharing a moment of time" but "sharing phase ancestry" (D2). Two particles are structurally identical (entangled) when their joint quantum state is entirely determined by a single structural node in the 4D block.

> **What is gained:** Phase ancestry is Lorentz-invariant. It does not require a preferred simultaneity surface. It does not privilege the preparation event's time coordinate; it refers to the quantum-phase relationship as a geometric invariant of the worldlines.
>
> **What is lost:** The intuitive temporal framing of the original hypothesis. "They share a structural node in the 4D spacetime block" is correct but lacks the immediate clarity of "they share a single moment of time." I accept this trade-off; precision matters more than poetry.

#### Step 3: Entanglement swapping in SII (revised -- MUST FIX #1)

**S5.** (REVISED -- replacing S18-S22) In entanglement swapping:

1. Source A produces pair (1, 2). Their structural identity is determined by node E_A.
2. Source B produces pair (3, 4). Their structural identity is determined by node E_B.
3. A BSM on (2, 3) is performed at event E'.
4. After E', particles 1 and 4 are entangled.

In SII: The BSM at E' is a *topological operation* on the structure of the 4D block. Before E', the block contains two separate structural features: the (1,2) feature anchored at E_A and the (3,4) feature anchored at E_B. After E', the block contains a different topology: a single (1,4) structural feature with E' as its node.

> **Why this is not circular (responding to Attack 1a):** The structural node E' is independently identified as the BSM event -- it is a specific physical event that can be located in spacetime, characterized by its Hamiltonian, and described without reference to entanglement. The structural identity of (1,4) is determined by E' in the same way that the structural identity of a common-origin pair (1,2) is determined by its creation event: a concrete physical event establishes the non-factorizability of the joint state. The circularity charge was that "sharing a moment = being entangled = sharing a moment." The revised version breaks this: "sharing a structural node = joint state determined by a specific, independently identifiable physical event." The node is identified by its physical character (BSM), not by the entanglement it produces.
>
> **Delayed-choice entanglement swapping (responding to Attack 1c):** In the block universe, the BSM at E' exists in the 4D block regardless of whether it occurs "before" or "after" the detections of 1 and 4. The structural topology of the block is a global, tenseless feature. Saying the (1,4) structural identity is "determined by" E' is a statement about the block's topology, not about a causal process occurring in time. There is no retrocausation because there is no causation in the relevant sense; there is only the global structure of the block. This is exactly the Wharton/Adlam/Price "all-at-once" view, and SII explicitly adopts it.
>
> **Admitted cost:** SII now depends on the block-universe ontology (P7). If one rejects eternalism in favor of presentism or the growing-block model, SII's treatment of entanglement swapping (especially delayed-choice) collapses. This is a genuine commitment with consequences, not a free move.

#### Step 4: Compatibility with ER=EPR (revised -- SHOULD FIX #2)

**S6.** (REVISED) SII is not a rival to ER=EPR; it is the *interpretive thesis* that ER=EPR's formal program implements.

> ER=EPR says: entangled particles are connected by non-traversable wormholes.
> SII says: entangled particles are aspects of a single structural feature of the spacetime block.
>
> The wormhole IS the structural feature. ER=EPR provides the geometry; SII provides the ontological reading of that geometry. In ER=EPR, one might ask: "what does the wormhole *mean*? Why do entangled particles have this geometric connection?" SII's answer: because they are not two particles connected by a bridge; they are one event (structural feature) that has two spatial endpoints. The wormhole is the geometry of the event's spatial extent.
>
> This is an honest positioning. SII does not have the mathematical machinery of ER=EPR. What it has is a philosophical thesis about what the mathematics describes. Whether this thesis is useful depends on whether it generates insights or research directions that the mathematics alone does not suggest.

#### Step 5: Monogamy (revised -- MUST FIX #3)

**S7.** (REVISED) I withdraw the v1 claim that monogamy is "temporal uniqueness." Instead:

In SII, entanglement is structural identity: the degree to which two particles share a structural feature. Entanglement entropy S_E = -Tr(rho_A log rho_A) measures the degree of structural identity. When S_E is maximal (maximally entangled), particle A's entire quantum identity is shared with B; there is no remaining identity to share with C. When S_E is submaximal, A retains some independent quantum identity that can participate in structural features with other particles.

This is not a derivation of monogamy; it is a *mapping* between the monogamy constraint and SII's vocabulary. Monogamy remains a theorem of QM; SII provides an ontological picture of *why* the theorem is true (structural identity is a finite resource, measured by entanglement entropy, that must be distributed among structural features). Whether this picture adds understanding beyond the theorem itself is a question I cannot answer within the framework; it is a question for the reader.

> **Partial entanglement (responding to Attack 6):** In SII, partial entanglement = partial structural identity. Particle A's quantum state is a superposition of "part of the (A,B) structure" and "independent." The degree of structural identity is S_E(A:B) / S_E^max. This is quantitative, not binary, and handles partial entanglement and entanglement sharing without the problems of the v1 "event is one event" language.
>
> **Admitted weakness:** This mapping is post hoc. I observe monogamy in QM and then describe it in SII language. SII does not predict monogamy; it interprets it. This is the same status as many-worlds' interpretation of the measurement problem: it does not solve the problem; it provides an ontological story about what is happening.

### Revised Conclusions

**C1.** (REVISED) Entangled particles are best understood as spatial projections of a single structural feature of the 4D spacetime block, not as two separate entities connected by an information channel.

**C2.** (REVISED) The structural feature is identified by its structural node: the physical event that determines the joint quantum state. For common-origin entanglement, this is the preparation event. For entanglement swapping, this is the BSM event. In all cases, the structural node is a concrete, independently identifiable physical occurrence.

**C3.** (REVISED) SII is compatible with no-signaling (structural features cannot transmit directed signals), Bell's theorem (structural identity is non-local in the QM sense), ER=EPR (wormholes are the geometric realization of structural features), and the block universe (structural features are tenseless elements of the 4D block).

**C4.** (NEW) SII is an interpretation, not a theory. It makes no predictions that differ from standard QM. Its value, if any, is in dissolving the pseudo-problem of "how does information travel between entangled particles?" and in providing an ontological framework that motivates formal programs like ER=EPR.

**C5.** (HONEST ADMISSION) The original hypothesis -- "entanglement is two particles sharing a single moment of time" -- does not survive adversarial scrutiny in its original form. The temporal framing is not Lorentz-invariant as stated, and it fails for entanglement swapping. What survives is the structural claim: entangled particles are one thing, not two things connected by a channel. The structural claim is defensible; the temporal claim is not, or at least not without significant qualification that removes its distinctiveness.

### What SII Adds to the Existing Literature (Honest Assessment)

Given the adversary's correct identification that the core ideas exist in Teller, Howard, Healey, Esfeld, Wharton, Adlam, and Maldacena/Susskind, what does SII contribute?

**1. Synthesis.** SII explicitly combines event ontology (Whitehead/Dieks), quantum holism (Teller/Healey), block-universe constraints (Wharton/Adlam), and ER=EPR into a single narrative. This synthesis may be useful even if its components are not novel.

**2. The structural-node concept.** Identifying entanglement with a specific structural node in the 4D block -- and showing that this concept extends to entanglement swapping through the BSM event -- may be a useful addition to the block-universe interpretive toolkit. Wharton's "spacetime-based account" works with Lagrangian boundary conditions; SII works with topological nodes. These may be equivalent in substance but different in emphasis.

**3. Phase ancestry as a Lorentz-invariant reformulation.** The concept of phase ancestry (D2) -- entangled particles share a quantum-phase origin that is preserved as a Lorentz-invariant scalar -- may be a useful precision tool for discussions of entanglement in relativistic settings. I have not seen this specific formulation in the literature, though the underlying physics (Lorentz invariance of the entangled state) is standard.

**What SII does NOT add:**
- New predictions.
- New mathematical formalism.
- Resolution of the measurement problem.
- Any experimental distinguishability from standard QM.

### The Fate of "Time" in SII

The original hypothesis was specifically about time: "Space separates them; time doesn't." After revision, SII is about structure: "Space separates them; but the structural topology of the 4D block connects them."

Is there any surviving sense in which entanglement is specifically *temporal*?

**One possible rescue:** In the block universe, structure IS spatiotemporal. A structural feature of the 4D block is not purely spatial (that would be a 3D object) and not purely temporal (that would be a worldline). It is a 4D object. Entanglement, as a structural feature, is irreducibly spatiotemporal. The original hypothesis was wrong to say "time, not space" -- the correct version is "the 4D structure, not a 3D causal process."

But there is one specifically temporal element that survives: the structural node is typically earlier (in most frames) than the measurement events. The topology emanates from a past event. The "temporal" character of entanglement is that its structural origin is in the past (or in the tenseless block, at a node that most worldlines reach before they reach the measurements). This is a vestige of the original "sharing a moment of time" idea, now reduced to "sharing a structural ancestor in the 4D block."

Whether this vestige is strong enough to justify the original hypothesis's temporal emphasis is, in my honest assessment, doubtful. The temporal character is real but not distinctive: all causal processes have structural origins in the past. Entanglement is not unique in this regard.

### Where This Leaves the Hypothesis

The original hypothesis contained a genuine insight: the correlations of entanglement are not produced by information transfer, and asking "how does the information get there?" is the wrong question. This insight is correct and important.

The original hypothesis also contained a specific proposal: the answer is temporal identity, the particles share a moment of time. This specific proposal does not survive. It is replaced by a structural identity claim that is less distinctive but more defensible.

The adversarial process did what it was supposed to do: it separated the genuine insight from the overreach, and forced the framework to find its natural level of precision. SII is that natural level. It is a defensible interpretation of entanglement, positioned within a well-developed literature (holism, block universe, ER=EPR), that offers a specific ontological picture (event identity, structural nodes, phase ancestry) without claiming more than it can deliver.
