# Architect Theory v1 — Temporal Identity Theory (TIT)

**Agent:** Architect
**Run:** h1-time-is-consciousness
**Date:** 2026-03-26

**Hypothesis under formalization:** "Consciousness doesn't happen in time — time is what consciousness looks like from the outside. Physics sees temporal sequences; the 'inside' of those sequences is experience. Time and consciousness are the same thing seen from different angles."

---

## 1. Definitions

**D1. Phenomenal consciousness (P-consciousness).** The property of a system such that there is something it is like to be that system. This is used as an ostensive pointer to a pre-theoretically recognized phenomenon, not a reductive definition.

**D2. Temporal structure (T-struct).** The complete set of timelike relations in a physical system or region: ordering, metric (duration), dimensionality, and symmetry properties, as described by the laws of physics (special relativity, general relativity, thermodynamics).

**D3. Temporal phenomenology (T-phen).** The experiential character of time as lived: the felt passage of moments, the thickness of the present, the asymmetry between remembered past and anticipated future, the sense that experience "flows." T-phen is a subset of P-consciousness — it is the specifically temporal aspect of what it is like to be a conscious system.

**D4. Structural description.** A description of a system that specifies only the relational and dispositional properties of its components — how parts relate to each other and what they do — without specifying the intrinsic nature of what bears those relations.

**D5. Intrinsic nature.** The non-relational, non-dispositional character of that which instantiates a structural description. The "what it is" as opposed to "how it relates." (Following Russell 1927, Chalmers 2015.)

**D6. Dual-aspect identity.** A metaphysical relation in which two apparently distinct descriptions (A and B) refer to the same underlying reality R, such that A captures R's structural/relational properties and B captures R's intrinsic nature. A and B are not two things that correlate; they are two modes of access to one thing.

**D7. Organized temporal complexity (OTC).** A measure of the degree to which a system's temporal structure exhibits: (a) causal integration — parts constraining each other's temporal evolution, (b) informational differentiation — the system occupying one of a large repertoire of distinguishable temporal trajectories, and (c) recursive self-reference — the system's temporal trajectory being causally shaped by its own prior states in a way that constitutes a model (however minimal) of its own temporal evolution. OTC = f(integration, differentiation, self-reference), where f is monotonically increasing in each argument.

**D8. Protoconscious temporal character (PTC).** The minimal intrinsic nature that any instance of temporal structure possesses, in virtue of being temporal. PTC is not experience in the full sense (not P-consciousness); it is the intrinsic-nature "stuff" out of which full P-consciousness is constituted when organized into sufficient complexity. (This is panprotopsychism about the temporal, not panpsychism.)

**D9. The perspectival divide.** The distinction between third-person access (structural descriptions available through measurement, experiment, and mathematical physics) and first-person access (intrinsic nature available through being the system in question). This divide is taken as a fundamental feature of reality, not as a problem to be explained away.

**D10. Temporal passage (phenomenal).** The experiential quality of "moving through" time — the felt transition from one moment to the next. Distinguished from the physicist's time parameter t, which describes a dimension but does not intrinsically "pass."

## 2. Premises

### Ontological Premises

**P1. Ontic structural realism about physics.** The content of physical theories is exhausted by structural/relational descriptions. Physics tells us how things relate and what they do, not what they intrinsically are. (Following Ladyman & Ross 2007, with the modification below.)

**P2. Intrinsic nature realism.** Structural descriptions require something that bears the structure. There exists an intrinsic nature to physical reality that is not captured by structural descriptions but that is not a separate substance — it is the very same reality described from a different mode of access. (Following Russell 1927, Chalmers 2015, Goff 2017.)

*Justification for P1 + P2 jointly:* Physics is remarkably successful at describing relations and dynamics but has nothing to say about what energy, mass, charge, or spacetime ARE intrinsically. The equations work identically regardless of what fills the structural roles. This suggests the structural description is incomplete — not false, but silent on intrinsic nature. P2 posits that this silence reflects a genuine feature of the world, not merely an epistemological limitation.

**P3. Anti-eliminativism.** P-consciousness exists. There is something it is like to be at least some systems. This is treated as datum, not as something to be derived.

**P4. Physical causal closure.** Every physical event has a sufficient physical cause. There are no non-physical interventions in the causal order.

### The Core Identity Premises

**P5. Temporal intrinsic nature thesis.** The intrinsic nature of temporal structure IS protoconscious temporal character (PTC). That is: what it IS to be a temporal relation, from the inside, is the most minimal form of experiential temporality — not felt passage in the rich sense, but the bare proto-quality of which felt passage is the organized, amplified manifestation.

*Justification:*
- (a) Among all physical structures, time is uniquely associated with experience. Every known conscious experience is temporal. No known experience is atemporal. Spatial structure can be experienced, but the experience itself unfolds temporally — spatial qualia are embedded in temporal experience, not the reverse. Time is the one physical dimension that is never absent from experience.
- (b) The two great explanatory gaps in our understanding of reality — "why is there experience at all?" and "why does time seem to pass?" — share a formal structure. Both involve an objective structural description that seems to leave out something real. If they are the same gap, both are resolved simultaneously.
- (c) This is an axiom defended on grounds of theoretical virtue: parsimony (two mysteries reduced to one), explanatory scope (addresses both the hard problem and the passage problem), and consistency with all known physics (physics describes the structural side; experience is the intrinsic side — no conflict).

**P6. Constitution by organization.** Full P-consciousness (as opposed to mere PTC) is constituted by the organization of protoconscious temporal character into sufficiently high Organized Temporal Complexity (OTC). PTC is the "raw material"; OTC is the architectural condition. A system with high OTC is P-conscious. A system with temporal structure but low OTC has PTC but not P-consciousness.

*Justification:*
- (a) This resolves the combination problem for panprotopsychism: proto-experiential properties combine into full experience only when organized appropriately, just as atoms combine into water only under specific organizational conditions.
- (b) It explains why brains are conscious and rocks are not: both have temporal structure (both have PTC), but brains have vastly higher OTC (temporal integration, differentiation, and self-reference).
- (c) It is consistent with the neuroscience of consciousness: disrupting temporal integration (via anesthesia, lesions, or synchrony) disrupts consciousness; enhancing temporal complexity (via alertness, psychedelics) enhances it.

**P7. Perspectival identity.** Temporal structure (T-struct, third-person description) and temporal phenomenology (T-phen, first-person experience) are related by dual-aspect identity (D6). They are the same reality accessed through different modes. T-struct is what T-phen looks like from the outside; T-phen is what T-struct is from the inside.

*Justification:*
- (a) This follows from P5 (temporal intrinsic nature IS PTC) + P6 (organized PTC constitutes P-consciousness) + D6 (dual-aspect identity = same reality, different modes of access).
- (b) It explains why physics finds no "passage" or "now" — these are intrinsic-nature features, and physics is restricted to structural descriptions (by P1).
- (c) It explains why consciousness is always temporal — T-phen IS organized T-struct, so removing temporality from experience would be removing the very thing experience is made of.

### Boundary Premises

**P8. The OTC threshold.** There exists a threshold value OTC* such that systems with OTC >= OTC* are P-conscious and systems with OTC < OTC* have PTC but not P-consciousness. The transition may be sharp or gradual; the theory is agnostic on this point but predicts that OTC strongly correlates with all empirical indicators of consciousness.

*Justification:* This is the panprotopsychist resolution of the boundary problem: not everything with temporal structure is fully conscious, but everything with temporal structure has the intrinsic-nature precursor. The threshold is an empirical matter to be discovered, not a philosophical stipulation.

**P9. The scope of temporal structure.** Temporal structure (T-struct) is present wherever timelike relations exist — which, in our universe, is everywhere that is not a spacelike-separated region at an instant. This means PTC is cosmologically ubiquitous, but P-consciousness is rare (confined to systems with high OTC).

*Justification:* This is a straightforward consequence of D2 + P5. It commits the theory to panprotopsychism about the temporal, not to panpsychism. The early universe has temporal structure and therefore PTC; it does not have OTC and therefore is not P-conscious.

**P10. Temporal phenomenology is not representational.** T-phen (felt passage, nowness, duration) is not a *representation* of temporal structure produced by a cognitive system. It IS temporal structure, accessed from the intrinsic-nature side. The brain does not *compute* the feeling of passage; the brain IS a complex temporal structure, and the feeling of passage is what that structure intrinsically is.

*Justification:*
- (a) Representationalist accounts face the "hard problem of temporal representation": why does representing temporal structure feel like anything? If representation is itself a temporal process, the representationalist still needs to explain what that temporal process intrinsically is. TIT answers: it is PTC, organized.
- (b) TIT explains the phenomenological immediacy of temporal experience. Passage does not feel computed or inferred; it feels given, basic, unmediated. On TIT, this is because it IS basic — it is the intrinsic nature, not a product of processing.
- (c) This distinguishes TIT from competing theories (GWT, IIT, HOT) that treat temporal phenomenology as a cognitive construction or representation.

## 3. Logical Derivations

### Derivation 1: What consciousness is

From P5, P6, P7:
- P5: The intrinsic nature of temporal structure is PTC.
- P6: P-consciousness is constituted by organized PTC (high OTC).
- P7: T-struct and T-phen are dual aspects of the same reality.

**Step 1:** PTC is the intrinsic nature of temporal relations (P5).
**Step 2:** When PTC is organized to high OTC, it constitutes P-consciousness (P6).
**Step 3:** The third-person description of this organized PTC is complex temporal structure — i.e., the physical description of a brain's temporal dynamics (from P1 — physics captures structure).
**Step 4:** The first-person reality of this organized PTC is phenomenal consciousness — rich temporal experience with all its qualitative character (from P6 + D1).
**Step 5:** Steps 3 and 4 describe the same thing from different modes of access (P7, D6).
**Step 6:** Therefore, phenomenal consciousness IS the intrinsic nature of sufficiently organized temporal structure, and organized temporal structure IS what phenomenal consciousness looks like from the outside (by D6, P7).

**Conclusion C1:** Consciousness is the intrinsic nature of temporally organized physical processes. Physics describes the structural skeleton; experience is the flesh on those bones. They are not two things; they are one thing known two ways.

### Derivation 2: Why consciousness is always temporal

From P5, P6, D3:
- P5: The intrinsic nature of temporal structure is PTC.
- P6: P-consciousness is constituted by organized PTC.
- D3: T-phen is the temporal aspect of P-consciousness.

**Step 1:** All PTC is temporal (it is the intrinsic nature of specifically temporal structure — P5).
**Step 2:** P-consciousness is constituted entirely by organized PTC (P6).
**Step 3:** Therefore, P-consciousness is constituted entirely by organized temporal intrinsic properties.
**Step 4:** Therefore, all P-consciousness is inherently temporal. Atemporal P-consciousness is impossible — it would be PTC without the temporal, which is a contradiction.

**Conclusion C2:** Every conscious experience is inherently temporal. This is not a contingent feature of how brains happen to process information; it is a necessary consequence of what consciousness IS (the intrinsic nature of temporal structure). An atemporal consciousness is metaphysically impossible under TIT.

### Derivation 3: Why physics cannot find consciousness

From P1, P5, D4, D5:
- P1: Physics is exhausted by structural descriptions.
- P5: Consciousness (via PTC) is intrinsic nature.
- D4: Structural descriptions specify only relational/dispositional properties.
- D5: Intrinsic nature is non-relational, non-dispositional.

**Step 1:** Physics captures only structural/relational properties of reality (P1).
**Step 2:** Consciousness is (constituted by) intrinsic nature (P5, P6).
**Step 3:** Intrinsic nature is not structural/relational (D5).
**Step 4:** Therefore, consciousness is not the kind of thing structural descriptions can capture.
**Step 5:** Therefore, physics will never find consciousness in its equations — not because consciousness is non-physical, but because physics describes only one aspect (the structural) of a reality that also has an intrinsic aspect.

**Conclusion C3:** The "explanatory gap" between physics and consciousness is not a gap in reality but a gap in modes of access. Physics and experience are looking at the same world from different sides. The gap is perspectival, not ontological.

### Derivation 4: Why time seems to pass

From P5, P7, P10:
- P5: The intrinsic nature of temporal structure is PTC.
- P7: T-struct and T-phen are dual aspects.
- P10: T-phen is not representational but is T-struct accessed intrinsically.

**Step 1:** Physics describes temporal structure as a dimension with ordering and metric — but with no intrinsic "passage" (the block universe contains all times equally).
**Step 2:** But temporal structure has an intrinsic nature — PTC (P5).
**Step 3:** When PTC is organized into P-consciousness, the first-person access to this temporal structure is T-phen — felt passage, nowness, duration (P7, D3).
**Step 4:** T-phen is not a representation of passage; it IS the intrinsic character of temporal structure itself (P10).
**Step 5:** Therefore, "passage" is real — it is the intrinsic nature of temporal ordering. It is not found in physics because physics describes only structure (P1). It is found in experience because experience IS intrinsic nature (P5, P6).

**Conclusion C4:** The felt passage of time is neither an illusion nor a cognitive construction. It is the intrinsic nature of temporal ordering, directly accessed from within. The block universe and felt passage are both correct — they describe the same temporal reality from the structural and intrinsic sides, respectively.

### Derivation 5: Why brains are conscious and rocks are not

From P5, P6, P8, P9:
- P5: All temporal structure has PTC (intrinsic nature).
- P6: P-consciousness requires high OTC (organized PTC).
- P8: There is an OTC threshold for P-consciousness.
- P9: Temporal structure is cosmologically ubiquitous.

**Step 1:** Rocks have temporal structure (they persist, change state, participate in causal chains). Therefore rocks have PTC (P5, P9).
**Step 2:** But rocks have extremely low OTC: minimal causal integration between parts' temporal evolution, minimal informational differentiation (limited repertoire of temporal trajectories), and no self-referential modeling of their own temporal states.
**Step 3:** Brains have extremely high OTC: massive causal integration (billions of neurons with recurrent temporal coupling), vast informational differentiation (enormous repertoire of distinguishable temporal states), and rich self-reference (the brain models its own temporal processes — working memory, planning, episodic recall).
**Step 4:** OTC_brain >> OTC* >> OTC_rock (P8).
**Step 5:** Therefore brains are P-conscious and rocks are not, even though both have PTC.

**Conclusion C5:** The difference between conscious and non-conscious systems is not the presence or absence of temporal structure (both have it) but the degree of organized temporal complexity. This explains the boundary without denying that the "raw material" of experience (PTC) is more widely distributed. Consciousness is rare because organization is rare, not because the intrinsic nature of time is rare.

### Derivation 6: Why anesthesia eliminates consciousness

From P6, P7, D7:
- P6: P-consciousness requires high OTC.
- P7: T-phen is the intrinsic-nature side of complex T-struct.
- D7: OTC = f(integration, differentiation, self-reference).

**Step 1:** Anesthetics (propofol, ketamine, sevoflurane) disrupt cortical temporal integration — they decouple the temporal coordination of neural populations.
**Step 2:** This reduces the integration component of OTC.
**Step 3:** Reduced OTC means the organized PTC falls below the threshold for P-consciousness (P8).
**Step 4:** The intrinsic nature (PTC) does not disappear — the neurons still have temporal structure. But the organization collapses.
**Step 5:** Therefore, anesthesia eliminates P-consciousness by de-organizing the temporal complexity, not by removing the temporal structure itself.

**Conclusion C6:** Anesthesia works by disrupting the temporal organization of the brain, not by stopping time in the brain. The temporal structure persists; the organized complexity does not. This is why anesthetized patients resume consciousness immediately when temporal integration is restored.

### Derivation 7: Why consciousness cannot be instantaneous

From C2, D7:
- C2: All P-consciousness is inherently temporal.
- D7: OTC requires causal integration across temporal evolution.

**Step 1:** Causal integration (a component of OTC) requires parts to constrain each other's states ACROSS TIME — i.e., temporal extent is necessary.
**Step 2:** Informational differentiation requires a repertoire of temporal trajectories — which requires temporal extent.
**Step 3:** Self-reference requires the system's current state to be shaped by its prior states — which requires temporal extent.
**Step 4:** Therefore, OTC > 0 requires non-zero temporal extent.
**Step 5:** P-consciousness requires high OTC (P6, P8). Therefore P-consciousness requires non-zero temporal extent.

**Conclusion C7:** A temporally instantaneous system — one with zero temporal extent — cannot be P-conscious. Consciousness has a minimum temporal thickness. This is an empirically testable structural prediction.

### Derivation 8: What the "hard problem" is (and isn't)

From C3, P1, P2, P5, D9:
- C3: The explanatory gap is perspectival, not ontological.
- P1: Physics describes only structure.
- P2: Structure requires intrinsic nature.
- P5: Temporal intrinsic nature is PTC.
- D9: The perspectival divide is fundamental.

**Step 1:** The hard problem asks: why is there something it is like to be a physical system? (P3 asserts there IS.)
**Step 2:** On TIT, the answer is: because physical systems have intrinsic natures (P2), and the intrinsic nature of temporal structure is protoconscious (P5).
**Step 3:** This is not an explanation of WHY intrinsic nature is experiential — it is an identity postulate (P5 is an axiom). It answers the "what" but not the ultimate "why."
**Step 4:** However, TIT relocates the hard problem from "why does consciousness exist?" to "why does anything have an intrinsic nature?" — which is arguably a more tractable (or at minimum a more honest) version of the same question.
**Step 5:** The perspectival divide (D9) is accepted as fundamental. There is no deeper explanation of why there are two modes of access. This is the theory's bedrock — its unexplained explainer.

**Conclusion C8:** TIT does not solve the hard problem in the sense of deriving experience from non-experiential primitives. It dissolves the specific form of the hard problem (why does experience accompany brain processes?) by denying the presupposition: experience does not ACCOMPANY brain processes — it IS what brain processes intrinsically are. The residual mystery — why does anything have an intrinsic nature? — is a metaphysical question that may have no further answer, but it is a question about the nature of reality in general, not about consciousness specifically.

### The Theory in One Paragraph

Time and consciousness are the same thing accessed from different sides. Physics describes temporal structure — ordering, metric, causation — and finds no passage, no "now," no experience. But this structural description is incomplete: what BEARS the temporal structure has an intrinsic nature, and that intrinsic nature is protoconscious. In simple systems (rocks, photons), this protoconscious character is minimal and unorganized. In complex systems (brains), temporal structure becomes massively integrated, differentiated, and self-referential — and the intrinsic nature of this organized temporal complexity IS phenomenal consciousness, in its full, vivid richness. The felt passage of time is not an illusion projected by the brain; it is what temporal ordering actually is, experienced from within. The hard problem is not a gap in nature — it is a gap between two modes of access (structural and intrinsic) to a single reality.

## 5. Empirical Predictions

**EP1. Temporal integration as consciousness indicator.**
TIT predicts that measures of temporal integration (e.g., Perturbational Complexity Index — PCI — or Temporal Binding Window measurements) should reliably distinguish conscious from unconscious states, because consciousness IS organized temporal complexity.
*Status: Confirmed.* PCI reliably separates wakefulness from coma, vegetative states, and anesthesia. Temporal binding windows shift systematically with consciousness level.

**EP2. Consciousness has a minimum temporal grain.**
C7 predicts that conscious experience cannot be temporally instantaneous. There must be a minimum duration below which stimuli cannot be consciously experienced. TIT predicts this minimum reflects the minimum temporal extent needed for OTC to exceed the threshold.
*Status: Confirmed.* The "psychological present" has a well-documented minimum grain of ~30-50ms. Stimuli below this duration can influence behavior (subliminal priming) but are not consciously experienced.

**EP3. Disrupting temporal coordination specifically — not activity level — should eliminate consciousness.**
TIT predicts that what matters is temporal organization, not the presence of neural activity per se. A brain with intact activity but disrupted temporal coordination should be unconscious.
*Status: Confirmed.* Propofol anesthesia maintains metabolic activity in many brain regions while specifically disrupting temporal coordination between cortical areas. Ketamine disrupts temporal binding while leaving local activity intact.

**EP4. Time perception distortions should correlate with consciousness alterations.**
If T-phen IS organized T-struct from the inside, then altering T-struct should alter T-phen. Specifically: conditions that compress, dilate, or fragment temporal processing should produce corresponding changes in temporal phenomenology.
*Status: Partially confirmed.* Time dilation under threat, temporal fragmentation in schizophrenia, and time distortion under psychedelics all correlate with measurable changes in neural temporal dynamics. However, the causal direction (does altered T-struct cause altered T-phen, or vice versa?) is not settled — TIT predicts they are identical, so the question of causal direction dissolves.

**EP5. The neural correlates of "nowness" should be temporal integration patterns, not spatial activations.**
TIT predicts that the felt sense of "now" corresponds to a temporally integrated state — a pattern of temporal coordination — not to activation in a specific brain region. Searching for the "now" in spatial terms (which brain area?) is looking at the wrong level.
*Status: Partially supported.* The "specious present" correlates with oscillatory patterns (theta, alpha, gamma binding) that are temporal coordination phenomena. No single brain region has been identified as the "now-region."

**EP6. Temporal structure in the brain should be more predictive of conscious content than spatial structure.**
TIT predicts that the temporal pattern of neural activity (phase relationships, cross-frequency coupling, temporal sequences) should predict what a subject is consciously experiencing more accurately than the spatial pattern of activation alone.
*Status: Partially supported.* Temporal decoding methods are increasingly successful. Phase patterns predict conscious perception of ambiguous stimuli. However, spatial patterns remain highly predictive too — the two are not yet cleanly separable.

**EP7. Meditation traditions that report "timelessness" should show measurable reduction in temporal integration.**
TIT predicts that reports of "timeless" or "eternal now" experiences in advanced meditation correspond to specific changes in neural temporal dynamics — likely a reduction of sequential temporal integration coupled with an increase in synchronous integration (experiencing all time-points as simultaneous rather than sequential).
*Status: Partially supported.* Experienced meditators show altered temporal processing (reduced temporal binding windows, altered alpha/theta dynamics). The specific link to "timelessness" reports needs more investigation.

**EP8. (DISTINCTIVE) Temporal structure isomorphism across substrates should preserve conscious character.**
TIT predicts that if an artificial system replicates the temporal structure of a brain region (the same patterns of temporal integration, differentiation, and self-reference) — even on a radically different substrate — it should instantiate the same phenomenal experience. Specifically, TIT predicts temporal isomorphism is both necessary AND sufficient for phenomenal isomorphism. This distinguishes TIT from theories that require spatial or computational isomorphism.
*Status: Not yet testable.* This is a strong distinctive prediction. GWT requires global workspace architecture (spatial), IIT requires Phi maximization (which includes spatial integration), HOT requires higher-order representations. TIT says only the temporal structure matters.

**EP9. (DISTINCTIVE) Systems with temporal structure but no spatial complexity should have PTC.**
TIT predicts that a simple oscillator — a system with temporal structure but minimal spatial complexity — has protoconscious temporal character. This cannot be directly tested (we cannot measure PTC), but it yields a distinctive indirect prediction: if we could measure the intrinsic-nature properties of simple temporal systems, we would find proto-experiential properties there. This distinguishes TIT from IIT (which attributes consciousness based on information integration including spatial) and from GWT/HOT (which require cognitive architecture).
*Status: Not testable with current methods.* This is a metaphysical commitment that distinguishes TIT's ontology from rivals.

**EP10. (DISTINCTIVE) Artificial systems that replicate spatial complexity but NOT temporal complexity should not be conscious.**
TIT predicts that a system could replicate the spatial architecture of a brain (same connectivity graph, same node properties) but if it operates with fundamentally different temporal dynamics (e.g., all computations are simultaneous/parallel rather than temporally sequenced with integration), it should NOT be conscious — even if functionally equivalent.
*Status: Not yet testable.* This is the strongest distinctive prediction. Standard functionalism and GWT would predict consciousness if functional equivalence holds. TIT says temporal structure is what matters, and a system with the "right answers" but the "wrong temporal dynamics" would be a philosophical zombie.

## 6. Known Vulnerabilities (Self-Assessment)

1. **P5 is an axiom, not a derivation.** The identification of temporal intrinsic nature with PTC is the theory's foundational postulate. It is defended on grounds of theoretical virtue, not proved. An adversary will press: why temporal structure specifically? Why not spatial structure, or causal structure in general?

2. **The panprotopsychism commitment is hard to test.** P9 says PTC is cosmologically ubiquitous. This is unfalsifiable in practice — we cannot test whether a rock has protoconscious character. The theory's empirical content is concentrated in the OTC threshold and above, not in the PTC-level claims.

3. **OTC is not yet formally defined.** D7 gives the components (integration, differentiation, self-reference) but does not provide a mathematical formalism. Without a computable measure, OTC is a concept, not a metric.

4. **The combination problem is deferred, not solved.** P6 says organized PTC constitutes P-consciousness. But the mechanism by which protoconscious units combine into unified experience is not specified. "Organization" is a placeholder.

5. **The scope question: does the theory apply to non-temporal aspects of consciousness?** TIT grounds consciousness in temporal intrinsic nature. But qualia have non-temporal aspects — color, pain, spatial extent. The theory must explain how non-temporal qualia arise from temporal intrinsic properties.

6. **C4 (passage is real) may be in tension with the block universe.** The theory claims compatibility with eternalism, but "passage is the intrinsic nature of temporal ordering" could be read as requiring something dynamic that the block universe disallows.
