# Run h4-sleep-timeless | Summary

**Date:** 2026-03-26
**Pipeline:** Architect v1 --> Adversary --> Judge --> Architect v2
**Hypothesis:** "Sleep isn't unconsciousness -- it's consciousness without time. Waking is what happens when consciousness and time couple."

---

## What Was Proposed

The architect built the **Temporal Decoupling Theory of Sleep (TDTS)**, which reinterprets sleep stages not as graded consciousness loss but as graded temporal decoupling:

1. **Waking** = consciousness + full temporal coupling.
2. **REM** = consciousness + distorted temporal coupling (explaining dream atemporality).
3. **NREM** = consciousness + severe temporal decoupling (explaining absence of reports -- not absence of experience).
4. **Anesthesia** = consciousness OFF (the actual comparator for unconsciousness).

The framework drew on TMS-EEG/PCI data (Massimini), sleep neuroscience (SWA, up/down states), dream phenomenology, meditation reports, and IIT.

3. Acknowledge that existing NREM consciousness evidence involves local temporal restoration, not consciousness-without-time.
4. Replace IIT with a theoretical framework compatible with temporal decoupling (IIT's Phi requires temporal transitions).
5. Cite prior art (Thompson, Windt, Metzinger) and distinguish the architect's contribution.

**SHOULD FIX (3 items):**

## What Changed in v2

1. **Prior art cited.** Thompson, Windt, Metzinger acknowledged. Contribution reframed as empirical operationalization + predictions.
2. **"Consciousness without time" defined** as a "non-ordered phenomenal field" -- phenomenal quality with temporal extension but no internal temporal ordering. Specious present addressed via the photograph/video analogy.
3. **P7 contradiction acknowledged.** All detected NREM consciousness involves local temporal restoration. The consciousness-without-time claim is explicitly flagged as speculative extrapolation.
4. **IIT replaced** with predictive processing (Friston, Clark, Hohwy, Seth), which naturally accommodates modular degradation of temporal predictions while preserving other aspects of conscious experience.
5. **Formalization fixed.** C_phenomenal (can persist without temporal coupling) distinguished from C_reportable (requires temporal coupling).
6. **Prediction 1 strengthened** with difficulty-matched controls.
7. **Prediction 4 added:** Subjective quality of awakening from sleep vs. anesthesia ("time restarting" vs. "everything restarting").

## Verdicts

### Plausibility: MODERATE

**For:**
- The hypothesis is consistent with known sleep neuroscience (NREM cortex is active, not silent).
- Contemplative traditions convergently report timeless awareness during deep sleep.
- Predictive processing provides a natural theoretical home for modular temporal degradation.
- The PCI data shows a qualitative difference between NREM and anesthesia that the framework explains.

**Against:**
- All empirical evidence for NREM consciousness involves local temporal restoration, not consciousness-without-time.
- The core phenomenological claim (experience during deep N3) is empirically inaccessible with current methods.
- The concept of "consciousness without time" is coherent on the revised articulation but remains exotic and unverified.
- GNW (a competing theory) predicts no consciousness during NREM, which is equally consistent with the behavioral evidence.

### Novelty: LOW (thesis) / MODERATE (predictions)

The thesis itself is not novel -- Thompson, Windt, and Metzinger have all published it. The contributions are:
- **Mildly novel:** Graded temporal coupling spectrum mapped onto TMS-EEG data.
- **Most novel:** Prediction 3 (local vs. global PCI decomposition for NREM vs. anesthesia). This is a genuinely new experimental proposal that extends existing methodology.
- **Novel:** Prediction 4 (subjective quality of awakening from sleep vs. anesthesia). Testable phenomenological prediction not previously articulated.

### Testability: HIGH (for predictions) / LOW (for core claim)

| Prediction | Testability | What it tests |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| 1: Selective temporal impairment post-awakening | High | Whether sleep selectively disrupts temporal processing |
| 2: Meditator NREM signatures | High | Whether contemplative training alters NREM consciousness |
| 3: PCI local/global decomposition | High (re-analysis of existing data) | Whether NREM and anesthesia differ mechanistically |
| 4: Awakening phenomenology (sleep vs. anesthesia) | Moderate | Whether temporal re-coupling and consciousness onset feel different |

The core claim (phenomenal experience during deep N3 without temporal structure) is NOT directly testable with current methods. This is a permanent limitation.

### Worth Pursuing? YES -- but reframed.

The hypothesis in its original form ("sleep is consciousness without time") is not where the value lies. The thesis is already in the literature, and the central phenomenological claim is unfalsifiable with current tools.

**What IS worth pursuing:**
1. **Prediction 3** (PCI decomposition) can be done NOW with existing data. If NREM and anesthesia show dissociable local/global PCI patterns, this is a meaningful contribution to the temporal-decoupling-vs.-consciousness-loss debate, regardless of the phenomenological interpretation.
2. **Prediction 2** (meditator NREM signatures) is testable with existing sleep-lab infrastructure. The specific prediction about qualitative report differences (d) would be the most informative result.
3. **The achromatopsia analogy** (consciousness-without-time as analogous to consciousness-without-color) is a potentially powerful framing that could advance the philosophical debate, if developed rigorously.

**What to drop:** The claim that TDTS is a new idea. It isn't. The value is in the empirical operationalization, not the conceptual framework.
