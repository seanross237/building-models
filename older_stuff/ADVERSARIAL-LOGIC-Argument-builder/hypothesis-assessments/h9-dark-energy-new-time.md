# Hypothesis Assessment H9 — "Dark Energy as New Time Being Generated"

**Date:** 2026-03-26
**Hypothesis:** "Dark energy isn't pushing the universe apart — it's new time being generated. The accelerating expansion is the universe creating more 'now.'"

**Core claim:** Dark energy is not a substance or field within spacetime but a property of spacetime itself — specifically, the ongoing production of new temporal extent. The accelerating expansion of the universe is a side effect of time being created. The universe is not expanding into space; it is "deepening into time."

---

## 1. Plausibility: 5/10

This scores in the middle because it gropes toward a real and deep feature of general relativity — the inseparability of spacetime geometry from its dynamics — while misidentifying what that feature implies.

**What supports it:**

- **The cosmological constant already IS a property of spacetime.** The hypothesis says dark energy is not a thing in spacetime but a property of spacetime itself. This is literally true of the cosmological constant Lambda. In Einstein's field equations, G_mu_nu + Lambda * g_mu_nu = 8piG * T_mu_nu, the Lambda term sits on the geometry side of the equation, not the matter side. It is not a source of energy in the conventional sense; it is a property of the vacuum geometry. The hypothesis is correct that "we can't find dark energy anywhere" is expected if dark energy is Lambda — you would never detect it as a particle or field because it is a feature of the manifold itself.

- **De Sitter space and exponential expansion.** A universe with only a cosmological constant (no matter, no radiation) is de Sitter space. In de Sitter space, the metric can be written as ds^2 = -dt^2 + e^(2Ht)(dx^2 + dy^2 + dz^2), where H = sqrt(Lambda/3). The scale factor grows exponentially with time. Crucially, in de Sitter space, the passage of coordinate time *directly produces* new spatial volume. Each tick of the clock creates exponentially more space. The hypothesis's intuition — that the flow of time itself is what generates expansion — is not a crazy misreading of this metric. It is a correct observation that in de Sitter space, time and spatial expansion are inextricable.

- **The FRW metric ties expansion to time.** In the Friedmann-Robertson-Walker metric, ds^2 = -dt^2 + a(t)^2 [dr^2/(1-kr^2) + r^2 dOmega^2], the scale factor a(t) is a function of cosmic time. Expansion is something that happens *as time passes*. The hypothesis reads this as "time is generating expansion," which is at least structurally consonant with the mathematics, even if it misidentifies the causal mechanism.

- **Cosmic time is not static.** Unlike Minkowski spacetime (where the manifold is given all at once, eternally), the FRW cosmology has a dynamic, evolving geometry. There is a real sense in which new spacetime is being generated as the universe evolves. The block universe view treats all of spacetime as already existing, but the hypothesis's intuition — that something genuinely new is happening as time advances — resonates with the "growing block" and presentist interpretations of time, which are minority but not absurd positions in philosophy of physics.

**What undermines it:**

- The Friedmann equations tell us exactly what drives expansion: the energy density and pressure of the contents of the universe. For the cosmological constant, the equation of state is w = -1, meaning the pressure is the negative of the energy density. This negative pressure is what drives accelerating expansion. The cause is mathematically specified. "New time being generated" does not appear in any equation and does not add to this explanation.

- "New time being generated" is not a concept that exists in general relativity. GR describes a 4-dimensional manifold with a metric. The manifold does not grow. In the standard mathematical formulation, all of spacetime — past, present, future — is a single geometric object. There is no process of time being created; time is a coordinate on a manifold that already exists (in the block universe reading) or a parameter that indexes the evolution of spatial slices (in the ADM formalism). Neither interpretation involves the *production* of time.

---

## 2. Internal Coherence

The hypothesis has a suggestive internal logic but contains a critical ambiguity and one likely self-contradiction.

**The ambiguity: What does "new time being generated" mean?**

There are at least three readings:

- **Reading A (trivial):** As the universe evolves, new moments come into existence — the future becomes the present. This is either (a) a restatement of the fact that time passes, which is not an explanation of anything, or (b) a commitment to the growing block universe, which is a philosophical position about the ontology of time, not a physical mechanism.

- **Reading B (geometric):** The 4-dimensional spacetime manifold is literally gaining new temporal extent. Time is being added to the manifold like pages being added to a book. This would require the manifold to have a boundary (the "present") that advances. GR does not naturally accommodate this — the manifold is defined once and for all, and its boundaries (if any) are singularities or conformal infinities, not moving frontiers.

- **Reading C (the interesting one):** The local rate of "time production" is not uniform. Dark energy corresponds to regions or epochs where more temporal extent per unit coordinate time is being generated, and this excess time production manifests as spatial expansion. This is the reading that could potentially make novel predictions.

**The self-contradiction:** The hypothesis says the universe is "deepening into time, not expanding into space." But the observable evidence that motivates dark energy IS spatial expansion — the redshift of distant galaxies, the luminosity distances of Type Ia supernovae, the angular scale of CMB fluctuations. These are measurements of spatial distances growing. If the hypothesis denies spatial expansion, it contradicts the observations it is trying to explain. If it accepts spatial expansion as a side effect of temporal deepening, then it needs a mechanism by which extra time produces extra space. The de Sitter metric provides such a mechanism (a(t) = e^(Ht) — more time, more scale factor), but that mechanism is already standard cosmology. The hypothesis would then be a verbal redescription of the standard model, not an alternative to it.

---

## 3. Relation to Known Physics

**The cosmological constant:**

Lambda is indeed best understood as a property of spacetime geometry rather than a material substance. The hypothesis is not wrong to frame dark energy this way. The vacuum energy interpretation (Lambda as the energy of empty space from quantum field theory) famously gives a value 10^120 times too large — the cosmological constant problem. The hypothesis sidesteps this by refusing to identify Lambda with any energy source at all, instead treating it as a feature of temporal dynamics. This is not a solution to the cosmological constant problem, but it correctly identifies the problem.

**De Sitter space:**

In de Sitter space, there is a deep relationship between time and expansion. De Sitter space has maximal symmetry (10 Killing vectors, same as Minkowski space), and can be sliced in multiple ways — some of which do not even have a preferred time direction. In the flat slicing (the one cosmologists use), the exponential expansion is indexed by a time coordinate, but this time coordinate is not unique. The space has no preferred "now" — different observers see different slicings. The hypothesis's notion of "creating more now" is in tension with the observer-dependence of time in de Sitter space.

**The FRW metric and the Friedmann equations:**

The first Friedmann equation: H^2 = (8piG/3) rho - k/a^2 + Lambda/3

The second Friedmann equation: a''/a = -(4piG/3)(rho + 3p) + Lambda/3

These equations specify H (the expansion rate) and a'' (the acceleration of expansion) in terms of energy density rho, pressure p, spatial curvature k, and the cosmological constant Lambda. The term Lambda/3 drives accelerating expansion when Lambda > 0. There is no additional degree of freedom corresponding to "time production rate." The equations are complete as stated. Inserting a new dynamical variable (time production) would modify the equations and produce different predictions — predictions that are currently not needed, since Lambda-CDM fits observations to percent-level precision.

**Does GR allow "time creation"?**

Not in any standard sense. In canonical GR (the ADM formalism), spacetime is foliated into spatial slices Sigma_t, and the Einstein equations govern how one slice evolves to the next. The lapse function N determines the rate of proper time flow between slices, and the shift vector determines how spatial coordinates are relabeled. The lapse function can vary — some regions age faster than others (gravitational time dilation). But the lapse function is a gauge choice, not a dynamical degree of freedom. You cannot have "more time" in one region than another in any gauge-invariant sense. Proper time along a worldline is a physical observable, but the total "amount of time" in a spatial region is not.

There is one exception worth noting: in cosmological models with an initial singularity, time itself begins at the Big Bang. In some sense, the Big Bang *is* the creation of time. The Hartle-Hawking no-boundary proposal explicitly treats time as emerging from a Euclidean (timeless) geometry. So "time creation" is not entirely alien to theoretical cosmology — but it is associated with the origin of the universe, not with the ongoing accelerating expansion.

## 4. Hypothetical Testability

The key question: can this hypothesis be distinguished observationally from standard Lambda-CDM?

**Test 1: Does the expansion rate depend on "time production" differently than on Lambda?**

If "time production" is just Lambda redescribed, the predictions are identical and the hypothesis is empirically vacuous. If time production introduces a new dynamical degree of freedom, then it modifies the Friedmann equations and could in principle produce different predictions for:
- The expansion history H(z) (measured by Type Ia supernovae, baryon acoustic oscillations)
- The growth rate of structure (measured by galaxy surveys, weak lensing)
- The CMB power spectrum

Current data constrains the dark energy equation of state to w = -1.03 +/- 0.03, consistent with a cosmological constant. Any "time production" model that deviates from w = -1 would need to fall within these bounds. This is a real constraint: the hypothesis must reproduce Lambda-CDM to high accuracy or it is already ruled out.

**Test 2: Inhomogeneous time production.**

If dark energy is "time being created," one might expect the production rate to vary — some regions producing more time than others. This would manifest as spatial variations in the expansion rate. Local measurements of H_0 (from Cepheids and supernovae) differ from the CMB-inferred value by about 5 sigma — the "Hubble tension." Could time-production inhomogeneity explain this? It's conceivable in principle, but the hypothesis does not predict the magnitude or pattern of the inhomogeneity, so it does not actually explain the tension — it just accommodates it post hoc.

**Test 3: Temporal anomalies.**

If new time is being generated, there might be subtle effects on clocks, frequencies, or decay rates at cosmological scales. Observations of distant quasar spectra constrain the stability of fundamental constants (fine structure constant alpha, proton-to-electron mass ratio) to parts in 10^5 or better over 10 billion years. No temporal anomalies have been detected at this precision. A time-production model would need to explain why "new time" has exactly the same properties as "old time" — why it is qualitatively indistinguishable.

**Test 4: Late-time vs. early-time behavior.**

Dark energy dominates at late times (z < 0.7 roughly). If dark energy is time production, the hypothesis implies that time production is accelerating. Why? What drives the acceleration of time production? Standard Lambda-CDM has a simple answer: Lambda is constant, and it dominates once matter dilutes enough. The hypothesis needs an equally clean mechanism, and it does not have one.

**Testability verdict:** Low. The hypothesis either reduces to Lambda-CDM (untestable because identical) or introduces new degrees of freedom that are not specified enough to make sharp predictions.

## 5. Strongest Version (Steelman)

The strongest version connects to the thermodynamics of horizons and the observer-dependent nature of time in de Sitter space:

> In a universe with a positive cosmological constant, every observer is surrounded by a cosmological event horizon at distance c/H. This horizon has thermodynamic properties: a temperature T = H/(2pi) (the Gibbons-Hawking temperature) and an entropy S = pi c^3 / (G hbar H^2) (proportional to the horizon area). The horizon entropy is increasing as the universe expands — the horizon area grows as matter dilutes and Lambda dominates. This entropy increase IS the arrow of time for that observer. In this precise sense, the accelerating expansion of the universe is generating the thermodynamic arrow of time: it is creating "more temporal asymmetry," "more irreversibility," "more now." The cosmological constant does not push space apart; it provides the thermodynamic gradient that makes time real — distinguishable from space, directional, irreversible.

This version is genuinely interesting. It connects to:

- **Gibbons-Hawking radiation (1977).** De Sitter space has a temperature. Observers in an accelerating universe are immersed in a thermal bath. This temperature defines a preferred arrow of time (entropy increases outward from the observer).

- **The holographic principle applied to cosmological horizons.** The de Sitter entropy S = A/(4G) of the cosmological horizon may represent the total number of degrees of freedom accessible to an observer. As Lambda dominates and the horizon stabilizes, this entropy approaches a maximum — the "heat death" of the observable universe. The approach to maximum entropy IS the flow of time, operationally.

- **Banks, Fischler, and others** have proposed that de Sitter space has a finite-dimensional Hilbert space (dimension ~ e^S), and that its dynamics is fundamentally about the approach to thermal equilibrium. In this picture, time really is "produced" by the cosmological constant in a precise sense: Lambda determines the size of the Hilbert space, which determines the timescale over which equilibration occurs, which determines how much observable temporal evolution there is.

This steelmanned version is speculative but anchored in real theoretical physics. It does not claim time is literally being created in new units; it claims that the cosmological constant is what gives time its thermodynamic character — its direction, its irreversibility, its "nowness."

## 6. Fatal Flaw

**The hypothesis confuses the parameterization of change with the production of the parameter.**

In general relativity, time is a coordinate that labels the sequence of spatial configurations. The scale factor a(t) changes with time. This means: as we move along the time axis, the spatial geometry changes. It does NOT mean: the time axis is being produced, extended, or consumed.

An analogy: A movie consists of frames played in sequence. You can say "the plot advances as the frames go by." You cannot say "the plot is caused by the production of new frames." The frames are the medium in which the plot unfolds; they are not the engine that drives it. Similarly, time is the medium in which expansion unfolds, not the engine that drives it. The engine is the stress-energy content of the universe (including the cosmological constant).

The hypothesis reverses the explanatory direction: instead of "expansion happens over time, driven by Lambda," it says "time happens over expansion, and Lambda is time-production." But this reversal has no mathematical content. In the Friedmann equations, t is the independent variable and a(t) is the dependent variable. Relabeling t as "produced time" and a as "a side effect" does not change the equations or their solutions. It is a verbal maneuver, not a physical theory.

The deepest version of this flaw: the hypothesis treats time as something that can be "more" or "less" — as if the universe could have more or fewer seconds in its future. But in GR, the amount of proper time along a worldline is determined by the metric along that worldline. It is not a free parameter that can be "increased" by some process. The metric already determines everything. There is nothing left for "time production" to do.

## Summary Verdict

| Criterion | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| Plausibility | 5/10 — correctly identifies dark energy as a property of spacetime geometry, then overreads the implication |
| Internal coherence | Moderate — the core metaphor is suggestive but underspecified, and the claim "not expanding into space" contradicts observations |
| Relation to known physics | The cosmological constant IS a geometric property; the hypothesis is right about that. But "time production" has no place in the Friedmann equations or the ADM formalism |
| Testability | Low — either equivalent to Lambda-CDM or underdetermined |
| Steelman | Surprisingly strong — connects to Gibbons-Hawking thermodynamics, de Sitter entropy, and the thermodynamic arrow of time |
| Fatal flaw | Confuses the medium of change (time) with the cause of change (the stress-energy content and cosmological constant) |

**Bottom line:** The hypothesis correctly senses that dark energy is not a "thing" in the way matter or radiation is a thing — it is a property of the geometry itself. This is the right instinct, and it is shared by most cosmologists. The error is in the next step: interpreting this geometric property as "time production." The universe does not need a mechanism to produce time; time is a coordinate, and the metric determines what happens along it. The steelmanned version — that the cosmological constant provides the thermodynamic basis for the arrow of time, and in that sense "generates temporal asymmetry" — is the most defensible reading, and it connects to real research in de Sitter thermodynamics. But even this strong version does not justify the original claim that expansion is "new time being generated." Expansion is expansion. Time is time. The cosmological constant relates them through the Friedmann equations, not through a mechanism of temporal production.
