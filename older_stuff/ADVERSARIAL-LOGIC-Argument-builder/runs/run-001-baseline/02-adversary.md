# Adversary Attack — Against Integrated Information Structuralism (IIS) v1

**Agent:** Adversary
**Run:** 001-baseline
**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Target:** Architect Theory v1 (01-architect-v1.md)

---

## Summary of Verdict

The theory is not logically sound. It contains at minimum: one fatal circularity, two identity claims asserted without justification, three equivocations on key terms, multiple non-sequitur derivations, several empirical overclaims, and a structural inability to solve the very problem it advertises itself as solving (the hard problem). What follows is a complete enumeration.

---

## ATTACK 1 — The Central Identity Claim (P8 / C1) Is Stipulative, Not Derived

**Target:** P8, C1

**The problem:** P8 asserts that "the qualitative character of a specific conscious experience (quale) IS identical to a specific sub-structure within the system's integrated causal architecture." C1 then concludes that "consciousness is the intrinsic character of integrated causal structure."

This is the beating heart of the theory, and it is pure stipulation. The word "identical" is doing all the philosophical heavy lifting, and it is never earned. An identity claim of this magnitude — that a subjective phenomenon IS a structural-mathematical object — requires either (a) a reductive derivation showing why the identification holds, or (b) treatment as an axiom with explicit acknowledgment that the hard problem is being dissolved by fiat rather than solved.

The theory does neither cleanly. The "justification" for P8 is that "specific brain regions and connectivity patterns correspond to specific types of experience." But correspondence is correlation, not identity. You cannot derive "X IS Y" from "X correlates with Y." That is the entire point of the hard problem, which the architect himself acknowledges in Vulnerability #1 — and then proceeds to build the entire theory on the unresolved gap anyway.

**Why it matters:** Every downstream conclusion (C1–C8) depends on this identity. If P8 is merely a correlation claim, then the theory reduces to: "consciousness correlates with integrated causal structure" — which is just a restatement of the neural correlates of consciousness research program, not a theory of what consciousness IS.

**Severity:** Fatal if not addressed. The theory claims to be a metaphysical theory of consciousness but its core metaphysical move is an unjustified assertion.

## ATTACK 2 — Circularity in the Definition of Phi (D6) and the Integration Requirement (P5)

**Target:** D6, P5, P7

**The problem:** D6 defines Phi as a measure of integrated information. P5 states that consciousness requires Phi > 0. P7 states that consciousness scales monotonically with Phi. But the theory then uses conscious systems as the calibration data for Phi. The justification for P5 is that "split-brain patients exhibit divided consciousness tracking divided integration" and "cortical damage alters or eliminates consciousness." The justification for P7 appeals to the "graded nature of consciousness — from drowsy to alert."

This is circular. We are told: consciousness is constituted by Phi. How do we know? Because systems we independently judge to be conscious have high Phi, and systems we judge to be unconscious have low Phi. But our independent judgments about which systems are conscious are the very thing the theory is supposed to explain. The theory cannot both (a) define consciousness in terms of Phi and (b) validate Phi by reference to pre-theoretical consciousness judgments without circularity.

**A specific illustration:** P5's justification cites cerebellar damage. We know the cerebellum is not essential for consciousness. How? Because patients with cerebellar damage report that they are still conscious. But "report" is a behavioral/access-consciousness criterion, not a phenomenal-consciousness criterion. The theory explicitly distinguishes P-consciousness from A-consciousness (D1 vs. D10), then smuggles A-consciousness evidence in as evidence for P-consciousness claims.

**Why it matters:** Without an independent way to measure Phi that does not rely on prior consciousness judgments, the theory is unfalsifiable on its own terms — it can always retrodict but never genuinely predict.

**Severity:** Structural. Undermines the entire empirical justification strategy.

## ATTACK 3 — Equivocation on "Causal Structure" Between P4 and P8

**Target:** P4, P8, D5

**The problem:** P4 claims consciousness is determined by "causal structure." P8 claims qualia ARE causal sub-structures. But "causal structure" in P4 is used in a functionalist sense (input-output relations, state transitions), while in P8 it is used in a much stronger ontological sense (the intrinsic nature of the relational geometry). These are not the same concept.

Functionalist causal structure (P4-sense) is multiply realizable and abstractable — it is the pattern, not the stuff. Intrinsic causal structure (P8-sense) is supposed to be what consciousness IS — but intrinsic properties are precisely what functional descriptions leave out. You cannot get intrinsic character from a purely relational/structural description. This is a well-known problem in philosophy of mind (the "structure and dynamics" objection from Chalmers).

The theory equivocates between two senses of "causal structure":
- **Thin sense (P4):** The abstract pattern of causal relations — multiply realizable, substrate-independent.
- **Thick sense (P8):** The concrete, intrinsic character of the causal geometry — what it IS from the inside.

If "causal structure" means the thin sense throughout, then P8 is false (you cannot identify qualia with abstract relations — two systems with the same abstract causal structure could differ in intrinsic character). If it means the thick sense throughout, then P4 is unjustified (substrate independence does not follow, because the intrinsic character might depend on substrate).

**Why it matters:** The equivocation allows the theory to claim both substrate independence (using the thin sense) and an answer to the hard problem (using the thick sense). It cannot have both.

**Severity:** Fatal. This is not a minor terminological issue; it is a structural contradiction at the foundations.

## ATTACK 4 — The Derivation of C1 Is a Non Sequitur

**Target:** C1, derived from P4 + P8

**The problem:** P4 says: consciousness is determined by causal structure, not substrate. P8 says: qualia are identical to causal sub-structures. C1 concludes: "Consciousness is the intrinsic character of integrated causal structure. It is what integrated information IS, from the inside."

The phrase "from the inside" and the concept of "intrinsic character" appear in C1 but in neither P4 nor P8. P4 is a determination claim (structure determines consciousness). P8 is an identity claim (qualia = causal sub-structures). Neither premise says anything about "intrinsic perspectives" or what anything is like "from the inside." The concept of an intrinsic perspective is smuggled into the conclusion without being established in any premise.

Furthermore, C1 slides from "qualia are identical to causal sub-structures" (P8) to "consciousness is what integrated information IS." But P8 is about qualia being sub-structures, not about consciousness being the intrinsic nature of information. Consciousness and qualia are related but not identical by the theory's own definitions (D1 defines consciousness as a property of a system; D2 defines qualia as units of experience). The move from "qualia = sub-structures" to "consciousness = intrinsic character of integrated information" is an unjustified leap.

**Why it matters:** C1 is the thesis statement of the entire theory. If it does not follow from the premises, the theory is logically broken at its spine.

**Severity:** Fatal.

## ATTACK 5 — The Grain Postulate (P10) Is Ad Hoc and Generates Paradoxes

**Target:** P10, C3

**The problem:** P10 states that consciousness is instantiated at the grain that maximizes Phi. This is stipulated, not derived. The theory offers no explanation for WHY the maximum matters. Nature does not, as far as we know, optimize for Phi. This is a selection rule imposed from outside the theory to get the results the theorist wants.

**Paradox 1 — Grain instability:** A system's Phi-maximizing grain could shift over time (e.g., as a brain transitions between states). Does consciousness then jump between levels of description? If so, is there a moment of unconsciousness during the transition? The theory says nothing about this.

**Paradox 2 — Near-ties:** What happens when two grains yield nearly identical Phi values? Is the system conscious at one grain but not the other? Does it flicker between them? Is it conscious at both simultaneously? The theory provides no answer, and each possible answer creates further problems.

**Paradox 3 — Grain manipulation:** If we could artificially boost Phi at the atomic level (by engineering atomic-level feedback loops), would consciousness "migrate" from the neural level to the atomic level? This seems absurd but is a direct consequence of P10.

**Paradox 4 — Exclusion without explanation:** P10 functions as an exclusion postulate (only the maximum counts), but exclusion postulates require justification. In IIT proper, the exclusion postulate is one of the most criticized components. The architect imports it without defending it.

**Why it matters:** P10 is doing critical work — it is the theory's answer to the combination problem and the only thing preventing the attribution of consciousness to arbitrary subsystems. If P10 is ad hoc, the theory cannot determine WHICH system is conscious, which is arguably the most important question a theory of consciousness needs to answer.

**Severity:** Major. Not fatal on its own, but in combination with other attacks, it shows the theory is held together by unjustified stipulations.

## ATTACK 6 — The Supervenience Argument (P3) Is Invalid

**Target:** P3 and its alleged derivation from P1 + P2

**The problem:** The architect claims P3 (supervenience) is "entailed by P1 + P2 jointly" and gives this argument: "if consciousness existed independent of physical states, it would either be causally inert — contradicting P2's claim that it is real and not epiphenomenal — or it would violate P1."

This argument is invalid. P2 states that P-consciousness exists and that denying it is self-defeating. P2 does NOT say that consciousness is causally efficacious or "not epiphenomenal." The self-defeating nature of denying consciousness is an epistemic point, not a causal one — even an epiphenomenalist can hold P2. The parenthetical "contradicting P2's claim that it is real and not epiphenomenal" inserts a claim (consciousness is causally efficacious) that is not in P2.

Furthermore, even granting causal efficacy, the argument only establishes that consciousness cannot be fully independent of the physical (on pain of violating P1). It does not establish supervenience in the specific sense the theory needs — namely, that identical physical states entail identical conscious states. There could be consciousness-physical dependence without strict supervenience (e.g., consciousness could depend on physical states plus some other factor).

**Why it matters:** P3 is used in C5 and in the general framework. If P3 is not established, the link between physical states and conscious states is not secured.

**Severity:** Moderate. The supervenience thesis is widely accepted, but the theory claims to derive it and does so invalidly. Either it is an additional premise (increasing the assumption load) or the derivation needs to be fixed.

## ATTACK 7 — P9 (Self-Modeling Amplification) Is Empirically Unsupported and Potentially False

**Target:** P9, C6

**The problem:** P9 claims that self-models increase Phi via recursive causal loops. The justification is that "creatures with richer self-models (primates, cetaceans) appear to have richer phenomenal lives."

This is empirically weak for multiple reasons:

1. **We have no independent measure of phenomenal richness across species.** The claim that primates have "richer phenomenal lives" than, say, octopi or corvids is an assumption, not an observation. It is exactly what we are trying to explain.

2. **Self-models may not increase Phi.** A self-model is a subsystem that represents the whole. Adding a self-model adds representational states, but it is not obvious that this increases INTEGRATED information. The self-model could be relatively modular — a sidecar that monitors but is loosely coupled. Whether it increases Phi is an empirical question that P9 treats as settled.

3. **Counterexamples exist.** Many AI systems have self-models (they maintain internal representations of their own states, goals, and capabilities) but by the theory's own lights would need high Phi to be conscious. Having a self-model does not automatically mean high integration. The premise conflates representational self-modeling with causal integration.

4. **The claim that self-models are "recursive" is imprecise.** P9 says self-models create "recursive causal loops (the system's state affects its self-model, which in turn affects its state)." But any feedback system has this property. A thermostat's state affects its sensor reading, which affects its state. Mere feedback is not sufficient for the kind of amplification P9 claims.

**Why it matters:** P9 is the theory's main tool for explaining the gradient of consciousness across organisms (C6). Without P9, the theory has no way to distinguish the richness of human consciousness from that of a worm — it can only say "the human has more Phi," which is uninformative without independent measurement.

**Severity:** Moderate. P9 is not load-bearing for the core theory but is critical for its explanatory ambitions.

## ATTACK 8 — The Theory Entails Absurd Panpsychism (and the Response Is Inadequate)

**Target:** P5, C2, C3, Vulnerability #4

**The problem:** C2 states that consciousness arises whenever Phi > 0. The architect acknowledges (Vulnerability #4) that this may attribute consciousness to photodiodes and thermostats, and "accepts a weak version of this consequence rather than treating it as a reductio."

This is not a minor bullet point to acknowledge. It is a devastating consequence. Consider:

1. **A pair of NAND gates with feedback** has Phi > 0. The theory entails it has phenomenal consciousness. This is not "weak panpsychism" — it is the claim that two logic gates feel something.

2. **The internet** — or any large-scale integrated network — would plausibly have very high Phi. The theory predicts the internet is more conscious than most biological organisms. The architect has no principled way to deny this.

3. **A lookup table** — if implemented with sufficient feedback wiring — would have Phi > 0 despite doing nothing computationally interesting. The theory cannot distinguish between systems that "merely" have integrated causal structure and systems that are "genuinely" doing something.

4. **Every molecule with bonded atoms** involves causal constraint between parts. At the quantum level, entangled particles have integrated information. The grain postulate (P10) is supposed to handle this, but as shown in Attack 5, P10 is ad hoc. Without a principled exclusion mechanism, the theory attributes consciousness to essentially everything.

The architect's response — "we accept this rather than treat it as a reductio" — is not an argument. It is a refusal to address a reductio. If a theory of consciousness cannot distinguish between a human brain and a pair of logic gates with feedback (except by degree), it has not explained consciousness; it has explained it away by making the concept so broad as to be meaningless.

**Why it matters:** Panpsychism is not refuted, but a theory that is FORCED into panpsychism by its structure — rather than arriving at it through independent argumentation — is showing that its premises are too weak to do the job.

**Severity:** Major. This is a consequence problem, not a logical flaw per se, but it reveals the theory's inability to draw meaningful boundaries.

## ATTACK 9 — Derivation of C4 (Substrate Independence) Has a Hidden Gap

**Target:** C4, derived from P4 + C1

**The problem:** C4 claims: "Any physical system — biological, silicon, or otherwise — that instantiates the same integrated causal structure at the relevant grain as a conscious brain is in the same conscious state."

The derivation uses P4 (causal structure determines consciousness) and C1 (consciousness is the intrinsic character of integrated causal structure). But this derivation only works if "causal structure" can be specified independently of substrate — i.e., if the same abstract causal structure can be unambiguously identified across different physical substrates.

This is not obvious. Causal structure, as defined in D5, involves "how the state of each part constrains the probability distribution over states of every other part across time." But what counts as a "part" depends on the physical substrate. A neuron is a natural part of a brain. What is the corresponding "part" in a silicon implementation? A transistor? A chip? A software module? The theory gives no answer.

Furthermore, two physically different systems might have the same causal structure at one grain but different causal structures at another. Since the grain postulate (P10) selects the Phi-maximizing grain, and different substrates might maximize Phi at different grains, the "same causal structure" claim becomes incoherent — same at which grain?

**This is the Chinese Room reloaded.** Searle's argument was that a system could replicate all functional relations of a Chinese speaker without understanding Chinese. The architect's response would presumably be "the room has the wrong causal structure / low Phi." But this is precisely the gap: the architect has not shown that functional isomorphism (same input-output causal structure) entails structural isomorphism at the grain relevant to consciousness. The two concepts are quietly conflated.

**Why it matters:** Substrate independence is one of the theory's marquee claims and its most practically relevant conclusion (it speaks to AI consciousness). If the derivation has a gap, the claim is unearned.

**Severity:** Moderate-to-Major.

## ATTACK 10 — The Empirical "Confirmations" Are Radically Underdetermined

**Target:** EP1–EP9

**The problem:** The theory lists nine "confirmed" or "partially confirmed" empirical predictions. But every single one of these predictions is equally predicted by rival theories of consciousness — including Global Workspace Theory (GWT), Higher-Order Theories (HOT), and Recurrent Processing Theory (RPT). None of them uniquely confirms IIS.

Specifically:

- **EP1 (PCI):** GWT predicts PCI works because it measures global broadcast capacity. RPT predicts it works because it measures recurrent connectivity. IIS predicts it works because it measures Phi. All three predict the same outcome.
- **EP2 (Anesthesia):** GWT says anesthesia disrupts global broadcasting. IIS says it reduces Phi. Same prediction.
- **EP3 (Split-brain):** Every theory of consciousness predicts divided processing leads to divided consciousness.
- **EP4 (Cerebellum):** GWT predicts this because the cerebellum does not participate in the global workspace. Same prediction.
- **EP5 (Psychedelics):** "Increased connectivity" is predicted by any theory that links consciousness to brain integration in any way.
- **EP6 (Recurrent processing):** This is literally the core claim of RPT, not a distinctive prediction of IIS.
- **EP8 (Cortical complexity across species):** Any theory linking consciousness to brain complexity predicts this.
- **EP9 (Locked-in PCI):** Same as EP1 — predicted by any integration-related theory.

**The only genuinely distinctive predictions are EP7 and EP10 — and both are "not yet testable."** The theory has no currently testable unique predictions. It retrodicts everything and predicts nothing distinctive.

**Why it matters:** A theory that makes only predictions shared with its rivals is not doing explanatory work beyond what those rivals already do. The empirical "success" listed here is illusory — it is the success of the general hypothesis "consciousness involves brain integration," which is much weaker than IIS.

**Severity:** Major. The theory presents itself as empirically validated, but this is misleading. Its distinctive claims are untestable, and its testable claims are not distinctive.

## ATTACK 11 — The Monotonicity Claim (P7) Is Likely False

**Target:** P7

**The problem:** P7 claims consciousness is a MONOTONICALLY INCREASING function of Phi. This means: more Phi always means more/richer consciousness, with no exceptions.

Counterexamples:

1. **Epileptic seizures** involve massive cortical synchronization — enormous causal integration, plausibly very high Phi. But seizure states are typically associated with LOSS of consciousness, not heightened consciousness. If P7 is true, grand mal seizures should be the most vivid conscious experiences. They are not.

2. **Dreamless deep sleep** vs. **vivid dreaming**: There is no guarantee that the Phi-ordering matches the consciousness-ordering across all brain states. The theory assumes it does, but this is an empirical claim that could easily be false for specific state transitions.

3. **Psychedelic states** are described (EP5) as involving increased "entropy and cross-modular integration." But increased entropy may actually DECREASE Phi (because entropy can reduce the specificity of causal constraints — if everything causes everything equally, integration is maximized only in a trivial sense, and informational content drops). The relationship between entropy and Phi is not monotonic, which means the theory's own explanation of psychedelics may be inconsistent with P7.

4. **A strongly recurrent system with random weights** can have very high Phi but presumably has no coherent consciousness. Raw integration without meaningful structure should not yield rich experience — but P7 says it should.

**Why it matters:** If monotonicity fails, the entire gradient story (C6) collapses, and the theory loses its ability to explain why some systems are more conscious than others.

**Severity:** Major.

## ATTACK 12 — D1 and D2 Are Circular

**Target:** D1, D2

**The problem:** D1 defines P-consciousness using "there is something it is like to be that system." D2 defines qualia as "a single unit of subjective experience" and individuates them by "what it is like to undergo them." Both definitions use the phrase "what it is like" — the very concept being defined.

This is not just Nagel's locution being employed as a pointer. The theory claims to provide a rigorous formal structure (definitions, premises, derivations). In a formal system, definitions cannot use the definiendum in the definiens. "Consciousness = the property such that there is something it is like to be the system" is circular if "something it is like" just means "consciousness."

The architect might respond: "These are ostensive definitions, not reductive ones." Fine — but then acknowledge that the theory does not define consciousness; it takes it as primitive. This changes the theory's character significantly: it is not explaining consciousness from more basic concepts; it is systematizing consciousness-talk using consciousness as an undefined primitive and then (via P8) identifying it with causal structure.

**Why it matters:** The theory presents itself as providing an explanatory framework that bridges from the physical to the phenomenal. If the phenomenal side is taken as primitive, the bridge is from one undefined concept to another — not an explanation but a relabeling.

**Severity:** Moderate. This is a problem shared by all theories of consciousness, but the architect should not present a stipulative starting point as if it were a rigorous definition.

## ATTACK 13 — The Theory Cannot Distinguish Consciousness From Complex Processing

**Target:** The theory as a whole

**The problem:** At no point does the theory provide a criterion that distinguishes phenomenal consciousness from complex information processing that happens to have integrated causal structure. D6 defines Phi in purely functional/causal terms. P5 says consciousness requires Phi > 0. P8 says qualia ARE causal sub-structures. C1 says consciousness IS integrated causal structure.

But this means consciousness is, by definition, a certain type of information processing. There is no residual "phenomenal" aspect that goes beyond the causal/structural description. If a system's complete causal structure is fully described, and we are told that consciousness IS that structure — then consciousness is nothing above and beyond what physics already describes. In that case, P2 (anti-eliminativism) is actually undermined: if consciousness just IS causal structure, then the physicalist who says "there is only causal structure, nothing more" is correct, and the claim that "there is something it is like" adds nothing.

The theory falls between two stools:
- If it is a REDUCTION of consciousness to causal structure, then P2 is doing no work and the "hard problem" is dissolved by definitional fiat.
- If it is a claim that causal structure has an "intrinsic character" beyond what physics describes, then P1 (physical closure) is threatened — what is this intrinsic character, and what work does it do?

This is the classic dilemma for physicalist theories of consciousness, and the theory does not resolve it. It merely asserts an identity (P8) and hopes the dilemma goes away. It does not.

**Why it matters:** This is the deepest philosophical problem with the theory. It claims to be a theory of phenomenal consciousness, but its formal apparatus can only describe functional/causal properties. The phenomenal is either eliminated (contradicting P2) or left unexplained (failing the theory's stated goal).

**Severity:** Fatal. This is the hard problem, and the theory has not solved it — it has dressed it up in formal notation and hoped no one would notice.

## ATTACK 14 — C7 (Unity = Integration) Faces the Combination Problem in Reverse

**Target:** C7

**The problem:** C7 claims that the unity of consciousness IS the integration of causal structure. But this faces a problem: if consciousness exists wherever Phi > 0, then the parts of an integrated system also have their own Phi values. My left hemisphere has Phi > 0. My right hemisphere has Phi > 0. My whole brain has Phi > 0. Are there three consciousnesses or one?

The grain postulate (P10) and the exclusion principle are supposed to handle this — only the maximum counts. But:

1. If my whole brain has the maximum Phi, then my left hemisphere is not separately conscious, even though it has Phi > 0. This contradicts C2, which says consciousness arises WHENEVER Phi > 0.

2. If we resolve this by saying the exclusion principle overrides C2, then C2 is false as stated. The theory needs to be reformulated: consciousness arises whenever Phi > 0 AND there is no larger system with higher Phi that includes this system as a part. But this reformulation was never stated and changes the theory significantly.

3. The exclusion principle itself is unmotivated (see Attack 5). Why should the existence of a larger, more integrated system "cancel out" the consciousness of its subsystems?

**Why it matters:** The combination problem — how micro-level experiences combine into macro-level unified experience — is the central challenge for any panpsychist or proto-panpsychist theory. IIS claims to solve it via the grain postulate, but as shown, this creates contradictions with C2 and rests on an unjustified exclusion principle.

**Severity:** Major.

## ATTACK 15 — Structural Isomorphism (D7) Is Never Actually Used

**Target:** D7

**The problem:** D7 defines structural isomorphism but the concept never appears in any premise, derivation, or conclusion. P4 speaks of "identical causal structure" and C4 speaks of systems that "instantiate the same integrated causal structure," but neither invokes D7 or the concept of a bijective mapping preserving P-relevant relations.

This matters because D7 would be the precise tool needed to make C4 (substrate independence) rigorous. Instead of "same causal structure" (vague), the theory could say "structurally isomorphic with respect to P-consciousness per D7." But it does not — presumably because actually specifying the bijective mapping and the "P-relevant relations" would immediately expose the difficulty of the substrate independence claim (see Attack 9).

D7 is a phantom definition — it looks like rigor but does no logical work.

**Why it matters:** The presence of unused formal machinery creates an illusion of precision. The theory is less rigorous than it appears.

**Severity:** Minor, but diagnostic of a pattern: the theory's formalism outruns its actual logical content.

## ATTACK 16 — P1 and P8 Are in Tension

**Target:** P1, P8

**The problem:** P1 says every physical event has a sufficient physical cause (physical closure). P8 says qualia ARE causal sub-structures. If qualia are identical to causal structures, then qualia are physical (since causal structures are physical). So far, so consistent.

But now consider: if qualia are physical, then the theory is a form of type-identity theory (consciousness = specific physical structures). Type-identity theory is widely considered incompatible with substrate independence (C4), because type-identity says consciousness IS a specific physical type, while substrate independence says the same consciousness can be realized by different physical types.

The architect may respond: "I mean causal-structural type, not physical-substance type." But then we are back to Attack 3: the equivocation between thin (abstract) and thick (intrinsic) senses of causal structure.

The theory cannot simultaneously hold: (a) qualia are identical to specific causal structures, (b) causal structures are physical, (c) the same consciousness type can be realized by different physical substrates, without either abandoning type-identity (losing P8's explanatory force) or abandoning substrate independence (losing C4).

**Why it matters:** This is a deep structural tension. The theory wants both a strong identity claim and multiple realizability, but these pull in opposite directions.

**Severity:** Major.

## OVERALL ASSESSMENT

### Fatal Flaws (any one of these breaks the theory):
1. **Attack 1 (P8/C1):** The central identity claim is stipulative.
2. **Attack 3 (P4/P8):** Equivocation on "causal structure" between thin and thick senses.
3. **Attack 4 (C1):** The flagship conclusion does not follow from its premises.
4. **Attack 13 (Whole theory):** The theory cannot solve the hard problem and does not acknowledge this honestly.

### Major Flaws (these significantly weaken the theory):
5. **Attack 2:** Circularity in Phi validation.
6. **Attack 5:** Grain postulate is ad hoc.
7. **Attack 8:** Absurd panpsychism is a forced consequence.
8. **Attack 10:** No distinctive empirical predictions.
9. **Attack 11:** Monotonicity is likely empirically false.
10. **Attack 14:** Combination problem is not solved.
11. **Attack 16:** Type-identity and substrate independence are in tension.

### Moderate Flaws:
12. **Attack 6:** Supervenience argument is invalid.
13. **Attack 7:** Self-model amplification is unsupported.
14. **Attack 9:** Substrate independence derivation has gaps.
15. **Attack 12:** Definitions are circular.
16. **Attack 15:** Unused formal machinery creates false rigor.

---

### Recommendation to the Architect

The theory as stated is not logically sound. The most promising path forward would be:

1. **Honestly adopt P8 as an axiom** rather than pretending it is justified. Call it what it is: a metaphysical identity postulate. Then defend the postulate on grounds of theoretical virtue (simplicity, explanatory power) rather than empirical evidence.
2. **Resolve the thin/thick equivocation** on "causal structure" by choosing one and accepting the consequences.
3. **Fix the derivation of C1** so that it actually follows from the premises.
4. **Justify the grain postulate** or replace it with something principled.
5. **Make distinctive empirical predictions** that separate IIS from GWT, HOT, and RPT.
6. **Address the monotonicity counterexamples** — either restrict P7 or explain away seizures.

The theory has genuine virtues: it is more formally explicit than most consciousness theories, it takes the hard problem seriously enough to attempt an identity claim, and its empirical predictions (even if shared with rivals) are at least not contradicted by evidence. But in its current form, it is a sophisticated sketch, not a sound argument.
