## REVISED FRAMEWORK: Temporal Decoupling Theory of Sleep (TDTS v2)

### Acknowledgment of Prior Art

The core thesis -- that deep sleep may involve consciousness without ordinary temporal structure, rather than absence of consciousness -- has been articulated by:

- **Evan Thompson** (*Waking, Dreaming, Being*, 2015): Argues for awareness during deep sleep, drawing on Indian contemplative traditions. Frames deep sleep as a state of "objectless, contentless awareness."
- **Jennifer Windt** (*Dreaming*, 2015): Develops the concept of "minimal phenomenal experience" (MPE) during dreamless sleep -- a form of experience lacking content and temporal structure.
- **Thomas Metzinger** (2020): Formalizes the MPE research program, proposing that "pure awareness" is a minimal phenomenal state that may persist during deep sleep and meditation.

**What this version adds:** The contribution of the present work is not the thesis itself but its empirical operationalization through three moves:
1. Mapping the temporal decoupling interpretation onto the TMS-EEG/PCI literature (Massimini et al.).
2. Proposing a graded spectrum model (waking/REM/NREM/anesthesia as positions on a temporal coupling axis).
3. Deriving three specific, testable predictions that discriminate between temporal decoupling and consciousness loss.

### Definitions

*D1-D3 unchanged from v1.*

**D4.** (REVISED) *Temporal decoupling* -- The partial or total unbinding of a system's conscious states from temporal structure. TDTS proposes that sleep stages represent graded degrees of temporal decoupling. **This is an interpretation of the available evidence, not a directly testable hypothesis about the phenomenology of deep N3.**

**D5.** *Unchanged.*

**D6.** (NEW) *Non-ordered phenomenal field* -- A conscious state with phenomenal quality (there is something it is like) that lacks internal temporal ordering. Analogous to a visual field perceived "all at once" (spatial extension without temporal succession), this is a phenomenal state with qualitative character but no before/after, no succession, no experienced duration. This is to be distinguished from:
- (a) *Instantaneous consciousness* (a zero-duration flash): D6 does NOT claim this. The non-ordered field may persist over objective time; what it lacks is *experienced* temporal structure within itself.
- (b) *No consciousness*: D6 claims there is phenomenal quality (however minimal). The state is not nothing; it is a field of minimal phenomenal character without temporal differentiation.

> **Addressing the specious present objection (Attack 3):** The specious present literature (James 1890, Dainton 2000, Phillips 2014) argues that experience requires a "thick present" with some temporal extension. D6 is compatible with this: the non-ordered phenomenal field may have temporal *extension* (it obtains over an interval of objective time) but no temporal *structure* (within that interval, there is no experienced succession or ordering). This is the difference between a photograph (spatial extension, no temporal structure) and a video (spatial extension plus temporal structure). The non-ordered phenomenal field is experiential-photograph-like: it is not a point, but it has no internal temporal sequence.

### Premises

*P1-P4 unchanged from v1.*

**P5.** (REVISED -- MUST FIX #4) *Withdrawn as stated.* The original P5 claimed that IIT supports the separability of consciousness from temporal structure. The adversary correctly showed that IIT's Phi is computed over a transition probability matrix and presupposes temporal dynamics.

**P5a.** (NEW -- replacement) *Predictive processing framework (Friston 2010; Clark 2013; Hohwy 2013).* In the predictive processing / active inference framework, the brain maintains a generative model that predicts sensory inputs. Consciousness, on some accounts (Hohwy 2013; Seth 2014), is associated with the quality of the predictions and the patterns of prediction error. Temporal predictions are a *subset* of the brain's predictive activity -- specifically, the predictions about temporal ordering, duration, and succession of events.

> **Why this helps TDTS:** In the predictive processing framework, temporal predictions are implemented by specific circuits (hippocampal theta, thalamocortical loops) and can be selectively impaired. If temporal predictions degrade (as during NREM, when hippocampal-cortical coupling weakens), the temporal aspect of conscious experience degrades, but other aspects of the generative model (spatial, emotional, somatic predictions) may persist if their implementing circuits remain active.

> **Key advantage over IIT:** Predictive processing allows for *modular* degradation of different aspects of conscious experience, because different predictions are implemented by different circuits. This makes the separability of temporal structure from consciousness a natural consequence of the framework's modularity, rather than a special claim.

**P6.** *Unchanged (GNW noted as predicting the opposite).*

**P7.** *Unchanged.*

**P7a.** (NEW) *Working assumption.* The link between neural temporal dynamics (oscillations, thalamocortical feedback) and temporal phenomenology (experienced succession, duration) is a working assumption, not a proven causal relationship. It is supported by consistent correlations across many paradigms (anesthesia, psychedelics, meditation, sleep) but deductive proof is lacking.

### Revised Derivation Chain

#### Step 1: Temporal structure as a modular dimension of consciousness

**S1-S2.** (REVISED)

**S1.** From P3 and P4, temporal phenomenology can be profoundly altered (dreams) or reportedly absent (meditation) while consciousness-indicative features persist. From P5a (predictive processing), this is expected: temporal predictions are implemented by specific circuits and can degrade independently.

**S2.** (REVISED -- SHOULD FIX #2) Formalize: Let C_phenomenal represent the phenomenal state (whether there is something it is like), and let C_reportable represent the accessibility of that state to memory encoding and verbal report.

> C_phenomenal = f(causal_complexity, prediction_quality)
> C_reportable = g(C_phenomenal, temporal_coupling, memory_encoding)

Key properties:
- C_phenomenal can be nonzero when temporal_coupling is zero (the non-ordered phenomenal field of D6).
- C_reportable approaches zero when temporal_coupling is very low (because memory encoding requires temporal sequencing).
- This explains the dissociation: NREM may involve nonzero C_phenomenal with near-zero C_reportable.

**S3.** (REVISED -- MUST FIX #4) Theoretical backing for separability comes from predictive processing (P5a), not IIT. In the predictive processing framework, temporal predictions (implemented by hippocampal theta and thalamocortical loops) are modular components of the brain's generative model. Disrupting them degrades temporal experience specifically, not consciousness globally. This is analogous to disrupting color processing (e.g., achromatopsia): consciousness persists without the color dimension.

> **Analogy test:** Patients with acquired achromatopsia (cortical lesion destroying V4) are conscious but cannot experience color. Their phenomenal field has a dimension removed. Nobody claims they are unconscious -- they are conscious-without-color. TDTS claims that deep sleep is, by analogy, conscious-without-time. The conceptual structure is identical; the only question is whether temporal structure is as separable from consciousness as color processing is. (This remains to be proven, but the analogy shows the claim is not incoherent.)

#### Step 2: Sleep stages as graded temporal decoupling

**S4-S5.** *Unchanged.*

**S6.** (REVISED -- MUST FIX #3) CLAIM: Interpret this as temporal decoupling. **Honest acknowledgment: All existing empirical evidence for NREM consciousness (P7, Boly et al., Siclari et al.) shows that conscious reports from NREM are correlated with local restoration of waking-like temporal dynamics (decreased SWA in posterior hot zones).** This means:

- The empirically *demonstrated* NREM consciousness is NOT "consciousness without time." It is local consciousness with local temporal structure.
- The hypothesized "consciousness without time" (the non-ordered phenomenal field during deep N3 with full SWA) has NOT been empirically demonstrated. It is a speculative extrapolation from the framework.
- The argument for consciousness-without-time during full SWA is indirect: (a) the cortex is not inactive during up states, (b) predictive processing allows for consciousness without temporal predictions, (c) contemplative reports convergently describe such a state. This is suggestive but not conclusive.

> [CHANGED: explicitly admits that the core claim goes beyond the evidence. The empirical weight of the argument now rests on the predictions, not on direct evidence for consciousness-without-time.]

**S7.** *Unchanged (REM as intermediate case).*

#### Step 3: Deep sleep as the limiting case (speculative)

**S8.** (REVISED) CLAIM (explicitly speculative): NREM Stage N3 *may* represent the extreme of temporal decoupling -- a non-ordered phenomenal field (D6) in which minimal consciousness persists without temporal structure. This is the interpretation most consistent with:
- The predictive processing framework (which predicts modular degradation of temporal predictions).
- The contemplative reports (which convergently describe awareness-without-time during deep sleep).
- The neuroscience of NREM (which shows preserved cortical activity during up states, not cortical silence).

But it is NOT directly supported by existing empirical data, which instead shows that wherever consciousness has been detected in NREM, it correlates with local temporal restoration.

**S9.** *Unchanged (contemplative convergence).*

**S10.** (REVISED -- MUST FIX #2) The "consciousness without time" state, if it exists, is a non-ordered phenomenal field (D6). It has the following properties:
- Phenomenal quality is present but minimal and undifferentiated.
- The state has temporal *extension* (it obtains over an interval of objective time, during up states) but no temporal *structure* (no experienced succession, ordering, or duration within that interval).
- It is analogous to a spatial phenomenal field experienced "all at once" without temporal sequencing.
- It is NOT instantaneous (addressing Attack 3). The specious present is accommodated: the state has the thickness of the specious present but without the internal temporal ordering that normally characterizes the specious present.

#### Step 4: Waking as temporal coupling

**S11-S12.** (REVISED)

**S12.** The TDTS spectrum:

| State | C_phenomenal | Temporal coupling | C_reportable | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|-------------|-------------|
| Waking | High | High | High | Full consciousness, full time |
| REM | High | Moderate (distorted) | Moderate | Consciousness, scrambled time |

**S13.** (REVISED) The subjective experience of "waking up" is, on the TDTS interpretation, primarily the re-engagement of temporal coupling -- the moment when thalamocortical circuits re-establish the temporal scaffolding that allows experience to be sequentially organized and encoded. The experience of consciousness "beginning" may be the experience of *time beginning*, not awareness beginning.

> This is the framework's most distinctive phenomenological claim and also its most speculative. It predicts that the subjective quality of waking from deep sleep should differ from the subjective quality of emergence from anesthesia, because the former involves temporal re-coupling (consciousness re-acquiring time) while the latter involves consciousness onset (consciousness re-acquiring everything).

### Revised Conclusions

**C1.** (REVISED) The TDTS framework proposes that sleep stages are better understood as graded temporal decoupling rather than graded consciousness loss. This interpretation is:
- **Supported** by: PCI data showing preserved cortical complexity during NREM (P1), distorted temporal phenomenology in REM (P3), convergent contemplative reports of timeless awareness (P4), predictive processing's prediction of modular degradation (P5a).
- **Challenged** by: the unfalsifiability of the N3 phenomenology claim (Attack 1), the fact that detected NREM consciousness correlates with local temporal restoration (Attack 4), and the dependence on an unproven neural-to-phenomenological link (Attack 2).
- **Not original** as a thesis (Thompson 2015, Windt 2015, Metzinger 2020). The contribution is the empirical operationalization via TMS-EEG/PCI and the specific predictions below.

**C2.** The hypothesis that deep NREM involves a "non-ordered phenomenal field" (consciousness without temporal structure) is speculative. It is the framework's most distinctive and most vulnerable claim. Its value lies not in its current evidential support (which is thin) but in the testable predictions it generates.

### Revised Predictions

#### Prediction 1 (REVISED): Selective temporal impairment after NREM awakening

**Statement:** Subjects awakened from different sleep stages and tested with *difficulty-matched* task pairs -- one requiring temporal processing (temporal order judgment), one requiring non-temporal perceptual processing (spatial orientation judgment) -- should show:

> A sleep-depth x task-type interaction: the temporal task should show steeper performance degradation with increasing prior sleep depth than the non-temporal task, AFTER controlling for baseline difficulty.

**Key control (added per SHOULD FIX #3):** Tasks must be matched at baseline (waking) for accuracy and reaction time. The prediction is a *disproportionate* temporal impairment, not merely greater impairment on a harder task.

**What this tests:** Whether sleep selectively disrupts temporal processing (TDTS) or impairs all processing uniformly (standard view). A significant interaction supports TDTS; no interaction supports the standard view.

**Testability:** High. Standard serial-awakening paradigm with added task battery.

**Honest limitation:** Even a positive result does not prove consciousness-without-time in N3. It shows that temporal processing is *selectively* vulnerable during sleep, which is necessary but not sufficient for the TDTS interpretation.

#### Prediction 2 (REVISED): Meditator NREM signatures

**Statement:** *Unchanged from v1, but with added context:*

Experienced meditators (>5,000 hours practice, specifically in traditions that cultivate "objectless awareness" -- Tibetan shamatha, Theravada jhana, TM) should show, compared to meditation-naive controls during NREM N3:

> (a) Higher PCI during N3 (even if still below waking PCI).
> (b) Increased gamma power during NREM up states specifically.
> (c) Higher frequency of conscious reports upon N3 awakening.
> (d) Qualitatively distinct N3 reports: "awareness without content," "presence without time," or "luminous darkness" -- matching descriptions from their contemplative traditions -- rather than the standard fragmentary thought-like mentation.

**Existing partial evidence:** Ferrarelli et al. (2013) found increased parietal gamma during NREM in long-term meditators. This supports (b). The remaining predictions (a, c, d) are untested.

**What this tests:** Whether contemplative training produces a measurably different form of NREM consciousness. A positive result on (d) specifically would be strong evidence that "awareness without content/time" is a real phenomenological category, not a retrospective confabulation.

**Testability:** High. Meditator sleep studies exist; adding PCI measurements, within-up-state gamma analysis, and structured N3 awakening interviews is technically straightforward.

#### Prediction 3 (MOST NOVEL): Dissociable PCI decomposition in NREM vs. anesthesia

**Statement:** The TMS-EEG response during NREM and during anesthesia should be mechanistically distinguishable when decomposed into local and global components:

> Define PCI_local as the Lempel-Ziv complexity of the TMS response within the stimulated cortical region (e.g., within a 3cm radius of the TMS coil) and PCI_global as the Lempel-Ziv complexity of the response in distal cortical regions (>6cm from the coil).
>
> **NREM prediction:** PCI_local remains moderate (preserved within-up-state complexity) while PCI_global drops sharply (disrupted long-range propagation due to down-state barriers). The ratio PCI_local / PCI_global increases relative to waking.
>
> **Anesthesia prediction:** Both PCI_local and PCI_global drop. The ratio PCI_local / PCI_global remains similar to waking (because both local and global processing are uniformly impaired).

**Why this discriminates between TDTS and standard view:**
- If NREM is temporal decoupling (TDTS), local processing should be preserved while long-range temporal coordination fails. Local complexity stays up; global complexity drops.
- If NREM is uniform consciousness reduction (standard view), both local and global complexity should drop proportionally.
- Anesthesia is the control: both views predict uniform reduction, so PCI_local and PCI_global should decline together.

**Testability:** High. This is a re-analysis of existing TMS-EEG data using spatial decomposition. Massimini's group (and others) have the raw data. The analysis requires defining ROIs and computing PCI separately within each, which is technically feasible with existing tools.

**Existing partial evidence:** Pigorini et al. (2015) showed that NREM TMS responses have an initial complex local burst followed by a stereotyped slow wave, which is qualitatively consistent with the PCI_local > PCI_global prediction. But the formal decomposition has not been done.

**What this does NOT test:** This tests temporal decoupling vs. consciousness loss as a mechanism. It does NOT test whether the temporally-decoupled state involves phenomenal consciousness. A positive result is necessary but not sufficient for the full TDTS thesis.

#### Prediction 4 (NEW): Subjective quality of awakening from sleep vs. anesthesia

**Statement:** If TDTS is correct, the subjective experience of waking from deep sleep should differ qualitatively from emergence from anesthesia:

> (a) Awakening from deep sleep: subjects should report that the transition feels like "time restarting" -- a sense of continuity of self/awareness with a gap in temporal experience. "I was here, then time wasn't passing, now it is again."
>
> (b) Emergence from anesthesia: subjects should report that the transition feels like "everything restarting" -- a sense of discontinuity, of consciousness itself beginning de novo. "I wasn't here, now I am."

**Testability:** Moderate. This requires structured phenomenological interviews immediately upon awakening from N3 and immediately upon emergence from anesthesia, with the same subjects (within-subjects design to control for individual differences). The challenge is that post-awakening reports are unreliable (sleep inertia, retrograde amnesia from propofol). However, the *pattern* of reports across subjects (rather than individual reports) can be statistically analyzed.

**Honest limitation:** Verbal reports are deeply unreliable for these states. Subjects may confabulate post-hoc narratives. The prediction is about *distributional differences* in report content, not about individual reports being accurate.

### Honest Assessment

**What TDTS v2 achieves:**
1. A clearly framed interpretation of sleep as temporal decoupling, grounded in prior philosophical work (Thompson, Windt, Metzinger) and connected to the TMS-EEG/PCI empirical literature.
2. Four predictions with varying degrees of novelty and testability, the most novel being Prediction 3 (PCI decomposition).
3. Honest acknowledgment that the central phenomenological claim (consciousness-without-time during deep N3) is speculative and currently beyond direct empirical reach.

**What TDTS v2 does NOT achieve:**
1. A proof that consciousness persists during deep N3. The framework interprets existing evidence but does not provide new evidence.
2. Resolution of the fundamental hard problem: how to distinguish "consciousness without time" from "no consciousness" using third-person methods. This is a permanent limitation, not a fixable bug.
3. Novelty of the core thesis. The contribution is operational and predictive, not conceptual.

**The hard question: Is the "consciousness without time" concept coherent?**

After addressing Attack 3 via the "non-ordered phenomenal field" articulation (D6), I believe the concept is coherent but exotic. It is no more incoherent than "consciousness without color" (achromatopsia) or "consciousness without spatial structure" (possible in certain meditation states). The key move is distinguishing temporal *extension* (the state obtains over an interval) from temporal *structure* (the state contains internal ordering). A state can have the former without the latter, just as a photograph has spatial extension without temporal structure.

Whether such a state actually occurs during deep sleep is an empirical question that cannot currently be answered. The value of the framework is that it generates predictions (1-4) that can be tested regardless of whether the central phenomenological claim is true. The predictions test the *temporal decoupling mechanism*, which is the scientifically accessible part of the framework. The *phenomenological interpretation* (that temporal decoupling preserves minimal consciousness) remains a philosophical hypothesis awaiting better tools.
