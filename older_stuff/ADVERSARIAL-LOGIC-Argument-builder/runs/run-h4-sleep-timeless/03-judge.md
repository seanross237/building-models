### Attack 1 -- Unfalsifiability of "consciousness without time"

**Adversary's claim:** The thesis that deep sleep involves consciousness is unfalsifiable because any absence of evidence (no reports, no memory, no behavior) is explained away as a consequence of temporal decoupling. This is structurally identical to panpsychism's unfalsifiability.

**Ruling: PARTIALLY LANDED. Severity downgraded: Major (from Fatal).**

The adversary is right that the core claim -- consciousness persists during deep N3 -- faces a serious verification problem. If consciousness-without-time is by definition unreportable and unmemorable, then direct first-person evidence is inaccessible. This is a genuine methodological problem.

However, I downgrade from Fatal to Major for two reasons:

1. **The panpsychism analogy is too strong.** Panpsychism claims consciousness in systems with no relevant internal structure (thermostats). The TDTS claims consciousness in a system (the sleeping human cortex) that has enormous internal structure, demonstrated causal complexity during up states, and yields conscious reports 30-50% of the time during NREM awakening. The prior probability that this system is conscious is much higher than for a thermostat. The unfalsifiability problem is real but context-dependent: it is harder to verify than to falsify. If NREM N3 consistently showed zero conscious reports on awakening, zero PCI, and zero complex cortical activity, TDTS would be in trouble. The fact that NREM shows *some* of all three weakens the "no consciousness" claim as much as it weakens the "full consciousness" claim.

2. **The architect does offer indirect tests.** Prediction 3 (anesthesia vs. sleep PCI decomposition) tests whether the *mechanism* of PCI reduction differs between sleep and anesthesia. If NREM shows preserved local complexity + disrupted global integration, while anesthesia shows both disrupted, this is indirect evidence for temporal decoupling vs. consciousness loss. It is not proof of consciousness-without-time, but it discriminates between two mechanistic stories, which is the best science can do with phenomenal consciousness.

That said, the adversary correctly identifies that the *central* claim (phenomenal experience during deep N3 is timeless rather than absent) cannot be directly tested. This is a permanent limitation of the framework, not a fixable bug.

**Verdict: MUST ADDRESS.** The architect must be honest that the central claim is empirically underdetermined and shift the argument's weight toward the testable predictions. The thesis should be framed as: "TDTS is the best *interpretation* of the available evidence, not a directly testable hypothesis about N3 phenomenology."

### Attack 2 -- Equivocation between phenomenological and neural temporal structure

**Adversary's claim:** The architect conflates subjective temporal experience with neural temporal dynamics, assuming the former depends on the latter without argument.

**Ruling: PARTIALLY LANDED. Severity: Major.**

The adversary raises a genuine philosophical concern but overstates it. The relationship between neural temporal dynamics and temporal phenomenology is indeed not established by deductive argument. However:

- It is one of the best-supported correlations in consciousness science. Disruption of gamma oscillations (temporal binding) correlates with disrupted temporal perception across many paradigms (anesthesia, psychedelics, meditation). The architect is not making an unusual assumption here; they are relying on mainstream neuroscience.

- The adversary's alternative ("neural temporal dynamics are constitutive of consciousness itself") is equally unproven. Neither side has a deductive argument. The architect's assumption is at least as defensible as the adversary's counter-assumption.

The real problem is that the architect does not *flag* this as an assumption. The argument treats the neural-to-phenomenological link as established when it is a working hypothesis.

**Verdict: SHOULD FIX.** Explicitly acknowledge that the link between neural temporal dynamics and temporal phenomenology is a working assumption, not a proven fact. This does not kill the argument; it calibrates the confidence level.

### Attack 3 -- "Consciousness without time" may be incoherent

**Adversary's claim:** Phenomenal experience requires at minimum duration. A truly timeless state is either an experience with temporal structure (contradicting the claim) or no experience at all.

**Ruling: PARTIALLY LANDED. Severity: Major, but not fatal.**

This is the deepest philosophical challenge to the framework. The adversary makes a strong case drawing on the specious present literature. However, I identify several responses the architect could make:

1. **The Buddhist concept of "momentary consciousness" (kshana):** Some Buddhist philosophical traditions posit consciousness that arises and passes in a single moment, without experienced duration. This is a contested concept, but it shows that the idea of durationless experience has been seriously developed, not merely asserted.

2. **The mathematical analogy:** A function can be defined at a point without requiring an interval. Similarly, phenomenal quality might be instantiable at a moment without requiring duration. The specious present literature argues against this, but the argument is not deductive -- it is based on the phenomenology of normal waking experience, which may not generalize to altered states.

3. **The "thick present" response:** The architect could argue that consciousness-without-time does not mean instantaneous consciousness but rather consciousness with a *non-ordered* present -- a phenomenal field that has extension (it is not a point) but no internal temporal structure (no before/after within the field). This is something like a spatial field experienced all at once, with no temporal succession. This is coherent, though exotic.

The adversary's argument is strongest against a naive reading of "timeless" as "instantaneous." The architect should clarify what they mean: probably something like response (3) -- a phenomenal field without internal temporal ordering, not a zero-duration flash of experience.

**Verdict: MUST FIX.** The architect must define "consciousness without time" more precisely. The current S10 is too vague. The concept is not obviously incoherent, but it requires careful articulation.

### Attack 4 -- P7 evidence shows local temporal restoration, not persistence-without-time

**Adversary's claim:** The NREM conscious reports (Boly et al., Siclari et al.) come from local cortical zones that show *decreased* SWA -- i.e., local restoration of waking-like temporal dynamics. So the evidence shows consciousness WITH temporal structure, not consciousness WITHOUT it.

**Ruling: LANDED. Severity: Major.**

This is the adversary's strongest empirical point, and the architect must take it seriously. The data clearly shows: wherever consciousness is detected in NREM, it correlates with local de-synchronization (waking-like temporal complexity). This is a problem for the TDTS thesis.

However, I note one saving response: the architect could argue that the studies detect *reportable* NREM consciousness -- the cases where sufficient local temporal structure exists to form a memory and produce a report. The *unreportable* NREM consciousness (the hypothesized "consciousness without time") would not show up in these studies precisely because it does not involve local de-synchronization and does not get encoded into memory. This is consistent with the framework but returns us to the unfalsifiability problem (Attack 1).

The architect is in a bind: the available empirical evidence for NREM consciousness specifically supports the standard view (consciousness requires local temporal complexity), and the TDTS-specific claim (consciousness without temporal structure) is empirically inaccessible. The architect should acknowledge this honestly.

**Verdict: MUST FIX.** Acknowledge that all existing empirical evidence for NREM consciousness involves local restoration of temporal dynamics. The "consciousness without time" claim goes beyond the evidence and should be flagged as speculative.

### Attack 5 -- Multiplicative model predicts C=0 when T=0

**Adversary's claim:** If C = C_base * T_coupling and T_coupling = 0, then C = 0, which is the opposite of the thesis.

**Ruling: LANDED. Severity: Minor.**

The adversary is technically correct. The multiplicative form is inconsistent with the thesis. However, this is clearly a formalization error, not a conceptual error. The architect's intended model is something like C_experienced = f(C_base, T_coupling) where f(x, 0) = x (consciousness persists without temporal structure but is not experienceable-as-temporal). The fix is trivial: change the formalization to match the intended meaning. E.g., use C_reportable = C_base * T_coupling (low when either factor is low) while maintaining C_phenomenal = C_base (independent of T_coupling).

**Verdict: SHOULD FIX.** Trivial repair. Separate reportable/accessible consciousness from phenomenal consciousness in the formalization.

### Attack 6 -- IIT misread: Phi requires temporal transitions

**Adversary's claim:** The architect claims IIT supports consciousness without temporal structure, but IIT's Phi is computed over a transition probability matrix, which is fundamentally temporal. Disrupting temporal transitions disrupts Phi.

**Ruling: LANDED. Severity: Major.**

The adversary is correct about the technical structure of IIT. Phi is computed from transition probabilities, which presuppose temporal evolution. The architect's claim that "a system can have high Phi in a single frozen state" is incorrect within IIT's mathematical framework.

However, I note a subtlety: the architect could retreat to a *modified* IIT-inspired position. The cause-effect structure of the cortex during an NREM up state (which lasts ~500ms) is a temporal structure, albeit brief. During each up state, there is a well-defined TPM with potentially high Phi. The architect's claim could be reformulated as: during NREM, consciousness flickers on during up states (with local Phi) and off during down states (with degenerate Phi). This is the "fragmented consciousness" interpretation of NREM, which some IIT researchers have explored (Tononi & Massimini 2008).

But this "flickering consciousness during up states" interpretation is *different* from "continuous timeless consciousness." It replaces the architect's thesis with a more conventional one: intermittent consciousness during NREM, correlated with up states. The architect must choose between the IIT-compatible interpretation (flickering, temporally structured consciousness during up states) and the TDTS thesis (continuous timeless consciousness). They are not the same.

**Verdict: MUST FIX.** Either abandon IIT as a theoretical support and find a different theoretical grounding, or modify the thesis to the "flickering consciousness during up states" interpretation, which is IIT-compatible but is not "consciousness without time."

### Attack 7 -- Prediction 1 does not discriminate from standard view

**Adversary's claim:** Steeper degradation of temporal tasks after deep sleep is predicted by any model where deeper sleep = greater cognitive impairment, because temporal tasks are harder.

**Ruling: LANDED. Severity: Minor.**

The adversary is correct that a difficulty confound exists. However, the fix is straightforward: use tasks matched for difficulty at baseline (during waking). If temporal order judgment and non-temporal perceptual detection are calibrated to the same accuracy level during waking, and temporal order judgment degrades more steeply with sleep depth, the difficulty confound is controlled.

The architect should have specified difficulty-matched controls. This is a design weakness, not a fatal flaw.

**Verdict: SHOULD FIX.** Add difficulty-matching to the experimental design. The prediction remains interesting with this control.

**Adversary's verdict:** NOT NOVEL. Thompson (2015), Windt (2015), Metzinger (2020), and contemplative traditions have all proposed this.

**Judge's evaluation:** The adversary is correct, and this is a significant finding. The architect did not cite any of this prior art, which undermines the presentation's scholarly credibility. - Metzinger's MPE research program (2020) formalizes the notion of "pure awareness" as a minimal phenomenal state that may persist during deep sleep.

The architect's specific contribution -- mapping this onto the TMS-EEG/PCI literature and proposing sleep stages as a "temporal coupling spectrum" -- adds empirical grounding to existing philosophical frameworks. This is a genuine contribution but it is an *extension* of established ideas, not a new thesis.

**Judge's novelty verdict on core thesis: NOT NOVEL. The architect must cite the prior art and clearly distinguish what is being added.**
