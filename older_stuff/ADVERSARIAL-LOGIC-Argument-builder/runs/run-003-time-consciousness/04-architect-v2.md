# Architect v2: The Relationship Between Time and Consciousness (Revised)

**Agent:** Architect (v2)
**Run:** 003-time-consciousness
**Date:** 2026-03-25

---

## Theory: Temporal Constitution Theory (TCT) — Revised

**Core Claim (unchanged):** Time and consciousness are neither identical nor independent. Consciousness is *constituted by* temporal structure (making time more fundamental), while the *phenomenal character* of time — its felt passage, directionality, and thickness — is constituted by consciousness. They are asymmetrically co-dependent: time can exist without consciousness, but consciousness cannot exist without time.

---

## I. DEFINITIONS

> **[FIX 2 — D3 Definitional Circularity]** The definition of consciousness has been stripped of substantive theoretical conditions. Temporal extension and integration are now premises (P4, P5), not definitional criteria. This ensures P4 is a non-trivial claim.

**D1. Physical Time (T_phys):** The timelike dimension of the spacetime manifold described by physics — characterized by: (a) a metric structure allowing duration measurement, (b) a partial ordering of events via light-cone structure, (c) no privileged "now," no objective passage, and no intrinsic arrow at the fundamental level. Under the block universe interpretation, all events at all times exist equally as parts of a four-dimensional structure.

**D2. Phenomenal Time (T_phen):** The experiential dimension of temporal consciousness — the felt quality of "now-ness," passage, duration, succession, and the asymmetry between remembered past and anticipated future.

**D3. Consciousness (C):** A system S is conscious at interval I iff there is something it is like to be S during I — i.e., S instantiates phenomenal properties during I. *(Minimal Nagelian definition only. No further conditions are included in the definition; necessary conditions for consciousness are stated as premises below and are subject to empirical and theoretical challenge.)*

> **[FIX 3 — Constitution Relation]** D4 has been split into a definition of constitution and a separate Asymmetry Principle (AP1) that makes explicit how asymmetry is established.

**D4. Constitution:** X is constituted by Y iff: (i) Y provides necessary structural conditions for X's existence, (ii) X cannot exist without Y, (iii) X is not identical to Y — X has properties that Y lacks, and (iv) Y's existence and character are explanatorily prior to X's existence and character.

**AP1. Asymmetry Principle:** The constitution relation in D4 is asymmetric when supplemented by an *independent existence claim*: if Y can exist without X, but X cannot exist without Y, then the constitution is one-directional (Y constitutes X, not vice versa). The asymmetry of TCT's chain is not built into D4 but is established by P1 (T_phys can exist without C) and P7 (T_phen cannot exist without C).

**D5. The Specious Present (SP):** The minimum temporal window over which conscious experience is integrated — empirically estimated at approximately 2-3 seconds for the "experienced present," with finer-grained temporal binding at approximately 30-50ms intervals.

**D6. Temporal Phenomenology (TP):** The set of phenomenal properties specifically related to time: felt passage, duration, simultaneity, succession, the open-future/fixed-past asymmetry, and the sense of "now."

> **[FIX 1 — Block Universe / Process Language]** Added D7 to explicitly define how "process" and "temporal extension" are to be understood within the block universe.

**D7. Temporally Extended Pattern (TEP):** In block-universe terms, a *temporally extended pattern* is a four-dimensional structure in the spacetime manifold that spans a non-zero interval along the timelike dimension and exhibits structured, information-carrying relations between its temporal parts. When this document uses words like "process," "integration," or "temporal extension," these refer to TEPs — static four-dimensional structures with certain properties — not to dynamic becoming. The "extension" is geometric, not processual.

## II. PREMISES

### Framework Assumptions

> **[FIX 6 — Acknowledge Physicalism as Framework Assumption]** P3 is now explicitly labeled as a framework assumption. A note on alternative frameworks is included.

**FA1. [Physicalism — Framework Assumption]** This theory is developed within a physicalist framework: consciousness is a physical phenomenon instantiated by configurations of matter/energy in spacetime. This is adopted as a working framework grounded in the success of neuroscience, causal closure considerations, and parsimony — not as a proven metaphysical truth. *Note on alternatives:* Under property dualism, C1 would need modification (consciousness would be constituted by temporal structure but have irreducible phenomenal aspects). Under panpsychism, P7 would need revision (see FA2). Under idealism, the entire chain reverses. TCT is most naturally a physicalist theory but its core structural insight (the T_phys/T_phen distinction and asymmetric linking) may be adaptable to other frameworks.

> **[FIX 7 — Acknowledge Panpsychism]** Added FA2 as an explicit acknowledgment.

**FA2. [Anti-Panpsychism — Framework Assumption]** This theory assumes that phenomenal properties are not fundamental features of all physical systems. Micro-phenomenal temporal experience is not posited. Under panpsychism, the chain would change: T_phen might be as fundamental as T_phys (or even identical to it at the micro level), and the C → T_phen direction of constitution would be reversed or eliminated. TCT is incompatible with strong panpsychism and would need significant restructuring under that framework.

### Ontological Premises

**P1. [Block Universe]** Physical time is best described by the block universe model: all events at all times exist equally as parts of a four-dimensional manifold. There is no objective "now," no objective passage, and no ontologically privileged present moment. *(Grounded in: special and general relativity's frame-dependence of simultaneity; no physical variable tracks "the present.")*

**P2. [Entropy Arrow]** The apparent directionality of physical time is grounded in the entropy gradient: the universe began in a low-entropy state (Past Hypothesis), and entropy increases in the direction of the temporal dimension we label "future." This entropic asymmetry is the only physically grounded arrow of time. *(Grounded in: second law of thermodynamics, statistical mechanics, the Past Hypothesis.)*

> **[FIX 1 — Block Universe Compatibility]** P4 and P5 are reformulated using D7's TEP language, eliminating process/dynamic vocabulary.

**P4. [Consciousness Requires Temporal Extension]** No single three-dimensional hyperplane (time-slice) of the spacetime manifold is sufficient for consciousness. Consciousness corresponds to temporally extended patterns (TEPs, per D7) — four-dimensional structures that span a non-zero interval along the timelike dimension. A single spatial configuration, no matter how complex, does not instantiate consciousness; what instantiates consciousness is a TEP with specific information-carrying relations between its temporal parts.

*Grounding:* (a) Neural correlates of consciousness are dynamic patterns (oscillations, recurrent processing, global ignition), all of which are TEPs. (b) Phenomenal content — even an apparently static visual experience — involves ongoing recurrent processing between cortical areas, which is a TEP property. (c) Under functionalism: the relevant "computational state" is not a single register snapshot but includes the *transition function* — the pattern of state-to-state relations — which is a TEP property in the block.

> **[FIX 10 — Functionalist Objections]** P4 now explicitly engages with the functionalist counterargument: the computational state relevant to consciousness includes transition relations, which are four-dimensional, not instantaneous.

**P5. [Consciousness Requires Informational Integration Across Temporal Parts]** Consciousness corresponds to TEPs in which information is integrated across temporally distinct parts of the structure — i.e., the later temporal parts of the TEP carry information about the earlier temporal parts in a way that creates a unified structure rather than a mere sequence of unrelated slices. *(Grounded in: Integrated Information Theory's core insight, the unity of consciousness, the binding problem.)*

**P6. [Phenomenal Time Is Real]** Conscious beings instantiate genuine temporal phenomenology: felt passage, duration, succession, now-ness, and temporal asymmetry. These phenomenal properties are real even if they do not correspond one-to-one with features of T_phys. *(Grounded in: the undeniability of first-person temporal experience; even if temporal phenomenology misrepresents physics, the phenomenology itself exists.)*

### Bridging Premises

**P7. [No Phenomenal Time Without Consciousness — under physicalist framework]** Temporal phenomenology (TP) exists only where consciousness exists. Under the physicalist anti-panpsychist framework adopted here (FA1, FA2), non-conscious systems (rocks, thermostats, empty spacetime regions) have temporal location and temporal structure but no temporal experience. *(This is a framework-dependent claim. Under panpsychism, it would be revised or abandoned. See FA2.)*

**P8. [No Consciousness Without Physical Time]** Consciousness cannot be instantiated outside of physical time. A structure that exists at a single point (with zero temporal extension) in the block cannot be conscious. *(Follows from P4.)*

> **[FIX 4 — P9a Falsification / Temporal Illusions]** P9 has been substantially revised. Sub-claim (a) is weakened to accommodate temporal illusions, and an account of divergence is provided.

**P9. [Temporal Structure Shapes Phenomenal Content — Revised]**
The phenomenal character of temporal experience is shaped by, but not identical to, the physical temporal structure of the underlying TEP:

(a) **Ordering (weakened):** Phenomenal temporal ordering *generally* mirrors physical temporal ordering but diverges systematically in well-characterized cases. Divergences arise when the neural mechanisms of temporal binding operate on integration windows that span multiple physical events, forcing reconstruction — as in the flash-lag effect (motion extrapolation), the color-phi phenomenon (retrospective interpolation), and postdictive effects like the cutaneous rabbit (spatial-temporal filling-in). These are not counterexamples to TCT but predicted consequences: if T_phen is constituted by consciousness (which operates on integration windows per D5), then T_phen reflects the *output of neural temporal processing*, not raw T_phys. Temporal illusions demonstrate the constitution relation in action.

(b) **Duration (covariance):** Felt duration covaries with but is not identical to physical duration; the relationship follows psychophysical laws (e.g., scalar timing, Weber-like sensitivity). The systematic relationship is well-documented; its imperfection is predicted by the theory (T_phen ≠ T_phys).

(c) **Arrow:** The felt arrow of time corresponds to the entropic arrow via the mechanism described in P10. See also the caveats under P10's revision.

**P10. [Memory Asymmetry Grounds Felt Arrow — Revised]**

> **[FIX 8 — Entropy Arrow Incompleteness]** P10 now explicitly acknowledges the hard problem gap, distinguishes causal from thermodynamic arrows, and addresses entropy reversal.

The felt asymmetry between past and future in conscious experience is *partially* grounded in the physical asymmetry of memory formation: memories are traces of past states, formed via entropy-increasing processes. We have memories of lower-entropy states and not higher-entropy states because memory formation is thermodynamically irreversible.

**Caveats and limitations:**

(i) *Hard problem gap:* P10 explains the *direction* and *structure* of felt temporal asymmetry but not *why felt passage exists at all* — why there is something it is like to experience time flowing. This is an instance of the hard problem of consciousness applied to temporal phenomenology. TCT does not claim to solve the hard problem; it claims to provide the structural account of temporal phenomenology's architecture within the physicalist framework. The hard problem remains open and applies to all phenomenal properties, not uniquely to temporal ones.

(ii) *Causal vs. thermodynamic arrow:* The felt causal structure of experience (causes precede effects) and the thermodynamic arrow (entropy increases toward the future) are related but not identical. The thermodynamic arrow may ground the causal arrow (via the asymmetry of records and traces), but this grounding is itself debated. TCT takes no strong position on the causal-arrow question beyond noting the connection.

(iii) *Entropy reversal edge case:* In a hypothetical local entropy-decreasing region, TCT predicts that a conscious system (if one could exist there) would form "memories" of higher-entropy states and "anticipate" lower-entropy states — effectively experiencing a reversed felt arrow. This is a strange but internally consistent prediction. Whether such a being would be *possible* (given that the complex structures needed for consciousness may require entropy-increasing contexts) is an open question.

**P11. [The Specious Present Has a Physical Basis]** The specious present corresponds to the temporal integration window of the TEP underlying consciousness. This window is set by physical/neural timescales (approximately 30ms for fine-grained binding, approximately 2-3s for the experienced present). *(Grounded in: EEG oscillatory dynamics, temporal binding experiments, Poppel's 3-second window.)*

> **[FIX 5 — Meditation and Timelessness]** Added P12 to address the case of consciousness without temporal phenomenology.

**P12. [Temporal Structure vs. Temporal Phenomenology Are Separable]** Consciousness requires temporal structure (P4: it must be a TEP) but does not always produce rich temporal *phenomenology*. Temporal phenomenology — the felt sense of passage, duration, and succession — can be attenuated or absent even while consciousness persists, provided the underlying TEP continues. Evidence: experienced meditators report states where the sense of passage and duration ceases while awareness persists; certain anesthetic and pharmacological states produce "timeless" conscious experiences; flow states compress felt duration. In these cases, the *temporal structure* of the conscious process (the TEP) continues — the neural oscillations, recurrent processing, and temporal integration persist — but the *phenomenology of time* is diminished or transformed.

This means C6 (consciousness is fundamentally temporal) must be stated precisely: consciousness fundamentally requires *temporal structure* (the TEP), not *temporal phenomenology*. The two can come apart.

**Derivation 1: Consciousness is constituted by temporal structure.**

    From FA1:        Consciousness is a physical phenomenon.

    ∴ (C1):          Consciousness is constituted by physical temporal structure.

> **[FIX 5 — Meditation]** Derivation 2 now uses P12 to strengthen the asymmetry: C can exist without full T_phen (meditation states), but T_phen cannot exist without C. This actually *strengthens* the asymmetry rather than undermining it, as the adversary feared.

**Derivation 3: Time and consciousness are asymmetrically related.**

    From C1:         C is constituted by T_phys.
    From C2:         T_phen is constituted by C.
    From AP1 + P1:   T_phys can exist without C (the block universe without

    ∴ (C3):          The dependence chain is: T_phys → C → T_phen.
                     Each arrow is asymmetric (the constituted entity cannot
                     exist without the constituting entity, but not vice versa).
                     Physical time is most fundamental; phenomenal time is least

**Derivation 4: They are not the same phenomenon.**

    From D1:         T_phys has no passage, no now, no intrinsic arrow.
    From D2:         T_phen has passage, now-ness, felt arrow.
    From D3:         C has phenomenal properties (qualia generally).
    These three have distinct property profiles.
    By Leibniz's Law: entities with different properties are not identical.
    ∴ (C4):          T_phys, C, and T_phen are three distinct (though
                     constitutively linked) phenomena.

> **[FIX 5b — Dual Aspect]** The Architect notes: this argument refutes *strict identity* between any two of {T_phys, C, T_phen}. It does not refute dual-aspect monism, which holds that two descriptions can pick out the same underlying reality while attributing different properties. TCT is compatible with (but does not require) the view that T_phys and T_phen are two aspects of a deeper neutral reality. The asymmetric constitution chain would then describe the structural relationship between the aspects, not between distinct substances.

**Derivation 5: The "flow" of time is a construct of conscious TEPs.**

    From P1:         T_phys has no objective flow or passage.
    From P6:         The felt "flow" is a genuine phenomenal property.
    From P7:         This phenomenal property requires consciousness.
    From P10:        The directional character of felt flow is grounded in
                     memory asymmetry, itself grounded in the entropy gradient.
    From P10(i):     The *existence* of felt passage (as opposed to its
                     direction) is not explained by the entropy account —
                     this is an instance of the hard problem.
    ∴ (C5):          The "flow" of time is not a feature of T_phys but a
                     feature of T_phen, constituted by conscious TEPs operating
                     along the entropy gradient. We can explain its *structure*
                     (direction, asymmetry) but not its *phenomenal existence*
                     within the current framework. The flow depends on conscious
                     TEPs; but these TEPs themselves depend on T_phys.

**Derivation 6: Consciousness is fundamentally temporal (in structure, not necessarily in phenomenology).**

    From P4:         Consciousness requires temporal extension (TEP).
    From P5:         Consciousness requires integration across temporal parts.
    From P11:        The character of consciousness is shaped by its temporal
                     integration window.
    From P12:        However, temporal *phenomenology* can be diminished while

    ∴ (C6-revised):  Consciousness is fundamentally temporal in *structure* —

                     The conflation of these questions is the source of much

## IV. CONCLUSIONS

**C1. Asymmetric Constitution:** T_phys → C → T_phen. Physical time constitutes consciousness; consciousness constitutes phenomenal time. The chain is asymmetric, grounded in independent-existence claims (AP1), and non-circular.

**C2. Time is more fundamental:** Physical time is ontologically prior to consciousness under the physicalist framework. Consciousness cannot exist without temporal structure (it must be a TEP), but temporal structure can exist without consciousness.

**C3. Phenomenal time is a product of consciousness:** Felt passage, the experienced "now," subjective duration — these are real phenomenal properties generated by conscious TEPs, not features of the block universe itself.

**C4. Consciousness is structurally temporal:** Temporality is constitutive of consciousness at the *structural* level (no TEP, no consciousness). But temporal *phenomenology* is a typical product of consciousness, not a necessary one — as demonstrated by timeless conscious states in meditation.

**C5. They are not identical:** Time (T_phys), consciousness (C), and the experience of time (T_phen) are three distinct phenomena linked by asymmetric constitution. This is compatible with but does not require dual-aspect monism.

**C6. The "flow" is real but phenomenal, and partially explained:** The direction and structure of felt flow are explained by the entropy gradient and memory asymmetry. The phenomenal existence of felt flow remains a hard-problem residuum.

**C7. Framework dependence is explicit:** TCT is developed within physicalism and anti-panpsychism. Its core structural insight (the three-way distinction and asymmetric linking) may survive in modified form under alternative frameworks, but the specific asymmetry claims depend on the framework.

## V. EMPIRICAL PREDICTIONS

**EP1. [Subjective Time Distortion]** Manipulations of conscious processing speed/integration should alter felt duration without altering physical duration. *Confirmed.* (Time dilation under fear, flow states, psychoactive substances, meditation.)

**EP2. [Unconscious Processing Is Temporally Structured But Not Temporally Experienced]** Unconscious neural processes should exhibit temporal structure (as TEPs in T_phys) but should not generate temporal phenomenology. *Confirmed.* (Subliminal priming, blindsight.)

**EP3. [Disrupting Temporal Integration Should Disrupt Consciousness]** Disrupting the neural mechanisms of temporal binding should disrupt consciousness. *Confirmed.* (Anesthesia disrupts cortical integration; seizures disrupt temporal binding and produce unconsciousness.)

**EP4. [Specious Present Varies With Neural Timescales]** Organisms with different neural timescales should have different specious presents. *Partially confirmed.* (Flicker fusion rate variation across species.)

> **[FIX 9 — EP5 Reclassified]** EP5 has been moved to the Conceptual Commitments section.

**EP5. [Temporal Illusions Reflect Integration Windows]** Temporal illusions (flash-lag, color-phi, postdiction, temporal order reversals) should correlate with the temporal integration windows of the underlying neural TEP. Specifically: illusion magnitude should scale with integration window duration, and manipulations that alter window duration (e.g., attentional load, arousal) should alter illusion magnitude. *Partially confirmed.* (Some evidence that attentional state modulates temporal order judgment thresholds.)

**EP6. [Memory Manipulation Alters Felt Arrow]** Manipulations that disrupt memory asymmetry should alter the felt directionality of time. *Partially confirmed.* (Korsakoff's syndrome, hippocampal damage produce disrupted temporal orientation.)

**EP7. [No Objective Passage in Physics]** Physics will continue to find no objective "now" or passage in fundamental equations, consistent with T_phen being a product of consciousness rather than a feature of T_phys. *Ongoing confirmation.*

**EP8. [Meditation Dissociates Temporal Structure from Temporal Phenomenology]** Advanced meditators reporting "timeless" states should show *preserved* markers of temporal integration in neural activity (ongoing oscillations, recurrent processing) even while *reporting* absence of temporal phenomenology. This is a novel prediction: if P12 is correct, the TEP persists even when T_phen is attenuated. *Testable and not yet systematically tested.*

## VI. CONCEPTUAL COMMITMENTS (Not Directly Testable)

> **[FIX 9]** This section collects philosophical commitments that are not empirical predictions but are important for the theory's identity.

**CC1. [No Instantaneous Consciousness]** A single time-slice of a brain (or any system) does not instantiate consciousness. Consciousness requires a TEP. This distinguishes TCT from theories that identify consciousness with instantaneous computational states.

**CC2. [Framework Dependence]** The asymmetric constitution chain T_phys → C → T_phen holds under physicalism. Under idealism, the chain reverses. Under panpsychism, the C → T_phen link is modified. TCT does not claim framework-neutral truth.

**CC3. [Hard Problem Acknowledged]** TCT explains the *structure* of temporal phenomenology (its direction, its integration windows, its covariance with physical time) but not its *existence as phenomenology*. This is a limitation shared with all physicalist theories of consciousness.

## VIII. THEORY SUMMARY (v2)

The revised Temporal Constitution Theory resolves the time-consciousness puzzle through five moves:

1. **Distinguish T_phys from T_phen** — stop treating "time" as univocal.
2. **Establish asymmetric constitution** via explicit Asymmetry Principle (AP1) — T_phys → C → T_phen.
3. **Reformulate in block-compatible language** — consciousness corresponds to Temporally Extended Patterns (TEPs), eliminating the process/block tension.
4. **Separate temporal structure from temporal phenomenology** — consciousness requires the former but not always the latter, as demonstrated by meditation and timelessness states.
5. **Be explicit about framework dependence** — TCT is a physicalist, anti-panpsychist theory; its asymmetry claims depend on these commitments.

The theory now addresses all five MUST FIX items and all five SHOULD FIX items identified by the Judge. It is stronger than v1 in its logical foundations, more honest about its limitations, and more empirically engaged (adding EP8 as a novel testable prediction).
