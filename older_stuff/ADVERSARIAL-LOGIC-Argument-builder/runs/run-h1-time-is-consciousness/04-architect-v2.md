## 1. Definitions

**D1. Phenomenal consciousness (P-consciousness).** The property of a system such that there is something it is like to be that system. (Ostensive pointer, not reductive definition.)

**D2a. Temporal structure — fundamental (T-fund).** The timelike dimension of spacetime and its properties: ordering, metric, dimensionality, symmetry. This is the temporal structure described by fundamental physics (relativity, quantum mechanics). T-fund exists everywhere there are timelike relations — i.e., cosmologically ubiquitous.

**D2b. Temporal structure — systemic (T-sys).** The temporal dynamics of a particular physical system: the patterns of change, oscillation, coordination, and causal influence that the system's parts exhibit over time. T-sys is an INSTANCE of T-fund — it is a particular physical system's trajectory through (and organization within) the fundamental temporal dimension. Neural temporal dynamics (oscillatory coupling, phase relationships, sequential activation) are T-sys. T-sys inherits its temporal character from T-fund: neural oscillations are temporal because they unfold along the timelike dimension.

**D2c. Temporal phenomenology (T-phen).** The experiential character of time as lived: felt passage, nowness, duration, temporal thickness. T-phen is the intrinsic nature of T-sys in systems with sufficiently high OTC.

**Ontological hierarchy:** T-fund is the fundamental level. T-sys is organized T-fund. T-phen is the intrinsic nature of organized T-sys (= organized T-fund). The term "temporal structure" in the premises and derivations always refers to a specific level, marked explicitly.

**D3. Structural description.** A description specifying only relational and dispositional properties. (Unchanged from v1.)

**D4. Intrinsic nature.** The non-relational, non-dispositional character of what bears a structure. (Unchanged from v1.)

**D5. Dual-aspect identity.** Two descriptions (A, B) refer to the same reality R: A captures R's structural properties; B captures R's intrinsic nature. (Unchanged from v1.)

**D6. Organized Temporal Complexity (OTC).** A conceptual measure (not yet formalized) of the degree to which a system's T-sys exhibits:
- **(a) Temporal integration:** Parts constrain each other's temporal evolution via recurrent causal coupling (not merely feedforward influence).
- **(b) Temporal differentiation:** The system occupies one of a vast repertoire of distinguishable temporal trajectories (high entropy over temporal state-space).
- **(c) Temporal self-reference:** The system's current temporal trajectory is causally shaped by representations of its own prior temporal states (working memory, prediction, temporal modeling).

OTC = f(integration, differentiation, self-reference), where f is monotonically increasing in each argument. **Status: conceptual, not formalized.** The formalization of OTC — defining the metric precisely, specifying f, and establishing computable algorithms — is the theory's most important open technical problem. Directional constraints: OTC should correlate with PCI (Perturbational Complexity Index), should be higher in waking than anesthetized brains, and should track temporal binding window measurements.

**D7. Protoconscious temporal character (PTC).** The minimal intrinsic nature of any instance of T-fund, in virtue of being temporal. PTC is not experience; it is the intrinsic-nature "raw material" out of which experience is constituted when organized. (Panprotopsychism about the temporal, not panpsychism.)

**D8. The perspectival divide.** The distinction between third-person access (structural descriptions) and first-person access (intrinsic nature). Taken as a fundamental feature of reality — specifically, a feature of how one reality can be accessed through different modes. NOT a gap between two substances; NOT merely an epistemic limitation.

**D9. Temporal pattern.** A specific trajectory through T-sys state-space — a particular sequence of temporal relationships among a system's parts, characterized by its integration topology, its differentiation profile, and its self-referential structure. Different temporal patterns are distinguished by their organizational geometry, not by their substrate.

## 2. Premises

### Ontological Premises

**P1. Ontic structural realism about physics.** Physics describes structural/relational properties of reality. The content of physical theories is exhausted by structure. (Unchanged from v1.)

**P2. Intrinsic nature realism.** Structural descriptions require a bearer. There exists an intrinsic nature to physical reality not captured by structural descriptions. This intrinsic nature is the same reality, accessed differently — not a separate substance. (Unchanged from v1.)

**P3. Anti-eliminativism.** P-consciousness exists. (Unchanged from v1.)

**P4. Physical causal closure.** Every physical event has a sufficient physical cause. (Unchanged from v1.)

### The Core Identity Premises

**P5. Temporal intrinsic nature thesis (REVISED).** The intrinsic nature of temporal structure (at all levels: T-fund, T-sys) is protoconscious temporal character (PTC).

*Justification (strengthened per judge mandate):*

- **(a) Constitutive universality:** Among all physical properties, temporality is uniquely constitutively present in ALL experience and constitutively absent from NONE. Spatial phenomenology can be absent (a being could have only auditory experience, with no spatial character). Electromagnetic, gravitational, and strong/weak-nuclear properties have no known necessary link to any aspect of experience. But temporal properties — succession, duration, change — are present in every experience whatsoever. Remove temporality from experience and nothing recognizable as experience remains. If any physical property is the experiential ground, temporality is the uniquely motivated candidate.

- **(b) Two-gap unification:** The hard problem of consciousness ("why does experience exist?") and the passage problem ("why does time seem to flow when physics describes a static block?") share formal structure: both posit an explanatory gap between a structural description and an apparently real qualitative feature. Identifying the two gaps reduces the mystery count from two to one. This is defended as theoretical virtue — parsimony — which is not conclusive but is probative when the alternative is leaving both gaps open.

- **(c) Axiom status:** P5 is a foundational postulate, not a derived theorem. It is defended on grounds of theoretical virtue (constitutive universality, parsimony, explanatory scope), not on grounds of deductive proof or direct empirical evidence. It stands or falls with the theory it supports.

**P6. Constitution by organization (REVISED).** Full P-consciousness is constituted by the organization of PTC into sufficiently high OTC. PTC is the raw material; OTC is the architectural condition.

*Constraints on the combination mechanism (new):*
- **Constraint 1 (Unity):** When PTC-bearing temporal processes are causally integrated (high temporal integration in D6a), their intrinsic natures compose into a unified experiential field. The integration IS the composition — there is no separate "combination step." From the structural side, we see causal coupling; from the intrinsic-nature side, that coupling IS experiential unification.
- **Constraint 2 (Diversity):** When the unified experiential field has high temporal differentiation (D6b), the experience is rich and variegated. Low differentiation yields dim, undifferentiated experience.
- **Constraint 3 (Self-awareness):** When the unified experiential field includes self-referential temporal modeling (D6c), the experience includes awareness of itself as experience — i.e., the system knows (experientially) that it is having an experience.

*Honest acknowledgment:* These constraints specify WHAT the combination mechanism must achieve, not HOW it achieves it at the intrinsic-nature level. The full mechanism remains an open problem. TIT shares this limitation with all panprotopsychist theories.

**P7. Perspectival identity.** T-sys (third-person description) and T-phen (first-person experience) are related by dual-aspect identity: they are the same reality accessed through different modes. T-sys is what T-phen looks like from the structural side; T-phen is what T-sys is from the intrinsic-nature side. (Clarified: this applies to systems with high OTC. In systems with low OTC, T-sys has PTC rather than full T-phen.)

### Boundary Premises

**P8. The OTC threshold.** There exists a threshold OTC* such that systems with OTC >= OTC* are P-conscious. The transition may be gradual. OTC* is an empirical matter to be discovered. (Unchanged from v1 in substance; clarified that the threshold is empirical.)

**P9. Scope of PTC.** PTC is present wherever T-fund exists — cosmologically ubiquitous. P-consciousness is rare (confined to high-OTC systems). (Unchanged from v1.)

**P10. Temporal phenomenology tracks brain-internal temporal structure (REVISED).** T-phen is the intrinsic nature of the brain's own T-sys — not a representation of external temporal structure. The brain's T-sys may or may not accurately track external temporal ordering. When it does, T-phen accurately reflects external time. When it does not (temporal illusions, time distortions), T-phen reflects the brain's own temporal dynamics, which have diverged from external time. T-phen is never "wrong" about the brain's own temporal structure — it IS that structure, from the inside. It can be "wrong" about external time, because it is not directly about external time.

### Qualia Premise (NEW)

**P11. Temporal pattern theory of qualia.** The qualitative character of a specific conscious experience (quale) is constituted by the specific temporal pattern (D9) instantiated in the relevant brain subsystem. Different qualia correspond to different temporal patterns. The mapping is: the quale IS the intrinsic nature of that temporal pattern.

*Justification:*
- **(a)** All neural representations are temporal patterns. Color is encoded by temporal firing patterns in V4 (spike timing, oscillatory phase, population temporal dynamics). Pain is encoded by temporal patterns in the anterior cingulate and insula. Spatial relations are encoded by temporal phase relationships between hippocampal place cells and grid cells. There is no neural representation that is not a temporal pattern.
- **(b)** Different qualia correspond to different temporal patterns — different organizational geometries of neural temporal activity. The redness of red IS the intrinsic nature of the specific temporal pattern that V4 instantiates when processing red light. The sharpness of pain IS the intrinsic nature of the specific temporal pattern that pain circuits instantiate.
- **(c)** This does NOT explain WHY this temporal pattern generates redness rather than blueness (the qualia-mapping problem). TIT acknowledges this as an open problem. The claim is structural: different temporal patterns -> different qualia. The specific mapping — which pattern corresponds to which quale — is an empirical and theoretical question that TIT identifies but does not answer. This is analogous to IIT identifying qualia with Phi-geometry without explaining why geometry X is redness rather than blueness.
- **(d)** The explanatory advantage over rival theories: TIT explains why ALL qualia have temporal microstructure (because they ARE temporal patterns from the inside), which is an empirical fact that other theories treat as incidental.

## 3. Logical Derivations

### Derivation 1: What consciousness is (REVISED)

From P5, P6, P7, D2a-D2c:

**Step 1:** T-fund (the timelike dimension) has PTC as its intrinsic nature (P5).
**Step 2:** T-sys (a physical system's temporal dynamics) is an instance of T-fund — it is organized temporal structure (D2b).
**Step 3:** Therefore, T-sys inherits PTC from T-fund: the intrinsic nature of a system's temporal dynamics is PTC (P5, Step 2).
**Step 4:** When T-sys achieves high OTC, this organized PTC constitutes P-consciousness (P6).
**Step 5:** The third-person structural description of this high-OTC T-sys is a complex physical system with rich temporal dynamics — i.e., a brain (P1, D3).
**Step 6:** The first-person intrinsic nature of this high-OTC T-sys is phenomenal consciousness (Steps 3-4, P7).
**Step 7:** Steps 5 and 6 describe the same thing (D5, P7): the brain's temporal dynamics and the mind's experience are one reality, two modes of access.

**Conclusion C1:** Consciousness is the intrinsic nature of sufficiently organized temporal structure. The brain's temporal dynamics (T-sys, structural description) and phenomenal experience (T-phen, intrinsic nature) are one reality known two ways. The "explanatory gap" between them is a gap between modes of access, not between substances.

*Note: the explicit hierarchy T-fund -> T-sys -> T-phen eliminates the equivocation identified in Attack 2. Each step specifies which level of temporal structure is in play.*

### Derivation 2: Why consciousness is always temporal (unchanged in substance)

From P5, P6, D2c:

**Conclusion C2:** Every conscious experience is inherently temporal. Atemporal consciousness is metaphysically impossible — it would be experience without the temporal intrinsic nature that constitutes experience.

### Derivation 3: Why physics cannot find consciousness (REVISED)

From P1, P5, D3, D4:

**Conclusion C3 (revised):** The gap between physics and consciousness is perspectival, not substance-dualist. Physics describes the structural/relational side of temporal reality; experience IS the intrinsic-nature side. They do not compete; they are complementary modes of access to the same world. The gap is real (there really are two modes of access — D8) but it is not a gap between two kinds of stuff.

### Derivation 4: Why time seems to pass (REVISED with block-universe argument)

From P5, P7, P10, D5:

**Step 1:** In the block universe, temporal ordering is a set of static relations: A is earlier than B is earlier than C.
**Step 2:** This temporal ordering, as structural description, is indeed "static" — all-at-once, no privileged "now," no transition.
**Step 3:** But dual-aspect identity (D5) does not require both aspects to share all formal properties. The structural description is characterized by mathematical properties (static, relational, all-at-once). The intrinsic nature is characterized by experiential properties (dynamic, qualitative, perspectival). These characterizations CAN differ — just as a musical score is spatially static but the music it describes is temporally dynamic. The score and the music have different formal properties; this does not make them descriptions of different things.
**Step 4:** The intrinsic nature of temporal ordering IS experientially dynamic — it is what ordering feels like from the inside. The ordering A < B < C, from the inside, IS the experience of A giving way to B giving way to C. The "giving way" is not an additional physical event; it is the intrinsic character of the ordering relation itself.
**Step 5:** "Passage" is therefore real — it is the intrinsic nature of temporal ordering (P5, P7). It is not found by physics because physics accesses only the structural side (P1). It is found by experience because experience IS the intrinsic-nature side.

**Conclusion C4:** Felt passage and the block universe are compatible. The block universe describes temporal ordering structurally (static, all-at-once). Felt passage describes the same ordering intrinsically (dynamic, qualitative). Both are correct. The apparent conflict arises from assuming that structural properties (static) constrain intrinsic properties (dynamic) — but dual-aspect identity permits the two aspects to have different formal characters.

*Acknowledged limitation:* This argument depends on the coherence of dual-aspect identity in general, which is debated in the literature. TIT inherits this debate but does not contribute a novel resolution. The argument here shows compatibility, not proof.

### Derivation 5: Brains vs. rocks (unchanged in substance)

From P5, P6, P8, P9:

**Conclusion C5:** Brains are P-conscious because they have high OTC (massive temporal integration, differentiation, and self-reference). Rocks have PTC (they participate in T-fund) but minimal OTC. The difference is organization, not raw material.

### Derivation 6: How anesthesia works (unchanged in substance)

**Conclusion C6:** Anesthesia eliminates P-consciousness by disrupting T-sys organization (reducing OTC below threshold), not by stopping temporal structure.

### Derivation 7: Minimum temporal thickness (unchanged in substance)

**Conclusion C7:** Consciousness requires non-zero temporal extent — metaphysically, not merely practically. OTC > 0 requires temporal extent for integration, differentiation, and self-reference.

### Derivation 8: The hard problem (REVISED)

From C3, P1, P2, P5, D8:

**Conclusion C8 (revised):** TIT does not dissolve the hard problem. It RELOCATES it. The original hard problem asks: "Why is there something it is like to be a physical system?" TIT's answer: "Because physical systems have intrinsic natures (P2), and the intrinsic nature of temporal structure is protoconscious (P5), and consciousness is what highly organized PTC is (P6)." The residual mystery: "Why is the intrinsic nature of temporal structure experiential?" TIT treats this as a brute fact (an axiom, P5), defended on theoretical-virtue grounds.

This relocation is progress for three reasons: (a) it unifies two mysteries (consciousness + passage) into one; (b) it transforms the hard problem from a question about a specific anomaly (brains) into a question about the nature of reality in general (intrinsic nature); (c) it brings consciousness within the ontological framework of Russellian monism, where it is a natural feature of reality rather than an inexplicable add-on. But TIT does not claim to answer the ultimate question of why intrinsic nature is experiential. That question may have no further answer.

### Derivation 9: Non-temporal qualia (NEW)

From P11, P5, P6, D9:

**Step 1:** All neural representations are temporal patterns — specific trajectories through T-sys state-space (P11a, D9).
**Step 2:** Each temporal pattern has an intrinsic nature — its PTC, organized according to the pattern's specific geometry (P5, D9).
**Step 3:** When the temporal pattern is part of a high-OTC system, its intrinsic nature is part of the system's P-consciousness (P6).
**Step 4:** Different temporal patterns have different intrinsic natures — different organizational geometries yield different experiential characters (P11b).
**Step 5:** Therefore, different qualia correspond to different temporal patterns: color qualia correspond to the temporal patterns in visual cortex; pain qualia correspond to temporal patterns in pain circuits; spatial qualia correspond to temporal phase relationships in spatial processing areas.

**Conclusion C9:** Non-temporal-seeming qualia (color, pain, spatial extension, emotion) are constituted by temporal patterns experienced from the intrinsic-nature side. They SEEM non-temporal because their temporal microstructure is not introspectively accessible — we experience the qualitative character (redness, sharpness) without perceiving the temporal pattern that constitutes it, just as we experience wetness without perceiving H2O molecular dynamics.

*Acknowledged limitations:* (a) This does not explain WHY pattern X yields redness rather than blueness. The qualia-mapping problem remains open. (b) The claim that all neural representations are temporal patterns is empirically well-supported but not proved — some representations may have non-temporal components. (c) The introspective-inaccessibility claim (we do not perceive our own temporal microstructure) needs independent justification.

### Derivation 10: Temporal illusions (NEW)

From P10 (revised), D2b, D2c:

**Step 1:** T-phen is the intrinsic nature of the brain's T-sys — the brain's own temporal dynamics (P10, revised).
**Step 2:** The brain's T-sys may diverge from external temporal structure (the brain can process events in a different order than they occurred, or assign different durations than objective measurement would indicate).
**Step 3:** When the brain's T-sys diverges from external time, T-phen tracks the brain's T-sys, not external time — because T-phen IS the brain's T-sys (P10).
**Step 4:** This produces temporal illusions: the subject experiences time as the brain organizes it, not as the external world presents it.

**Conclusion C10:** Temporal illusions are explained by the divergence between brain-internal temporal dynamics (which T-phen faithfully IS) and external temporal structure (which T-phen is not directly about). T-phen is never wrong about the brain's own temporal structure; it can be "wrong" about external time because it is the intrinsic nature of the former, not the latter.

*Note:* This means TIT's grand claim is more precisely: "Consciousness is what the brain's temporal dynamics look like from the inside" — not "consciousness is what the time dimension of the universe looks like from the inside." The connection to cosmic T-fund is mediated by P5 (T-fund has PTC) and D2b (T-sys is an instance of T-fund). But the experiential content of a specific conscious moment is determined by the brain's T-sys, not by T-fund directly.

### The Theory in One Paragraph (Revised)

Time and consciousness are the same thing accessed from different sides. Physics describes temporal structure — ordering, metric, dynamics — and finds no passage, no "now," no experience. But temporal structure has an intrinsic nature: protoconscious temporal character (PTC). In simple systems, PTC is minimal. In complex systems like brains, temporal dynamics become massively integrated, differentiated, and self-referential (high Organized Temporal Complexity), and the intrinsic nature of this organized temporal complexity IS phenomenal consciousness. The full diversity of qualia — color, pain, space, emotion — arises from the diversity of temporal patterns in the brain, each with its own intrinsic experiential character. The felt passage of time is not an illusion; it is what temporal ordering IS from the inside, compatible with the block universe because the structural and intrinsic aspects of reality can have different formal properties. TIT does not solve the ultimate mystery of why intrinsic nature is experiential; it relocates the hard problem from a question about brains to a question about the nature of reality.

## 5. Empirical Predictions (REVISED)

### Shared predictions (consistent with TIT but also predicted by rival theories)

**EP1. PCI distinguishes conscious from unconscious states.**
*Status: Established.* Shared with IIT, GWT, RPT. Consistent with TIT's claim that consciousness requires organized temporal complexity.

**EP2. Minimum temporal grain for consciousness (~30-50ms).**
*Status: Established.* Shared with all processing-based theories. TIT adds the metaphysical claim that this is a constitutive, not merely practical, limitation.

**EP3. Anesthesia disrupts temporal coordination specifically.**
*Status: Established.* Shared with IIT, GWT. Consistent with TIT.

**EP4. Time perception distortions correlate with consciousness alterations.**
*Status: Partially established.* Shared with attention-based theories. Consistent with TIT.

**EP5. Neural correlates of "nowness" are temporal coordination patterns.**
*Status: Partially supported.* Shared broadly. Consistent with TIT.

**EP6. Temporal patterns predict conscious content.**
*Status: Partially supported.* Note: spatial patterns also predict content (adversary correctly flagged this). TIT predicts temporal patterns should be MORE predictive than spatial patterns when the two are cleanly dissociated — this is partially distinctive but difficult to test.

**EP7. Meditation "timelessness" corresponds to altered temporal dynamics.**
*Status: Partially supported.* Shared with attention-based theories.

### Distinctive predictions (unique to TIT or shared only with closely related theories)

**EP8. Temporal isomorphism across substrates preserves conscious character.**
TIT predicts that an artificial system replicating the temporal dynamics of a brain region (same integration topology, differentiation profile, self-referential structure — same T-sys) on a different substrate should instantiate the same experiential character. Critically, TIT predicts temporal isomorphism is SUFFICIENT — spatial isomorphism is not additionally required.
*Distinguishes from:* IIT (requires Phi-structure isomorphism, which includes spatial integration), GWT (requires global workspace architecture), HOT (requires higher-order representational capacity).
*Status: Not yet testable.* Requires brain-computer interface or neural prosthetic technology.

**EP9. Simple oscillators have PTC.**
TIT predicts that any system with temporal structure has protoconscious temporal character. This is untestable by current methods (PTC is not directly measurable).
*Status: Not testable.* Metaphysical commitment.

**EP10. Spatially complex but temporally simple systems should not be conscious.**
TIT predicts that a system with brain-like spatial connectivity but fundamentally different temporal dynamics (e.g., all computations occurring in a single simultaneous step with no temporal integration) should NOT be conscious — even if functionally equivalent on input-output behavior.
*Application to AI:* Current transformer-based AI processes information in massively parallel forward passes with minimal recurrent temporal integration. TIT predicts these systems are not conscious regardless of their functional sophistication. If future AI systems incorporate recurrent temporal dynamics resembling biological neural oscillation and temporal binding, TIT predicts they would move toward consciousness.
*Distinguishes from:* Functionalism (predicts consciousness follows functional equivalence regardless of temporal implementation), GWT (may be implementable without recurrent temporal dynamics).
*Status: Not yet testable.* But the prediction is precise: functional equivalence + different temporal dynamics = different consciousness status.

**EP11. (NEW) Temporal but not spatial disruption should alter qualia character.**
TIT predicts that if we could selectively disrupt the temporal patterns in a brain region while preserving its spatial connectivity and mean firing rates, the qualia associated with that region should change — even though the "spatial" representation is intact. Conversely, disrupting spatial connectivity while preserving temporal patterns should have less effect on qualia character.
*Distinguishes from:* Spatial-connectivity theories predict the opposite pattern.
*Status: Not yet tested.* But the technology for temporal-specific perturbation (e.g., optogenetic manipulation of oscillatory phase without altering mean rates) is approaching feasibility.

## 6. Known Vulnerabilities (Revised)

1. **P5 remains an axiom.** The constitutive universality argument strengthens the motivation but does not prove the identification. A competing theory could identify the intrinsic nature of temporal structure with something non-experiential, and TIT has no knock-down argument against this.

2. **OTC is not formalized.** The directional constraints in D6 are a start, but the theory cannot make quantitative predictions until OTC is a computable metric.

3. **The combination problem is constrained but not solved.** Constraints 1-3 in P6 specify the target, not the mechanism. The intrinsic-nature side of the combination process remains unspecified.

4. **The qualia-mapping problem is open.** P11 explains that different temporal patterns generate different qualia, but not which pattern corresponds to which quale. This is shared with IIT and all structural theories.

5. **The passage/block-universe argument depends on the coherence of dual-aspect monism.** If dual-aspect monism is incoherent in general, C4 fails. TIT inherits this debate but does not resolve it.

6. **EP8-EP11 are not yet testable.** The theory's distinctive empirical content is concentrated in predictions that require future technology. Until then, TIT is empirically indistinguishable from rival theories on all testable predictions.

7. **The retreat to brain-internal T-sys (P10 revised, C10) weakens the original grand claim.** The hypothesis was "time IS consciousness from the outside." The revised theory says "the brain's temporal dynamics ARE consciousness from the outside." This is more precise and more defensible, but it loses the cosmic sweep of the original hypothesis.
