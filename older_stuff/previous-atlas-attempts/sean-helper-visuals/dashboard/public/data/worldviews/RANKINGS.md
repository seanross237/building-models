## Key Patterns

**What the top scorers share:**
- Built on *existing formalized research programs* (AdS/CFT, Boyle-Finn-Turok, NCG, categorical QM)
- Inherit real mathematical machinery, not just vocabulary
- Have at least one quantitative or falsifiable prediction
- Score 3+ on Compatibility (don't contradict established physics in tested regimes)

**What drags scores down most:**
- Mathematical Precision 1-2/5 (verbal analogies without equations) — the #1 killer
- Lorentz invariance recovery from discrete substrates — unsolved across the board
- Stacking multiple speculative frameworks without demonstrating consistency
- Predictions placed at Planck scale with no low-energy proxy

**Category performance:**
- **Pairwise Hybrids** average ~20.5 — combining two lenses doesn't automatically improve on either
- **Problem-Targeted** average ~20.0 — ambitious scope, but formalization rarely matches ambition
- **Focused Deep Dives** average ~19.5 — narrower but more honest about limitations
- **Pure Worldviews** average ~19.1 — too much to explain with too little formalism
- **Adversarial** average ~18.5 — conceptual tensions often survive the synthesis attempt
- **Wild Cards** average ~18.8 — high variance (24 to 11), most creative but least disciplined

**The #1 worldview (WV 46 - CPT Cosmological Boundary)** wins because it's the only entry that is actually a *published physics paper* with *computed predictions* built on *unmodified SM+GR*. It doesn't add new physics — it adds a boundary condition and derives consequences. That's what rigor looks like.
