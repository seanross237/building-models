## Chosen Theory: Multi-Species Fracton Compositeness (MSFC)

**Core idea:** N fracton species with internal index a = 1,...,N. The tensor field A_{ij}^{ab} carries spatial (i,j) and internal (a,b) indices. Dipole condensation breaks both spatial dipole symmetry (→ gravitons) and internal symmetry (→ gauge bosons).

**Why this was chosen:**
- Unanimously ranked #1 by Plausibility (6.7), Novelty (7/10), and Feasibility (9/10) agents
- Directly extends proven FDCG machinery (Goldstone theorem, gauge enhancement)
- Genuinely novel — simultaneous spatial + internal symmetry breaking from multi-species fracton condensation is unpublished
- Very high fail-fast: iteration 1 of investigation tells us if spin-1 Goldstones exist

## Phase B Plan

### Iteration 2: The Critical Calculation
**Question:** For N=2 species of fractons, does dipole condensation produce spin-1 Goldstone bosons?

**Steps:**
1. Write the action S[A_{ij}^{ab}] for N=2 species with the most general quadratic + quartic terms consistent with rank-2 gauge symmetry and O(2) internal symmetry
2. Identify the full symmetry group G (spatial dipole × internal)
3. Determine the condensation pattern: which symmetries break?
4. Apply Goldstone theorem: count and classify all Goldstone modes
5. Key question: are any Goldstones spin-1 (vectors)?

**Success condition:** At least one spin-1 Goldstone mode exists with the correct gauge-field structure (rank-1 gauge symmetry)
**Failure condition:** All internal Goldstones are spin-0 (scalars). This would kill MSFC.
