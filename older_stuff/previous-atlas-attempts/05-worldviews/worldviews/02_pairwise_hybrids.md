The Page curve is a natural consequence: early radiation carries little mutual information because the codebook has not yet been significantly altered, but past the Page time the checksums force correlations into the output stream to maintain global consistency.

**Most Striking Prediction.** There exists a fundamental bandwidth bound: no physical process can transfer more than one bit per Planck time per Planck area. **Most Striking Prediction.** The number of generations of fermions (three) is the number of irreducible simple objects in the fusion category associated with the physical 2-category. This number is computable from the categorical data and is predicted to be exactly three, with no fourth generation possible regardless of energy scale, a prediction testable at any future collider.

## Worldview 21: Information + Complexified

**(a) Unified Picture.**
Information channels in nature are fundamentally complex-valued, not real. Every physical quantity has a real part encoding spatial information and an imaginary part encoding temporal information. The complex number field is not a mathematical convenience but the native data type of reality. Compression of complex-valued data (analogous to Fourier or wavelet compression) automatically produces Lorentzian geometry as its efficient encoding format. Wick rotation—the standard physics trick of replacing time with imaginary time—is reinterpreted as switching which component of the complex information you choose to read out: reading the real part gives you Euclidean (spatial) physics, reading the imaginary part gives you Lorentzian (temporal) physics. Quantum amplitudes are complex because they carry both spatial and temporal information simultaneously. The phase of a quantum state encodes its temporal evolution, while the modulus encodes its spatial distribution. Interference arises because complex data channels can constructively or destructively combine their temporal and spatial components.

**(b) Prediction for the Arrow of Time.**
The arrow of time is an informational phase constraint. Because temporal information lives in the imaginary component, and the imaginary part of a complex number has a natural orientation (positive versus negative imaginary axis), time inherits a directionality that space lacks. Entropy increase corresponds to the diffusion of complex information into higher imaginary components—literally, information migrating from real (recoverable, spatial) to imaginary (irrecoverable, temporal) encoding. This predicts that in regimes where Wick rotation is exact (such as certain thermal equilibrium states), the arrow of time should weaken or disappear, and that near phase transitions the distinction between temporal and spatial information should blur, producing measurable anomalies in thermalization rates.

**(c) Most Striking Prediction.**
If information is natively complex-valued, there should exist physical channels that transmit purely imaginary information—pure temporal data with no spatial content. These channels would be experienced as time flow without spatial extent: observable as anomalous temporal correlations between events at the same spatial location that cannot be explained by any spatial signal propagation. This would manifest experimentally as superluminal temporal correlations (not violating causality, since no spatial information is transmitted) detectable in high-precision clock comparison experiments.
