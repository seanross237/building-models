## Worldview 97: Mutual Emergence — Nothing is Fundamental

**Unified Picture.** No phenomenon is more basic than any other. Space emerges from entanglement. Entanglement emerges from quantum fields. Quantum fields emerge from spacetime geometry. Geometry emerges from information. Information emerges from distinguishable states. Distinguishable states emerge from thermodynamics. Thermodynamics emerges from statistical mechanics. Statistical mechanics emerges from dynamics. Dynamics emerges from spacetime. The circle closes. Every element of physics presupposes every other element. The universe is a self-consistent loop with no foundation, no starting point, no bottom turtle.

Formally, this is a fixed-point structure. The universe is a solution to a self-consistency equation: U = F(U), where F is the functional that maps a set of physical laws to the phenomena they produce, and the phenomena are themselves the physical laws. This is Wheeler's "law without law" made concrete. The mathematical structure is that of a bootstrap — analogous to the S-matrix bootstrap of the 1960s but applied to all of physics simultaneously. Just as the bootstrap program constrains the S-matrix by demanding self-consistency (crossing symmetry, unitarity, analyticity), the mutual emergence framework constrains all of physics by demanding that every phenomenon both produces and is produced by the others.

**Is this coherent?** It is coherent in the same sense that a dictionary is coherent: every word is defined in terms of other words, and no word is defined in terms of something outside language. The dictionary is self-consistent but not unique — many dictionaries are possible. The analogous question for physics: is the self-consistent loop unique? If so, mutual emergence explains why the universe is the way it is. If not, it merely constrains the universe to a family of possibilities.

**Key Prediction.** The bootstrap predicts that all physical constants are uniquely determined by self-consistency. There should be zero free parameters in the final theory. Any apparent free parameter (the fine structure constant, the cosmological constant, particle masses) should in principle be calculable from the self-consistency condition alone. A near-term test: if the bootstrap is correct, there should exist currently unknown mathematical relations among the Standard Model parameters — predictions that one parameter is a function of others, derivable from self-consistency.

**Biggest Gap.** Circular definitions can be vacuous. The risk is that U = F(U) has too many solutions (making the framework unpredictive) or no solutions (making it inconsistent). The gap is demonstrating that the self-consistency equation has a unique, physically realistic fixed point. No one has written down F explicitly. The S-matrix bootstrap succeeded for strong interactions because the constraints were specific enough (crossing + unitarity + Regge behavior) to almost uniquely fix the amplitude. The total bootstrap needs analogously specific constraints, and we do not yet know what they are.

## Worldview 98: ONE Thing is Fundamental — The Monist Universe

**Unified Picture.** The universe has exactly one primitive entity. Everything — space, time, matter, forces, probability, information — emerges from it. What is the best candidate?

The candidate is **process** — not a thing, not a substance, not information, not computation, but the bare concept of *transition*. A single, untyped, primitive transition relation: something becomes something else. No specification of what, no specification of how, just the bare fact of becoming.

From process alone: When processes compose, they form networks. The network topology generates space. The irreversibility of processes generates time (non-invertibility of the transition relation). Persistent patterns in the network — processes that feed back into themselves — are matter. The constraints on which processes can compose are forces. The multiplicity of paths through the network is probability. The compression of network descriptions is information. The minimum description length of a network region is entropy. The speed at which process-signals propagate through the network is the speed of light. Self-referential processes — processes that act on the network itself — are gravity (the network reshaping itself). The indistinguishability of certain process rearrangements is gauge symmetry. The granularity of the network is the Planck scale.

This is close to Whitehead's process philosophy, but mathematized. It is also close to causal set theory (where the causal order is primitive) and to the Wolfram model (where rewrite rules on hypergraphs are primitive). The key distinction is that process is more primitive than any of these: it is not a causal order (which presupposes events), not a rewrite rule (which presupposes a substrate), but bare becoming.

**Key Prediction.** If process is fundamental, then the most basic observable is not a field value or a particle position but a transition rate. The framework predicts that at the Planck scale, physics reduces to pure transition rates with no continuous geometry — a "pre-geometric" phase. This pre-geometric phase should leave a signature in the cosmic microwave background: a universal damping of correlations at the highest multipole moments (beyond ell ~ 10^4) that reflects the discreteness of the process network. This is distinct from (and additional to) Silk damping and other known suppression mechanisms.

**Biggest Gap.** "Process" is almost too primitive to be useful. The challenge is to show that a specific mathematical formalization of bare process — perhaps a category with objects and morphisms, where only the morphisms (transitions) are primitive and the objects (states) are derived — uniquely produces the physics we observe. Category theory is the natural language, but going from abstract category theory to the Standard Model and general relativity requires an enormous chain of derivations that no one has completed. The gap is the gap between philosophical clarity and mathematical specificity.

This is close to — but more specific than — several active research programs: extended TQFT (Lurie, Freed), the holographic principle (Maldacena), the ER=EPR conjecture (Maldena and Susskind), the complexity=action conjecture (Brown, Susskind), and the thermodynamics of spacetime (Jacobson). **Key Prediction.** The most distinctive prediction of the convergent framework is that the partition function of quantum gravity has a dual interpretation: it is simultaneously a thermodynamic partition function (sum over Boltzmann weights) and a computational partition function (sum over algorithmic complexities).
