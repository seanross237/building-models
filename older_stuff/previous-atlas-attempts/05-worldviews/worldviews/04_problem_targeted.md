## Worldview 76: Unify the Born Rule and the Second Law

**Key prediction:** If the Born rule and the Second Law share a common origin, then violations of one should correlate with violations of the other. Specifically, in quantum systems prepared in states where the Born rule is tested with high precision (e.g., quantum tomography experiments), the rate of apparent Born-rule deviations should be bounded by the system's entropy production rate. A system at thermal equilibrium (zero entropy production) should obey the Born rule exactly, while a system driven far from equilibrium should exhibit small but measurable deviations from |psi|^2, with the deviation magnitude proportional to the entropy production rate. This is testable with current quantum computing hardware operating at varying distances from thermal equilibrium.
