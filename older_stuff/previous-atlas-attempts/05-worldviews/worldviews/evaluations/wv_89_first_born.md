## Assessment

Worldview 89 is the result of a specific construction rule — take the first alternative interpretation of every tenet, then add cohomological time — and the evaluation must be read in that context. It is not a theory that emerged from attempting to explain a particular phenomenon; it is a combinatorial object, and its strengths and weaknesses largely follow from that origin.

The cohomological time claim is the most substantive element and was evaluated in depth for WV-39. The verdict there applies here: sheaf cohomology is the correct mathematical language for asking when local descriptions fail to globalize, the identification of H|ψ⟩=0 as the zero-obstruction condition is structurally interesting, and the presheaf whose H¹ represents time is never defined, making every subsequent claim conditional on solving that foundational problem. Nothing in WV-89's additional tenet reframings provides tools to address this gap. The computational-resonant vocabulary actually makes the gap harder to bridge, because sheaf cohomology over configuration space and "resonant sampling of a discrete substrate" are not natural bedfellows — they require different mathematical infrastructure, different ontological commitments, and different research programs.

The computational substrate interpretations — causal graph rendering, compression artifacts, computational load as mass — are coherent individually and collectively produce a picture that has been pursued seriously in the form of causal set theory, Wolfram's Physics Project, and digital physics programs. The worldview's version of this picture does not add to those programs; it restates their intuitions without the mathematical content those programs have developed. A more productive construction would have been to identify specifically which aspects of causal set theory or Wolfram models correspond to the tenet reframings and evaluate the resulting hybrid against known results in those fields.

The consistency problems are more serious here than in any single-tenet worldview because the component reframings were designed independently of one another. The tension between statistical conservation (momentum as translational consensus) and exact gauge invariance (gauge symmetries as compiler invariances) is a real contradiction unless the worldview specifies a resolution. In the causal set approach to quantum gravity, Lorentz invariance has been shown to emerge in a suitable continuum limit if the causal structure is chosen appropriately, but achieving this requires fine-tuning the update rules in a way that contradicts the "resonant sampling" picture, which implies that the discrete substrate preferentially excites certain modes rather than others. This is not addressed.

The novel prediction is the worldview's best asset. The pulsar timing array context is real, the NANOGrav collaboration has active searches for cosmic string signatures, and a quantized time-dilation offset near cosmic strings that is distinct from the standard GR conical deficit angle prediction would be a genuinely novel observable. The problem is that the prediction follows from an underived step: why does a cosmic string change the cohomological class of the presheaf? A cosmic string creates a conical deficit in spacetime geometry, not a topological change to the configuration space. For the cohomological class to jump, the presheaf itself must change discontinuously — its sections, restriction maps, or site topology must be different on the two sides of the string. None of this is argued. The prediction has the right phenomenological flavor but lacks the derivational chain that would make it falsifiable.

The parsimony score of 1/5 reflects not a design flaw but the worldview's explicit construction. "The First-Born" is by design the maximally non-default position within the tenet system, and parsimony is not its goal. The evaluation criterion is applied uniformly: a theory that requires simultaneous acceptance of twenty non-standard ontological commitments scores low on parsimony regardless of whether those commitments are individually interesting.

The worldview is most productively read as an existence proof that the cohomological time framework is compatible with a computational-resonant universe picture at the level of verbal description. Establishing mathematical compatibility would be a more significant result.

## Key Strengths

- The cohomological time prediction — quantized time-dilation offset near cosmic strings via pulsar timing arrays — is the correct phenomenological shape for a falsifiable prediction: it targets an active observational program, proposes an effect distinguishable from GR, and names a specific physical context (cosmic strings) where the mechanism would be activated
- The identification of dark matter and dark energy as artifacts of causal graph rendering (resonant sampling below threshold; compression artifact of expansion) is one of the more imaginative unifications of the pair, and it connects them to the same underlying substrate rather than treating them as independent posits
- The computational-resonant vocabulary is internally consistent across the component interpretations at the qualitative level: everything from mass to gauge symmetry to the speed of light fits coherently into a picture where a discrete computational substrate compresses, renders, and samples
- The cohomological time reframing's structural connection to quantum contextuality (which is itself a cohomological phenomenon, per Abramsky-Brandenburger) is the most mathematically grounded single claim in the worldview, and it does not depend on solving the open presheaf problem — it is a conceptual alignment that holds even at the current level of development

## Critical Weaknesses

- The presheaf whose H¹ represents time is not defined anywhere in the worldview; without specifying sections, restriction maps, and the site topology, the cohomological time claim has no mathematical content — this gap is not peripheral but is the load-bearing step of the entire framework, and the computational-resonant vocabulary of the other tenets provides no tools for filling it
- Conservation laws as statistical consensus and gauge symmetries as exact compiler invariances are incompatible without a specified mechanism relating them; if conservation is approximate (survivor bias, emergent consensus), and gauge invariance is exact (compiler freedom), then the worldview contains a contradiction at the level of what counts as a fundamental versus derived feature of physics
- Recovering Lorentz invariance from a discrete computational substrate is a hard, unsolved technical problem in causal set theory even with optimally designed update rules; the worldview asserts the causal graph rendering picture without engaging this problem, and the "resonant sampling" framing makes it harder to solve by implying mode-dependent dynamics that would generically break isotropy
- The cosmic string prediction requires that the cohomological class of the presheaf jumps discontinuously across the string's conical deficit — but a conical deficit is a smooth geometric feature (it produces a holonomy, not a topology change), and why this would alter the presheaf's cohomological structure is not argued; the prediction is currently unfalsifiable in practice because no quantitative value for the offset is derivable
- The combination of twenty non-standard tenet interpretations is not synthetic — none follow from the others, and the cohomological time framework is not derived from the computational substrate picture; the worldview is an overlay of independent commitments, each of which carries its own burden of justification, making the total evidentiary demand larger than any individual worldview while the explanatory return is not correspondingly larger
- Sheaf cohomology requires a well-defined site (a category with a covering topology) over which to define sheaves; the natural candidate — configuration space of the universe — is the output of a complete quantum gravity theory, not a known input; this circularity, identified already in WV-39, is reproduced here without any new tools to address it, and the computational-resonant vocabulary does not provide a substitute construction
