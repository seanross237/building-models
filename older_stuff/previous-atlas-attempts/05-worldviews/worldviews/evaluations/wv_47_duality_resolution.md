## Assessment

Worldview 47 occupies an unusual position in the evaluation space: it is the most experimentally focused of the interpretive worldviews, and its central virtue is the specificity of its empirical signature. The fringe visibility power law — V ~ (δx)^α with a non-trivial exponent characterizing the transition between wave and particle regimes — is a clean, falsifiable prediction that distinguishes this theory from both standard decoherence (smooth monotonic curve) and sharp complementarity (step function). For an interpretive proposal about QM foundations, generating a concrete measurable difference from the standard theory is a significant achievement. It is what raises the Novel Predictions score above the average for this class of worldview.

The core conceptual move is that quantum entities are fractal-like structures whose apparent ontological character (wave or particle) depends on the resolution at which they are sampled. The Born rule is reinterpreted as a coarse-graining integral that washes out sub-resolution structure, leaving standard QM statistics intact at any fixed scale. This is clever: it allows the theory to be empirically adequate by construction while predicting deviations precisely in the intermediate regime where the coarse-graining transition is visible. The analogy to Hausdorff dimension — where a fractal lives "between" integer dimensions — provides genuine conceptual scaffolding, and the connection to RG fixed points as self-similar resolutions is one of the more interesting speculative links in the focused deep dive series.

However, the theory's weaknesses run deep. The most fundamental problem is that the central mathematical object — the "duality dimension" D_wp — is not derived from anything. Hausdorff dimension is a well-defined quantity because there is a metric on the space whose Hausdorff measure can be computed. No analogous metric is defined on the space of quantum measurement outcomes. "Fractal substructure" of a quantum entity means nothing precise until one specifies: fractal in what space, with respect to what measure, and with what coarse-graining operation. The Born rule coarse-graining claim is stated but not formalized: what is the measure on the sub-resolution substructure? How does integrating over it reproduce Born rule probabilities rather than something else? Without this, the theory's empirical adequacy is asserted rather than demonstrated.

The infinite self-similar recursion deserves scrutiny it does not receive. "At still finer scales, further particle-like nodes appear, ad infinitum" — this is physically required for the fractal claim to hold, but it creates immediate problems. Physical scales have known limits: the Planck scale (~10^-35 m) ends the applicability of any semi-classical geometric picture. If the recursion terminates at the Planck scale, the fractal is a finite approximation, which changes the dimensional analysis substantially and may cause the power law to cross over to standard behavior at energies accessible to future experiments. If the recursion does not terminate, it requires structure below the Planck scale that conflicts with essentially all approaches to quantum gravity. This is not a minor technical footnote — it is a constraint that must be addressed for the theory to be taken seriously.

The Lorentz invariance problem is similarly non-trivial. Resolution-dependence requires a preferred notion of scale. In non-relativistic QM this is unproblematic, but in relativistic QFT, "detector spatial resolution" is not Lorentz-invariant — a boost changes what counts as coarse or fine. If D_wp is a fundamental constant of an entity, it must be Lorentz-invariant, but the power law V ~ (δx)^α involves δx, which is a length that transforms under boosts. The theory as stated applies cleanly only in the non-relativistic regime, and its extension to relativistic particles (where complementarity experiments are most precisely performed) is unaddressed.

The compatibility score of 3/5 acknowledges the theory's strongest asset — it is the only worldview in this set that explicitly preserves standard QM statistics at every fixed scale by design — but penalizes the unresolved conflicts with decoherence theory and the Lorentz invariance gap. Standard decoherence theory does not predict a sharp threshold in V vs. detector resolution; it predicts a smooth degradation of visibility with measurement strength, parameterized by coupling to the environment. The worldview claims this is distinguishable from its own power law, but the prediction would need to be sharper — a specific exponent, not just "non-trivial scaling" — to be testable given the precision required to distinguish power-law from smooth monotonic decay in a finite experimental range.

The quantum computing implication — that intermediate measurement strengths carry richer information than weak measurement theory predicts — is the most practically interesting downstream claim, but it is stated in one sentence without any calculation. Weak measurement theory (Aharonov, Albert, Vaidman) is a mature framework with precise predictions for intermediate measurement outcomes. Any claim that it systematically misses fractal substructure needs to engage with that literature quantitatively, not just gesture at it.

Overall, WV 47 is a specific and earnest attempt to give wave-particle duality a dynamical rather than purely epistemic interpretation. Its Novel Predictions score is the highest single-criterion score in this evaluation and reflects genuine scientific seriousness. The theory fails to reach the middle tier primarily because its central mathematical object (D_wp, the fractal substructure) is undefined in any formal sense, and the physically required infinite recursion collides with the Planck scale in a way the theory ignores. It would benefit from doing two things: (1) specifying precisely what space the fractal structure lives in and providing the Born rule derivation explicitly, and (2) either incorporating a Planck-scale cutoff and computing how it modifies the power law, or explaining why the recursion can continue below the Planck scale. Either path would sharpen the theory considerably.

## Key Strengths

- The fringe visibility power law (V ~ (δx)^α in the transition region of which-path experiments) is a genuine, falsifiable prediction distinct from both decoherence theory (smooth monotonic) and sharp complementarity (step function), testable with existing Mach-Zehnder technology and variable-resolution detectors
- Born rule preservation by construction (as a coarse-graining integral over sub-resolution structure) gives the theory automatic empirical adequacy at any fixed scale — a clever design choice that avoids the usual problem of interpretive additions conflicting with standard predictions
- Three independent experimental signatures (fringe visibility scaling, multi-scale self-similar correlations in photon arrival statistics, richer information at intermediate measurement strengths) provide triangulated opportunities for falsification
- The connection between RG fixed points and self-similar resolutions is suggestive and non-trivial: if RG flow reflects genuine multi-scale ontology rather than a calculational convenience, this reframes a major tool of QFT in a way that could generate contact with other worldviews in this series
- Conceptually clean reframing of complementarity: replacing the sharp wave/particle dichotomy with a continuously parameterized duality dimension D_wp is a genuine interpretive move, not merely a relabeling

## Critical Weaknesses

- D_wp is undefined: "duality dimension" requires a metric on the space of measurement outcomes; without one, the Hausdorff dimension analogy is purely verbal, and the power law exponent has no derivation — D_wp is a free parameter fitted to data, not a prediction
- The infinite self-similar recursion ("at still finer scales, further particle-like nodes appear, ad infinitum") terminates at the Planck scale in any realistic theory; neither the cutoff physics nor its consequences for the power law are analyzed
- Lorentz invariance: "detector spatial resolution" δx is not Lorentz-invariant; a power law in δx has no covariant generalization as stated, limiting the theory to non-relativistic contexts where complementarity is least precisely tested
- No engagement with decoherence theory, which already provides quantitative smooth-curve predictions for the transition region; the claim of a distinguishable power law requires a derivation of the exponent, not just a qualitative assertion of non-trivial scaling
- The Born rule coarse-graining claim is asserted but not demonstrated: the measure on the sub-resolution substructure is never specified, so it is not shown that integrating over it actually yields Born probabilities
- No engagement with existing intermediate-measurement experiments: weak measurement data (Aharonov-Albert-Vaidman formalism) and quantum eraser experiments probe exactly the regime this theory claims is anomalous — the absence of observed deviations in this literature is a real evidential constraint, not addressed
- No connection to no-go theorems (Kochen-Specker, PBR): adding ontic substructure to quantum entities must contend with these results, and the theory does not discuss how D_wp-structured entities avoid contextuality or ontological incompleteness conclusions
