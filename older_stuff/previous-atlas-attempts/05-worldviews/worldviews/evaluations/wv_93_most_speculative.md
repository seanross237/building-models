## Assessment

Worldview 93 occupies a singular position in the worldview space: it is constructed by explicit maximization of distance from established physics on every tenet simultaneously. This is not a theory in the scientific sense — it is a boundary probe, a stress test of what happens when no anchor to known physics is retained. Evaluated against the criteria on their merits, the score is predictably low. But understanding *why* it scores low on each dimension is more informative than the number.

The internal consistency failure is the most fundamental problem. Twenty independent maximally-radical reinterpretations are not a coherent system — they are a collection of conceptual gestures that have not been checked against one another. The worldview's authors appear aware of this: the "Biggest Gap" section is unusually honest, stating directly that combining verbal sketches does not produce a theory. This self-awareness is notable but does not change the assessment.

The mathematical precision score of 1 is the correct floor. The one term that has genuine mathematical content — the Fisher metric on law-space — is borrowed from information geometry, where it has a precise definition as the Riemannian metric on the space of probability distributions parameterized by some parameters. But "law-space" is not a space of probability distributions in any specified sense; it is a metaphor for the idea that physical constants might vary. Using the name of a precise mathematical object without specifying how that object instantiates in the claimed framework is not formalization — it is mathematical vocabulary serving a rhetorical function. The prediction about correlated constant drift has the right structure for a falsifiable claim (it predicts a specific correlation pattern, not just variation), but without the Fisher metric being defined on the relevant space, the correlation structure cannot be derived, and the prediction cannot be distinguished from any other model predicting correlated constant variation.

The compatibility score of 1 deserves comment because the worldview is maximally incompatible by design — but it is important to be precise about what this means. The worldview is not asserting that GR and QM are wrong in their experimental predictions; it is asserting that the ontological picture underlying them is wrong and that the same observational facts can be reinterpreted without spacetime, without local states, and without fundamental constants. This is a coherent philosophical position. The compatibility criterion, however, asks whether the framework reduces to established theories in appropriate limits, and the worldview provides no reduction mechanism. Without showing that "information density gradients" reproduce the Einstein field equations in some limit, or that "retroactive constraint propagation" reproduces the Born rule statistics, the framework does not satisfy compatibility. The assertion that it could do so is not the same as demonstrating it.

The explanatory scope score of 3 is the one area where the worldview punches near its weight. The ideas gestured at — gravitational entropy, non-commutative geometry, computational self-reference, information-theoretic origins of quantization — are not randomly chosen metaphors. They correspond to active research programs. The lazy-evaluation interpretation of dark matter is a novel and concrete reframing: it asserts that regions of apparent dark matter density are gravitational effects from matter outside the past light cone that have not been "computed" into local observables. This is wrong as physics (it is not clear what "not yet computed" would mean observationally, and it does not reproduce the spatial distribution of dark matter inferred from lensing), but it is a specific enough claim to be falsifiable in principle, which puts it ahead of most dark matter alternatives in terms of testability per ontological commitment.

The Fisher-metric constant-drift prediction is the worldview's most substantive contribution. The claim is that if constants are law-space equilibria, then their evolution is not arbitrary — it follows the geodesics of the information geometry on law-space. If law-space is a well-defined Riemannian manifold, this prediction would be as specific as any in physics: the time derivatives of all constants would be proportional to the gradient of the effective potential on the manifold, and the ratios of their derivatives would be fixed by the metric coefficients. That is an extremely constrainable prediction. The entire scientific value of the worldview is concentrated here — not in the manifesto, but in the implied program of making law-space precise. If someone wrote down the Fisher metric for the Standard Model parameter space, derived the geodesic equations, and computed a predicted correlation matrix for constant drift rates, that would be a genuine piece of new physics. The worldview points at this but does not do it.

## Key Strengths

- The Fisher-metric / correlated-constant-drift prediction has genuine predictive structure: it constrains the *correlation pattern* of constant variation, not just variation itself, which is a more specific and testable claim than most variable-constant theories make
- Several individual reinterpretations point at active and legitimate research programs (Verlinde's entropic gravity, Connes' non-commutative geometry, Wheeler's it-from-bit, computational quantum gravity), meaning the directions are not purely invented
- The lazy-evaluation interpretation of dark matter, though physically incorrect as stated, is specific enough to be falsifiable and is a genuinely novel framing
- The self-aware "Biggest Gap" section demonstrates that the worldview correctly diagnoses its own fatal weakness, which is a necessary precondition for future development
- The synthetic picture — physics as a self-referential information structure computing itself into existence retroactively — is a coherent organizing principle, not an arbitrary collection

---

## Critical Weaknesses

- The framework has no mathematical formulation by its own admission; it is a manifesto, not a theory, and cannot generate quantitative predictions
- Internal consistency fails on multiple dimensions simultaneously: retroactive causation conflicts with directed temporal error-correction; conservation law denial conflicts with CPT arithmetic inevitability; the denial of spacetime conflicts with every other component that requires a background geometry
- The Fisher metric on law-space borrows a precise mathematical object without specifying the probability model or parameter space it is defined over, making the central novel prediction underivable
- Compatibility with established physics is abandoned across the board with no reduction mechanism supplied, meaning the framework cannot explain why standard physics works as well as it does
- Parsimony is the worst possible: twenty independent non-standard ontological primitives with no derivation relations between them
- The correlated constant drift prediction is currently unbounded in magnitude; without a defined metric, existing precision measurements (10^17 per year on alpha) cannot falsify the prediction — they can only constrain a free parameter that the theory does not specify
- The framework cannot distinguish between "physics described as information processing" (a representational choice compatible with standard physics) and "information processing is the fundamental ontology" (a substantive metaphysical claim) — this equivocation runs through every component
