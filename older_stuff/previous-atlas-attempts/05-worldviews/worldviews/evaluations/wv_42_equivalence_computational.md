## Assessment

WV42 is built on a single sharp insight: the equivalence principle (gravitational mass equals inertial mass) has always been a brute postulate in both Newtonian mechanics and general relativity. Einstein elevated it to a founding principle of GR but did not explain it — he took it as a datum from nature and built a geometry around it. The worldview asks the right question: is there a deeper reason these two quantities are identical? The answer proposed — that both measure computational cost of state-update, so their equality is definitional rather than coincidental — is philosophically motivated and not trivially dismissible.

The holographic argument is the clearest strength. The claim that a region's maximum computational load is bounded by its boundary I/O bandwidth, and that this recovers the Bekenstein-Hawking area scaling, is structurally in the right neighborhood. The Bekenstein bound is already an information-theoretic result, and connecting it to computational throughput is a natural extension of known derivations (see Susskind, Bousso, and Lloyd's work on computational limits of black holes). This is not a new result the worldview derives — the connection is pre-existing in the literature — but framing it as the holographic principle's *reason* rather than its restatement is at least conceptually honest.

The core problem is circularity, and the worldview acknowledges it without resolving it. "Computation" in physics means the evolution of a physical system according to its dynamics — a rock rolling down a hill is performing a computation in the sense relevant here. If "computational operations per unit time" is defined using physical time and physical state transitions, then saying mass equals computational cost is saying mass equals mass measured in different units. To escape this, the worldview needs a substrate-independent definition of computation — a formal complexity theory that exists prior to and independent of any physical instantiation. Such a thing arguably does not exist. Kolmogorov complexity is substrate-independent but uncomputable and dimensionless; it does not generate a quantity with units of kg. Church-Turing computation is also dimensionless and rate-independent. No path out of this circularity is offered.

The predicted gravitational anomalies from complex computation are the worldview's would-be empirical teeth, and they are pulled by the worldview's own estimate: ~10⁻⁴⁰ relative excess gravitational mass for a system performing irreducible computations. For reference, the best current gravitational measurements achieve precision at roughly 10⁻⁵ to 10⁻⁹ depending on the regime. The signal is 31 to 35 orders of magnitude below detection. This is not a prediction in any practical sense — it is a regime marker saying "if the theory is correct, the effect is here, and it is invisible." The quantum-computer prediction is in the same situation. These predictions technically distinguish the theory from standard GR (which predicts zero anomaly from computational complexity) but they are not falsifiable in any meaningful timeframe. A theory can be saved from empirical challenge indefinitely by placing its signatures below the noise floor.

The mathematical formalization is too thin to evaluate rigorously. The reinterpretation of the stress-energy tensor as "computational load density" requires specifying: (1) what the load density field is as a tensor, (2) how to compute it for a given physical system, (3) what its covariant divergence is and whether energy-momentum conservation is recovered, and (4) whether the resulting Einstein field equations are the standard ones or modified. None of this is provided. Similarly, "gradient of computational load density" as a definition of gravity needs a covariant expression. Is it the four-gradient of a scalar field? A divergence of the stress tensor? These are not equivalent, and the choice determines whether the theory is even generally covariant.

## Key Strengths

- **Addresses a real explanatory gap.** The equivalence principle is genuinely unexplained in standard GR — it is a postulate, not a theorem. Asking why it holds is a legitimate foundational question, and the computational reinterpretation is a non-trivial answer rather than a restatement of the mystery.
- **Holographic connection is structurally motivated.** The argument that I/O bandwidth at a region's boundary bounds its internal computational throughput, and that this recovers area-scaling of maximum information content, is in the right neighborhood of known results and provides a potential *reason* for the holographic principle rather than just a description of it.
- **Three distinct novel predictions** are offered, which is more than most worldviews in this set produce. The quantum-computer prediction in particular is striking and clearly demarcated from anything in standard GR.
- **Cosmological constant address, even if wrong in magnitude, identifies the right puzzle.** The observation that empty spacetime has a minimum gravitational energy density (the cosmological constant) and that this might correspond to a base computational overhead of flat spacetime maintenance is at least asking the right question. The magnitude failure is conceded honestly.

---

## Critical Weaknesses

- **Circularity is unresolved and may be irresolvable.** Defining computational cost using physical time and physical state transitions makes "mass equals computational cost" a tautology. The worldview needs a formalism in which "computational operations" are counted prior to physics, and no such formalism is provided or even pointed to.
- **"Irreducible computation" is undefined.** The prediction that complex dynamical systems show excess gravitational mass requires a precise definition of irreducibility (in the Wolfram sense? Kolmogorov incompressibility? Circuit depth in some universal basis?) that does not presuppose the physical system's dynamics. Without this, the prediction class is empty — there is no procedure for determining which systems qualify.
- **The Planck energy per computational operation coupling is a free parameter with no derivation.** The worldview invokes this coupling to estimate signal magnitudes but does not derive it. It is inserted to make the anomalies small enough not to have been observed, which is the opposite of a prediction — it is a parameter tuned for invisibility.
- **Lorentz invariance of computational rate is unaddressed.** A "computational operations per unit time" rate transforms under boosts. If a fast-moving computer has its operations Lorentz-dilated, does its gravitational mass change in the way this theory predicts? That would generate novel gravitational time-dilation effects from relative motion in computation-heavy systems, distinct from standard relativistic mass-energy. This is either a testable consequence the worldview ignores or a consistency problem requiring resolution.
- **Mathematical precision is insufficient to compute anything.** No field equation, no action, no Hamiltonian, no covariant expression for the computational load density field. The reinterpretation of R_mu_nu and T_mu_nu is a label swap without an algorithm for computing either quantity from first principles.
- **The cosmological constant magnitude failure is conceded but not addressed.** Identifying the cosmological constant with a base computational overhead of spacetime is a reasonable conceptual move, but the worldview admits this does not yield the correct value. The observed cosmological constant is ~10⁻¹²² in Planck units — reproducing this number from any theory is one of the hardest problems in physics, and this theory does not attempt it.
