## Assessment

Worldview 85 is doing philosophy of physics, not physics. That is not a disqualification — the relationship between the block universe and quantum mechanics is a genuine and underexplored problem in foundations — but it sets a ceiling on what the evaluation can find. The worldview is well-reasoned within its philosophical register, makes no technical errors in its use of the decoherence program, and the core analogy is intellectually honest about what it is doing. The function-as-block analogy is the best part: it correctly identifies that "existence as a complete specification" and "existence as an evaluated classical value" are different things, and it imports this distinction from mathematics where it is entirely uncontroversial. This is clean conceptual work.

The fundamental problem is the tension at the core of the analogy that the worldview does not confront. In mathematics, f(x) = x² fully determines f(17) = 289 — the specification contains the answer; evaluation is epistemological, not ontological. If the worldview is using the function analogy honestly, then the block universe contains all definite outcomes and measurement merely reveals them. This is a hidden-variable theory, and it faces Bell's theorem: no local hidden-variable theory can reproduce all quantum correlations. The worldview does not acknowledge this implication. The only escape is to say that the "formal specification" underdetermines outcomes — the block is a wave function, not a classical field — but then the function analogy breaks down, because f(17) = 289 is not a superposition of values pending evaluation. The worldview needs to pick a lane: either outcomes are pre-determined (hidden variables, Bell problem) or the block is genuinely quantum (superposition at all times, but then "lazy evaluation" is not adding anything that decoherence + many worlds does not already say more precisely).

The identification of decoherence as the evaluation trigger is the worldview's most technically grounded move. Decoherence is the correct physics of classicalization and the worldview is right that it provides a physical mechanism for the transition from quantum superposition to classical definiteness. But the worldview claims more than decoherence delivers: decoherence explains why interference terms become negligibly small for macroscopic systems and why outcomes are effectively classical, but it does not explain why a single outcome is observed (the preferred basis problem is partially resolved; the single-outcome problem is not). Calling decoherence "the evaluation trigger" imports a definiteness that decoherence alone does not license — decoherence produces an effectively diagonal density matrix, not a single definite outcome. The worldview is using "lazy evaluation fires and a definite classical record precipitates" language that overstates what decoherence actually does unless you have already committed to something like many-worlds (where all branches are definite) or collapse theory (where collapse is the actual evaluation trigger, not decoherence).

The wave function as "the block" is the worldview's most interesting specific claim. The identification of the wave function with the block universe — a complete, timeless specification of amplitudes for all spacetime events — is a real proposal that connects to the path integral formulation of QM and to the Hartle-Hawking no-boundary proposal in quantum cosmology. In those frameworks, the wave function of the universe is indeed a timeless object specifying amplitudes over all geometries. The worldview is gesturing at something real here even if it does not develop it. If this identification were made precise — wave function of the universe = block, decoherence = evaluation, branches = evaluated regions — the worldview would be describing something very close to the Everett interpretation plus decoherence, which is already a coherent framework with a substantial technical literature. The worldview adds "lazy evaluation" as vocabulary but does not add machinery.

The 19/35 score reflects a worldview that is conceptually honest, philosophically motivated, technically informed but not technical, and unable to produce novel predictions or formal precision. It is a useful framing device for thinking about the block universe and quantum mechanics together, and the function analogy is a good pedagogical tool. It does not constitute a theory.

## Key Strengths

- The distinction between formal existence (specification) and evaluated content (specific classical outcomes) is a genuine conceptual contribution that dissolves one formulation of the apparent contradiction between block universe and quantum indefiniteness without requiring new physics
- The identification of decoherence as the evaluation trigger correctly anchors the proposal in established physics rather than invoking a separate collapse mechanism — the worldview is working with the standard decoherence program, not against it
- The wave-function-as-block identification is the most technically precise move in the worldview and connects it, even if obliquely, to real programs in quantum cosmology (Hartle-Hawking, path integral formulations) where a timeless quantum state specifies a superposition over all spacetime geometries
- The two-layer ontology (block as promise, measurement as fulfillment) is introduced economically — it is a single new distinction rather than an accumulation of new entities, which limits the parsimony cost
- The worldview correctly identifies that "pre-existence and deferral coexist because the block is the promise and observation is the fulfillment" — this is a coherent summary of how the Everett interpretation relates to block universe ontology, even if the worldview does not frame it that way

---

## Critical Weaknesses

- The function analogy is self-undermining: if f(x) fully determines f(17), then the block contains all definite outcomes and measurement is epistemic rather than ontological — this is a hidden-variable theory, which faces Bell's theorem; the worldview does not acknowledge this consequence and provides no escape from it
- Decoherence produces an effectively diagonal density matrix, not a single definite classical outcome; saying "the lazy evaluator fires and a definite classical record precipitates" overclaims what decoherence delivers and borrows the language of definiteness without licensing it — the single-outcome problem is not resolved, only redescribed
- The worldview makes zero novel predictions; it is observationally equivalent to standard QM by construction, which means it cannot be tested and cannot be falsified; in physics this limits its status to interpretive commentary rather than theory
- The mathematical content is null: no equation, no specification of what "formal structure" corresponds to in the Hilbert space formalism, no quantitative criterion for when decoherence has "sufficiently" fired the evaluator, no connection to any calculable quantity
- The worldview does not address the preferred basis problem: decoherence depends on the system-environment split and the choice of pointer basis, but the worldview presents evaluation as if it has a unique preferred output; this is an unearned simplification that hides the real difficulty
- The extension to general relativity is entirely absent: in curved spacetime the notion of a "global" block is coordinate-dependent, the causal structure of decoherence events is non-trivial, and there is no discussion of how the lazy evaluator interacts with the equivalence principle or with event horizons
