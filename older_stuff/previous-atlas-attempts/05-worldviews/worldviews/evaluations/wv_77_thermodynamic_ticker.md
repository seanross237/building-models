## Assessment

WV77 is an attempt to dissolve the apparent conflict between discrete computational substrate and continuous thermodynamic physics by deploying a standard move from statistical mechanics: coarse-graining washes out microscopic discreteness. The central intuition — that a system with 10^120 discrete updating variables per step looks continuous to any observer whose measurement resolution is orders of magnitude coarser — is correct as far as it goes. Brownian motion, discrete molecular collisions, and lattice QCD all demonstrate that discrete substrates can produce smooth emergent behavior. The worldview is pointing at a real phenomenon.

The most interesting piece is the arrow-of-time argument. WV77 claims that the thermodynamic arrow is a consequence of computational irreversibility: each tick of the clock is a logically irreversible operation, dissipating logical entropy into the state space. This is structurally similar to Landauer's principle and has genuine content — if the fundamental dynamics are informationally irreversible, then coarse-grained entropy increase follows from the structure of the dynamics rather than from special initial conditions. This is a better answer to "why does entropy increase?" than invoking the past hypothesis, and it is in the same territory as WV43 (which handles this relationship more carefully). The worldview does not, however, show that computational irreversibility at the tick level implies monotone coarse-grained entropy at the thermodynamic level — it asserts this via the Landauer link without deriving it.

The CLT argument for apparent continuity is stated but not executed. The claim is that 10^120 independent updates per tick smooth out into a continuous distribution by the central limit theorem, just as Brownian motion appears continuous. This is plausible but requires that the updates be approximately independent, that the relevant observables be sums over many local updates, and that the tick rate be fast enough that inter-tick timescales are sub-Planckian. None of these are demonstrated. More critically, the CLT argument applies to noise around a mean trajectory — it does not by itself show that the emergent equations are the specific ones (Navier-Stokes, Boltzmann transport, Fourier heat equation) claimed. For those equations to emerge, the update rules must lie in specific universality classes under renormalization group flow. This is a deep and nontrivial requirement — not all discrete update rules converge to the right fixed points — and WV77 asserts it without any specification of the rules.

The unfalsifiability problem is structural, not contingent. WV77 explicitly states that the discreteness lives below the resolution floor of any thermodynamic instrument, making the two descriptions "complementary rather than contradictory." This is intellectually tidy but scientifically inert: a framework that is untestable by stipulation offers no experimental purchase. The worldview would need to identify phenomena at the resolution boundary — near-Planck-scale thermodynamics, early-universe relics of discrete ticking, or Planck-suppressed corrections to thermodynamic equations — to recover empirical traction. None are offered.

The deepest problem is the preferred foliation. A universal discrete clock — a single global tick sequence from state S(n) to S(n+1) — requires a preferred frame of reference. In special relativity, no such frame exists; simultaneous updates across the universe are observer-dependent. The worldview does not acknowledge this tension. A discrete global tick is incompatible with Lorentz invariance unless the ticks are defined on a spacelike hypersurface selected by some additional physical law, which would itself constitute a violation of the relativity of simultaneity. This is not a minor technical problem — it means the framework, as stated, cannot be extended to relativistic thermodynamics, quantum field theory, or general relativity without a fundamental rewrite of what the "tick" means. Since the worldview explicitly claims to underlie all of macroscopic physics including relativistic thermodynamics (Navier-Stokes, Boltzmann transport), this incompatibility is fatal to the stated scope.

## Key Strengths

- **The coarse-graining intuition is sound.** The observation that a system with a sufficiently large number of discrete degrees of freedom per tick will appear continuous to coarse-grained measurements is correct and well-supported by physics (statistical mechanics, lattice theories, Brownian motion). The worldview is pointing at a real mechanism.
- **Arrow of time via computational irreversibility is a genuine idea.** Grounding the thermodynamic arrow in the logical irreversibility of the computational tick — rather than in special initial conditions — is a more fundamental explanatory structure if it can be made precise. The Landauer connection is appropriate and physically motivated.
- **The CLT analogy to Brownian motion is illuminating.** Using Brownian motion as a proof-of-concept that discrete-to-continuous emergence works is pedagogically strong and empirically grounded. The analogy is accurate and the reasoning is valid at the level of intuition.
- **"Computes in ticks but flows in rivers" is a clean framing.** The distinction between the computational micro-description and the thermodynamic macro-description is philosophically clean and captures something real about the relationship between substrate and emergent behavior.

---

## Critical Weaknesses

- **Unfalsifiability by construction.** By stipulating that discreteness lives permanently below any possible thermodynamic measurement resolution, the worldview removes itself from empirical contact. A physically meaningful theory should identify at least one regime where its discrete nature produces observable corrections. WV77 explicitly forecloses this. The framework can be coherent and still be scientifically empty if it makes no predictions that differ from a fully continuous theory.
- **The global clock violates Lorentz invariance.** A universal tick sequence S(n) → S(n+1) defines a preferred foliation of spacetime. Special relativity forbids preferred foliations. This is not reconciled or even acknowledged. Extending the framework to cover relativistic phenomena — as claimed — would require redefining the tick in a covariant way, which is a nontrivial and unsolved problem in discrete spacetime physics (see: causal dynamical triangulations, causal set theory). WV77 does not engage with this literature or the problem.
- **Update rules are unspecified; the renormalization claim is empty.** The worldview claims that continuous equations (Navier-Stokes, Fourier, Boltzmann) are emergent fixed points of the discrete update rule under renormalization. But no update rule is given. Without a specified rule, the renormalization group cannot be applied, no fixed points can be computed, and the claim cannot be checked. The assertion that the right equations emerge is not a prediction — it is a hope stated as a fact.
- **Statistical independence of updates is assumed, not derived.** The CLT argument requires that the 10^120 per-tick updates are approximately independent so that sums over them converge to Gaussian distributions. Physical systems have correlations — long-range in critical phenomena, medium-range in fluids. If the updates are correlated, the CLT does not apply, the convergence to smooth equations breaks down, and the worldview's main mechanism fails. This assumption is central and unexamined.
- **The 10^120 figure is a free parameter without justification.** The worldview's claim that discreteness is hidden requires a specific relationship between tick rate, number of degrees of freedom per tick, and measurement resolution. The figure 10^120 is presumably motivated by the number of Planck volumes in the observable universe, but this connection is not made explicit, and the relationship between this figure and actual thermodynamic instruments is not worked out. It functions as a numerical flourish rather than a derived constraint.
- **No account of quantum mechanics.** The worldview claims to underlie all macroscopic physics via thermodynamic emergence, but does not address how quantum mechanics fits into the computational substrate. If the ticks are classical logical operations, quantum superposition and entanglement do not emerge from them without additional structure. If the ticks are quantum operations, the framework is a variant of quantum cellular automata with all the associated formalization requirements — none of which are developed.
