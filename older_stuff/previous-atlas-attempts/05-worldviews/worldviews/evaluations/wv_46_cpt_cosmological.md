## Assessment

Worldview 46 is one of the most scientifically disciplined entries in this collection. Unlike most alternative cosmologies, it does not invent new degrees of freedom, does not introduce free parameters, and publishes its central calculations in refereed journals where they can be checked. The framework is a genuine theory — not a research program outline or a metaphor dressed in equations — and it earns most of its score through concrete, verified computation rather than scope-of-ambition claims.

The foundational move is conservative in spirit and radical in consequence. CPT symmetry is already an exact symmetry of every renormalizable quantum field theory, confirmed to extraordinary precision in the laboratory. Boyle, Finn, and Turok's claim is that if CPT is imposed as a cosmological boundary condition at the Big Bang — rather than merely as a local symmetry of the Lagrangian — the initial state of the universe is uniquely determined. The conformally invariant vacuum is the only state invariant under CPT reflection at a point. This vacuum is automatically homogeneous, isotropic, and spatially flat (in the conformal sense), which is why the flatness and horizon problems dissolve: they were never problems of dynamics, but of the initial state, and the boundary condition selects the initial state uniquely. There is no fine-tuning because there is no choice.

The dark matter prediction is where the theory is most precise and most striking. The three right-handed neutrinos are required to complete the CPT-symmetric spectrum — there must be right-handed partners for the three left-handed neutrinos, exactly as there is an anti-universe to partner the universe. Their masses are then constrained by two requirements: (1) leptogenesis must generate the observed baryon asymmetry, and (2) the gravitational production rate during the CPT-symmetric phase must produce the correct dark matter relic abundance. These two constraints, combined with the measured neutrino mass splittings, overdetermine the system and yield a specific mass scale (~5 × 10^8 GeV) without any tuning. This is a parameter-free prediction of a dark matter mass scale, which is extremely rare in cosmology. The fact that this mass is far above collider reach and far above direct-detection thresholds is not a failure of the theory — it is what the calculation gives — but it does mean the prediction is currently untestable except by ruling out all other dark matter candidates.

The baryogenesis mechanism is the theory's most operationally testable pillar. The CP-violating phase δ in the PMNS neutrino mixing matrix is the same parameter that drives leptogenesis in this framework. DUNE's primary physics goal is to measure δ. If DUNE finds δ = 0 or π (CP conservation in the lepton sector), the Boyle-Finn-Turok leptogenesis mechanism is ruled out. If DUNE finds δ in the currently preferred range (~-π/2), the mechanism is consistent and the quantitative prediction for the baryon asymmetry can be checked. This is an unusually direct connection between a particle physics experiment and a cosmological mechanism.

The most significant scientific concern is the CMB power spectrum. Inflation's most important achievement is not solving the horizon problem — it is predicting a nearly scale-invariant power spectrum of density fluctuations from quantum vacuum fluctuations, including the precise shape of the acoustic peaks. The CPT boundary condition predicts a scale-invariant spectrum at leading order (n_s slightly below 1), but deriving the acoustic peak structure from the CPT-invariant vacuum state without invoking a period of exponential expansion is a non-trivial calculation that has not been completed. The 2018 and 2022 papers establish that the spectrum is consistent with Planck data at the level of the scalar spectral index and tensor-to-scalar ratio, but they do not compute the full transfer function from the CPT-symmetric initial conditions. This is a genuine gap, not a fatal flaw, but it means the framework has not yet demonstrated it can replace inflation's full predictive content.

The mirror anti-universe is unobservable by construction, which raises an interpretive question about falsifiability. However, this is not a unique problem: parallel universe branches in the Many-Worlds interpretation are also unobservable, and the CPT-mirror universe is at least logically necessary (it follows from imposing CPT at the Big Bang) rather than multiply postulated. The unobservability concern is primarily philosophical rather than scientific, since the observable predictions of the framework — r, δ, and the baryon asymmetry — are all accessible in principle.

The theory scores low on unification relative to its other scores because it does not address quantum gravity and does not attempt to unify forces. This is a deliberate scope choice, not an oversight: Boyle, Finn, and Turok are solving cosmological problems with known physics, not building a TOE. Within that scope, the framework is exceptionally well-executed.

## Key Strengths

- Parameter-free prediction of dark matter candidate mass (~5 × 10^8 GeV sterile neutrino) derived entirely from boundary conditions plus measured neutrino mass differences — no free parameters to tune
- Direct, testable connection between the CP-violating phase δ in the PMNS matrix and the baryon asymmetry; DUNE can falsify the mechanism within the decade
- Suppressed primordial gravitational waves (r ≪ 10^-3) is a sharp, distinguishing prediction that CMB-S4 and LiteBIRD can probe as an absence signal
- Simultaneously resolves flatness, horizon, and monopole problems; produces the correct baryon asymmetry; identifies a specific dark matter candidate — all within unmodified Standard Model + GR, with no new free parameters
- Published, peer-reviewed, and mathematically computed (not merely proposed): the spectral index, gravitational production rate, and relic abundance calculations are in the literature and checkable
- The CPT boundary condition is a logically minimal posit: it asserts that a known exact symmetry of QFT also holds as a global cosmological symmetry — this is the smallest natural extension imaginable

## Critical Weaknesses

- Full CMB power spectrum (acoustic peaks and transfer function) has not been computed from the CPT-symmetric initial conditions; the framework's compatibility with the detailed CMB is assumed by analogy with inflation rather than derived
- The dark matter candidate is too heavy for any direct detection experiment and too weakly coupled for any collider; confirmation requires either ruling out all other candidates or finding an indirect signal whose interpretation is model-dependent
- The pre-Big-Bang mirror universe is unobservable by construction; the theory cannot be falsified by any observation of the mirror sector, only by its observable-sector predictions
- No mechanism for dark energy or the cosmological constant; the framework accepts ΛCDM's dark energy description without modification and does not address why Λ has its observed value
- The "uniqueness" of the CPT-invariant vacuum — the claim that it is automatically flat and isotropic — relies on conformal invariance, which breaks at energy scales above the conformal symmetry-breaking scale; the argument requires that the universe begins in a conformally invariant phase, which is an additional assumption not derived within the framework
- No connection to quantum gravity: the framework treats the Big Bang as a smooth boundary point amenable to CPT reflection, which is not obviously consistent with a quantum treatment of the spacetime singularity itself
