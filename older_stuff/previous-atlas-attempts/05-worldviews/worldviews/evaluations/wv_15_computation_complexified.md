## Assessment

Worldview 15 is the most mathematically grounded entry in the computational family and a significant step up in rigor from the pure computational worldview (WV1). Its central insight — that the formal trick of Wick rotation is not a trick but a literal change of basis in the state space of a complex-valued automaton — is genuinely novel and mathematically well-motivated. Complex analysis is not being imported as metaphor here; it is being proposed as the native arithmetic of the underlying dynamics, which means the framework inherits the full machinery of holomorphic functions, residue theory, and analytic continuation as working tools. That is a qualitatively different relationship to mathematics than frameworks that use information-theoretic vocabulary analogically.

The fine structure constant prediction deserves particular attention because it is rare in this worldview class to see a prediction that is (a) specific in form, (b) falsifiable with existing technology, and (c) not just a post-hoc restatement of something already known. The claim that α is algebraic rather than transcendental, and computable from automaton topology as a ratio involving π, is bold enough to be falsified by one high-precision measurement. Current measurements of α (via anomalous magnetic moment of the electron, quantum Hall effect, and atomic recoil) are good to parts per billion and future ion trap experiments will push further. If α turns out to be irrational in a way inconsistent with any small-integer ratio times a power of π, the prediction fails cleanly. This kind of clean falsifiability is a genuine scientific virtue.

The compatibility score reflects real unresolved tension. The framework explicitly claims the path integral is a finite sum (UV finiteness from discreteness) and that divergences are continuum artifacts — this is a well-known aspiration shared by causal set theory, loop quantum gravity, and Regge calculus, none of which have fully succeeded. The specific challenge here is that the complex-analytic update rules must, in the appropriate limit, reproduce not just free QFT but the full interacting Standard Model with its specific gauge group U(1) × SU(2) × SU(3). The singularity-structure-to-forces mapping (poles → charges, branch cuts → flux tubes, essential singularities → confinement) is vivid and internally consistent at the level of analogy, but deriving non-abelian gauge invariance — which is the actual structure required — from complex function singularities is a research program, not an achieved result. The consistency of quantum chromodynamics with confinement is itself not fully proven analytically even in continuum QFT (it is a Millennium Prize problem), so attributing it to essential singularities in the update rule is a promissory claim of considerable ambition.

The Lorentz invariance problem is less severe here than in WV1 because complex-analytic structure provides more geometric rigidity than purely real or Boolean update rules — but it remains unresolved. The automaton must be shown to have a continuum limit with exact Lorentz invariance, not merely approximate invariance; the fine structure constant prediction would be undermined if the Lorentz-invariance correction and the fine structure constant correction were of comparable magnitude.

Overall WV15 occupies a distinct position: it has genuine mathematical content, a falsifiable near-term prediction, a clean unification thesis, and a physically motivated derivation target (α from residues). Its weaknesses are the standard ones for discrete-substrate theories, but here they are weaknesses of an incomplete program rather than fundamental category errors.

## Key Strengths

- The Wick rotation as change-of-basis is a mathematically precise identification, not an analogy — this is the worldview's most important conceptual contribution and gives it a genuine foothold in established physics
- The fine structure constant prediction is specific, algebraic in form, and falsifiable with current technology; it is the strongest near-term experimental prediction in the computational worldview family
- Complex-analytic update rules inherit a powerful formalism (residue theorem, analytic continuation, monodromy) that could in principle support quantitative derivations — the mathematical toolkit exists and is well-developed
- UV finiteness via discrete path integral is a cleaner claim than most quantum gravity approaches: finite sum vs. regulated continuum, with no free cutoff parameter
- The factor of i in quantum mechanics receives a genuine structural explanation rather than being treated as a formal convenience — this addresses a question standard QM simply accepts as given

---

## Critical Weaknesses

- The singularity-to-forces mapping (poles → charges, branch cuts → flux tubes, essential singularities → confinement) is asserted without any specific holomorphic function or derivation; non-abelian gauge invariance, which is the actual empirical target, is not obviously obtainable from singularity structure alone
- The fine structure constant prediction, while specific in form, has not been computed from any actual automaton — the claim that a residue of the "fundamental pole" yields α ≈ 1/137 requires a specified update rule, which is absent; the prediction is currently unfalsifiable in practice because there is no rule to compute residues from
- Lorentz invariance recovery from a discrete complex automaton is undemonstrated; the complex structure helps geometrically but does not solve the problem, and the known isotropy of space to 10⁻²⁷ precision sets an extremely high bar
- Automaton topology is entirely unspecified: what topology? what dimensionality? what symmetry group of the lattice? These choices determine the physics and none are given; the framework is a schema for a theory, not a theory
- The claim that the path integral is a finite sum is only as UV-finite as the automaton is discrete — but the continuum limit, which is needed to recover smooth spacetime at sub-Planck scales, reintroduces the question of whether divergences return; discrete theories that are UV-finite can still be IR-divergent or develop new continuum-limit pathologies
- Gravity as dynamical spacetime geometry is unaddressed; the framework discusses path integrals over geometries but does not explain how spatial geometry becomes dynamical (i.e., how Einstein's equations emerge) from complex update rules
- The algebraicity claim for α is in tension with the general expectation from quantum field theory that coupling constants receive contributions from all orders of perturbation theory, suggesting transcendental values; this tension is not acknowledged
