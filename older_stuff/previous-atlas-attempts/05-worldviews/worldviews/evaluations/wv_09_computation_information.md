## Assessment

Worldview 9 is the most developed representative of digital physics / it-from-bit thinking, combining Wolfram-style computational ontology with Zeilinger/Wheeler information-theoretic metaphysics. Its strongest move is the reinterpretation of conservation laws as checksum protocols and unitarity as computational consistency: this is not merely a metaphor but a structurally interesting claim — it suggests that the conservation laws we observe are *necessary* features of any self-consistent discrete computation, not contingent facts about our universe. The Page curve derivation sketch is also genuinely in the right ballpark of current theoretical work (island formula, replica wormholes), so the worldview is at least pointing in empirically live directions.

The worldview's central weakness is the gap between its conceptual vocabulary and actual physics. Terms like "decompression," "codebook," and "bandwidth constraint" carry intuitive weight but have no established map onto Hilbert space formalism, field theory, or differential geometry. Until "computational depth" can be given a precise definition that reduces to proper time in the GR limit, and until the "information graph" can be shown to reproduce Lorentz-invariant propagation, the framework remains a promissory note. Discrete spacetime models generically break Lorentz invariance at the Planck scale, and this worldview offers no mechanism to recover it — a serious empirical liability given tight constraints from gamma-ray burst observations (Fermi LAT).

The parsimony score is deliberately low: the framework does not actually eliminate ontological baggage, it relocates it. Positing an information graph as fundamental merely defers the question of what realizes the graph, what runs the computation, and why the initial state was what it was. These are not smaller mysteries than the ones the worldview purports to dissolve. The unification score is the genuine bright spot — the conceptual compression of Bekenstein, holographic, and Bremermann bounds into a single inequality is an insight that survives even if the broader program fails, and it connects to ongoing research in quantum gravity (holography, emergent spacetime).

---

## Key Strengths

- **Informatic unification:** The claim that the Bekenstein bound, holographic entropy bound, and Bremermann limit are all facets of a single computational-information inequality is a real and non-trivial insight, connecting black hole thermodynamics, quantum information, and relativistic causality.
- **Measurement and entanglement reframing:** The decompression account of measurement and mutual-codebook account of entanglement are the most conceptually crisp stories this framework generates; they correctly identify that measurement is irreversible classical information generation and that entanglement is a non-local correlation structure.
- **Black hole information:** The checksum-consistency argument for unitarity is structurally compatible with the current mainstream resolution (Page curve via island formula), giving the worldview contact with active theoretical physics.
- **Falsifiable pixelation prediction:** The informatic noise floor in gravitational-wave detectors is a genuine novel prediction that distinguishes this worldview from standard GR + QFT, even if the amplitude is not yet computed.

---

## Critical Weaknesses

- **No formalism:** The entire framework is verbal. Without equations, the worldview cannot make quantitative predictions, cannot be checked for internal consistency by formal methods, and cannot be compared rigorously against experiment. This is the most important limitation.
- **Lorentz invariance problem:** Graph-based discrete substrates generically break Lorentz invariance. Gamma-ray burst photon arrival time data (Fermi LAT, GRB 090510) already places constraints on Planck-scale Lorentz violation tighter than naive lattice models predict. This worldview does not address the problem.
- **Substrate regress:** Calling information fundamental does not eliminate ontological baggage — it requires an unexplained executor, an unexplained initial state, and an unexplained reason why the graph has the topology it does. The parsimony gain is illusory.
- **Derivation gap:** No Standard Model particle or coupling constant, no specific spacetime dimension, no cosmological constant value follows from the computational-information framework as stated. Until it can reproduce even one number from first principles, empirical adequacy remains a claim rather than a result.
- **Circularity in checksum argument:** The claim that unitarity is forced by computational consistency assumes a closed, deterministic computation — but this assumes unitarity to explain unitarity. The argument needs an independent motivation for why the underlying computation must be unitary.
