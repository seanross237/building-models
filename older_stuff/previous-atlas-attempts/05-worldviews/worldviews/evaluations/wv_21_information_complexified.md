## Assessment

WV21 is built around a genuinely sharp central observation: complex numbers are the native language of quantum mechanics, Wick rotation is an exact transformation between physical regimes, and the Lorentzian signature of spacetime can be viewed as a sign-alternating structure analogous to what emerges when you square a complex quantity. From this, the worldview proposes that nature's fundamental data type is complex-valued information, with the real part encoding spatial content and the imaginary part encoding temporal content. That is a crisp, provocative thesis. The identification of quantum phase with temporal information, quantum modulus with spatial information, and interference with the combination of those channels is conceptually coherent as far as it goes.

The problem is that the worldview stays almost entirely at the level of reinterpretation rather than derivation. The claim that compressing complex-valued data "automatically produces Lorentzian geometry as its efficient encoding format" is stated as a fact but is not a theorem. Complex data compression — Fourier analysis, wavelets — produces efficient representations of functions, not spacetime manifolds. To get a metric from compression you need an additional argument connecting information geometry to spacetime geometry, and that argument is not provided. Similarly, "entropy increase corresponds to diffusion of complex information into higher imaginary components" is an intriguing gloss on the second law, but without a Hamiltonian, partition function, or dynamical law governing the imaginary component, it is not a derivation of thermodynamic irreversibility — it is a relabeling of the mystery. The same pattern recurs throughout: the real/imaginary split is used to name the distinction between spatial and temporal without explaining why that split is stable, canonical, or unique.

The novel predictions are the clearest asset. The prediction that the arrow of time should weaken in Wick-exact thermal equilibria is specific enough to be falsifiable in principle — although identifying a physical system that is exactly Wick-equivalent rather than approximately so is nontrivial, and the signature of "weakened" temporal directionality would need to be operationally defined. The prediction of "purely imaginary information channels" producing superluminal temporal correlations is bold and distinguishes this worldview from standard QM, but it introduces a new physical carrier that is given no interaction Hamiltonian, no coupling constant, and no connection to any existing experimental anomaly. Without that, the prediction is unfalsifiable in practice despite being specific in language.

---

## Key Strengths

- **Central thesis is crisp and non-trivial.** The claim that the Lorentzian signature and quantum complex structure share a common origin in complex-valued information is not an obvious point — it connects two things (spacetime geometry and QM formalism) that are usually treated as structurally independent.
- **Reinterpretation of Wick rotation as a readout choice** is a genuine conceptual contribution. Rather than treating Wick rotation as a calculational trick, WV21 proposes it as a physical degree of freedom. This is in the same spirit as Hartle-Hawking no-boundary and Picard-Lefschetz path integral approaches, and is worth taking seriously.
- **Two testable predictions** are offered rather than one, and both are connected to real experimental domains (thermalization behavior and precision clock comparisons). This gives the theory a non-trivial falsification surface.
- **Parsimony is better than average** for this class of hybrid worldview. The theory does not posit new particles, hidden variables, or extra dimensions — it reinterprets existing mathematical structure.

---

## Critical Weaknesses

- **The real/imaginary decomposition is not canonical.** A complex number has no preferred decomposition into real and imaginary parts without a phase convention. If the "spatial" and "temporal" information content of reality depends on a choice of phase, the theory has a hidden gauge freedom it does not address. If the decomposition is supposed to be canonical, the worldview needs to specify what fixes it.
- **The derivation of the Lorentzian metric is not a derivation.** The argument that squaring complex information amplitudes produces the Minkowski signature relies on an analogy (cross-terms produce mixed signs) that breaks down immediately under scrutiny: squaring a generic complex number $z = a + ib$ gives $|z|^2 = a^2 + b^2$, which is positive-definite. Getting a minus sign requires specific algebraic choices (e.g., split-complex numbers, or a specific inner product structure) that are not specified in the worldview.
- **"Imaginary information diffusion" as entropy is undefined.** The proposal that entropy increase corresponds to information migrating from real to imaginary encoding needs a dynamical law. What governs the rate of migration? What prevents it from running backward? Until a Hamiltonian or master equation is given, this is a metaphor for irreversibility, not an explanation of it.
- **The purely imaginary channel prediction lacks a physical mechanism.** A channel that transmits "purely temporal data" with no spatial content requires a carrier field with a specific dispersion relation (infinite phase velocity, zero group velocity, or something more exotic). No such field is defined. Without a field equation, the prediction of superluminal temporal correlations cannot be computed, designed for, or falsified.
- **Compatibility with GR is unestablished.** The claim that Lorentzian geometry emerges from complex information compression requires that the imaginary sector decouples from the real sector in the classical limit to reproduce Einstein's field equations. No argument is given that this decoupling occurs or that the theory does not modify classical GR at solar-system scales where GR is precisely tested.
- **Interference explanation is circular.** The worldview says interference arises because complex data channels can combine constructively or destructively. But this is just restating that complex amplitudes interfere — it does not explain *why* physical observables obey Born Rule probabilities rather than some other function of the complex data.
