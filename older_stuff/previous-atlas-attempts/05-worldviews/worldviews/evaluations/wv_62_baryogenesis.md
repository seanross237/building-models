## Assessment

Worldview 62 attacks baryogenesis with genuine theoretical ambition: rather than invoking beyond-Standard-Model particles (leptoquarks, heavy Majorana neutrinos, BSM gauge bosons), it attempts to derive the Sakharov conditions from reinterpretations of existing physics — a two-boundary cosmology, complexified temporal causality, and a meta-thermodynamics of conservation laws. This is a conceptually interesting strategy, and the unification score of 4/5 reflects that it succeeds in connecting phenomena that are ordinarily treated as independent inputs.

The central narrative has genuine explanatory force at the qualitative level. The claim that CPT exactness follows from two-boundary dynamics rather than being a local symmetry is a real theoretical idea with precedent in two-state-vector formalism (Aharonov, Vaidman). The claim that a phase rotation in complexified causal structure at the Lorentzian transition selects a preferred orientation — and thereby selects matter — is a creative extension of Wick rotation as ontology. The claim that baryon number conservation is a frozen fluctuation of a higher-level thermodynamic process connects to ideas in the "landscape" of effective field theories, however informally.

However, the theory collapses at the level of mathematical precision in a way that is especially damaging for a claim as quantitatively sharp as baryogenesis. The baryon-to-photon ratio eta is measured to nine significant figures ($6.1 \times 10^{-10}$). Any theory that claims to explain baryogenesis must eventually produce this number. WV62 announces that eta should be calculable from a complexified phase angle and that it should be related to the Jarlskog invariant by a formula involving pi and the number of fermion generations — but neither claim can be evaluated without a formalized theory. The Jarlskog invariant is itself a measured quantity derived from CKM matrix elements; the relationship claimed here has no derivation and could easily be falsified if someone were to formalize the framework.

The internal consistency problems are significant. The two-boundary future condition is supposed to mandate departure from equilibrium, but the law-space thermodynamics picture is supposed to explain baryon number violation as a high-temperature fluctuation near the Big Bang. These are two different mechanisms for the same Sakharov condition (departure from equilibrium), and they need to be shown to be the same mechanism rather than two stories pasted together. The roles of Pauli exclusion and winding-number angular momentum in baryogenesis are completely unaddressed — they are listed among the component interpretations but appear nowhere in the explanatory narrative.

The most experimentally tractable prediction — that post-selected antimatter experiments at CERN should show reduced variance in CPT-sensitive observables — is genuinely sharp and near-term testable. ALPHA's hydrogen-antihydrogen spectroscopy and BASE's proton-antiproton magnetic moment comparisons have reached ppm-level precision and could in principle detect anomalous variance suppression. This is a real scientific target. However, the prediction is stated as a qualitative direction (anomalously small variance) without a predicted magnitude, which means an experiment would confirm the direction but could not do a precise numerical test of the theory's parameters.

The compatibility issues, particularly around retroactive constraint propagation and the two-boundary cosmology, are not insurmountable — Penrose's conformal cyclic cosmology and Hartle-Hawking no-boundary proposals show that cosmological boundary conditions can be given mathematically rigorous treatments. But WV62 inherits the theoretical costs of those frameworks without doing the work to embed itself in them.

## Key Strengths

- Derives all three Sakharov conditions from a single unified framework rather than treating them as independent inputs — baryon number violation from law-space thermodynamics, CP violation from complexified temporal causality, departure from equilibrium from two-boundary dynamics
- The ALPHA/BASE anomalous variance prediction is experimentally accessible with existing apparatus at current or near-future precision levels, making it a genuine near-term falsification target
- Connecting the origin of CP violation to the origin of temporal asymmetry via complexified causal structure is a non-trivial conceptual insight — the imaginary phase of time providing the physical asymmetry between matter and antimatter is internally motivated by Wick rotation taken seriously as ontology
- The law-space thermodynamics picture of conservation law emergence provides a natural account of why baryon number is conserved now but could be violated early — the "temperature" of law-space has cooled, freezing the symmetry — which is more explanatory than the Standard Model's treatment of baryon number as a contingently approximate symmetry
- No new particles, forces, or coupling constants are introduced — the theory attempts to derive the asymmetry from structural features of existing physics rather than extending the particle content

## Critical Weaknesses

- Zero mathematical formalization of any component: no action for complexified causal structure, no partition function for law-space thermodynamics, no derivation of the two-boundary cosmological constraint on baryon number — the theory cannot compute eta or any dimensionless ratio, making its quantitative predictions aspirational rather than actual
- The claim that eta is related to the Jarlskog invariant by a geometric factor involving pi and fermion generation count is stated without derivation; this relationship, if wrong when eventually computed, falsifies the theory's key prediction entirely
- Internal mechanism redundancy: two separate components (two-boundary future condition and law-space cooling) both address the same Sakharov condition (departure from equilibrium) without being shown to be the same mechanism or to produce the same answer
- Pauli exclusion (15.2) and angular momentum winding number (3.1) are listed as contributing interpretations but are completely absent from the explanatory narrative — their presence in the worldview selection is unaccounted for
- The retroactive constraint propagation from the future boundary conflicts with standard relativistic causality in a way that requires careful delimitation; extending it to cosmological baryogenesis means the entire universe's matter-antimatter ratio is being constrained by an unspecified future boundary condition whose physical content is not defined
- The Lorentzian transition phase-rotation mechanism for matter selection requires that the universe "rotates" through complexified signature space at a specific rate and in a specific direction — both the rate and the direction are free parameters with no independent constraint, giving the mechanism significant fine-tuning freedom
- No connection to leptogenesis: the Standard Model's most viable current baryogenesis mechanism runs through lepton asymmetry via heavy right-handed neutrino decays; WV62 does not engage with this mechanism or explain why its alternative would dominate
