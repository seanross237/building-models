# Q11 — Perovskite A-Site (Opus, Run 2)

## PLAN
1. **Extract constraints:** Must be *organic* A-site cations that *independently* form *3D* lead halide perovskites (APbX3). Governed by Goldschmidt tolerance factor (t ≈ 0.8–1.0), which depends on A-site cation radius.
2. **Baseline:** Cs+ (inorganic reference), MA+ (methylammonium), FA+ (formamidinium) are the three classic 3D perovskite formers — present in all options.
3. **Evaluate four candidate additions by ionic radius and experimental evidence:**
   - Aziridinium (~2.50 Å): Confirmed 3D AZPbBr3 (Becker et al., 2020). Valid.
   - Ethylammonium (~2.74 Å): Too large, forms lower-dimensional structures. Invalid.
   - Methylhydrazinium: Forms some 3D structures but debated classification. Not the canonical fourth.
   - Dimethylammonium (~2.72 Å): Too large, forms 1D/hexagonal. Invalid.
4. **Select:** Aziridinium is the experimentally confirmed fourth organic cation → Option B.

## END_PLAN

## ANSWER: B

**Reasoning:** The three established A-site cations for 3D lead halide perovskites are Cs+, MA+, and FA+. Recent experimental work (circa 2020) demonstrated that aziridinium (AZ+), a three-membered ring amine with an effective ionic radius (~2.50 Å) comparable to FA+, successfully forms a stable 3D perovskite AZPbBr3. This made it the fourth known cation capable of independently forming 3D lead halide perovskites. The other candidates (EA+, DMA+, MHy+) either have ionic radii too large for stable 3D perovskite formation or produce lower-dimensional structures.

**Confidence:** Medium-high. Aziridinium as the fourth 3D perovskite-former is a well-documented recent finding, and the size-exclusion argument against EA+ and DMA+ is robust. Minor uncertainty around methylhydrazinium, but the canonical answer in the literature points to aziridinium.
