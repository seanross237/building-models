1. Recall the 2D exciton binding energy formula: E_b(n) = R_y / (n - 1/2)^2 where R_y is the exciton Rydberg energy and n = 1, 2, 3, ...
2. Extract the 1s binding energy from the given data: the band gap is 3 eV and the 1s resonance peak is at 1 eV, so the 1s binding energy is E_b(1s) = 3 eV - 1 eV = 2 eV.
3. Solve for R_y using n=1: E_b(1) = R_y / (1 - 1/2)^2 = R_y / (1/2)^2 = 4 * R_y, so R_y = 2 eV / 4 = 0.5 eV.
4. Compute E_b for n=3: E_b(3) = R_y / (3 - 1/2)^2 = 0.5 / (2.5)^2 = 0.5 / 6.25 = 0.08 eV. The Rydberg energy for n=3 is 0.08 eV (i.e., the n=3 exciton binding energy is 0.08 eV; the exciton Rydberg constant itself is 0.5 eV).
